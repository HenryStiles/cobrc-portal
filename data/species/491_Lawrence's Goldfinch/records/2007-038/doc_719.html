Habitat

Residential area on the outskirts of Grand Junction, with mature deciduous trees, mostly widely spaced. Semidesert shrubland, lowland riparian forest, and pinyon-juniper forest in the near vicinity.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Eagle Optics 8x42 binoculars.
Distance:  Roughly 50 feet.
Light:  When bird perched on the top of the feeder hanger it was in direct morning sunlight. When it was feeding it was in slight shade. Sun was to the left and behind us, low.

Description of the Bird

Size and shape like Lesser Goldfinch. Significantly smaller than House Finch in direct comparison. Bill was pale. Clean glossy black cap and very small black chin, very sharp borders to both. Most of bird pale pearl gray. White undertail and whitish undertail coverts. Bright yellow patch on lower breast and upper belly, in kind of an oval shape. Wings and upperide of tail dark, nearly black. Strong yellow highlights in wing, including a large patch in the primaries and most of the coverts, connected by a thin strip of yellow. Tail was slightly forked.
Behaviors: Bird was visiting a thistle seed feeder, occasionally hanging upside down from the perch in order to reach the seed.
Call: Bird was silent.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Adult male Lawrence's Goldfinch is unmistakable. the finchlike bill and habit of hanging upside down to eat thistle seed eliminates Golden-winged Warbler and other non-finches. It is the only finch with yellow in the wings except for Pine Siskin, which was eliminated by the gray streakless back and belly, large yellow breast patch, and black cap and chin.
