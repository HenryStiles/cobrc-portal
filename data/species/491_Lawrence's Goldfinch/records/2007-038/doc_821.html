Description of the Bird

bird was about the same size and shape as nearby Lesser Goldfinches, and noticeably smaller in direct comparison to House Finch. The bill was pale pinkish, short and conical. The legs were also dusky-flesh colored.

The bird had a glossy black forecrown and chin, connected over the base of the bill, creating a masked look. The rest of the head, back, and most of the underparts were pale pearly gray. There was a fairly large patch of bright yellow on the chest. The wings and tail were black, with a large patch of yellow in the primaries, and on the greater and secondary coverts. The tail was slightly forked, and from underneath had two patches of white. The undertail coverts were white, and the lower belly whitish, slightly paler than the pearly gray on the remainder of the underparts.
Behaviors: The bird was seen sitting on top of the pole the thistle feeders were on, and on the feeders themselves. When feeding, it would hang upside down on the feeder perch.

During the second observation the bird was also observed feeding near the ground on seeds in the garden, and also sitting on the top of a large bush and calling as described above.
Call: None heard during the first observation. During the second the bird spent a few minutes perched on the top of the large bush near the feeder and gave three different calls: a clear, high pitched, metallic "tink", like the sound of striking a tiny bell; another high, metallic "tink" that was flatter in tone and shorter in duration; and most often a falling and rising "tuwee?". This particular call appears to most closely match the courtship call from the literature, but is not a perfect match.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other species were ruled out by the small size, conical bill shape, black mask, yellow patch on the chest, overall gray coloration, and extensive yellow in the wings.
