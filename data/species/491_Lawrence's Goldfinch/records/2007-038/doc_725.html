Description of the Bird

The bird was with other goldfinches, and was a similar shape and size. The forehead, top of the head and chin were jet black, the rest of the head and back was gray. The bird had bright yellow wingbars, which contrasted with the black primaries. There were also more yellow feathers below the wingbars. The upper and middle breast was also a bright yellow. The bill, legs and feet were gray. Didn't notice much on the tail; it wasn't the brightest part of the bird.
Behaviors: Sitting on a thistle feeder, and feeding from it mostly in an upsidedown position. Also seen briefly in a nearby tree, and flying to and from the feeder.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Alternate or breeding

Similar Species Discussion

American Goldfinch is bright yellow and black at this time of year. Lesser Goldfinch has a black cap, and is completely yellow below - also has white, not yellow, wingbars. The females of these species do not have black on the forehead or chin. Pine Siskin is mostly brown , not gray and yellow.
