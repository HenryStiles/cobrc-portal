Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Binoculars, Scope

Light:  Good (sunny, clear skies)

Description of the Bird

I first scanned the reservoir with my scope from a distance, looking for shorebirds. Along the northern edge of the reservoir I noticed a coot-like bird with a conspicuous red shield on its face. I walked to the dam that lines the southern edge of the reservoir and  looked at the bird again through both my scope and my binoculars. It had a dark/black body, brownish back, white stripe along the side, red shield, red beak with yellow tip, yellow-green legs. It did not vocalize. Most of the times I saw it, it was foraging along the edge of the reservoir and wending in and out of the cattails that grow along the northern edge. A few times, it swam short distances. Once I saw it walk onto a log.

 

Of note, the bird continued to be seen by multiple observers after my observation dates. It was still present at the same location as of 4/24/2022. 

Similar Species Discussion

Also considered: American Coot, but the red shield immediately set it apart, and then other distinguishing field marks were also obvious.

Previous Experience

This was my first sighting of a Common Gallinule, i.e. it was a life bird. I had previously encountered the Common/Eurasian Moorhen (Gallinula chloropus) during visits to Germany.
