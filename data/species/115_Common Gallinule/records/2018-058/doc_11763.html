Description of the Bird

This was a small sized water bird the size of a coot. The back was dark brown. The underside of the bird was black with a white rump. The distinct bill was red with a yellow tip. The flank had a thin white stripe.

The bird was on the water hiding in the cattails. It finally swim from right to left across the back edge of the open area at 7:21 am. It did make another brief appearance at 7:29. The first observation was for about 15 seconds. Because it was surrounded by tall cattails, I did not get a phone. However, Michael Lester who was on the bottom rung of a ladder did manage to get a few photos. 


The bird did not vocalize during these two sightings.

Similar Species Discussion

American Coot was eliminated due to the red bill. 
A Purple Gallinule was eliminated due to the lack of purple.
Teal species were eliminated due to the shape of the bird and the red bill.

Resources Used

None - I am very familiar with this species. I lived in Florida for 12 years and saw Common Gallinules in my back yard on Crooked Lake. When I got home, I compared this bird with the Sibley Guide.

Previous Experience

While living in Florida for 12 years, I encountered this species at least once a week. There were a few pairs on the lake that we lived on.
