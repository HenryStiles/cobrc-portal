Description of the Bird

The bird was already sitting 1/2 out of the cattails when I arrived. The bird was about the size of an American Coot. The bird looked blackish with a red shield/bill. The bill portion was thicker like a coot and had a orangish yellow tip. The undertail portion at the back was pure white. There was a thin white line along the sides/flanks. It was initially preening and then slowly walked across the open water area while several times picking something from the matted weed edge. It then walked into the cattails on the other side of the open water.

Similar Species Discussion

The shape and movements were very suggestive of a member of the rail family. The purple gallinule has a lighter body color of bluish purple and does not have the white line along the sides. A Purple Swamphen is much larger, has a lighter body color of bluish purple and also a light grayish head. An American Coot is also black, but has a white shield and bill and no white line along the sides. All other rails have either a short stubby bill or a longish spiky thin bill and all, except the small Black Rail, have a brownish body with light barring.
