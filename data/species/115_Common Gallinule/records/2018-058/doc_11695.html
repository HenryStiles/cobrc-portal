Description of the Bird

Large dark bird foraging in the marsh. A Virginia Rail passed by and the Gallinule way easily twice as long and much larger in size, therefore about 15-18 inches in size. Dark bird with distinctive, bright red bill/shield on face. White stripe on side of body and white undertail. No vocalizations. The bird would forage near the edge of the reeds for a couple minutes, disappear into the reeds and reappear a couple minutes later. It did not swim at all, but foraged on foot. Several good looks for a minute or so at a time, totally about five minutes of observation in the 10 minutes I was there. Poor photos included.

Similar Species Discussion

It had a similar size and shape as a American Coot. bright red bill/shield and white stripe on side eliminated AMCO. No other common local species considered.
