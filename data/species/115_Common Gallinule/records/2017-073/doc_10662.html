Description of the Bird

I could see that the beak was reddish-orange with a yellow-orange tip, which ruled out the American Coot.  The shape of the bill was a little longer and thinner than a coot's or sora's, but not as long and thin as a Virginia Rail's bill.  I didn't see the mottling on the plumage that I would expect from a rail, either.  The only markings I noted on an otherwise solid-dusky colored body were the 2-3 white dashes on the flanks/near wings, and a little bit of gray/white sprinkled on the face near the beak.  The tail was very short with a few white marks as well.
