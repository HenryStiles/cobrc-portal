Description of the Bird

Coot-like waterbird with dark gray back and undersides with bright red frontal shield and white upper flank markings separating the lower brown back from lighter gray flanks.  Wings gray at base but chocolate brown otherwise as seen in flight and while swimming. White undertail coverts seen alos. Flies 2-3 times but only for a very short length, 10 meters or so.  Bobbed head when swimming which it did a lot (the swimming part).  Pecked on the water surface where a mat of algae and pond weeds were common. No calls heard.

Similar Species Discussion

Purple Gallinule is noticiably deep purple which this bird was not. Purple Swamphen very large and very purple.
