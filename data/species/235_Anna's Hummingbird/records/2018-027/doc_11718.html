Description of the Bird

Green hummingbird, appearing - overall - a bit larger/heftier then BC or Rufous Hummingbird. 
Uppersides dull green. Underparts gray with broad dull green sides. Throat grayish with iridescent feathers occupying most of middle, flashing red to red-pink in proper light. White dash behind eye. No buff on sides nor rufous on tail. Tail with white tips to at least outer two feathers. Inner primaries narrow, but not to degree of Archilochus hummingbirds. Bird's shape, overall color, and throat pattern reminded me very much of Anna's Hummingbirds in my yard when I lived in western WA.
The above is from notes, not from photos

Inner primaries broader than in Archilochus, not as trim as in those species. Inner primaries more even in width than seen in BTAH. Bird in primary molt and tail molt. Wings nearly reach tip of tail 

Similar Species Discussion

Archilochus - differently sized and shaped inner primaries
Broad-tailed - is longer tail with rust at base of outer rectrices. Sides not as dark, often buff washed
Costa's - Paler, not pink/red on throat, lacks broad green sides

Molt timing is typical of Anna's and atypical for members of other widespread genera of US hummingbirds
