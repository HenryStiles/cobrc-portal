Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized hummingbird, a bit larger than a Rufous Hummingbird would be, noticeably smaller than a Magnificent Hummingbird, and about the same size as a Broad-tailed Hummingbird. The overall shape was dumpy compared to that of a Selasphorus hummingbird. The bill was relatively short, maybe 25 percent longer than the length of the head, and completely black. The legs and eyes were also black.

The forecrown of the bird was gray, with a scaly look. There were a few iridescent pink feathers that were only visible when the bird was looking at us. The rear crown was a mix of iridescent green feathers and grayer feathers. There was a spot of white both behind and in front of the eye, below which there was a inconspicuous dark gray mask. The throat was pale gray, with white edges to the feathers giving it a scaly look. There was a patch of iridescent pink feathers in the center of the throat, and a few, scattered pink feathers along the lower margin and rear edge of the throat. The upper chest was whiter than the throat and other feathers of the underparts, giving the bird a faint white collared look. The flanks of the bird were broadly iridescent green, with a narrow stripe of off white between the flanks. The undertail coverts were white.

The back and rump of the bird was iridescent green. The wings were black, and about as long as the tail. The uppertail was iridescent green, the underside was mostly black, with large white tail tips on the outer feathers.
Behaviors: The bird was mostly observed visiting a feeder, which it would visit every 15-20 minutes. It would also perch on branches nearby for a few seconds before and after visiting the feeders; when not in the area it flew off to points unknown.
Call: The bird was occasionally heard to give a fairly hard call note.
Plumage: molting into alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of hummingbird were ruled out by the pink feathering on the throat; a number of species such as Magnificent, Blue-throated, Green Violet-ear, etc., were ruled out by the smaller size.

Costa's Hummingbird was ruled out by the larger size, pink feathering on the throat, and less dumpy shape.

Rufous and Allen's Hummingbirds were ruled out by the lack of rufous anywhere on the bird, larger size, and pink feathering on the throat.

Broad-tailed Hummingbird was ruled out by the lack of rufous in the sides, dumpier shape, lack of rufous in the tail, and pink in the crown and further back on the sides of the throat.

Ruby-throated and Black-chinned Hummingbirds were ruled out by the larger size, dumpier shape, and pink in the throat and crown.
