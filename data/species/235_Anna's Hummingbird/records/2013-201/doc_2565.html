Description of the Bird

Location:  Just north of Purgatoire Ave 100 yards east of NE corner of Purgatoire Ave & Colorado Blvd in Colorado City, SW Pueblo County.

Behavior: Perched on twig atop 10' bush occasionally darting from perch like a flycatcher & catching gnats before returning to same perch. 

Voice: Bird made no sound.

This Hummingbird was about same size as Broad-tailed Hummer but with a larger head, thicker neck, and more pot-bellied stockier appearance. This comparison is also true compared with a nearby female Black-chinnedHummer(BCHU), which appeared slightly smaller than the Anna's. Anna's bill also appeared shorter than the BCHU's.

The Anna's was greenish above from crown to upper tail coverts with dapple gray underparts becoming slightly greenish toward the flanks near the wings. Throat was heavily spotted dark gray but showed no reddish or rose colored tones sometimes present on some immature males.
 
Face had dark loral line and fairly dark gray auriculars giving bird a noticeable masked appearance contrasting with whitish supercilium and post ocular white spot. I later read in Howell, Hummingbirds of N.America(2002) that this masked appearance is often typical of immature male Anna's.

Tail was mostly black except for small white tips on outer rectrices; interior rectrices were green. Tail just slightly projected past wing tips.

Dark primaries on folded wing of perched bird were noticeably thicker than those of nearby BCHU. The thick wings of Annas tapered to rounded tips.

There was no buff or rufous colors observed on underparts or tail of this Anna's.

Similar Species Discussion

Black-chinned Hummer females most often have whitish virtually unmarked underparts and appear smaller headed with more narrow primaries than Anna's. Overall a much more slender appearing bird than Anna's. Immature male Black chinned has more diffused speckled throat and lack the heavily speckled and dapple gray underparts of Anna's.

Immature Broad-tails have buffy underparts and rufous in basal portion of tail. Anna's I saw lacked any rufous or buff colors anywhere on bird. Anna's tail projects less beyond wing tips than Broad-tail's more noticeable tail projection.

Costa's females & immatures are paler faced and lack the dapple gray underparts of Anna's. Costa's wings do not project beyond tail like Anna's.

Anna'sxCosta's hybrids: These birds do sometimes hybridize but I've never seen one and have no experience with this hybrid. All I can say is I saw no Costa's plumage nor size characteristics on the Anna's described in this report.
