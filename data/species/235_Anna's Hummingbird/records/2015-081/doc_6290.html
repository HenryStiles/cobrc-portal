Description of the Bird

I initially observed a " silent" hummingbird(no metallic noise when flying like broad-tailed) flying around feeder; it perched on low aspen limb near feeder. I looked thru bins at the bird and immediately noticed red gorget and crown, notched tail. Got great looks multiple times for approximately two hrs. It fought off both broad-tailed and black-chinned hummingbirds coming to feeder. I referenced Sibleys, Peterson's "Hummingbirds of North America" and iPhone app iBird Pro:North America to confirm ID. Pics on iPhone, will attempt to attach.

Similar Species Discussion

See previous note but pretty obvious since had red crown and gorget. I looked at pics of other hummingbirds for comparison, none did.
In this area I have seen the "BIG FOUR": Black-chinned, broad-tailed, rufous and calliope in the past; well acquainted with these and knew this bird was different.
