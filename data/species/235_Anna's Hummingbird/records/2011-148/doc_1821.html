Description of the Bird

Small hummingbird (about 4" in length), with a moderately short (about 1 inch), straight, black bill. Green on upperparts turning to gray on front (above bill), with matt-black wings. Upperparts of outer tail feathers (R3-R5) basally gray-green, with uneven subterminal blackish band, and variable white terminus ranging from almost no white (R3) to large white tip (R5). Underparts off-white except for white patch on the lower belly, faint dark-green spotting on sides of belly and breast, and triangular patch of dark scales on the center of the throat. The eye was black, with a dark gray diffuse auricular patch extending back from the eye, and a small black patch of feathers in front of the eye. A thin whitish line bordered the upper side of the eye, and the auricular patch, expanding into a bright white spot just behind the eye.
Behaviors: Perched on a thin branch of a bush or a tree near a hummingbird feeder, with frequent prolonged visits to the feeder, where it mostly perched while feeding, and occasionally hovered near the feeder.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Small size eliminated Blue-throated and Magnificent Hummingbirds, and Plain-capped Starthroat. Lack of rufous in the tail eliminated Rufous, Allens and Broad-tailed Hummingbirds. Lack of rufous in flanks eliminated Calliope Hummingbird. Straight bill eliminated Lucifer, Black-chinned and Costa's Hummingbirds. White line over the eye ruled out Ruby-throated Hummingbird. Additional supporting field marks were the gray (not white) underparts, the neat patch of dark feathers on the center of the throat, the uneven subterminal dark tailband, and the pattern of white spots on the outer rectices.
