Description of the Bird

After recognizing the Anna's call he came to the feeder. The stock head appearance was very different from the resident Broad-tails and Black-chinned Hummingbird. When his back was to me you could see the stock appearance and the more squared tail that extended past his wings. There was a definite lack of any kind of rufous coloring to the bird. He was very gray chested with a hint of greenish color on sides or flanks. The gorget was immature transitioning to adult. Rosy spots on the head and a much fuller gorget on the center of throat.
Behaviors: The bird feed on several plants including sage and sunset hyssop. When he came to the feeder the lack of tail pump was obvious. He visited the feeder every 20-30 minutes during his stay. When not feeding he sat on the top of the apple tree singing and eating small flying insects.
Call: First noticed the bird by its beezy-beezy song. Very familiar with this song from the travels in California and Arizona
Plumage: immature male
