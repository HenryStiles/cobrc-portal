Description of the Bird

I observed the bird through a spotting scope at 20X at a distance of 30 feet, while it was perched.

It was a medium-sized, relatively stocky hummingbird with a greenish back, a grayish forecrown, and a straight, relatively short black bill. The top of the head and the nape were green.

The underparts were a somewhat dingy off-white color, with pale greenish-gold flanks and a triangle-shaped gorget (tapering to a point at the top). I did not see any color in the gorget while the bird was perched, in overcast conditions.

The face showed a bold white spot to the rear of the eye. On close examination, I could see that the spot extended behind, not above the eye.

The forehead had a very gentle slope.
Behaviors: flying and feeding like a hummingbird

Similar Species Discussion

The main species to rule out are Costa's, Black-chinned, and Ruby-throated. The key field marks I used were:
1) Female bird (white tail spots) and small pink gorget
2) Very gentle slope to the forehead
3) Green cap and gray forecrown
