Description of the Bird

An immature bird was first observed coming to a feeder in the late morning by sunlight reflecting off 'gorget' feathers on the side and top of its head. The bird was a small, gorgeted and green backed type hummingbird. The hummer had a partial rose colored gorget with some rose colored feathers in both auriculars and multiple rose colored feathers in the crown. The head and neck was otherwise a dull greenish gray with a white post ocular spot. Under parts were a dull gray with grayish green terminal ends to the feathers.

Similar Species Discussion

Most N American hummers were eliminated by the presense of irridescent feathers on the crown. Costa's was eliminated by the color.
