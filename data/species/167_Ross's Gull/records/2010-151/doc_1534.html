Description of the Bird

very small gull with a rosy blush to its ventral surface. Petite all-black bill, very long gray wings at rest; light gray underwings with broad white trailing edge to secondaries and inner primaries. Black tips on outer primaries without mirrors from ventral view. In flight, and when close, grayish collar; tail all white and wedge-shaped. Legs reddish with darker orange-red feet. Face with smudge around dark eye. Auricular dark gray spot. Very broad white trailing edge when dorsal view of wings presented.
Behaviors: Pelagic flight mode - hunted close to the water, plunge-diving at least 8 times in a one 30-minute period. Photographed with entire body, except wings underwater.

Search and probe mode: Oberved for 5 hours moslty walking on sandy, very small gravel island where waves continuosly washed wave scum onto the edge of the island. Bird walked in a pigeon-toed fashion up and down the edges of the beach, probing every 20-30 seconds, mostly picking at the surface and not insurting bill into the sand. Also drifted with small waves as they "crashed" onto shore in that zone where there was constantly churing of the sand there.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult winter

Similar Species Discussion

Little Gull is the species that comes closest in size and structure. Little Gull does NOT have the wedge-shaped tail. Adult Little Gull would have much darker underwings. Adult Little Gull would show shorter wings, would likely have a dark cap, and the dark remains of a collar would be more pronounced (extend farther down hindneck) than on a ROGU.
