Description of the Bird

A small gull; considerably smaller than Ring-billed Gulls which were present. Almost completely white above, except for a little black round the eye and a very small ear patch which was well behind the eye. The underwings were almost all a dark gray; not black. The all white tail was very noticeably wedge shaped, which was easily seen when the bird banked and turned. The bill was small and appeared to be black; too distant to see the color of the legs.
Behaviors: Flying over the center of Cherry Creek Res and very regularly going down to the surface to pick a morsel up. Would stay for just a few seconds and then take off again. 3 or 4 times the bird sat on the surface for several minutes at a time.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Closest species is Little Gull, but that bird nearly always has some black on top of the head in winter, the ear patch is larger, the underwings are black and the wingtips are more rounded than this bird. Tail is not diamond shaped.
Ivory Gull has white underwings and square tail.
Bonaparte's Gull is larger, has black wingtips and a very conspicuous white flash on the forewing.
All other NA gulls are darker above than this bird.
