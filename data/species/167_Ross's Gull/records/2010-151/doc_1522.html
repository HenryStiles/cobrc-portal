Description of the Bird

A small gull, Bonaparte's Gull sized with long, pointed wings. Mantle was pale uniform grey with concolorus wings, all the way out to the tips of the primaries as seen from above. A white trailing edge to the wing was wider near the primary/secondary junction, making the white trailing edge look slightly wedge shaped from above on flying bird. The under-side of the bird was white with a pink tinge. The underwings were gray, but appeared dark in flying bird. The legs were dark orange, bill was small and black. The head was white with a dark smudge around the eye, and a small dark ear spot. Some pale grey encompassed the neck and upper breast. The tail was white, long and wedge shaped due to the central retrices being longer than the rest.
Behaviors: The bird was flying in a bouyant fashion around the lake, dipping to the water for food and occasionally landing on the water, much like Bonaparte's Gull behavior.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Little Gull has dark underwings, but wings are rounded with white edge extending to primaries and has short squared tail.
Bonaparte's Gull has white wedge on leadging edge of wing and has pale underwings with small black tips on primaries. Neither bird has pink hue.
