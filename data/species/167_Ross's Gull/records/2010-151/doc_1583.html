Description of the Bird

Small pale gull, the size of nearby Bonaparte's Gulls. Body whitish with tinge of pink. Tail white, distinctly wedge-shaped with central tail feathers much longer than outer. Upperparts pale gray, nearly concolor from mantle to wingtips. Whitish trailing edge to wing on upper & lower surfaces, no black wingtips. Underwing darkest part of bird but still fairly pale gray. Wings long- when walking or swimming gave the bird a very attenuated appearance. Legs orange-pink. Bill short, straight, black. Eye black, looks larger due to black smudging around eye. Gray post-occular spot, elongated vertically.
Behaviors: Bird spent much time foraging somewhat tern-like with buoyant flight alternating with short hovers and shallow plunges. Also spent time resting on the water and on a small sand bar. Also foraged in surf zone of sand bar reminiscent of a plover feeding style.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Small size rules out most gulls. Bonaparte's were around for direct comparison and showed dark wingtips & more patterning in wings among other differences. Little Gull shows much darker underwing and would appear significantly smaller than Bonaparte's. Shape of tail diagnostic- no other gull that I'm aware of shows this.
