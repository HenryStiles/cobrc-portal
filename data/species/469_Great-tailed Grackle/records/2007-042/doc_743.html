Description of the Bird

General impression of a very large Common Grackle. Differing from Common Grackle, this bird had an extremely long tail, approximately the same length as the body. The all-black bill was also much longer and deeper than expected for a Common. Otherwise, the black plumage had a glossy purplish-blue sheen, more obvious on the head, mantle, and wing coverts than the rest of the body. It also had yellow eyes and black legs. In flight, the tail was deeply v-shaped.
Behaviors: Walking along the edge of Rio Blanco SWA lake, eventually flying to a small patch of cattails in the northwest corner.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: adult
