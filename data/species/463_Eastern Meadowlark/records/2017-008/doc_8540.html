Description of the Bird

I first noticed the bird when we flushed a small group of meadowlarks as we drove slowly along the dirt road from JM State Park and SWA. When it landed in front of vehicle (on my side) it showed far more white than its companions or what one might expect in a WEME (more than 2/3 of spread tail). After landing, the bird stood rather upright on ground about 15-20 feet away and was easily visible. The cheek was bright white including the malar, with absolultely no intrusion of yellow into the white malar. Crown striped brown and white, showing more contrast than nearby WEMEs. The line behind the eye was quite blackish and the supercilium white, like the cheek. The throat was yellow. The wing coverts and tertials were barred like WEME. Later I could see yellow on chest and belly with the black chest V of a meadowlark. The sides white with blackish markings, but I did not look closely here (so, can't say streaks, spots, black, brown, etc). After 30 or so seconds, the bird flew landing about 25 feet away facing largely away. The brown central tail feathers were barred but lacked dark longitudinal markings (ie, parallel to shaft of feathers). The uppertail coverts were striped like EAME (that is, had dark along shaft of feathers with some dark cross markings. Buzzy zhit call heard twice at close range. Dave and I gave pursuit, flushing the bird several more times; again, the extra white on tail was quite clear, when we did flush it, but we wer unable to relocate bird on ground. Note description was written within 5 minutes of the bird last being seen

Similar Species Discussion

Western Meadowlark should not have such a white cheek and supercilium (which is a primary mark used by Will Russell for picking Lilian's out from Westerns in AZ). The dark markings on the head were darker than those of Western Meadowlark as well, though not quite as black as I'd expect in nominate EAME. The amount of white on the tail was beyond that seen in WEME. The pattern on the uppertail coverts was typical of EAME (both nominate and Lilian's). The call note was typical of Eastern and unlike anything I've heard from a WEME

Nominate Eastern Meadowlark should have had some markings parallel to feather shaft on tail, tertials, and wing coverts. The bird appeared to have too much white on tail for nominate Eastern, but that is just an impression

I'd expect a hybrid to be more intermediate, with atypical call and less white in tail than this bird (in addition to other ID marks)

Note that I am uncertain how to tell a first-year bird from adult (hence my age = ?) at this season.
