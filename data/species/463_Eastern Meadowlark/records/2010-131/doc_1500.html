Description of the Bird

I was jogging at the Craver Middle School Athletic Field and did not have binoculars. But I heard the above call notes well, which best confirms ID.

I saw a mixed flock of Western(WEME) & Eastern Meadowlarks(EAME) fly off the field several times and land on a fence. As they flew off I heard the Eastern's call notes.

The backs and wings of the EAME were light sandy brown with diffuse speckling showing little contrast with the rest of back. I saw no difference between upperparts of EAMEs & WEMEs. I did notice the white on outer tail feathers of EAMEs were noticeably broader than WEMEs even without binoculars. Upperparts & tail feather patterns suggest Lilian's subspecies.

When perched on fence, I didn't notice any difference in underparts between the 2 species possibly due to not having binoculars. All birds were pale yellow with a somewhat faded slaty black "V" on breast. Crowns on all birds were light with contrasting dark stripes. Bills on all birds were fairly long and slim.
Behaviors: Birds flew off athletic field landing on split rail fence, then returned to field where they foraged a bit before flying off for good.
Call: A loud rising "veet" not unlike the single rising note call of House Finch; also a burry high pitched rattle "zitzitzitzitzit."
Plumage: probable Lilian's

Similar Species Discussion

The best distinction I noticed were the EAMEs call notes. I've never heard the House Finch like "Veet" from a WEME. The WEME's rattle call is noticeably lower "churchurchur" etc. in contrast to EAME rapid "zitzitzit" etc.

The only difference I could see without binoculars were the broader white tail feathers of EAME in contrast to narrower ones of WEMEs.
