Description of the Bird

Medium-sized songbird with long, dull, slate-colored, slender and pointed bill. Dark crown with median stripe. Yellow from eye to bill. Sides of face white with black post-ocular stripe. Yellow throat. Upperparts buff, brown, and black with streaks and bars. Underparts bright yellow, sides and flanks, white, streaked with black. Black V-shaped breast band. Short tail with extensive white in outer rectrices.
Behaviors: Sitting on fence, calling and singing. Foraging in grass. Sitting on telephone wire, singing.
Call: Heard both song and call. As described in National Geo.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Western Meadowlark ruled out by voice and also extensive white on face and tail of this bird.
