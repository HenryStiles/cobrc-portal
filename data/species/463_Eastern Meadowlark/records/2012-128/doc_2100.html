Description of the Bird

The bird was bird noticed by Alison Kondler when she asked me what was making "that call". I said that right off I did not know. We found the caller at the top of a tree on the north side of the cemetery and “discovered” it to be a meadowlark.
This meadowlark was SEEN purched atop a tree and the only call notes heard are best described as "Bzzzrt", VERY different from any Western Meadowlark. The bird called 20+ times. Each time the same call. No other vocalizations were heard. The bird’s face was whitish, more so that the Western Meadowlarks we saw in the area. Unfortunately when the bird flew it flew away from me it flew along a line of trees at the edge of the cemetery that prevented me from seeing the tail. If someone has documented that Western Meadowlarks can make that same call, I'll change my identification to WEME/EAME.
About 20 Western Meadowlarks were also seen in the fields in the area and all of them make the “Cluck” or “Chuck” call notes. We could not relocate this birds after spending about 20 minutes.
Behaviors: "Sat" and called from the top of a tree, then flew down and across the street to the west into a large plowed field with many Western Meadowlarks.
Call: "Bzzzrt" call note. Not at all like the "Chuck" or "Cluck" notes of the numerous Western Meadowlarks in the fields around the Cemetery
Plumage: N/A

Similar Species Discussion

Western Meadowlark - Darker face not "whitish"; Call note is a "Chuch" or "Cluck" not "Bzzrt" like Eastern Meadowlark.

Previous Experience

I have seen and heard Eastern Meadowlarks in Northeastern Colorado many years ago when a small population live near Jumbo Reservoir near Crook, Colorado. I have seen and heard many Eastern Meadowlarks in Arizona, Florida and other states. I have seen and heard THOUSANDS of Western Meadowlarks in Colorado over the last 27 years of birding (they also nest behind the house and I've NEVER heard the "Bzzrt" call from any of them).
