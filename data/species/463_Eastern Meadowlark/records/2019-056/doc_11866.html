Description of the Bird

The bird was first detected by song.  It was singing the pure, slurred, flutelike songs of Eastern Meadowlark (recording included with this report), familiar to me from when I lived in Oklahoma.  After a little searching, I located the bird perching and singing on fenceposts at the edge of a hay field.  The bird sang constantly while I was there.  I did not hear it call.

The visual characteristics also support the ID of Eastern rather than Western Meadowlark.  The bird had the high-contrast face of a breeding-plumaged Eastern Meadowlark, including a bold, black postocular stripe and a bright white lower half of the face and malar.  

Similar Species Discussion

Western Meadowlark was considered but was eliminated by song and facial characteristics.
