Description of the Bird

Bird was perched atop tall stalk like other common Western Meadowlarks in the area, but song sounded off so I took a picture. Upon inspection it had a whiter cheek area and less yellow throat than Western variety. 

Similar Species Discussion

Western Meadowlark. Song was difinitively different. Had white malar area.

Resources Used

Checked Merlin Bird ID, asked an ornithologist friend, Josh Engel, for ID based on my picture and description. Bird was found again by one of his contacts on the evening of 6/12 in the same location, she was able to get audio record of the song and call. 

Previous Experience

I am pretty familiar with the Western Meadowlark's song and appearance having run into them quite frequently at the Westerly Wetlands, Rocky Mountan Arsenal, and Barr Lake. The Eastern sounded very distinct, which caught my attention.
