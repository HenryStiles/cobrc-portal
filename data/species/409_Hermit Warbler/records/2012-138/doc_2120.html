Description of the Bird

Warbler shaped bird, warbler bill. Smaller than a YRWA. Round yellow face, dirty-white throat, chest, belly, vent. Crown slaty-gray-green which extends down through the mantle. Lower back and tail dark slaty-gray with two prominent white wing-bars. No streaking on sides.
Behaviors: Nothing notable. Acted like a warbler. Quiet.
Call: None
Plumage: Juv.

Similar Species Discussion

Townsends Warbler: Has obvious dark facial patch, more greenish back and yellow on the upper breast, streaking on sides.

Black-throated Green Warbler: Very green on crown and upper back, yellow in vent area, streaking on sides.

Golden-cheeked Warbler: Dark bar through the eye and streaking on the sides.
