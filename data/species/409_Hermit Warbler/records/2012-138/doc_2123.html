Description of the Bird

Warbler by bill, size and foraging methods. Yellow-headed with diffuse light gray auricular patch surrounded on all sides by yellowish including above the base of the dark bill. Back was dull gray and wings were greenish gray with two whitish wingbars. Breast was dull whitish with hints of buff at throat and just barely hints of darker flank streaks but not usually visible. Throat was very light buff yellow but it could also appear just dull whitish. Tail was about the same color as the back and the legs were dark gray.
Behaviors: Actively foraged in weeds and sunflowers, sometimes on the ground. It did not appear to be interested in the seeds but on probably searced for the insects on the plants and probably the insects on the ground under the plants. Often foraged below eye level but also seen high in the insect-eaten leaves of a Siberian elm. Active not staying still or more than a second or two.
Call: call notes when it flew but I can't describe them other than to say they were unique and not like the YRWAs, NAWAs, and BTBW nearby
Plumage: 1st year female

Similar Species Discussion

Olive Warbler - brighter yellow with dark mask through the eye and yellow extends down to the breast

Black-throated Green Warbler - always has the yellow at the distal ends of the flanks just in front of the undertail coverts. Also 1st winter female has noticeable dark, but indistinct wide flank streaks.

Townsend's Warbler - always has defined darker auricular patch surrounded by yellow and with diffuse wide flank streaks.

TOWA x HEWA - this fairly common hybrid should have some flank streaking and more yellow on breast. They also should show a yellow wash on the flanks and a different, brighter yellow on the face (Warblers, Dunn and Garrett).

Resources Used

Warblers by Dunn and Garrett, Big Sibley, Stokes Guide.

Previous Experience

I have seen a lot of Hermit Warblers when I worked and lived in northernmost CA, a common migrant in spring, but I have little experience with fall birds and I have never knowingly seen the HEWA x TOWA hybrid. I have seen a few BTGWs in fall but not many.
