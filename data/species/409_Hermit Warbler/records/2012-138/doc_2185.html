Description of the Bird

The photos, especially in aggregate (many observers over several days) would provide more detail.
The crown and back looked olive-gray and utterly unmarked to me; the crown seemed a bit more olive than the back, which typically appeared grayer, but this was variable with light. In some photos, vague subtle streaking is visible on back and some fine stippling on crown, but I did not appreciate this in the field.

The "face" - supercilium, auriculars, post-auriculars, malar, submalar, etc - appeared, overall, lemon-yellow with the exception of an olive wash in the auriculars and moustachial, the apparent intensity of which varied with lighting conditions. The olive appeared darker towards the rear of the auriculars. The olive crown did not quite make it to the beak, so that there was a small yellow "forehead."

The lesser wing coverts appeared gray with some dark markings. The median coverts blackish, edged in gray and white. The greater wing coverts were similarly marked, resulting in two prominent white wingbars. The folded flight feathers were not studied at all in the field. The only notable comment that I have to make is that, in my photos, the greater primary coverts appear gray in every photo, c/w adult female and not immature male (which should be olive per Garrett and Dunn).

The throat looked dull buff to me as did the flanks. There was no streaking beneath, and otherwise the underparts looked dull whitish. The undertail had extensive white
Behaviors: Feeding on ground eating seeds!!! At one point was on sunflower with siskins probing around.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Adult, fall

Similar Species Discussion

BTGreen would've shown more distinct eyeline, better defined auriculars, streaking on flanks. Townsend's x Hermit Warbler, of course, offers the most potential for confusion. A "HETO" should have some combination of yellow on the undersides, streaking on the sides, brighter green on back. Also, I think that the gray lesser coverts are a Hermit Warbler mark. In other words, all the markings observed were within range of Hermit Warbler, and no "Townsend's" markings were present to suggest hybridization.

Comments: The BRC will undoubtedly note that though I'd swear the throat was a dull buff, there are good quality pics that show prominent dusky gray there. Other photos don't. I am certain only one bird was involved, and that the gray/almost blackish could only be seen in throat-on views, in certain light, during which blackish feather edgings stood out more prominently. Both first-fall males and adult females can show this.
