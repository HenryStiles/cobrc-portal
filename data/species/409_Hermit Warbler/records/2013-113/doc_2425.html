Description of the Bird

I was birding at the grove of trees at Upper Queeens Reservoir on Sunday afternoon, and while looking through Yellow-rumped Warblers, and other migrants, I found a male Hermit Warbler. The warbler had a all yellow face and head, black chin and throat, gray upper parts (including the back), two white wing bars on both wings, the wings were grayish. Under the black throat, the breast, belly and undertail coverts were all white, no streaking on the sides. The bill was dark, small and thin, like other warblers. The eyes were dark and the legs were dark. The warbler was a little smaller than the nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers.
Behaviors: Staying low with other warblers.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Black-throated Green Warbler has a green back, not gray like this bird, along lacks an entire yellow face like this bird.

Townsend's Warbler has dark cheek patch, lacking in this bird.

hybrids would show streaking below the throat into the breast area, and more streaking on the sides, and greenish colored back, all lacking in this bird.
