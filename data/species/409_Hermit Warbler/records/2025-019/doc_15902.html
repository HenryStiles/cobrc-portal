Description of the Bird

We were birding the area hoping to get lucky and find a wayward warbler and immediately upon entering the side-draw, an interesting song was heard repeatedly.  I knew this had to be one of 'black-throated' warbler species but initially did not know which.  I got Merlin out and it did not pick up or identify anything but Lindsay spotted the bird high in a conifer quickly thereafter and on first view I saw what I knew was a male Hermit Warbler foraging.  It was constantly moving between trees and stayed quite high in them making good photos difficult but over the course of an hour of following its frequent song, we managed 4 or 5 sightings (2 of which were prolonged enough to obtain decent photos).      

Description:  New World warbler species with yellow head with darker nape, black throat, white belly and outer tail feathers and gray back.  Wings had two white wingbars.  Bird was singing a, sometimes variable, up and down song "wheeta wheeta wheet SEET SEET" intermittently (every 15-30 seconds) while we were there.  It was actively forageing high in the conifers and moving around the hillside and eventually returning to where we had first seen it.  It was independent and did not seem to be interacting closely with any other birds.

Similar Species Discussion

Townsend's Warbler:  no black face patch or dark streaking on flanks

Townsend's x Hermit Warbler hybrid:  clean white underparts (lacking streaks) and pure yellow head/face did not give impression of mixed genes. 

Black-throated Green Warbler:  gray rather than greenish back and lack of black flank streaking 

Previous Experience

I first saw this species at Jim Hamm Nature area in Longmont in 1999 (vagrant on RBA) and have had a dozen more encounters since, from breeding birds in Northern California and Oregon, many in migriation in mountains of Southeast AZ and wintering birds in Chiapas and Guatemala.
