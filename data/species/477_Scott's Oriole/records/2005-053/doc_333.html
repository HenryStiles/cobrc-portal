Description of the Bird

The bird was black and bright yellow. It had a black head, throat and upper breast, a black back, and black wings and tail. The lower breast, belly, and rump of the bird were bright yellow. It had a black eye and a sharp, long, straight bill. It was slender and typical in shape for an oriole.
Behaviors: The bird was ranging over a wide (200 m square) section of PJ hillside. During the hour plus that we observed it (on and off), it would go silent for periods of twenty minutes or more, then sing for ten to twenty minutes while foraging in the upper parts of junipers. We were able to approach it, on a couple of occasions, within about 20 feet.
Call: Male sang frequently during the hour or so that we observed it. Two song types were heard over the course of the hour; the bird would repeat one song type over and over again many times before switching. Song type 1 was ca. 1.5 sec long and consisted of a quick, somewhat syncopated series of clear whistles, ending in a higher, upslurred note. Tone quality was clear, rich and musical, typical of orioles, without any burry, harsh or noisy components.
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

No other yellow orioles are expected in Colorado, but for the sake of argument, Audubon's does not have a black back. Western Tanager is eliminated by vocalizations, black head and sharp black bill. Other black-and-yellow birds in range (goldfinches) are less than half the size of this bird and totally different in pattern, shape, vocalizations and habits.
