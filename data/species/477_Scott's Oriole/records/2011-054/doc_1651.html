Habitat

Decorative shrub, leafed out green, white blossoms now spent, fruits to 1/4-3/8 in. berries which go to purple in fall and winter.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Bushnell Legend, 8X26
LUMIX FZ35, 18X
Distance:  est. 3 feet through a picture window at closest.
Light:  Sunny bright day, high sun. Oriole was well lit most of the time.

Description of the Bird

Approximately 7 in. long, having a black beak and eyes, black breast and back, bright yellow underside,
white streaks on the black wings, and gray legs and feet.
Behaviors: This fellow was feeding on the spent blossoms of a
small shrub located just outside a window. At times he would be nearly upside down in his effort. The oriole was not as skittish as the tanagers, which were also feeding on the same bush. The tanagers moved away if they caught sight of me, but the oriole stayed to feed. Assuming it was the same bird, the oriole returned two more times within 4 hours.
Call: Unable to hear as I was inside the house.
Plumage: black breast, black back, bright yellow
