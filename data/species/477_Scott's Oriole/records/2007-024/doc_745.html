Description of the Bird

Black head back and wings and bill. The breast from the upperbreast down to the belly and undertail was bright yellow. Basal half of tail was mostly yellow with dark central line down the tail. Wings had yellow leeser coverts and 2 white wing bars in the median and greater covert positions. See photos
Behaviors: flying around and staying high in pines and locusts of the Lamar cemetery
Call: call was a nasal chenk, song was a clear whistled warble similar to western meadowlark
Plumage: adult definitive

Similar Species Discussion

no other oriole has a black head and back. Audubon's oriole has no wing bars but is superficially similar.
