Description of the Bird

the bird was a large oriole, with a medium length thin and pointed bill that had a slight decurve. The bill was a dark grayish blue, as were the legs.

The head, back, and chest were all black, forming a black "cowl". The remainder of the underparts were yellow, as were the uppertail coverts and rump. The bird had a yellow shoulder patch, and two bold white wingbars on otherwise black wings. The tail was black, with the upper part of the outer tail feathers being yellow.
Behaviors: The bird was coming to a hummingbird feeder in the ponderosa forest.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All warblers were ruled out by much larger size. Tanagers were ruled out by larger size and shape. Most other oriole species were ruled out by the lack of any orange on the bird, as well as the black cowl and yellow underparts.

Audubon's Oriole was ruled out by the black back, giving it a cowled rather than hooded look, and the yellow outer tail feathers.

Female Orchard Oriole was ruled out by larger size, black cowl, brighter yellow coloration, black wings with yellow shoulder patch, etc. A very yellow Hooded Oriole was ruled out by the black cowl, brighter yellow coloration for even the yellowest of Hooded Orioles, etc.
