Description of the Bird

The bird was the size and shape of a Bullock's oriole, with an entirely black head and back and a black bib coming halfway down the front of the bird, to a rough rounded border with the bright yellow of the rest of the underparts. The wings were mostly black, with a conspicuous yellow patch at the shoulder (lesser coverts) and a single thin white wingbar below that. I did not see any other white on the flight feathers at all. The tail was mostly black, with yellow patches at the base on each side. When the bird flew away from me, I could see a yellow rump.
Behaviors: The bird was quite active, moving from treetop to treetop in the neighborhood, coming down to perch on low junipers at least two different times, and once visiting a hanging seed feeder.
Call: the bird was silent
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other yellow oriole is even remotely likely here. More likely would be yellow variant of Baltimore or Bullock's Oriole. The apparent lack of white edges to the flight feathers, the extensive black bib with a rough border, and the tail pattern eliminate BAOR. Bullock's is way wrong in wing and head pattern. I do not think any conceivable combination of BAORxBUOR, even assuming yellow pigmentation, could match this bird.

Resources Used

I am looking at Sibley as I write this. He reminded me of the yellow rump of the bird, and taught me that SCOR actually does have white edges to the primaries in fresh plumage (I've never seen them in the field). I also closely examined the BAOR and BUOR illustrations to write the Similar Species account.

Previous Experience

I have seen SCOR four times now in Colorado as well as a few times in Arizona, once in Texas, and most recently in Sinaloa, Mexico, seven weeks ago, when I studied the bird carefully (in hopes it would NOT be a Scott's Oriole).
