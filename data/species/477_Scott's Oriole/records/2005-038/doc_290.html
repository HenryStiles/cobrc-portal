Description of the Bird

A beautiful, bright adult male oriole, the head, throat, chest, and back were glossy black. The rump, uppertail coverts, and belly, and vent were bright yellow. The wings were black or blackish, except for two white wingbars and yellow lesser coverts. The lower wingbar was thin, but more obvious even than the upper wingbar, which was only just discernable as a different color relative to the yellow lesser coverts. The tail was mostly black, but with a yellow base, at least on the outer rectrices. The bill was typical for an oriole, being dark except for a bluish base to the mandible.
Behaviors: Responding to playback of Scott's Oriole song and calls, it perched for view in a bare or dead deciduous tree.
Call: A low-pitched, somewhat blackbird-like, chuk
Plumage: Definitive Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Audubon's Oriole has a green back and Black-vented Oriole lacks wingbars and has an entirely-black tail. No other "yellow" orioles are at all similar.
