Description of the Bird

A striking black and yellow bird. The bird shows a long, pointed gray bill. The entire head, nape, back, and upper breast is black. The body feathers below the black upper breast bib is yellow, including the flanks, belly, and tail coverts. The Wings are primarily black, with white wing bars and pale fringing on the leading edge of the flight feathers. The tail feathers have yellow bases and black ends, each about half the length of the tail. The tail is fairly long. The feet and legs appear gray. The bird was fairly large (larger than any warbler, for example) but not especially heavily built.
Behaviors: The bird was seen moving around the neighborhood, presumably foraging for food.
Call: Bird was heard calling, but quality of call not noted
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The size, bill shape, and pattern of yellow and black rule out birds besides orioles. Male Baltimore Orioles have a completely black head but have orange bodies. Male Audubons Orioles have greenish yellow backs and all black tails. Other orioles lack all black heads, etc.
