Description of the Bird

It was seen in a tree along with a m/f pair of Bullock's Orioles.  Similar in size and shape to the Bullock's pair.  Under parts conspicuously yellow as compared to the orange tinted Bullocks'.  Head, back, wings and breast were black.  Belly, undertail coverts and underside of tail were lemon yellow.  The underside of the tail had a broad, black terminal band.  I heard an oriole-like song that I did not recognize but I'm not sure it came from the Scott's Oriole.

The orioles appeared to be feeding through the top of a medium sized cottonwood with leaves just starting to open.


See Photos:  The underparts of the oriole shown in the three photos have an orange cast.  This is a photographic artifact, cause unknown.  The actual color was lemon yellow.

Similar Species Discussion

The yellow colored underparts, the black head, throat and breast, and the black terminal band on the tail destinguish it from all other likely adult male orioles.
This includes Baltimore, Bullock's, Hooded and Orchard.
