Description of the Bird

I was assisting Rachel Hopper leading a Fort Collins Audubon field trip to Fossil Creek Reservoir. While scoping waterfowl from the walkway over the water on the south side of the lake, I looked up at a flock of Redheads and quickly noticed an alternate-plumaged Long-tailed Duck with them. The flock circled over the lake for awhile, and then landed to the north of the field group. As the date is on the late side for Long-tailed Duck in Colorado, and more importantly, the observation is of a bird in alternate-plumage, details on such occurrence is important.

Over the course of the next half-hour, all pertinent field marks were observed. The bill was short and rather high at the base, being black basally and pink anterior distally with a black nail to the tip. The head and neck were extremely dark brown with a large, sharply-defined patch of gray on the sides of the head. The eyes were somewhat pinkish in color. The back was brown with distinct russet scapulars. The breast was very dark brown, while the remainder of the underparts was white. The uppertail coverts were also dark brown and lead to a very long, extended narrow, pointed blackish tail.
Behaviors: Observed flying and swimming on the water.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Alternate

Previous Experience

I have observed thousands of other Long-tailed Ducks across the Great Lakes, East Coast, and Alaska, as well as a few others in Colorado. The only other time I have seen males in alternate plumage was in Alaska, where I have seen roughly 50.
