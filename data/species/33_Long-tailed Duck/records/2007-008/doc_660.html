Habitat

Coryell Ranch Ponds were formerly used as trout rearing ponds. The ponds are deep and have food to support diving ducks. Goldeneyes, Scaups, Ring-necked Ducks, and Ruddy Ducks are frequent visitors there. The ponds are surrounded by cattails and flanked by the Roaring Fork River and associated riparian habitat. Nearby habitats are Ag land, Urban, and P/J.

Description of the Bird

This diving duck was slighted smaller than the Barrow's Goldeneyes that accompanied it. References show BAGO at 17 - 21" and LTDU at 14 - 18". The bird had a short dark bill and a striking white face that contrasted with a dark patch extending from the lower cheek onto the neck. The crown and nape were also dark. Breast, back, and wings were all dark, with white or light flanks and rump. The bird had a short but pointed tail. In flight the bird stood out among the goldeneyes with it's white face and and dark wings, while the goldeneyes showed extensive white patches in the wings.
Behaviors: On 12/10/06 Laurel Williams found a female Long-tailed Duck on Coryell Ranch ponds near Carbondale in a flock of Barrow's Goldeneyes. Several other birders tried but could not relocate the bird. On 12/16/06 on the way to the Roaring Fork CBC Al Levantin stopped at Blue Lake (Eagle) at 715AM and refound the LTDU among 30 plus Barrow's. He and Dick Filby returned fifteen minutes later and the Long-tailed Duck and most of the Barrow's had departed. At 930AM Linda Vidal and her count team found the Long-tailed Duck with 46 Barrow's Goldeneyes at Ranch at Roaring Fork ponds east of Carbondale. At 1030AM I stopped with my count group and we found the Long-tailed gone and all but 10 of the Barrow's Goldeneyes were gone also. At 1215PM Dick and Al found the LTDU again at Coryell Ranch ponds northwest of Carbondale with about twenty Barrow's. Coryell Ranch ponds are about 10 miles from Blue Lake. At 130PM we were all back at Coryell Ranch ponds where all we found were four coots. After a few minutes of discussing how these birds were moving around we prepared to leave and a flock of 20 Barrow's came in with the female Long-tailed Duck among them. The bird dived occasionally while on the ponds.
Call: Did not vocalize

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was shaped like a duck, it was not a loon, grebe, cormorant, or alcid. White-faced ducks and geese; "blue" Ross's and Snow Goose, Fm Wood Duck, Spectacled Eider, Steeler's Eider, Harlequin Duck, Surf Scoter, Black Scoter, White-winged Scoter, Bufflehead, Hooded Merganser, and Ruddy Duck. The bird was a diving duck eliminating both geese and the Wood Duck. The bird was slightly smaller than the Barrow's Goldeneyes it associated with. Size eliminates the larger Spectacled Eider and White-winged Scoter, and the smaller Bufflehead. The bird showed extensive white on it's flanks. Steller's Eider, Harlequin Duck, Surf Scoter, Black Scoter, White-winged Scoter, Hooded Merganser, and Ruddy Duck all have dark flanks, leaving Long-tailed Duck as the only possibility among ducks.
