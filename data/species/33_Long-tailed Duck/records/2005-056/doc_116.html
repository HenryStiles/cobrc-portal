Documentation of Long-tailed Duck
2005-056

Observer Information

Reporter:  Doug Faulkner  6035 Parfet St.,   ARVADA, CO  80004-4648
E-mail:  zebrilus@gmail.com
Other Observers:  None.; John Tumasonis and several others on 20 May.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Long-tailed Duck
First Date/Time:  5/21/2005
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  about 5 minutes
County:  Weld
Specific Location:  Crom Lake. About a quarter mile south of Road 90 on Road 31.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Fairly small farmer's pond with no emergent vegetation; appeared to be quite shallow.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica scope with 20-60x lens
Distance:  about 300 feet
Light:  Direct and harsh, with the sun behind the bird.

Description of the Bird

Smallish brown and white duck, smaller than nearby Gadwall; head was mostly a rich brown except for a large white eye patch that extended from near the bill widely around the eye then tapering to a point; the short, flat bill had a slight pinkish wash to the posterior half; the upperparts were mostly the same brown as the head except for some pale edging to the feathers; the underparts I could see were purely white; there was a slight brownish wash to the lower neck region.
Behaviors: Swimming with Gadwall and Mallards.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: transitional into adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

None.

Resources Used

None.

Previous Experience

I have seen several in Colorado over the past six years.

Memory
