Description of the Bird

Initially I saw three loons together and when I got the scope on them I noticed one was much larger than the adjacent Common Loons.  Since I didn't have a long telephoto lens with my camera, I used my phone to digiscope the loons.  The Yellow-billed Loon was much larger and thicker necked than the Common Loons. It was also lighter colored overall, particularly on the neck and face. Its light colored bill looked massive compared to the darker bill of the Common Loons and it seemed to hold it at a more upward angle. Much of the color on the head and neck appeared brownish gray rather than the cool, darker grays on the Common Loons.  Looking at my field guides and comparing them with my photos and videos, this bird appears to be a juvenile (as expected to to its vagrancy).  The bill, while large, at times doesn't seem as large those of an adult. 

Similar Species Discussion

Only logical comparsion would be a Common Loon and this loon was much larger and lighter colored. Look at my initial eBird checklist with a video showing both species (eBird Checklist - 2 Jan 2026 - Ridgway SP--Bay View Day Use Area - 6 species) for my best comparison between the two species.

Previous Experience

I have very limited experience with this loon species, but lots of experience with Common Loons.
