Description of the Bird

the bird was a large loon, the same size or larger than a Common Loon. The overall shape was quite bulky (for a loon), with a lumpy head with a very large lump above the eye, and a flat crown behind the eye contributing to this look. The neck was quite thick. The bill was very small for a YBLO, with the smallest gonydeal angle I've ever seen on this species. It was completely a straw yellow color, incluing the culmen. The eye was reddish, and looked quite small on the large head. The bird held it's bill above the horizontal for most of the observation.

The crown, nape, and sides of the neck were a straw-brownish color. The face, chin, and throat were white. Becuase of the white face and pale brown surrounding the face, the bird had a fairly "blank" look when compared to the darker head of COLO. The back was a medium to dark brown, with bold white feather edges giving the appearance of bold white bars down the length of the back.
Behaviors: The bird was seen loafing around on the reservoir, and only actively diving towards the end of the observation.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

All species of Loon other than Common were ruled out by the large size, lumpy head, heavy shape and thick neck, white bars on the back, etc.

Common Loon was ruled out by the pale head, blank look, white bars on the back, small-eyed look, completely yellow bill, heavier shape, habit of holding the bill above the horiztal, and overall paler look.

Additional Information

Comments: Report made from photos taken of the bird.

I am writing this report to show that this bird and the bird that showed up at Chatfield a week or two later are different birds. Both records are currently accepted as the same bird, and I believe this to be in error. To demonstrate this, I put an insert on photo number one of the Chatfield bird, taken by Glenn Walbek and used with his permission.

To sum up why I believe them to be seperate birds, the bill shape is very different, with the Chatfield bird having a heavier bill with more of a gonydeal anlge, more typical of the species than the Boyd Lake bird, the pattern of white on the face is different, with the Chatfield bird having more extensive white behind the eye, and the head shape appears different (though this could be affected by the bird's behavior when the pic was taken), with a less lumpy look on the Chatfield bird.
