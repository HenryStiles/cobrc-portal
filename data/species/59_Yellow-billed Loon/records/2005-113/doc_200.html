Description of the Bird

Very large, pale loon in direct comparison with basic-plumaged Pacific Loon (Gavia pacifica). The overall coloration of the loon was pale brown, lightest on the head and neck. The large bill was mostly pale yellow, with a straight culmen and lower mandible that sharply angled up 2/3 distally, accentuating the head tilted up look. The bird's head had a pronounced forehead bulge, as well as a darker brown patch on the auriculars.
Behaviors: On both days I observed it swimming, and on the first day in flight for about 5 minutes.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon (G. immer) comes closest in size, but that species is darker overall, lacks a pronounced forehead bulge, and has a gray bill that is "dagger" shaped (culmen and lower mandible curvatures are similar).
