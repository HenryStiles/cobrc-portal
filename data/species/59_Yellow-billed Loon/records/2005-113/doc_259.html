Description of the Bird

the bird was a large loon, about 10 percent large than the Common Loon with which it associated. The bird appeared bulkier and "taller" than the Common Loon, in part due to the fact that the Yellow-billed would hold it's neck straight instead of bent, and the fact the neck appeared thicker. The bird's head was more blocky, with a larger lump over the eye. The bill was pale yellow, and had a strong gonydeal angle about halfway along the lower mandible. The culmen was straight. The bird would hold it's bill at a slight upward angle, while the Common Loon held it straight.

The overall head color was much paler than the nearby Common Loon, a pale straw color with almost no darker color in it, the exception being a somewhat faint but noticeably darker spot behind the eye. The bird's throat was white, and there was a white crescent above the eye, connecting to the white throat, creating a "blank" look to the bird's face. There were two small white indentations into the straw colored head, one just below the eye, and one about halfway down the neck.

The bird's back was a light brown, darker than the head, but paler than the back on the nearby Common Loon. There were bold white horizontal stripes along the length of the back, created by pale edging to the back feathers. The bird's sides were the same color as the back. The chest was white.
Behaviors: the bird would spend most of its time out on the reservoir diving and preening, and spent a good deal of its time in the company of a basic plumaged Common Loon.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Red-throated Loon was eliminated by large size and bill, pale head, bulky biuld, and brown back with barring.

Pacific and Arctic Loons were eliminated by large size and bill, pale bill, pale and blocky head with white indentations, and brown back with barring.

Common Loon was ruled out by larger size, pale bill with straight culmen, paler back with barring, much paler head with contrasting spot behind the eye, by the posture at which it held it's neck and bill, and by bulkier head shape.
