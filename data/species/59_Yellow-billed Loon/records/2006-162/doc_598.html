Description of the Bird

Large loon with pale straw-yellow bill. Bill appears upswept due to fairly straight culmen. Culmen a shade darker on basal third, otherwise same straw yellow as the rest of the bill. Face and neck diffuse yellowish-brown. Paler buffy highlights around the eye, below the gape, and on the throat. Breast and belly white, with brown vent strap. Toes on feet dark gray, webbing between them pink. Back dark brown with well-defined rows of pale feather edging- almost a laddering effect. Lower right side of neck has a wound that disturbs the feathering- appears to possibly be a curved gash, perhaps caused by eagle talon or fish hook. Bird habitually keeps right eye closed, too, but can open it.
Behaviors: Bird seen diving & feeding on large prey item that bulged its neck briefly, but spends most of its time sleeping, preening, or just slowly cruising the lake.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Arctic, Pacific, and Red-throated Loons smaller, with smaller bills and more gray coloration, etc. Common loon more gray, lacks barring this strong on the back, has grayer bill with all dark culmen to the tip and different shape, etc.
