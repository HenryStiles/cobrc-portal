Description of the Bird

The bird was a large Loon, with a large, pale bill which it held at a slightly upturned angle. The top of the head and neck were brown, but it had a distinct, lighter auricular patch behind the eye. The front of the neck was light. The rest of the bird was also brown, with lighter brown stripes over the back.
Behaviors: Sitting in a very small patch of open water on a quite small reservoir. The bird was mostly resting, but preened a little.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Juvenile Common Loon is much darker, does not have a light auricular patch, nor does it have a pale bill. It also generally keeps its bill horizontal, rather than upturned. The bird was larger than Pacific or Arctic Loons, which have a much darker head, neck and back than this bird. It was much larger than a Red-throated Loon, and its juvenile has a gray neck and head without a distinct, pale auricular patch.
