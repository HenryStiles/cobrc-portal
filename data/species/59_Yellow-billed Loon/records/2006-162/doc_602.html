Description of the Bird

Large diving bird with a pale bill and pale culmen. Overall light face and neck color compared to a basic plumage Common Loon. Neatly patterned whitish markings were present on the birds back. The bird had dark brownish, triangular shaped auricular patches and reddish-pink webbing on its feet with darker toes. The bird had a small blemish (smaller than a penny) on the right side of the base of its neck that could have been due to preening or could have been a wound. The bird's right eye appeared normal during my first observation, however, when seen four days later, its right eye had swollen shut.
Behaviors: During my first observation of the bird, it was continually preening with a special focus on the base of the right side of its neck. The bird blinked often, but otherwise floated around by itself. The bird did not dive either time I observed it.
Call: None
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon. The identification of the bird as a Common Loon was eliminated due to the presence of the brownish triangular auricular patch and the pale culmen.
