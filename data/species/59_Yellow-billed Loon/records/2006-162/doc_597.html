Description of the Bird

This is a big ass loon. The bill by itself identifies this as a YBLO. The distal half of a YBLO has to be pale, both, mandible and maxillae for it to be a YBLO. In this bird the very tip of the bill is yellow in good light. The remainder of the bill is a very pale light gray, slightly paler from that seen in COLO, but see below. The face is very pale also, especially the very light tan cheeks and the area surrounding the eye. The nape and crown are the darkest parts of the head, and are medium brown but very much lighter than COLOs nape and crown. The auriculars are pale brown. The chin, throat and neck are the palest part of the bird, and are a dirty white. During the hour that I watched the YBLO, it held its bill up at a ~30 degree angle to the surface of the water except once when it looked into the water. The angle on the lower edge of the bill is strongly angled up along the distal one-third of its "lower madible" which was quite noticable. The width of the bill is noticeably thick and much thicker than that found in all of the other four loon species.
The back is barred with medium brown and pale checkers.
Behaviors: While I watched this bird, it drifted slowly along the eastern and eastern middle of Erie Reservoir. It kept its right eye mostly closed (90% closed) the entire time I was there. It did not dive or flap its wings, did not preen, but seemed to keep track of the birders viewing this loon. It eventually moved close to the east end of Erie Reservoir where I digiscoped the bird, taking about 100 exposures. The bill was constantly held at an upward angle to the water.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

All loons have the same basic shape. Only Red-throated Loon holds its bill at the same approximate angle as YBLO, but the bill of RTLO is extremely thin in comparison to this bird.

Common Loon (COLO) can look similar at a distance but at this close range, the bill of COLO is light gray with a distinct dark culmen, which the YBLO did not and does not have. Also the COLO has a fairly dark nape and crown, much darker than that of YBLO, and the area around the eye of COLO except for the white arc and white upsidown crescent below the eye is in a dark part of the COLO head unlike the light brown on the same area of YBLO. The double bump on the COLO and YBLO on their heads is simlar in both species, but the bill on COLO is noticeably shorter and the bulkiness of the neck and size of the body of the YBLO at this close range is noticeable and not the same as the smaller billed, smaller headed COLO.
