Description of the Bird

the bird was a large loon, slightly larger than Common Loon, though it was never seen in direct comparison with that species. The bird had a blocky head with a large lump above the eye. The bird would hold it's neck stretched, and thus looked "taller" than I would expect from a Common Loon. The bill was yellow, and the culmen was straight. There was a strong gonydeal angle about halfway along the lower mandible. The bird for the most part held its bill at a slight upturn.

The bird's head was a pale straw color, with a darker cap and back of the neck, and a fairly bold darker spot behind the eye. The throat and chest were white, and there was a white crescent above the eye, which when combined with the white throat created a somewhat "blank" look. There were two small white indentations into the pale straw color on the neck.

The back and sides of the bird were a medium brown, with bold pale barring along the back. Overall the bird appeared much paler than Common Loons elsewhere on the reservoir.
Behaviors: the bird would spend its time out in the middle of the southern portion of the reservoir, preening and diving.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

All Loons other than Common were ruled out by large size, blocky head, pale head and yellow bill, and barring on the back.

Common Loon was ruled out by the yellow bill with a straight culmen, pale straw colored head with darker spot behind the eye, by the wider neck, by the habit of holding its neck straight up, giving a taller look, by the blockier head shape, and by the paler back with pale barring.

Additional Information

Comments: I believe this bird to be a different one than present in early and mid November, because that bird had an entirely pale head, with no darker contrasting cap and back of neck, had a much less bold spot behind the eye, and overall appeared to be a paler bird. The bird present in November had been searched for many times in later November and could not be found, and no YBLO had been seen on the lake since then until this bird showed up.
