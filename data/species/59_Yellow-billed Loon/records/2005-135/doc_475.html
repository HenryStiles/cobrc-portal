Description of the Bird

This bird is a large loon with a massive straw-colored bill. It had a clean white face. The back was pale and checkered. The bird dove frequently. The bird, when not diving, held its bill at an upward angle to the water. Culmen angled upwards. Auricular patch visible, but not in the photo. Nape light gray. Large blocky head.
Behaviors: Frequent diving for crawfish.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon - COLO looks a lot like this bird but there are subtle difference in structure and plumage. COLO has gray bill not as light as the bill of this bird. Although COLO can hold it bill at an angel upwards to the water, it usually only does so for short periods. The YBLO held its bill upwards most of the time it was not looking into the water. Juvenille COLO has a darker nape and often a white partial collar and its face is mostly gray unlike the YBLO here. the head of a COLO is blocky but the distance between the forehead bump and the rear bump is less than in YBLO. Although the bill color of the YBLO was not bright as on breeding birds, the distal half was very light and not any shade of gray as in COLO.

Resources Used

Sibley guide to Birds of N. america. I also looked at photos of Juvenile YBLOs on the NEt and found some that perfectly matched the Big J bird

Previous Experience

I have been fortunate to see a few YBLOs in northern Alaska on their breeding territories, and I have seen about 5 juvenile YBLOs here in CO including one at Big Johnson Reservoir swimming with 3 COLOs. I have studied them at Pueblo Reservoir during the winters they have remained their including the one that swam in the protected waters of the North Marina back when there was more water in the reservoir and it was easy to study that one at close range. I have also seen and photographed one in Monterey Bay one April.

Additional Information

Comments: This may have been the same bird that was at Chatfield Reservoir. I did see that one fairly close. The Chatfield bird was last seen on the same day as this one arrived at Big Johnson and after this bird left (same day) a YBLO appeared in the afternoon at Pueblo Reservoir. I personally believe that the Chatfield bird left land for a short time at Big J and then continued to Pueblo Reservoir, all on the same day.
