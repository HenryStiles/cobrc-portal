Description of the Bird

This is a large loon with a double bump on its head, the proximal bump sticking up significantly.
The diagnostic bill was pale, dull ivory-colored without a dark culmen. It angled noticeably upwards at the midway point on the lower edge of the lower mandible. The posture was distinct, with the entire head pointed at an upward angle while it swam. When well-lit, the face was light tan with a darker roundish auricualr patch. When in the shadows the darker auricular patch was difficult to see, and the overall coloration of the head was more brown. The eye was dark, and appeared smallish on the large face. The back was checkered. It appeared to be a large loon.
Behaviors: Swam slowly near the dam, only diving when it had to pass near me by a point of land. Was not actively hunting.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The other double=bumped head on a loon species is the much more common, Common Loon. COLO has a gray bill, not a light ivory one, and it has a different posture, without the bill being pointed upwards at an angle, and COLO does not have a angled lower mandible. I observed about 10 other loons that were COLOs in the area today and they were noticeably darker on the face and nape.
