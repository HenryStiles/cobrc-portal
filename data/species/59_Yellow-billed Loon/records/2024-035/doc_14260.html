Description of the Bird

Joe Tuttle is the first eBird observer I can find, but he reports the bird as "continuing."   The bird was in breeding plumage, white and black checkered back, white and black necklace, large yellow bill.  I did not hear it vocalize.   The two loons were about 50 meters apart most of the time until the Common Loon dived and came up underneath the Yellow-billed.   The Common Loon then began the pursuit!   The two loons were seen flapping their wings and running on the water but neither took to the air at first.   About 5 minutes later the Common Loon again began chasing the Yellow-billed which caused it to take flight.

Similar Species Discussion

Plumage closely matched that of a breeding Common Loon, however the the large bill was obviously yellow and mostly upturned while drifting.

Previous Experience

First time seeing a Yellow-billed Loon in breeding plumage.   Have observed several others in Colorado.   Have observed many Common Loons over the years, including in breeding plumage.
