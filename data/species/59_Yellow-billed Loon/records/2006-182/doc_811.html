Description of the Bird

the bird was a large loon, with the long, low-riding body, thick and fairly short neck, large head, and large, straight bill. It was noticeably larger and a little bulkier, with a thicker neck, than nearby Common Loons that were on the same plane of the reservoir. The back was noticeably browner and paler than those same common loons, and the neck was significantly paler, with a straw brown color quite different from the Common Loons. The bill was very large for a loon, again quite a bit larger than the bills of the Common Loons. It was yellowish in color, and the bird held it above the horizontal for almost the entire observation.
Behaviors: the bird was swimming out on the middle of the reservoir and diving occasionally.
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than loons were ruled out by the shape of the bird; Double-crested Cormorant especially was ruled out by the bulkier shape, much larger bill, and overall shape differences.

Pacific and Red-throated Loons were ruled out by the larger size and bulkier shape than expected for those species, and the brown rather than gray color. Common Loon was the hard one to rule out, but the overall brown color, noticeably larger size, and paler overall color that was straw brown helped. The very large yellow bill held above the horizontal also pointed to the ID of Yellow-billed rather than Common Loon.
