Description of the Bird

Very large pale-appearing loon. Head bulky with bump at rear or crown and prominent bump at front of forehead. Bill whitish at base, more straw color toward tip. Basal half of culment dusky. Otherwise no dark on bill. Bill hefty, held upwards. Crown pale sandy brown with round spot, moderate size, at rear of auriculars. Rear on neck sand brown. Vague partial band at base of neck. Chin, throat, upper anterior neck white/whitish. Sides medium dark. Back (folded wings mostly) medium-dark brown with distinct pale grayish edges making the back look banded. Initially would dive, disappear for 30 seconds or more and then appear 100+ yards away from location of initial desent. 

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon with silvery bill, darker brown on head, lacks spot towards rear of auriculars, not has beefy bump at front of crown
