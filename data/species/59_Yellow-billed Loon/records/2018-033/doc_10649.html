Description of the Bird

Originally spotted from the road above where the bird was swimming, it appeared large and fairly pale-colored to the naked eye - an interesting combination.

Once seen in binoculars, it was immediately obvious what it was. 

Simply put: a large, blocky-headed, pale-bodied loon with a large straw-colored bill.

It was in the company of a far more expected COLO.  While virtually identical in structure, the YBLO was notably larger and resembled a COLO which had been dropped in bleach (with the head held in longer than the body).

A large, low-slung diver.  Thick necked.  Blocky head pale with a bit of a dusky-gray cap and a tiny gray spot behind the eye.  Cheek and nape virtually white.  Bill large and dagger-like, pale gray close to the face changing to a straw-color halfway toward the tip.  Lower mandible's bottom somewhat rounded.  Collar pale dusky gray.  Chest white.  Back appeared gray with white edging on the feathers giving the back a scalloped appearance.  Flanks similar.

eBird Checklist: https://ebird.org/view/checklist/S42725146

Similar Species Discussion

Loons were the only "similar" species considered and all were easily eliminated as this bird was larger than the COLO with whom it was associating.  The coloration eliminates COLO, the size all others.

Previous Experience

This is the fourth YBLO I've seen (Boulder, Douglas and Arapahoe birds being the prior three).  Not a lot of experience, but this is a fairly distinctive bird at close range in good light.
