Description of the Bird

Viewed for 25min from approx 75m with binoculars and spotting scope in poor light (dawn, heavy overcast, haze.) on the north shore of Standley Lake near the paved boat launch. Large loon, clearly bigger than nearby Mallards. Diving occasionally. Heavy bill with straight upper surface and curved lower surface giving an upturned appearance. Bill appeared mostly light gray except when turned to the best light, when the tip 2/3 showed a lighter yellowish cast; color most apparent in attached photos. Pronounced peak on the forehead. Cheeks light gray with darker auricular spot. Light gray throat, dark gray-brown collar, bright white upper breast visible above water. Sides and flanks fairly uniform gray-brown. Back a slightly darker gray-brown with distinctly contrasting scalloped tan stripes. Separated from Common Loon by bill shape and color, and lighter head and body plumage with tan/brown tones.
