Description of the Bird

This large loon was seen next to two smaller and smaller-billed Common Loons. The key features are the ivory-colored bill, upturned some of the time with a dark basal half to the culmen, the whitish face with a dark smudge on one of the auriculars, and whitish circles around the beady eyes. The sides of the neck were buffy, the chin, throat, and upper breast white.
Behaviors: Sent much of its time underwater. No signs of it catching any. Traveled long distances during the 40 minutes I watched it.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon is smaller with darker neck and gray, shorter bill, that doesn't angle up at the culmen. Darker face on COLO
