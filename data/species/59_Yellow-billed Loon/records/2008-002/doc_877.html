Description of the Bird

A large loon, the bird was quite pale overall. The bird's bill was large and, except for about the basal half of the culmen (which was dark), was entirely pale yellow. The mandible had a distinct gonydeal angle and turned distinctly upward there.

The forehead was very steep (almost vertical) and formed almost a right angle at its meeting with the crown. The crown and parts of the face were medium brown with that coloration extending down the face behind the eye. The malar region, chin, and throat were whitish and the auriculars were partly light brown and partly medium brown. The nape and the back of the neck were about the same color as the crown, but the color graded paler the farther down the neck and toward the front, which was whitish. About halfway down the neck, there was an intrusion of the whitish from the front into the brown of the side and back. There was no distinct line of demarcation on the side of the neck between the whitish of the front and the brown of the side and back.

The upperparts were dark brown with some paler bits, but I could not determine whether those were the distinct pale fringes of juvenal plumage, the somewhat vague paler fringes of formative, or the vaguely pale bits of definitive basic.

What I could see of the underparts was white. I could discern no tail. The wings were colored and patterned like the upperparts.
Behaviors: Swimming, with occasional head-dunkings to look underwater.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenal or basic

Similar Species Discussion

All other loons eliminated from consideration by combination of relatively pale head, very pale and upturned bill. Arctic Loon also ruled out by neck pattern. Red-throated Loon also ruled out by bill size. Common and Pacific loons also ruled out by habit of holding bill above the horizontal.
