Description of the Bird

A large, pale loon. Bill large, yellowish, upswept, habitually held above horizontal. Head blocky, with knob above eye. Overall head color pale grayish-yellow brown, with slightly darker auricular patch, crown, and forhead. Cheeks, throat, and breast paler, almost white. The back of the bird was a bit darker and very scaly looking due to prominent pale edging on the back feathers. Seen right next to a Common Loon the bird was distinctly larger.
Behaviors: Swimming, loafing, preening periods alternating with long feeding dives.
Call: Soft hooting vocalization heard when bird was swimming with Common Loon.
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Arctic, Pacific, and Red-throated Loons smaller overall and have smaller bills. A direct comparison with a Common Loon that swam with it for a while showed the bird to be larger, and have a paler, larger yellowish bill. The overall paler color with a yellowish tinge along with the blocky head also rule out Common Loon, as does the strong scaly pattern on its back (not as distinct in Common Loon.)
