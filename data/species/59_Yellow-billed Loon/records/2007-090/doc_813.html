Description of the Bird

the bird was a large loon, with the typical long body riding low on the water, fairly short but thick neck, and large, heavy bill, about twice the size of nearby Mallards and Gadwall. It appeared larger than the Common Loon that was also on the reservoir, but I never saw them in direct comparison. Compared to the Common Loon, the bird was bulkier, with a "lumpier" head and larger bill. The bill had a fairly strong (for a loon) gonydeal angle, and was straw yellow in color, with a darker culmen. The bird held the bill above the horizontal for the entire observation.

The bird's head and neck were a medium straw-brown color, paler on the sides of the neck, where it morphed into the white throat. There were two "notches" into the brown neck, one not far below the eye, and one near where the neck meets the chest. The back of the bird was medium brown, noticeably darker than the neck but noticeably paler and browner than the nearby Common Loon. There were fairly bold white horizontal striped across the back, made up of the pale edges to the back feathers.
Behaviors: The bird was swimming on the west side of the reservoir. For about half the observation it was preening and flapping; for the other half it was diving frequently. It would stay under for long periods and move long distances, so was hard to follow.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

All species of Loon other than Common were ruled out by the large size and heavy build, as well as the neck pattern. Common Loon was ruled out by the larger size, larger, heavier bill with stronger gonydeal angle that was yellow in color, and held above the horizontal, the paler brown neck, and the paler and browner back with horizontal stripes.
