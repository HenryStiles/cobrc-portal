Description of the Bird

I spotted this loon while driving by the area and the first thing that came to my mind was that it was holding its bill in an upward angle, so my first thought was this is probably either a Red-throated or Yellow-billed. While watching the bird when I stopped it would usually hold its bill upward other than times when it would put its face in the water presumably looking for food, which was quite often. The first thing I noticed was how light the face was and that the outer half of the bill appeared yellow. The yellow bill was very large and boat shaped, meaning flat on the top and very curved on the lower mandible. The cheek is very white with the white going up to or just past the eye. It has a brown back of the neck going all the way up the top of the head. It was darkest where the top of the bill meets the head and lightest on the sides of the neck. I was able to see a hint of an ear patch on the bird. The overall body coloring was fairly light brown, at least light compared to other loons. It also had white lines going across the back of the body. The overall size was a very long, large and bulky bird.
Behaviors: Swimming and diving in the reservoir
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: First Summer

Similar Species Discussion

The way it held its bill at an upward angle most of the time I watched it lead me to think it was either a Red-throated or Yellow-billed Loon, but the overall heaviness of the bill easily eliminated Red-throated Loon. The yellow bill and the boat shaped bill eliminated everything else. The white going to/above the eye eliminated everything but Red-throated, which again was easy to eliminate from this bird. With the darkest brown spot on the bird being where the top of the bill meets the head and the lightest being the sides of the neck eliminates everything else. The white lines going across the back of the bird also eliminates everything else. The hint of an ear patch also eliminates all the other birds.

Additional Information

Comments: I have two emails from two experts (Paul Lehman and Steve Mlodinow) that say why they think this bird is a Yellow-billed Loon, but they were looking at photos taken from west slope birders. When I took the photo unfortunately it had moved locations going towards the west giving for poor lighting, but if I could have gotten pics from when I first saw it they would definitely show everything.
Paul Lehman email - Looks like a Yellow-billed. Outer half (at least) of culmen looks pale. Rest of bill color fine, as is bill shape (long and uptilted shape toward tip). Double bumped head looks fine, and there is the suggestion of the dark post-ocular spot. I've seen a couple summer Yellow-billeds that at a distance were only 'maybe's' due to their looking a bit darker than they 'should,' but then when one got closer they became classic Yellow-billeds. This bird looks like that.
Paul

Steve Mlodinow email - We have the joy of a fair number of summering loons around here
Sometimes, the silvery bill can glint in sun, looking whitish... thus resembling a YBLO
I've seen the occasional COLO that looks to have a huge YBLO like bill, and others with a double bump on bill
But this bird shows all of these features at once. And though in some photos the bill looks like it might be silvery in the sun, others really seem to show a truly straw colored bill. I can see a hint of the ear patch (which I've also seen on faded summer COLO) in the first two pics of this bird.
And in winter, I've seen ad YBLO at fairly close range without any apparent auricular spot at all.
And are we sure this is a first summer bird?
Looking at the pics again, and barring the utterly strange and useless shot, I still remain convinced it is a YBLO for the reasons PEL mentioned
Steve
