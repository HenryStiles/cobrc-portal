Habitat

Deep end of Pueblo Lake State Park at the Sailboard Launch near the damn. In the NE area of the reservoir.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski 8x32 binoculars, Swarovski 65mm spotting scope
Distance:  50 - 100 yards
Light:  42-46 degrees, clear skies, 0-5 wind with calm water

Description of the Bird

Behavior -.both birds stayed on the water surface during the whole observation. They swam from NE corner past the Sailboard Launch and continuing around toward the campground area.

Calls - none heard

Finder – not sure but the first observers listed on eBird is Chris Knight and Al Garder on 1/8/22

Age/Plumage – These blocky headed loons had light creamy yellow bills, straight upper mandible, tipped up bill posture, light all the way to the culmen, and were bigger than the Common Loons in the vicinity. The neck was noticeably thicker as well. The birds were in basic/winter plumage. 

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon – small with less of a blocky head, thinner neck, and most importantly had a gray bill with black at the culmen.

Red-throated Loon – thin gray bill vs. thick yellowish bill, RTLO also is lighter on the cheek vs a dark head for the YBLO.

Pacific Loon – thin dark gray bill vs. thick yellowish bill, PALO is also lighter below the cheek vs. a dark head for the YBLO.

Resources Used

I referred to the National Geographic Guide and the Sibley Guide.

Previous Experience

I have 6 sightings on eBird. The first was in Alaska 6/2005, Boyd Lake SP Colorado 12/2014, South Platte River Colorado 1/2018, Standley Lake Park Colorado 3/2020, Pueblo Reservoir SP Colorado 1/2022 and this observation.  Five of the six observations were in basic plumage.
