Description of the Bird

I was driving kids to school and flushed a group of doves off of the road. One was very small compared to the Mourning Doves that it was foraging with on ground! My first words were "oh my gosh that has to be a Inca Dove" which I thought would be more likely. The plumage characteristics I noted as the bird flushed were black "corners" on the tail with small white marks in the very corner and it was obviously a small dove species. Markings were very different from Inca Dove which I am more familiar with because I lived in the Phoenix, AZ area for years and saw often at my folks place near Apache Junction (I did see COGD in the area also but rarely). I managed to obtain a "not so good" photo which I posted to eBird (http://ebird.org/ebird/view/checklist?subID=S23435705). I provide other plumage characteristics in my eBird report. I sent this photo to Tony Luekering to see if he would be able to identify the bird. I admit that I was testing "the master" with this terrible photo (!) and here is his response: "Looks like a COGD.  Is that right?  Killer yard bird, if so!"!!

Similar Species Discussion

Inca Dove: markings on the tail were wrong. When I saw this small dove on the ground it did not have scalloped feathers on its back. The white markings on the tail were small and isolated to the very corners also.
