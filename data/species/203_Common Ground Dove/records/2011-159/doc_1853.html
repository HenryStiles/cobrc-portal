Habitat

Sand and gravel island in the middle of the S Platte River, with many smallish trees and some bushes.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x40 binoculars
Distance:  Closest approach was about 20 meters.
Light:  The sun had gone behind a cloud bank, but there was plenty of daylight to see the bird well.

Description of the Bird

A very small dove; much smaller than Mourning Dove and more like the size of a largish sparrow. The head and hind neck were gray with some scaling. The face was slightly reddish; the eye looked dark and so did the bill. The upperparts, including the very short tail, were a uniform dark brown/gray. The wing coverts were also brownish, but with distinct black spots. The breast was also quite heavily scaled. The legs and feet were a quite bright shade of red. When the bird flew, the outer half of the wings showed much deep red with some black, and the outer tail showed black feathers.
Behaviors: Flying between the ground and small trees, trying not to be photographed by the people chasing it.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Winter

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was much smaller than a Mourning Dove, with a short, square tail. This eliminates most of the doves, including the small Inca Dove. It was scaled only over head and upper breast, not scaled all over (including back and wing coverts) like an Inca Dove. The scaling and drab plumage separate it from the similar, but rarer, Ruddy Ground-Dove.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Have seen this species many, many times in Mexico and further south; seen Inca Dove and Ruddy Ground-Dove also lots of times. Also seen all the other North American doves lots of times.
