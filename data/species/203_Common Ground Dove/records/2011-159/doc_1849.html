Description of the Bird

A small dove that walked on the ground. Head, nape, and breast scaly in appearance. Bill two-tone with dull redish base and dull blackish distal two thirds. Eye dark brown. Face tan with ruddy tones. Crown darker than hindneck and scaly. Back, sandy brown but with olive overtones and not markings on it. Tail not tapered and with small white tips (as seen in a Joe Roller photo). Darkish comma markings on wings. Wings have faint rusty wash. Primaries not visible. Belly with a sandy wash. Breast with faint ruddy hues.
Behaviors: Walked only. It was either feeding on small grass seeds or picking up grit from the road. Others saw it fly.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ruddy Ground-Dove - this species lacks scaling on its chest and has black bars on the scapulars (unlike COGD). Bill color is solid, not two-toned like COGD.

Inca Dove - another scaly small dove but this species has a long tail with white edges on the outer rects (COGD only has the white tail tips).
