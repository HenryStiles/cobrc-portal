Description of the Bird

On 12 Nov 2011, Tim Smart and I pulled into the eastern portion of Lions Wayside Park near Julesburg. As we did so, a flock of birds burst forth from the ground, almost all of which were American Goldfinches. I noted that one bird flew with the rapid wingbeats and steady flight of a ground-dove. I called out "ground-dove," but neither Tim nor I could see it at the spot we thought it had landed. I wandered one direction, and Tim headed straight for where we thought the bird had originally alighted. A few minutes later, I was somewhat surprised to see a small dove with a short tail and much rufous in the wings fly past me at about eye-level, merely 20 feet distant. It landed in a tree about 30 feet away, a bit above eye level. I was able to see that its tail was gray, black towards the outer edges, with small white corners. The back was dull brown. The crown and nape blue-gray. Some pink on the face was visible. The tail was shorter than that of an Inca Dove, and the scaling visible to me was limited to crown and face. I turned around to call to Tim, but he'd already walked up behind me. He had been watching the bird when it flew in my direction. Eventually we got it in a telescope at about 40-50 feet away. The orange-red bill with black tip was apparent. The brown wings were noted to have three even blackish "stripes" on one wing; these stripes were more broken up on the other wing. A few small dark spots were noted in the scapulars. The underparts appeared warm brown, perhaps pinkish-brown, but were not well seen.
