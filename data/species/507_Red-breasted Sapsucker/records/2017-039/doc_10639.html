Description of the Bird

Bird - a sapsucker that was hanging on to the trunk of an Australian Pine. Long bill for chisiling, stiff tail, big feet and strong thick neck. The brief but quality view of the bird yielded and bright red head, nape, chin and chest. Also visable was a white lore and bold wing stripe. The belly was light yellow moving to white at vent and feathers were grayish tipped showing a fine spotting. 


Behavior - it mostly stayed on the back left of three pines working on new wells. I could see small bits of the tree flying through the air as the bird excavated new wells. It would move to the back of the tree and out of sight for 5 minutes at a time and then move back to the same original spot. Only one time did it change from this behavoir. At that time it moved down the trunk and became unobscured at which time I took the poor back lit photos attached.


Vocalizations - none

Similar Species Discussion

Red-breasted Sapsucker - eliminated this species due to 1. solid red head vs white and black stripes, 2. yellow tinge to belly vs white and black spotting, 3. darker central back vs more white spotting, 4. lacked the thick black chest band.
