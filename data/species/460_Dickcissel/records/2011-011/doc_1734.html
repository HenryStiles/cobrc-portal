Habitat

Dense patch of snowberry approximately 1m high, 10 m radius. Mixed with tall grass and Canada thistle. 50m from irrigation ditch, 100m from tall cottonwoods.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Eagle Optics Ranger 8x42 binoculars
Distance:  ~15
Light:  Partly cloudy, sun overhead, very good light.

Description of the Bird

Sparrow shaped but a bit larger, with bright yellow breast. Male had distinct black throat patch and rufous colored shoulders, light gray belly. Female had yellow breast, light gray belly and rufous shoulders. No black throat patch.
Behaviors: Male was singing from nearby fencepost, female was bringing food back to hidden nestling in dense, short shrub. Female was very cautious, and called for ~20min in shrub where nestling was hiding. Got a good long look at her while perched approximately 15 feet away, as well as male perched approximately 40 feet away. Both were trying to distract me from area and nestling.
Call: Male song: "see,see,dick,dick,dick"
Female call: short "pwik"
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Eliminated grasshopper sparrow which nests in similar habitat and is much smaller, w/ shorter tail and different call/song.

Resources Used

Sibley Field Guide

Previous Experience

Experience doing point counts in Colorado short-grass prairie and identifying priarie species. First Dickcissel sighting.
