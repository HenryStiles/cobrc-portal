Description of the Bird

This bird was first heard, then spotted. The bird was approximately 100ft. away. Binoculars were first used, then spotting scope was utilized for a better view. The bird was on a wire singing when first viewed. The rufous shoulder patches were the first field mark noticed. When the bird turned, the yellow breast and accompaning black 'V' became the prominant field marks. The eye mask was easily identified.
Behaviors: This bird stayed on the wire singing for approx. 30 minutes.
Call: The distinct 'dick-dick-cissal' was easily heard.
Plumage: Breeding Plumage

Similar Species Discussion

the black 'V' on the breast is similar, but smaller than on a Western Meadowlark. Shape of bird was not similar to W. Meadowlark. Also, song very different.
