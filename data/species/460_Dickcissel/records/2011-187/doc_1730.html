Description of the Bird

Bird was found by observer hearing distinctive song. Visuals were obtained (see photo). Black bib and yellow breast were noted along with bold stripe over eye. Size was that of a large sparrow.
Behaviors: Bird moved from perch to perch in my yard (fenceposts, bushes, and small trees) singing from different locations. Habitat was agricultural/riparian and alfalfa fields were present nearby during all detections. Singing was infrequent but heard all days bird was present.
Call: Song was heard, but not recorded. Bird sang at least a few times all days it was present at location.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

From Western Meadowlark by smaller size and distinctive song.
