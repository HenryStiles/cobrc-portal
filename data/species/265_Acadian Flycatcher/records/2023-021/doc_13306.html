Description of the Bird

Beth Payne and I went to Bell Grove at Chico Basin Ranch around 10:55am on May 22, 2023.  I always like to go to the boxelder trees, there are two of them, and they seem to be birdy usually.  While looking for birds in the boxelder trees, I noticed an empidonax flycatcher up in the leaves.  I quickly noticed the flycatcher had a wide orange lower maniable.  That quickly made me want to continue to look at the bird.  I noticed in the was pale underneath, had a white complete eye ring, then noticed a very green back, and a bit of a crest as well.  The bird seemed to have a long tail.  After seeing all of these things, I was very confident the bird was an Acadian Flycatcher and not something else.  I was able to get other birders at Chico Basin Ranch, to come to Bell Grove, and look at the bird.  Bill Maynard who is extremely careful birder was able to see it, and agreed with the bird being an Acadian Flycatcher, so I let the word out, so other birders could try to see it.  I believe everyone who came got to see the bird.  I left at 3pm, though others were there until 7:40pm, and the bird continued to show off for all.

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-bellied Flycatcher - would have yellow throat and more yellow underneath, and more compact flycatcher, this bird was bigger.

Resources Used

Looked at the new Empidonax book, that was in my car.  Everything matched this book.

Previous Experience

I have seen Acadian Flycatchers mostly in the eastern US (including in 2022 in Louisiana).  Though, I was lucky to find the first state record on May 12, 2009 at Van's Grove, Bent County, that was seen by ten other birders.
