Description of the Bird

The bird was clearly an Empidonax flycatcher and not a Wood-Pewee because it was not all dark on the back and wings. The first marks to notice were that it had a complete whitish eyering and a whitish throat. Next I looked at the lower mandible, which was orange and quite wide when the bird tipped its head sideways. The back and the head of the bird were greenish and not grayish. Two white wingbars, as on most Empidonax flycatchers. The chest and belly were pale: not white but a tinge of yellowish color. I found the primary projection difficult to see until I looked at the photo taken by Joan Grant's husband, which showed it slightly. This feature shows much better on the photos taken by Glenn Walbek, which I've used to accompany this report. The bird was silent. 

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-bellied Flycatcher: bird did not have a yellow throat or a really yellow chest and belly.

Cordilleran Flycatcher: bird was not nearly yellow enough and eyering was not extended at the back.

All the other regular Colorado Empidonax are much grayer than this bird and do not have such a wide bill.
