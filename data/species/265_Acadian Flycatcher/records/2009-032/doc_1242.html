Description of the Bird

Large empidonax flycatcher with long and very wide based lower mandible. Lower mandible all orange-yellow. Very long primary extension, 5-6 primaries extended past the white edged tertials. Head and back olive-green. Wings dark brown or black with two very distinct white wing-bars. Rear of crown with distinct “peak” (see photograph). Narrow eye-ring, slightly wider behind the eye (see photograph). White throat offset by light grayish breast band. Pale yellowish belly; undertail coverts appeared white in the evening light. Tail slightly notched and seemed to have white webs to the outer retrice.
Behaviors: Joe Roller, Brandon Percival, Mark Peterson and I relocated this bird in the same grove late in the day (18:20). The bird was actively feeding on flying insects. Between "sallies" the bird would hold VERY still and seemed to disappear in the grove. The bird, while I observed it, did not flick the tail nor the wings (which is why it was difficult to locate until it sallied for the next insect meal). The bird fed "high" in the trees and was only seen once below my eye level. This bird stayed mostly to the "interior" of the grove and rarely was seen in an "outside" or "edge" tree.
Call: No vocalizations heard.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Other empids are smaller body-wise and have shorter and narrower bills, most with dusky tips to the lower mandibles. Primary projection on this bird was longer than other empids. No other Empid flycatcher looks this close to a Wood Pewee.
Yellow-bellied - has strong yellow belly and flanks; bill not as long and head not "peaked".
P-S and Cordilleran - has strong yellow belly, bill not as wide at the base; has strong tear-drop shaped eye-ring. Short primary projection, not long as in Acadian.
Gray - ruled out by coloration and dark bill tip
Dusky, Hammond's, Least eliminated due to very small bill with dark tip; Dusky and Least also by short primary projection.
Willow and Alder eliminated due to straight and not slightly notched tails, smaller bills that are not nearly as wide as the base; Primary projection is medium and not long as in this Acadian.
Eastern and Western Wood-Pewee - larger with indistinct eye-ring. Lack strong wingbars except in juvenile plumage. Dusky tips to Pewee bills.
