Description of the Bird

This adult male was bright blue-green on back and head with dark wings and all white under. It was smaller than a near Killdeer. It swooped and dived and flew just like a Tree Swallow. I have observed them many times in the mountains where they nest, so I know them well.
Behaviors: It swooped over a group of weeds on the frozen lake several times like it was trying to gleen insects from the weeds.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Adult Male

Similar Species Discussion

I used the Sibley field guide and compared it to the Violet-green Swallow. It was bluer and no geen on the back and the face was not white.

Additional Information

Comments: I would like to quote Bob Cohen a local expert on Tree Swallows.

"This is probably a bird that has made some poor decisions and consequently is in "the wrong place at the wrong time." One wonders what could have caused this to occur in this individual. It is most likely a bird of the year, as younger birds are more likely to make such mistakes (as we commonly see in vagrant migrants); if indeed it is a yearling, then according to its coloration it is male, as females of that age are more brownish dorsally, as you probably recall."

"As to this bird finding food there, it's quite possible that it is finding some insects (most likely midges) and/or seeds lying on the snow surface or insects (again most likely midges) clinging to the weeds. Tree Swallows will take immobile insects and seeds from snow and ice surfaces, as well as immobile insects lying on water surfaces. (Such insects and seeds may have been wind-blown considerable distances before being located there; however, as you no doubt know, Barr Lake has a tremendous production of midges during the warmer months, and most of the small insectivorous birds that are relatively early spring and fall migrants there depend on commonly finding them clinging to the smaller branches of the trees along the shoreline.)"
