Description of the Bird

I heard some Sandhill Cranes flying over at Nee Grande Reservoir, and looked at them and saw an adult Whooping Crane with them. The bird was larger than the nearby Sandhill Cranes in flight. The bird had a white neck, white wings, with a lot of black on the wing tips. The underparts were white, with darkish legs trailing behind the tail. The bird was seen for a few minutes, it the flock went out of sight.
Behaviors: Flying with a flock of Sandhill Cranes.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was big, too big for all other white wading birds. The large area of black on the wing tips, elimnate other species too. Wood Stork is smaller than Sandhill Cranes, and this bird was larger.
