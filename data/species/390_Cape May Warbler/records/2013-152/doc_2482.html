Description of the Bird

I think the photos are more than adequate for these birds' identification. The question is: How many birds. At the CBR Headquarters, a male CM Warbler was seen, maybe at 9:30 or so. About 30 or 45 minutes later, we headed towards Rose Pond and immediately encountered a Cape May Warbler, and then another, both males. It was rather unclear if we'd seen two or three of these lovely things. My photos of the bird at the Hdqts and at Rose Pond were not fabulous; however, both birds show a tiny yellow spot near the upper rear portion of the supercilium, and I can see no clear differences between the two. This one peculiarity seems unlikely to be a random thing. The other male at Rose Pond had a much larger white wing panel, establishing the presence of at least 2 Cape May Warblers at Chico Basin that day. I can not say for certain that there were only 2 birds, but it seems most likely so.
Behavior: feeding at upper levels of medium height trees such as willows
