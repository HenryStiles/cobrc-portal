Description of the Bird

Van Truan and I are were birding in Pueblo City Park, when I spotted a warbler in the pines.  It was quite yellow, with dense streaking in the front, which quickly made me think a Cape May Warbler.  I explained where the bird was, and Van was able to also see it.  We tried to photographed it, and couldn't see it after the first views.  I had to leave, and we didn't see it again that day.  Quite a few birders gathered on the morning of November 18, and we were able to see it again, and get some photos in the shade mostly.  On November 19th, I saw it again in the morning, and then went back in the afternoon, when the bird was being very cooperative, staying in a pine tree for an hour.  On November 20th, I saw the bird during the morning hours.  I hear other birders saw it during the afternoon that day as well.  No one saw it on November 21st.  My good photos are from Nov 19th, in the afternoon.  See photos.

Similar Species Discussion

Since it wasn't a dull bird, it was easily identifed as a Cape May Warbler.

Resources Used

None.  Not sure if the bird is a first fall male, or a female.

Previous Experience

I've seen a few Cape May Warblers in Colorado, since the first one in December of 1990 in Colorado City, Pueblo County.  I've seen others in the US, in Florida, one in California, and other states.
