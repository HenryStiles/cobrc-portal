Habitat

The bird was in a 1/2 acre stand of Ponderosa Pines on a ranch with mature riparian woods, water and conifers. Habitat is isolated on Eastern plains.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss Victory FL, 8 x 32
Distance:  as close as 15 ft
Light:  Sunny skies, we were looking west with excellent light when the bird apeared on the end of the branches.

Description of the Bird

A gray, plain looking warbler with a pale yellow wash to the face, breast and undertail coverts. A gray auricular patch with some yellow wash behind the auriculars. Some dark streaking below, greenish rump and sharply pointed bill. See photos!
Behaviors: Foraging deliberately in Ponderosa Pines with other warblers, primarily Audubon's Yellow-rumped Warblers.
Call: none
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Female CMWA is similar to several drab looking warblers, mainly females and youngsters. The bill shape, grayish-green rump and pale yellow curling around the auriculars helped me ID the bird as CMWA. Young, plain looking AUWAs are similar but the features described above, along with the proximity of many AUWAs allowed me to elliminate that species.

Resources Used

Previous Experience
