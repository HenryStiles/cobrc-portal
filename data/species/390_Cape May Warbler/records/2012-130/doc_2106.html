Description of the Bird

A medium-sized, short-tailed "Dendroica" type warbler seen flying into tree with flowers and small fruit. The shape was rather distinctive and caught my eye. Through my binoculars, I saw a short-tailed, very dull "Dendroica" type warbler with a short fine bill. The bird's underparts were dirty white without hint of yellow or green. The underparts had moderate intensity and moderately crisp (though still somewhat blurry and streaking about same color as upperparts) streaks across the chest and down the sides. The throat was dull colored with faint gray malar stripe and some faint streaking at the lowermost throat. Face rather plain, but with dull pale broken eyering and faint supraloral stripe -- auriculars not differing from crown nor back in color. Sides of nape a tad paler, but not as prominent as most CMWAs. It had weak whitish wingbars. Bill small for any "Dendroica" type warbler. Dull contrasting greenish rump patch seen when bird flew away. Back overall grayish. Secondary edges not visible to look for green there.
Behaviors: Feeding in flowering/fruiting tree. Did not see what it was eating, focusing on field marks
Call: Not heard
Plumage: first fall female

Similar Species Discussion

Blackpoll Warbler would show some yellow/green, have bolder supercilium and wingbars. Palm Warbler would've had bright vent, prominent supercilium. Yellow-rumped Warbler would have different distribution of streaking, brighter rump, larger bill, longer tail among other marks.
