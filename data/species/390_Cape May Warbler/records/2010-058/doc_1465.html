Description of the Bird

-blackish crown
-yellow neck (except in center back)
-rufous cheek patch and rufous color above eye (yellow immediately above and beneath eye)
-olive green back with black streaking, yellow over the rump
-olive green wings with white greater coverts
-yellow throat and breast with black streaking
-whitish under lower belly and undertail coverts
-black bill and black legs
Behaviors: -first appeared at water feature in a feeding frenzy
-flying almost straight up and down about 12" (appearance of jumping) catching flying insects mid air
-feeding in weed patches and lower branches of pinions and junipers where there were groups of small flying insects near water
-after a couple of hours not so frantically feeding but still focused on feeding enough that not much attention was paid to the observers
-fed in the open which made easy observations
Call: I did not hear it
Plumage: adult male

Similar Species Discussion

When it first landed and was moving so rapidly I could just catch a glimpse of the rufous color on the face so my choices were Blackburnian or Cape May. As soon as a good full view of the side of the face was observed, (with markings described above) the Blackburnian was eliminated.
