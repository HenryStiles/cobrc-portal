Description of the Bird

Nice bright male with yellow breast thinly streaked with black vertical lines. The yellow face had a chestnut auricular patch, a yellow supercillium, and a thin black eyeline. The bill was very thin and dark. The crown was dark, the back was dark greenish with faint dark streaks. It had yellowish uppertail and undertail coverts. I forgot to look at the leg color as I was way too excited and tried, without success to digiscope the bird.
Behaviors: Forage slowly in lower level of Siberian elm while singing. Moved about and eventualy moved higher. After it stopped singing, I couldn't refind it. It moved about 150 feet distance in about 10 minutes.
Call: yes, it sang a very high-pitched, thin, upslurred notes of short duration. The song is how we located the bird.
Plumage: breeding

Similar Species Discussion

really the only wood-warbler with the chestnut ear patch on a yellow face and yellow supercillium and yellow belly with black verticle streaks. Nothing else looks this good and is still called a bird.
