Description of the Bird

It was a medium-sized warbler with a proportionately short tail.

The belly and undertail coverts were an unstreaked white.

It had medium (not real fine or coarse) black streaking on the upper flanks, leading up to the throat, but the center of the breast was unstreaked (streaking outside an inverted V pattern). The upper breast and throat had a yellow background to the streaks.

The face was bright yellow, with a bold chestnut ear patch.

The crown was a dull green.

It had a modest white wing patch on dull, greenish wings.
Behaviors: It stayed high in deciduous trees, next to Boulder Creek. It occasionally flew from one branch to another, or from one tree to another, and in between those times it moved around in the tree's foliage.
Call: It gave a warbler-like song from near the top of a tree. I am not familiar with the songs of most warbler species, so I did not use the song in my identification.
Plumage: breeding plumage

Similar Species Discussion

Because this was a breeding plumage adult male, separation from other species was quite straightforward. Perhaps Magnolia and Blackburnian Warblers would be the closest in appearance. The yellow breast and throat, with black streaking running all the way up to the throat, rules out both species, as does the combination of yellow throat and chestnut ear patch.
