Description of the Bird

Bird was about 10' off the ground, foraging in the newly leafed out branches, moving along branch looking for bugs, moving quickly around in a contained area about 5' in diameter the whole time. No calling, focused on eating. It was a YEWA within 2' of it and a YRWA about 10' away but they didn't seem to mind each other. There was also a porcupine in the tree about 10' from where it was feeding. Light wind. Clearly a female warber by body shape and pale coloring. ID'd when I got home and could look closely at the pictures and my book.

Similar Species Discussion

I was the most experienced birder in the group. The others relied on me for ID. I looked through my warbler book focusing on bill shape, color, and length, absence of much of a supercilium, light streaking on flanks that didn't quite meet in the middle of the breast, light wing bars, and very light eye ring.

I also considered the Palm and Pine Warbler females. I eliminated the Palm because the supercilium on my bird was not prominent enough. And on the Pine Warbler the bill was much fatter.

Resources Used

- After I decided what I thought the bird was, I ran it by Aaron Shipe for his opinion. He felt confident it was a Cape May female.

- I didn't realize that a Cape May female had been seen by Brandon Percival and a couple others the day before at the Chico Basin HQ Pond. We saw the bird just south of the Chico Basin Rose Pond. Christine Alexander has a photo which would seem to indicate the bird we saw was the same bird they saw the day before.
