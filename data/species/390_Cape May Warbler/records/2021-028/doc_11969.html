Description of the Bird

The rufous cheek patch was diagnostic. Yellow eye brow, chin and lower cheek continuing around to back of neck, broken by green on back of nape that extends between crown and back. Green back. Yellow underside with dark streaking. I wondered if this may be a young male as it had limited white on greater coverts and the crown feathers appeared to not be fully darkened. Working along willows and a beaver dam at or below eye level. Likely feeding on very small midge hatch. Associating with a few yellow-rumors warblers. Gave a few, single note, very high thin “si” call notes.

Similar Species Discussion

 from any other yellow/green wood warber by facial pattern at first - chestnut cheek limits all other warblers.
