Description of the Bird

the bird was identified as a warbler by it's small size (about the size of a Yellow Warbler), longer tail, and thin, pointed bill. That bill was black, and appeared a bit longer than normal, but this was hard to tell for sure.

The face had a small patch of chestnut; otherwise it was bright yellow. The throat, chest and flanks were yellow, and there were very bold black streaks on the flanks. The few times I was able to see the cap it appeared to be solidly black. The wings were dark/black, with a very bold white wing bar. The lower belly was whitish, as were the undertail coverts. The tail was fairly long, and had large white patches that covered most of the center of the tail.
Behaviors: The bird was very high up in a cottonwood, and did not move about much. It was singing persitently, for about 10 minutes total, after which it flew off to points unknown.
Call: The bird was found by it's very high-pitched song, which was made up of piercing, short, thin notes, four in total. The song was fairly hard to hear in the high wind.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of warblers were ruled out by the distinctive face pattern with chestnut patch, boldy streaked flanks, yellow chest and flanks, bold white wingbar, and the distinctive song.
