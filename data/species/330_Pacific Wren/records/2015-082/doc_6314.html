Description of the Bird

Small, stub-tailed wren that was very brown overall.  The overall buffy color and the buffy throat led to the conclusion of Pacific Wren (shown in photos).  The bird was singing a long and complex song that lacked the trill note heard in a Winter Wren song.  I believe others recorded the song. 

Similar Species Discussion

Winter Wren was eliminated because of the more mechanical song that lacked the trill in the middle as well as the buffy color on the throat. 

Previous Experience

I do not have much experience with stub-tailed wrens, but I have seen 2 Pacific Wrens in New Mexico as well as several in Alaska.  I have only seen a handful of Winter Wrens and I have only seen 1 or two since they were split.
