Description of the Bird

I was carefully birding my way in Rocky Mountain National Park up Endovalley and the one-way road of Old Trail Ridge Road (my first of the season). It was a calm wind morning with clear crisp skies and 54 degrees at the start. 

At mile 6.2 from the start of the one-way road, I encountered a breeding pair of American Three-toed Woodpeckers. While observing this pair actively feeding young in their nest cavity, I heard a song that was distant, and my mind flashed to the Redwoods NP in California. I didn’t hear it sing again for about 10 minutes. When it sang the second time, I heard it clearly and knew it was a Pacific Wren. This is my favorite bird song in the US and know it well. I immediately ran down the road about .1 mile to the nearest location of the song. I waited about ten minutes until it sang again. I did make a crude recording with my iPhone 7 as the wind had just started blowing a little. The bird had moved down slope further from my location. I waited another 20 minutes in that general area and never heard the bird sing again. I never did see this bird.

What I heard – a series of medium spaced trills each lasting about 1 second that connected into a 7 second song. The pitch range was limited and without accent. The song did not crescendo or decrescendo and was typical for this species.

Similar Species Discussion

These are for songs only

House Wren – was eliminated by the length of song being shorter than Pacific Wren. The HOWR song is more nasal that this song was lacking. Also, House Wrens don’t usually occur at that higher elevation.

Cassin’s Finch – the CAFI song is in short pieces that doesn’t last as long as the PAWR. Also, the Cassin’s Finch sings individual notes that are clear. This song didn’t have anything like that.

Townsend’s Solitaire – this song is a series of single notes and short warbles that are relatively clear in pitch. The song I heard was longer pieces of trills, without short single notes and not singable. The Solitaire’s song also has accented portions which the song in question did not.
