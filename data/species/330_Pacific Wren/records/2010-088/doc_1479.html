Description of the Bird

A very small ruddy-brown bird, with a very short tail. The entire upperparts were cinnamon-brown, slightly darker on the crown and nearly unmarked. The contrastingly paler superciliary was buffy with that color extrending into the loral region. The ear surround was somewhat paler than the back and mostly unmarked. The underparts from chin through breast were entirely warm buff with the chin just slightly paler than the rest of the underparts. The flanks were warm brown with strong dark-brown to blackish barring and the undertail coverts were dark with scattered whitish spots.

The very short tail, less than half of which extended beyong the tips of the undertail coverts, and the wings (except as noted below) were rusty with distinct black bars. The outer primaries were dark with pale buff spots of approximately the same color as the superciliary.

The very thin and short (barely longer than the bill-eye distance) bill was mostly black with a palish base to the mandible and the legs were pink.
Behaviors: The bird perched on various bare limbs of a small, dead (?) tree, doing typical wren deep-knee-bends and calling at us a few times. It flew with buzzy wingbeats and looked somewhat akin to a winged rock in flight.
Call: Heard a few times well, a short, high-pitched doubled 'czit' with an almost hummingbird-like quality
Plumage: not juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Winter Wren was ruled out immediately by the vocalizations heard, being shorter, higher-pitched, and more reverberant than similar calls of Winter Wren; Chris, whose ears are considerably better than mine, reached that conclusion almost immediately. Additionally, though the bird was near the pale end of salebrosus Pacific Wren, it was still much ruddier with distinctly warm buffy underparts that barely contrasted from chin to breast and with little in the way of blackish markings on the upperparts and virtually none on the breast.
