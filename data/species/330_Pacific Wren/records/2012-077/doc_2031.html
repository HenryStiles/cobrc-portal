Description of the Bird

The bird was a small (4 - 41/2") stub-tailed type wren with a short tail. The overall color was medium to dark brown on the back, wings and crown. The throat was light brown transitioning to darker brown on the lower belly. The bird had a pronounced tannish eyebrow. The bill was longish and spikey like a typical wren. The tail was short and dark brown and marked with lighter brown bars. The folded primaries appeared to have tannish to light brown spotting or bars.
Behaviors: The bird would forage along the frozen Poudre River that flowed through the Environmental Learning Center (ELC) in Fort Collins at the parking lot area. While foraging the bird would sometimes give a double chip foraging note(s).
One time while the bird was upstream of the sign area in the ELC, it sang it's song for about 2-3 seconds. While foraging, the bird would disappear into the brush and thickets along the river, sometimes for numerous minutes. At other times, the bird would forage in the open along the ice edge of the river. It was during these times along the ice and edge of the brush that it would give it's foraging call note(s)
Call: I heard the bird sing it's song once and I heard the bird give it's un-agitated forage call 4 times. All vocalizations were identical to Nathan Pieplow's vocalizations for a Pacific Wren on his Earbirding site.
Plumage: Adult?

Similar Species Discussion

Winter Wren was ruled out by the vocalizations and the darker appearance of this bird.
