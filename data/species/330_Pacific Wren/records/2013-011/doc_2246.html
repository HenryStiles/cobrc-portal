Description of the Bird

This bird appeared smaller than a House Wren. It was somewhat pot-bellied with a very short thin tail upturned and almost perpendicular to its body. The overall color of the bird was a very dark rufous with little contrast of upper and underparts making ID fairly easy. Throat was a rich orange buff slightly lighter than rest of dark rufous underparts.
Undertail coverts were darkly barred. Bill was dark & slim.

I saw no white spotting on blackish rufous wings, nor barring on belly. The bird seemed darker rufous than most Pacific Wrens I've seen, especially on the underparts.
Behaviors: Darted about thick riparian habitat staying low to the ground, occasionally popping up on a twig for good views.
Call: A fairly loud & somewhat burry repeated "jimp, jimp."
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Winter Wrens are paler than Pacifics. Winter Wrens have dirty whitish & less rufous underparts, more barring on underparts, and more mottling on rufous upperparts with often visible white speckling on wing coverts.
