Description of the Bird

Chris Knight and I were birding at Valco Ponds #4 and #5, when we first heard a wren, giving a chip similar to Wilson's Warbler.  We got looks at it, and it seemed right for a Pacific Wren, rather than Winter Wren.  The throat was dark, not light, the eyeline was more buffy, not white.  The spots in the wings seemed more buffy then white.  Clearly a Winter or Pacific Wren, a short tailed wren.  Ed Landi's recording can be heard here: https://macaulaylibrary.org/asset/410200651  Since the e-bird reviewers confirmed this sighting, I decided to document this sighting for the CBRC.  I know, at least one of my previous possible Pacific Wrens wasn't accepted, since the photos weren't great.  So, I wasn't sure I should document this sighting or not.  See my photos of this bird, hopefully they show what you want to see, for a Pacific Wren, and recording is good enough as well.

Similar Species Discussion

In my opionion on this bird, Winter Wren can be elimnated from the throat color, eye line color, the spots on the wings, and the recording.  Though, since we get Pacific Wrens from Alberta, that aren't as easy to tell, unlike the ones further west, I am never sure how different looking they are from Winter Wrens.
