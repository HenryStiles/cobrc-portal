Description of the Bird

Bird was a small kinglet sized wren with a short stubby tail <1/2 body length, and held upright almost perpendicular to plump body. Dark horizontal bars crossed the tail.

Upperparts were dark rufous brown with little to no mottling nor speckles on crown and back. Bird had a buffy supercilium. Bill was thin and dark. Wings were rufous brown with dark vertical bars.

Underparts were a bright uniform rich buff including throat which did not contrast at all with buffy breast and upper belly, making distinction from similar Winter Wrens fairly easy. Barring was confined to extreme lower belly and undertail coverts. Legs were a dull flesh color.
Behaviors: Foraged among stick piles along Greenhorn Creek upstream from Rye Trout Farm. Occasionally perched on stick piles giving good looks.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Winter Wren has paler dull underparts and pale whitish throat & supercilium lacking the rich buffy underparts and throat of Pacific Wren. There is often more mottling and speckles on the back of Winter Wren, which often have white spots on the wings. Bird I saw lacked white wing spots and had little if any mottling or speckles on the back.

Previous Experience

I've seen many Winter & Pacific Wrens--former in MO, latter on west coast of USA. Also seen about a dozen of each in CO. In the Rye/Colorado City area of SW Pueblo County there have been about as many Pacific as Winter Wren records.
