Description of the Bird

A "stub-tailed" wren: small wren with short tail, held cocked. The bird was first found while climbing up a tree trunk about 2 feet above ground, hopping from spot to spot, probing, as it moved upward. It was remarkably uniform rufescent/chestnut in color. Face looked uniform (I could not distinguish any streaking in auriculars) except a short, paler, poorly defined dash of supercilium starting behind eye. Back unbarred. Tiny tail was barred black on rufescent. Spotting/barring on underparts limited to lower belly. Golden buff checkering on folded primaries (that is, along lower edge of folded wing). Bill shorter than that of HOWR. Call typical djit-djit, somewhat resembling WIWA or RCKI heard frequently. See media

Similar Species Discussion

Color was too deeply chestnut on back for WIWR, less marked below than typical of WIWR, face more uniform than typical of WIWR, no barring on back (often present on WIWR). Call was that of PAWR, not WIWR
