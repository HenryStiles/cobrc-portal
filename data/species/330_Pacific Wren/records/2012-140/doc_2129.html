Description of the Bird

The main thing about this stub-tailed wren, was the orange overall color, including the throat. See photos.
Behaviors: Staying low, other birds present were: Ruby-crowned Kinglets, Hermit Thrushes, Dark-eyed Juncos, Orange-crowned and Wilson's Warblers, also a Hammond's Flycatcher.
Call: I heard a bird chipping softly, that sounded similar to a Wilson's Warbler chip. Giving two noted calls. I saw the bird, and it was a very orangy stub tail wren.
Plumage: fall

Similar Species Discussion

"Eastern" Winter Wren: The overall orange color was wrong for a this species. Also, they have white throat, and the call is different.

Resources Used

From Steve Mlodinow:

"To me this bird's coloration is typical for PAWR, as is face pattern. I've not studied wing pattern and underpart markings as well as I should have , so asking Tony might be good idea. Of course the voice trumps all. From my perspective, the only slightly troubling feature is the relatively long bill. Nonetheless, I believe this to be a Pacific Wren."
