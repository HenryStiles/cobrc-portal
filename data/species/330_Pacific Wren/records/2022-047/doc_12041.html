Description of the Bird

Dark brown wren with light supercilium (beige/light brown) with light spots on flank and tail, which was cocked. I was first alerted to its presence when it gave a sharp, double-syllable jip-jip call, then was able to find it next to the creek, which was several feet below in a little gully. Roger Massey was able to photograph the bird and allowed me to include his photos in my checklist (my camera wasn't working at the time).

The bird had first been found and reported on the previous day by Dorian Anderson as a Winter Wren. I and several other birders also reported it as a Winter Wren, but after several audiorecordings were examined by expert birders, the bird was determined to be a Pacific Wren.

Similar Species Discussion

Initially, we thought the bird was a Winter Wren, because it's less rare than Pacific Wrens. I had only seen a Pacific Wren once in my life, and had never (and still haven't) seen a Winter Wren, so I'm not qualified to properly identify this bird.
