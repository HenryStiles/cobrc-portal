Description of the Bird

The mockingbird was a typical slender, long-tailed adult. With the white wing patches (in flight) it is rather unmistakable- the only winter resident with which it could be confused would be a Northern Shrike, which would show much more contrast between white wing patches and black wings than the mockingbird, which has gray wings and tail.

The bird had the typical narrow line through the eye, and a long slender bill (as opposed to the eye patch and hooked bill of the shrike).
Behaviors: The behavior was not particularly diagnostic- the bird was flying around in an open woodland, and gave us numerous excellent views while perched.
Call: Bird was silent
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

As described above, the only bird with which the mockingbird could be confused is a shrike. Overall contour feather contrast and bill shape and length were sufficient to differentiate.
