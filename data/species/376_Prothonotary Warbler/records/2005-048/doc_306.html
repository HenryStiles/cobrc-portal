Description of the Bird

A bright yellow, blocky, big-headed warbler free of wing bars or other subtle highlights or streaking. The head, throat, and breast were bright yellow, free of streaks. There was only a hint of greenish tinting on the nape and auriculars. The bill was dark gray, straight, and pretty heavy for a warbler. The back was yellowish green. The belly was white, beginning a bit in front of the legs and continuing back through the undertail coverts and through the underside of the tail feathers except for their dark tips. The legs and feet were gray. The wings lacked wing bars or other highlights, appearing mostly steely gray, with darker surfaces in the ends and inner parts of the flight feathers. The upper part of the tail matched the wings, with darker tips. The tail appeared to be short, giving the bird a compact overall jizz.
Behaviors: The bird was foraging along the brushy edge of an irrigation canal. It would probe among the overhanging branches and debris, and occasionally engage in flycatching behavior. At one point it caught a large, wingy insect, perhaps a moth, after a short chase with several bill-clacking misses before the capture.
Call: Mostly silent, a few squeaky call notes heard
Plumage: 1st year female

Similar Species Discussion

The combination of bright, clean yellow head and breast, white belly and vent, and steely blue-gray wings and tail without wing bars or other highlights, along with the stocky jizz, are unmistakable.
