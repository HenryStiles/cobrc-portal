Description of the Bird

Small passerine (about 5 inches) with thin, pointed bill. Short tail with white flashes when spread during takeoff for flight. Upperparts not well observed except for gray wing with faint paler gray single wingbar (tips to secondary coverts). Top of head dark yellow. Underparts mainly bright lemon-yellow with white lower belly and vent. Yellow throat and face.
Behaviors: Foraging in grasses beneath trees at edge of a small lake. Flitting among low branches.
Call: none
Plumage: basic, formative

Similar Species Discussion

The only other warblers with an entirely yellow head is Yellow Warbler, which lacks gray wings and white lower belly and vent, and Blue-winged Warbler which has obvious black eyeline and white wingbars. Other warblers with yellow breast and white vent have mostly gray heads and/or white wing-bars including Magnolia Warbler, Tropical and Northern Parulas, Blackburnian Warbler, Kirtland's Warbler, Yellow-throated Warbler, Grace's Warbler, Canada Warbler and Olive Warbler. Yellow-breasted Chat is much larger, dark gray on head. Rufous-capped Warbler has rufous and white on head.
