Description of the Bird

A striking yellow bird! Stocky build and about the size of a House Finch with a stubby tail. Dark narrow and pointed bill, brilliant yellow head and breast, the eye was a black spot in the yellow face. Nape and wing coverts olive colored, wings and tail blue-gray with no wing bars. The primaries showed some white edges and there was some white in the tail as well, white vent and untertail coverts also.
Behaviors: Came to water feature to drink and fed on bugs in Gambel Oak
Call: Did not vocalize
Plumage: Adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

PROW is unique in appearance and is unlikely to be confused with other species. The only other species remotely similar is Blue-winged Warbler. BWWA has a black eye-line and white wing-bars that separate it from Prothonotary Warbler.

Previous Experience

I have limited experience with Prothonotary Warbler but have found them in North Carolina, Florida, Texas, a couple of locations in the Arkansas Valley, and one at Prewitt Reservoir (all spring birds). I have see fewer Blue-winged Warblers, Belize, High Island, TX, and Chico Basin.
