Description of the Bird

Very large, long-necked, white waterfowl swimming on Standley Lake. The lone bird swam mostly by itself so direct size comparison was not possible, but based on other waterbirds (e.g., Western Grebe) at similar distance this individual dwarfed them.

Plumage was almost entirely pure white with a bit of staining on the head. Long neck held perpendicular to the water, not bent as is typical with Trumpeter Swan. The clearly defined black eye appeared as a distinct dot on the white face, with the tiniest hint of a black loral patch connecting it to the bill. The smallish black bill, with smoothly curved culmen, had a small, oblong yellow patch near the loral area. The forehead was rounded where it abutted the bill.
Behaviors: Swimming by itself.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Trumpeter Swan is most similar. This individual differed from that species in having a distinct eye separated from the bill with a small loral patch (much thicker patch in TRUS), a smallish bill similar in shape to a bell curve (L. Semo's description at time of observation), the small, oblong, yellow spot on the bill, and the forehead feathering rounded, rather than v-shaped as in TRUS.
