Description of the Bird

The three adults were entirely white, with long necks. Even at the distance, I could clearly see their black eyes distinctly, connected to their bills by a miniscule amount of black lores. Their bills were smallish with curved culmens. At least one bird had a small, yellow patch, looking like a teardrop coming from one eye. Also, the white cheek feathering against the black bill clearly showed the straight perpendicular contact below the gape, and then an abrupt curve back towards the eye. The two immatures were the same size as the adults, with mostly pinkish bills.
Behaviors: Swimming together as a group.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult and immature

Similar Species Discussion

Trumpeter Swans have larger, straighter bills - a feature I paid particular attention to on these birds -, the eyes are less pronounced with a thicker black loral patch connected them to the bill, and the cheek feathering forms a nearly straight angle from the lower mandible to the eye (on the observed adults this line was straight perpendicular at first then abruptly angled back near the gape).
