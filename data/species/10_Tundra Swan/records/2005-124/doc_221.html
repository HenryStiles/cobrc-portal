Habitat

Reservoir surrounded by reeds and occupied by about 200 other waterfowl.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica APO-Televid 77 scope with a 20-60X eyepiece.
Distance:  Varied from 50-150 yards.
Light:  Sun was out initially and the bird was viewed from the SE corner and the SW corner of the reservoir.

Description of the Bird

Large bird, larger than the nearby Canada Geese. Very white, long neck. Black bill with a small spot of yellow in front of the eyes. Top of the bill curved down from eye to eye, did not look like "V".
Behaviors: Bird just gently swam around the reservoir with its head up. Did not see it try to feed.
Call: Did not make any sound.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The question was whether it was a Tundra Swan or Trumpeter Swan. The id of Tundra Swan was based on the yellow spot in front of the eyes and the shape of the top of the bill. The bird was plenty close enough to see both clearly.
