Description of the Bird

The bird was an obvious swan, large in size (about 30 percent larger than nearby Canada Geese), entirely white plumage except for the head, and a small bill (well, compared to the bill of an American White Pelican!) that was black.

The bill was about the length of the head, and fairly thin, with a noticeable concavity to the culmen. There was a small yellowish spot a the base of the bill adjacent to the eye. The bill was connected to the eye by a thin strip of black skin that was narrower than the width of the eye. The contour of the bill against the cheek feathers was angled about midway along the contour, not a smooth contour like on a Trumpeter Swan.

The head of the bird was a dirty brown, as was the neck, thought the darkness of the brown decreased along the length of the neck so that the lower neck was almost entirely white. The neck was long, about 3/4 of the length of the body. The rest of the bird's plumage was clean white. The legs were black. The tail was very short, and entirely white.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of it's time swimming on the pond at the Sambrito Wetlands, which is actually in New Mexico, but at one point it was flushed by a Bald Eagle, and flew a fair distance into Colorado before returning to the pond.
Call: none heard
Plumage: first spring

Similar Species Discussion

Trumpeter Swan was ruled out by the small yellow spot on the bill, the angled cheek contour, the concavity of the culmen and daintiness of the bill, by the entirely white plumage except for the head and neck, by the relatively short length of the neck, and by the relavtively small size compared to Canada Geese
