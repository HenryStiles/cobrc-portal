Description of the Bird

the birds were obvious swans, very large with all white plumage and long thin necks. The young bird was the same shape, but with dusky brown plumage, darkest on the head, and with random white feathers mixed in, especially on the wings and chest.

The bills on the birds were small, significantly more so than the heads, with a noticeable concavity to the culmen, though on the birds in this group that I studied this was not as great as on some Tundra Swans I have seen. We I saw the birds at close distance later in the observation I was able to see yellow patches of varying size at the base of the bills on three of the birds. The bill on the young bird was pink in the center, with a black tip and a black base, and perhaps a bit blacker than usual for this time of year. The eyes on all of the birds was isolated from the bill, barely connected by a thin strip of black skin, though this mark varied among individual birds. The cheek contour had an obvious sharp angle about halfway along the length.

The necks of the birds were only about 3/4 the length of the body. On a couple of the birds I was able to see a uneven curve to the back, with a noticeable peak just rear of center, but on some of birds this was not evident.
Behaviors: I first found a single adult on the reservoir, and watched it for about an hour and a half, when four more swans flew in. They then all joined up and swam out in the middle of the lake. When I looked at them a little later with Joey Kellner they had come up onto the southwest shore of the reservoir and were preening.
Call: none heard
Plumage: four adults and one juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

All other species other than swans were ruled out by the large size, long and thin neck, and white plumage. Mute Swan was ruled out by the black bill and straighter neck for the adults, and by a pinker bill, straight neck, and unevenly dark plumage for the youngster.

Trumpeter Swan was ruled out by the small bills with noticeable angle to the cheek contour, eyes isoated from the bill, and especially the yellow patches at the base of the bill on the adults. Neck length and shape of the back were used as supporting evidence.
