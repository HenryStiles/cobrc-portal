Description of the Bird

These were very large, all white birds, with long neck. All the birds were very nearly the same size. The necks were held with a kink at the base, at least those did that I saw that did not have their heads tucked, sleeping. The bills of those that I viewed with their heads not tucked looked virtually identical, except that I only noted a yellow patch (at the base of the maxilla in front of the eye) on two individuals (though they were quite far away and I am not sure that I could have noted yellow on many of the birds, even it were there) and I here describe them as one.

The meeting of bill and feathering at the gape was vertical. About halfway up the bill from the chin to the eye, the bill-feathering interface angled back to the eye, pinching off the eyes in isolation. The bills did not seem long enough to be those of Trumpeter Swans (TRUS) and met the forehead at something of an angle.
Behaviors: Swimming and loafing

Similar Species Discussion

TRUS eliminated by neck posture and details of bill structure. Mute Swan ruled out by bill color and pattern, neck posture.
