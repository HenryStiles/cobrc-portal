Description of the Bird

The bird was an obvious swan, significantly larger than nearby Canada Geese, with an all-white plumage (except for the head). The head was light brown, darkest on the crown, becoming progressively whiter down the neck. I think this was due to staining rather than brown feathers present in young birds. The bird's neck was about 3/4 the length of the body. The bill was black, with a noticeable (and fairly large compared to most Tundra Swan's I've seen in Colorado) yellow spot at the base. The "cheek contour", where the feathers meet the bill on the cheek was noticeably angled near the top, unlike the clean sweep of Trumpeter Swan. The overall bill size was smaller compared to the head than I would expect for Trumpeter Swan, and the culmen noticeably concaved.
Behaviors: The bird was swimming and standing in shallow water, either feeding or preening for the entirety of the observation
Call: none heard
Plumage: possibly adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

all species other than swans were ruled out by the shape, size, and color. Trumpeter Swan was ruled out by bill shape, size, neck length, cheek contour, and, especially, the yellow spot at the base of the bill.

Mute Swan was ruled out by very different shape, especially bill shape, and yellow spot at base of the bill, among a host of other characters.
