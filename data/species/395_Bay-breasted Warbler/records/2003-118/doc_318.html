Description of the Bird

A bright male warbler, the bird had black forehead, lores, and auriculars contrasting with rich dark chestnut crown, chin, throat, and upper sides. The nape was a pale creamy yellow, as was the chest, which color than faded into white on the belly. The lower sides and flanks were a paler chestnut than the upper sides. The bill, eyes, and legs were dark.

The back was gray streaked with black (or black streaked with gray) without paler braces. I did not notice the rump color at the time. The tail was dark with distinct white spots on the inner webs of the 2 or 3 outer pairs of rectrices, with the spot on the outermost being largest.
Behaviors: foraging on and near the ground
Call: none heard
Plumage: Alternate
