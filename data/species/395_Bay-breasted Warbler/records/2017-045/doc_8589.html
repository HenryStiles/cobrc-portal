Description of the Bird

Continually singing, high pitched repetitive. Loud enough to be heard over waterfall below it. It moved around forest singing as it went. Song consistent with Sibley app. The bird had a dark red throat, flanks and crown with dark face and off white/cream colored breast and on sides of head. Dark gray wings with two strong white wing bars. I observed it while it was singing. Absolutely no doubt

Similar Species Discussion

The only similar species, color-wise, is the Cliff Swallow, but this was no swallow. It was in dense forest, alone at times moving along branches. Wing bars, red flanks and crown also clearly differentiates them.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I am an experienced birder and have seen literally hundreds of Cliff Swallows, but the Bay Breasted is a lifer for me.
