Description of the Bird

This bird was similar in size to nearby Yellow-rumped Warbler but with a shorter tail which was dark on top with white spots below.

Head and throat were brilliant chestnut with black auriculars and a pale buffy nape. Back was gray with dark vertical stripes with one extending into and bisecting buffy nape.

Wings were darker gray with two distinct white wing bars on each wing.

Underparts were pale buff with chestnut flanks extending and melding into chestnut throat.

Legs were dark as was short slim bill.
Behaviors: Actively foraged and sang in 20' tall oak trees with young leaves.
Call: A thin high-pitched "tzeetz-tzeetz-tzeetza-tzeetz-tzeetz" similar to A. Redstart but higher pitched and a bit softer.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Breeding Chestnut-sided Warbler is probably most similar, but it has a yellow crown with white cheeks & throat in contrast to the chestnut color in these areas of breeding male Bay-breasted Warbler.
