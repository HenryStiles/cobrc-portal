Description of the Bird

A large slow-moving Dendroica Warbler

The bird was high overhead in a cottonwood tree. so my view was mostly ventral. The lower breast and belly were creamy white. The tail was dark with some tailspots visible briefly. The throat was very dark brown and the breast and sides were rich chestnut brown. The head was dark brown-black and there was a creamy ear patch on the side of the head
Behaviors: Feeding slowly in a cottonwood
Call: none noted
Plumage: Breeding male

Similar Species Discussion

Not many other North American singbirds have a brownish breast with the cream-colored ear patch.
