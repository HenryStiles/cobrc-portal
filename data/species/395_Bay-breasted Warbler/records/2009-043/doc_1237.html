Habitat

Dense ripariian habitat of scrub oak, cottonwood, willow, & russian olive surrounding cattail marsh.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x40 Zeiss binocular
Distance:  closest observation was about 20 feet
Light:  Good light-mostly cloudy. Bird mostly NW of observer.

Description of the Bird

Crown & throat were deep chestnut-rufous with rufous becoming lighter as it extended down the flanks. The rest of underparts were creamy beige.
Black mask extended across face well past both eyes to nape.
Nape was creamy beige bisected by thin dark line.
Eye & bill were dark; bill was thinner than Vireo but fairly thick for a warbler.
Back was gray with dark vertical streaks.
Wings were dark with two prominent white wing bars on each wing.
Tail was dark & fairly short(about 1/3 body length).
Behaviors: Bird sang during most of observation's duration, while slowly foraging mostly in scrub oak.
Call: "See-see-see-see-seesa-see." Similar to A. Redstart but softer, clearer, and less burry. Song also similar to Blackpoll Warbler but a bit louder.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Male Chestnut-sided Warbler has yellow crown & white throat.
Male Blackburnian warbler has blackish crown and dark streaks on flanks. Like Bay-breasted, it can have a rufous wash on flanks, but Bay-breasted lacks streaks on flanks in all plumages.

Previous Experience

I've seen several Bay-breasteds in several states, many Chestnut-sideds, & several Blackburnians in several states from fall to breeding adult plumages.
