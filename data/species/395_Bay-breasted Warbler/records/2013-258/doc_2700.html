Description of the Bird

The bird was foraging almost exclusively in an Austrian Pine (Pinus nigra) infested with aphids (I believe the species is Eulachnus rileyi, The Powdery Pine Needle Aphid) AND on other insects either preying on the aphids or grazing on their sugary excrement ("honeydew").  Insects I observed it eat, or noticed it eating in the photos of other people, included a species of yellowjacket wasp (Vespula sp.) and an unidentified black wasp.  No doubt it also ate various ladybird beetle species present.

Of note, it appeared to me the bird took rest breaks from its active, almost continuous feeding on aphids to rest and digest.  These rest breaks were usually about 15 minutes in duration and usually involved the bird resting on a branch in the interior of the tree in the sun.  While resting it appear to gently sway and occasionally twitch.

Similar Species Discussion

See photos.  Key features seemed to be the bright white, wide wingbars, average-sized warbler bill, buffy undertail, very subtle "bay" coloration on the flanks (only visible in certain lighting conditions and angles).  The rump (see photo) was all gray with black streaks, which seems to indicate its gender as male.  The black streaks on the back were fairly heavy, possibly indicating the gender as male, also.  Bill too petite for Pine.  Legs were somewhat misleading in that they were not black but rather pink, appearing almost yellowish in certain photos.  This could lead to confusion with Blackpoll but the rather plain face with reasonably prominent, buffy eye arcs was good for Bay-breasted, not Blackpoll.  Buffy undertail good for Bay-breasted, not Blackpoll.
