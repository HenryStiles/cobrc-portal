Description of the Bird

This is to establish the first county record for this species.  It was regularly visiting the back yard at the home of Cynthia Madsen in the town of Centennial.

A sapsucker shaped bird that perched on the side of a Black Walnut tree trunk.  From the forehead, top of head, back down to the nape was a speckled dirty black and white.  There was a broad white crescent over the dark eye.  At the point the forehead meets the bill began a broad white stripe that extended down the malar region across the face to the base of the neck.  The throat area below that was black extending down to a slight blacking bib at the very top of the chest with a red patch with a few white flecks in the center of this area.  The bill thick, pointed and black.

As the bird was perched, the back was white with a dirty gray barring bordered by black wings on either side with a slight white patch that ran horizontal at the front of the folded wing.  In flight, the wing was black with a broad white slash over the coverts and lines of thin white speckles extending across the flight feathers.  The rump was white.  The tail was white with black barring in the center and all black outer tail feathers. The flanks were white with black/gray barring.   The chest was whitish extending down to the belly which had a yellow wash.  The under tail coverts where white.

Similar Species Discussion

The most likely candidate would be a Red-naped Sapsucker.  That species obtains adult plumage by the time of year of this observation.  Additionally, the throat area of this bird had an established black margin that would not exist on a red-naped.

Previous Experience

I have seen numerous Yellow-bellied Sapsuckers in Colorado.  I also observed them while living in the midwest years ago and more recently when visiting my son at college in Tennessee where a resident bird worked the tree directly outside of his dorm window.
