Habitat

While in the area he stays in the cottonwood from which the woodpecker bar hangs or nearby silver maples in my or my neighbor's yard.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss 10x40B binoculars and naked eye.
Distance:  As close as 15-20 feet
Light:  The bird has been seen in sun and shade.

Description of the Bird

Black head and back with white wing markings. Orange breast with black chin. Orange supercilium with black line through eye to black back; has blackish cheek patch under eye. Rump is orange, tail dark feathers.

It is the black coloration on his cheek that says he has some Baltimore Oriole in his past breeding. And the white on his wings is not as strong as on a Bullock's.

Size 9"
Behaviors: He has been coming to our Woodpecker Bar and Bell feeders for over a month. He eats from the feeder then sometimes sits in the tree in our or our neighbor's yard for up to an hour before flying off. He has been seen at any time between 7:00 AM and 1 PM. I have never seen him in the afternoon.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Male Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Color, size, conformation, bill. I determined he was a hybrid bullock's/baltimore oriole because of the dark cheek. Also, he does not have the solid white patch on his wing.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide to Birds for reference on the Oriole species and hybridization.

Previous Experience

I have observed orioles (though not this closely) for 20 years.
