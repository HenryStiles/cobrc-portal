Description of the Bird

Immature plumage: blue on crown of head, long, sinuous reddish neck, reddish color continued into the blue of the wings, white stripe down center of throat and white breast, white underparts, and white rump, yellow green legs and long, dagger-like yellow bill.  Smaller than Great Blue Heron.  Non-vocal.  Wading and hunting for prey in shallow waters of Cottonwood Marsh at Walden Ponds in Boulder County, CO.  Standing, sometimes on one leg and preening on the small islands in the pond.

Similar Species Discussion

Great Blue Heron was considered, but there were several Great Blue Herons also at the pond and they were noticeably much larger (e.g. taller and stouter) and more uniform in grey-blue color.

Green Heron was considered, but Green Heron is smaller and tends to remain more tucked making it look almost hunch-backed occasionally.  Though it has a long neck, it's not as strongly 'S'-shaped or sinuous.  Adult birds are also rusty and green, not reddish or blueish and younger birds are brown and white with a streaked breast.


Little Blue Heron was considered, but they are more uniform in color, not 3-colored, with no white breast, underparts, or neck as adults. Immatures are white not 3-colored.
