Description of the Bird

Long grayish bill, darker near tip,   Yellow lores and facial skin. Reddish hindneck with long white neck and white belly. Blue gray upperparts with purplish maroon feathers showing.  Long yellowish legs. Seen walking between small islands foraging.  Also viewed preening on island.  

Similar Species Discussion

Nearby Great Blue Heron was much larger, blue upperparts. Little Blue Heron would be all dark for adult, solid white neck for juvenile. Snowy Egret would be all white with dark bll, dark lores. Reddish Egret would have more shaggy look and solid rufous neck. 

Resources Used

Original ID was by Peter Gent and posted by Christian Nunes.  After viewing the bird myself and taking many photos, I reviewed both National Geographic Sixth Edition and Sibley Second Edition, second printing to confirm that all field marks observed and recorded equal immature Tricolored Heron. 

Previous Experience

The only experience I have with this species is literature pertaining to the similar species identification pitfalls. Zimmer and Kaufman.  I have experience with Snowy Egrets and Great Blue Herons and the smaller Black-crowned Night-Heron and Yellow-crowned Night-Heron.
