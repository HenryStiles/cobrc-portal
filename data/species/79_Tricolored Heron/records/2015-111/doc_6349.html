Description of the Bird

This was obviously a heron type bird.  My magnification was high when I first saw it, but I quickly realised this was much smaller than a Great Blue Heron and not an Egret.  The neck and rear of the face were a rich chestnut, with a white stripe down the front of the neck.  The wings and back were a dark bluish color, with a distinct chesnut patch on the wings.  The belly was white, and the legs were yellow.  The bill was very long and yellowish, but I did not note the eye color.  I was almost sure what it was, and a quick trip to the car and look in Sibley confirmed the bird as a juvenile, or immature, Tricolored Heron.  After a few minutes the bird did the rapid running and feeding typical of this species (and Reddish Egret), but it also did more stately feeding more typical of other heron species.  It did not make any sound that I could hear.

Similar Species Discussion

Great Blue Heron and Green Heron eliminated by size.  Egrets and immature Little Blue Heron eliminated because not all white.  Little Blue Heron and Reddish Egret eliminated by plumage characteristics of chestnut and white neck, chestnut patch on wing, yellow legs, and very long bill.

Resources Used

A quick look in small Sibley, and a longer look in the large Sibley and National Geographic guide when I got home.

Previous Experience

I have seen many of this species, and all the other US Herons and Egrets, especially when in Florida and the Gulf Coast.  I have seen a few in this juvenile plumage before.
