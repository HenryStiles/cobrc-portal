Description of the Bird

Dark heron with white central breast/belly. Standing on shoreline near great blue heron (which dwarfted it) and snowy egret (this bird slightly larger). As it flew it showed white underwings.
Behaviors: Standing hunched at waters edge facing me. Flew a few feet down the shoreline then again stood facing water.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Combination of size (in direct comparison with snowy egret) and general dark coloration rule out about any heron except maybe little blue. Pattern of white on belly and underwings eliminate little blue.

Resources Used

Actually, I reported this to COBirds and received an email from a fellow birder asking me to submit a report. At the time of the sighting I thought "Oh, tricolored heron, that's cool. New bird for me in this patch." Didn't realize that this was a "rare bird report" species.

Previous Experience

I have seen probably thousands of tricolored herons and prior to that "Louisiana herons" in nearly 50 years of birding inc. living on the NJ coast and in coastal FL. I think this is only my second in CO.
