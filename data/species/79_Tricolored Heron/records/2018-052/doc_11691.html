Description of the Bird

Following is what I put on my eBird checklist S46407202.  "Blue heron feeding/running in shallows on far side of cattails from me. I was not able to judge size or get a long look. Blue head. Blue sides of neck with white throat and reddish streaks. Appeared to have white plume on back of head. Photo."
My initial view of the heron was "face on" from above the belly. (The heron was partially obstructed behind some cattails between us).  The view I had allowed me to directly see the white throat and I could see reddish streaking on the white throat. The heron started running/dashing after prey towards me and then quickly turned along the far edge of the line of cattails.  When it stopped moving, I was able to walk a little farther down the path (northeasterly) where I quickly took my one and only photo.  At this point I saw the blue head, blue sides of the neck and an apparent plume on the back of the head. The bird then moved to an area I could no longer see it.  I tried moving farther along the path and even stood on a slight rise, but was not able to find another vantage point for viewing. 
The only behavior I noted was the bird was actively hunting while I watched it.  It initially was stalking prey and then took off in a flash running towards me only to stop and quickly turn sideways to me while it stabbed at something in the water.  This was the point I took my photo. Shortly after this the bird ran after something again and I could no longer see it behind cat tails and brush.  

Similar Species Discussion

Similar species considered:
Great Blue Heron-  I ruled out GBHE because the side of the face and head appeared all blue and the eye appeared dark since it was surrounded by the blue. GBHE would have had rufous on the side of the neck in addition to streaking on the throat, the face and head would have had more contrast with several color tones and a light colored eye.  In addition, a GBHE should have been visible above the cat tails and brush, this heron was shorter and more easily hidden from view when standing near the vegetation.
Little Blue Heron- I ruled out LBHE because of the white throat with red streaking.  LBHE would have been a similar blue coloration but the sides of the neck and throat would generally be a rust/purple color unless it was a juvenile transitioning.  I guess if it was a juvenile transitioning it might have some white on the throat, but it would not have had red streaking on the white.
Green Heron- I ruled out a GRHE because the neck was too long for a GRHE and I don't believe I would have been able to see a GRHE in this same area unless it was not behind vegeation.
