Observer Information

Reporter:  Larry Griffin   
E-mail: 
Other Observers:  Tom and Mary France; I saw and identified the bird first. My companions, Tom and Mary France, then saw the bird and agreed with the identification. Tom was able to get a photo of it in the marsh on the N side of the road.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Tricolored Heron
First Date/Time:  5/5/2010
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  15 minutes
County:  Weld
Specific Location:  First seen in the wetland on the S side of WCR 48 between WCR 43 and 47. It then flew NE over us and landed in the marsh on the N side of WCR 48.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Scope and bins.
Distance:  Approximately 150 feet.
Light:  Good. The sun was behind us toward the south.

Description of the Bird

Heron shape, similar in size to a Little Blue Heron. White belly, blue-gray wings, back and neck.
Behaviors: Hunting in a marsh, heron style.
Call: Not calling
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Little Blue Heron is probably the most similar in appearance, but it does not have a white belly.

Resources Used

Sibley Field Guide to Birds, Western North America edition. Yes, it was consulted.

Previous Experience

Extensive.

Notes made AFTER observation

Comments: Upon returning on 06/11/2010, Tom and Mary France believe there may have been two individuals present because shortly after photographing the bird, one was seen some distance away in the opposite direction. Although they did not see two birds simultaneously, they feel strongly they would have seen the bird fly past them if it was the same bird.
