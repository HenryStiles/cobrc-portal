Description of the Bird

This was a Snowy Egret-sized heron that flew in and landed on the sand spit in front of me.  I studied it in the scope, with a view from the side.

The upperparts were dark (when it was standing), with a sharp transition to clean white underparts.

The bill was very long.

Two narrow white plumes trailed behind the head.

The back had a pale tan fluffy appearance.

Similar Species Discussion

The overall shape, appearance, and behavior meant that it was a heron or egret.  The dark upperparts ruled out Great, Snowy, and Cattle Egrets.  It was far too small to be a Great Blue Heron, and much too lanky to be a Night-Heron.  The plumage was completely wrong for a bittern.  The dark upperparts and tan back ruled out Little Blue Heron.
