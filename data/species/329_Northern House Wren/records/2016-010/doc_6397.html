Description of the Bird

Immature sparrow in the company of ~50 WCSP and SOSP. Large Zonotrichia sparrow, faint but distinct yellow patch on forehead. Otherwise fairly drab underparts and and face. White wing bars. Lack of black borders to yellow crown patch, and pale gray, slightly bicolored bill (not bright orange/pink, nor solid, cold gray like WTSP) identified it as an immature bird. Flushed with other sparrows from a field of tall weeds (5-7 feet high) and perched in a tree at the edge of the field about 25 feet away. Less flighty that WCSPs, remained perched for nearly 5 minutes while I observed and referred to Sibley Guide to Birds, mobile version, to confirm age

Similar Species Discussion

No other sparrow has overall drab coloration combined with obvious yellow crown patch. White-crowned Sparrow most similar, but lacks yellow crown patch in all ages. Also has cleaner head pattern (distinct, clean brown crown stripes), lacking on this bird, and White-throated Sparrow has clean white throat with obvious borders, and bold head pattern like WCSP. This bird had neither. Also, grayish bill, slightly bicolored, eliminates all other Zonotrichia species.

Previous Experience

I have seen GCSP in Colorado on several occasions, including a wintering bird at Tunnel Drive in Fremont County for 3 consecutive years. I've seen numerous GCSP in Oregon. I see thousands of WCSP each year, and also typically see WTSP and HASP multiple times each year in CO. I see all of Colorado's regular and uncommon sparrows annually
