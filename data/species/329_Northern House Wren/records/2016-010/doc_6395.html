Description of the Bird

Small, drab wren, brownish-gray coloration overall. Indistinct supercillium allowed elimination of of most wrens. Bill of moderate length and very obviously decurved, longer and more strongly decurved than WIWR or PAWR. Obvious barring on slightly more reddish tail seen in flight. Plain facial pattern with narrow, complete eye ring. This is a familiar species during the breeding season

Similar Species Discussion

Most other wrens eliminated based on lack of strong supercillium. WIWR and PAWR are most similar, but neither has a decurved bill of similar length

Resources Used

Sibley Guide to Birds, mobile version, used briefly

Previous Experience

This is a common breeder in Colorado that I see often, more than any other species of wren. I have observed all North American wren species on numerous occasions
