Description of the Bird

This bird looked exactly like the adult interior bird in David Sibley's guide on page 481. The white eye-ring was distinctive. It had the pale gray brown head, throat stripe, white chin, buffy along the shoulders. Photos included.
Behaviors: What first caught my eye about this bird was the way it ran like a thrasher, no hopping. It mingled with Juncos, a White-crowned Sparrow and an Am. Tree Sparrow. It would feed on the ground with them, eating millet, and frequently fly to my brush pile. It would also occasionally spend time in my aspen trees. This bird almost constantly flicked it's tail and held it high when running.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Too pale and small for a Five-striped sparrow, no black on throat which a Black-throated would have.
