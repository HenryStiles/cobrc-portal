Description of the Bird

Small duck (slightly smaller than nearby Blue-winged Teal) with striking white eyebrow, white stripes on wings that contrast with underparts, strong contrast between dark brown head and breast and light gray belly. Typical dabbling duck feeding behavior, and spent most of its time activey feeding. Occasionaly preened or rested, but mostly seen actively feeding. Observed flying twice, but only a short distance (~10-20 m). Stayed along the same <100m of shoreline all day, with a flock of Blue-winged and Green-winged Teal, Northern Shovelers, dowitchers, and a few other species of ducks, shorebirds and waders.

Similar Species Discussion

None, really, because the big white eyebrow is so iconic of this species in this plumage. Dabbling behavior rules out diving ducks, small size rules out most non-teal, and none of the three teal species here (GW, BW, Cinn) have a big white eyebrow or the stripey contrast between the resting wings and underparts. White eyebrow was also a proper eyebrow, not a white crown as in American Wigeon (this bird's crown was brown like the rest of its head, minus the eyebrow).
