Description of the Bird

Bird was spotted treetop on the Russian olive branches.  Was primarily facing the opposite direction, though would occasionally turn its head to the left or right.  When first spotted, I remember seeing the creamy yellow belly and grayish overall with some brownish in its tail feathers.  Had white throat.  What really stood out to both of us was the quite thick bill that it possessed.  Was quite puffed up also, though it was a bit chilly.  It remained in same location for the 3 or so minutes that we observed it.  Don't believe that it called out and we never saw it fly.  Being relatively new to CO and not knowing what bird I might have been seeing, I decided to take images to share and identify.

Similar Species Discussion

For me, I thought it looked like a western kingbird ... and I had no idea if that was a possiblity in Mesa County in the winter.  While common other times of year, the WEKI isn't normally seen in the winter here.  Not that a TBKI is either, but that very prominent thick bill, along with the coloration was key.
