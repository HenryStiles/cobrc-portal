Observer Information

Reporter:  Brenda Beatty   
E-mail: 
Other Observers:  husband, Gordon Beatty; at our feeders, yes.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Redpoll
First Date/Time:  12/25/2012
Last Date/Time:  12/25/2012
Duration (total time in view):  20 minutes
County:  Douglas
Specific Location:  approximately 5 miles south of Sedalia, and 5 miles west of Castle Rock, Christy Ridge subdivision, along C-105.
Number:  6
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Multiple Birds - Mixed
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

mixed scrub oak and meadows, feeder on second story deck, adjacent to aspen tree.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Eagle Optics Ranger, 8 x 42
Distance:  8 ft., through window, feeder on deck rail.
Light:  don't remember the cloudiness, but since i saw throughout the day, the light was eventually behind me.

Description of the Bird

slightly smaller than house finch, and paler
red primarily in forehead
wing bars
deeply forked tail
black chin
paler reddish breast
streaked sides
Behaviors: I noticed them (6) at my feeder Christmas day, absentmindedly thinking they were goldfinches, because of the wingbars. then decided to take a closer look, and could see the reddish "hat", smallish size, and forked tail! they were on the thistle feeder, primarily.
Call: finch-like, with trills
Plumage: winter
