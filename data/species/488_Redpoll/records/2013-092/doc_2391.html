Habitat

Backyard feeder, located near Blue Spruce, 2 deciduous trees (ornamental pears), large shrub (Apache Plume). Feeder hangs in a clear area near these features.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 8x42 Ultravid HD binoculars, Canon Eos 7D with 100-400 zoom lens.

Light:  Light overcast, but clear, without precipitation.

Description of the Bird

Finch-like, but smaller than house finches in yard at the same time. Red cap and pink breast with small yellowish bill obvious. Prominent black highlights around eye and bill. Broad flank streaks excluded Hoary Redpoll. Broad white wing bars. Photographed.
Behaviors: We had been out. Came home and bird appeared on our tube finch feeder, which had a mix of sunflower chips and niger seed. Bird was single, unusual to us as all the birds we've seen this winter have been in flocks of various sizes. Remained in yard for about 10 minutes, spent entire time on feeder, then left, not to be seen again by us.
Call: None
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Not House Finch, based on size, distribution of color, yellowish bill, and dark facial markings. Not pine siskin, lacked any yellow, also bill color and distribution of color (red cap + pink breast). Not Cassin's finch, based on size, color distribution, lack of eye ring. Not purple finch, this bird is too refined, lacks raspberry hue, and has much more delicate bill. As noted, prominent flank streaking excluded H. Redpoll.

Resources Used

The Sibley Field Guide to Birds of Western North America, Copyright 2003 (Knopf, publisher). Grabbed the kitchen copy for quick confirmation.

Previous Experience

Travels to Alaska in the summertime have yielded many sightings. In Colorado this year, have seen several flocks, and one individual bird, most recent sightings were in January of this year, with extensive study of flock that was spent many days at Lee Martinez Park in Ft Collins.
