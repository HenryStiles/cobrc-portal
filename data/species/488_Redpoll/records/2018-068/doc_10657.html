Description of the Bird

I first saw this bird on a branch 3 feet above the feeder. I thought a Sparrow with a red cap. I grabbed the camera and he (she) came to the feeder. It was feeding with some American Goldfinch. I took several pictures and it left. I looked it up in Sibleys. I saw the Common and the Hoary were very similar. It returned and I got a few more pictures and a 9 sec viedo of it on the feeder. It returned one more time about 9:30, for a very short visit. I hope it returns in the morning.

Description: A small bird about the size of the American Goldfinch. It has a small yellow beak, with black on its face similiar to the House Sparrow. A small black bib below the beak, and black above the beak between the eyes.  The eyes were dark, with a white eyebrow above the eye to the ruby red cap. The Eyebrows meet in the center of the forehead with some black on the center of the forehead.  There is no eyering, but the facial black comes to the front of the eye. There is white above, behind and under the eye. I did not see a eyeline behind the eye. There is a light brown mottling in the cheek area. The breast and belly are white. The Sides are white with dark broad streaking. I could not see any fine streaking on the flanks. The feet and legs are Black. There is some streaking to the under tail coverts, and the tail is light grey and notched. I did not see any pinkish color on the breast. The back of the head looked a mottled brownish color. It's back was grey brown. It had one small white wing bar, and one larger white wing bar..

Similar Species Discussion

I eliminated the Hoary Redpoll, as I thought the streaking on the sides were broad and not fine on the flanks, and secound, because I could see some fine streaking on the undertail coverts.
