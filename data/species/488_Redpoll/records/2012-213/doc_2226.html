Description of the Bird

Small finch with red cap, small conical mostly yellow bill, with brown back with dark vertical streaks. Black on chin surrounded by red. A male present had red extending onto the flanks and breast. Dark broad streaks were on the flanks of all three birds. Brown ear coverts with frosty face. Tail short, undertail coverts with streaks. Wings dark brown with large white wingbar and white-edged wings and tertials. Lower belly white.
Behaviors: Feeding on black oil sunflowers at feeders and on the ground. Also perched in conifer waiting to come down to feed.
Call: none heard
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Hoary Redpoll - usually with smaller bill that looks like it was pushed into the face, but newer characters are unstreaked undertail coverts on Hoary and sometimes a lighter frostier, less brown appearing back.
