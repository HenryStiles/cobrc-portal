Viewing Conditions

Light:  Was horribly backlit at finch feeder. When it moved to the nearby trees was, at times, in full late afternoon sunlight.

Description of the Bird

Small, brown-streaked, tiny yellow bill, black mask, extensive black on throat, red forecrown, pink wash on breast and extending down on sides of flanks
Behaviors: Bird was very actively feeding from finch feeder. Was first seen fending off a Pine Siskin. At times would fly to nearby Russian Olive or Elm tree and sit quite still.
Call: chid chid chid, tjip, tjip
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Was seen with Pine Siskins and House Finches. Quickly eliminated from both as they do not have a black mask or black on the throat. This bird had deep red on forecrown, whereas House finches have more extensive red to red orange.
