Description of the Bird

Two males and one female were observed.
A small finch similar in size to a siskin, but with very distinct red coloring and yellow bill. Males and female both had a red cap, yellow bill and dark face. Males had pink on the breast, and all three had dark streaking on the flanks.
Behaviors: The birds were feeding at two thistle feeders amongst a small flock of Pine Siskins, and American Goldfinches. Other birds were also present and feeding from a sunflower feeder including House Finches and House Sparrows. The birds spent 30 to 45 seconds at the feeders and then flushed to nearby trees as a flock, before filtering down one by one back to the feeders and repeating the cycle the whole time we watched.
Call: There were many birds at the feeders and we were unable to single out calls from the Redpolls.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was very easy to separate from similar species. The unique markings and size easily separate it from all other finch species in this area. The only similar species possibility that I know of is Hoary Redpoll and the streaking on the undertail coverts of the birds we observed eliminates that possibility.
