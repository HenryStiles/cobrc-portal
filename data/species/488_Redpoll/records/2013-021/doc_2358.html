Observer Information

Reporter:  Nic Korte   
E-mail: 
Other Observers:  Larry Allison; Dave, Diane and Jackson Trappett drove up there a few days later and reported a larger number of redpolls on WSBN.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Redpoll
First Date/Time:  1/1/2013
Last Date/Time:  1/1/2013
Duration (total time in view):  ~2hours
County:  Mesa
Specific Location:  V road, North of Plateau Creek
Number:  30
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Multiple Birds - Mixed
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Pinyon/Juniper on the hillside, sage scrub below.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10 x 42 bins, but often the birds were so close that no optics were needed
Distance:  as close as 6 ft
Light:  Both sides of the car and road in perfect light...multiple observations of the birds as the flocks moved back and forth along the roadside. We turned around and then went through the flocks a second time.

Description of the Bird

See photos.
Behaviors: Foraging within a much larger mixed flock of rosy finches.
Call: There was such a large flock of birds that the song or call was not heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The most similar in terms of shape is a pine siskin, but these had the red "poll."

Resources Used

Sibley's, Experience

Previous Experience

I have seen redpolls previously in CO--a pair came to a feeder in Routt Co a few years ago and I drove up to see it.
