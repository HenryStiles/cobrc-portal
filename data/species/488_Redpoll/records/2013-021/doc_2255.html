Description of the Bird

1. Birds were in a very rural area within large (hundreds) of rosy finches of all three types.
2. Size, yellow bill, shape of bill, coloration pattern were diagnostic.
Behaviors: You'll note there are three in one of the photos, I attached. At least once, in a different location, I had six redpolls in the same binocular view. I took over 60 photographs in the hour or so we spent with these birds. I have photos where all of the birds besides the redpolls were black rosy-finches and others where they were all gray-crowned.
Call: Within large flock of rosy finches-call not noted, probably not really discernible in such a large group of birds
Plumage: adult winter

Similar Species Discussion

I suppose a house finch or a cassin's finch--which are larger, thicker, less-pointed and darker bill

Resources Used

Sibley's, Stokes new book

Previous Experience

I see house finches every day. I saw a common redpoll in Steamboat Springs some years ago.
