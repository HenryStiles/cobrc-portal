Habitat

Busy E -W Bighorn Rd. to sourth within 70', I-70 to north 250 yds. away but up on viaduct. I-70 the south boundary of Eagles Nest Wilderness. So a sort of "frontier" between wilderness & residential.
Lodgepole pines, spruce and aspen surround house at small distance from feeder. Provide launch area & escape for birds at feeder. Below is my dormant backyard garden, a narrow strip, and some small shrubbery.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss Victory 10 x 32 bins
Distance:  3 to 5 ' depending on whether on deck or feeder.
Light:  Feeder on west side of house. Sun behind ridge after 3pm. Birds usually appeared on snowy days with less sun, but the feeder & deck were within 5' of my window.

Description of the Bird

Small, Siskin-sized bird, finch tail, many with very rosy or pink markings on breast & body, distinct red yamake on males. Females much more muted coloring. Will send photos from darkish day if they can be attached or will email separately. Distinctive little goatee like dark coloring on chin of males & around bills. Females predominantly streaky brown backs, both with light-colored breasts where not red or pink depending on gender. This rosy coloring grew more pronounced over 6 wks. when I observed these birds, presumably the same flock.
Behaviors: I have a sunflower seed feeder in winter & a thistle sock I put out after Christmas. The first pair or two of Redpolls showed up December 29th or so. Soon there were as many as 20 which arrived always just as a snow storm was about to hit or had come in overnight in East Vail (8500' elevation at my roofline, feeder off 2nd floor deck outside window by kitchen sink). They appeared several times a week till I moved out Feb. 15th including that day.The Redpolls were hungry, greedily so, monopolizing feeders, but I did not see them actively being aggressive to chickadees, House Finches, or others. They just seemed to crowd out the others from feeder and deck railings.
Call: Mostly eating, fussing at other birds.
Plumage: Moving into breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Way too small for House Finches. More like Pine Siskin but coloration all wrong though brown streaking on backs similar until you looked closely. In 20 years in East Vail I have never seen Redpolls.

Resources Used

Sibley, the large one as well as Western Birds.

Previous Experience

I see Pine Siskin a lot in late winter and certainly in summer. This year instead of Cassin's which I used to see regularly at feeder, I have House Finches. I am a veteran of Atlas II.
