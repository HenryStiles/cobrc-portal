Description of the Bird

Three birds seen in flight only (about 3-4 meters above the ground) and though they all appeared similar, one seemed paler than the others -- this bird was possibly a male. They were obvious Carduelis finches (small birds with small bills, some streaking underneath, and with obviously notched tails) that I first thought were Pine Siskins (PISI). However, their calls alerted me to the possibility that they were redpolls, so I studied one carefully as the small flock flew by. This bird was obviously paler than most PISIs would be and the underside of the wings was pale, completely lacking the usually obvious yellow or yellowish wing stripes on the underside of the wings of PISI. The upperside of the wings also had no yellow, instead having two fairly obvious white (not yellow) wing bars.
Behaviors: Flying past us; calling
Call: Two calls given: the very chattery "chi-chi-chi" and the sweet and rising "weee"; both calls were somewhat siskin-like, but the first was harder, more chattery and the second was thinner
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

PISI, the most similar species, is eliminated on wing pattern (both upper and under) and on call notes; no other small, streaky finches at all likely
