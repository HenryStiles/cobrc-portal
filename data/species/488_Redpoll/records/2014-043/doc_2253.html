Light:  Great light conditions (both days). Sunny and bright. Some wispy clouds.

Description of the Bird

Pine Siskin-sized bird foraging on ground with juncos. Primary coverts were light-edged and mantle and flanks streaked. Red fore-crown and black throat and lores very visible. Notch in tail visible as it was hopping around on ground. Please see photos.
Behaviors: Bird was foraging on ground eating sunflower seeds that had dropped from my tray. (2nd CORE [later date] was hanging on cloth feeder eating thistle. Both ate only seeds when present.
Call: none heard
Plumage: probable adult female or juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Pine Siskin & both regular goldfinch species lack red fore-crown and black face markings.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have seen this species once in Larimer County, Colorado and once saw a small flock near Rochester, NY during winter.
