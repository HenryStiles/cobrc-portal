Description of the Bird

Small striped finch with predominate brown and gray tones. Male crown was dark with blood red tones. Bill was sharply pointed, straight, and yellow with dark culmen and at base. Face at base of bill was blackish. Breast was washed with reddish pink and darkly striped on sides and flanks. One prominent whitish wing-bar and faint second upper wingbar.

Females were similar without pinkish-red wash on breast.
Behaviors: Birds were feeding at feeder and then dropped to ground to husk seeds.
Call: Not heard well
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Pine Siskins are superficially similar but lack the red on crown and breast and have a dark bill. They also exhibit yellow in primaries and at base of tail.

Purple, Cassin's, and House Finches are somewhat similiar but all lack the blackish face with a smaller yellow bill. Also, the males have more extensive red tones and generally less striping on sides of breast and flanks.
