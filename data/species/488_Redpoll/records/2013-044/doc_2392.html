Habitat

High altitude evergreens prevailed. Birds were in fairly heavily treed portion of yard on feeders.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 8X42 Ultravid HD binoculars.
Distance:  on average, ~40 feet.
Light:  Bright and sunny, cloudless, birds were observed in shadier area of yard, but views not impeded.

Description of the Bird

Small flock of eight redpolls, males with red caps and breast, females with red limited to cap. Smaller than house finches, with small yellow bills, prominent streaking on flanks. Feed as a flock on tube feeder, which contained niger seed.
Behaviors: Flock fed actively, intermittently flying to cover in nearby evergreens, then returning as a group to what seemed to be the preferred feeder.
Call: A few call notes were heard as birds fed.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Not Pine Siskins, as these birds showed red coloration (lacking yellow wing markers), had obvious yellow bills. Not Cassin's or House Finches, based on size, distribution of red coloration, and yellow bills, not Hoary Redpolls because of prominent and heavy flank streaking.
