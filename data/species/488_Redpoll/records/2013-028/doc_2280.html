Description of the Bird

Visited Aspen Village subdivision where the redpolls had been noted on CO Bird a day or two earlier. Found about 10 birds in top of some small trees in back of a residential lot. Birds appeared to be eating on tips of small limbs or berries near top of tree. The red on top of head was obvious on most of the birds. On most, I could see the broad streaks on the flanks and upper back. Many had pink on the chest, varying from very little to one bird that had extensive. Lighting was poor but could see the yellow bill on one bird. Could not get good views of the undertail coverts. Coordinates are 39 degrees 17' 28" N, 106 55' 22" W, just west a couple houses from intersectiion of Buckskin and Ridge Trail Streets. There were perhaps additional redpolls that I could hear in other trees nearby but I was short on time and did not check. I did not have a camera with me.
Behaviors: Eating and flitting about in the top of the bushes/trees as finches do.
Call: Not observed
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Smaller than Pine Grosbeak. Small red top knot on heads eliminated Pine Siskin. Small red top knots, smaller bill, and yellow bills eliminated House, Purple, and Cassin's Finch. Small red top knots, smaller bills, yellow bills eliminated immature Crossbills.
