Description of the Bird

small finch with small all-yellow bill. Blackish below and above the yellow bill. Breast white with buffy wash and with wide dark gray streaks. Undertail coverts white but with dark streaks. Back brown with dark vertical streaks. Crown with red "poll" that was quite noticeable.
Behaviors: came into feeder with pieces of sunflower seeds where it competed with AMGOs and PISIs for the food.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult winter

Similar Species Discussion

No other species with the red "poll". Pine Siskin has same streaky flanks and back but with yellow wingbar and no red on top of head. The age was determined by Tony Leukering and agreed on by Peter Pyle to be an adult female.

Resources Used

None for I.D. Extensive online article studied that first appeared in North American Birds or Birding ?, that discussed separation of subspecies of Common Redpoll and Hoary Redpoll subspecies.

Previous Experience

I have seen many in breeding areas of northern Alaska in summer, but few in winter, and none for some time.
