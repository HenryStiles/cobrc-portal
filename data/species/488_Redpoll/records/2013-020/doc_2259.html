Species:  Redpoll

Specific Location:  4641 CR 8, Fraser
Number:  20
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Multiple Birds - Mixed
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

mountain forest -- heavily logged due to pine beetles, much more open land now. Spruce, fir and aspen just away from deck. Also much shrubbery. They like to sit high up in the aspens.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  just eyeballs mostly. Also have powerful binocs which we used to clarify the red caps and colors
Distance:  10
Light:  daylight, feeders are on deck just outside living area, facing roughly southwest

Description of the Bird

small, chickadee-sized, with red caps. Wing bars, many with reddish breasts.
Behaviors: flocks. Mixing in with the other species at the feeders. sit together in distant trees waiting for a chance to all swoop in together. They're eating sunflower chips.
Call: not heard so much, as it's cold outside
Plumage: red caps and breast

Similar Species Discussion

We have giant flocks of grey crowned rosy finches, clearly much bigger. Also chickadees, nuthatches are always around. We've had pine siskins in the past, but these have red caps and red breasts, so not those at all. I've also seen Cassin's Finch once or twice, but those are also definitely bigger, and the red caps were standing up like Mohawks on those

Resources Used

Sibley's, Birds of the West, eBird/All about Birds

Previous Experience

I've been watching birds at these feeders for several years, mostly all logged on eBird.

Notes

Memory

Additional Information

Comments: I'm hopeful they're still there, as we haven't been for 2 weeks and it's been especially cold. Feeders may have been emptied. There are a lot of feeding birds up there. Pine beetle logging created a lot more friendly habitat. Someone should study this.
Time: 7 a.m. to dusk
Elevation: 9000
