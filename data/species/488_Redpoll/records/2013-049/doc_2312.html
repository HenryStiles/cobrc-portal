Description of the Bird

Small, pale finches described here as one, though with comments about differences in sexes/ages; with yellow bill; variably bright red crown patch separated from black above bill by white or whitish forehead (barely minimal in some individuals); white superciliary; streaked gray-white auriculars; grayish-brown rear crown and nape streaked darker; chests and sides variably pink (in males) or white (in females and possibly some imm. males); sides with obvious dark brownish-gray streaks; white belly, vent, and undertail coverts, with many of latter with dark shaft streaks
Behaviors: attending feeders
Call: chittery calls like those of Pine Siskin, but more run together than exhibited by that species
Plumage: Formative and/or basic

Similar Species Discussion

Pine Siskin lacks red on crown, underparts; Hoary Redpoll is paler, with usually smaller red crown patch, less streaking on underparts, little or no streaking on undertail coverts or rump, and often have a noticeably shorter and relatively deeper bill; males typically have paler pink on chest and sides
