Description of the Bird

I will not spend too much time on the identification of these birds, since it is quite obvious and has become very familiar to Colorado birders over this winter of a major redpoll irruption.

Small streaky finches with distinctive red caps ("polls") at the forecrown. There is no other species with which this bird can be confused except for the well-known debate about common versus hoary. This flock did not contain any birds that i would consider a good candidate for hoary.
Behaviors: Flying back and forth between two dead trees, perching in the open. Occasional trips to the ground to a patch of snow to eat snow (drink).
Call: Chittering characteristic of flocks of these birds
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

There are no other species with which this bird can be confused. The small red cap is diagnostic.
