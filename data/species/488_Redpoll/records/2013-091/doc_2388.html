Description of the Bird

About the size of a chickadee... bit larger. Dark at base of bill. Red cap. Female streaked breast. Male pink breast. Wing bars. Forked tail.
Behaviors: Came in to feed at feeders (3 max). Also saw some (5 max) while walking the neighborhood, with flock of MOCH.
Call: Didn't hear.
Plumage: adults

Similar Species Discussion

I am familiar with CORE in Maine where I live. I know to look if it was a Hoary, but noted the darker color and streaking on flanks. Didn't see undertail coverts. Male also seemed too pink to be a Hoary.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide App. Yes, we did look at the app when viewing the birds, more to teach my cousin, as this was a new bird for him.

Previous Experience

I see CORE in Maine in the winter time. Have not ever seen a Hoary.
