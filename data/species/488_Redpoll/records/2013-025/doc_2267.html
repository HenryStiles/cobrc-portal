Description of the Bird

Distinct red caps on all individuals. Slightly smaller than House Finches in same area. Small, sharp, yellow bills with black down chin and across eyes. I see them as having a rather "pinched" expression. Overall much more pale than other finches or sparrows in area. Chest overall white-ish with brown streaks down flanks. Half of individuals had pale red flush on chest. Backs brown mottled.
Behaviors: Roosted as a group. Preferred nijer seed to sunflower. They've been at my feeders for about a month now and I'm hearing about them at other homes in the area.
Call: I didn't get a good read on the calls. There were too many other birds around.
Plumage: I would assume non-breeding plumage.

Similar Species Discussion

The House Finches around have a grey cap. We didn't see any Cassin Finches around, but they are generally more brown, especially the chest. These birds had distinct yellow bills.

Resources Used

Nat. Geo. field guide to NA birds, Kaufman field guide. Sibly Guide.

Previous Experience

The past few years I've had a few of these in my yard for short periods of time. This year is the greatest number and length of time they've been around.
