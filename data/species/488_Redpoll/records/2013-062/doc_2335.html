Description of the Bird

An assortment of little yellow-billed finches sporting little red yarmulkes, some with red chests and all with brown backs and rivulets of brown streaking on their flanks feeding in a swale of sunflowers in the company of two American Tree Sparrows.

The number of 35 was reached at by counting several times over after the very mobile flock had launched into the air and re-settled onto a different patch of sunflowers. I hit 33 several times with birds I knew were sitting on the ground in the snow and not visible for my counts. I'd estimate the number was actually closer to 40-45 when they all flew.
Behaviors: Keeping together is a general flock. All flew together off to the east.
Call: Variety. Including some very-goldfinch like calls, as well as buzzy calls.
Plumage: All

Similar Species Discussion

No other finch except for HORE has a little red cap and pink/red on the upper chest with streaking on the flanks.

I looked long/hard for a possible HORE, but every bird I saw had warm brown tones.
