Habitat

Open lacustrine, low brush. Thick sunflowers with a row of taller trees nearby.

Viewing Conditions

Light:  Light conditions did not impact the observation. Sun was behind me and slightly left (west) as I watched the birds, not obscured by clouds.

Description of the Bird

Found three small finches together. Obvious red frontal-crown patch. Dark lores and chin. Sides had thick brownish streaks; back also streak brown. One prominent white wing bar with a less obvious white bar above it. Very short, stout, yellow bill. Fairly average-length tail, slightly on the short side. Two males were present and had a reddish wash across the breast. I only managed a single photo of a single bird's rump, and it looks surprisingly unstreaked, but nothing in any of my other photos suggests Hoary Redpoll to me. I will include the rump photo simply for completeness.
Behaviors: Primarily foraging on sunflowers near lake shore. Calling to one another occasionally, and making short flights within sunflowers. After watching and photographing for several minutes, I moved forward a few steps. Two of the birds flew out of sight, and the third flew high into a nearby tree, at which point I decided to leave them alone.
Call: Occasionally gave thin rising call note.
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

All three birds appeared too streaky on their sides for Hoary Redpoll. I did not get a good look at undertail coverts on any of the individuals. The only decent view of rump that I managed, I captured in a photo, which I have included. The rump appears quite unstreaked, more like Hoary, however the side streaking was quite bold. I did not feel comfortable interpreting the stubbiness of the bill as Common or Hoary.

Pine Siskin was eliminated by the red crown, stubby yellow bill, and lack of yellow on wings, breast, or tail.
House, Cassin's, and Purple Finch females were eliminated by red crown, small size, dark lores and chin, and stubby yellow bill.

Resources Used

The Sibley Field Guide to Birds of Western North America was used shortly after sighting to confirm the identification.

Previous Experience

This was a life bird for me at the time. I have never confirmed seeing a Hoary Redpoll. I frequently see Pine Siskin and House Finch, and have experience with Cassin's and Purple Finch as well.
