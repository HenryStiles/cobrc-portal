Description of the Bird

the bird was about the size of a Robin, though the relatively longer tail and slimmer body gave it a longer, thinner look. The bill was black with a paler gray base to the lower mandible, and long (about 3/4 or so the length of the head), fairly thin (about half again the thickness of the eye at the base), and with a slight but noticeable decurve. The iris was bright yellow, with an orange tinge when seen in the shade. The legs were a dusky gray, with a pinkish tinge.

The bird's cap was a medium brown with a distinct graysih tinge, blending into a cold gray cheek patch. Some gray feathers extended from the cheek patch to the nape, which was otherwise mostly warm medium brown. The back was medium brown, with a warmer and slightly more rufous tone than the cap, but with some gray-edged feathers. The wings were a warm medium brown, with a distinct rufous tinge, especially on the edges of the flight feathers. There were also two thin white winge-bars. The uppertail coverts and tail were a warm medium brown, with a distinct but not strong rufous tinge, about the same color as the flight feathers.

The bird's underparts were white, with bold and heavy black streaking across the chest and down the flanks. The belly was clean white. The undertail coverts were white with some small and hard to see dark streaks that I could only discern in the scope. The bird's throat was white, with an indistinct black malar stripe.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of its time either perched still in dense bushes, or scratching around under those bushes for food. It would occasionally perch out in the open on a branch, once high up in a tree, and once even on the roof of a nearby house. It would also spend some time next to a small waterfall and pool nearby.
Call: none heard
Plumage: unkown

Similar Species Discussion

All species of Thrasher other than Brown and Sage were ruled out by the clean white underparts with heavy black streaks. Sage Thrasher was ruled out by the longer, thicker, and decurved bill, warmer brown tone to the upperparts, and cleaner, darker, and heavier streaking below. Wood Thrush was ruled out by slimmer shape, longer tail, long, decurved bill, browner upperparts, and streaking rather than spotting below.

Brown Thrasher was ruled out by the gray cheek patch, upperparts that were only slightly tinged rufous, heavier and darker streaking below, with not hint of brown in any of the streaks, and slight streaking on the undertail coverts. The bill shape, with slightly more of a decurve and slightly heavier, was used as a supporting mark.
