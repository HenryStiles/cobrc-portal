Habitat

It was mostly staying it the bushes and tall grasses that was in the yard, but it would go into the short grasses in the open in the yard every once in a while.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski Spotting Scope and Lieca 8X32 BN Binoculars
Distance:  At the closest it was about 80 feet away
Light:  At first the light was in front of us, but then it moved to the south of us. We still got great views of the bird.

Description of the Bird

This bird had a long, blackish bill. The back was dark brown. The dark black streaks on the bird was on the breast, belly, and also a little on the undertail covert. It had gray on the face.
Behaviors: The bird was digging and scratching on the ground, mostly in some tall grass and a few times it flew over to the fish pond. It usually was in bushes or tall grass, but a few times it was out in the open in the yard.
Call: I never heard it
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Curve-billed, Bendire's, California, Crissal, and Le Conte's Thrashers
These thrashers don't have streaks on the breast and belly. They also don't have the same color of brown, if at all, on the back.
Sage Thrasher
It is more gray than brown on the back and it has a short slightly curved bill.
Brown Thrasher
It has a bright brown back. Not as long curved bill and it has a light lower mandible and a dark upper mandible. The streaks on the BRTH also are not as dark as the LBTH.
