Description of the Bird

On the morning of 1/14/2006, I saw a Brown Thrasher and another "Brown Thrasher, that looked a bit strange. The overall coloring was different, it had a dark bill, gray cheek patch, orangish/yellow eye, white underparts with black streaking. The bird wouldn't give me clear views, just partial views. The first photo was taken on 1/14/2006.

On, 1/27/2006. Bill Maynard went to Chico Basin Ranch, and re-found the "odd" thrasher, and got excellent photos of it and he was sure the bird was indeed a Long-billed Thrasher. After seeing the his photo, I decided, it looks like one to me. So, I went to Chico Basin that afternoon, and met Mark Peterson there, who had already seen the bird. I good scope views of it. Seeing everything I had seen before, though saw it in the sunshine and good like, instead of the cloudy overcast light from the 1/14/2006. My second photo is taken on 1/27/2006.
Behaviors: The bird would mostly stay hidden in the russian olive trees, behind branches. It would come out for a quick clear view, though overall it was very hard to get great views of.
Call: none
Plumage: adult
