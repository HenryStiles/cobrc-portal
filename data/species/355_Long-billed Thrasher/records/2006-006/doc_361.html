Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Long-billed Thrasher

Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  45 minutes
County:  El Paso
Specific Location:  Chico Basin Ranch, It is about 1 mile south of Hanover. Then there is an area with a lot of trees and Russian Olives and that is where it was hanging out.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

It was hanging out in a long row of dense Russian Olives.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski spotting scope and Lieca 8X32 BN Binoculars
Distance:  At the closest I was about 40 feet.
Light:  Every time I saw the bird I was between the bird and the sun.

Description of the Bird

The was a darker brown bird. It had a long curved bill that was mostly all black. Black streaks on the breast, belly, and it goes down onto the undertail coverts. It also has a gray face.
Behaviors: Each time I saw the bird I heard it scratching on the ground first. Then about 5 to 10 minutes after listening to the scratching then it would fly up to the Russian Olives and sit there for a few minutes.
Call: The only noise I heard from this bird is scratching through the grasses and leaves on the ground.
Plumage: Unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Curve-billed, Bendire's, California, Crissal, and Le Conte's Thrashers
These thrashers don't have streaks on the breast and belly. They also don't have the same color of brown, if at all, on the back.
Sage Thrasher
It is more gray than brown on the back and it has a short slightly curved bill.
Brown Thrasher
It has a bright brown back. Not as long curved bill and it has a light lower mandible and a dark upper mandible. The streaks on the BRTH also are not as dark as the LBTH.

Resources Used

The Sibley Guide to Birds before and after the observation and also the National Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America while I was going through my photos.

Previous Experience

I have seen two Long-billed Thrashers. One in Texas and one in Mexico.
