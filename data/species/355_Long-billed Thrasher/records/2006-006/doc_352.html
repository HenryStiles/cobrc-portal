Habitat

Line of Russian Olives that had a very thick matting of dead Tumble Weeds around their bases.

Description of the Bird

A Brown Thrasher was seen very nearby along with this bird. The size and structure of the two birds was essentially the same. There were four main differences between the birds. First, the color of the top of the head, entire back and upper tail were a quite dowdy shade of brown, as opposed to the much richer brown/rufous of the BT. Second, the entire face and back onto the foreneck was a dark gray, as opposed to a much lighter shade of brown to gray in the BT. Third, the bill was entirely black with no lighter color on the mandible, and looked just a touch longer than the BT bill. Fourth, the breast streaking, especially on the sides, was black and bold on a white background, as opposed to less bold on an off-white background in the BT. The Long-billed Thrasher seemed to have an orangier eye, but this is a pretty subjective fieldmark. Finally, both birds had two white wingbars and darkish legs.
Behaviors: The bird would feed and hide very low to the ground in thick tangles especially when people came close. However, a few minutes later it would appear higher in the Russian Olives and sometimes eat their friut. This behavior is typical of the Thrasher family.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Winter

Similar Species Discussion

From Brown Thrasher by back color, face color, all black bill and bolder breast spotting; see above.
Bold breast streaking eliminates all the other Thrashers except for Sage Thrasher. A Curve-billed Thrasher was also seen at the same location. From Sage Thrasher by size, back color, bill size, etc.
