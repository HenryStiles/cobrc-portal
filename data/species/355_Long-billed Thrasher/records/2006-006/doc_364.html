Description of the Bird

Large thrasher, more similar in coloration, plumage pattern, and shape to Brown Thrasher than other Toxostoma. The all-dark bill was fairly long, slightly curved - longer and more curved than Brown Thrasher, but less so of each than Curve-billed Thrasher. The crown was a dirt brown color that extended onto the back, wings, and tail. The long tail (approximately the same length as the body minus head) lacked any remarkable features such as spots or bands. The bird had an extensive gray face, starting at the lores through the eyes including the entire cheek. The eyes were a yellow-orange. The wings were mostly plain brown, except for two black wingbars tipped in white on the greater and median coverts. Underparts were basally white, starting at the chin and extending throughout to the vent region. Bold black streaks starting at the malar extended to the flanks and belly. I did not notice heavy streaking on the undertail coverts, but there appeared to possibly be some faint markings.
Behaviors: The bird was found in thick underbrush, but seen most often perched in Russian Olives. Once, for a less than a minute, I observed the bird foraging on the ground, scratching at the leaf litter.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Brown Thrasher is most similar (there was one in the area for easy comparison). This bird lacked the bright rust color of Brown Thrasher, as well as having a longer, more curved bill, an extensive gray face, and white underparts with bold black streaking (Brown Thrasher has buffier underparts and thinner, more spotty streaking).
