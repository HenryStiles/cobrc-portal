Description of the Bird

Three birds were seen in the distance south of our postion.  Two were obvious Turkey Vultures (large, black and with a pronounced dihedral), the third bird was slightly smaller and black, but still had a slight dihedral.  The birds proceeded to approach us and then soared overhead allowing for some great views and comparisons of the buteo with the vultures.

Large, mostly black buteo.  Very long, relatively "thin" wings (for a buteo).  Underwing pattern similar to nearby Turkey Vultures, meaning black underwing coverts and white flight feathers that were finely barred.  Tail longish and narrow two dark bands and three white bands (terminal, mid-tail, and one at the base of the tail).

After seeing the tail bands and noting that the wings were not short and thick (i.e. not paddle-shaped), the name Zone-tailed Hawk was quickly attached to the bird.  Several people in our group had cameras and took pictures.  Rob Raker's photos turned out the best and are attached to this record.

The birds continued to soar past our position towards the dam and we lost sight of them.

Similar Species Discussion

Long thin wings and thick black and white tail bands eliminate most buteos.

Dark morph Broad-winged Hawk & dark morph Short-tailed Hawk are a "small" buteos, tail bands are roughly equal in size (BWHA) or thinly banded (STHA), neither soars with a dihedral; wings ralatively short and thicker.

Common Black Hawk VERY broad, paddles-shaped wings, tail short with a single thick, black tail band.

Harris's Hawk & Red-souldered Hawk have reddish wing linings not black, tail with either a single white, thick tail band, or many thin bands, wings not long.

Resources Used

Reference material: No books, "apps", or other material resources needed or used to identify this bird.  Identification fairly straight forward with decent views.
