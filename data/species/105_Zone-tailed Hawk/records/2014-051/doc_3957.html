Description of the Bird



The bird was spotted flying low over the trees in the SW corner of Lamar Community College Woods—a black raptor-type bird that showed a strong dihedral in its swooping flight pattern.  The first impression by all viewers was that of a Turkey Vulture.  The underwings were Turkey Vulture-like white dark flight feathers on the leading edge of the wing but contrasting pale tones on the trailing edge.  In my view, this dark/light pattern showed much more contrast than a Turkey Vulture shows. 
There was not a significant trailing black edge on the wing.

Viewed in flight, the head was clearly black and feathered.  Bright yellow legs stood out against the lower belly as well as a similarly bright yellow cere that was shaped like a reverse “C” on the face behind the bill as the bird flew from left to right.

The tail was long-ish, ruling out Common Black Hawk (similarly, the wings seemed relatively thin).  However I could not pick out a strong black and white pattern on the
tail, it seemed uniformly dark to me.  Consequently I judged the bird to be an immature.



Similar Species Discussion

Turkey Vulture would have a feather-less red or black head.

Black Vulture would have white at end of wings.

Common Black Hawk would have a shorter tail and black trailing edges to the wing.
