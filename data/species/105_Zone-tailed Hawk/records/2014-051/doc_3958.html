Description of the Bird

Bird flew in and looked like a Turkey Vulture both in size and flight style but only seen briefly in flight before landing in a Cottonwood.  When perched, all dark (black) bird minus a few "gray" spots on the belly area.  The undertail also appeared gray/white when perched.  The cere was bright yellow as were the legs (probably still are).  When the bird took off the underwings were "white" with dark barring as was the spread tail.  This gave the tail a gray appearance.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Black-Hawk:  Very similar in overall size and structure.  COBH does not look like a TUVU in flight as this bird did and COBH has a large dark trailing edge to the wings.  I also am not sure but I do not think you typically have a COBH that is all black with a immature tail pattern?

Turkey/Black Vulture:  Feathering on the head of the subject bird and bright yellow cere/legs ruled out either Vulture.

Dark Morph Broad-winged Hawk:  Overall size, bill size, typically BWHA does not have that bright of yellow cere and legs and flight style is not dihedral like the subject bird.

Dark morph Ferruginous Hawk:  Lack of rufous coloring anywhere on the bird, the gape did not project as far back as a FEHAs would and the tail patttern was different.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have only seen three or four ZTHAs prior to this and all of them were in flight only.  This was the first perched ZTHA which initially caused me a bit of confusion as it seemed to be long-legged like a COBH.  I have seen close to 20 COBHs and hundreds to thousands of all other species considered.
