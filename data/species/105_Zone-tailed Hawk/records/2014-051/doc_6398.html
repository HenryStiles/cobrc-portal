Description of the Bird

First seen in flight. Resembled a Turkey Vulture, and I initially called it that until seeing the finely barred tail. Mark Peterson shouted 'Common Black Hawk', which clued me in to the fact that this was not a TUVU. I also realized that COBH was not right either, because this bird had a finely barred tail (obvious in flight), and finely barred black and white remiges, but was otherwise dark overall, including the upperwing (visible when the bird banked north and briefly disappeared from sight). In flight, it also showed the typical characteristic "tilting" glide with wings in a V shape (a la TUVU, which gave me the initial inpression), and unlike COBH which soars/glides on flat wings with tails spread. We refound the bird perched nearby, and Mark obtained photos. Perched showed all dark plumage, massive yellow feet, yellow cere (though bill not quite as heavy or long as COBH and lacking yellow on lores), and white speckling on breast 

Similar Species Discussion

See previous and photos.
From TUVU by head (see photo) and barring on underwing and tail in flight.
From COBH by tail pattern (finely barred vs broad white band on Ad), structure (longer, narrower wings and appearance of longer tail), flight pattern (rocking V-shaped glide), and details of head pattern on perched bird (yellow cere obvious but lacking yellow on lores like COBH, and bill somewhat smaller/shorter than COBH).
From dark morph RLHA by habitat, time of year, behavior, flight profile, and those big honking feet.
From other dark morph Buteos by plumage details (finely barred tail and remiges from below, lacking "border" to wing like BWHA or STHA; head pattern unlike Harlan's RTHA or SWHA).
