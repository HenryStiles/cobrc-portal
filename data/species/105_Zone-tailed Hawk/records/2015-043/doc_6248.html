Description of the Bird

Dark raptor floating initially floating by itself in the wind over Burlington Cemetery. Initially thought it was a Turkey Vulture and wanted to verify it as a county bird. It came low over the cemetery and I realized it had a thick white band on the underside of the tail and a small dark bill with a yellow base. I couldn't really see the other thin white bands on the tail but the legs were definitely yellow. The wings were much too narrow for Common Black-Hawk and the tail pattern was wrong. Having watched ZTHA and COBL at Tubac Bridge in AZ during migration, I felt confident this bird was a Zone-tailed Hawk!  After the initial sighting, my wife and I chased the bird around Burlington where it merged with at least a dozen Turkey Vultures, floating over the town.  I was surprised how similar it was to a Turkey Vulture until you had it in binocular view.

Similar Species Discussion

Turkey Vulture was eliminated by the white band on the tail and the small bill with yellow base.
Common Black-Hawk was eliminated by the narrow wings and incorrect tail pattern.
Black Vulture was eliminated by wing pattern which should show silver fingers on the tips of wings.

Resources Used

Personal experience from viewing Zone-tailed Hawk, Common Black-Hawk, Black Vulture, and Turkey Vulture in Arizona.

Previous Experience

I have viewed Zone-tailed Hawk, Common Black-Hawk, and Black Vulture in Arizona several times including at Tubac Bridge in March during hawk migration.  I have seen Turkey Vulture many times in a number of different states.
