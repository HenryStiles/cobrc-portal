Habitat

Purgatory Valley with rocky ridges

Description of the Bird

Flew across  CR 504 from roost tree in plains cottonwood by Purgatoire River to the north and landed in a short snag with a rock face in the background. After about 1 minute it flew upslope maybe 150 feet to a snag slightly higher in elevation. began to preen. After about another minute it took off to the east and circled mostly below the top of the ridge above the Painted Bunting location and as it gained altitude it disappeared over the ridge to the north not to be seen again that day. 

Similar Species Discussion

All dark phase buteos look somewhat similar but this raptor had small white spots on its belly and its face coloration with white and wide yellow cere were distinctive (see Kathy Mihm-Dunning's photo of the 1-year-old bird) In flight wings have white spots towards the wing tips, the primaries are all heavily barred and the wings are in a "V" shape. Outer three primaries are long and thin. This bird has just completed its first year and is molting, (brown and black primaries) from a 1st year bird to an adult as it only takes two years to become a full adult. In flight white spotting visible on belly and white splotches behind the neck.
