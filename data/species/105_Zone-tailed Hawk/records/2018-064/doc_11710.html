Description of the Bird


Bird 1:
Initially seen flying overhead from behind some large cottonwoods with TUVU.
Smaller than a TUVU, but with a superficially similar light/dark underside pattern.
A molting mess.
Felt to be a second year bird.
Undersides dark/almost black from throat through vent with scattered random white feathers
Wing linings and axillaries similarly dark
Primaries and secondaries appeared pale with narrow striping consistent except at the tips where some adult feathers were already in place with their wider dark ends while most were still in a juvenile state
Tail a mix of longer juvenile feather with narrow bands of dark while the notably shorter adult tail feathers were clearly incoming with the wider dark terminal band and a light band above clearly visible
In flight, wings appeared fairly narrow
When the bird perched, the entire backside appeared largely dark brown with some white feathers visible at the nape and scattered on the back
Legs bright yellow with black toenails
Bill large, black tipped but otherwise yellow
Eye dark
Silent

Bird 2:
Flew out from behind the cottonwoods in much the same manner, but then quickly flew off to the west where it landed in a tree where it was easily viewed and photographed for several minutes. Probable adult bird.
Overall an almost completely sooty black buteo with some brownish tones
Tail narrowly banded at the base with a band of white and then black towards the tip with a very narrow white terminal band visible on the underside of the tail when the bird was perched
Wing linings and axillaries sooty black
Primaries and secondaries viewed from underside appeared pale with narrow dark banding except at the tips which were wider forming a dark trailing edge
Legs bright yellow with black toenails
Bill large, black tipped but otherwise yellow
Eye dark
Silent

Similar Species Discussion

Very few species have the combination of such dark coloration with tail banding and the appearance of the undersides of the primaries and secondaries

TUVU: Size, tail banding, bill and so many other things.

COBH: Eliminated by the narrow banding at the base of the tail, tail length and narrowness of wings

Dark morph BWHA: Eliminated by overall color with the sooty tones, narrow banding at the base of the tail, narrow dark banding in the primaries and secondaries and the very prominent large yellow bill.

All other buteos eliminated by structural and coloration issues.
