Description of the Bird

Flew south to north at a height of about 40 feet almost directly over my head as I walked along road in C Loop of campground (I stopped when I saw the bird!). At first assumed it wouild be Turkey Vulture because it had the general shape and size of TV. But then noticed a couple of key points that distinguished it. Long and relatively narrow winged blackish buteo with rather long somewhat narrow tail. Holding wings in a dihedral. Larger head than Turkey Vulture and dark in color not red, protruding significantly in front of the remainder of the body, much more so than in the vulture. Mostly black tail, with a notable white band in the middle of the tail and at least one narrower white band closer to the base (as seen from below). Remiges paler than blackish wing lingings, more gray than black, as seen from below.

Similar Species Discussion

Turkey Vulture: Larger, dark head. Prominent white bands on tail.

Common Black Hawk: Much narrower wings and longer narrower tail (Black Hawk has very wide wings and a short wide tail). Habitat also did not fit this species.  

 

Previous Experience

I have seen this species and Common Black Hawk many times in both New Mexico and Arizona, especially since I started working as a birding guide in Arizona in 2017 and then moved back to New Mexico, but also starting in the 1980's when I travelled extensively in Latin America. I see Zone-tailed Hawk multiple times a year, most often in Los Alamos County. Of course, Turkey Vultures are common throughout the west and I see them very regularly.
