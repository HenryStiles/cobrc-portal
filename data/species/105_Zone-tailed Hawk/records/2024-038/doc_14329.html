Description of the Bird


Details

Pulled over to look at black spot on a sprinkler pivot approx 2-300 yards distant -- large uniformly black buteo with lengthy tail, yellow legs and yellow cere with a whitish/gray base. Very distant photo revealed two white tail bands, bottom one larger and complete -- upper band narrower and appeared shorter or incomplete. As I was setting up my scope, the hawk disappeared, but I was able to relocate -- through scope in flight soaring. Long wings, slightly tapered toward the tips -- remiges underneath slivery gray in contrast with darker leading edges and body and held wings in a slight dihedral -- mimicking a turkey vulture. last seen heading NE over the Sangres.

The bird was probably an adult bird as it had well developed tail bands and it was uniformly black without noticeable white spots or streaks. 

In addition, between the time of the photo and setting up my scope and then refinding the bird (ca. 2 minutes) it had covered a remarkable distance and height and I never observed its wings flapping. 

Media

Similar Species Discussion

I suppose  I considered Common Black Hawk for a brief moment when I saw the bird sitting at a distance through binoculars although the habitat ruled it out and when I saw the bird in flight as described, I ruled out that and any other species. I did consider dark morph Red-tailed Hawk (Harlan's) but the uniformly black feathering, the bright white tail bands, and bright yellow cere excluded Harlan's.

Previous Experience

I have seen Zone-tailed Hawk probably 20-25 times in AZ, TX, Mexico and Central America. Usually in flight.
