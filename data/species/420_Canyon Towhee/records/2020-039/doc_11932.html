Description of the Bird

Large emberezid passerine uniform gray-brown color except for dull rufous crown and vent area. Pale thin eyering. Gray short conical beak. Throat and lower belly washed with buff. Dull slaty malar stripes, tail and small diffuse spots on upper chest and sides of breast. Dark eyes, legs, feet. Frequently flicked wings and tail in unison. Weak flyer. Vocalized typical low pitched screechy call note. A higher-pitched cardinal-like call note was attributed to the towhee during a short period when it seemed agitated and flew
from its perch. Similar in size to nearby Spotted Towhees. 

Several other observers viewed this bird on 12/20/20 including Josh Bruenning, Skyler Bol and Rachel Hopper. On 12/22/20, observes included Shawn Mason, Dave Farmer (excellent photos in eBird), and Joe Mammoser, among others. 

Similar Species Discussion

Rufous-crowned Sparrow is a potential vagrant similar in plumage with darker malar stripes, brighter rufous crown and white eye ring, smaller size, different calls. Curve-billed Thrasher has longer thinner beak. Green-tailed Towhee has olive shaded flight feathers and brighter head/throat pattern. Both are rare in winter. Two similar towhees never recorded in Colorado are Abert’s Towhee with blackish face and California Towhee with brown cap with differing call notes.
