Description of the Bird

A bird of typical towhee shape and size. It had a reddish brown cap. Grayish/brown face with flecks of a lighter buffy brown on the cheeks and lores. The throat area was buffy brown with vertical streaks forming a slight necklace. The back of the neck, back, shoulders, upper wings and upper tail coverts were a uniform grayish brown. The upper tail was a dark gray with white tips on the outer two tail feathers. The belly was lighter and the undertail coverts were a warm cinnamon color. The bird was coming to a feeder at a house.
Behaviors: The bird would come in to feed around a bird feeder. It came in low to the ground and would typically scratch on the ground in and around the feeder. It did fly up on the feeder briefly then back to the ground.
Call: None
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

California Towhee is eliminated by no cap, darker plummage and lack of necklace, and it would be seriously out of range. Abert's Towhee is very dark around the lores and it does not have a cap. It too would be way out of range.
