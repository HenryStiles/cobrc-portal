Description of the Bird

I was driving west bound on Hwy 36 from the Last Chance rest area. About 12 miles east of Last Chance a larger bird caught my eye sitting on the fence wire against a post on the north side of the road. At least, it seemed large with mile after mile of Mourning Doves and Western Meadowlarks seen sitting on the wires up until then. I made a U-turn and pulled over onthe south side directly across the road from the bird. I was able to observe the bird for about a minute before it flew up and away from me to the north to the pole of a cattle pen about 50 yds away. The bird was pigeon shaped and appeared a bit bigger than the standard Rock Pigeon. It had a bill that was yellow with a black tip. The head was a uniform light gray which extended down the front of the neck and into the chest area where it appeared darker with a purplish hue. The feet were yellow. When the bird flew up it turned and I could see the back, shoulders and upper tail coverts were the same uniform gray. I could see the white crescent on the collar and glimpsed a bit of the iridescent spot. The top wing tips were darker to almost black and the tail had a broad band of light gray.
Behaviors: The bird was sitting on a wire. It got jittery when I stopped to view it and then flushed.
Call: None
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Red-billed Pigeon would have had a red bill, red feet and a reddish hue to the back of the neck and upper breast. Rock Pigeon would have had a white cere on the bill and no yellow feet. Also, they tend to have some type of patterning or bars on the wing and a lighter/white rump.
