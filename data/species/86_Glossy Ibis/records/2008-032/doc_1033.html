Description of the Bird

Bird was a large wader with a long decurved bill. It was with a flock of about 25 other plegadis ibises. This bird did not have any white feathering around the facial skin or around or behind the eye. The facial skin was a dark grayish-blue bordered by paler gray-blue edge at both the top and bottom edges. These lighter edges extended from the gape to the eye, but not around or behind the eye. The iris was completely dark brown without any hint of red. The bill was brownish-gray. The body was dark brown and the wings were dark with glossy greenish irridescense. The legs were a dark brown except the knees were reddish. This individual was about the same size as the other ibises, which were White-faced Ibises.
Behaviors: It was associating with a small flock of WFIB and feeding at the shoreline of Pastorius Reservoir. It was in grasses that were about 1-foot tall and it moved in and out of these grasses.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced is very similar and it is the common plegadis ibis in SW Colorado. The critical differences in alternate plumage are: lack of any white feathering around the facial skin, gray-blue to gray facial skin without any reddishness, totally dark iris and not any hint of red, warmer brown bill color rather than gray, and brown legs with reddish knees. A White-facedxGlossy hybrid is also a possibility. However, a hybrid would show at one or more traits of a WFIB which this bird did not.
