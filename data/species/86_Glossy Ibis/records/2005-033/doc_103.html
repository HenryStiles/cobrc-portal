Description of the Bird

Two birds were seen. The first Ibis had entirely cold gray legs and a gray bill. The facial skin above and below the lores was sky blue. The lores themselves were dark gray/black. Eye color was also dark gray/black. The second bird was identical with the following exceptions; The blue facial skin was also sky blue, however it the lines above and below the lores was significantly thinner. Also, leg color on the second bird was also cold gray, except for small areas of red on the knees.
Behaviors: The two Glossy Ibis were foraging in a flooded field with 8 White-faced Ibis.
Call: none
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis was elimated by the combination of sky blue facial skin surrounding dark lores and a dark eye. Also, there was no hint of red on the legs of the first bird, and only a slight hint of red on the knees of the second bird.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Observed many glossy Ibis in Rhode Island and in the Southeast. I have observed many White-faced Ibis in Colorado, and several other western states.

Additional Information

Comments: I consulted Kaufmann's "Advanced Birding" after the sighting and Kaufmann mentions that red coloration is not out of the range of variation for Glossy Ibis. It is interesting that the same bird also had less prominent blue surrounding the lores. Maybe a few White-faced genes.
