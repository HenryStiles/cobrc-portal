Description of the Bird

The bird was obviously a Plegadis ibis, with a long, decurved bill, rusty/green plumage, fairly long legs, and hunch-backed posture. The bill was brown and the eyes were also brown. The facial skin was blue and showed no evidence of purplish or pink as would be shown in a hybrid. The borders to the blue facial skin were a light blue, narrow, and extended from the base of the bill to the eye. Body plumage was rusty colored, save for iridescent green/purple across the lower mantle, wings, and tertials. No bronze color was present in the tertials. The legs were dark gray with contrasting red tibia-tarsal joints. The bird was also the largest ibis in the flock, which sometimes can be a useful tool.
Behaviors: Actively foraged in wet meadow. Usually had head down and foraged in a swinging motion. Also observed in flight.
Call: None heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis was eliminated as that species has more of a gray bill, has red facial skin, red eyes, has a bright, thicker white facial border that extends posteriorly to surround the eye, has a bronzy tint to the tertials, and has red legs throughout. A hybrid Plegadis was not considered as the facial skin would be violet in color, it would have light red eyes, and would have brighter and pinker tibia-tarsal joints.
