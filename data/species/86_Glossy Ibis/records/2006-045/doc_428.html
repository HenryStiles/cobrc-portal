Description of the Bird

Very similar in size, color, and shape to White-faced Ibis except for the differences noted below. Instead of white feathers bording the bill and eye, this bird showed light blue skin that traced the edges of lower and upper mandibles to the gape and above and below to the midpoint of each eye - there was definitely no lighter color (blue or white) past the midway point of either eye. Outlined by the light blue facial lining, the facial skin was dark, nearing black, and I could find no hint of purple or pink coloration. The eyes were dark brown without any hint of red, although eyes of nearby White-faced Ibis could be readily seen as red. The legs were dark with bright red "knees", differing dramatically from the red legs of the White-faced Ibis. Also, I noted that the bill was entirely brown, whereas the White-faced Ibis bills were all gray. Lastly, this was the largest Glossy Ibis in the group of 45 ibis.
Behaviors: Spent the entire time feeding in wet field with the other ibis.
