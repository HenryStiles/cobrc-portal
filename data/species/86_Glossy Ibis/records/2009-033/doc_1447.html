Description of the Bird

The bird was typical for a Plegadis ibis, being about 2 feet tall, having relatively long legs, glossy brown-green-maroon overall plumage, and long decurved bill. The bill was somewhat yellowish-gray in shade. Compared to nearby White-faced Ibis, this bird had blue feathers along the edge of the facial skin that extended from the base of the maxilla and mandible posterior to about the lower and upper center of the eye that formed pale blue lines emanating from the bill. Instead of being red, the facial skin (lores) was a very dark gray. The eyes were dark brown, not red. The mantle and wings seemed a bit more bluish than adjacent White-faced Ibis, which seemed to have a bit more yellowish-green cast to those areas. The legs were mainly a dull brownish-dark gray color, although the tibia-tarsus joints were maroon in color and contrasted with the majority of the leg.
Behaviors: The Glossy, as well as the White-faced, were feeding in a wet, grassy field. They would drop and lift their heads continually in a sewing machine manner as they walked through the tall grass.
Call: The ibis flock (mostly White-faced) were giving a grunting call as they fed.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis, which were nearby, would have a white border along the facial skin that would extend completely around the rear of the eye. Those lines would also be thicker than the thin blue lines of a Glossy. White-faced would also exhibit red facial skin and would possess a red eye. The legs of White-faced are also generally red.

Hybrid birds, which seem to becoming more common, would possess a mixture of characteristics between the two parental species. In general, the lines bordering the facial skin would be intermediate in length between Glossy and White-faced and are usually pinkish in shade. The facial skin of hybrids is usually purplish-pink. The eye color of intermediates is usually a very dark dark, a blended color between the bright red of White-faced and the brown of Glossy. The color of the legs of hybrids vary, but usually are a dark gray but have more extensive maroon extending from the tibia-tarsus joints onto the tibia and tarsus portions of the leg.
