Description of the Bird

The Glossy Ibis was with about 70 mostly alternate plumaged White-faced Ibis. It was an obvious Plegadis ibis in alternate plumage. The head, neck and body were an iridescent glossy chestnut with green and purple highlighted wings. The bill was long, decurved and brownish in color. It was walking in tall grass so I could not see it legs at any time. The bare facial skin was dark with a thin pale blue border that thinned behind the eye. The bird was approximately 50 yards off the road but was easy to pick out from the many White-faced Ibis using binoculars. We also observed the bird through a Leica scope.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding almost continuously during the entire observation period. As a result most of time we would wait for it to raise its head up out of the grass to see the distinguishing characteristics of the species.
Call: no vocalization heard
Plumage: adult in alternate plumage

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis is a very similar species that was observed in direct comparison to the reported bird. The dark facial skin bordered with a think blue line rather than a white ring of feathers was the primary field mark used in the determination.
