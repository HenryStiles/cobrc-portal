Description of the Bird

Bird was a medium large wader with a long decurved bill. It was with a sizable flock of about thirty plegadis waders. This bird did not have any white feathering around the facial skin and the facial skin was a dark grayish-blue bordered by a paler gray-blue edge both above and below the facial skin at the base of the bill and behind the gape. The iris was dark without any sign of red. The bill was brownish-gray with the tip being more grayish. The body was a glossy blackish reddish brown and the wings were dark with glossy greenish highlights. The legs were a dark brown except the knees were reddish. This individual was slightly larger than average for this group of plegadis waders.
Behaviors: It was associating with a flock of White-faced Ibis and feeding at the shore-line of Pastorius Reservoir.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced is very similar and the common plegadis wader in SW Colorado. Critical differences in the Spring are lack of white feathering surrounding the facial skin, bluish facial skin edged top and bottom with lighter bluish lines, dark (not reddish) iris, warmer and browner bill coloring, and dark legs with reddish "knees."

Resources Used

None were used during sightings. Susan and I are in the habit of carefully scanning all plegadis flocks - particularly in the Spring - for possible Glossys. We have shared ID points for Glossy from several sources.

Previous Experience

Glossy is the common ibis in the east where I birded for many years and White-faced is the common ibis in SW Colorado where I have now birded for eight years.
