Description of the Bird

Description: A Plegadis ibis, with a mostly deep rich chestnut body and highly iridescent wings. The bird was feeding in a flock of 77 WF Ibis (yes, counted) a large percentage of which were immatures. Per recommendation of Mark Peterson, I scan such browsing ibis by looking at leg color. I found a bird with entirely dusky grayish legs. There was no pinkish hues, even at the "knee" joint. I next looked at the facial skin, which was leaden gray, with no purple or pinkish hues. Along the top border of the facial skin was a whitish line of skin (with pale blue tinge) starting just above eye and going to bill. Along the bottom border of the facial skin, a similarly colored thinner (but longer line) descended from bottom edge of eye to bottom edge of bill. These lines did not connect behind the eye. The eye was a very dark brown, and no red could be seen even in bright light. Bill color not noted (I've seen quite a bit of variability in bill color of WF Ibis that does not seem age related. I am not sure if this is due to lighting, substrate being fed on...; in any case, I have started to ignore - right or wrong - Plegadis bill color.) The bird had a chestnut-colored head and neck (perhaps with some green on crown), but no streaking, so it was it should've been an adult in alternate plumage
Behaviors: feeding in short-grass wet field with WF Ibis
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

WF Ibis eliminated by eye and leg color, color of facial skin and its borders, lack of white feathering around facial skin.
