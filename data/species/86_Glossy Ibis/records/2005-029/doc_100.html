Description of the Bird

A large, richly chestnut colored wading bird with iridescent wing feathers and a long grayish downward-curved bill. A bit larger than most of the two dozen or so White faced Ibis present. Clearly a Plegadis Ibis, this individual stood out primarily because of its dark eye, bluish white facial border that did not wrap behind the eye, and its dark bluish gray facial skin. Additionally, the legs were darker overall than the nearby White faced Ibis, typically appearing muted pinkish gray but brightening or darkening depending on the angle of the light.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding in a flooded meadow with two dozen or so other Plegadis Ibis. One of the other birds appeared to be a hybrid Glossy x White faced ibis, but the rest were typical White faced Ibis.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was separated from White faced Ibis and hybrid Glossy x White faced Ibis by its strong characters of dark eye color, dark bluish gray facial skin color, and pattern of a thin bluish white facial border that didnt wrap behind the eye. Secondary field marks included the overall darker legs and slightly larger size. There was a hybrid Glossy x White faced Ibis present, occasionally in the same field of view, for additional comparison.
