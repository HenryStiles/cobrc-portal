Description of the Bird

Not much to add to that which can be seen in photographs; Both birds were picked out from the 70+ Plegadis ibis wandering about by their darker and duller legs. I then looked at their heads, which lacked streaking, and appeared entirely rich chestnut in one (though photo shows small amount of green), and slightly capped in green in the other. The first bird was larger than the second, though the two did not particularly associate with each other. I next focused on the face. Both had dull leaden colored facial skin. On both birds the eye was a very dark brown, lacking any red or pink color, even in bright light. Both birds had a pale stripe of skin bordering the facial skin superiorly and inferiorly. In bird one, this line was sharper and had a hint of sky-blue color. On the second bird, the the line was more diffuse, and more white-gray... but no hint of pinkish hues. Both birds had legs that were darker and duller than the adult WFIB present, with a slight suggestion of pink or purple hues at "knee" joint. For other marks, see photos
