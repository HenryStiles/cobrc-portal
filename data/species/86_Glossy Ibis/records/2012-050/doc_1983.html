Description of the Bird

Larry Arnold and I, around 5:15 pm, were looking at a flock of nearly 100 White-faced Ibis. The lighting was stunning, really making the most of these birds beauty. The Glossy lifted its head, and its leaden facial skin bordered above and below - on the skin - with bright powder blue lines was readily obvious, and quite different from the surrounding birds. The pale blue lines did not extend beyond the eye, and there was absolutely no white feathering. The eye was dark dull brown, unlike the easily seen cherry red eyes of the surrounding WFIB. The bird, alas, was not visible below the neck.
Behaviors: Roosting with WFIB
Call: not heard
Plumage: alternate
