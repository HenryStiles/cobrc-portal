Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 77mm scope and 32x WW lens.
Distance:  closest was about 90 feet
Light:  Fair. Very overcast. I had to get close before I could see the color of the lores. Before I suspected that at least one bird was a Glossy Ibis, based on the narrowness of the lore and the comparison of it to the flock of White-faced Ibis.

Description of the Bird

This is a reddish bronze ibis with dark eye and dull but glittering bronze green wings. The outstanding feature was the very narrow bluish lores, two parallel lines come above and below the dark eye (to about the center of the eye) but do not completely extend behind and surround the eye. The bill was horn color, long and down curved. The upper leg color dark gray and the knees did not have any rufous color on them. The lower legs below the knees and feet were in the water. The tail was short and bronzy shinning greenish.
Behaviors: Resting and foraging with bills down in shallow water near a known point for loafing migrants. Also seen in flight, where they circled many times before landing again.
Call: nasal soft flight calls given by whole flock including White-faced Ibis while circling reservoir
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Young White-faced Ibis can have a dark eye and indistinct lores. The one bird however was an adult with smart bluish lores and dark eye. The second bird, is more problematical and will be written up separately as it was not in alternate plumage and may have some characters of White-faced Ibis in its facial features.
