Description of the Bird

Large wading bird about 2' tall with long dark legs and long down-curved, sickle shaped, & grayish brown bill. Legs were partially obscured by reeds, but they looked dark without any reddish colors on them.

Eye was dark and reflected no reddish colors. Two bluish white lines, one above and one below the eye, ran from the bill to the eye but did not meet behind the eye. Neck was long, thin, and dark.

Body was uniformly dark brown with frosted greenish gloss, sometimes obvious depending on light especially on the wing coverts.
Behaviors: Bird stood still or slowly foraged amidst tall reeds with White-faced Ibis
Call: None
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis & hybrids usually show some red in and around the eye and on the legs. In adult White-faced, white lines circumvent the rear of the eye unlike Glossy where lines never meet behind the eye.
