Description of the Bird

On April 24, 2010 I observed one adult Glossy Ibis with at least two hybrid Glossy x White-faced Ibis and ~ 100 White-faced Ibis in a grassy meadow on the north shore of Union Reservoir in Weld County, Colorado. The birds had been first found by Bill Prather the previous day.

The pure Glossy Ibis had the characteristic blue facial skin boarder, had a dark brown iris, and it lacked any white feathering on the face. The blue facial skin did not wrap around the back of the eye. The legs were not particularly bright, but had noticeable pinkish knees. The bill was an even pale gray. Overall, there was not much of a difference in plumage coloration between the Glossy and adjacent White-faced Ibis.
Behaviors: The bird fed actively during most of the 1 hour of observation. The flock took off two or three times because of passing traffic, but generally they were rather comfortable and allowed prolonged study. When flushed, they would quickly settle back down, one even landing in the middle of the road. The behavior of the Glossy Ibis was no different from any of the White-faced Ibis. No antagonistic behavior was noted.
Call: none
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis is similar. Hybrids are common between the two species, and the main ID conundrum is separating out those individuals. Two hybrids were present in the flock, offering a wonderful comparison. The ID issue has been under scrutiny for decades, and the ID features used are commonly cited by many field birders. The ID issue was treated in an issue of Colorado Birds by Tony Leukering sometime in the last year.

Resources Used

A Sibley Guide was probably consulted at some point after the observation, but I felt comfortable with the ID of all the Ibis at the time of the observation.

Previous Experience

Extensive experience. Glossy Ibis is a common bird in Rhode Island where I studied them for years. I have studied hybrids in TX. Having lived in the West for many years, I have had the opportunity to study thousands of White-faced Ibis. This is a group that I feel very confident with.
