Description of the Bird

I came across a small group of ibis in a wet grassy marsh at a location called Spanish Village (see below). There were 2 ad WF Ibis, 5 1st-year birds, and the Glossy. The Glossy stood out initially because of its face pattern, which was easy to see: leaden gray facial skin bordered (on skin, not by feathers), with narrow sky-blue lines above and below. The line above the eye was a bit thicker, and shorter as it traveled from the front-top of the eye forward to the base of the culmen. The lower line was thinner but distinct, dropping diagonally from the lower anterior edge of the eye to the lower base of the bill. The eye was dark, without any evidence of red, which was easily seen in all 7 WFIB. The bill was dusky gray as were the legs, with no evidence of pink tinges. There was no streaking on the head. Otherwise, the bird's body and neck plumage, shape and size were similar (though not necessarily identical to) the nearby adult WF Ibises.
Behaviors: Probing in wet grassy marsh
Call: none
Plumage: Adult Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

WFIB or hybrid should show some pale feathering around face, red in eye, pink hues to face.
