Description of the Bird

A Plegadis ibis with 47 other ibis, the bird stood out by having quite thick blue borders above and below the bare facial skin, which was quite dark blue. These borders did not extend behind the eye and did not meet in front of the eye. The bill was an odd brownish color. The legs were nearly entirely dark, with just a bit of pinkish-red at the tibio-tarsal joint.

The plumage was all dark, being a deep chestnut on the body with the darkest such being on the head. The wings were entirely iridescent, variably metallic green and purple, including the tertials, which did not contrast with the rest of the wing, particularly the greater coverts.
Behaviors: foraging in flood-irrigated alfalfa field
Call: none heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis is ruled out by the entire suite of characters that separates the two species: dark blue facial skin with paler blue borders above and below, dark legs (not red), tertials concolorous with greater coverts, dark eye (not red).

A hybrid is eliminated from consideration due to the same features, none of which were in any way intermediate. In fact, there were two hybrids in this flock (unlike the flock that contained the previous GLIB of the day, with was otherwise all White-faced) that sported various intermediate features.
