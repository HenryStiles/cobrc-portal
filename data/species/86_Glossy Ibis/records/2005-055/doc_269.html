Description of the Bird

The bird was clearly an ibis, a dark-plumaged, long-legged wader with a long decurved bill. It was associating with four very similar White-faced Ibises. I here reproduce the exact text of my field notes with abbreviations written out: "Pale neon blue facial skin--extends across forehead to top of eye--no farther--no facial skin/pale feathering behind eye; eye color dark brown; another blue line from base of bill to bottom of eye; between these lines and out to a vertical line with base of bill, facial skin dark cobalt blue. Legs dark gray, same color as bill, except for pinkish wash around the "knees." Direct comparison with White-faced Ibis. Range to bird 60 meters; prolonged scope views. Full sun. 2:15 PM. Bird to the northwest. Photos, distant, by Andy Boyce."
Behaviors: The bird was in a flock with four White-faced Ibis, feeding actively along the southwest shore of the lake, in an area where vegetation (weeds/grass) comes quite close to the shoreline. It appeared to be feeding in the shallow water. Later in our observation the birds took to standing around and preening or sleeping.
Call: none
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis were associating with this bird and direct comparisons were possible. The White-faced Ibis had red eyes, reddish facial skin, variable amounts of white feathering around the reddish facial skin, including behind the eye; and redder legs than the Glossy.

Resources Used

We had no field guides with us.

Previous Experience

This is my fourth time seeing Glossies in direct comparison with White-faced Ibises in Colorado. I see Glossy Ibis more than annually, now.
