Description of the Bird

Large dark bird with very long down-curved bill and long legs. As I was looking through the ibis flock one ibis clearly had thin, blue lines extending from the eye to the upper mandible and to the lower mandible. Upon further inspection of the ibis the eyes were brown, the lores were dark and the legs were gray. The blue lines stopped at the bill and did not extend behind the eye The knees were only slightly reddish.
Behaviors: About 30 ibis were feeding along the edge of a small farm pond probing in the mud. All were White-faced Ibis except for the one I am reporting.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis have an extensive white border around face, red eyes, lores and legs. The Glossy Ibis was feeding the the flock of ibis which made it really easy to see the contrasting field marks. I could see no indication that this ibis was a hybrid.
