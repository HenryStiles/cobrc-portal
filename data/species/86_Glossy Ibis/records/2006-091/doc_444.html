Description of the Bird

Superficially similar to White-faced Ibis in structure, size, and coloration. The Glossy was in a group of about 20 White-faced Ibis, feeding alongside them so comparison of every feature was easily made. Of note, this bird had light blue facial skin that lined the uppermandible across the forehead back to the midsection of the eye from above. A thin light blue border also lined the lower mandible along the gape to the midsection of the eye from beneath. I studied this feature very carefully for signs of white feathering or of any coloration extending beyond the midsection of the eye and did not detect either. The facial skin between the light blue borders and in front of the eye was dark. I noted four other features also consistent with Glossy Ibis. First, the legs were dark gray except for the "knees", which were bright red. Second, the tertials were the same color as the rest of the wings - a green gloss; whereas, in White-faced Ibis the tertials are a bronzy color that contrasts with the greenish gloss of the rest of the wing. Third, the eye was entirely dark, again differing from White-faced Ibis, which I could easily detect reddish eyes on all the individuals I examined. Finally, the bill was dull brown, distinctly different from the gray shown by White-faced ibis.
Behaviors: The bird was actively feeding in a small marshy farm pond with a group of White-faced Ibis.

Plumage: adult alternate
