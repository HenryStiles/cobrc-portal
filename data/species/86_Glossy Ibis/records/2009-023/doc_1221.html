Description of the Bird

This was a dark ibis. Three of us searched a flock of about 30 birds for one with thin blue edges to the facial skin. My nephew, Dan, found one and asked me to look at it. The bird had thin blue facial skin that reached, but did not surround the dark eye. It had grayish legs with reddish joints. Although I looked, I could not see the exact color of the eye. It appeared to be without any reddish tones.
Behaviors: Feed in mud with other dark ibises.
Call: grunting growl
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The only other choice for a dark ibis with thin blue facial skin is a hybird, Glossy x White-faced Ibis. I am not familiar with all the combinations for this hybrid, but the Ramah bird had a dark face, not reddish or violet, what appeared to be a dark eye, not a red eye, and legs that were gray with red "knees" , not reddish legs.

Previous Experience

I have seen a about 20 Glossy Ibis in CO and many in the east, and only a few hybrids that seemed obvioius as such. I have seen two birds that I suspect as hybrids but overall they had mostly features of WFIB.
