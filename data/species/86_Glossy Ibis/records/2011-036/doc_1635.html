Description of the Bird

Birds were about the size of a Snowy Egret and were on the slightly larger than the accompanying White-faced Ibises. Body coloring was dark brown, that appeared slightly richer (more rurous) around the neck and head. Flight feathers were irridescent with tones of bronze and greenish metallic. Bill was long and decurved and brownish-gray. Legs were dark grayish-brown with distinctive reddish taurus joints that were conspicuous, especially when compared by accompanying WFIB. The most distinctive parts of the birds were the powder blue facial skin extending from the bill to the front of the eye which was edged with white. The facial skin ended at the front of the eye and did not extend over, under, or around the eye. The eye appeared black.
Behaviors: One bird was seen at rest with about 250 other plegadis ibises. It was preening while I observed it allowing me to closely observe the bird's head from several angles. Also, since it was at the near edge of the flock the legs were clearly visible.

The second bird was several hundred yards away from the first, and was actively feeding with a group of WFIB. This individual approached to within about 75 feet of me while I observed in through my 32x scope providing me with exceptional views, ost of which were side or head-on.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The possibility of a hybrid WFIBxGLIB was considered and rejected. All field marks were seen well and extremely vivid without any trace of WFIB influence. Facial skin was perfect for Glossy and ended at eye. There was no white feathering at or around eye. Legs were reddish only at the taurus joint. Eyes were completely dark without any reddish hint.

Resources Used

None. I am familiar with both species and look for Glossy Ibises each spring as the flocks pass through Durango. Also, I have seen GLIBxWFIB hybrids in the Durango area and am familiar with the subtle differences.

Previous Experience

As noted above I am very familiar with both species and their hybrids. I purposefully search spring flocks for GLIB and GLIBxWFIB hybrids.
