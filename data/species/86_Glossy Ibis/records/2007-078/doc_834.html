Description of the Bird

Bird was a medium large wader with a long decurved bill. It did not have any white feathering around the facial skin and the facial skin was a dark grayish-blue bordered by a brighter and lighter blue edge both above and below the facial skin at the base of the bill and next to the gape. The iris was dark with absolutely no reddish highlights. The legs were a dark brown except the taurus joints were reddish. The bill was brownish and warmer toned than the WFIBes it was associating with.
Behaviors: It was associating with a large flock of White-faced Ibises that were feeding at the shoreline and into the shallow water. There was also one apparent White-faced x Glossy hybrid that was intermediate in facial skin color and had a glint of reddish in the iris.
Call: not heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced is similar and the common plegadis wader in Colorado. Critical differences of Glossy from White-faced are the absence of any white feathering around facial skin, completely dark iris, absence of any red facial skin which is bluish instead, and reddish taurus joints instead of dark. In addition the bill was brownish and not gray. This bird was not a Glossy x White-faced hybrid because all the field marks were well studied in excellent lightand offered no hint of White-faced genes. In comparison the same flock included one bird that was clearly a hybird. While the facial skin was decidedly bluish without any white feathering, the eye had a touch of reddish to the iris and the taurus joints were as dark as the legs above and below the joint
