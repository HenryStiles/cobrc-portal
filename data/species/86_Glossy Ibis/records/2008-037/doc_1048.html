Description of the Bird

An obvious Plegadis ibis, the bird was with 47 White-faced Ibis. The bird had obvious blue borders above and below the dark blue facial skin; the borders did not extend behind the eye. The bill was grayer than were the bills of the neighboring White-faced Ibis and the legs were mostly dark with just a suggestion of reddish ankles. The tertials were iridescent purplish-green and were concolorous with the greater coverts, unlike the tertials of the neighboring White-faced Ibis.
Behaviors: Foraged in a flooded alfalfa field, probing into the ground.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis ruled out by facial pattern and coloration, tertials coloration, and bill and leg colors. There are no other ABA-area species of similar appearance.
