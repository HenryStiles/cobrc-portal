Description of the Bird

The overall shape, size and color of this bird was similar to the accompanying White-faced Ibis. The Glossy Ibis was slightly larger than the others, standing about 2 ft. tall. This was clearly visible when the birds were side by side.
Bill: Long, thin and decurved. Grayish in color.
Face: Facial skin and lores gray bordered by light blue skin not meeting behind the eye.
Eye: Brown
Legs: Long and gray with reddish ankle joints.
Upper parts: Head, neck and back warm chestnut brown with a glossy iridescence to the lower back.
Under parts: Throat, breast and belly warm chestnut.
Wings: Mostly metallic green/bronze.
Behaviors: All three birds were foraging by probing into the grasses or shallows along the edges of the ponds. Occasionally a car would pass and they would get up and fly to another part of the pond. Flight was with feet and neck extended.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Adult Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White-faced Ibis has a white bordercompletely surrounding a reddish face. The iris is red. The legs are bright red.
