Habitat

On a wooden foot bridge over a dry creek bed with willows and small trees surrounding the bridge. There is a dry hillside to the north of the described location, a road to the south with Bear Lake to the southeast.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 12x50 binoculars
Distance:  20 to 30 ft. Estimated
Light:  The Brambling was frequently in direct sunlight and sometimes in shade. The light was from behind and to the right of the observers. The light only enhanced the quality of the observation.

Description of the Bird

Discovered on 11/19/12 by Mike Henwood. Many photos taken. Gray face and crown extending down the back and side of the head with a black stripe either side of the gray on the back of the head and nape. White eye-ring. Black forehead and malar stripe. Light lores. Bill mostly yellowish. White rump and lower back when flying. Black wings with small white wingbar and large buffy orange wingbar and same color shoulder. The buffy orange extends across the breast, on the throat and chin and on the sides. White belly and undertail coverts. The back was light brown with black markings. Tail mostly black. About House Finch size.
Behaviors: Feeding on the ground with Tree Sparrow, House Finches. Would first land in a tree above the feeding area at Bear Creek Lake Park and then fly down to feed with the other ground feeding birds.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Immature or Basic

Similar Species Discussion

A Stonechat would be similar and both have white rumps but it lacks the buffy or tawny orange breast to the extent it was present on this bird and although it does have white on the wing, it lacks the buffy orange this bird displayed on the shoulder. I am unaware of any records for Stonechat in Colorado but there are 3 prior records of Brambling in Colorado. Lazuli Bunting could be viewed as similar but it doesn't have a white rump, tawny orange on the shoulder, a gray head and nape with black stripes either side of the nape and back of the head and has a thicker bill.
