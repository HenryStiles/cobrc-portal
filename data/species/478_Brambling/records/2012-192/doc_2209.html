Description of the Bird

Orange-dominated long-tailed finch with robust conical bill, similar in size to nearby House Finches. Bill mostly yellow, with gray tip. Heady mostly gray, with black feathering right above bill and dark-tipped gray arriculars, dark tipping thicker towards bill. Creamy white thin eye-arcs meet behind the eye, lores gray. Nape has a gray central stripe with a nearly black stripe bordering it on both sides. Back orangish-gray with scaly black patterning. Breast and throat nearly solid orange, with same orange color blending into scapulars. Belly white, with a tint of orange on undertail coverts. Flanks orange, with dark gray spotting towards tail. Wings boldly patterened with wide orange scapulars, wide black stripe formed by median coverts, followed by another orange band formed by the greater coverts (outer few greater coverts more white-tipped, indicating 1st-winter bird.) Tertials dark with wide orange fringe, other flight feathers dark with whitish fringe. Tail long, indented, and dark with thin pale edging. Legs pinkish-orange. Inner underwing coverts orange, which along with extensive orange on breast and throat allows sexing to male.
Behaviors: Bird was associating with loose flock of mostly House Finches and American Tree Sparrows, coming to seed on wooden bridge along pedestrian trail. In between feeding bouts it stayed hidden in thick willows.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 1st-winter male

Similar Species Discussion

Very few other birds I can think of would show this combination of black and orange. A brief glimpse in heavy cover might suggest Varied Thrush but size and structure along with plumage details completely rules this out. Generally, clear looks reveal an unmistakeable ID.
