Description of the Bird

First off, the Brambling was similarly sized to the American Tree Sparrows that it was feeding with. It had a yellow bill. Its crown was a grayish color and down its neck was the same. Its back was mostly a black/dark gray with some orange mixed in. Its primaries were black. Just above these was orange on what I believe are the greater coverts (I am only mildly versed in bird structure). Just above that was black. Just above that, on the lesser coverts, was orange. It had a white rump that showed prominently when it flew into the surrounding bushes. We all thought that the white rump was a beautiful feature of the bird. Its tail was black on top. I never clearly saw the underside of the tail. Its undertail feathers were white. Its legs were a pinkish gray. Its whole underside up until the chest was white. From the chest up to its chin was an orangish. Now, the face was quite complex to describe. I will try my best. The vague description is: a lot of gray with a little bit of white and black mixed in. The eye was black. Around the eye was a white ring that had a little bit of black in it as best as I could tell. The white eye ring did not look perfectly complete. In front of the eye, was a whitish patch extending to the bill. All over the face was a grayish. Extending behind the eye was a slightly darker gray than the rest of the face. It looked like it had an eyestripe at one time and it happened to be smeared. On the border of the face was a blackish lining.
Behaviors: The Brambling was feeding with Dark-eyed Juncos, House Finches, American Tree Sparrows, Black-capped Chickadees, and a Black-billed Magpie. It would fly in with the House Finches and was similar in actions to all the smaller birds that were feeding. It would not come out of the bushes that often and when it did come out, it would not stay long.
Call: I was not there long enough to notice a call or song. It might have called while I was there but I did not notice it.
Plumage: 1st Winter

Similar Species Discussion

American Tree Sparrows are similar in size. I cannot really think of any birds that are similar in colorations. The orange and black mixed with gray set it off from other species. I didn't have to eliminate much as others had already discussed the identification.

Resources Used

Others identified before me. I did look at my Sibley guide for information.

Previous Experience

I have never seen a Brambling and I have never seen anything that looked like it.
