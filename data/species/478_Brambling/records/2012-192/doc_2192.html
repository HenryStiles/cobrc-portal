Habitat

Relatively open, scrubby habitat. Brambling seemed to be staying in some dense bushes along the edge of a gully that ran through an open part of the park. Large cottonwood trees around edge of lake.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica Ultravid 10x42 binoculars
Distance:  60 feet
Light:  Clear and sunny, but with sun at back at all times. During earlier sightings, sun low and bird was mostly in shadows. During later sighting, seen in good, diffuse light as sun was behind the one cloud that was in the sky.

Description of the Bird

Smallish, sparrow/finch-like bird. About the same size as nearby House Finches (Carpodacus mexicanus) and American Tree Sparrows (Spizella arborea). Mostly gray head and nape, with black flecking/feathering around edge of auriculars and on crown. White partial eye-ring. Throat, breast, and "shoulder" tawny-orange. Mostly tawny-orange wingbar, with otherwise black wing. White belly. Back with black feathers edged extensively with tawny orange. Pale tawny color on sides with some black markings. Mostly black tail, with white rump (seen only briefly in flight). Mostly yellow bill.
Behaviors: Seen briefly 4 times over the course of 4-5 hours. It would come down from the bushes and feed on seed on the ground that had been put out for the Bramblilng (and House Finches and sparrows it was associating with). Very flighty, and would only remain on the ground in the open feeding for about a minute at a time. It would disappear into the nearby bushes for 20 minutes to an hour at a time.
Call: Not heard.
Plumage: First-fall (basic plumage)

Similar Species Discussion

Very few (if any) confusing species to be eliminated. Only Fringillid species possible with tawny-orange breast and shoulders, with bold wing bar and black wings. Other species with red, or some variation thereof (red-orange, rosy-red, or pink). No other sparrow with unmarked tawny-orange throat, breast, and shoulders, with black wings and strong orange wingbar. Other species may have some plumage similarities, but the combination of conical bill and unmarked tawny-orange breast rules out any of these.
