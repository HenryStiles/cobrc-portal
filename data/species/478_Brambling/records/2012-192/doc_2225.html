Description of the Bird

Bill stout with yellow base out 3/4 to gray tip on maxillae and on mandible. Small patch of black feathering just behind bill base. Face dull light brown with pale dingy gray cheeks. Broken light eyering. Pale area in front of dark eye. Throat and breast orange. Wings dark with orange contrasty panel. Nape dark. Back brown with light orange feathers. White wingbar and white flag on black part of wing. Coverts seem to have two ages of feathers which points to 1st year bird. Belly creamy white as is undertail but undertail has faint orange tones too. Tail longish. Other subtle colors best seen by viewing photos.
Behaviors: Skulker but did come into open to feed on seeds scattered on the ground. For sure ate black oil sunflower seeds. Retreated when frightened into dense coyote willows.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

None really, but Common Chaffinch had to be eliminated but it lacks all of the orange tones which are replaced with rosy brown tones on the face and breast.
