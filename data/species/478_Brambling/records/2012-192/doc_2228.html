Habitat

The bird was first seen in short grass prairie about 75 yards from the lake shore. It then flew to a brushy area about 250 yards to the northwest. The brushy area consisted of mostly small willows along a shallow ravine with a few small trees(box elder).

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Vortex Stokes DLS 8 x 42
Distance:  15- 20 yards to the footbridge from the observation point.
Light:  Clear to partly cloudy with good light. In the afternoon, the sun was behind us as we observed the bird feeding on the footbridge and the light was excellent.

Description of the Bird

Head - sometimes tufted appearance with peak or crest at the rear. Gray head with auricular region outlined in faint black. Narrow black bar just above bill with rest of forehead gray. Finch like bill light yellow with dusky tip. Dark eye on gray cheek. Back of head and down neck two jagged parallel black stripes. Mantle was brown with black edgings on feathers. Underparts were orange/rust with a white belly. The rust was more intense on the shoulders than the breast. Had a few light, black spots on the flanks. Yellowish undertail coverts with notched tail. The wing coverts(as seen in the photos) show orange upper scapulars with a black wing bar (lighter on the outer coverts), then an orange bar with the outer coverts showing some white with fresh orange color on the inners.
Behaviors: When I first observed the bird, it was in short grass feeding with some American Tree Sparrows. All I could see was the head, and at first I thought it was a Harris's Sparrow (which had been seen a couple of days earlier in the area), but then I realized the head was gray rather than brown like a winter Harris's Sparrow. As I watched the bird, some of the birds feeding on the ground flew up into a small tree, and when the Brambling flew I could see the orange or rust on the breast and sides. The bird landed on a limb 10-15 up in the small tree along with some of the American Tree Sparrows. Before I could get a definitive look, the birds flew to the northwest.
Call: did not hear bird vocalize
Plumage: first year male

Similar Species Discussion

When I saw the bird, I was not able to identify it. After looking at the photos, Cynthia Madsen and I determined it was a Brambling and probably a female or first year bird.
