Description of the Bird

Finch-type bird a bit larger and huskier than a House Finch. Large mainly pale yellow bill with black feathering between the eyes over the bill; white eye ring which might have been broken at the top; black and gray streaking on head; buffy face; orange breast and white belly; black wings with bright orange wing patches/bars; short white line on primaries orthogonal (I’ve always wanted to use the word “orthogonal” in a bird write-up) to the wing bars; black tail.
Behaviors: Feeding on scattered seed on the ground with House Finches and American Tree Sparrows. Would remove to nearby willow shrubs between feedings.
Call: NA
Plumage: basic "fall male" by Nat Geo 2
