Description of the Bird

Single bird feeding on the ground with Cassin's finches and white-crowned sparrows (millet and sunflower seeds put out by homeowner). Found by homeowner (Laura Stump). Photographs taken from kitchen window, approximately 50 feet from bird. I did not go outside for fear of spooking the bird, so did not hear any calls that it might have made. From descriptions online and in Sibley, it appeared to be an immature female. Seen at approximately 7:20am first day and similar times the next two days. Seen for a fair amount of time the first and second morning, only briefly on final day when it was also seen by fellow birder Donna Stumpp. Not seen later in the day, only mornings for three consecutive days. Has not been seen at this location since April 19th around 7am.

Similar Species Discussion

Unique plumage. Longspurs considered but eliminated due to orange/rust on breast and wings. Very obviously not towhee or anything more common. Bird looks identical to brambling pictures online and in Sibley bird book.

Previous Experience

I am a longtime birder but have had no experience with a brambling. When I first saw it, I knew it was something entirely different than I'd ever had in my yard, so I immediately began photographing it.
