Habitat

riparian. small creek that widens to a tiny pond. lots of Russian Olive, Cottonwood, Tamarask and dead branches. The bird landed on a dead branch.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon D7500. Sigma 150-600 lens, fully extended
Distance:  maybe 35 yards
Light:  bright light

Description of the Bird

 Bird was silent.  no obvious behavior.  not eating, or apparently hunting for food, although there were probably 25 American Robins,  and many Cedar Waxwings whose major intent was to get a drink of water and eat Russian Olives.  Was he doing the same thing?  I didn't see him do either.  Only one bird.  Juvie.  Was ID's by folks at What's this bird when I submitted pictures to their FB page.  It is a life bird for me.  I, Jill Smith, was with a friend, Dottie Russell, who also saw the bird but didnt get pictures, nor could she ID it.  

Similar Species Discussion

Humm... I originally when looking for Evening Grosbeaks, so they were on my mind.  Thought it might be a juvie, as I've never seen a juvie Evening. Eliminated it primarily due to the bill.  Also considered odd (for my area) Finch Species.  (Brown capped or Gray-crowned) due to the stripe being to show on the head and bill shape was not far off.  Eliminated simply because in checking Sibley's  it just wasn't a good plummage match.  Left it as a mystery until I could upload and submit it to What's this Bird? on FB (ABA hosted)
