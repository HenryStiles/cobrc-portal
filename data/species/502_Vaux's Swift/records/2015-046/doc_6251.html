Description of the Bird

I was birding alone along the Arkansas River, below Pueblo Reservoir dam.  I was walking west, from Cottonwood Picnic Area, along the south side of the Arkansas River, when I noticed a swift flying around with swallows (many Violet-green Swallows, among others).  The flight pattern of this swift, seemed off for Chimney Swift.  The bird had a very white throat, and paler rump, and short tail.  The wing span seemed shorter than nearby Violet-green Swallow.  The bird didn't make any noise, like Chimney Swifts usuallly do.  See photos.  They show the white throat, paler rump, short tail, short wings.  

Similar Species Discussion

Chimney Swift -- darker throat, darker rump, longer tail, different flight pattern.

Resources Used

I looked at my Sibley Guide later, and confirmed that the bird looked very much like a Vaux's Swift.  I have seen Vaux's Swifts in Washington State, though don't know them very well.  I know Chimney Swifts, since they breed in Pueblo and SE Colorado, where I mostly bird.  I felt like the swift wasn't a Chimney Swift (since it was flying differently), though I wanted people to look at the photos to make sure.  

Chris Wood's comments: "Very cool. Don’t see anything that looks particularly unlike Vaux’s here. Paler underparts are good. Pale contrasing rump and even slightly paper tail than uppers good. Wing shape probably impossible to judge from photos, but looks good."
