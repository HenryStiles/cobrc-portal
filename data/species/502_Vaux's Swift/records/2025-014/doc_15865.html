Description of the Bird

Three of us noted the swift simultaniously as it passed very close to us and continued to do so for the next several minutes. Tyler and Brenda both had cameras and over 200 photos were taken over the next several minutes. The swift was small with quick wing beats. Size was judged by the nearby swallows which were also at close range. Swift was grayish overall with off white breast, white throat and light brown rump. Bill appeared to be quite small. From photos wings looked short but tail long. No calls were heard. Records indicate, that if accepted, this would be the fifth record for Colorado and the 2nd record for Montezuma County.

Brenda Wright, Tyler Lausten, and Coen Dexter were searching through the very large flock of swallows when the swift suddenly appeared about three feet above the ground and at times within 10 feet or nearer to us. The swift remained in the area for the next several minutes and kept returing to our position near waters edge. 

Similar Species Discussion

Chimney Swift are very similar but do not have a white throat and a pale rump. The bill of our swift was very small as noted in the photos. White-throated Swifts and Black Swifts are much larger than swallows. Our swift was smaller than the swallows.

Previous Experience

We have had many extended trips to the west coast, Mexico in winter, Costa Rico, and Panama. We have studied swifts for years in our travels so Brenda and my experience runs deep in paying close attention to details when studying swifts.

Chimney Swift is always a likey contender. We have seen several in western Colorado they pass through our area and likely nested in Palisade in Mesa County in two locations in the past. We have photos of the Chimney Swifts at Totten Reservoir as well. 

We did report a Vaux's Swift in Montrose County but it failed the Colorado Bird Records Committee as we did not have photos.
