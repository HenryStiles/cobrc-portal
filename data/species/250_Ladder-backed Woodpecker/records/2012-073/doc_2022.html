Description of the Bird

I heard the rattle call of the LBWO a couple times before it registered, then it called a couple more times, overcoming my initial disbelief that it could be possible. I continued listening, and heard the rattle once or twice more, and it sounded perfect for LBWO. So, I pulled out my I-Phone and played the rattle call at the bird. It popped up onto a dead snag about 100 feet away and called again. I could see a woodpecker that seemed between Downy and Hairy in size with a back/wings that had black and white bars entirely across the dorsum of the bird from where wings crossed near base of tail to uppermost back, with no unbroken area in striping. The crown was black or blackish down to nape. The bird had a white supercilium, and dark auriculars. That was about all I could see. The bird called once more, the tape "repsonded" and the woodpecker flew away from me and downwards, back into the woods and into cover
Behaviors: See above
Call: Repeatedly gave the "rattle call" which consists of a couple harsh/squeaky notes, then perhaps ten notes which crescendo downwards then level out at end. Bird called approximately 8-10 times.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

The call of this bird is unique. It resembles that of Downy Woodpecker, but is harsher and does not whinny downwards as much. The completely barred back and wings eliminates all other CO woodpeckers, except RB Woodpecker, which lacks this bird's facial pattern.
