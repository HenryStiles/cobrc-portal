Description of the Bird

This is to document a Colorado Least Sandpiper from December 2006.

Brown-backed, brown-headed peep, with whitish belly and chin, dark thin bill with slightly drooped tip, and yellowish legs.

Breast with distinct brownish band, dark centers to scapulars, little or no primary projection, small head, short tail.
Behaviors: Fed exclusively amongst Arkansas River rocks where there were a quite noticeable plethera of insects. The two times that I saw this bird, it foragged with one and then two Killdeer, seeminly using them as its sentinel.

Often fed with crouched posture, seemingly looking up all of the time. Fed exclusively among rocks quite close to shore, walking amongst and disappearing among the wet beach cobbles where the most abundance of insects seemed to be.
Call: none heard
Plumage: nonbreeding
