Description of the Bird

A very small peep, estimated at half the length of a nearby killdeer, which would place it at approximately 5-5.5 inches in length; brownish on upperparts, somewhat mottled appearance to upperparts; brownish wash extending across upper breast and throat; remaining underparts white; head without salient features, bill slightly decurved, about the length of the head (perhaps 1 inch); legs thin and short, not obviously dark, but actual color difficult to discern.
Behaviors: Foraging in shallow water and on mud
Call: none
Plumage: Non-breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Non-breeding plumage of Semipalmated and Western sandpipers, and Eurasian stints, are grayer with less markings on chest. Wings flush with tail rules out Baird's and White-rumped Sandpipers. Smaller than Dunlin or Pectoral Sandpipers.
