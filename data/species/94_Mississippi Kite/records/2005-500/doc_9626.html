Description of the Bird

We kept seeing a strange hawk fly around the nieghborhood for several days. It was small with a buoyant flight. We never could get close enough to get an I. D. On June 5 we were standing in the yard saying goodbye to company (fellow birdwatchers) from California when Ellene pointed up and said "What is that?". We looked up to see the Kite. Brenda ran in a grabbed the camera. We had very good looks as the bird circled higher and higher. The hawk was meduim sized with a white head and gray body. It had long pointed wings with short outermost primaries. It looked falconlike with a slow buoyant flight. The belly was gray and the tail had a couple of rows of spots. A first summer Mississippi Kite was seen in June of 2004 in Norwood 20 miles to our southeast. This bird is a first summer also so this is a different bird that was seen in Norwood. We did not hear any calls from the bird. Some brown in the wing and white rows of dots in the tail suggested the kite was a first summer bird.

Similar Species Discussion

We don't have any Kites in our area. The bird was smaller then a Red-tailed Hawk which is our most abundant hawk. Cooper's and Sharp-shinned hawks do not have solid gray breasts. Tail was not banded (only a few rows of spots a close range) and shorter than one would expect to an accipiter.
Snail kites are larger with a notched tail. White-tailed Kites (wouldn't that be great) have a white tail and underparts. Other kites are not expected.
