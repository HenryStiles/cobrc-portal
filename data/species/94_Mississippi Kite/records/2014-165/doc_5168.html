Description of the Bird

I'm not going to worry about grammatical correctness in this case. The only reason I'm entering this is because I noted that it was a Weld review species and has not been entered into the official records on the CBRC website for Weld at all. This description is for at least one of the birds. Unfortunately, that is all I have photos for. However, the others were noted to be the same shape and size as the bird described and they all had banded tails. All of the birds were associating with each other to some extent. I only saw the birds from underneath. Size was roughly that of American Crow. Body color (the part of the body I could see) was uniformly a light gray. Had a small, dark, hooked bill. Relatively short head. Overall wing shape was fairly pointed (more so than Red-tailed Hawk, but not as much as Forster's Tern for example). Triangle shaped tail with rounded end. Tail was dark-brown/black with white/gray banding. At most, three light bands were noted.

Similar Species Discussion

Sharp-shinned Hawk and Cooper's Hawk were considered because of the banded tails and relative sizes. They were eliminated due to the pointed wings (both Sharp-shinned and Cooper's have distinctly more rounded wings) and body coloration/pattern (these had unstreaked gray bodies while Sharp-shinned and Cooper's have barred red ventral sides as adults and brown streaked ventral sides as young birds).

Previous Experience

Some experience. I've seen these birds in Colorado, but not like they can be seen elsewhere in the southeastern US. I have seen a number of Sharp-shinned and Cooper's.
