Description of the Bird

Small to medium size raptor. George Steele called us and said he had a Mississippe Kite in his yard. Of course, I said "no way". Upon arriving at his house a hawk was seen perched in a tree in his back yard. Immediately, I knew it was a kite. The bird was very light gray, lighter around the head than the rest of the body. The bill was small and the reddish eye was surrounded by a black mask.  A few white spots on the back and folded wings and a couple of rows of white spots on the tail which suggest the kite was a first summer bird. We never saw the bird fly but it was reported flying around Norwood catching grasshoppers for the next few days and eating them on the wing.

Similar Species Discussion

Swallow-tailed Kite is larger, two toned white and gray. White-tailed Kite have a black wing patch which is visible when perched. Sharp-shinned and Cooper's hawks, our default hawks, are well known and their chest patterns are reddish in adults and streaked in young birds. The kite had a plain gray chest.

Resources Used

We looked at several field guides while observing the kite. The four of us realized the bird was not expected in our area so we left no stones unturned.

Previous Experience

Brenda and I are well traveled and have seen all kites in the Americas. In fact Brenda has photographed many kites.
