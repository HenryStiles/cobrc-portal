Description of the Bird

Basic size and shape that of Mallard. Body quite dark brown, contrasting sharply with paler tan head and neck. Dark brown eyeline and crown. Tail brown. Undertail coverts dark brown, without sign of being paler as is often case in Mexi x Mallard hybrids. No upcurled tail feathers. Bill olive with some yellow tinge. Internal markings on scaps and upper tail coverts. Narrow white border to blue-ish speculum
Behavior: Loafing, then swimming off, in company of Mallards
Call: Not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Mallard should show paler vent, paler body, not as contrasting head pattern, broader white edges to speculum. If male hybrid, curled tail feather often (usually?) present as would solid dark patch on uppertail coverts. Mottled Duck would have no anterior white border to speculum and narrower posterior white border, less distinct eyeline and crown.
