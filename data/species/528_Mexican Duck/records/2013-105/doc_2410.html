Description of the Bird

A Mallard like duck, the was darker overall, with all yellow bill. See photos. The bird wasn't dark enough for American Black Duck. The lack of dark gap spot on the bill, elimnates Motted Duck. This bill looked pretty typical for a Mexican Duck.
Behaviors: Seen swimming on the pond in the middle of Pueblo City Park.
Call: none
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Not dark enough for American Black Duck. Lack of black gap spot, elimnates Mottled Duck. Not a female Mallard, since the bird had an all yellow bill. There was no hint of green in the head, so it didn't appear to be a Mallard x Mexican Duck hybrid.

Resources Used

I looked at Sibley Guide, to see the difference from Mexican Duck, Mottled Duck, and American Black Duck. I knew the bird wasn't an American Black Duck, though wasn't totally sure about Mottled Duck.

Previous Experience

I've seen a couple of Mexican Ducks in Colorado, including one at Pueblo City Park during early 2012. I've also seen Mexican Ducks in Arizona and west Texas. I've seen Mottled Ducks in Florida and Texas, and American Black Ducks in Colorado in 1990s, also in New England in recent years.
