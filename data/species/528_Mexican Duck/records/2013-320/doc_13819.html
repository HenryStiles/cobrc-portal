Description of the Bird

I was organizing photos and came across the ones submitted with this report that appear to show a male Mexican Duck with a male and female Mallard. These photos were taken during the Pueblo Reservoir CBC prior to Mexican Duck being elevated to species status in 2018.  This description is based entirely on the photos.  Dark-bodied duck similar in size and structure as the accompanying Mallards - with general plumage characteristics more similar to the female than the male. The head and neck were pale and sharply contrasting with the dark body.  The bill was greenish-yellow.  Uppertail coverts lacked any indication of a curl.

Similar Species Discussion

Mottled Duck: lack of a black spot at gape; also this bird's face appears to have streaking similar to the female Mallard while Mottled Duck lacks facial streaking.

American Black Duck: the bird doesn't appear to be dark enough for that species.

Previous Experience

Very limited, perhaps just a few pure and hybrid individuals total.  Also limited experience with Mottled Duck and American Black Duck.
