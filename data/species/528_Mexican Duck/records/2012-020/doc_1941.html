Description of the Bird

Very similar to the bird seen at same location in May 2011. Size and shape similar to that of male Mallard. This bird is at the dark end of the Mexican Duck spectrum. As a whole, its body appeared blackish with a chestnut hue that was caused by the blackish sides and chest being edged in chestnut. Chest color no different from that of sides in most views, slightly darker in some views, though subtly so. Scapulars blackish with narrow chestnut fringing. Back feathers black with very narrow dull gray edging. Tertials showed subtle silvery stripe. Tail from behind looked dark with narrow whitish edging on outermost rectrix or two. Speculum purple-blue with narrow white borders, particularly anterior. Neck and head were grayish and finely streaked, with a sharp line of demarcation between neck and chest. Blackish crown and eyeline. Legs red. Bill bright yellow with hint of olive. Like the bird in Firestone last spring, this bird is darker than the bird in Pueblo.
Behaviors: Lounging with a group of Mallards, not apparently paired with any female
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

American Black Duck would be darker yet and lack rufous edgings. Mottled duck would not have had streaking on cheek as this bird did. Mexican Duck x Mallard intergrade should show some features of Mallard, which this bird did not (tail/uppertail feathers not curved upwards, either... left out of main description)
