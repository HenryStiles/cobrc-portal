Description of the Bird

Pair. Male substantially darker than female Mallard. Size of male. Chest a bit browner than remainder of underparts. Underparts dark through vent. Chest contrasted markedly with paler neck and head (which had fine streaks, unlike MODU). Crown and eyeline blackish. No curled tail feathers. Tail dusky with narrow edge of white, typical of MEDU (eg, Pueblo bird from a couple winters back). Uppertail coverts mottled (typically solid dark - actually iridescent green - in hybrid males). Bill solid, bright, yellow with a hint of olive-orange. No black markings on bill except nail.
Female distinctly darker than nearby female Mallards. Shapr contrast between chest and neck/head in darkness. Head markings darker than that of Mallard. Tail brown, edged with a bit of white, seen only when viewed from side. Bill orange with black speckling on saddle. 

Similar Species Discussion

Mallard x Mexican hybrid. Male lack any characters that would point to Mallard, including some of the more subtle ones involving undertail covert darkness, uppertail covert pattern, and curl on tail feathers. Similarly, female is darker than Mallard with appropriately brown tail and chest contrast with neck/head. Well within range of Mexican Ducks in AZ and nothing to indicate Mallard heritage
