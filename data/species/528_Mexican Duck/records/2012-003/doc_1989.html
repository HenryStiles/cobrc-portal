Description of the Bird

A dark-plumaged Mallard-type duck, with a bright orangish-yellow bill with a black nail and bright orange legs. The bird was darker-plumaged than all other Mallards present, lacked any curl to the upper tail coverts, and had a dark tail with paler fringes and internal markings.
Behaviors: Wandering around with the tame ducks, getting fed handouts
Call: none heard
Plumage: definitive basic maybe

Similar Species Discussion

Male Mallards have white tails, curly upper tail coverts, and very different plumage; female Mallards have black saddle on orange bill and tails with extensive white; Mottled Duck has black gape spot lacking on this bird; American Black Duck has even darker plumage, less pale in rectrices, and purple speculum, unlike the blue speculum of this bird and with males having greenish bills and females dull yellow ones.
