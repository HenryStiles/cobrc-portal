Description of the Bird

Duck somewhat resembling female mallard. Body darker than that of mallard, mottled black and rich brown. Chest slightly darker and vent slightly paler than remainder of underparts. Head and neck quite contrastingly paler, being a pale tan-like color with fine streaking throughout except on chin, which was slightly peachy in color. Crown blackish. Blackish eyeline. Tail gray. No curled tail feathers. Spread underwing like that of Mallard, without dusky markings of Mottled Duck or Am Black Duck. Speculum iridescent, usually appearing bluer than more purple speculum of Mallard. Narrower white borders, anteriorly and posteriorly, than that of surrounding Mallards. Bill entirely yellow-orange, without darker markings. Size of male Mallard
Behaviors: Feeding with American Wigeon and Mallards at park pond
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Mottled Duck would lack white trailing edge of speculum, would have some dusky markings on underwing, different bill color. Am Black Duck darker, no white borders to speculum, different colored bill. Mallard x Am Black Duck should have more purple speculum, some green on head, some dark markings on underwing coverts. Mallard x Mexican Duck should have broader edges to speculum, some green on head, some white on tail. Per Pyle (see resources), slightly darker/richer chest is consistent with Mexican Duck. Sources below also mention slightly paler vent. Darkness of body typical of that of Mexican Duck, inconsistent with that of hybrid, which one would expect to be paler.
