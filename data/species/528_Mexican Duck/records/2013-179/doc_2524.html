Description of the Bird

General appearance like that of a female Mallard with a rather dark body contrasting sharply with paler tan-brown head and neck. Crown dark brown as was line through eye, contrasting with pale head. Legs reddish. Tail brown. Blue-ish speculum with relatively narrow white borders. Size equivalent of male Mallard, not female. Bill olive with some yellow hues. No curled tail feathers. Uppertail coverts mottled.
Behavior: Mostly sleeping on island in pond.
Call: Note heard
Plumage: Male

Similar Species Discussion

Mallard or intergrade with Mallard would not be as dark bodied, typically shows less contrast between body and head/neck, usually has less pronounced head markings, should not have totally brown tail, typically has broader borders to speculum. Mottled Duck would be darker and more patterned and would lack borders to speculum.
