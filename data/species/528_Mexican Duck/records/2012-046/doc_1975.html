Description of the Bird

This bird was somewhat similar to a Mexican Duck seen at this location in March, but was not as dark bodied. The "darkness" of its body was more like that of the Pueblo bird than the earlier Firestone bird from this March and last May. Body overall blackish-brown with chestnut hue caused by blackish sides and chest edged in chestnut. Internal markings in feathers noted, ruling out Am Black Duck (or hybrid of that species). Chest color same as that of sides in most views, but subtly darker in some views. Scaps blackish with narrow chestnut fringing. Back feathers black with hint of dull gray edging. Tertials showed subtle silvery stripe. Tail same color as body. Speculum purple-blue with narrow white borders, particularly anterior. Neck and head grayish and finely streaked, with a sharp line of demarcation between neck and chest. Blackish crown and eyeline. Legs bright reddish orange. Bill yellow with hint of olive. Black on nail of bill seemed to extend a bit beyond nail, unlike bird in March.
Behaviors: Standing on vegetation covered island, paired with female dabbler. Scuttled to water, swam out onto pond, then behind island once it noticed us.
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

American Black Duck would be darker, and should not have internal markings in body feathers. Hybrids should show some white in tail, likely different speculum pattern, some green on head (which this bird lacked). Mallard would be paler, have broader white edges to speculum, white on tail, subtly different bill color. Mottled Duck would lack white on speculum, lack streaking on chin/cheek
