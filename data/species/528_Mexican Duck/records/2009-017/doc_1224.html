Description of the Bird

A hen-plumaged, Mallard-sized Anas duck with darker body plumage than nearby hen Mallards (including the one with which it appeared to be paired), a yellow-olive bill with black nail, and orangy-red legs. In flight, we noticed two white borders to the colored wing speculum (which was blue). See photos for plumage details.
Behaviors: Flying and feeding in the company of a female Mallard (which appeared to be of the nominate subspecies), and in the company of other Mallards and Anas ducks. Seen first in flight, later on the water, then while resting in tall grass at border of water, and finally flying away with the female Mallard.
Call: none given
Plumage: Definitive alternate

Similar Species Discussion

By size and plumage, the only other contenders would be Mallard, Black Duck, and Mottled Duck. This bird differed from the female Mallard nearby in both bill color, and overall body plumage. It did not have the tail pattern of an adult male, ruling out a male in "eclipse" plumage. A Black Duck would have a purple speculum with no white border, and Mottled Duck should have only one white border to the speculum. Extralimital ducks are also possible, but most differ from these species in bill color, face pattern, and other details.

Resources Used

I have seen Mexican Duck in AZ, TX, and Mexico, and have much experience with Mottled and Black ducks.
