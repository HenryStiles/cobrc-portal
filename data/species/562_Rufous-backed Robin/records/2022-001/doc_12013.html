Description of the Bird

To find the bird I hiked almost all the way around the pond to where I could hear and see American Robins. It was still quite foggy, and the birds were not up and about yet. In the northeast corner of the riparian area, I spotted the Rufous-backed Robin sitting near the center of a Russian Olive Tree that still had quite a few berries. The bird was all puffed up. Originally found by Coen Dexter and Brenda White, many birders flocked to Cortez to see this bird.

Bird description – the same size and shape as an American Robin, this bird was much more rufous. The back, coverts, and scapulars were orangish red as well as the belly. The tail, primaries, secondaries, and head were cold gray. The throat was a dirty white with fine vertical black streaks that faded into the rufous. This robin had a bicolored yellow and gray bill with black in front of the eye. The auricular patch was slightly darker than the crown and lacked any white around a dark eye.

Before the bird took flight with the rest of the American Robins it was associating with, it did look like it had a ‘hair ball’ that it might be trying to dislodge. Nothing came out. I didn’t hear any vocalizations unfortunately. The bird rustled its feathers and took flight to the NW side of the pond.

Similar Species Discussion

Since this bird is a thrush, I compared it to the close American Robins. It was eliminated due to the lack of white around the eyes, the rufous upper wing and back which a robin has medium dark gray, the bill color being bicolored verses a robins mostly yellow bill. The clean demarcation of a robin’s throat the gradual fading of the Rufous-backed Robin, and the overall muted colors compared to the American Robin.

I eliminated the Hermit, Wood, Swainson’s and other thrushes due to the coloring of the gray and rufous even though it is the same general shape as those species. 

Resources Used

I studied the Rufous-backed Robin before my trip using Sibley and eBird photos. This is my third sighting of this bird in the USA.

Previous Experience

I have seen this species three times total. 11/25/09 at Santa Gertrudis Lane in Arizona, 1/15/21 at Patagonia-Sonoita Creek Preserve in Arizona and this observation. Each time I specifically looking for this species and was prepared for the difference between an American Robin and this Rufous-backed Robin.
