Description of the Bird

The bird was the same shape as the American Robins it was associating with, but a little smaller in overall size and bulk. The head and upper back were gray. Most of the back, wings and belly were rufous.  The primaries were gray and black, as was the tail. The bill and legs were a quite bright yellow. The throat was a white background, but with several dark streaks going to the upper breast. The eye was dark, but there was a reasonable sized red orbital ring that was easily observed. I was not sure whether it was a full adult, or a bird born in 2021, as I'm not sure whether the adult plumage fades a little in winter. I did not hear any distinctive calls.  Maggie and I were birding with the finders of the bird: Brenda Wright and Coen Dexter. 

Similar Species Discussion

American Robin has an all dark back, dark legs and is a little larger.

Eye-browed Thrush does not have any rufous in the wings.

Varied Thrush and Dusky Thrush have quite a lot of black in the plumage, which this bird did not.  

Resources Used

Sibley and National Geographic field guides.

Previous Experience

I have seen this species a few times in west Mexico.  Seen Varied Thrush several times in CO. Never seen Eye-browed Thrush or Dusky Thrush.
