Description of the Bird

Brenda and I found the bird in the AM of 18 January. We have had a lot of experience with this species or we could has just passed it off as another American Robin. We checked ebird records and the three weeks prior to finding the bird, 11 parties birded that area so one can only guess how long the Rufous-backed Robin may have been at Denny Lake.

Rufous back and wings set this species off from American Robins. It is smaller, has a different flight pattern (quick and direct), yellowish bill and orange eyering and lacks the eye arcs of the American Robin. Head has a gray tone. 

Similar Species Discussion

American Robin is a little larger, has eye arcs, has brown wings and back and call often. We eliminated all other thrushs from all the great photos.

Resources Used

Upon first finding the bird Brenda took photos. We then ran back to the truck to look at Sibley's to make sure of the ID before we reported it. Upon returning to the house we downloaded the photos and put the word out. We did check and at that time we did believe it to be a first record for Colorado.

Previous Experience

We have spent a lot of time along the west coast of Mexico (two winters). where is species is fairly common. Also we have seen 4 or 5 Rufous-backed Robins along the Santa Cruz River in Arizona about 4 winters ago.
