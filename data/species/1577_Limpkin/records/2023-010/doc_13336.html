Description of the Bird

A single wading bird feeding at the edge of grass and a muddy stretch in a flooded field.  The grass hid the bird so only the back, neck and head were visible.  The back was a uniform brown with white streaks and splotches.  The long neck was brown and heavily streaked in white. The crown was brown,  The face was light tan with a large brown eye. The long curved bill was very thick. The upper mandible was dark on the top ridge and yellowish below with a black tip. The lower mandible was yellowish with a black tip.

Similar Species Discussion

An immature Black-Crowned Night Heron has similar patterns but is less brown, a yellow eye,  shorter neck and short bill.  An immature Yellow-Crowned Night Heron is closer in color and pattern but has a red eye, shorter neck and a short bill.
