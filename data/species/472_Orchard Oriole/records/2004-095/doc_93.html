Description of the Bird

This small oriole had a black hooded head and upper back. The tail was also black. The lower chest, belly, and undertail coverts were a dark brick red. The wings were black except for a white wingbar, a brick red shoulder patch, and with some white feather edges on the flight feathers. The bird stayed high in a cottonwood tree and flew away from that perch, so I was not able to view the rump. The bill was black and oriole-shaped and the legs were dark. The bird was smaller than the Bullock's Orioles in the same area.
Behaviors: I saw the bird fly into a large cottonwood tree and it looked slim and somewhat smaller than the Bullocks Orioles that nest in this area. It foraged high in the cottonwood for several minutes and chaged perches several times. It flew to a smaller cottonwood and then back high into the first cottonwood.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The smaller size and brick-red coloring of this bird easily distinguishes it from Bullock's and Baltimore.
