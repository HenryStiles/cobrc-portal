Habitat

on fence post of former ranchland / disturbed grassland. Nearby are river w/ cottonwoods, homestead with Russian Olive (flew into RO), and slopes with Pinyon Pines.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Binoculars
Distance:  300 feet
Light:  sunny

Description of the Bird

No calls.  Sitting on fence post. darkbrown/black head, heavy dark bill, white breast and belly, white secondaries, dark brown/black wings and back.  When in flight, white was continuous across secondaries and rump.

Similar Species Discussion

All other medium-sized or larger juvenile woodpeckers and sapsuckers that might be in this area - Lewis', flicker, hairy,re-naped, williamson's.  None had the same pattern of markings.

Resources Used

Have seen juvenile red-heads in other locations, merlin app, Sibley guide, all about birds.org

Previous Experience

Have seen these juveniles  and the adults numerous times in the midwest.  Saw red-headed once at RM Arsenal in Denver.  Have birded Chaffee County extensively in last 3.5 years as resident, so I knew this was unusual here and therefore looked at other juvenile woodpecker options first.
