Habitat

There are a few large trees around, there are mostly Choke Cherry bushes and Mountain Mohagany in the area. There are some old buildings in the area and fenches, some wooden fence posts. There is also a small running stream.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 8x32BN Binoculars and a Swarovski spotting scope
Distance:  Got within 30 feet of the bird.
Light:  I saw it in every angle. At times the lighting was great to see the bird and others the bird was between me and the sun. There were very few clouds around when I saw the bird.

Description of the Bird

Bright red head and throat. Long grayish sharp bill. Black back and tail. White wing patches on secondaries and white rump. White belly and breast.
Behaviors: It was usually sitting vertically on tree branches and trunk. It would never let anyone get lose without flying away. It would occasionally sit in the open vertically on wooden fench posts. When I was digiscoping it, it would fly like it was flycatching up into the air, then come back and land back on the post.
Call: Didn't hear it
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Acorn Woodpecker - Red on top of the head. White and black face. Black breast. Streaked Sides. Black and white on back and wings.
Red-naped and Yellow-bellied Sapsuckers - Red on front of head, and back for Red-naped, and males have red on throat. Black upper breast. Yellowish belly with streaking.
Red-breasted Sapsucker - Has red on breast, throat, and head. Streaking on a yellowish washed belly.
Red-bellied Woodpecker - Red on head and back of neck with a whitish face, female only has red on back of neck. Black and white bars on back.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide to Birds and the National Geographic Guide

Previous Experience

I have seen all the similar species except Red-breasted Sapsucker. I have also seen a lot of Red-headed Woodpeckers.
