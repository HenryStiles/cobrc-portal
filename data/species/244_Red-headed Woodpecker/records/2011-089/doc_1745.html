Description of the Bird

I was in a grass hay field and saw the bird on a wooden fence post along a fence line a long distance away. When I looked through my binocs, I saw it was a Red-headed Woodpecker. Startled, I hurried to get closer for a better look. The bird had a red head, black back, and prominent white wing patches. When it sallied out to fly catch, the red head, black back, and large white patches on the rump/secondaries were prominent.
Behaviors: The bird was on a wooden fence post along a fence line in the middle of a grass hay field. The bird clung to the fence post like a tree, rather than perching on top of the post. The bird would sally out and fly catch, then return to the fence line and land on a post (sometimes the same post, but not always). It slowly made it's way along the fence line.
Call: did not call or sing.
Plumage: adult plumage

Similar Species Discussion

A Red-headed Woodpecker is pretty striking and hard to miss. I didn't really consider any other species.
