Description of the Bird

It was a medium-sized woodpecker with a complete, bright unstreaked red head and a gray bill.

The underparts were bold, clean, unstreaked white.

The back was solid black, except for clean white wing patches.

Its call was somewhat similar to that of a Red-bellied Woodpecker, but not as harsh.
Behaviors: It was clinging to the side of a phone pole, in a manner typical of a woodpecker.
Call: Its call was somewhat similar to that of a Red-bellied Woodpecker, but not as harsh.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

An adult Red-headed Woodpecker is one of the easiest species in North America to identify. A Red-naped Sapsucker would have red on the head, but would not have a full red head or solid black upper back. The large white wing patches on this bird further nail down the identification as a Red-headed Woodpecker.
