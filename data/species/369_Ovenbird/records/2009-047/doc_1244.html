Viewing Conditions

Light:  Sunny and towards my back when first observed. Bird was in a shadowy oak brush. Also observed in almost full sun while I was in shadows when bird was on the ground. Also seen in full shadow under brush when I was in shadow, but facing towards the sun.

Description of the Bird

Small bird (warbler sized) with two dark stripes on the crown of the head with rusty colored stripe in between these two stripes. Pink colored bill. Dark spots on sides of upper breast, rather large in size.
Behaviors: Bird was very skittish/shy. Moved away quickly when spooked by cars/people. Stayed mostly on the ground hopping along. Didn't scratch at leaves on ground like a thrush. Very quite in underbrush unlike a thrush.
Call: Not singing/calling
Plumage: Unknown

Similar Species Discussion

There was a Hermit Thrush seen in the same area at the same time, and was able to get good looks at that bird as well. This bird had significantly more spots, was bigger, and made much more noise while scratching on the ground. I also was able to make a comparison between the bills of the two birds and the Ovenbird's was a very pink bill and the Hermit Thrush's was darker. Also the top of the head of the Hermit Thrush was all one color with no stripes, while the striping on the head of the Ovenbird was very obvious.

Resources Used

I used both Sibley's Guide to birds of the Western United States as well as the National Geographic field guide to Birds of the United States. I used Sibley after we had left the sight of the bird, but did use National Geographic while the bird was in front of me.

Previous Experience

I just returned from a trip to TX in the Lower Rio Grande area where we were able to observe Ovenbirds for long periods of time and with great viewing areas (no low branches to hide birds from view).
