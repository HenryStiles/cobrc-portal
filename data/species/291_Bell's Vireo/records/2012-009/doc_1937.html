Description of the Bird

This was a small skulking bird of dense brushy habitat on Chico Basin Ranch. When I first saw it I thought it was probably a warbler. The bird was yellowish underneath with a dull whitish throat. The face was gray green with what appeared as an upper and lower eye arc but photos show it has a faint spectacle. The bill was gray with a hooked tip as in vireo, not warbler. The wings were gray edged in yellow. There were two whitish wingbars but the upper one was mostly concealed. The upperparts were dull greenish changing to green gray on the head. The tail seemed short for a vireo and the undertail coverts had a light yellow white cast.
Behaviors: Skulked around at head height in a Russian olive thicket. Grabbed a small green caterpillar. Always had parts of its body hidden behind low twigs.
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown - fresh

Similar Species Discussion

Cassin's Vireo - although one of my photographs suggests a CAVI, this bird was smaller and thinner than a CAVI with different behaviors (always skulking). The upperwing bar was faint unlike in CAVI and it did not have the pronounced spectacle of a CAVI. Also the upperparts were a duller green. Bird suggested a warbler at first and not one of the Solitary vireos.
Slightly resembles a White-eyed Vireo but that species has a strong yellow spectacle and a bull necked look in addition to the white eye that would be visible at the distance I observed this bird.

Resources Used

I consulted Big Sibley, Stokes guide with photos, and the Crossley ID Guide. Brian Gibbons thought this bird could be a CAVI based on the photo of the bird from behind but he agreed it was a BEVI when I sent the other photos of the bird from the side.

Previous Experience

I have seen a few, mostly in breeding areas in eastern Colorado, where the plum thickets are structuarally similar to the Chico habitat where this bird was located.
