Description of the Bird

Smaller vireo, yellowish underside, very slight eyeline, white throat--blending into belly, weak white wingbars, greenish upperside, dark eye.
Behaviors: No behaviors seen--bird captured in mist net at Rocky Mountain Bird Observatory's Barr Lake State Park banding station.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Pre-formative

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo has a much more pronounced eyering, is larger overall, has a darker crown, and is duller in overall coloration.

White-eyed Vireo has distinct spectacles.

Resources Used

No resources used for assistance with ID.

Previous Experience

Extensive birding experience throughout Texas, where BEVI is a fairly common summer resident, along with the somewhat similar WEVI.

Extensive bird banding experience in Texas and Mexico with WEVI and WAVI, and in California with WAVI. First BEVI I have personally observed captured at a banding station.
