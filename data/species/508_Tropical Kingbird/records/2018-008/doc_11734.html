Description of the Bird

These are my ebird Checklist comments from Oct. 5: This Kingbird "Lacked high contrast white malar found on Cassin's, but also lacked white tail edges as on Western.  Grey on head appeared paler overall, more consistent with Western.  Forked tail didn't seem consistent with either Western or Cassin's per Sibley's illustrations."

I didn't hear any calls at the initial observation.  It was windy and getting cold.  I first saw the bird on the power lines along the entrance road.  During the time I watched it, it moved from there to the other side of the road (westward) and up and down the fence line there and into the trees and a couple time back to the power line.  It was flycatching at times in typical flycatcher style.

I returned after initial sighting (A cold front had come thru and I went to warm up and get some food, the temperature had dropped alot) and didn't refind the bird.
I left and came back again after a M. Chavez said someone (Michael Lester) went to look and had posted a ebird checklist that he saw.

I returend for a second time a little after 5 pm to the same area.  I met there and talked to Michael Lester who had taken a few photos (in poor to fair light) and had heard the bird call.  I looked at Michael's photos and it was definitely the same Kingbird.  On his photos I saw in addition to my comments above, that the extent of yellow on the undersides went very high and close to throat which definitely ruled out wither Cassin's or Western Kingbird.  He said he did hear it call and said based on his previous experience with both Couch's and Tropical Kingbirds in the Southern U.S., that this call was definitely a Tropical.

Returning before sunrise Oct. 6,  Aaron Shipe and I were trying to refind with no luck at first.  After sunrise and some rising temperatures and improving light the bird reappeared up by the storage units on the barbed wire fence.  Two birders that pulled in after us had been able to spot it there.  It was perching for fairly long periods of time allowing great views.  I saw it was same bird.  It moved some but not alot at first.  Not only was I able to see much better in the light, the above characteristics, but I and Aaron clearly heard the bird call.  This call was a match for the call sound recordings (Audubon sound files) I compared it to.  It had a noticeably different call than Couch's.  It was a less complex, rapid twittery call fairly high pitch that lacked any of the more nasal sounds and the more complex phrasing as found on Couch's.  I tried to record it but didn't have success capturing it when it called.  Aaron had got a sound recording.

Similar Species Discussion

Couch's was eliminated by the very different call as previously decribed in behavior section.

Western was eliminated by visual and also call.  Western doesn't have: Forked (notched) tail, very extensive yellow undersides, lack of white tail side edges, larger bill.

Cassin's was eliminated by visual and also call.  Cassin's doesn't have: Forked (notched) tail, very extensive yellow undersides, lack of pale/whitish tail terminal color, larger bill.

Call of all three other Kingbirds here didn't match the call of this bird.  Call I heard was decribed in behavior secton. 

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I've seen numerous Western Kingbirds, a few Cassin's and this was the first Tropical Kingbird i've seen.
