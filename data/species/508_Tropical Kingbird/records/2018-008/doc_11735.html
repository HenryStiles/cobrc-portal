Description of the Bird

Yellowish Kingbird similar in size and plumage to Western Kingbird.  However, it had a much longer and more substantial bill, had a darker ear patch and the yellow extended all the way up the chest to the white throat.  It also had a distinctly notched tail, which could be seen both when it was sitting and in flight.  Also in flight, the bird did not have the distinct white outer tail feathers of a Western Kingbird.  All these features can be seen in the photographs taken by Joey Kellner that are attached to this report.  This species is difficult to identify from Couch's Kingbird, but the bill was somewhat longer compared to Couch's, and the bird several times gave the twittering call typical of Tropical Kingbird.

Similar Species Discussion

Western Kingbird eliminated by much longer bill, darker ear patch, yellow up to the throat, and notched tail.  Couch's Kingbird eliminated by somewhat longer bill, and twittering calls given by this bird.

Previous Experience

I have seen many hundreds, if not a thousand, Western Kingbirds in Colorado and elsewhere, and a similar number of Tropical Kingbirds on many trips to Central and South America.  I have seen Couch's Kingbird a few times in south Texas and eastern Mexico.
