Description of the Bird

Tyrannus kingbird by shape and yellow belly. Noticeably nothced dark gray tail with very slight light edges in perfect light but not light on outer webs of rectrices or on tail tips. Belly is quite yellow, not light yellow, from upper breast to undertail coverts. Back is light greenish in good light (see photos).  Chin and throat are whitish. Forehead, head, and nape are light gray. Blackish bill is longish and stout.  


Although I did not hear the bird call, the recordings and spectrograms of its call on ebird (see and listen to Richard Bunn recording) eliminates all other kingbird species and eliminates hybrids with other flycatchers. 

Similar Species Discussion

Couch's Kingbird was eliminated by recorded calls and spectogram of calls. Thick-billed Kingbird has a much stouter bill. It has been sugested that the spacing of the folded primaries, some larger gaps, eliminates COKI but it is the spectrogram and tape recording that separates this TRKI from all other species. 

Resources Used

I looked online for all sources comparing COKI to TRKI and looked at photos of hybrids between WEKI x STFL and other known examples  of kingbird hybrids. I looked at my photos of TRKIs from Central and South America and the few photos I have of COKI from South Texas.

Previous Experience

I have seen over 100 TRKIs in Latin America but only about 10 COKIs and none recently.
