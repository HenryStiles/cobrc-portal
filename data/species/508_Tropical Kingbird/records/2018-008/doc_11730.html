Description of the Bird

From my eBird report (https://ebird.org/view/checklist/S48963921):

Around 5pm I received an eBird rare bird alert with a report of Western Kingbird from Frank Farrell. The description sounded pretty good for a Tropical Kingbird, if not a weird/molting Western Kingbird, so I decided to chase it immediately. Frank's description said:

Lacked high contrast white malar found on Cassin's, but also lacked white tail edges as on Western. Grey on head appeared paler overall, more consistent with Western. Forked tail didn't seem consistent with either Western or Cassin's per Sibley's illustrations.

I arrived at the South Plate Reservoir around 5:30pm and relocated the bird almost immediately. It was on the wires along the entrance road on the southwest side of the reservoir, across from the gated dirt road. The lighting at that time was not great (particularly given the cloud cover), but I was able to note a relatively long bill and yellow belly and breast, extending up to the throat. As Frank noted, the head was paler gray than Cassin's and lacked a strong malar, however, I was unable to really take note of tail edging due to poor lighting. Flight photos show a somewhat notched tail, but photo quality and the bird's angle may be contributing to this appearance. The bird then gave a single "twittery" call completely unlike Western. It lacked the trailing buzz of and did not seem as high pitched as a typical Couch's Kingbird. I last saw it flying north along the wires, but I did not see how far it went. I (joined by several others) spent the next hour or so looking without luck, though it may have just gone to roost for the night.

As far as my experience goes, I have previously lived in southern Arizona for several years where I've encountered many calling Western, Cassin's, and Tropical Kingbirds (and one Couch's). I have also spent a fair amount of time in southern Texas where I've seen and heard a number of Couch's and a few Tropicals (sometimes simultaneously).

Similar Species Discussion

Western and Cassin's Kingbirds have more "explosive" and varied calls, and a grayer chest. Cassin's head and chest tend to be darker as well with a contrasting malar.

Couch's Kingbird has a higher pitched call and a trailing buzz. Otherwise nearly identical by sight (though I believe it may be possible to differentiate the two species by subtle differences in bill length/size and primary projection)

Resources Used

I have a decent amount of experience with this species, so was able to ID it as Tropical from call immediately; however, I also used the Sibley app at the time to confirm.
