Description of the Bird

First detected by unfamiliar flycatcher chattering, and when I looked to it's conspicuous perch at the top of some Russian Olives, noted a large kingbird, structurally reminescent of Tropical, Couch's and Gray Kingbirds. I observed a tall head, large bill, and strongly notched tail. Although lighting was dark from overcast skies, I also noticed that the yellow on the belly was perhaps a little more extensive than a Western Kingbird should show, while the contrast of the pale patch just below the bill was somewhat intermediate between Western and Cassin's, and finally, I could not find any white on the outer tail feathers after straining very carefully. By then I was confident that I was viewing a Tropical/Couch's type bird, but could not recall either vocalization by heart, so I brought out my smart phone and started recording the talkative bird (via film). After obtaining a satisfying recording and with the bird still on it's perch making a racket, I quietly played Couch's, then Tropical Kingbird from the Sibley app just to compare them to what I was hearing. The high and slightly descending pid-d-d-d-d-d-d-d calls that my bird was making matched much better with Tropical Kingbird vocalizations rather than the vastly different toned Couch's, and later at home I found some more Tropical Kingbird recordings on Xeno-Canto which perfectly matched my bird's vocalizations. 
Almost as soon as I looked up from my phone after looking at Sibley's recordings, the bird suddenly jumped off it's perch and in it's stiff kingbird flight, flew straight towards my two young brothers and myself, passing directly over our heads and proceeding northwest. It wasn't flying very high at all (maybe 6 meters up), but somehow appeared to have a purpose to keep going. We though the bird may have landed once more on the distant telephone wires, but determined (by thorough scanning of the horizon through optics) that it had also resumed it's flight to the northwest soon afterwards. 

Similar Species Discussion

As I looked at the bird for the first time, I had to be certain I wasn't seeing an odd Western or Cassin's Kingbird. The bill was large for a Western, and the head taller than I am used to, while the pale chin was almost but not quite contrasting enough to be a Cassin's. Looking at the tail I observed absolutely no visible white on the outer retrices, while the tail as a whole appeared quite heavily notched, two more strikes against Western and one more against Cassin's. Once those were eliminated and it was just a matter of Tropical/Couch's, I had to pull up some recordings from Sibley's to compare to my bird which was making high and slightly descending "pid-d-d-d-d-d-d" calls. I soon found that Couch's make sounds that are nothing like this, and so the only option I was left with was that this bird was indeed a Tropical Kingbird.

Resources Used

A Moto G 5th Generation smart phone to record the bird. Sibleys Birds app and later a variety of Xeno-Canto recordings were used to compare Tropical and Couch's Kingbird vocalizations. No resources other than my personal knowledge and experience with different kingbird species were used in IDing the bird visually.

Previous Experience

I have seen hundreds of Western and Cassin's Kingbirds in the past and know most of their vocalizations. This is my third observation of a Tropical Kingbird ever, the other two being in California and Florida. I have no personal experience with Couch's Kingbird.
