Habitat

The bird was in Connected Lake St Park, a large riparian park with lakes, canals, trails, swampy areas heavily wooded with Cottonwood, Tamarisk, Russian Olive, and Willow. The bird was in a Tamarisk when I found it.

Description of the Bird

I turned the corner of the trail and saw a rather plump-looking warbler-sized bird in a sparse tamarisk right on front of me. With my binoculars up, I realized this was not a "regular". The bird was feeding quietly, jumping between the sparse branches, obviously looking for food. It was not moving fast, or far, doing little hops, climbing up a branch. I knew what it was right away, the overall bird was so strikingly different than any of our regular birds that I immediately know what it was. I watched it until it flew, then ran back to the car and wrote some notes, called Larry Arnold and Rich Levad. Observations - warbler sized bird, pure white from undertail coverts, entire belly, up to breast. Black throat, cheeks up to and covering eyes. Black bill, small and thin. Dark blue from top of bill, covering crown, covering nape, mantle, scapulars, coverts, secondaries, and primaries. White wing patch was very prominent at base of primaries. Black streaks were quite jagged that ran from breast along sides and flanks. I also noticed when the bird turned and I could see the underside of the tail that the white extended on tyhe underside of the tail clear to the end of the tail. The eye looked dark - black. Sorry - I forgot to look at leg color.
Behaviors: I only saw the bird for a minute - it was feeding, not paying attention to me at all, slowly working it's way around in the tree, hopping some, jumping from branch to branch. Then it seemed to startle, and flew off into the heavy tamarisk.
Call: The bird did not call.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

I'm not sure what similar species would be...Cerulean? It had a black throat instead of white, and did not have white wing bars. Black-throated Gray Warbler - It had a blue back, no white in face, no wing bars.
