Description of the Bird

A largish, dull warbler, it had a large bill for a warbler. The crown and face were dull olive green and the throat was off white. The dull whitish eye rings were broken fore and aft by a faint dark eyeline, contrasting in front of the eye with a pale yellowish supraloral spot. The back was dull olive and unmarked. The underparts were dull cream with extensive but vague olive streaking on the sides and flanks. The undertail coverts were bright white -- the brightest plumage on the bird.

The wings were quite long with long primary projection and with whitish wingbars formed by pale tips to the greater and median coverts. The upper bar was whiter and longer than was the lower bar. The bill and legs were dark, though the mandible had a paler base.
Behaviors: Foraging in planted pines and also on the ground amonst pine-needle litter.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Large white tail spots rule out most other ID contenders (e.g., Yellow-throated Vireo, Yellow Warbler, Blackpoll Warbler). Palm Warbler, that has a similar tail pattern, ruled out by white undertail coverts and too-strong wingbars.
