Description of the Bird

This was a long-billed warbler. It was overall dull greenish with a hint of yellow at the throat and chin. It was relatively short-tailed, with dark checks, and the wings, with two dull whitish wingbars, contrasted little with the body color.
Behaviors: Fed in a sluggish manner, exploring both deciduous and conifer needles. Fed on "sap" oozing from a sliver maple, and attempted to pick up a dark insect from the top side of a small maple twig. Explored upside down, right side up and twisted in all manners while exploring. Checked twigs, leaf buds, broken parts of twigs for food itemes. Sometimes silent and sometimes vocal chips from tallest parts of trees. Often foraged alone.
Call: loud chips given on occassion, distinct from those of Myrtle and Audubon Warblers
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

The other dull winter warblers are: Orange-crowned, Tennessee, and the two look alikes: Bay-breasted and Blackpoll, plus the non warbler, Olive Warbler looks a little bit like dull PIWAs.
OCWA and TEWAs don't have wingbars.
Olive warbler has different tail spots and notched tail, plus it is more gray and less green with a contrasty auricular.
All BBWAs would be brighter than this bird, and they would have longer primary projection and wings and they would have buffy undertail coverts unlike this PIWA
Blackpoll would have very long wings, long disant migrant, contrasty bright white undertail coverts and would have a streak, vs. unstreaked back. They woud show some faint ventral streaking, especially at the sides of the breast.
