Description of the Bird

A largish warbler with some bright yellow patches that caused some confusion at first. This was because much of the bird's plumage was the very dull and grayish Juvenal plumage, but with patches of 1st-Basic plumage as delineated:

a bit of green on the forecrown;
yellow supraloral patch;
some yellow on the chin and throat;
a few patches of green on the upperparts, including most of the paler green on the rump; and a couple larger patches of yellow on the chest and sides with some olive streaking.

The mostly-dark bill was fairly large for a warbler and the legs were dark. The wings were long and dark with bright white wingbars formed by tips to the greater and median coverts. The tail was dark with very large white tail spots on the outer pair of rectrices, with the spots on the R6s occupying almost the entire length of the feathers.
Behaviors: Foraging in deciduous trees in the campground, in pines in the pine stand immediately west of the campground, and along 3 Rd near the entrance kiosk to for the State Park. It also chased Eastern Bluebirds fairly often (for whatever reason).
Call: None heard by me
Plumage: Juvenal molting to First Basic

Similar Species Discussion

In Juvenal plumage, it could be tricky, but the color pattern evident of First Basic plumage is fairly distinctive. However, the real clincher are the huge white tail spots not really matched by any other North American warbler.
