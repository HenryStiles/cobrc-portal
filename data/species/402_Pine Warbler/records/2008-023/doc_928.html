Description of the Bird

Slightly larger than a Red-breasted Nuthatch, and shaped like a warbler with a longish notched tail, and a slender bill. Head greenish with a yellow throat, black bill and eye, and broken yellow orbital (eye-ring); underparts bright yellow on the breast, with gray, diffuse streaks on the sides, white on lower belly and vent, dark gray wings with bold white wing bars and white edges to the tertials, dark gray tail, unstreaked green back, dark legs and feet.
Behaviors: Feeding on chopped peanuts.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other warbler matches the field marks observed. Yellow-throated Vireo has a thicker bill and spectacles. Bullock's Oriole (female) would be larger.
