Description of the Bird

I was looking through the pine trees at Fairmount Cemetery in Lamar and noticed a different dull warbler with the Yellow-rumped Warblers. The bird looked the same size as the Yellow-rumped Warblers. The bird had a gray head, gray mantle, grayish wings with two thin white wing bars on each wing. The tail looked gray as well. The tail looked long. The bill was dark and thin. The bird had a thin white partial eye ring, on the top and bottom, the ear patch was gray as well. The throat was pale gray, as were all of the belly. The undertail coverts were more white. The legs were dark. The bird was very dull.
Behaviors: Moving around in the pine needles with a large flock of Yellow-rumped Warblers.
Call: none
Plumage: first fall

Similar Species Discussion

Blackpoll Warblers in fall would have some yellow on the throat, also yellow legs and has a short tall.

Bay-breasted Warblers in fall would be more brighter, with a greenish back.
