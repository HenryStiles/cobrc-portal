Description of the Bird

This was a bright yellow (ventrally) bird with a warbler-like bill.

The feature that stood out most was the bright yellow chin, throat, and breast that constasted strongly with a whitish lower belly and very olive-green crown, face and back. It had gray-olive wings with two strong whitish wingbars. I forgot to look at leg color. On the face, there was a yellow eyering the also extended to form a yellow spectacle, very noticeable.
Behaviors: First seen briefly perched high in a tree in the sun, then flew off to a distant tree and then flew almost immediately out of sight. It then reappered low in a Siberian elm where it preened for a few seconds but mostly sat with feathers fluff out and facing the sunlight.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult winter

Similar Species Discussion

Bay-breasted Warbler, Blackpoll Warbler, and Prairie Warbler are somewhat similar.

This bird does not have the breast streaking of Blackpoll and Prairie. Blackpoll doesn't have as much yellow ventrally, with a streaked back, unlike this bird. Bay-breasted is never as yellow ventrally as this bird and it usually shows at least some buff on the flanks. Prairie face has similar yellow spectacle but the yellow also extends to the rear of the spectacle towards the back of the head in a unique pattern. Prairie flanks streaks usually quite prominent unlike this unstreaked bird.
