Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium-sized warbler, about the size of nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers. It was fairly "chunky" in shape. The bill was black, quite long, straight, and thick (for a warbler; just a little less than the thickness of the eye). The legs were black, with the "soles" of the feet a fleshy yellow color.

The bird's cap, auriculars, and back were a grayish-brown color, with a very, very slight yellowish or greenish tinge, barely noticeable. The bird had a bold white split eye-ring and a fainter, dusky white supraloral. The sides of the neck, extending onto the nape just behind the grayish-brown auricular, was a slightly but noticeably paler grayish-brown. The bird's throat was off-white with a slight buffy tinge, especially on the sides of the throat, with the center of the throat being almost pure white. The bird's chest had a buffy wash across it, and the sides and flanks had the same buffy wash, though paler, and fading near the undertail coverts. There were also some very faint and indistinct streaks on the lower flanks. The belly and undertail coverts were white. The wings were dark gray, with two somewhat bold wingbars that were slightly off-white. The bird had a long tail that projected well beyond the undertail coverts, and and was mostly white below, with small dark gray tail corners and a small area of dark gray at the base.
Behaviors: the bird was a mixed feeding flock moving around in the upper parts of small locust trees in the area.
Call: none heard
Plumage: first basic

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species of warbler were ruled out by the pale underparts with white belly and undertail coverts and no streaking, and pale buffy wash across the chest, as well as the long and heavy bill and plain brownish upperparts.

Palm, Nashville, and Orange-crowned Warblers were ruled out by the white undertail coverts, as well as long tail and long heavy bill.

Cape May Warbler was ruled out by the unstreaked underparts and long heavy bill.

Blackpoll Warbler was ruled out by the whitish throat, lack of any underpart streaking, long tail, lack of gray patch on the sides of the neck, and brownish back.

Bay-breasted Warbler was ruled out by long tail, mainly whitish underparts, white undertail coverts, brown back, and long, heavy bill.
