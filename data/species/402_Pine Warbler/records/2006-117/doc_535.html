Habitat

Rather dense stand of mature willows with dense understory of cockleburs and other weeds, with a few shrub-sized willows also.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swift 8x42 binoculars
Distance:  approximately 30 feet at closest approach
Light:  Bird was seen from a variety of angles, mostly in indirect sunlight. Partly cloudy conditions made for pretty good light.

Description of the Bird

Here I reproduce my field notes verbatim: "Medium-large, chunky warbler. Yellow head and upper breast, white belly and undertail coverts. Two white wingbars. Didn't get great look at back color, but impression was green. Andrew Spencer and others claimed wings were 'blue,' but I never saw this. Face pattern 'messy' and a little complicated--definitely a faint darker line through the eye and what appeared to be a brighter yellow eyebrow. Faint dusky streaks on sides of chest."
Behaviors: Bird foraged actively and silently at 15-25 feet in willows (mid-upper levels).
Call: bird was silent
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

From field notes: "First call was Blue-winged Warbler, but the bird never looked like BWWA to me. White on underparts was too extensive; eyeline not sharp and bold; color of yellow didn't seem right; back and face and wings were wrong color; 'jizz' was wrong. Faint dusky streaks on sides of chest also rules out BWWA. Not Yellow-throated Vireo because no spectacles; not as sharp a contrast between yellow and white on underparts. Large white tail spots obvious in flight."

Resources Used

I looked at Sibley after jotting down my field notes.

Previous Experience

I have not seen that many Pine Warblers--maybe a dozen or so. This was my third in Colorado and my first in five years. I am more familiar with the confusion species, Blue-winged Warbler and Yellow-throated Vireo.
