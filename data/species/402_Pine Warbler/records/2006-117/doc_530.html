Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized warbler, about the size of nearby Townsend's Warblers, though appeared to be a bit heavier in build. The bill was black, and appeared to be typical of Dendroica. The legs were black.

The crown and face were a yellowish olive-green. The bird had a paler area behind the auricular, and along the eyebrow, and as a crescent under the eye, making the auricular are stand out a bit. The back was the same yellowish olive-green color. The wings were bluish-gray, with two bold white wingbars. The rump was the same color as the back. The tail was grayish, with large white tail corners.

The throat and chest were bright yellow, with some fairly faint olive-colored streaks about the same color as the upperparts on the sides of the chest. These streaks were hard to see from a distance, but fairly evident at close range. The belly and undertail coverts were white.
Behaviors: the bird was foraging at mid to upper levels in the trees, associated with a large flock of Wilson's, Yellow, and Townsend's Warblers. It could be hard to keep track of.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Fall plumaged Bay-breasted and Blackpoll Warblers were ruled out by the overall bright coloration of this bird, including much yellower upperparts. Townsend's Warbler was ruled out by the much brighter upperparts, much fainter streaking below, darker face with less yellow, etc. Dull Blue-winged Warbler was ruled out by more extensive white below, more complex facial pattern, faint streaking on the sides, etc.
