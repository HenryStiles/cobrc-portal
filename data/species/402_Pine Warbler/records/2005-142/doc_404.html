Description of the Bird

Relatively large warbler with largish bill. Dark upper mandible, pinkish lower mandible. Dark eye. Crown grayish-olive. Upperparts unstreaked olive-green. Dull yellow throat and upper breast, some slight dark streaking on flanks but difficult to see. Rest of underparts white, including undertail coverts. Yellowish broken eye-ring and yellow lores extending partly over the eye. Olive auriculars bordered by yellow extending up from neck. Wings blackish with grayish tertial edges and 2 whitish wing-bars. Long blackish tail. Legs somewhat of a darker fleshy color.
Behaviors: Would generally fly in with goldfinches and land in either an Aspen tree or Cottonwood. Drank at heated bird bath. Started out eating chipped sunflower seeds at feeder but then mostly ate suet. Would often sit quietly in Aspen for long periods of time, especially with cold weather.
Call: Chips heard.
Plumage: 1st year

Similar Species Discussion

Separated from Bay-breasted and Blackpoll by unstreaked upperparts, brighter yellow underparts clearly separated from white lower belly and yellow that extended around the rear of the auriculars. Bay-breasted should show no streaking on underparts. Blackpoll has more greenish-yellow underparts with lighter legs and longer undertail coverts.
