Description of the Bird

A dendroica type warbler which could be compared in size and jizz to an adjacent adult male Audubon's Warbler . Appeared to have a stouter bill in comparison to the YRWA , complete yellow throat extending to upper breast , lower breast , flanks and undertail coverts white , some faint dark streaking on left side and flanks shows up in photo ( although I did not notice this while observing ) , dark auriculars , pale eye-ring which appeared to be whitish rather than yellow , dark line from eye to base of bill , no pale supercilium or superloral , pale yellowish neck patch ,two pale wing bars on dark wing , tail viewed from below appeared to be damaged or in moult and outer tail feather appeared to be white. Never got a good view of the back as bird was always above our heads.
Behaviors: Actively gleaning insects from small bushes and trunk ,branches of a Hackberry tree. In company with flock of Black-capped Chickadees , Juncos , House Finches ,Audubon's Warbler , and Pine Siskins.
Call: one hard chip call , responded to playback song.
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow throat and upper breast and white lower breast and undertail coverts rules out imm female Bay-breasted and Blackpoll , white wing bars rules out imm female Prairie warbler , lack of pale supercilium rules out imm female Blackburnian and Cerulean , lack of significant breast or flank streaking rules out female Cape May , size and jizz including long tail rules out immature Northern Parula , dark auricular , pale neck patch ,white lower breast from adult male Audubon's Warbler.
