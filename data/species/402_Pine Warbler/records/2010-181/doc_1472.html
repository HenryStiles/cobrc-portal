Other Observers:  David Leatherman found it in the yard and pointed it out to me; David Leatherman was the original finder and identifier

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Pine Warbler
First Date/Time:  10/19/2009
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  hour
County:  Prowers
Specific Location:  in my farm yard 5 miles south of Lamar on Highway 287 and a little over a quarter of a mile to the west
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

-moving water feature
-Bluegrass and perennial rye lawn
-Pinions, junipers, honey locusts, elms
-a few flower beds and weedy patches
-main drawing would be that for several miles in any direction there is no other water but only crop land and grass land - at times very dry

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x42 binocs and camera auto zoom lens
Distance:  15 to 20 feet
Light:  excellent
but the bird stayed high for most of the time and in the shadows within the trees

Description of the Bird

-olive crown, head, back and cheeks
-yellow spectacles
-lighter neck patch
-dark grayish wings with two white wing bars
-dark grayish tail with white outer feathers
-bright yellow throat and breast with blurry streaking on sides
-white lower belly and undertail coverts
-dark legs and black bill
Behaviors: -for the most part it stayed high in the trees
-twice we thought it left and went west but it returned
-I would have difficulty pointing to any specific behavior other than "warbler like"
Call: Dave Leatherman heard it - I would have difficulty identifying it by song
Plumage: adult male

Similar Species Discussion

-first winter female Blackpoll had a more gray neck, the yellow throat color is not as bright, and it lacks the yellow spectacles and contrasting cheek patch
-first winter Hermit has a plainer face pattern and does not have as definitive of streaking on the sides
