Description of the Bird

Mark Yaeger reported to me that he and Barb Yaeger saw the Pine Warbler in Pueblo City Park on the morning of December 3, 2005. At 1:30pm I was able to find the warbler with other birds. The bird was similar in size to the American Goldfinches near by. The bird had a green head, and back, without any streaking. The wings were black with two obvious wing bars on each wing which were white. The tail was dark. The eyes were dark, with some yellow over and under the eyes. The bill was thin like a warbler, though a bit long. The bird had a bright yellow chin, throat, and upper breast with some dark streaking down the flanks. The belly and undertail were white. The yellow went further down than of a Yellow-throated Vireo, which only has yellow to the throat. The legs were dark.
Behaviors: The bird flitted around warbler like with other birds, including American Goldfinches. The bird stayed fairly high in the tree.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-throated Vireo - Bill is more thicker and the yellow is on the chin and throat, not below that. This species shouldn't be here in winter.
