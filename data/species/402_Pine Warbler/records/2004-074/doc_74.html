Description of the Bird

A largish, bright yellow-and-green warbler singing from pines. The crown, nape, auriculars, back, and rump were all a bright green that was not quite as dark as olive and unmarked. The underparts from the chin to the upper belly were bright yellow and there was blurry olive streaking on the sides. The lower belly and undertail coverts were unmarked white.

The wings were dark with two distinct white wing bars formed by tips to the median and greater coverts. The secondaries and tertials were black with vague gray fringes. The wing linings were seen to be yellowish.

The tail was dark, but with extensive white spots on the outer two pairs of rectrices (which can be seen on my pix). The R6 spot, though restricted to the inner web, was large, occupying more than half the feather, but with the feather's tip being dark.

The legs were blackish, as were the eyes. The bill was also black and relatively thick for a warbler.
Behaviors: Foraging and singing in pines.
Call: sang frequently -- a long, fast, musical trill with no accented endings
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other warblers and similar species of other groups (e.g., Yellow-throated Vireo) ruled out by song and/or bright yellow-and-green plumage. Prairie and Kirtland's warblers specifically ruled out by song and by face, tail, and underparts patterns.
