Description of the Bird

The bird was approximately the size and shape of a Yellow-rumped Warbler.

It was fairly inactive for a warbler.

Its cap, nape, and mantle were a dull, uniform, unstreaked yellow-green. The face was that same color, but with a broken bright yellow eye ring and a partial bright yellow collar.

The throat and breast were bright yellow, unmarked except for a bit of coarse, low contrast streaking at the sides of the breast. The belly and undertail coverts were clean white.

The bird had bold white wingbars.
Behaviors: foraging in the foliage of a tree, like a warbler, but less active than most warblers
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

From the size, shape, and behavior, I can mostly narrow the choice to a warbler. However, Yellow-throated Vireo should at least be considered. The partial yellow collar, streaking at the sides of the breast, and lack of bold yellow spectacles rules this out.

Among warblers, possible choices (besides Pine Warbler) include:
immature Blackpoll Warbler
immature Bay-breasted Warbler
immature Townsend's Warbler
immature Blackburnian Warbler (perhaps)
Townsend's or Blackburnian Warblers would have a prominent supercilium. The breast streaking and white undertail coverts rule out Bay-breasted Warbler. The bright yellow throat, unstreaked mantle, and partial yellow collar rule out Blackpoll Warbler.
