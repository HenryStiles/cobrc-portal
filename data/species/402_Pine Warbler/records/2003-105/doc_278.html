Description of the Bird

A largish warbler with fairly bright green upperparts and bright yellow chest. The face was mostly green, but with yellow eye rings, broken fore and aft by thin blackish eye lines, surrounded the dark eyes. The supraloral area was yellow. The bird's bill was quite substantial for a warbler, being relatively thick-based and fairly long.

I could discern no dark-centered feathers on the green back, which was somewhat darker than the brighter green rump. There were vague olive streaks on the sides that extended onto the flanks. The belly and vent region were white.

The wings were dark, with obvious white wingbars formed by the tips to the greater and median coverts, respectively. The tail was also dark, but with very large white tail spots. The bill was black and the legs appeared dark.
Behaviors: Staying with a small mixed flock of birds within the vegetation of a large Blue Spruce; it finally came down to investigate vigorous pishing.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was too bright green above (without dark back streaking) and too bright yellow below (with olive streaking on sides) to be much of anything else other than Blackpoll Warbler. However, the olive green face with eye arcs/eye rings more obvious than the dark eye line (opposite in BLPW) and the very bright yellow throat eliminate that species from consideration.
