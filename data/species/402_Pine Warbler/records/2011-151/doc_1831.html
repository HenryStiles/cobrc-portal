Habitat

Oak tree, with many bugs. This tree on this morning had a male Black-throated Blue Warbler, two Townsend's Warblers, and a few Yellow-rumped Warblers, as well as this bird.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8x42 leica binoculars and camera
Distance:  30 meters up in the tree
Light:  Sunny, bird mostly in the shade.

Description of the Bird

A warbler flew in to the oak that I was looking at. I quickly realized the warbler wasn't a Yellow-rumped Warbler. The bird was quite buffy underneath, whitish wing bars, and long tail. I also noticed a dark ear patch, with lighter eyeline and eye arches above and below each eye. The bill was thin and seemed long with the pink color on the lower mandible. A look at the back appeared to not have any streaking, and was greenish/gray. Look at photos.
Behaviors: Stayed high in the oak tree.
Call: none
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

The long tail eliminates the short tail warblers, such as Blackpoll. Bay-breasted has streaked back, and light earpatch color.

Resources Used

none, I've seen enough eastern warblers, I was very confident what the bird was, as soon as I saw it.

Previous Experience

I saw a Pine Warbler that was very similar looking to this bird a couple of years ago in the winter in Pueblo City Park.
