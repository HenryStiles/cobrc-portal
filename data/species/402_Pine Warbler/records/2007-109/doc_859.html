Description of the Bird

Overall a large sluggish warbler with a good-sized bill with a long tail and short, bright white undertail coverts.

Tail was distinctive - long with long white tail spots, as viewed from underneath, with only the tail tips showing dark edges. Tail, dorsally, only seen on occasion, but it appeared to have the two outermost tail feathers broadly edged with white.


Back was brownish green and unstreaked. Head dull yellowish with contasting darkish cheeks. Bill was large. Wings greenish with two dull whitish wingbars. Throat was the brightest part of the bird, yellow contrasting with less yellowish head and dull yellow-gray ventral surface. Crown contrasted a bit with the face and was concolor with the back. Leg color was dark gray.
Behaviors: Foragged in pines, a species that resembled ponderosa, but with more orange bark and cones that appeared to not open, similar to ones used in Pueblo and Fremont counties by sapsuckers.

Gleaned tiny objects from pine needles. Also perched for short periods on the edge of the buildings where it was possibly drinking water. Foragged for less than 30 seconds in deciduous tree, in what appeared to be the emerging leaf buds.

Always slow and deliberate, often disappearing center of dense branch tips where needles were most dense.
Call: I didn't hear but Mark Peterson heard its characteristic chip
Plumage: 1st winter probably

Similar Species Discussion

Blackpoll Warbler - has pink legs and much shorter tail, and much longer wings. Its wingbars are whiter. Also Blackpoll has noticeable dorsal streaking.

Bay-breasted Warbler - more olive dorsally with noticeable dorsal streaking; wingbars whiter than pine and throat not as yellow. White on outertail not as noticeable as in Pine. Bay-breasted usually quite buffy underneath except for tail spots. Tail noticeably shorter and undertail coverts extend farther on to tail.
