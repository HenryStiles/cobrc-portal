Description of the Bird

A largish warbler overall bright yellow and green. The crown, nape, and back were all olive-green. The dark eyes were surrounded by extensive bright yellow eye arcs only barely split by a dark eyeline; the lores were bright yellow.

The wings were dark with obvious, well-defined white wingbars formed by the tips of the median and greater coverts, the upper bar being brighter and better defined than the lower bar, which was wider. I could discern no obvious molt limits in the greater coverts and all wing feathers appeared quite dark-centered, thus the bird may well have been an adult.

The chin, throat, chest and upper belly were all bright yellow, except that there was some vague olive streaking on the upper sides. The lower belly and undertail coverts were unmarked white, though there was a strap of duller yellow extending from the flanks across the vent.

The tail was dark above with the outer rectrices being almost completely white (the only visible dark being a thin edge on the inner web of the R6s); from underneath, the tail looked almost entirely white
Behaviors: The bird primarily perched for the length of observation, either in a Scots Pine or some deciduous tree after I inadvertently flushed it from another Scots Pine.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Basic possibly Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Though in some plumages Pine Warbler can readily be confused with other warblers (Bay-breasted, Blackpoll) and, even, vireos (Yellow-throated), this probably adult male is quite distinctive. Separating features include the dark, not blue, legs and extensive white in the tail (separating from YTVI), the lack of darker streaking on the upperparts and the olive side streaking (separating from the two aforementioned warblers).
