Description of the Bird

A primarily yellow colored warbler with wing bars and contrasting eye ring. Fairly long billed for a warbler. Most of the head is grayish greenish yellow, with brighter yellow eye ring and supraloral. The throat is also clean yellow. The nape and back are about the same grayish greenish yellow as the head. The wings are primarily gray with two white wing bars. The flight feathers are edges with paler gray fringing. The tail is also gray, with white centered outer retrices. Most of the breast andd belly appears yellow, with faint vertical grayish streaking. White feathering begins around the legs on the belly and continues onto the undertail coverts. The bird appears to be long tailed for a warbler.
Behaviors: The bird was active, appearing for short periods of time. The pattern I observed three times was first hearing the chip notes, then seeing the bird in nearby pine trees. Then it would come to a feeder mounted on a ground floor office window. It sometimes also foraged on the bricks and eaves of the office building.
Call: Flat chip note heard as it fed in pines, under eaves, and on feeder
Plumage: Aduld winter

Similar Species Discussion

Other primarily yellow colored warblers do not have the combination of white wing bars and white vent and underwing covert areas. Orange crowned Warbler, for example, lack these highlights, as do Yellow Warbler, Prairie Warbler, Palm Warbler, and Wilsons Warbler. Other warblers with white wing bars do not show the long bill and tail, faint breast streaking, and yellow spectacles of Pine Warbler.
