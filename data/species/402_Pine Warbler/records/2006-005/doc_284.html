Description of the Bird

I saw the bird from the front as it looked at me, giving me an impression of a bright yellow-breasted bird with a clean white belly. The warbler type bill was dark and fairly pointed. The face was dull greenish yellow with bright yellow spectacles and lores. The breast was minimally streaked with greenish yellow on an otherwise bright yellow background. I did not get a good look at the wings or other upperparts as the bird flew off while I was retrieving my camera.
Behaviors: The bird briefly appeared on a low branch of some bare deciduous bushes in a light snow flurry in the vicinity of a feeder setup. The homeowner reports the bird feeding on suet cakes. Other birds around included a Yellow rumped Warbler, juncos, chickadees, and nuthatches, etc. but the Pine Warbler appeared to act independently as most of the other birds loitered for long times.
Call: surprisingly, bird was silent
Plumage: Adult winter Bright looking bird

Similar Species Discussion

Other primarily yellow colored adult warblers do not show the white belly and vent areas in combination with bright yellow spectacles and lores. Orange crowned Warbler, for example, lack these highlights, as do Yellow Warbler, Prairie Warbler, Palm Warbler, and Wilsons Warbler.
