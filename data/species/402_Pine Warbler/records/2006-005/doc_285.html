Description of the Bird

A largish and bright green-and-yellow warbler, the bird had a large bill for a warbler. The crown and auriculars were green and the superciliary, broken eye rings, and chin and throat were bright yellow. The bird's rump was paler green than the back. The chest and sides were bright yellow, with the latter also sporting vague olive streaking that extended onto the flanks. The lower belly and vent were bright white.

The wings were quite long (primary projection exceeding the length of the longest tertial) and sported bright whitish wingbars, with the upper bar being more obvious and longer than the lower bar. The tail was dark with huge white tail spots in the outer two pairs of rectrices, the R6 spots being nearly the length of the tail. The bill and legs were dark, though there was a paler base to the mandible.
Behaviors: Foraging on suet chips fallen to the ground from a tree-mounted suet feeder and also flycatching (temp was in the 60s) from bare deciduous trees.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

The combination of very long wings, unmarked green back, bright yellow eye rings, throat, breast, and sides, and olive streaking on sides and flanks, and huge R6 tail spots eliminate all other similar species (e.g., Yellow-throated Vireo and Blackpoll Warbler).
