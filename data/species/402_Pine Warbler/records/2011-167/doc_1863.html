Habitat

We live in a foothill with Ponderosa "Forest" stand to the West & Ponderosa savanna to the East; bird appeared on second floor porch at feeders

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8 x 42 Eagle Optics Denali binoculars
Canon Digital EOS with a 100mm Macro lens
Distance:  10-12
Light:  Sun was south south east of bird & observer; I was able to obtain some 17 digital photos through windows or glass door of bird in all positions; lighting was not an issue for most photos

Description of the Bird

Size: about 5", or smaller than a dark-eyed junco but larger than a kinglet.
Shape: slender bird compared to a junco; a much longer thin bill & much longer tail compared to a kinglet'
Plumage: overall yellowish below & greyish above;head with yellow spectacles around eyes & faint ear patch; throat bright yellow & belly white; sides with faint streaking; undertail coverts white; back olive grey with no streaking; pair of white wing bars evident.
Behaviors: Bird fed on black oil sunflower seeds & on suet; this is consistent with pine warbler food selection at feeders elsewhere in winter.
Call: not given
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

First thought it was an orange-crowned warbler since that would be the most likely winter resident. Presence of white undertail coverts, prominent wing bars & yellow eye spectacles eliminated orange-crowned. Next eliminated blackpoll warbler with presence of eye spectacles, ear patch & unstreaked back.
