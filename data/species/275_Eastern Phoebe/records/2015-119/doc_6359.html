Description of the Bird

Bird was flycatching with Mountain Bluebirds. We did not hear any calls from the Eastern Phoebe, only the bluebirds.
There are few records of Eastern Phoebe in western Colorado. Brenda and I have not seen one for 24 years in western Colorado and there are only about 6 records total.
The flycatcher was nearly the same size as the Mountain Bluebirds. The bird had a dark head, light gray throat, and yellowish white belly. The tail was dark brown and the back of light brown. The feathers looked fresh. The bill was dark, long and pointed as one would expect for any phoebe. The tail was in constant motion, circular in nature.

Similar Species Discussion

Black Phoebes are fairly common around Nucla. Black Phoebes have a dark (black) head and chest. The belly is white and does not have a yellow wash. Say's Phoebes are also fairly common around Nucla. Say's have an gray head can chest and the belly is orange with a black tail and a gray back. All three species of phoebes are closely related but easy to tell apart by field marks.
Western Wood-Pewees are a little smaller and smaller billed. There head color is not darker then the back and the tail projectings are much longer. They lack a yellowish belly. Olive-sided Flycatchers migration is over. They also have a gray head and is the same color as the wings, lack a yellow belly with a vested look. Most have white patches on there back.
All empids are small and have other field marks that don't match.

Previous Experience

We know nearly all flycatcher species in North and South America. On the ranch in Nebraska we had Eastern Phoebes nesting under our bridge. Both Say's and Black nest around Nucla. So do both, Olive-sided Flycatchers and Western Wood-Pewees. We do have 5 species of empids nesting also in western Montrose County.
