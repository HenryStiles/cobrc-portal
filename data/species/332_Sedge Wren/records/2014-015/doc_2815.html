Description of the Bird

While walking along the dam at Prewitt Reservoir, I heard the sedge wren calling (audio recordings included with this report).  I walked down and was able to get views of the wren multiple times through binoculars at close range as the wren moved about at the edge of a small marsh.  It had the familiar appearance of a sedge wren:  small, buffy, short bill, pale throat, low-contrast supercilium, barred wings (photo included with this report).

Similar Species Discussion

This sedge wren was distinguished from other wrens by call ("chadt") and by the barred wings.
