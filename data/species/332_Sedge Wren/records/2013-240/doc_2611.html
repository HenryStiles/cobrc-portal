Description of the Bird

1-Small wren with relatively short tail, tail held cocked much of time. Brightly streaked dark and light back and brightly (but finely) streaked crown. Pale supercilium. Buffy sides bright in color but not as dark or chestnut as typical of Marsh Wren. Call, given repeatedly, a husky "Chit" with a bit of buzz to it, sounding very much like recording on iBirdPro. Surrounded by the group of six of us, giving us all views, some photos by Glenn (and possibly others?) 

2-About 15 minutes after we left bird number one, in a similar spot of marsh and wet grass, I heard a second bird called about 10 times. It was across water from me, so I couldn't get to it. After listening to it, seeing some movement in vegetation, but not seeing bird, I played tape... and all movement and noise ceased. A couple others from group came over, but we were unable to get at location bird was calling from

Similar Species Discussion

Marsh Wren: unstreaked crown, more chestnut than buff in color and darker, different call note. Second bird ID'd by call alone.
Streaked back eliminated all other wrens. Thin bill (non-sparrow like) eliminated all "marsh sparrows."
