Description of the Bird

Small brown wren with a short bill. Undertail coverts warm cinnamon. Tail spiky and short. Back distinctive with darker brown blotches and white vertical streaks. Bill short with lower mandible standing out with its yellow coloration. Throat whitish, breast whitish, belly pale cinnamon. Face unpatterend.
Behaviors: Skulky but it did stay in the open, mostly behind low olive twigs at the edge of a wet sedge meadow. It was interested in watching me and did a quarter circle around me.
Call: loud scolding notes identifialbe as a wren but not to species
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Marsh Wren is similar but that species has a strong white supercilium and the pattern of white streaks on the back is different with streaks less widespread and concentrated on a chestnut-colored back. Also, the bill length and shape is different, longer and less broadly based in MAWR. The tail of MAWR is also longer than in SEWR. There were two MAWRs present nearby but they were actually more skulky and they were in the cattails and not in the sedge/olives. HOWR were also present but they don't have the warmth or streaked back of SEWR.
