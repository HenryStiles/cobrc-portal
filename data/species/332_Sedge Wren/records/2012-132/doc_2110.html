Description of the Bird

Small buffy wren in tall wet grass with dark crown streaked buffy, and dark back streaked narrowly with white. Its face pattern was weak. Belly was rich buffy ochre, and chest was paler off-white. Relatively short tail (c/w House Wren, let's say) was often held cocked. Bird responded to Sedge Wren song, often coming in close and calling repeatedly. Seen multiple times for duration of 5-15 seconds each time, probably totalling about 60-90 seconds in all.
Behaviors: See description
Call: Call somewhat like a Lincoln's Sparrow with a distinct squeaky quality added. Once or twice it broke out into a muted form of its song, a couple notes followed by a trill.
Plumage: undetermined

Similar Species Discussion

Marsh Wren with solid cap, darker overall, different call, usually rustier beneath. House wren longer tailed, no streaking above.
