Description of the Bird

I was pishing at an area of tall wet grass and cattails at side of road, the type of place one might hope for Sedge Wren or LeConte's Sparrow. Two SOSP and two LISP popped up, then a MAWR. Several birds other birds were calling. I heard sharp, slightly buzzy, chip that I recognized as quite possibly that of Sedge Wren. I played a Sedge Wren song. A bird popped up about 20 feet away. It was a pale buff wren with extensive yellow buff (not red-buff typical of MAWR) on sides and had a modest pale supercilium, a hint of an eyering and streaking on head. Bill short. The throat was whitish. The bill was smallish for a wren and the tail modestly longish (a bit shorter than that of House Wren). Wings heavily marked with pale spots/bars. The bird popped up twice, for 15-20 seconds each time, then wouldn't come out again. I did not see the bird's back well. 

Similar Species Discussion

Marsh Wren: solid cap, different colored sides, different call
House Wren: lacks distinct pale supercilium, has bolder eyering, no streaking on crown. HOWR has longer billed
