Description of the Bird

Small, mousy bird with habitually cocked tail in slender, decurved bill, clearly a wren. Maxilla mostly gray, mandible yellow. Dark eye with weak supercillium. Crown streaked with alternating light and dark brown stripes. Throat & upper breast pale gray. Flanks buffy reddish brown, feet & legs dull pink. Wings very checkered looking, including checkered scapulars. Back streaked with white-edged dark brown stripes. Tail short, barred, often cocked upright.
Behaviors: The bird responded to pre-recorded playback, emerging from tall wet grass into low, brushy Russian Olives where it called and sang.
Call: Bird sang and called in response to recorded playback. Calls and song the bird gave matched well with pre-recorded tracks.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

House Wren (also seen in the Russian Olives) browner overall, lacking crown stripes and bold wing checkering. Marsh Wren has bolder supercillium and lacks crown striping and checkering on scapulars. Voice a good match for Sedge Wren, not for other wrens.
