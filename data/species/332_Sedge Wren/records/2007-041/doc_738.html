the edge of a Pond with Cottonwoods and tall grasses

Description of the Bird

The bird was identical to the one in the Sibley guide
I have a few photos that are submitted
Behaviors: It moved very rapidly through the Cottonwood Trees. The bird made short flights staying primarily within three feet of the ground. As I got close to the bird it's tail would rise before it flew to another perch. I did see it on a number of occasions gleaning insects from the branches.
Call: It began with a few sharp chips then a rapid series of sticatto chips. The call was identical to the Recorded call (Stokes eastern I believe) that was played back.
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Prior to this sighting, I have seen all of the North American except this one. I have banded several house wrens and just a few days earlier there I had seen several Marsh Wrens at Lake Estes and it did not look like any wren that I had seen in the wild up to that point.

Resources Used

The Sibley Guide to Birds Page 388

Previous Experience

I have sen several Marsh Wren over the years and have banded dozens of House Wrens and this bird did not look lie or sound like either of those.
