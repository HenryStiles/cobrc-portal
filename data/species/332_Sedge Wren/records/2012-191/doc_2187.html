Description of the Bird

Short tailed, buffy wren, with streaked back and head. deep ochre buff (rather than cinnamon buff) flanks. Face rather plain in c/w Marsh Wren
Behaviors: Mostly skulking about in tall wet grass. Called vigorously in response to having Sedge Wren song played, and came in closer.
Call: Emphatic sweet chip, like a Lincoln's Sparrow or Fox Sparrow, with a superimposed squeaky quality.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Call unique, at least among wrens and sparrows. Marsh Wren with unstreaked crown, bolder face pattern, different overall coloring, esp on flanks. House Wren, unstreaked back and crown. Sparrows thicker billed
