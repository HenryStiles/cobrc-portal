Description of the Bird

Buffy bodied wren; weak, streaky lines of white and some black around the eye; streaking on head; white/whitish throat; heavy streaking on back, of black and white. 

Single-note calls a heavy "chat," consistent with Sedge Wren "chat_calls ND" in Sibley eGuide to Birds (Android). 

The bird was in some tall, thick (sedge?) grasses right along the trail, in a marshy collection of water; the grasses transitioned into cattails away from the trail. Though it popped up once long enough for the attached photos, it otherwise stayed down in the grasses, calling single notes occassionally and moving low within the grasses.  I did not hear it call from the cattails, only the grasses. 

Similar Species Discussion

The only competitor I considered was Marsh Wren, based on the habitat, vocalizations, and visual characteristics I had of the bird.  I ruled out Marsh Wren because the call did not have the "rattle" character of this species, the lack of a distinct eyeline, and the light buffy/white coloring of the breast and body.
