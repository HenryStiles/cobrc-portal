Description of the Bird

Size and shape indicated that this was either a Marsh Wren or Sedge Wren. In direct comparison to Marsh Wrens seen directly before and after this birds sighting , had a streaked crown not a flat brown crown , indistinct white supercilium after the eye , buffy flanks , some black and buffy barring on the wings , and a shorter tail.
Behaviors: Even more skulking than the Marsh Wrens. perched briefly on a cattail and then flew very short distance into denser cattails. No response to pishing or playing tape after this initial sighting.
Call: None given
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

A direct comparison with Marsh Wrens showed the differences with head streaking , supercilium, flank colouration , wing barring , and tail length.
