Description of the Bird

I had been repeatedly playing song of Sedge Wren attracting multiple MAWR and SOSPs. I'd stopped playing tape and heard Sedge Wren call 5 or 6 times. Call roughly like combo of a Dickcissel's flight calls and a Lincoln's Sparrow's chip -- thus, a buzzy and sweet chip. I played Sedge Wren song again, and a bird (SEWR/MAWR) flew away. I Mmved towards where landed and pished. Low in the sedgy growth a bird approached. The overall color was yellow-buff rather than chestnut-buff. The crown was finely streaked in white. The back was blackish streaked with white. The bird had a relatively weak pale buff supercilum. The barred tail was held cocked upwards most of the time. The throat was white and what I could see of the sides were yellow-buff. The bill was wren-like (ie, not like a sparrow). I continued to pish, the bird called twice more before flying 10 m or so away. Despite another 10-15 minutes of pishing and recordings, the bird did not respond again. 

Similar Species Discussion

Marsh Wren
MAWR are darker, with a basically solid crown. Their overall color is more red (chestnut) than yellow buff. MAWR have a white, or near white, rather than buff crown. MAWR have a rather different call.
