Habitat

Short sedge meadow, knee-high, with standing water, 1-2 inches of water and tall 6-8 feet tall bullrushes with standing water.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10x42 bins
Distance:  as close as 6 feet
Light:  good, sun overhead, bird low so not affected by glare.

Description of the Bird

This was a tan and light brown wren with a whitish throat. The bill was short, with light lower mandible and curved slightly downward. The tail and wings were heavily bared with buff and dark brown bars. The back had fine light streaks. The crown was without a strong pattern. Overall slender and small.
Behaviors: Flew out of knee-high sedges into the edge of 6' tall bullrushes where it chipped and skulked. I went into the rushes and it came as close as 6 feet where I could look down on it without binoculars.
Call: sharp chip notes from hiding spot in dense bullrushes
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Marsh Wren has longer bill, darker back with more contasty streaks and a different head pattern.
