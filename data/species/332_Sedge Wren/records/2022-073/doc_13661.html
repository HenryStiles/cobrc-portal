Description of the Bird

I first heard the bird calling along the South Platte River and immediately recognized it as a wren but was unsure of the species. Shortly after the bird hopped into view along and I realized I was looking at a Sedge Wren rather than any of the more expected species for Colorado in winter. 

The bird was identified as a Sedge Wren based on overall cinnamon brown color with streaked back and wings as well as a pale throat.  

Similar Species Discussion

Pacific/Winter Wren was quickly eliminated by longer tail and overall lighter brown color. 

Marsh Wren was eliminated based on extensive streaking on the back and nap as well as overall cinnamon color vs the darker brown overall color of Marsh Wren.

Additionally recordings of the bird's calls eliminate any other species.

Previous Experience

I have observed several Sedge Wrens in Texas along the gulf coast as well as a Vagrant  in Broomfield Colorado. I am very experienced with Marsh Wrens both in Colorado and other states.
