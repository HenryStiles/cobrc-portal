Description of the Bird

The bird was more reddish than I remember this species being, looking quite similar to the Pacific Wren in La Plata County. See photo showing throat, head and wings. Sibley's 2014 edition does not do this bird justice. The bird was found by Kathy Mihm Dunning downstream the day earlier (Feb 16). I walked up and down the river playing various call notes to try to get this skulker into the open. After approximately 80 minutes, I played one series of calls (Sibley #3 on phone app). I saw a bird fly in low and presumed it would be the bird - it was. It proceeded to fly around me, never more than 50 ft or so, calling it's song sparrow-like call note repeatedly for perhaps 20-30 minutes (see audio file). It also gave a call occasionally like that of the call I had played to bring it in. Ryan then arrived, and the bird was coming to within less than 10 ft of us, where he was able to get great views and hear the bird vocalize.

Similar Species Discussion

PAWR - eliminated by very different vocalizations.

Previous Experience

Winter wrens were fairly common, though rarely seen, in Michigan. The call notes were familiar to me. The Pacific Wren in Durango has given me experience with the call notes of PAWR.
