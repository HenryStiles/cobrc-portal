Description of the Bird

Peter Gent was the only person who saw this bird. He said it was not dark enough to be a Pacific Wren and it had flecking in the throat.

Gender is based on the fact that this bird was singing in July in suitable breeding habitat.
Behaviors: The bird was singing along a 50 m stretch of high mountain stream in July. There were numerous large downed trees and heavy underbrush.
Call: The identity is mainly based on recordings of this bird's song. It was more musical than the song of Pacific Wren.
Plumage: not observed

Similar Species Discussion

Pacific Wren was eliminated by comparing the recordings of both Pacific and Winter Wren on Nathan Pieplow's blog earbirding: http://earbirding.com/blog/archives/826

Resources Used
