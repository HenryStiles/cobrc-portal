Description of the Bird

A long bodied passerine bird (about 10 to 12 inches with the tail, but not more than a foot long) with a graduated tail. Bright rufous tail, back, wings,nape and head. Breast, belly and flanks dirty white to very pale yellowish; boldly streaked with dark brown. Throat brighter white and surrounded or bordered by a "collar" of broken vertical streaks. Bill dark, rather long and slightly curved at the tip. Eyes with bright yellow iris and black pupils. Some white on the sides of the face (but not a well defined pattern) on brownish background. One or maybe two very faint white wing bars. Undertail coverts appeared white and unstreaked.
Behaviors: Observed feeding on the ground with some "thrashing" behavior, but not very vigorous. It kept to the willows and skulked about on the ground or in the willows at a height of 5 to 6 feet and below. Flight was undulating or oscillating.
Call: Initially heard calling from across the CO river giving a loud two syllable call that I was completely unfamilar with. It was similar to a brief part of the song on the "Stokes Eastern Bird Songs" CD. It gave a loud "chuck" call as it landed low in dense willows behind me. This call matches one on the Stokes CD rather well.
Plumage: only one

Similar Species Discussion

Long-billed Thrasher is brown not rufous like this bird and has a gray face and streaked undertail coverts unlike this bird.
Bendire's Thrasher is dirty gray/brown not rufous and has small "cheveron shaped" spots on the breast and belly instead of streaks.
Curve-billed, Crissal, Le Conte's and California Thrashers are gray and have relatively longer bills and no streaks. California has dark iris.
Sage Thrasher is gray/brown above not rufous and are smaller with a much shorter bill
Wood Thrush's rufous color matches but the breast is spotted not streaked and it has a white ring and the bird in question did not. This thrush has a shorter tail than my bird and the thrush's tail is not graduated. Also the Wood Thrush has pink legs and my bird did not. Bill is all wrong
Veery is the right color but too small with the wrong bill shape and again spots on the breast.
Hermit Thrush has the correct color tail but its back, nape and head are not rufous, its bill is wrong and again the spotted breast as well as an eye ring.
Swainson's Thrush is not even close in any real manner
