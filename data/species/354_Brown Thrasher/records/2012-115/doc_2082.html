Viewing Conditions

Optics:  none used, bird was very close. Chose to watch with eyes only
Distance:  10-12 ft.
Light:  Clear day, sun was to my back. Bird was seen in full sun as it flew across the road. Was in full sun when it lit, briefly, on a branch.

Description of the Bird

Larger than the robins close by. Very rufous back, wings and and crown. Thin black and white wing bars. Long rufous tail. Yellow eye. Breast and belly thinly, but boldly streaked dark blackish brown on buffy almost white underparts. Streaks were more rufous on flanks and sides of neck. Very striking. Relatively long legged.
Behaviors: Bird was seen in flight with a flock of Robins. Robins may have spooked it up. It flew across the road in front of me and lit very briefly in the open. Then it dove deep into the undergrowth.
Call: Bird was not vocalizing
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Bird was seen with American Robins. Quickly eliminated those because of color, and tail length.
Similarly patterned Wood Thrush would be very rare here. Lacked the boldly patterned face of Wood Thrush. Bird in question had a Yellow eye, Wood Thrush has a dark eye. Wood thrush would have shorter bill. Wood Thrush would appear "spotted" not streaked.

Long billed Thrasher eliminated due to rareness of that species and bold Rufous coloration of Brown Thrasher. Long-billed Thrasher would have very gray face and the streaking would have been blacker...Long-billed would have lacked the rufous streaking on sides of breast.

Eliminated from Sage Thrasher as that bird would not have been Rufous, is not boldly streaked and has a shorter bill and shorter tail. Sage Thrasher would have been smaller than the American Robins close by. This bird was a little larger than the robins.
