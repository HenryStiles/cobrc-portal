Description of the Bird

I am reporting this bird as GLAUCOUS-WINGED x HERRING GULL, not pure glaucous-winged.

The bird was large -- similar in length to nearby herring gulls. It had a bulky body; large, flat-topped head; and large, mostly dark bill. Plumage was pale overall with darker patterning. The darkest color in the folded primaries was similar to the darkest color in the checkered pattern on the main body. The colors captured by my camera vary among the photos; I would consider the photo with the glaucous-winged x herring gull in front of a herring gull most representative of the colors my eyes saw.

The indicators that the bird was a hybrid with herring gull include (1) the prominent patterning on the wings and (2) patches of somewhat lighter color in the mostly-black bill.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding on dead fish along with about 150 other gulls (ring-billed, herring, and Thayer's).
Call: unknown
Plumage: first winter

Similar Species Discussion

Pure glaucous-winged gull was eliminated based on the prominent wing markings and hints of lighter color on the bill.

Pure herring gull was eliminated based on the pale plumage overall.
