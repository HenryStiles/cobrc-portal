Description of the Bird

A large, hooded oporonis (formerly) with a complete white eyering popped up in front of me briefly.   It flew into the smartweed that was growing a foot off the ground, concealing the ground.  I thought it MAY be a young Mourning Warbler based on my initial brief look of the complete eyering.  It struck me as not being a MacGillivray's Warbler.  I got the attention of Mlodinow and Larson and told them what I saw, indicating where I thought it was.  We converged on the spot and it popped up again whithin view of all 3 of us.  It was large, long and with a large bill.  It was yellow underneath and appeared to have a brownish-gray hood.  The throat looked paler than the rest of the hood, a buffy-brown color.  The eyering was thick, white and complete, giving the bird a large-eyed appearance.  While looking at the bird, Steve Larson noted outloud, "the bird has very long undertail coverts, reaching to near the tip of the tail".  Steve Mlodinow excalimed "Connecticut Warbler".  The bird flew low into the smartweed at the base of a tree closer to the canal.  We approached it agin and again it popped up on a low branch giving us another view for a few seconds before it flew a great distance down the canal and was not seen again, even though we tried to find it. We all compare our observations/notes and agreed on the bird's ID, concluding it was a 1st year bird, based on the color of the hood.  

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler was eliminated by the complete, white eyering, color of the hood (brownish-gray vs. gray) and overal size.  1st year Mourning Warbler was eliminated by the hood color, thickness of the complete white eyering, and size and shap of the bird.  The extended undertail made it look like a banana.  The bird was noteably larger than Wilson's Warbler and Common Yellowthroat that it was seen with.
