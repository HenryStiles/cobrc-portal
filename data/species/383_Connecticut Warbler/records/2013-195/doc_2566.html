Description of the Bird

I'd been pishing for a while and a nice collection of birds had gathered around. Glenn was headed towards me, as was Steve L, with some willows in between us. Glenn called out that he had an interesting warbler, possibly a Mourning, possibly something else, and that it was in the willows betwixt us.... which was about 15 feet from any one of the three of us. Steve L had a good look at the vent, and noted that the undertail coverts stretched nearly to the end of the tail. I could see the bird skulking in the willows for several minutes, getting brief tantalizing glimpses here and there... Then it popped up in front of me, in plain view, for about 15-20 seconds. Having seen many in my youth, Connecticut Warbler popped to mind immediately.

This was a large "Oporornis" with a large, fairly extensively pinkish, bill... larger than that of a Mourning or MacGillivray's Warbler. The bird had a complete hood that was grayish (though not as pure gray as that of Mourning or MacG), including a complete bib. The throat was tan-buffy, contrasting with lower portions of bib. The bird had a moderate thickness, complete white eyering bolder than I've ever seen in a Mourning Warbler. There was no supra-loral stripe. The underparts were yellow, but not vivid yellow, with olive or olive-brown sides. The upperparts were olive-brown. The bird was on the same branch as a male Common Yellowthroat and wad distinctly larger than that bird.

Given the distinct gray on hood, the bird should be an adult female per Dunn and Garrett's Warbler book, though other texts state that immature males can appear this way.

Similar Species Discussion

Mourning Warbler would've had smaller and differently shaped bill, brighter underparts, not as bold eyering.. and I've never seen a MOWA that quite matches the bib/throat pattern of this bird. The very long undertail coverts seen by Steve L also eliminate Mourning Warbler. Many imm MOWAs (the ones with yellow throats), though not all, have a pale supra-loral stripe. 

MacG even shorter undertail coverts, eyering arcs

Nashville Warbler very different shape, no bib, other marks
