Description of the Bird

Very large, plump warbler. Full eyering is mostly white, but buff tan/yellow in front area above upper mandible.  Large, bi-colored bill, with darker upper mandible and lighter lower mandible.  Tannish-Brown hood, much lighter buff/tan throat.  Breast and Belly bright yellow.  Olive-gray back and upperparts.  Wings more gray with olive edging.  No subloral line.   UTC reach more than half way to tip of tail, creating appearance of short tail.  Caught in mist net #6 on 8:10am net run at Bird Conservancy of the Rockies Barr Lake State Park Banding Station.  

Similar Species Discussion

Common Yellowthroat eliminated based on measurements of wing, tail, primaries and review of measurements in Pyle and based on overall size, size of bill and bi-colored bill, bi-colored eyering, lack of pale area in lower belly.   

Mourning Warbler elimated based on measurements of wing, tail, primaries and review of measurements in Pyle and based on size of bill and bi-colored bill, bi-colored eyering, and long UTC and short tail projection.
