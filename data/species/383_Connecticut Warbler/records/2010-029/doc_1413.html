Description of the Bird

This bird was easily separated as one of the three look-alike Oporornis Warblers by its gray hood, yellow underparts and olive-green back. Specifically, this bird had a very long bill that was dusky on top and pink of the "lower mandible". The legs were dull pink. The light gray face gradually became greenish distal to the bird's nape. The white eye ring was very noticeable. (I saw the bird in the hand for about a minute for close examination). The yellow undertail coverts were examined and they almost reached the end of the bird's tail.
Behaviors: I refound the bird about an hour after it had been banded and released. It was seen over 5 minutes moving sluggishly from 3-7 feet off the ground, not really feeding, but moving to stay in cover, or so it appeared. At one time it walked a few steps on a horizontal branch. I also saw it fly (a second time) into a msit net. The bird's behavior then changed dramatically.
Call: non heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler - there were lots of this species in the area but not in the olives where the CONW was located. Mac Warblers have split white eye arcs, with gaps on both sides creating an upper arc and a lower arc. The males of that species have a dark gray bib, unlike the medium gray that extended to the yellow belly on the CONW. The undertail coverts of the MAC do not come close to the length of the CONW undertail coverts.

Mourning Warbler - similar to MAC but with no eye arcs and no eye ring like in CONW. The Mourning Warbler face is significantly darker than the CONW face and the bib terminus is much darker than the CONW bib. The undertail coverts do not approach the length of the CONW under tail.
