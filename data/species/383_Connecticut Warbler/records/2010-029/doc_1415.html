Description of the Bird

The bird was a quite large, chunky warbler that was about 6 inches long. The bird had a dark gray hood, which extended down onto the upper breast, and a very conspicuous, complete white eye ring. The whole upper part of the bird was a uniform light greeny brown, and the whole breast, flanks and the under tail was a bright yellow. The bill was quite large for a warbler and the lower mandible was light. The legs were a bright flesh color. The length of the under tail coverts was not noted.
Behaviors: The bird often stayed quite still for several minutes. It did appear to forage occasionally, and flew around to different branches of the Russian Olive 5-6 times. One time after it flew to another thicker branch, it then walked along the branch for 3-4 feet.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Male MacGillivray's Warbler has a darker hood and a split eye ring, and is much darker to blackish on the upper breast. Alternate male Mourning Warbler has a darker hood and mostly no eye ring. It is even blacker on the upper breast than MacGillivray's. Connecticut is larger, but this was very difficult to assess without a direct comparison with the others.
