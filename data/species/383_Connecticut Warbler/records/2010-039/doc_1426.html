Description of the Bird

A large yellow-green and yellow warbler with a complete, even-width, white eye ring. Dorsally, from the undertail coverts forward, the bird was yellow. The yellow on the belly gave way to light gray on the upper chest and throat with a clear demarcation. The throat and head were the same shade of gray and gave a hooded appearance. Ventrally, from the crown back, the gray of the head blended smoothly with the green-yellow back. The leg color was not noted and neither was the tail projection.
Behaviors: The bird was originally found when it flew low (1-2 in above the ground) through dense undergrowth and alighted on a low branch (< 6 in above the ground). The bird sat motionless on the branch giving clear views less than 20 ft from me. The bird dropped to the ground and began walking, Ovenbird like, while searching the leaf litter on the ground. The bird repeated this behavior of dropping to the ground, walking and then perching low (< 6 in) and sitting motionless. During my observation, the bird never flew higher than 6 inches above the ground, and when it flushed across the path and into the dry marsh, it never flew higher than about 3 ft. The bird was silent during the entire length of my observation, only vocalizing when it flushed across the path.
Call: A short call note was given once or twice as the bird flushed. The call note was a "chip" or "cheep" note similar to the call note of MacGillivray's Warbler, but considerably less harsh. Aside from the call note, the bird was silent.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Both Spring Male MacGillivary's and Mourning Warblers should show darker gray to black at the transition from chest to throat. This transition in the individual I observed was even-toned and gray with no darker markings. In addition, MacGillivray's Warbler never shows a complete eye ring (Dunn and Garrett, 1997, pg 485).

First Spring Female Mourning Warbler with a strong eye ring would look similar, but should have yellow on the throat. The individual I observed had a gray throat.

Nashville Warbler has a complete white eye ring but a yellow throat and is much more active and usually feeds higher off the ground.

Female Common Yellowthroats can show a thin complete eye ring, but also show yellow throats and white bellies. The individual I observed did not have a yellow throat and showed continuous yellow from undertail coverts to chest.

The behavior of walking on the ground is particular to Connecticut Warbler amongst the species discussed above. The birds overall silence is also suggestive of Connecticut Warbler.
