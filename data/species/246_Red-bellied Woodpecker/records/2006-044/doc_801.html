Description of the Bird

A relatively large woodpecker and typical in shape and profile of other Melanerpes. The bill as rather long, black, and chisel-shaped. The nasal area was composed of puffy orange feathers contrasting with a gray crown. The rear crown and nape were red. The rest of the face, throat, breast, belly, and flanks were buffy-white. The mantle and wings were black basally with extensive white bars laterally defined across the entire region. The buffy-white undertail coverts were scaled black. The uppertail coverts were white and the tail was mostly balck, except for interrupted white along the central and outer rectrices.

I was able to obtain poor photographs of the bird, but that are diagnostic for the species.
Behaviors: Perched in Siberian Elm. Occasionally chiseled at bark.
Call: Gave a sharp, loud kwirrr call.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Gila Woodpecker differs by having a red crown, lacks red on the nape, and has barred uppertail coverts. Golden-fronted Woodpecker has a yellow nasal area, has an orange nape, and lacks white in the tail. Red-headed Woodpecker has an all red head and completely black upperparts.
