Description of the Bird

Medium-sized woodpecker (larger than Downy, smaller than flicker; about the size of Hairy), with finely barred black and gray upperparts, gray underparts, pale red wash on lower belly, gray head with bright red nape and hind-crown, pale red rictal tuft above a straight black bill; bill about an inch long; tail and rump mottled black and white.
Behaviors: Climbing on tree trunks
Call: call a repeated "chuck"
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Golden-fronted Woodpecker has yellow not red on head, clean white rump and clean black tail.
