Description of the Bird

An obvious "Centurus" woodpecker, with black-and-white barred upperparts, reddish-orange nasal tufts, gray forehead and forecrown, red rearcrown and nape, grayish-white underparts (I could not see the center of the belly), with a black tail with the central rectrices being barred black and white.
Behaviors: foraging on deciduous trees
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Golden-fronted Woodpecker (at least US populations) has an entirely black tail (except for the outer web of r6) and a yellow patch on the rearcrown and nape
