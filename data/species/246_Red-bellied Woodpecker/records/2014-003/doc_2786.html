Description of the Bird

Female, approximately 9 inches. Heavily barred back and wings. Relatively long-necked with fairly long bill. Red nape (actually it's orange...but the books all say red). Small orangish/red patch (nasal tufts) where bill meets face.  Rest of head (face)  and underparts pale. Speckled  rump with mostly white central tail feathers. Only occasionally were we able to observe slight reddish wash on the belly.

Bird fed actively during it's almost three month long visit, would often commandeer the suet feeder. She and the male Ladder-backed, which arrived a few weeks later, were often present at the same time.

Please note: report is being done from notes taken at the time and from memory.

Similar Species Discussion

Told from Northern Flickers, which were also present by size, overall paler coloring, much more heavily barred back, lack of black on neck and clean underparts, and no red wash on underwings.

Previous Experience

This was a life bird for us at the time. We were familiar with Hairy Woodpeckers, Downy Woodpeckers and Northern Flickers, which were all present at the feeders during the time the Red-bellied Woodpecker was present.
