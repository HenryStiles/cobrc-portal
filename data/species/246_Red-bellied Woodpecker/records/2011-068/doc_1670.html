Habitat

Feeding in cottonwoods (Populus sp.). Overall area was wet riparian with a pond, small creek, and feeders.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Alpen 8x42 binoculars, Canon 40D with 400mm fixed lens.
Distance:  30-50' Estimated.
Light:  Light was good, though shady. Bird was unobscured by vegetation.

Description of the Bird

Typical woodpecker shape. Bill longish, stout, and black. Head beige-gray-brownish with orangish above bill and on chin and backside of head (nape area). Breast/belly same color as head (without the orange highlights). Back/wings black with horizontal white stripes. Undertail coverts white with black centers of feathers.
Behaviors: Hopping along tree parallel to trunk or branch in typical woodpecker fashion, pecking at the bark occasionally. Would also fly between different trees on occasion.
Call: Did not vocalize.
Plumage: adult
