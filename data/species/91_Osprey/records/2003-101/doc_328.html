Description of the Bird

A large, long-winged raptor, the bird had been present for some time at Pueblo Reservoir prior to the Christmas Bird Count. The bird was basically white below and brown above, except that it had a distinct and wide brown eyeline, some white creeping onto the crown from the superciliary, and some brown mottling on the chest. The wings were held with the wrists forward and elevated, creating a distinct 'M' shape in two different planes. There was the suggestion of pale fringing on the primary coverts and buff mottling in the black wrist patch that both suggest a bird in worn Juvenal plumage. The tail was thinly banded with black and white.
Behaviors: flying

Plumage: probably Juvenal
