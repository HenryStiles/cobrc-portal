Description of the Bird

This was a bright orange bird with two distinctive white wing bars on dark wings. It was somewhat smaller than a robin - it was obviously an oriole. The head, face, underparts, and tail were orange. The malar area was a really bright orange. There was black between the eye and bill which then extended some way down onto the throat. The back was a dull orange with distinct streaks. The eyes were black and the legs gray. The bill was mostly gray, with a small black patch on the mandible, see attached photograph. Experts Chris Wood and A Jamarillo say it is an adult female because the wear on the primaries and tail was uniform, whereas a first-year bird would have had non-uniform wear.
Behaviors: Sitting in a pine tree and regularly coming to feed.
Call: none heard
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

The only other orangish orioles with streaked backs that occur in North America are first-year Bullock's and Baltimore Orioles. This bird had no black line behind the eye, a very orange malar region, and the lower breast and flanks were orange, not gray as in a first-year male Bullock's Oriole. A young Baltimore Oriole does not have the face pattern of this bird.
