Description of the Bird

Clearly an oriole. Icterid bill, distinctly thic at base, pointy at tip; thicker-based that Bullock's Oriole bill (per photos and memory). Bill bicolored; basal half or two-thirds of lower mandible contrastingly silver; rest of lower mandible and upper mandible dull dark horn. From any distance, bird looks mostly orange. Belly clear lemony-yellow, becoming more suffused with orange as you move towards bill. Brightest orange in malar region adjacent to black of mask. Crown and back an olive faintly suffused with orangey. Slightly greener on crown, grayer on back. Flight feathers and coverts dull grayish, each with thin but distinct white edgings of even thickness, except slightly wider edgins on greater coverts, forming a fairly distinct upper wingbar. Shortest tertial also has a barely wider edge. Undertail coverts sligtly brighter yellow; small grayish area in ventral region. Back feathers each have thin dark distinct central streaks. Black mask connects eye to base of bill, does not encircle eye; top and bottom edges of eye bordered by orange feathering. Black feathering extends down along base of bill and continues down chin a short ways. Black continues behind eye barely in a super-thin point. Looks like it extends farther back than it really does due to its blending with the crease between feather tracts. Eyes black. Tail dull olive on top, with orange-yellow suffusion all the way down feather edges. No brownish cast to the tail in my view. Legs dull gray horn.
Behaviors: Feeding from ground and platform feeders; sometimes sitting in spruce interior for long periods.
Call: Bird was not heard to vocalize.
Plumage: first winter

Similar Species Discussion

Bullock's Oriole is more expected. It should have a thinner bill, without the strong contrastingly silvery base; it shouldn't have the faint but very precise streaking on the back; it should not be quite as strongly orange on the cheeks or the belly; and its black mask should extend farther behind the eye.
