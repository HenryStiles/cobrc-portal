Description of the Bird

This bird has all the field marks of a Streak-backed Oriole; Dark orange, almost reddish malar, finely streaked back, bright orange belly goes all the way down past the legs, bright orange/yellow undertail coverts. Deep black mask with no line behind the eye. Lower mandible dark tipped, beak large. Short primary projection.White tipped lesser and greater coverts.
Behaviors: This bird was first seen eating roasted peanut halves. Over the course of the last 3 days it has tried every food at the feeder, eating mostly grape jelly, suet pellets and peanuts. Roosts deep in a spruce tree or juniper tree. Likes feeding when feeders are not too busy.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: First Year

Similar Species Discussion

My first thought was Bullock's Oriole, but I had no time before work to follow up. Until Rachel mentioned SBOR, then I looked in the book and realized what it was. I had been thinking it was way to bright looking for a Bullock's.
