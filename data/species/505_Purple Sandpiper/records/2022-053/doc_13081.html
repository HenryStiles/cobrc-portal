Habitat

Mud flats at first on the west side of Lake Henry, then on the rocks at close range.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10 x 42 Leica binoculars and camera and Leica spotting scope
Distance:  20 to 100 meters
Light:  overcast and cold and breezy

Description of the Bird

Clearly Purple Sandpiper and not a Rock Sandpiper, see photos.

Similar Species Discussion

Rock Sandpiper would have buffy tones on the breast, which this bird didn't have.

Resources Used

Looked at shorebird books, to make sure it wasn't a Rock Sandpiper, though was sure while watching it, that it was a Purple Sandpiper.

Previous Experience

Saw the adult Purple Sandpiper in Colorado in Summit County, also seen quite a few in MA, NH, and ME.

I have only seen Rock Sandpiper on St. Paul Island in Alaska, haven't seen one in the Lower 48.
