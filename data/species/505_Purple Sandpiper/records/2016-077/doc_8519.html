Description of the Bird

The bird was observed foraging along a rocky shoreline and ice edge, occasionally working its way up a small creek where it would become obscured by snow. This was a rather stout and rotund shorebird similar in size to a Killdeer. Its legs were short and orange, and the bill was tapered with a slight droop near the end. Bill color was dark with a dull orange base. There was a fair amount of streaking and spotting on the lower part of its breast and flanks. Head and neck was gray with a small amount of white around the eye, while the back was more of a grayish-brown. The narrow white stripe on the posterior part of its wing was visible when it made short flights along the shoreline.

Similar Species Discussion

Dunlin was eliminated due to the coloration of body, bill length/structure, and the leg and bill color (Dunlin lacks the orange bill base and legs).
Rock Sandpiper would be overall lighter in coloration, and the bill base and leg color would be more of a dull yellow, along with the fact that the circumstances seem far better fit for a PUSA.
Surfbird eliminated by probability, bill structure (stout), and tail pattern
