Description of the Bird

Small sandpiper, about 8 inches in length (+/-), dark grey head, dark eye, black bill changing to orange at the base (more so on upper mandible than bottom) with slight decurve downwards.  Dark grey back, with slightly darker grey wings, progressing to near black on primaries.  Dark grey coverts with slight light gray border.  Grey upper breast progressing to white with grey spots on lower breast extending to flanks and belly.  White undertail.  Dull orange legs and feet.

Bird was working shallow water along mud flats at inflow of small creek.

Similar Species Discussion

More likely birds for Summit county:


Spotted Sandpiper - Structure of bird more stocky than Spotted Sandpiper.  Dark upper chest wrong for Spotted Sandpiper.  Overall, this bird was far darker grey than a Spotted would be.


Least Sandpiper - Bird was larger than expected for Least.  Dark breast and spotting on flanks wrong for Least. Bill and leg color incorrect as well.


Solitary Sandpiper - Structure of bird more stocky than Solitary Sandpiper.  Dark upper chest wrong for Solitary Sandpiper.  Overall, this bird was far darker grey than a Solitary would be.  Bill and leg color incorrect as well.




Less likely birds for Summit county:


Black Turnstone - Bird was not as sooty as a trunstone, Orange on billis wrong for Black Turnstone. 


Dunlin - Bird was darker than a Dunlin, with darker upper breast.  Bill and leg color wrong for Dunlin.
