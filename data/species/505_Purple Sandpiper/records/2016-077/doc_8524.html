Habitat

Along the Blue River where it enters Dillon Reservoir

Description of the Bird

Chunky medium-sized sandpiper about the size and general shape of a Dunlin; primary coloration on the upperparts and breast a soft medium gray; scapular feather centers had a rusty-brown tinge; flight feathers and coverts edged in white; underparts basally white with flanks and belly streaked/spotted with same gray coloration as upperparts; vent area mostly white with a few gray spots; tail solidly gray and extending past wingtips; bill modestly long and only very slightly curved, orange coloration obvious at the base of the bill then blending and fading to black along its length to the bill tip; dark eye; whitish complete eyering, loral spot, and vague eyebrow. legs orange.  The bird was foraging within the stream by probing its bill into the stream's substrate bed.
