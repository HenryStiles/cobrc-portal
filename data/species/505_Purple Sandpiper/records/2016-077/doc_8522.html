Description of the Bird

A medium sized shorebird about the size of a Dunlin.  Completely dark gray head and upper breast, black eye with a thin light eyering.  Medium sized bill that was quite bright orange at the base and dark at the tip.  Dark gray upperparts, but with light edging to the feathers.  Lighter from the middle of the breast to the tail, but with lines of darker chevrons from the middle of the breast to the legs. Quite bright orange legs and feet. Bird was feeding along the edge of the reservoir and in the creek. The bird did not call.

Similar Species Discussion

Much darker than a winter plumaged Dunlin.
Not as large or chunky as a Surfbird, and a considerably longer bill.
Chevrons on breast and orange base to bill compared to Black Turnstone.
Much darker than most other winter shorebird species.
Difficult to tell from Rock Sandpiper.  National Geographic guide says Purple bill base and legs are orange-yellow, whereas Rock has greenish-yellow bill base and legs. Sibley guide also says Purple Sandpiper has distinctly orange-tinged bill base and legs.  This is the main field mark used to separate these species.  Another smaller clue is that ebird shows no Rock Sandpiper reports away from the west coast, whereas Purple Sandpiper clearly wanders inland every so often.
