Description of the Bird

This was a large sparrow that is bright rufous on the tail, primaries, secondaries, tertials and greater coverts. The coloration abrubtly changed to slate gray on the bird's primary coverts, median coverts, mantle nape, crown, and sides of face. The bill was strong and a light gray (except in one photo it has a yellow cast). The crown was peaked on the back, and the breast had a background of whitish with wide arrow-shaped dark brown streaks which continued down onto the flanks. There was a large prominent stick pin dot in the upper breast and the lower belly remained unstreaked. The legs and feet appeared dull pinkish. There were very thin and faint whitish wing bars present.
Behaviors: The bird was first heard in a deep tangle of tumble weed and dense Russian olives. It came out and perched to investigate my pishing and perched about 5 feed off the ground but still in the tangle.

When I played tape of western fox sparrow song, the bird came in imediately but only presented itself for brief periods of time, retreating into the brush piles and chipping. After more song playback the Fox Sparrow responded with 5 or sing phrases of its song.
Call: yes heard both call and song. Call loud a bit Canyon Towhee-like. Song responding to tape of western fox sparrow
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Song Sparrow is the closest look alike species. This bird was significantly larger than the White-crowned Sparrows with which it foraged. Song Sparrow never has the all gray face that this bird had. This bird is stocky unlike the more delicate Song Sparrow. This bird was round-headed and not delicate like the whimpy Song Sparrow.

This was most likely one of the Slate-colored Song Sparrows based on coloration. It was not a Sooty Fox Sparrow which doesn't have the contrasty dorsal surface that this bird had. Likewise it was not a Red Fox Sparrow or Canadian Rocky Mountain Fox Sparrow which always show red on the face and reddish brown breast streaks. Also, based on bill size it was not a Thick-billed Fox Sparrow which has a very large bill compared to the just large bill of this bird.
