Description of the Bird

On 30 May and 2 June, I encountered relatively large numbers of ibis at Lower Latham and Beebe Draw. Some odd-balls, I chose to photograph rather than write-up a description. I can say that on this bird, the face pattern seemed to show lines like a Glossy (not in color, but the presence of pale lines on the skin) plus a very limited amount of pale feathering. The bird seemed intermediate in coloring. So, I photographed it.
Behaviors: feeding in wet grass
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The eye in the field did not seem as obviously red. The white feathering around the face seems far to limited for a WFIB and the facial skin duller than it should be on a WFIB. However, the pinkish hues around the facial feathering and on the facial lines are not normal for GLIB, and the red in the eye seems to rule out that species as well. To me, this bird seems to show features that both species lack, and in other characters, an intermediate appearance.
