Description of the Bird

The bird was first found by homeowner John Ealy on April 25, and seen by him on April 26, and by John and me on April 27. The oriole is a brilliant yellow with black masked face, wide black bib, long bill, black tail, and a strong white wingbar. It was about as long as a starling but not nearly as chunky. John saw the bird just before I arrived on April 26. After it failed to return for 90 minutes, I played a hooded oriole song, which it responded to twice.  I heard it call "veek," which sounded close to the hooded oriole call on the Sibley app. 

Similar Species Discussion

Orchard oriole considered, but the color of this bird is more vibrant. It is smaller. It has distinct white wing patches. I posted a pix of this bird on FB and the ID was confirmed by Mark Stackhouse, a Mexican birding guide, and Greg Miller, a top ABA big year birder.
