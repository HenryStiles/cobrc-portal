Description of the Bird

The bird was about the size and shape of a Townsend's Solitaire.  The crown, head and underparts were a bright yellow tending to hints of orange in the cheek and upper breast.  The chin, front part of the ears and very upper breast were black, and the black included the eye.  The bill was black of medium lemgth and slightly downcurved.  There was a large white upper wing bar/patch and a much thinner wingbar below.  The rest of the back was black, although when it was very close there were still some pale edges on the feathers of the upper back, which I took to be remnants of the winter plumage.  The tail was quite long and totally black.  I failed to notice the leg color.  The bird hopped around in the scrub oak, but did not go to the feeders, where John Ealy said it was going.  I did not hear it make any sounds.

Similar Species Discussion

Bullock's and Baltimore Orioles were eliminated by the different face patterns and straight bills that they have.  Orchard Oriole was eliminated because of the head pattern, yellower than the first year male, and this bird was larger than an Orchard Oriole.  Streak-backed Oriole was eliminated by the upper back being black, not streaked, the black extended farther back onto the ears than in Streak-backed and the bill was curved and not straight. This bird was much smaller than Altamira and Spot-breasted, and Scott's and Audubon's Orioles are yellow, not yellow/orange.

Resources Used
