Description of the Bird

Bird had been noted for 4 days by home owner (John Ealy), as he reported to us. Medium sized slender yellow to orange bird who was an obvious oriole, with black on face and chin, and black on back, making striking contast to yellowish orange body.  Two wing bars, with upper broader, and additional white streaking on wings. . Black tail.  Stronger and larger bill than would be expected in an Orchard Oriole.  Bird did not call. First seen when it flew into a large chamisa, where the bird stayed "hunkered in" for a few moments before flying to some evergreen trees.  It spontaneously perched up in one of the trees, where it was entirely visible.  We never saw it going to feeders in homeowners yard.

Similar Species Discussion

Facial markings excluded both Bullock's and Baltimore Orioles. More orange color and more extensive black on face different than expected in Orchard Oriole, as well as longer, more decurved bill. Black on back also consistent with Hooded Oriole.  Altamira Oriole would have not been expected, but this bird lacked orange median coverts, ruling that possibility out.  Back solid black, ruled out Strak-backed oriole. Bird had been topic of discussion on CFO's rare bird alert.

Resources Used

Knowledge of usual orioles in our area, and tips from other birders, including home owner and discussion on rare bird alert.  Only oriole encountered on the trip, although we saw it for 15 minutes, we were in the area for 70 minutes total.

Previous Experience

Limited personal experience with this species, although we had seen male birds in Portal Arizona ~6 years ago. We have seen numerous Orchard Orioles of all plumages in Colorado, and recently in Tennessee.
