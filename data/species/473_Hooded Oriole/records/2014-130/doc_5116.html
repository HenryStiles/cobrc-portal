Description of the Bird

No calls were given. The oriole came to jelly feeders and hummingbird feeders and took a bath in the water feature. The oriole was quite yellow on the breast as compared to the common Bullock's Orioles in our yard. The long decurved dark bill looked quite different than the bill of Bullock's Orioles feeding at the same jelly feeders. The Bullock's Oriole bill is straight and not as dark. The Hooded Oriole is a little smaller when we were able to directly compare the size of the two oriole species.

Similar Species Discussion

Orchard Oriole female is brighter yellow, has a samller bill and is overall a smaller oriole. The female Bullock's Oriole has a gray area from the chest to the belly, a straight bill and is slightly larger than the Hood Oriole when having a direct comparison as we did have at the feeders. The female Baltimore Oriole could be eliminated for the same reasons are the Bullock's Oriole.
Other oriole species that could be found in western Colorado can also be eliminated by either size (Scott's Oriole female) or field marks (Streaked-backed Oriole).

Resources Used

We had most field guides open and had several opportunites to study the oriole as it made multiple visits to the feeders over the two day period. National Geo and Sibley's may have been the most useful but also looked at many photos on the web of all oriole species found in North and Central America.

Previous Experience

Brenda and I have seen most all North, Central and South American oriole species in a numberous travels in the Americas. We are very familar with Hooded and Orchard Orioles.
