Description of the Bird

Bird was a medium-small oriole with a thin, longish, slightly decurved bill, mostly black with base of lower mandible silver. Its tail was rather long for an oriole and appeared entirely black. It had a black back and black wings with a solid white wingbar on the greater coverts and some other white feather edgings that were poorly defined. It had a black throat patch that was much more extensive than that of the many Bullock's Orioles in the area, stretching down to the upper breast and up to include the eye. The forehead, crown, nape, rump and the remainder of the underparts were bright orange, roughly the same color as on the male Bullock's Orioles.
Behaviors: Bird fed actively in the trees and flowering bushes around the farmhouse, ranging from quite high in the cottonwoods to as low as 8 feet or so in the bushes.
Call: The bird was heard to call once clearly: it made a short, ca. 0.5 sec, rising "zeep," mostly clear-toned with a slightly metallic quality. I also heard a snippet of the bird's song at one point. I did not hear enough of it or hear it clearly enough to describe it well, but it seemed to be slightly weaker, faster and more varied than the simultaneous song of the nearby Bullock's Oriole.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was bright orange, which eliminated female Orchard Oriole, as did its black back. The long tail, decurved bill, black back and limited white in the wing eliminated Streak-backed Oriole.
