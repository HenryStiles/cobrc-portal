Description of the Bird

This oriole appeared very yellowish overall with an orange wash on the head, nape and breast. Black back with a yellowish-orange rump. Yellowish underparts. Black bill, long and slightly decurved. Black patch on lower face extending to eyes and down over the throat. Long black tail. Black wings with two narrow white wing-bars. Legs grayish.
Behaviors: Foraging in an Elm tree.
Call: silent
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Altamira Oriole is a bigger bird with a thicker, straighter and shorter bill. Altamira also has an orange, wedge-shaped patch on the wing and a smaller black mask on the face that does not extend as far over the eyes, lores and auriculars.
