Description of the Bird

It was yellowish orange, with black wings, throat, and tail. The black on the throat extended to the eye and just above the bill. A large top white wing bar and a small bottom wing bar. The black on the back extends to the back of the neck.
Behaviors: It was usually hidden in some deciduous trees, but it would occasionally sit in an open spot in the trees.
Call: Didn't hear it call or sing.
Plumage: breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Bullock's Oriole - There is a black eye line, black throat, black on the head extending down the back of the neck, black back, and a partial black tail. A large white wing bar. The orange on the bird is a darker orange than the Hooded Oriole.
Altimira Oriole - Orange bird, including orange on the median coverts. Black throat and black lores. Thick short bill. Black on the back, wings, and tail.
Spot-breasted Oriole - Orange bird. Black on the throat extends up to the black lores, but there is a distinct split. Black spots on an orange breast. Black tail. White on the tertial edges.
Streak-backed Oriole - Dark orange bird almost reddish. Two distinctive white wing bars. Orange back with dark streaks extending to the back of the neck. Black on the throat extends to the eye. Black tail. Grayish colored flanks.
