Description of the Bird

the bird was a small oriole, about the same size as nearby Bullock's Orioles. The bill was fairly short, conical with a thin, pointed tip, and a slight decurve. The base of the bill, especially the lower mandible, was blue-gray. The culmen and outer half of the bill was black. The eye was dark. I did not see the leg color.

The cap, nape, and most the head of the bird was a yellowish-orange, which would vary depending on the light from a fairly yellowish color with only a slight orange tinge, to an intense orange with a slight yellowish tinge, approaching the color of nearby Bullock's Orioles, but always with a more purely orange color and no red tinge. The face of the bird, from about the eye and lores to the upper chest were black, forming a black bib. The remainder of the underparts were yellowish-orange, with the same variation as the orange part of the head, though less intense, especially on the lower underparts.

The back was black. The wings were also black, with a singe large white wingbar, and with some white edges to the coverts and flight feathers. The rump and uppertail coverts were orange, and the tail was black.
Behaviors: The bird was seen on both occasions high in a cottonwood tree, and did not seem to be associating with other orioles in the area.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species of Oriole, including young males of Orchard, Streak-backed, and Bullock's were ruled out by the orangish color with black bib, combined with a black back. Furthermore, Bullock's Orioles were ruled out by the lack of a black eye-line and more extensive bib, as well as a single and smaller white wingbar. The wingbar pattern also ruled out Streak-backed Oriole, though the solid black back was a more important difference.

Altamira Oriole was ruled out by the lack of an orange shoulder patch, more extensive black on the bib, especially below the eye, and the less intense orange color, especially in certain lights. Smaller size, and thinner bill were also used to eliminate Altamira Oriole.
