Description of the Bird

The bird was a similar size/shape to the double-crested cormorants nearby, but with some different features. The bird had a buffy head and neck, down to its ruff (indicating a female), as well as white dots and streaking along its wings. It was perched in a dead branch at the edge of a pond, spreading its wings. 

Similar Species Discussion

Double-crested cormorant was eliminated because this bird had a distinct buffy collar and head/neck, as well as white wing streaks/dots. Juvenile cormorant eliminated because the line between buffy and black on breast would have been more indistinct. Beak also pointier than cormorants. 

Previous Experience

I am very familiar with double-crested cormorants and have seen them many times, so it was very clear this bird was different from that species. This was my first anhinga.
