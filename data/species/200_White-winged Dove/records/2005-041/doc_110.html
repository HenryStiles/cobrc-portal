Description of the Bird

Noticibly larger than a mourning dove -- more like the Eurasian collared doves that have been visiting our yard this spring.

Without field glasses, the white feathers at the curve of the closed wing could be seen. Overall color was grayish beige.

With 8x42 binocs, the beak appeared less petite than a mourning dove's; the eye was red, surrounded by naked blue skin; there was a black blotch near the ear; no dark marking on the neck as with a collared dove; no black dots on the folded wing as with a morning dove; feet were red; tail seemed shorter and broader than a mourning dove's.
Behaviors: The bird was about 25 ft. up a small cottonwood tree in our backyard. A feeder stocked with sunflower seeds and millet hung from a low branch.There were other birds present, including mourning doves and at least two Eurasian collared doves. First sighting was around noon and the bird soon flew off. Around 5 p.m. it returned, spending some time on the ground and some time in the tree. It preened briefly. Larry Lechner was able to get some pictures before it flew off. To our knowledge it has not returned.
Call: Not noted.
