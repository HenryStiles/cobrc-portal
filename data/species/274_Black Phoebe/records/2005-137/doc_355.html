Description of the Bird

About 7 inches long, blackish gray head, breast, upperparts and tail. Grayish tips to wing coverts and gray edges to tertials. White belly. Black bill and feet.
Behaviors: Perching on twig and rocks close to surface of Arkansas River, sallying to feed on insects on water surface.
Call: sharp "pik"
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Eastern Phoebe is paler gray on upperparts and head, and lacks blackish breast.
