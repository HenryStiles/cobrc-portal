Observer Information

Reporter:  C W   
E-mail: 
Other Observers:  Nick Komar; The Black Phoebe was originally found sometime in mid-December in the same area.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Black Phoebe
First Date/Time:  2/3/2006
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  20 minutes
County:  Pueblo
Specific Location:  Valco Ponds just beneth the Pueblo Reservoir dam. Where the bird was: walk from the parking area to where you can see the river. A few yards to the east is a path going down to the waters edge, walk east about 75 yards and the bird was on a stick on the North side of the river.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

It was on the bank of a river occasionally flying out and eating bugs off the top of the river.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski spotting scope and Lieca 8X32 BN Binoculars
Distance:  It was about 25 feet
Light:  I was between the bird and the sun. There were no clouds out when I observed the bird.

Description of the Bird

It was a medium sized flycatcher. It had a black head, back, breast, and wings. The head was lightly darker. It had an white belly.
Behaviors: The bird would sit on a branch and then fly down and catch a bug off the top of the water. It kept doing the tail motion that phoebes do, flick the up and down.
Call: It kept giving a high pitched tseew
Plumage: Unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Eastern Phoebe
There is no black on the breast. It also has a lighter head and back.

Eastern, Thick-billed, and Gray Kingbirds
They don't have any black on the breast. Thick-billed and Gray isn't black, but gray. The back of Eastern is more gray than black.
