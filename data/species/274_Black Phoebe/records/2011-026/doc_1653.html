Habitat

The yard has water feature that was being drained because so much blow dirt the past winter months had settled in the bottom. The mud was left to dry for easier removal so there were a couple of small puddles that the phoebe kept catching insects over. The phoebe would perch on the ground on the rock sides and then dip down into the pond over the puddles. At other times it would perch in low branches of locust trees and sally for insects. At times it would sit on the lawn catching insects in the grass. None of the flowering bushes were in bloom and not all the leaves were on the trees yet. The only green in the area was the Kentucky Blue/Perrennial Rye lawn. There are some cedars and pinion pines.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8x42 binocs, and auto zoom camera
Distance:  as close as 5 1/2 feet
Light:  excellent

Description of the Bird

-phoebe shape
-same size as Eastern Phoebe, a little smaller than a Say's Phoebe
-all black except for a white belly and undertail
-unmistakeable coloration
Behaviors: perched - then sallied for insects - and back to perch
behavior same as Eastern and Say's both of which I am familiar with
Call: not noted
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

I first noticed the shadow of the phoebe sallying for insects through drawn drapes on an eastern window. I assumed it was the Say's Phoebe back in the yard. When I drew the drapes, I was totally surprised at the all black bird with the white belly perched on a lower limb.

Resources Used

Sibley's Field Guide had been my resource, but I immediately knew what it was without looking. I then looked it up to find the range and called for Janeal and Dotti to come confirm the sighting.

Previous Experience

This was the first live Black Phoebe I've seen, but I've had both Say's and Eastern Phoebes in the yard from time to time and am familiar with them.
