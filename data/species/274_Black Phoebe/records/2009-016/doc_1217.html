Description of the Bird

This bird is a modest-sized flycatcher (7”) with distinctive black to dark gray plumage on its head, back, throat, upper breast, wings, tail. The belly and undertail coverts are white with the white coming to a point on the breast. The back of the head has a slight peak and the tail is long.
Behaviors: The bird was perched on a low branch at the edge of Cottonwood Marsh. While perched it flicked its tail. It then moved to another low, exposed perch.
Call: none
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Other phoebes (and most other birds) were eliminated by the black and white plumage pattern of this bird.

Slate-colored Junco was eliminated as this bird did not have white outer rectices. Nor was jizz, habitat or behavior consistent with junco.
