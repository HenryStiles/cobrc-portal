Habitat

I built a small pond from an old root-cellar just outside my kitchen window. There is also a 3’ tall berm on top of which is a smaller pond lined with boulders from which comes a waterfall lined with large rocks. From the window, on the other side of the pond and waterfall, is a hog-wire lined vegetable garden. The whole yard (four town lots) is surrounded by very tall willows and cotton-woods, as I am just yards from our little river that runs through the town. I plant lots of bird-friendly trees and shrubs; I don’t use poisons, and I feed birds. People tell me this is a very birdy yard. At the time, my garden was ready to harvest, and there were water lilies on the pond. And while the fish and moving water keep mosquitoes at bay, there were lots of gnats, bugs and dragonflies at the time. The Bk.Phoebes regularly came to the boulders at the top pond.

Description of the Bird

Perhaps the most similar "looking" bird to a Black Phoebe might be a Dark-eyed Junco-slate...at least in my mind. Both are quick, dark birds with light bellies. However, if one is familiar with the two birds, a quick look at the bill will tell a lot. The Black Phoebe's bill is thin, black, and sharp; while the Junco's bill is pink and thicker.

The next thing I discern is their respective bellies; the Phoebe's white comes up from its belly to a point while the Junco’s "line of demarcation" is horizontal. Is that "line" on the flycatcher called a vest; it rather looks like one. And the phoebe is somewhat smaller, I believe.

As to behavior; I watch Junco's feed on seed I scatter around bushes and plants. They are like chickens, scratching around looking for goodies. I don't know if Phoebes even eat seed; they always seem to be hawking bugs that fly near my pond. For several days I watched one Black Phoebe come to the upper "pond", to a particular rock. He'd hawk from there, taking quick sallies across the water or sometimes seem to jump straight up to catch a "fly-over".

As I may have mentioned, I often saw two and the day I reported I saw three Black Phoebes together at a single time. At first I wondered if they were young, but I guess not, as all were quite dark. The way the white feathers came up around their sides or low on their feet...they looked young and downy. I imagine they were just cold.

Call: Sounds? I'm really bad with bird calls. I love the soft chuckles of the Juncos as they seem to always come in large groups that chat back and forth like chickens. The Phoebe, usually alone, has a high-pitched pip that sounds like Hip, Hip Hooray. It’s like two-part sound; two going up first (hip, hip), then going down for the second (hoo, ray)...one after the other. Perhaps I imagined one was calling his fledglings, as I do usually see them singly and the white on their sides seemed so wispy...rather line a youngster, I’d imagined.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

First of all, Juncos don’t hawk insects and I've not seen Phoebes eat seed. Also, the thing I notice that is different about these birds is their heads. The Junco’s head is round and on a visible neck; the Phoebe seems not to even have a neck. Also, his feathers are almost spiky (like a jay's crest) but it often seems to be "slicked" backwards, making his head seems to slope up and back. I've never seen one raise this "crest", if even that is what it is. Anyway, perhaps it's the "no neck" look or maybe that back-flattened crest, but for a rather slender, long-tailed bird he looks somewhat blocky.
