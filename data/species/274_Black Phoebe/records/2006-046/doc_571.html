Description of the Bird

Black head and breast. Black upperparts. White belly and undertail coverts, the white forming a point into the black breast. Black straight flycatcher bill. Dark eye.
Behaviors: Bird in small stream channel west of Plateau Creek, perched in low bushes and making flycatching sorties, then returning to same or nearby perch. It flicked its tail frequently while perched, in the manner of a phoebe.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Other flycatchers, such as Western Wood-Pewee, but they don't have the combination of black and white on the underparts, don't have the phoebe tail flicking habit, and don't occupy the same habitat. Other phoebes, such as Say's, have an entirely different color pattern and tend to occupy drier habitats.
