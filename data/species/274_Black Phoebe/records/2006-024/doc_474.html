Description of the Bird

This is a small flycatcher. Its head, back, chin, throat, breast and tail are all dark gray. A white belly "V's" up into the breast and extends to the undertail coverts.

No other North American bird has this color pattern.
Behaviors: Spent much of its time perched inside a concrete structure that had water at the bottom, part of the Fountain Sewage Treatment and Settling Ponds.

Flycatching infrequently for small insects around the sewage treatment plant, especially in a pit surrounded by high concrete walls the gave shelter to the bird and to the insects on and above the water.
Call: BLPH distinctive call notes heard, mostly when the bird changed locations. Call like a loud Yellow-rumped Warbler sometimes in succession
