Documentation of Black Phoebe

Solo on the 1st of Jan.; There has been a Black Phoebe reported on and off through the late fall and early winter in the area below the dam and above the Valco Parking Lot by Brandon Percival and other observers. I have seen it or one inland in the cottonwoods about 200 feet

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Black Phoebe
First Date/Time:  1/1/2007
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  20 minutes
County:  Pueblo
Specific Location:  Arkansas River. about 100 meters downstream from Valco Ponds Parking Area and using same rocks that were used by a BLPH last winter.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Rocks and over hanging twigs along the north shore of the Arkansas River. Large River rocks at the edge of and in the river seemed to be prefered perches, but also foragged in edges of tangles overhanging the river bank.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 77mm APO scope with 32x WW lens, naked eye, and Leica Ultravid 10x42 binoculars
Distance:  as close as 30 feet
Light:  Very good. Clear day and light at my back.

Description of the Bird

A dark-backed, light-bellied upright flycatcher.

Dark gray face with dark eye difficult to see as is the same dark coloration as the head. Crown peaked.
Chin, throat, breast, back, wings, and tail slate gray.

Belly and vent, and undertail coverts white.

Bill, short, thin and dark. Long tail and longish wings.
Behaviors: Active feeder on rocks along Arkansas River. Feeds on insects at water's edge, occasionally flying up to as high as 8 feet above water. Usually calls as it flits off to another perch. Very active. Spent entire time on north river bank where sun most likely warmed the river rocks where insect seemed much more active. Traveled with a Say's Phoebe. Pumped tail many times. Never stayed on same rock for more than 10 or 15 seconds but often came back to the same 10 or so perches.
Call: call a loud clear chip, similar in some regards to the loud chip of American Pipit and similar to but not as sharp as the Say's Pheobe with which it associated.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Slate-colored Junco. Different posture. This bird was upright. Different bill shape. Different call notes. There were a number of subspecies of juncos around for comparison.

Resources Used

None

Previous Experience

Yes, This species is now regulary seen in this spot in the winter, this being the second consecutive winter for it to be recorded in Pueblo.
