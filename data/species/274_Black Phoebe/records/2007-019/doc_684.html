Habitat

Low shrubs and small trees along a fast-flowing river adjacent to rock overhangs. Surrounding habitat was rather barren rimrock with some pinyon-juniper forest.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Eagle Optics 8x50 binoculars
Distance:  75 feet
Light:  Direct afternoon sun from the south; birds were to the east of me.

Description of the Bird

Both birds were identical. They were about the size and shape of Say's Phoebes, but they were sooty coal-black all over, except for a snow-white patch on the belly that extended about halfway up the breast to end in a sharp point. They had no wingbars or apparent markings in the tail. They had long straight bills and their heads had a vaguely peaked or crested look.
Behaviors: The birds were flycatching from perches right along the streambank, never more than about 12 feet off the water. They spent almost all their time either perched or flycatching inside the airspace of the river.
Call: One or both birds made a brief, high-pitched sharp chip note several times, but it was difficult to hear over the noise of the river.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Black Phoebe is distinctive. For argument's sake, Slate-colored Junco was eliminated by darker color, pointy upper limit of white on breast, lack of white in tail, size, shape, vocalizations, and actions.
