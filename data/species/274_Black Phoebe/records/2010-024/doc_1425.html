Description of the Bird

A small black and white flycatcher. The bird was entirely black with white extending from the mid belly posteriorly past the legs. The bird was only slightly larger than the Barn Swallows with which it was fighting.
Behaviors: The bird was flycatching and aggressively vying for perches with resident Barn Swallows. The bird would often perch on the bridge on Carter Ct. that crosses the NE trending irrigation canal. When we played a tape of Black Phoebe, it flew to the paved bike path we were on and perched in a nearby tree, and sang back.
Call: Singing and calling. The songs heard were the a quick "phoebe" similar to Eastern Phoebe but shorter and given rapidly and a plaintive "beeee" slightly rising and then descending.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Hybrid Black Phoebes, which have occurred in Larimer and Fremont Counties show smudgier upper bellies and throats. In addition, hybrid Black Phoebes have been observed to sing combinations of at least three distinct strophies. The bird I observed had crisp black and white markings and sang only two distinct strophies (fee-bbe and beee) suggesting that it was not a hybrid.

Eastern Phoebe shows a much yellower belly and a white throat. The bird I observed was entirely crisp black with a black throat suggesting that it was not an Eastern Phoebe.

Say's Phoebes show a grayer overall head back with a pinkish belly, Say's Phoebes are also noticeably larger than Barn Swallows.
