Description of the Bird

Small bird, estimated 7 in. head to tail. Black head, upper breast, back, wings, and tail. Undertail coverts and belly white, extending upward to a peak along the breast. Dark eye, thin black bill.
Behaviors: Sitting on low bridge rail, fence, and post alongside a ditch/canal. Frequent flycatching activity, sometimes dropping out of sight down into the canal.
Call: Occasional call. Slightly downslurred tseeep.
Plumage: Adult plumage

Similar Species Discussion

Black and White plumage of this species is quite distinct. Closest in appearance (if one could call it close) would be Olive-sided Flycatcher, but that species has substantial white leading up to throat and other markings are more in the brown/olive coloration. Similarly, Eastern Phoebe does not have the dark breast coloration and is generally lighter in overall appearance.
