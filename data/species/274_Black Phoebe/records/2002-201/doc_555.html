Description of the Bird

the bird was a fairly small flycatcher, the shape and size as a Say's Phoebe. The posture of the bird was upright, like a flycatcher, and not horizontal. The tail was fairly long, and the bill was short and thin. The bill and legs were black.

The plumage of the bird was entirely black, except for a white belly.
Behaviors: The bird was seen on shrubs along a large river, flycatcher over the river and occasionally landing on rocks in the river.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Most similar species, such as Dark-eyed Junco, were ruled out by the flycatcher shape of the bird, with an upright posture. Junco was also ruled out by the small and thin bill, darker black plumage, and behavior. All other species of flycatchers were ruled out by the mostly black plumage with white belly.
