Habitat

As Magnificent Frigate Birds do not land, it ( a male I believe) was in the air which is its normal habitat sans there normal coastal food sources.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Panasonic Lumix Point and Shoot....optically zoomed to 12x( equivalent to 300mm)
Distance:  estimated at 60 feet from my float tube...then up to over 500 feet above the lake.
Light:  The light was perfect...partly clouded with very high white clouds, but more than 50% blue sky.

Description of the Bird

Very large Black Bird, with white throat. First observed "attacking" an Avocet nesting area. Viewed from a float tube, in North Park, Delaney Buttes Lakes. The bird was approximately 60 feet from me, when I looked up at a pair of Avocets driving it off. I had seen Harriers and Crows being driven off prior, and when I first saw the Magnificent Frigate Bird swooping in, I thought it to be a Cormorant or Osprey, and then immediately saw the sharp pointed forked tail, and the bill, as well the bent wings of the Frigate...I have seen Magnificent Frigate Birds in Mexico, Corpus, and southern Florida, including one "stealing" food or ofal from the gullet of a Boobie in flight, so I knew within seconds what I was seeing, and I knew it would be very rare. I had to dig out my camera from a waterproof bag, and was able to photograph the Frigate as well as one of the Avocets, as all three soared to at least 500 feet in less than a minute. After about 60 seconds the Magnificent Frigate bird left the area.
Behavior: The Magnificent Frigate bird came down towards an Avocet nesting area, within 20 to 30 feet of the ground and about 60 feet from me. The Avocets attacked and drove the Frigate bird to an altitude of at least 500 feet above the lake. All three birds were soaring. This event took about 60 seconds. I took four photos...a very difficult task, as I had to dig my camera out of a bag. And it being a Lumix Point and Shoot, I zoomed to 12x, or about 300mm equivalent. It was difficult to find the birds on the screen in the mid day sun, getting the camera to focus on the partly cloudy day, but I did get 4 shots, all which identify the Magnificent Frigate bird, as well as one shot with one of the Avocet in the picture as well. The MFB soared off to the south and the Avocets returned victorious
Call: none
Plumage: Black White Throat

Similar Species Discussion

It was very large...at first glance I thought of a Cormorant or Osprey, both of which may be seen on the a fore mentioned lake. But then I saw the forked tail, beak, and unique wing shape, so I immediately started to get my camera.

Resources Used

I have seen many Frigate birds in Mexico and Florida. I did use Sibley and Stokes books to verify. And of couse I have pictures..which I sent to a very serious birding friend of mine, who scoffed at the idea until he saw the photos.

Previous Experience
