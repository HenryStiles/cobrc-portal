Description of the Bird

VERY large wingspread, dark (upperwing and underwing), narrow and pointed wings.  The third or fourth primary on BOTH wings was slightly shorter.  Dark bib, white breast and chest and dark belly and tail.  The dark belly encroached onto the white lower chest centrally (not sharply pointed, but more extensive centrally).  Tail with what looked to be white edges to the outer rectrices.  Bill long, large, hooked and straw-colored.  Tail DEEPLY forked with both the left and right outer feathers being approximately the same length.  Underwing dark with a few white-colored markings in the axcillaries (see photos).  An eye-ring was seen, but photos no conclusive to exact color.  A "buffy" leading edge to the upperwing, near the bend of the wing was barely apparent on some photos.

 

DEFINITELY a different individual from the female reported several days prior at John Martin Reservoir (JMR) by Sami Colvin.  Sami's photos are located on his eBird checklist here:  https://ebird.org/checklist/S75865266   

This JMR bird's "bib" had some dark feathering encroaching into the white breast and and additional dark feathering on the side of the breast that the Cherry Creek bird lacked.

 

Similar Species Discussion

Great Frigatebird - Adult female lacks a black "bib" and has much white on the throat and breast.

Lesser Frigatebird - Adult female DOES have a black "bib", but has more extensive white in the axcillaries (a "spur") which this birdlacked (only minimal feather edges are white in the axcillaries on a Magnificent).  Lessers have a white collar that this Cherry Creek bird lacked.
