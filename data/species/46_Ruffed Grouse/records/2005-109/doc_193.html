Description of the Bird

Initial sight of the 1st bird was from the left side of the bird showing the crest on the head. The bird spent several minutes on top of a log about 20 to 30 feet in front of me. The bird turned to the right showing the tail spread out and the distinct dark tail band. The band looked black with brown above it. As the bird turned further to the right the dark feathers in the neck ruff came into view. The over all size and shape of the bird was slender with the tail being well pronounced. The second bird was observed several different times with a partial tail band, the band was observed on the outer tail feathers. The bold dark bars on the flanks of the 2nd bird were very noticeable.
Behaviors: The 2 birds were found in a dense Aspen grove. The first bird, assumed to be a male was on a log about 15 to 20 inches in diameter. The second bird when I first saw it was walking on the ground about 40 feet from the first bird.
Call: I did not hear the drumming that others in the sighting party heard
Plumage: brown in between the gray and rufous

Similar Species Discussion

While the Spruce Grouse have some barring on the sides they are a darker bird with a short tail and no distinct dark almost black tail band. The Blue Grouse has some barring also however it is a slightly larger bird with no crest. While it does have a darker tail it does not have distinct dark band at the base of the tail. The Sharp-tailed Grouse does have a raised crest however it has more of a pale almost white belly and side. It does not have the barring seen on the Ruffed Grouse. Also the bird has a tail that is pale in color that ends in a point. White-tailed Ptramigan is too small of a bird.
