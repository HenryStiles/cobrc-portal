Description of the Bird

the bird was a grouse significantly smaller than Blue Grouse seen immediately after the observation. The overall color was a gray-brown, with much less of a blue-gray color than the Blue Grouse, and more of a browner look. It also appeared a little paler in flight. When seen in flight I was able to see the bold black subterminal band on the otherwise brownish tail. The tail was rounded in shape. In the very brief look I got of the bird on the ground I was able to get a quick glimpse of a wispy crest, reminiscent of a Northern Cardinal.

When the bird was flushed it flew low over the ground, and the wingbeats were very quiet.
Behaviors: I saw one bird that I flushed from the ground, and heard another a few minutes later.
Call: a bird was heard drumming, sounding like a distant bird taking off, an increasingly fast series of very low-pitched "whump"s. This may or may not have been the bird originally flushed.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All species of Grouse other than Blue and Sharp-tailed were ruled out by location, habitat, bold black sub-terminal band, wispy crest, brownish gray color, etc.

Sharp-tailed Grouse was ruled out by the rounded tail without a point, bold black sub-terminal band, overall color darker than Sharp-tailed, and by habitat and location. Blue Grouse was ruled out by smaller size, brower and paler coloration, by the wispy crest, and especially by the bold black sub-terminal band. The flight style, low over the ground and very quiet, is also not typical of Blue Grouse.
