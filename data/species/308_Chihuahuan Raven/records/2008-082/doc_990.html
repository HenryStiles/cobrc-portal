Description of the Bird

The Chihuahuan Raven was calling outside window (about 9:00 AM 5/28/08) on grate-type awning 2 feet from window. I stood on my desk (awning on outside window about 4 feet up from top of desk) and looked at raven which was geetting either water or something out of grate). It lifted head and called in an obvious agonistic social interaction with another or to proclaim "my area." At that point you could see the long pointed neck feathers of a raven (not as shaggy as a Common Raven's, but definitely not crow-like). The nasal bristles covered much of the beak (compared to Common Raven) and were closer to the tip (over half - about 2/3). But the obvious characteristic was the white based neck feathers (not gray like Common - have had these in hand and looked at them). It had the neck feathers fluffed while calling and showed the white - easily seen. Another characteristic was the head was not quite as pronounced - smoother conours to the eye and the body seemed not quite as big as a common, but definitelty bigger than a crow. The wings were long but I could never see the the tail from the angle he was at (kind of cocked at a 45 to 55 degree towards me).
Behaviors: He was calling when first spotted him (seemed like an agonistic social interaction with another raven that I did not see). He started getting something out of the grate awning when I eased up onto my desk to peer out the window. It was fishing something out of grate or was getting a drink of water because it finally lifted head to drink. It started calling again several times (5 or 6) before it finally noticed me. It looked at me quite intently, but did not spook. Finally he flew off (after about a minute).
Call: Call - Was typical raven cra-a-a-a (not caw like crow) but with higher pitch (crow-like quality) than Common Raven. Called several times (at least 10 calls) in a social display on awning outside window. Cra-a-a was sometimes cra-a-ock having a hard end to call. When I heard it, I thought it was a definite possible Chihuahuan, but have heard Commons give a similar call.
Plumage: Adult - had good sheen on feathers

Similar Species Discussion

Common Raven - voice was higher pitched, head not as pronounced (smoother conours), neck feathers not as shaggy, nasal bristles longer compared to tip of bill, and white-based neck feather (not gray).

American Crow - Cra-a-a call instead of ca-a-aw. Long lanceolate neck feathers instead of more shorter, more rounded tip feathers of crow. Larger features (bill, head) than crow. Nasal bristles covered more of bill than in crow. Finally, thouugh I have seen white wing feathers in crow, have not seen white-based neck feathers.
