Habitat

blue grama shortgrass prairie with sunflowers on edge

Description of the Bird

Multiple birds with variations on the amount of buff color in the face and on the breast.  However, Ammodramus by flat head and large pinkish bill.  Bright pinkish legs. Back with scaly appearance due in part to dark-centered feathers with fresh light edgings. Top of head flattened and with lightish central crown with black streaks on both sides of center. Bill bulky and pinkish.  Ear coverts distinctive with two dark spots on the back of the auriculars.  Face with distinct pattern with "double malar" that is probably a moustachial streak and a malar but distinctive nonetheless. Distinctive shape to necklace with fewer spots up near throat, widening to the sides and then continuing down a short way on the flanks.  Overall bright and lighter than other lookalike sparrows from a distance. When sparrow flock flushed, Baird' were often the birds remaining on the fenceline.  Seemed only to be seen close sunflowers, often jumping up to fence and then often dropping out of sight, often by falling straight to the ground but at first the birds would also fly a short distance and remain on the wire. They seemed to spook a little easier later in the week.

Similar Species Discussion

Vesper Sparrows were also present but seemingly in more weedy areas to start but later in the week more common and in all habitats with sunflowers.  Shorter tail with more white in the outer tail feathers.  Darker head with auricular area without light spots. Overall a browner more chunky bird.


Grasshopper Sparrow - juvs also with necklace but with flatter crown and often with thick, buffy and often orange lore. Much smaller when seen together which was the case the first day.

Resources Used

Rising's Sparrow book almost entirely but I looked in the NEW Sibley too.

Previous Experience

I have seen them twice in NW North Dakota at Lostwood NWR in response to a tape of their song where they were very cooperative like the El Paso County birds.  Also, once near Kindersley, Saskatchewon, while I was working on a waterfowl banding project in late August through mid-September.
