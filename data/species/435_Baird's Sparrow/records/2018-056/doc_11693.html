Description of the Bird

Bird ran out of the grass, stopped on the road for a minute (long enough for me to take a few pics) then flew back into the grass. Flat head, yellow crest with darker bands on either side of crest, large beak, dark mustach, light streaking on breast, mostly yellow/buff head.

Similar Species Discussion

Vesper sparrow-too small of bill, lacks enough yellow
Grasshopper sparrow-lacks yellow crest and streaking on the breast
Lincoln's sparrow-too much gray on head and has buffy streak on breast
Savannah sparrow-too small of beak, does not have flat head

Resources Used

I used Sibley Birds of the West for initial ideas then All About Birds website. I then posted it on the CFO (Colorado Field Ornithologists) and DFO (Denver Field Ornithologists) facebook pages for confirmation. Numerous people agreed that it was a Baird's sparrow.

Previous Experience

I have never seen a Baird's sparrow, but have seen Vesper and Savannah sparrows.
