Description of the Bird

Bird was about 15 feet from me in bright shade. It was about the size of a nearby CC Sparrow, though very different in structure, with a largish flat head and big bill and short tail. The colors of both birds appeared quite true in the lighting they sat in. The bill of the Baird's was fleshy colored with a darker culmen. The supericlium, auriculars, and submoustachial areas were all moderately bright orange-buff. The auriculars were bordered below and behind by a distinct blackish line ending superiorly in a thickened area that appeared as a blackish spot (very similar to the El Paso County birds but bolder). There was a thin black malar stripe below the buffy submoustachial. This malar stripe extended down to the chest streaking. The bird had a complete whitish eyering. The crown was dark without detectable median stripe. The throat was white or off-white (not closely examined.) The chest was washed in a pale buff. The chest had medium-thickness blackish streaks that formed a band across it. The streaks were not fine like a Lincoln's or broad, as is often depicted for juv Baird's). Some streaking extended down sides. Upper belly white or nearly white. The rest of the bird was not visible. The views were excellent and marks were easily seen. Notably, I struggled with the dark auriculars and broad streaking depicted in most sources for juv Baird's. The photo at https://www.flickr.com/photos/sloalan/6296341918/ has streaking that was just as I remembered that of the Fox Ranch Baird's and the auricular pattern that is rather similar. The color of the bird I saw was not as intense, probably due to different lighting conditons (the bird at Flickr appears to have been photographed at dawn or dusk). Note that the bird on the Flickr photo faced its photograper much as the bird I saw faced me.

Similar Species Discussion

Grasshopper Sparrow juv: would have a white median stripe, yellow buff instead of orange-buff (or "ochraceous" as it is often called), and lacks well-defined border to auricular and malar stripe. 
Savannah Sparrow: among other marks - smaller bill, yellow not orange-buff.
Henslow's Sparrow: olive or olive-buff on face. Bolder eyering. 
LeContes: Gray auriculars, white median stripe, smaller, and lacks border to auricular
