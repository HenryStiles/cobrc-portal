Description of the Bird

Two of these birds were first reported on July 25th, and documented by audiotape (one bird only posted on Xeno-canto.org and submitted to CBRC by NK; both birds were taped singing simultaneously on July 26th by Eric DeFonso). This submission reports audio observation of both males from the same locations as heard on previous days, plus documents one of them with photographs taken by Dan Durda. A third bird was observed only by Dan Durda at 7:30 AM slightly further south of the two singing males, near the railroad crossing on C.R. 5. 

The first bird was photographed by Dan Durda (seen and heard by all three of us) when it perched along the barbed wire fence on the west side of C.R. 5 about 1.7 mi north of Buckeye Rd., at 6:10 AM. The first three photos accompanying this document show this bird. The photos show a worn adult with just traces of breast streaking, barely visible lateral throat stripes, and weak broken mustache stripe (terminology used in Sibley Guide); also dull coloration on head (no obvious ochre wash).

The second photographed bird (seen only by Dan) is shown perched on a thistle (final two photos). This bird seems to be much less worn, with clear streaks on upper breast, and lower flanks, and clear lateral throat stripe and broken moustache stripe, and ochre wash to head. This bird does show some feather wear, and may indeed be a third adult or possibly a hatch-year bird born much earlier this summer (perhaps from a first brood).

Similar Species Discussion

Grasshopper Sparrow juveniles are similar but lack obvious lateral throat stripe and moustache mark, and ochre wash on head. Vesper Sparrow is longer-tailed and smaller-headed. Savannah Sparrow and juv Brewer's Sparrow lack ochre tones of head, are smaller headed and more streaked below.
