Description of the Bird

Small sparrow singing from perches atop thistle weeds in a small section of grassland, and also disappearing among grasses at ground level; very pale (white) base color to breast but otherwise viewed too distantly or too briefly to assess plumage details. Only one bird seen. Two birds gave unique Baird's Sparrow song simultaneously. There may have been more than two singing males, as at different times, songs were heard from at least three different directions.

Similar Species Discussion

All Ammodramus genus species and Savannah Sparrow were considered, but song was unique to Baird's.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide mobile phone app for sound recordings of sparrows.

Previous Experience

No experience with Baird's Sparrows other than listening to sound recordings. Limited sight and audio observations of all other similar species, except for extensive personal experience with singing Grasshopper and Savannah Sparrows in Colorado since 1997.
