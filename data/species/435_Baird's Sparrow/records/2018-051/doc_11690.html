Description of the Bird

See photos. 5 birds heard/seen singing at once. One territory had a non-singing bird in it that was not treated aggressively and was an adult, presumably a female. All were short-tailed and relatively large-billed and large-headed smallish sparrows. They were white below with a dark streaking across chest and to some extent down sides. Intensity and width of streaking varied with apparent wear. Folder greater wing-coverts were dark brown (with some degree of pale at base, variable) with modestly contrasting buff tips, but not really a distinct wingbar. Upper parts striped dark and medium brown. Auriculars grayish (at times seemingly tinged olive) with ochre supercilium above. Upper rear auricular with blackish spot or parial frame. Typically another blackish spot or line along posterior auriculars, and then bordered by narrow dark brown below. This was surround behind (collar) and below with ochre). Distinct but not bold white or near white eyering. Crown streaked dark brown with paler central stripe from narrow to moderate width and from pale to moderate ochre in color.
