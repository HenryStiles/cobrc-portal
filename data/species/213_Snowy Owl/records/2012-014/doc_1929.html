Habitat

Short grass plains, grazed. Private pasture lands with minimal clumps of grass/brush and scattered snow.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  VanGuard Signature 69 15x-45x scope and Leica 7x35 binocs
Distance:  about 600 ft.
Light:  great. Looking SSW at bird, sun to left.

Description of the Bird

Overall white with dark gray barring. Top of head and nape were gray giving a dark appearance to these areas. White facial disks. Barred back, chest and belly with grayish tone. Barring was heavy, but not as black as some pictures I have seen.
Behaviors: Just sittig and watching on the ground, turning head periodically. Fairly quiet during 15 minute observation
Call: N/A
Plumage: Believe juvenile female

Similar Species Discussion

All other Owls eliminated because of the dark gray barring on white. Gyrfalcon eliminated because of general appearance.

Resources Used

I did look at several sources (Sibley's, Cornell Bird website, the internet), to try to make a determination whether 1 year old female/male or adult female. Several pictures that I found noted immature females with almost black barring and 1 year old males/adult females with grayish barring. Cap and nape gray and extensive darkish gray barring (not black) looked identical to Stokes Field Guide to North American Birds 1 year old female. However, I saw some immature male pics that were similar except lacking dark nape.

Previous Experience

This was only second wild Snowy Owl - seen several in zoos (adult males and females). I have seen all other North American owls (except accidentals). I have been birding for 35 years.
