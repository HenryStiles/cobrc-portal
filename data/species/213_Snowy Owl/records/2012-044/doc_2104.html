Habitat

Disked farm land with weedy strip along pipeline where the owl rested. Adjoining fields were weedy and probably good hunting for voles and mice.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10x42 BA binoculars, Leica APO Televid 62 with 16x48x ocular. Mary had her Swarovski 77 scope.
Distance:  Guess of 200 ft
Light:  Owl was directly north of us, so winter sun at 1:30-2:32 p.m. was shining directly on the bird at the top of a slight incline. There was a skiff of snow on the disked farm field between us and the owl on 1/8/12, but about 4" on the field on 2/21/12. Bird very visible on the pipeline.

Description of the Bird

Typcial ~23" tall juvenile Snowy Owl coloration: white face with eyes and bill visible, no ears, rotund body, brown barring on breast, back, wings, and top and back of head.
Behaviors: Juvenile Snowy Owl was resting ~100 yds from 138th Ave, Adams Co., CO in ~8"-tall weedy strip of pipeline land that was parallell to the road and the high point of the surrounding area. It appeared to be resting and was swivelling its head around to view the area. It sat in the same spot for ~1 hr and was viewed by others.
Call: None heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Large, rotund owl standing usually upright on ground on U.S. plains or along lake wetlands in winter with no ears, dark feather barring except for white face can only be a Snowy Owl. I have found and seen many of them.
