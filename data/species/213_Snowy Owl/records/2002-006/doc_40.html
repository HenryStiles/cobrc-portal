Description of the Bird

It was a large, white owl, sitting on a fence post in open grassland.

It had a clean white face, throat, and central upper breast. The white on the breast had a "V" shape.

It had a dusky nape, with prominently barred wings, flanks, and belly.

It had a darker gray or black tail, as seen when the wings were folded.

The eyebrows were obvious (i.e. darker than the face).

It had white, heavily feathered feet, with short legs.
Behaviors: During my observation, it was sitting on a fence post in an open field.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult female or immature

Similar Species Discussion

The ID of this bird was quite easy. Its size, compact shape, and very rounded head immediately points to an owl. The white color, short legs, and heavily feathered feet narrow the choice to a Snowy Owl. Some Great Horned Owls seen in Colorado in winter are very pale, but they would have shown clear ear tufts and facial markings in the view I had of this bird.
