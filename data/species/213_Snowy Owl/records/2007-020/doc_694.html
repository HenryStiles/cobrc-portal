Description of the Bird

This big white large-headed bird without noticeable ear tufts was an owl. The facial disc was completely white. Its irises were bright yellow with large dark pupils. The crown was white with fine dark streaking. The wings back and breast had dark thick streaking on a white background. The legs appeared feathered. The feet were not visible.

The top of the wings, visible when it flew were white with dark streaks, the wings very broad, the wing beats slow, the wing tips very rounded.
Behaviors: Perched on top of road cut and flew three times to fence posts. Its flight was always low often below the height of the barbed wire fence. When first spotted perched on a cut bank. it remained motionless until it became aware of our presence. It then spent much of its time looking at us.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Ist winter male possibly

Similar Species Discussion

There are no similar large, mostly white, earless owls. A leucistic Great Horned Owl would have ear tufts. Barn Owl is much smaller and has a heart-shaped face and reddish back and is not as large.

Comments: I feel that this bird is a first winter male and not an adult female, the two choices. There are still significant dark streaks both ventrally and dorsally. Although the face is pure white, all plumages of Snowy Owl have all white faces.
