Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Snowy Owl
First Date/Time:  1/28/2012
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  40 minutes
County:  Prowers
Specific Location:  County Roads LL and 12, about .2 north of the canal on the west side of Road 12
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

-large bales of corn stalks left in a harvested irrigated corn field
-5 to 8 miles south of Thurston Reservoir

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  binocs 10 x 42
Distance:  100 to 200 feet
Light:  clear and sunny

Description of the Bird

-all white large owl
-faint brownish spots sparsely spaced on wings and center tail feathers
-very faint brownish barring on sides
Behaviors: -lethargic
-half asleep perched on top large feed bale
-when observers spooked it, it flew further away from the group to the next bale back but made very few movements
Call: n/a
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

the only owl I know that appears totally bright white

-and the other observers

observed the Snowy Owl that was found in El Paso County - my observation was in January 2010
