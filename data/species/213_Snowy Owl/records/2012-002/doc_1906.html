Large plains reservoir

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Just about everything.
Distance:  Starting at about 50 feet as it flew by, then at 3,800 feet
Light:  Good conditions--the bird flew by to the north and then was observed to the east with the sun in the west.

Description of the Bird

Large, heavy, virtually all-white bird which in flight had rounded wings and a slow wing beat. Upon landing the bird showed a rounded head, short tail and a small amount of black barring and spotting and a faint black crown.
Behaviors: The bird flew in front of my car at close range and continued on flying with slow heavy wingbeats across a frozen reservoir until reaching the far shore where is swooped up to perch on a wooden utility pole and swivel its head back and forth.
Call: NA
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Too fat, short billed and rounded winged for a Glaucous Gull.
