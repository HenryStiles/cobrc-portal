Description of the Bird

Nearly all-white, large owl. No ear tufts, black bill, yellow eyes. Feathered feet. Light brownish-gray spotting on upper wing surfaces, a speck or two of black on head and body (precluding full adult male?) Very light gray tipping on most body feathers.
Behaviors: Mostly sleeping on ground or on fence post. At 16:30 began hunting from perches such as a TV antenna and roof tops. One unsuccessful plunge to the ground was witnessed a little after 17:00.
Call: not heard
Plumage: sub-adult

Similar Species Discussion

The only other North American owls this large would have to be leucistic or albinistic to be this white, but the lack of ear tufts precludes Great Horned and the head proportions and lack of a radar dish-like facial disc precludes Great Gray. The yellow eyes preclude Spotted or Barred. Barn Owls can look pretty white but are smaller, lack heavily feathered toes, have totally different proportions, such as longer legs, etc.
