Description of the Bird

Large white owl with yellow eyes.  Overall, bird was white with a few dark subterminal terminal markings on the back, wing coverts and primaries.  Feather edges not crisp, sharp and "neat" indicating an adult bird (whereas a 1st winter bird would have very crisp and clean edges to the coverts and primaries).

Bird found at sunrise along Hwy 94 perched on a telephone pole.  The bird sat unbothered as we turned around and stopped nearby for photos.  After several minutes a passing car slowed down and the owl flew to the north and was not relocated by us (it went behind some hills to the north).  Was later refound in the field to the north by Glenn Walbek, Mark Peterson, Norm Erthal, Loch Kilpatrick, M. Obs.).
