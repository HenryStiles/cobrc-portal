Description of the Bird

Warbler was with other warbler species and Chipping Sparrows. The warbler was about the same size as the sparrows and two Nashville Warblers bathing in the same waterhole. The warbler was somewhat smaller then the Yellow-rumps. What first caught my eye was the yellow-orange color of the head and throat. The warbler had a very pronounced yellow-orange eyebrow which extended from the bill to the neck. Infact the hole face was yellow-orange except the light brown area of the auriculars and lores. The bird had two very bold, white wing bars and faint streaks of dark gray on the flanks. The belly and undertail coverts were off white. I was above the bird part of the time and could see the head, back and upper tail. The bird did have pale streaking on the back. The head was uniform gray as was the tail.
Behaviors: The warbler was bathing with several other small birds. They would fly up into a small aspen then back to the water for another quick splash about before again preening and drying off in the aspen. A culvert under the road had created the water hole the birds were using to bath. The water was colored red from the clay but did not affect the plumage color of the birds. The two Nashville Warblers yellow colors were still yellow.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Could have been a HY male?

Similar Species Discussion

When I first noted the warbler the orange-yellow color got my attention. It was bathing and I thought maybe the reddish water had created the orange color. Townsend's Warbler, which can be fairly common in fall migration, is a good candidate. I was aware of this while observing the bird and looked for a dark band or area about the upper chest or throat. None was observed. Black-throated Green Warbler is also a candidate but lacks streaking on the back and in most plumages has some black on throat and chest.
