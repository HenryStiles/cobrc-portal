Description of the Bird

Unmistakable small warbler sized and billed bird--bright orange face with black markings including a black cap, a black eyeline before and behind the eye with an orange crescent under the eye and a jagged black patch below the eye; broad white wingbars; orange throat with a little bit of difussed orange on an otherwise pure white chest, belly and vent region; a few black streaks on the flanks; and a black back other than the wingbars.
Behaviors: Actively feeding in riparians trees including cottonwood, apple and ash.
Call: Different from Yellow and Wilson's which were the other warblers present
Plumage: breeding
