Description of the Bird

This small, active passerine was comparable in size, shape and behavior to the Yellow Warblers in the same vicinity. No other warbler combines black, white, orange and yellow.

The head was orange and black with a broad orange supercilium, narrow central crown stripe, side of neck, under eye arc and throat. The orange of the throat and upper breast transitioned to yellow on the belly and white on the undertail coverts. The sides of the breast, belly and flanks were yellow to white with black streaks. This bird has prominent white wing patch and pale yellowish braces.

See photos at: http://www.flickr.com/photos/waltmandavid/5760222506/in/photostream/
Behaviors: Behavior was typical of warblers, actively gleaning small insects and worms in cottonwood, box elder, ash and apple trees.
Call: silent
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

No other warbler has this striking orange-and-black head pattern and large white wing patch on a black back and wings.

All other orange, black and white birds are not warblers.
