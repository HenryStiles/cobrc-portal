Description of the Bird

the bird was a small passerine with a fairly short tail and long wings. The most obvious feature of the bird was the bright flame-colored throat, along with the eyebrow and sides of the neck. The auricular was black and triangular shaped, with the lower corner running into bold black streaks that ran down the side. The crown and back were also black, with bold white stripes on the back. The wings were also black, and the bird had a bold white wing panel. The upper belly was pale orangish-yellow, and the lower belly and undertail coverts were white. The uppertail was black, and the lower tail almost entirely white with small black tail corners.

The bill was thin and pointed, and both the bill and legs were black.
Behaviors: the bird was foraging high in tall trees, and was moving down the riparian corridor quite fast. The bird was generally very hard to see or stay on.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other bird species were ruled out by the combination of size and bright orange throat combined with face pattern, etc.
