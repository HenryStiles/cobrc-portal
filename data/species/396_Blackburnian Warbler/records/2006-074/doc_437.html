Description of the Bird

It had an orange face with black auriculars and eye stripe. The throat was the brightest of the orange on the head. It had a black crown aroung an orange stripe and a few orange spots on top of the head. It had yellow lower breast and upper belly. Black stripes on the sides and flanks. It had a black and white back. White lower belly and undertail covert.
Behaviors: It was usually half way up one of three different Cottonwood Trees. It was in the same set of trees both days that I saw the bird.
Call: High pitched song: Tsi tsi tsi tsi tsi tsiiiii.
Plumage: breeding adult

Similar Species Discussion

Cape May Warbler - It has rufous auriculars, yellow neck, and a dark cap on the head. Black streaks that go up to the throat. Yellow breast and upper belly. White greater coverts on a greenish back.
Olive Warbler - No streaking. Gray back with a few white wing bars. Dark orange head with a dark cheek patch. Gray belly.

Previous Experience

I have never seen a Blackburnian Warbler until this one. I have seen a few Olive Warblers, but never a Cape May Warbler.
