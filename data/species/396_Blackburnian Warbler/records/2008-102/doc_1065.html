Description of the Bird

A typical Dendrioca warbler size and shape, about Townsend's Warbler size. This bird was buffy-yellow on the throat, breast and supercilium. It was brightest on the upper breast and the color faded to a pale buffy-yellow on the lower breast and flanks. The belly was mostly whitish as were the undertail coverts. The upper side of the bird was much darker. The wings were dark brownish-olive with two prominent white wingbars. The back was brownish-olive and had some faint darker streaks and 2 pale braces down the lateral of the back (visible photo b) theese are definitive for BLBW. The darker auricular of the face continued to the back of the neck. The bill was dark-tipped and a flesh base, the legs were dark flesh colored. The sides of the bird from the upper breast area to the flanks had very blurry dark streaking. The crown was a similar dark brown-olive as was the back. The eye had two faint eye arcs, one above and one below the eye
Behaviors: Flitting around actively, sometimes in the company of a femal Yellow Warbler. the bird was seen in Siberian Elm, Eastern Red Cedar and Cottonwood trees.
Call: A chik or tsik note, fairly soft
Plumage: immature female

Similar Species Discussion

The almost orange tinge to the buffy-yellow breast eliminates the very similar Cerulean Warbler. The most similar species Townsend's Warbler was eliminated by the pale back braces (eliminates all other Dedroica species). Also the dark auriculars that continue to the neck hleps in eliminating Townsend's Warbler.
