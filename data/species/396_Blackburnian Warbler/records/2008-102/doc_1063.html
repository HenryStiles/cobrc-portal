Description of the Bird

Brian Gibbons located a dull female Blackburnian Warbler on the morning of 7 September 2008. Lisa Edwards and I went to try to refind it. We found the bird at midstory. It was a warbler based on the small thin bill and rapid flitting movenments. The most distinctive feature was the grayish auricular which was outlined by the yellowish broad supercillium and the orange-yellow chin and throat. The yellow of the throat continued to the breast and the belly was white. There were faint dusky flank streaks. The tail was longish. The next most prominent feature were the bold whitish wingbars, contrasting with a dark wing. On the bird's back were faint whitish streaks that contrasted with a darker gray back. The bill was light colored and the feet were dark.
Behaviors: The bird was by itself and foraged amongst the leaves of a plains cottonwood. It moved throughout the tree gleaning insects from the leaves. Once, fell down about 15 feet in a spiral flight to a different level in the trees.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st basic

Similar Species Discussion

This is the only warbler with a combination of whitish streaks on its back, and a dark auricular surrounded by yellow, and with a orange-yellow chin, throat, and breast.
