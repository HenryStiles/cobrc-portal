Description of the Bird

This bird was accepted with ascention record 52-89-40. However, the observation was made for Jefferson County and never documented directly across the Platte River in Douglas County.

A dendroica shaped warbler with a gray cap, and a broad yellowish supercillium. The auricular area was gray and light yellow below, in the throat and down through the chest. It was white from the belly through the undertail coverts. There was a hint of streaking on the flanks. The back was grayish with obvious pale yellowish white braces on the back. It was gray down through the rest of the back to the rump. The bird was missing its tail. There where two white wingbars on the wings.
Behaviors: The bird was moving around and feeding actively in a mixed flock along the edge of the river.
Call: None heard
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Townsend's Warbler would have brighter yellow, broader supercillium and more of an olive coloring on the back and no braces. Black-throated Green would also have a broader supercillium of brighter yellow, more of a white throat and chest and a brighter green back with no braces.
