Habitat

This is a linear row of cottonwoods, peach-leaf willosw with some Russian olives, but the bird was in a plains cottonwood along the dam that creates Rose Pond.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica Ultravid 10x42 mm bins.
Distance:  About 30-40 feet up and maybe 20 feet away from tree
Light:  Very good. Sun was at my back.

Description of the Bird

This part was easy. It was a warbler by high, thin song. The bright orange face with dark auricular was strongly noticeable as the bird finally came into view after having heard it sing for about five minutes. The throat was flame orange too. The wingbars seemed to merge to for a large white panel of white on a dark wing. It had a yellow orange washed breast with dark flank streaks. I didn't look much at anything else.
Behaviors: Mostly sang, but when I finally saw it, it moved down from the tallest leaves to just above mid-tree height and foraged among the cottonwood seeds, apparently for insects there.
Call: sang partial song about every 30-40 seconds - I didn't know it, but knew it was one of the four highest-pitched eastern warblers, and for sure not the stacato notes of Blackpoll Warbler.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

This is really the only warbler with the flaming orange and black face. A 1st year male Red-faced Warbler has a tiny bit of orangey look but is mostly dull red.

Resources Used

I use Big Sibley to see if any other species has the flaming orange on the face and ventral surface.

Previous Experience

I have seen scores of these birds in the boreal winter in South America, mostly in Ecuador, Venezuela, and Peru and a few in the U.S. including an especially bright male at Sondermann Park in Colorado Springs a few years ago where it spent almost a week foraging high and singing.
