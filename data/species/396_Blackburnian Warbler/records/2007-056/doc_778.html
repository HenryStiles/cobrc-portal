Description of the Bird

I was looking at group of birds which included Wilson's Warblers, Black-capped Chickadees and other birds, when I spotted a bird that quickly identified as a Blackburnian Warbler. The main thing was the throat, which was all bright yellow/orange. Also, two white wing bars, and a dark face patch, similar to Townsend's Warbler (which I had seen earlier in the day), except this face patch was triangle, instead of rounded like Townsend's Warbler. The bird had a yellow thick eye line which started at the bill and went behind the face patch, and then down to the throat. The bill was small, and some light in color. The bird had some streaking on the sides. As mentioned before, the throat was yellow/orange (I think first fall male would most likely work for this bird). The breast and belly seems close to the same color as the throat, though the throat was a little more yellow/orange than the yellowish breast and belly. The undertail coverts were whiter. The top of the head looked dark. The back was darkish, with two lighter long streaks. The wings were dark with the two white wing bars on the both sides. The tail seems somewhat short.
Behaviors: Bird was first fairly low in salt cedars, and then also in the cottonwoods and other trees, with a mix flock of birds.
Call: none
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Townsend's Warbler -- face patch rounded, doesn't have the yellow/orange face. Since the bird was a brighter Blackburnian, I think most other warblers can be ruled out with just the throat color.
