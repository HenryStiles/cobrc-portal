Description of the Bird

A small warbler-sized bird with thin, pointed beak. Back black with white "braces" forming a non-connected "V" on the back. Nape and crown black. Throat and upper chest orange-yellow (more yellowish than the firey orange throat of a spring male). Lower chest and belly yellow with bold black "streaking" along the sides and flanks. Undertail coverts white. Black triangular auriculars extending just barely to the back (no distict separation). Two bold white wing bars were barely, but distinctly separated.
Behaviors: Foraging at the tops of several Plains Cottonwoods. Loosely associating with serveral other species in a mixed flock (Orange-crowned, Yellow-rumped and Wilson's Warblers, and two Townsend's Warblers (1 adult male, 1 immature female), Ruby-crowned Kinglets, etc.
Call: No vocalizations were heard.
Plumage: First fall
