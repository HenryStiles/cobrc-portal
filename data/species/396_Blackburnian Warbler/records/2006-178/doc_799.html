Description of the Bird

The bird was a typical Dendroica in shape and profile. The head was a mixture of black and orange. The short, thin bill was black. The crown and nape were black and that black extended narrowly to the middle of the forehead. The supercilium, post-auriculars, side-neck, throat, and upper breast were a light orange. The lores and auriculars were black and extended ventrally as a stripe to the sides of the upper breast. The mid- and lower breast were buffy-white with narrow black streaking across the flanks. Undertail coverts were white. The upperparts were mainly black, except for broad white longitudinal stripes across the mantle. The wings were black, expect for two broad white wing bars or panels across the coverts. The tail was mostly black, although, when in flight, whitish outer rectrices were noted. The legs appeared black.
Behaviors: Flitted and foraged in undergrowth.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Townsends Warblers are a contrasty green and yellow overall, have black extending thicker across the crown, have black throats, and lack the auricular pattern of Blackburnians.

Previous Experience

Have seen hundreds of others across the Midwest, East, and South. Have seen one additional one in Colorado prior to this observation. This species used to nest in the spruce tree by my bedroom window in northern Wisconsin.
