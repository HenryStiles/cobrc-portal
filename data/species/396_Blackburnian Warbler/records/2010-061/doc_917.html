Description of the Bird

A sprightly Dendroica, I first found the bird in Coen and Brenda's back yard as we were sitting down to dinner on the patio. I thought that it was going to be a Townsend's Warbler (TOWA), but as soon as my binocular hit it, I yelled out, "Blackburnian Warbler!"

The bird was a medium-sized Dendroica. It had an average Dendroica bill (not too thin, not too thick, not too long, not too short) that was grayish, except for a noticeable black culmen and dark tip. The forehead was yellow-green, immediately above the forehead was green and that color darkened as it went back over the crown such that the back half of the crown was quite dark. There nape was grayish-green and unstreaked, contrasting somewhat with the green, streaked back. The back streaking was black, though there were two distinct and obvious whitish braces. The rump was greenish and the the tail was blackish with large white spots on the inner webs of at least the outer two pairs of rectrices.

There were distinct and yellow (with a bit of an orange aspect) supercilia that flared behind the eye. A dark olive eyeline connected behind the eye with the same color auriculars. The slightly orange yellow of the chin and throat wrapped up around behind the auricular in an obvious ear surround, separating the darker face from the darker upperparts. There was distinct olive streaking on the sides extending down through the flanks. Otherwise, the underparts were the same orangey yellow, though fading through straight yellow on the belly and becoming whitish or white in the vent region and on the undertail coverts. I did not note whether the undertail coverts were streaked, though they should not have been. What I saw of the legs was dark.
Behaviors: Foraging rapidly in a short tree in the back yard, scanning the upper surfaces of leaves.
Call: none
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

TOWA is most similar, but that species lacks pale braces and, except for adult males, has little or no black streaking on the upperparts (the bird was not an adult male). Additionally, TOWA lacks the black bar just anterior to the white tips or the median coverts that form the bird's upper wing bar.
