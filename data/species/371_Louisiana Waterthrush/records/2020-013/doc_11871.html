Description of the Bird

The bird was warbler size, with a long spiky bill and would bob its tail as it foraged. The back, wings and tail were a chocolate brown color. The crown of the head was also brown. There was a distinct white eyeline that broadened at the back end. The eyeline was white from the front tip to the back end. The center of the face was also brown with a whitish streak between the center of the face and a darked moustacial stripe. The moustacial stream then extended towards the shoulder. The throat was clean white with no streaks. The undersides were white with diffuse brownish splotches. The splotches and white undersides extended down to the vent. The sides of the flanks were a very pale cream or buff that blended to the white at the center of the lower belly. The undertail coverts appeared to be clean white. The legs were pinkish/orange. It would forage along the edge of Spring Creek in Fort Collins and out into the shallow water. At times it would seek refuge behind the branches, grass and roots along the bank.

Similar Species Discussion

Northern Waterthrush is ruled out by the color and shape of the white eyeline, by the clear white throat, by the browner color of the splotches underneath, and by the color of the legs.
