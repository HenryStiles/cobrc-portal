Description of the Bird

A small passerine foraging among rocks in the stream and bobbing its tail. Brown on top with brown streaking below. Clean throat, white eyeline widening at back, buffy flanks but white breast otherwise.

Similar Species Discussion

Northern Waterthrush is quite similar and would be more expected in this part of the county, although it would be early for a Northern Waterthrush. It was eliminated by carefully observing that it had a clean white throat with no streaking, a rather sparsely streaked breast which was white, and a eyeline which broadened towards the rear and was white.

Resources Used

Binoculars, photos, and this website: https://www.sibleyguides.com/2012/08/identification-of-waterthrushes/. 

Previous Experience

I'm a recent transplant from Virginia/West Virginia and we get both waterthrush species there. I've used the eyebrow shape and the throat pattern to differentiate these species in the past, and knew right away to check these characteristics.
