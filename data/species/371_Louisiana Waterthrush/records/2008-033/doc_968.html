Habitat

Moving stream with areas of mud flats and small rock. Areas of brush along streams edge to provide shelter.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss Victory 8 x 42 binoculars
Distance:  average of 10- 20'
Light:  Very cloudy and overcast day. Quite cool, in fact I drove through a snow shower to observe the bird. By staying later into the day, the sun finally decided to show a little.

Description of the Bird

pale olive back, streaking on the chest and flanks, streaking was lacking on the throat area. very white supercillium and long reaching towardsthe back of the head( nape area), longer bill than Northern Waterthrush. Bubblegum pink legs. Habitat that it was found in suggests Louisiana over Northern, but you can't always speculate- especially during migration. But habitat does have an influence on where you find bird species.
Behaviors: Bird was feeding along the waters edge picking at the surface. The bird was bobbing its tail up and down and to the side very slowly and fluidly. Much slower that the Northern Waterthrush. This bird was around its favorite habitat- Brushy banks with a running stream and low dwelling bushes.
Call: no song that I heard, just a sharp "Chink" call as it was flushed
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

pale phase of the Northern Waterthrush. Distinguished from Northern Waterthrush white under parts and buffy flanks, the white and much broader supercillium and bubblegum pink legs.
