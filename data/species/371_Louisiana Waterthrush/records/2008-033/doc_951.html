Description of the Bird

By behavior and overall looks easily a waterthrush with the following of note

--Bright pink legs

--A supercillium that was rather indistinct in front of the eye (in fact seen head on it almost disappeared), but was really bright and broad behind the eye, continuing so far beyond they eye it almost reached the back of the head joining the line from the other side. Well, not quite, it just looked like it was going to do that, especiall viewed head on.

--White and largely unmarked throat

--Distinct buffy wash on flanks, especially just where the legs joined the body.
Behaviors: I watched the bird feed actively in on gravel banks in Left Hand Creek under the cover of the Pike St. bridge for more than 75 minutes. Then it began to get more active flying up and down the creek from the bridge and into stream side brush on the north side.

The bird had a very noticable and unusual "tail wag". It would jerk its tail down and then bring it back to place with a slower and somewhat sideways motion.
Call: a loud chip heard on a few occasions when not feeding
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Northern Waterthrush would have a different eyeline, leg color, throat and tail wag behavior.
