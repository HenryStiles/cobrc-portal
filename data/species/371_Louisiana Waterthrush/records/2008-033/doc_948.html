Description of the Bird

Roughly sparrow-sized. Above brown (all one shade), below white with brown streaks on flanks and chest. Streaking sparser than I normally see on a Northern (streaking did not form a necklace at the throat, for example). Some buff wash on the flanks. Streaking did not include throat, which was white. Strong white supercilium, narrow at lores, wider above and behind eye, with a slight flare onto the nape. Legs bubble-gum pink. Vent white, with no wash. No wingbars. Wings brown with longish primaries. Tail brown. Bill thin and pointed, longer proportionally than Yellow-rumped Warbler bill.
Behaviors: Found wading in Lefthand Creek where creek was shallow and rocky. Consistently wagged tail with a twisting motion (a semicircular path, rather than the straight up and down of Northern Waterthrush). Sometimes walked along the creek's edge to feed. Roosted in srtreamside willows. Was not observed above eye level.
Call: Did not vocalize in my presence.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Most similar to Northern Waterthrush, which sometimes has a white supercilium, but which never flares onto the nape; has denser streaking on the chest and flanks and a streaked throat; has darker legs; and wags tail with straight up and down motion.
