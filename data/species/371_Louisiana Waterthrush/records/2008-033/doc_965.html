Description of the Bird

Large, long legged mostly brown and white water-loving warbler. Bill fairly long, straight, and stout, nearly black. Strong white supercilium that extends well onto the nape, widening a bit at the posterior end. Supraloral portion of this pale stripe narrow and tinged buffy. Crown grayish-brown, with that color continuing on to the back, wings, and tail creating otherwise unmarked upperparts. A darker brown eyeline contrasts with somewhat splotchy cheek, with a whiter malar and grayish-brown malar stripe separating the white, nearly unmarked throat. The breast and belly have a white ground coloration, marked with dark brown streaks made of elongated spots nearly connected but with pale gaps throughout. The streaks are paler on the upper breast and flanks, surrounded by a tinge of salmon-buff coloration. The undertail coverts appear to be unmarked white. The legs are a vivid bubble-gum pink. The primaries only reach about the end of the undertail coverts, and appear a little darker than the dorsal surface of the tail. Upon close inspection the primaries can be seen to have very thin pale edges.
Behaviors: The bird was spending most of its time in a culvert under a bridge on Lefthand Creek. It was foraging on rocky bars, mostly at the water's edge but also more in the middle of the bars. It made a few forays out of the culvert both up and downstream, mostly staying along the water's edge but once flying up to a nearby backyard hedge. Whenever the bird stayed in place it bobbed its tail fairly slowly, with the tip of its tail describing a U shape. The bird walked from place to place instead of hopping.
Call: Loud call note heard a few times, similar to Northern Waterthrush.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Few other warblers this brown and white- traits lead immediately to a waterthrush. Northern Waterthrush often tinged more yellow on underparts but whitish individuals lack salmon-buffy tint around flank stripes. Unmarked throat and bubble-gum pink legs typical of Louisiana, not Northern. Bi-colored supercilium, (buffy in front of the eye, white behind) typical of Louisiana, not Northern. Extension of supercilium onto nape with widened flare at the posterior end typical of Louisiana, not Northern. Bill longer and stouter than expected in Northern Waterthrush.
