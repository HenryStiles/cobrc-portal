Description of the Bird

This was a waterthrush based on the short tail, white supercilium, pinkish legs, tail-bobbing, white breast with dark streaks.

The bird will be separated from Northern Waterthrush in the comparison portion of the summary.
Behaviors: Fed constantly, mostly on small aquatic insects on rocks and at water's edge. Tail movement, especially a slow tail roll out to the left followed to a tail roll out to the right and then back to the left again, over and over.
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The only other look-alike species is Northern Waterthrush and to some degree Ovenbird, both congeners.

Eliminate Ovenbird, based on that species's orange crown alone.

Comparison between Longmont waterthrush with white-bellied Northern Waterthrushes.

White supercillium

Longmont bird had a very white, broad white supercillium, that stayed broad and did not taper to a point behind its eye. It was continued a considerable distance past the eye, out as far as the upper back. The anterior portion of the white supercillium was slightly grayish. In comparison, the super of a NOWA tapers as it passes the eye, and is less broad and less white. This character was noticeable when the Longmont bird moved out of the dark shadows. The width of the super was noticeable at least 50% of the time; but at some times the broad width was not noticeable.

Tail length. Releative to the overall length, the Longmont bird had what appeared to me immediately as a significantly shorter tail than the more commonly seen NOWAs.

Overall body shape. Perhaps because of the short tail, the overall appearance of the body of the Longmont bird was chunky and footall shaped, compared with what I think of as a more streamlined NOWA

Back color

When the Longmont bird came out of the shadows, i noticed that the browns were noticebly lighter than the gray brown of NOWA.

Basal color of breast and belly

The Longmont bird was whiter with fewer less well-defined and browner streaks, than what I know from NOWA, which has spots usually on the throat and darker streaks and cleaner streaks on the flanks.

No dark spots on throat vs. noticeable spotting on NOWA. This character was looked for and it was unspotted on the throat.

Leg color. Compared to NOWA this bird had noticeably pinker legs, not the dusky pink legs of NOWA. This was the real color pink.

Tail motion. this was the most noticeable character. The bird moved it tail out to one side and out to the other side in a manner unlike the up-and-down tail pumping of NOWA
