Description of the Bird

Larger warbler sized bird very similar to Northern Waterthrush. Very white underparts with slight buffy wash on flanks. Clean white throat. Obvious white eye arch below eyes. Bill looked larger then Northern. White supercillium that went behind eye and was quite broad. Very pink legs. Brownish blurry streaky on flanks. Tail wagging was circular rather than the up and down of a Northern.
Behaviors: Actively feeding with characteristic tail wagging at all times. Would often go hide in rushes under the stream bed.
Call: some chips heard
Plumage: adult
