Description of the Bird

Overall, the bird had a dark back and wings with white undersides showing dark longitudinal streaking. The bird had a broad white supercilium which did not taper posterior to the birds eye. It's throat was white and unmarked and bordered by dark malar stripes. The flanks showed a faint salmon colored tinge which extended posteriorly towards the bird's vent. The bird also had bright pink legs.
Behaviors: The bird was observed voraciously eating small invertebrates amongst the rocks in the stream. The bird constantly bobbed its tail in an exaggerated U shape with the bobbing motion involving nearly the birds entire body.
Call: None
Plumage: Spring Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Similar species include Northern Waterthrush.

Northern Waterthrush is excluded due to the pinkish tinge present on the flanks and near the vent coupled with the white underside color. Additionally, the uniformly broad white supercilium, bright pink legs and exaggerated tail bobbing suggest Louisiana Waterthrush over Northern Waterthrush.
