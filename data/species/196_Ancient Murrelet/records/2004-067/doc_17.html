Description of the Bird

Small alcid, very much smaller than nearby Gadwalls. Yellow bill, dark eye, black face and nape. A white patch extended from behind the eye onto the lower face and the breast. The back was a uniform dark gray, as was the inner half of the wings - the outer wings were black. The body near the waterline and the under tail was a dirty gray. Did not see the underwings or breast because the bird did not fly. The chin was dark, but was a smudge rather than uniform. Sibley shows the adult with a dark chin, and the juvenile with a rather light gray smudge. Thus, I am not sure if this was a juvenile, more likely, or an adult.
Behaviors: Sitting on a small lake, and occasionally flapped wings. Did not fly or dive.
Call: No sounds heard.
Plumage: Basic winter

Similar Species Discussion

Long-billed Murrelet - bill color, head and upper body was black not brown, face pattern, flanks and undertail darker.
Marbled, Kittlitz's, Xantus's and Craveri's - same as above.
Auklets and Dovekie - not the right shape, color, etc.
All other Alcids - too small.
