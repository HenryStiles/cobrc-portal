Description of the Bird

A very small bird in comparison to Gadwall nearby. The head and hindneck were mostly black, except the throat was mostly white with black intermixed and two bold white eye crescents; the short, thin bill was dusky yellow; Underparts were completely bright white, except for gray barring along the flanks; the upperparts were a solid medium gray color; the tail was very short; in flight the bird always looked like it was about to land - flying with its head slightly higher than the rest of the body; flight was very rapid, with the short pointed wings little less than a blur; the underwings showed white underwing coverts and gray flight feathers.
Behaviors: Mostly rested on the water, although I did see it fly from one end of the lake to the other when I first arrived.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: immature
