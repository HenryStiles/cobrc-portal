Description of the Bird

An obvious loon (large swimming bird with big head, straight bill, slightly larger than nearby Western Grebe) with distinct, pale fringes to all upperparts feathers. The forehead was blackish and the rear crown and nape were medium gray, bordered on the sides of the neck by a vertical strip of blackish. The chin and throat were white, bordered on the neck sides by the same strip of blackish - there were no intrusions of white into the blackish strip and no dark intrusions into the white throat. The center of the chest was white and the sides and flanks were dark.

The dark eyes were bordered above by the gray crown and below by a whitish patch stretching from the base of the bill through the auriculars. The bird had no pale eye ring, as most other loon species sport.

In comparison to that of the Western Grebe nearby, the bill was thicker-based and shorter; the bill was vaguely bicolored, dark above, grayish below. I did not see the legs
Behaviors: Swimming and diving from the water's surface, the latter of which it accomplished without leaping at all, just stuck its head under water and had its body follow the head.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Arctic Loon eliminated by size only slightly larger than WEGR, by head being held horizontal (no tilt upward), smaller bill than that species would have, and no distinct white flank patch. Common Loon ruled out by smaller size, bill size, lack of eye ring, and neck pattern. Red-throated Loon eliminated by larger size, bill size and shape, lack of eye ring, and back and neck patterns.
