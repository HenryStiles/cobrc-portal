Description of the Bird

This was a relatively small dark-backed loon. The bill was straight and gray with a dark gray or balckish culmen. It was medium-sized in proportion to the bird. The head above the eye and back of the neck were dark gray, while the head below the eye and the front of the neck where white except for an obvious dark line across the throat where the head joins the neck. The head had one modest bulge where the forehead meets the crown, but this was not a prominent feature as in a Common Loon. The white of the front of the neck extended down to the upper chest at the waterline. The back, rear-end, and flanks were all dark gray or blackish and no white showed above the waterline anywhere on the body. The underparts were only briefly and partiallyobserved when the bird dove.
Behaviors: The bird cruised the lake surface and dove leisurely while I observed it. It did not preen or fly. At times it swam within a 100 yards of the shore where I was observing it.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Size eliminates larger loons as does bill proportion and shape. Red-throated Loon would be much lighter and have more extensive white on face and neck and bill would appear upturned. Arctic Loon would be quite similar, but doesn't have the dark band across the top of the neck and shows white on the flanks at the waterline.
