Description of the Bird

The bird was floating in the water, slowly moving around.  There were no other birds within several hundred feet of it.

It was obviously a loon, based on drab plumage, horizontal posture, low, broad body shape, overall size, and long, dagger-like bill.  I had been hoping to see a Common Loon there, and after finding the bird with binocs, my first thought was that it was a Common Loon.  As soon as I observed it in the scope, the bird did not look like a Common Loon. 

It had a decidedly lighter build than a Common Loon.  The bill was straight and relatively slender.  On the side of the neck, there was a straight, vertical boundary between white in front and medium/dark gray in back.  The boundary between white and gray was horizontal across the face and through the eye.  The head had a gently rounded, not blocky shape.  The upper surface of the neck and cap were a smooth gray.  The back had a bold checkerboard pattern.  

My main reason for thinking this was a juvenile, not adult, was the straight white-dark transisition line running all the way up the side of the neck to the chin.

Similar Species Discussion

I ruled out Common Loon via the following characteristics:
Smaller bird, more slender bill
Straight transition line between white and dark on side of neck
Straight horizontal transition between white and dark across face
Gently rounded head, not blocky
Smooth gray upperparts, not dark brown

I ruled out Red-throated Loon via:
Dark gray upperparts
Straight bill
Bold checkboard pattern on back

I ruled out Arctic Loon via:
Lack of white flanks

Resources Used
