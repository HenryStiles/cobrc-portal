Description of the Bird

A loon seen near a couple Common Loons that was smaller, slimmer, and neater-plumaged. The straight, dark bill was noticeably smaller than that on the nearby COLOs and did not have an upturned mandible. The gray on the head nearly surrounded a pale patch below the eye that extended from the base of the bill through the auriculars. The crown and nape were gray and paler than the forehead and the dark vertical strip along the side of the neck. The dark vertical strip was straight and clean edged with no dark in the white throat and no white crossing the dark strip.

The chin, throat, and upper chest were white, the sides and flanks were dark. The upperparts were dark with, even at the long distance, obvious pale fringes to all feathers creating thin bands of pale crosswise on the back. There was no tail to speak of and I did see the legs. The eyes appeared dark and were not surrounded by pale eye rings.
Behaviors: Swimming on water's surface, from which it dove underwater once while I was watching.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

COLO eliminated by small size, small bill, neck pattern, lack of vague eye ring. ARLO ruled out by small size, small bill held horizontally, dark flanks. RTLO eliminated by upperparts and neck patterns, lack of eye ring, larger size, bill shape and posture.
