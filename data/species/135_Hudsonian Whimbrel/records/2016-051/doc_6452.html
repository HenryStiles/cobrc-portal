Description of the Bird

While looking at the continuing Western Gull on Pond 115, I saw a curlew-like shorebird in a marshy/mudflat area across the road from the large pond. I glassed the bird and clearly saw a long black decurved bill, boldly striped head with a dark eyeline, and an overall brown color (not the warm buffy coloration of LBCU). Another car passed and spooked the bird, which then flew around Pond 115 several times allowing for good looks in flight, with distinctly barred brown auxilliaries. 

Similar Species Discussion

Long-billed Curlew would be the only other expected large shorebird with a decurved bill. This bird lacked the overall warm color of LBCU, especially in the underwings; LCBU would be clean cinammon colored, while this bird was dull brown and barred underwing. The bill was also shorted than LBCU, and LBCU would not show a dark line through the eye. Bristle-thighed Curlew was eliminated by the lack of a light band across the tail. European/Siberian Whimbrel ssp. were also eliminated by the lack of white on the rump/lower back. 

Resources Used

I have seen many Whimbrels and Long-billed Curlews on the Texas coast during spring and fall migration, so I was familiar with the species and did not use a guide book in the field

Previous Experience

From Texas originally, I have seen Whimbrels on the Texas Coast almost annually for most of my life. I have also seen Whimbrels in California before. Long-billed Curlews are a species that I have even more experience with than Whimbrel, and are a species I have seen annually on the Texas coast and in the farmland surrounding Austin.
