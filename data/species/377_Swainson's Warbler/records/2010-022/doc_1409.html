Habitat

wetland stream with standing water, scattered willows and cottonwoods and lots of mud with debris in it. The bird foraged exclusively in the mud but very close to the water.

Optics:  Leica 10x42 Ultravid bins
Distance:  30 feet
Light:  sun was mostly overhead with bright light. Lighting was a little harsh.

Description of the Bird

The was an all-brown-backed small bird with a long pointed warbler bill. The crown was a warm brown. It had a light supercillium and a dark eyestripe. The undersides were light, some parts buff and other offwhite. The dorsal surface of the tail was rusty brown. The tail at times (when rump tilted up) looked short, but at other times it appeared to be long. The tibio-tarsus was bright pink, darkest pink at the joint. Without wingbars.
Behaviors: This was a hungry bird that stayed in a 10 foot radius and ate "stuff" out of the mud, often opening its mouth incredibly wide as accentuated by its very long bill. The highest the bird went was about 8-14 inches off the ground (wet mud with very shallow water adjacent to its feeding spot).
Call: non heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo looks superficially like this bird, but it has a hooked vireo bill, greener back, whiter ventral surface, without the brown and rufous brown tones. It doesn't forage in the mud for extended periods.

Arcitc Warbler has a supercillium the curves upwards, difffernt posture and no rusty tones, and darker gray legs.

Worm-eating Warbler has distinctive striped head pattern.

None of the above have the very long bill of Swainson's, the longest of any warbler.

Additional Information

Comments: The length of this bird's bill was very interesting as was its approach to foraging. It often opened its bill at what seemed an extreme width and shoved it into the mud and closed it. It also stuck its head inside of dead leaves in the mud, often without success.
