Description of the Bird

First seen flying past and was unsure what it was.  Was intrigued by its "warm" brown tones, plump body, longish bill, longish tail (the bill and tail length gave the bird an even proportioned, symmetrical profile in terms of how much bird protruded in either direction from the wings).  Positively identified when it hopped up about 15 feet away and sat on the top black strap of a 3-layered strap fence used to keep horses from leaving the windbreak and getting into alfalfa field north of the windbreak.  At this point I had maybe 5 seconds to believe what my eyes were seeing: a large warbler, a very big pale yellow beak (think, Barbra Streisand); rusty cap; white supercilium; large black eye; black line thru the eye (strong in front of eye, weak extending behind eye into the nape area); pale gray underparts tinged with yellow; medium brown body, wings and tail; long tail; bright white undertail coverts; very bright pink legs that seemed long for a typical warbler.

The only things I saw the bird do were flip leaves in the think leaf litter, quiver its tail, walk and fly about 3-5 feet off the ground from one area of the windbreak to another.  The only sound I heard it vocalize was a quiet, high-pitched "seet" while flying.  On a few occasions I also heard it sorting thru the leaf litter (not as loud as a towhee but similar).

Similar Species Discussion

Worm-eating Warbler has a black crown stripe and gray undertail coverts, bill is gray, not yellowish.

Ovenbird has striped underparts and orange on the crown, big white eyering.

Catharus thrushes all have spots or stripes underneath, much different profile, smaller beaks, larger in size.

Previous Experience

I have seen one in Colorado (Bill Howe found this bird, Fort Collins, documented in CFO Journal).  I have seen two in south Texas (High Island's Smith Woods).  I have stared at this bird's picture in the books many a time, memorizing the field marks for just such a moment as I had.
