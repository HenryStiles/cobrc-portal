Description of the Bird

This bird was first found by David Leatherman.  I first saw the bird in flight in heavy shade, and the distinctive shape (large head extension in front of wings about matched the length of body and tail behind wings) and coloration (dark above, pale below, without streaking anywhere) convinced me that it was the bird, but I was not willing to "count" it given such a poor look.  Some 15 minutes later, Janeal and Kathy refound the bird and got me on it.  It was a chunky, medium-sized warbler, with a substantial bill that was mostly pale.  The upperparts were brown (from forehead to tail tip) contrasting with a creamy supercilium and off white to creamy chest and belly.  The bird had a thin, blackish eyeline (stronger behind the eye than in front), dark eyes, and pink legs.  I did not see the vent and undertail coverts region well, but what I saw was pale.  The bird foraged on the ground among dead leaves.  I did not hear it vocalize.

Similar Species Discussion

There is really no similar species, but Worm-eating Warbler ruled out by head pattern (lack of dark lateral crown stripes) and foraging behavior.  Sparrows obviously ruled out by structure, bill size and shape, and plumage pattern.
