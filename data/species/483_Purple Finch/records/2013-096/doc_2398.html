Habitat

wooded (gambel oak) yard on canyon edge

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  naked eyeballs, Swarovski 8.5 X 42 binoculars and camera equipment
Distance:  10 - 20 feet
Light:  variable

Description of the Bird

A dark finch with bold face pattern and no trace of an eyering. See photos.
Behaviors: Visiting sunflower seed feeder, occasionally bathing in water feature.
Call: none
Plumage: female-type

Similar Species Discussion

House Finch and more closely Cassin's Finch. This bird is darker than most Cassin's Finches and has a black mask with no trace of eye-ring.

Resources Used

experience, photos of PUFI and CAFI and Sibley's Guide to North AMerican Birds

Previous Experience

I have had Cassin's Finches at my feeders for an entire winter (2008). I have seen Purple Finches 20 or so times in CO, KS, and NC

Notes

Notes made AFTER observation

Additional Information

Comments: I know of only one other Douglas Co. record and I suspect this is the longest staying PUFI recorded in CO, seemingly over-wintering nearby.
