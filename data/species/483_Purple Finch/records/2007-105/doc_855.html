Description of the Bird

See photos and similar species.
Behaviors: Brandon, Tony and I got out of the car and I immediately saw this bird perched in a conifer. Before lifting my binocular, I said "Purple Finch". Tony and Brandon came over and we were able to see and photograph the bird at leisure. It remained in the same tree the entire time we saw it, just changing perches until if flew off. It never vocalized, even when it flew off.
Call: None
Plumage: female-plumaged

Similar Species Discussion

Cassin's Finches appear more finely streaked on the underparts (this bird with thicker, more blurry streaking). Undertail coverts on Cassin's Finch are finely streaked (this bird unmarked undertail coverts). Cassin's Finch has less distinct face pattern (supercilium and malar on this bird very pronounced). The culmen on this bird was also more curved, not straight and long as on Cassin's Finch.
