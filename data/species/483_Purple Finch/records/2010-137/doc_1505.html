Description of the Bird

This House Finch sized bird had mottled brown streaked back, brownish wings with faint wing bars plus fairly long primary extension to base of its darker brownish tail.

Underparts were very bright white with heavily streaked breast and upper belly. Lower belly and under tail coverts were brilliant white & unstreaked.

Head was brown with very distinct white supercilium and bold white malar stripe. These stripes were separated by brown auricular patch.

Bill was conical in shape appearing shorter and stubbier than Cassin's Finch. I did not see legs nor bill color well.
Behaviors: It perched high in trees giving its slurred series of notes several times suggesting an immature male.
When flying from tree to tree and finally flying off, it gave several of its distinct "pik" notes.
Call: A rising slurred "shreelayup" not unlike a Pine Grosbeak and more slurred than the Cassin's more distinct notes of "gid-dee-yup." When in flight, bird gave a flat plaintive "pik" note several times.
Plumage: probable immature male

Similar Species Discussion

Some Cassin's Finches can look very similar to Purples. But Cassin's have streaked under tail coverts as do some Purples. But if the undertail coverts are undoubtably unstreaked as on bird I saw, this field mark almost always indicates a Purple Finch.

The "pik" flight call note also strongly indicates a Purple Finch.
