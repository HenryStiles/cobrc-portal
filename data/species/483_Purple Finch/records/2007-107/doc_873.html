Description of the Bird

5 females and 2 males were observed. The females had distinct supercilium and moustachial stripes. These facials stripes were as distinct as the most distinct I've ever seen on Cassin's Finch. The females had distinct striping across the breast that was had more brownish color to it than a plain gray striping that would be more expected in Cassin's Finch. On one female plain white undertail coverts could be seen.

The males had extensive pinkish red over the head, across the breast and down toward the belly. There was no differentiation between the shade of red on the crown and on the head, breast and so forth (ruling out Cassin's Finch). There was red on the back and even some in the wings. No distinct brown striping was apparent on the breast.
Behaviors: The birds were associating together in a large flock of robins, starlings, waxwings and so forth at the back of a home that seemed to have some sort of ditch running along its back fence. House Finches were present can seen to be distinctly different.
Call: not heard (inside a car)
Plumage: male and female

Similar Species Discussion

Clearly distinct from nearby House Finches. Males had different red shade that was more extensive and had no brown chest stripes. Females had distinct facial stripes.

Cassin's Finch ruled out as males were entirely different in pattern of red on the head and females were more brightly colored and at least one showed no striping on undertail coverts.
