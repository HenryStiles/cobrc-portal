Description of the Bird

Walking between buildings on the Front Range Community College - Larimer campus, I heard this bird before I saw it. In my initial notes that I made regarding the song, I only noted that the bird's song was more hoarse/deeper than that of a House Find. In regard to the call, I noted that as the bird flew away it was giving a call that I could best describe as a deep "chip" similar to a Red-winged Blackbird. When I saw the bird before it flew, my observations were as follows. It was roughly the size of a House Finch. It had red down its chest, around and over the top of its head, and all the way down its back to its tail. I could not make out any distinct, crisp, brown streaking in the red on its sides. Also, there was no streaking on its white undertail. That is all that I wrote down at the time.

Similar Species Discussion

House Finch and Cassin's Finch were considered as possibilities. While the song was definitely different than a House Finch, I don't think my description of it could separate the two (I don't think stating that they were different or it was more hoarse/deeper firmly identifies it). It certainly couldn't separate Cassin's Finch. The call, however, eliminated House Finch because of its similarities to Red-winged Blackbird. I've heard Cassin's Finches give a few different calls before, but I've never heard any that sounded identical to the calls produced by this individual. The visuals eliminated House Finch due to the clean white undertail, red on the back, and the red on the sides. In all these particulars, I'm not aware of any cases where House Finches look like this. Again from the visual side of things, Cassin's Finch was eliminated by the clean white undertail that lacked brown streaking and the noticeable red on the sides. While Cassin's does have a hint of reddish on its sides, it is fairly faint and in the case of stronger-than-normal red streaking, it is usually mixed with white producing an overall faint red effect still (I've not seen an individual with distinct red on its sides).

Previous Experience

I have a few memories of seeing these while still living where I grew up in Maryland, but I never intensively studied them. One year previous to this sighting, I had been back to MD in winter and seen them and two months previous, I had been to the west coast and seen them. I see House Finches fairly regularly (basically every single day except for the occasions where I'm stuck all day inside a building with no windows looking at any bird feeders). I've seen a number of Cassin's Finches as well.
