Description of the Bird

Haemorhous (formerly Carpodacus) finch fist found on Fountain CBC by Floyd Group. Female type plumage retained two years by males and shows some signs of molting in some reddish feathers. 

Purple vs. Cassin's

From my photos from 16Jan16

bird has unstreaked undertail coverts a character of PUFI, streaked in CAFI
face pattern with two whitish bands eliminates House Finch
bill shorter and with curved culmen not seen on Cassin's which as a longer, more pointed bill with a straight culmen
lacks white eyering found in Cassin's
Breast streaking wide and blurry or not neatly delineated as it is in CAFI 
Primary project seems long but probably not as long as seen in CAFI

Didn't try to separate Eastern PUFI vs. Western PUFI

Similar Species Discussion

bill size (short) and curved culmen eliminates CAFI.  Face patern eliminates HOFI.

Previous Experience

Seen both western and eastern PUFIs in California and Maine plus a few PUFIs, but not, many in Colorado. Seen CAFIs in CA and in Colorado, some years a lot of them. Photographed and studied both species, but mostly CAFIs.
