Description of the Bird

The plumage is definitive basic but that was not an option.  Also, I am doing this report mostly from memory but also reviewing photos.

The bird never made any calls.  The bird showed "purple" all over the the dorsal side of the body and most of the ventral side excepting the lower belly and undertail coverts.  The culmen looked curved to my eye and the overall bill seemed shorter and stouter.  No obvious eye-ring.

Similar Species Discussion

Cassin's Finch was eliminated by bill structure, lack of eye-ring and lack of streaks on the undertail.  The streaking on the sides were heavier than expected on Cassin's.  The nape was red/purple and the crown did contrast as much as it typically would on a Cassin's.

House Finch was elminated by the lack of streaking on the undertail coverts, the extensive red/purple all over the bird and the facial coloring.  I also believe that typically a PUFI is longer winged which I thought this bird was but nothing to compare to in the field at the time and the tail had a deep fork to it compared to a typical HOFI which is more squared off.

Those were the only two species I considered at the time.  I suppose I could try to rule out Pine Grosbeak and Common Rosefinch but I don't think it worth my time for this individual.  ;)

Previous Experience

While living in CA I saw these birds on occassion, yes I think this bird is not from that population but adult males are almost impossible to tell apart anyway.  I have seen 100s of PUFIs.  I see House Finches almost everyday and have for 30+ years.  I have seen 1000s of Cassin's Finches and grew up with them at my feeders.
