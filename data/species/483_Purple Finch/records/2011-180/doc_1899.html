Description of the Bird

Similar to familiar house finches, except for bold head pattern, no eye ring, deeply notched tail. Head pattern was distinctive enough that I knew I had not seen one like this in Boulder. The closest match in my Sibley's guide is with eastern purple finch (F). Photos available.
Behaviors: At my backyard feeder (sunflower seeds) for about 15 minutes on Christmas day (2011), with a mixed flock of house finches and American finches. Behaved pretty much the same as the house finches, but readily distinguishable from these by head pattern. My backyard borders on Boulder open space. After feeding for several minutes, the bird flew west, towards foothills. Not seen since.
Call: not heard
Plumage: n/a

Similar Species Discussion

House finch: I have seen too many of these to confuse them with the subject bird. The latter had bold head pattern and crisp streaking on breast, neither of which is present in house finch.

Cassin's finch: Subject bird had no eye ring and bolder head pattern than typical in Cassin's. Also deeply notched tail. All of this is apparent in photos.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Never seen one like this in Colorado. Have occasionally seen purple finch while visiting family in New England.
