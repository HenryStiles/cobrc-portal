Description of the Bird

Approximately 6" bird with stout, conical bill, prominent white streaks on head above and below the eye.  Short, dark streaks on flanks, no streaks on undertail or mid belly.  No eye ring.  Color a much darker, richer brown than nearby House Finches.  Female-plumaged so it could have been a female or first-year male.  Did not hear any calls as we were inside the house. 

Similar Species Discussion

Considered Cassin's Finch, which is common in this area.  The female-plumaged Cassin's Finch head pattern is not as distinctive as the bird we saw, and a Cassin's would have a pale eye-ring.  Cassin's would also have streaking on the undertail coverts. 

The nearby House Finches had unmarked heads and a much less distinct pattern of streaking than the Purple Finch. 

Resources Used

National Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America, Fifth Edition, p 456.
The Sibley Guide to Birds, copyright 2000, page 528-529.
We sent a link to David's Flickr photos of the bird to Tony Leukering and Tom Litteral, who each independently confirmed it as Purple Finch.

Previous Experience

House and Cassin's Finches are common in Steamboat Springs, and we have had large numbers of both at our feeders in past years.  However, so far this winter we have not seen any Cassin's Finches at our feeder.  We have seen Purple Finches in several locations around the US, but do not have extensive experience with them.
