Description of the Bird

Finch was first reported on 5 January 2009 and maybe as early as 5-7 December 2008 as this date is recorded in Colorado Birds, Vol 43 No 3. July 2009, page 220. Finch was coming to a feeder which gave a great opportunity to view field marks.
Approximately the size of House Finches it was feeding with at the feeder. Female or first year winter male Purple Finch. Bold head pattern with pronounced white eyebrows, bill between House Finch and Cassin's Finch, chest, short brown streaks and not as dark and narrow as expected for a Cassin's. Undertail coverts were completely white and lacked and noticeable streaking. The noticeable malar (lateral throat stripe) may suggest this finch is an eastern race.

Similar Species Discussion

House Finch and Cassin's Finch are the most problematic. House Finches were present and the direct comparison made it easy to eliminate them. Cassin's are our default finch in spring, summer and winter but few winter at low elevation. Female Cassin's have a weak eyebrow, streaks on the undertail coverts, and narrow streaks on chest and breast on a whitish background. Cassin's bill is strong, long and pointed and straight bill.
The Purple Finch had a strong eyebrow, no streaks on undertail coverts, wide dark streaks on chest and breast on an off-white background. Bill was not as long and pointed as would be expected for a Cassin's and there was some curve on the upper mandible.

Previous Experience

Purple Finch has been encountered many times in our travels on the west coast and in the east. I have only found Purple Finch, however, three times in Colorado. There is a record in which I sumitted in 2001, Vancorum, Montrose County with photos to CBRC. E-bird has another record that I sumitted with photos from Nucla.
