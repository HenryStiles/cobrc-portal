Description of the Bird

A "Carpodacus" finch first noted because of its call: pit, pit. Face with solid dark auriculars contrasting sharply with clean white supercilium and white submoustachial. Broad brown malar stripes merging into moderately thick brown chest streaking against white background, making bird very contrasty No eyering. Bill pudgier, not as long, and somewhat stouter, than that of Cassin's Finch. Bill all dark. Bird not seen "lower" than upper wing.
Behaviors: Sitting in upper branches of tall bush
Call: pit-pit
Plumage: imm male or female

Similar Species Discussion

Cassin's Finch would have finer streaking below, streaks within supercilium, longer bill, different call, eyering. House Finch would have much duller face pattern, different call.
