Description of the Bird

Same size, basic shape as a House Finch. But different color and bill. Showed drab greenish-brownish back and pale crescent marking below eye. Greenish color, heavy streaking on chest and belly, and smooth, rounded head shape seemed to rule out female Cassin's. Color and subdued contrast of head pattern suggested "Pacific" bird. Was seen in the vicinity of female House Finches, which are common at our feeders. Was definitely not a female House Finch. In addition to the color and the crescent below the eye, the bill appeared darker and heavier, and the culmen appeared to be basically straight.
Behaviors: foraging, feeding underneath our feeders
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult/1st year identical
