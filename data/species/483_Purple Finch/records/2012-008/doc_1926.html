Description of the Bird

A female-type plumaged Carpodacus finch visited my feeder briefly on two days. The strongly contrasting brown-and-white face pattern with a whitish supercilium and submoustacial stripe with relatively broad, dark malar stood out at first glance. This bird also had notched tail with white, unstreaked undertail coverts.

Both sightings were too brief or at such an angle that bill shape and primary projection were not noted.
Behaviors: (S)he liked hulled sunflower seeds.
Call: none
Plumage: female-type

Similar Species Discussion

The strongly contrasting face pattern easily eliminated House Finch. The pattern was also more strongly contrasting than that of Cassin's Finch. This bird did not exhibit the pale eye-ring of a Cassin's Finch and had a dark, broad malar and unstreaked undertail coverts unlike Cassin's Finch.
