Description of the Bird

A midsized grebe. Smaller than Western Grebes it was seen with, but larger than Eared or Pied-billed. Neck proportionately shorter than Western Grebe. Crown, neck and back dark. Head somewhat square at back. Check, chin and throat white. Bill yellow and heavier than Western Grebe bill. Neck rufous. Flanks gray.
Behavior: Bird was swimming on Lake John often near Western Grebes. On July 13, it was diving frequently. On July 14 it was diving less.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Breeding adult

Similar Species Discussion

White cheek eliminated Horned Grebe. Rufous neck eliminated Western or Clark's Grebe.
