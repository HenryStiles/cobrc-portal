Description of the Bird

It was in with peeps, but much larger than any of them. It was similar in size to a Killdeer, but much chunkier, shorter, with a horizontal posture.

The underparts were clean white, at least as seen from behind.

The upperparts were gray. The wings had a scaly pattern. The back and mantle were a smooth gray.

The face was gray.

The bill was of medium length, quite thick near its base, then thinning, with a slight curve down.

The legs were pale
Behaviors: feeding with peeps on a mudflat
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

From the bird's size (determined well, based on direct comparison to other birds) and shape, and the bill length, other possible species are:
Pectoral Sandpiper
Dunlin
Stilt Sandpiper
Ruff

The scaly grayish upperparts rule out Pectoral Sandpiper. The pale legs and chunky posture rule out Ruff. The thick bill and pale legs rule out Dunlin. A Stilt Sandpiper would be much less chunky, would have a longer bill, and more obviously yellow-green legs.
