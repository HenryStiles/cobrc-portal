Description of the Bird

Smaller than BBPL and larger than Stilt Sand. 
Kept company with 5 BB Plovers
Gray above, white below. I could see paler edges to scaps and wing coverts in photos, but could not make this out in field
Black legs, short to medium length
Bit of grayish smudging on sides, more extensive on sides of chest. 
Whitish supercilium and gray line through eye to bill. Throat white. Crown gray as back. 

In flight, prominent white stripe along posterior edge of secondary and primary coverts, more prominent along secondary coverts. Tail gray. Rump pale but finely barred. 

Bill black, slightly drooped, and a bit longer than is typical of Red Knot

Similar Species Discussion

Dunlin: smaller, longer bill, white rump with dark central stripe, etc
Great Knot: Darker and more distinct markings on chest, different facial pattern
