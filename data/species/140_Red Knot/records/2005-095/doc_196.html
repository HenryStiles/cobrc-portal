Description of the Bird

The bird was a shorebird about the size of a Killdeer, though shorter wings and a bulkier build gave it the impression of being a bigger bird, despite the shorter legs. The bird had a bulky build, with a short, thick neck, short wings that barely extended beyond the tail, and a heavy bill contributing to this impression.

The bird's crown was meduim gray, becoming streaky towards the forecrown, which was whitish. The bird had a thin but bold white very pale gray or white supercilium, extending almost all the way back to the nape. There was then a very thick gray eyestripe, the same color as the crown. The throat was white with dense gray streaks. The chest of the bird was white with very dense but small gray spots. The flanks of the bird were boldy spotted/barred with gray on white. The belly and undertail coverts as far as I could see were white.

The nape of the bird was the same gray color as the crown, connecting to the crown and eyestripe. The back of the bird was also this color, completely uniform, as were the wing coverts. The flight feathers were blackish.

The tail of the bird was the same gray color as the back, and the uppertail coverts and rump were also gray, though slightly paler, giving the bird a uniform look in flight, with no contrasting patches of color. The wings had a faint but noticeable white wing stripe.

The bird's bill was black, and very slightly longer than the head, and very thick (almost twice the thickness of the eye at the base, tapering to a little less then the thickness of the eye at the blunt tip). I did not notice the color of the legs in the field, but they were very short (for a shorebird), giving the bird a squat appearance.
Behaviors: the bird was with a flock off Killdeer on the edge of the water, foraging for food. When approached too closely, it flushed with the Killdeer to a different part of the reservoir.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of shorebirds were ruled out by the uniform gray back, and especially the overall uniform color in flight, without a white rump.

Willet was ruled out by much smaller size, as well as very different wing and rump pattern.

Yellowlegs were ruled out by smaller size, much shorter legs and neck, thicker but shorter bill, and lack of a white rump.

Wandering Tattler was ruled out by stronger face pattern with a bold supercilium, dense speckling on chest and barring on flanks, different shape, with a fatter and less elongated look, shorter and entirely black bill, and faint white wing stripe on the wing in flight.
