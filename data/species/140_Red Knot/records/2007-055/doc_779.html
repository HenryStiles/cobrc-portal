Description of the Bird

This bird was a plump dowitcher sized and shaped bird, but having a much shorter bill, a bit more than half the length of nearby L-b Dowitchers. Its legs also appeared proportionately shorter than Dowitchers.

Bird was overall gray above and whitish below with a whitish supercilium and somewhat peaked crown.
The smooth unscaled gray upperparts suggested an adult bird. Whitish underparts became a bit grayer on the breast with diffuse dusky speckling on lower breast and obscure dusky barring on flanks. I did not see bird fly, but at rest the primaries extended about even with the tail giving the bird a somewhat long-winged appearance.

Bare parts: Bill & eye were black; legs were grayish olive.
Behaviors: Bird did very little during observation time mostly standing in a stationary position and occasionally probing to feed.
Call: None given
Plumage: Winter

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was plumaged similar to a winter plumaged Dowitcher, but had a much shorter bill and proportionately shorter legs unlike Dowitchers and similarly looking shorebirds.
