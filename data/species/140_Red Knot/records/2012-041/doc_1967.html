Description of the Bird

A cute little shorebird roughly the size of a Killdeer. He was mostly grey to grey-brown with fine pale edging to the coverts and mantle, no markings in the feather centers, buffy-whitish underneath with fine grey spots, sparse towards the rear and becoming more dense in the chest, white belly and undertail. A dark eyeline with a thin white supercilliam and brownish cap. A short, tubular looking bill reminding me of a Semipalmated Sandpiper's bill, but a bit thicker and a bit straighter. Head seemed small for the plump body. Legs were dark!
Behaviors: Feeding on the shore of a lake as shorebirds do, and traveling along. The bird never flew during our observations.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Several other shorebirds in winter plumage are similar, experience helped me identify it, I think the bill shape is unique.
