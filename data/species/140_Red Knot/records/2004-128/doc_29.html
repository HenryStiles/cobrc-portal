Description of the Bird

Large size comparable to Killdeer, 'sturdy' body shape correct for knot, grayish back, streaky throat and breast, barred flanks. Leg color dull yellow. Straight black bill, dark line from bill back to behind eye. Dark eye.
Behaviors: Pair of birds moved slowly along mud at edge of water. Not running like peeps or plovers, methodical in searching / feeding.
Call: None
Plumage: adult non breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Sanderling, Dunlin, Ruddy Turnstone were all present and clearly identified. Most sandpipers, yellowlegs, plovers were also there. This one was quite distinctive.
