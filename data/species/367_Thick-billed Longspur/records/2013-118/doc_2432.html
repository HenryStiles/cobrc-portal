Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x binoculars

Light:  Bright hazy morning sunshine. Calm winds, 45 degrees.

Description of the Bird

Male: finch sized with white cast to head and body. Distinctive black stripe through eye and bold black chest band. Tail had prominent white outer tail feathers. Chestnut patches on wings. Very long "spur" claw.

Female: finch sized, lighter brown striped body. Lesser chestnut patches on wings. Very long "spur" claw.
Behaviors: Ground feeding much like horned larks. Flight was light with minor undulations. Gleaning seeds on roadside.
Call: Flight call was a light piping
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Other longspurs or horned larks. The broad black chest band and chestnut wing patches used to eliminate other species.

Sibleys, Nat. Geo. guidebooks.
