Description of the Bird

Sparrow-like passerine with short, square tail. Very drab pale brown plumage, unmarked on underparts, mottled on head and upperparts. Tail feathers bright white with black tip except for mottled brown central tail feathers, most obvious in flight. Median coverts were chestnut.
Behaviors: Eating gravel on roadside, in company of Horned Larks. Frequent short flights.
Call: dry rattle call given in flight
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

McCown's Longspur is the only sparrow-like bird with combination of chestnut median coverts and white tail with black terminal tailband.
