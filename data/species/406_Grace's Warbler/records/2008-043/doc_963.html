Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized warbler, a little smaller than nearby Yellow-rumped Warbler, but larger than nearby Orange-crowned Warblers. The bill was thin, pointed, and black; the legs were also black.

The crown was medium gray, with a black lower border adjacent to the supercilium. The supercilium was broad, short (it came to just behind the eye), and bright yellow. The bird had a thin gray eye-line, which merged with the medium gray nape and back. The throat and uppermost chest were bright yellow. The remainder of the underparts were white. The bird had bold black streaks down the flanks. The back and wings were medium gray, and the bird had two bold white wing bars. The upper tail was gray, the lower tail was mostly white, with small black tail corners.
Behaviors: the bird was foraging actively near the top of a tree, mostly associating with an Orange-crowned Warbler.
Call: The bird gave a soft, inconspicuous chip, much quieter and softer than the chips of either Yellow-rumped or Orange-crowned Warblers.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species of warbler were ruled out by the combination of the yellow throat, gray back, white belly, and black flanks down the flanks. Yellow-throated Warbler was ruled out by the broad, short, yellow supercilium, the lack of a white patch behind the auriculars, and the grayer face without a bold black auricular area.
