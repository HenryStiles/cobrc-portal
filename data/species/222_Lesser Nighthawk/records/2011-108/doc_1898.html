Description of the Bird

Nighthawk had short rounded wings and wingbar nearer the tip than the Common Nighthawks it was flying with. The leading primary wing feather on both wings were shorter than the second feather which was the longest of all primary wing feathers.
The overall color of the nighthawk was buff. In Common Nighthawks there where speckles of white but in this nighthawk the speckles were tan or buff colored. Even the wingbars were an off white. There was some molt in the secondary wing feathers. The tail of this nighthawk was a little shorter than the Common's tail. It was a male because of buff banding on the tail and buff throat patch.
Behaviors: The nighthawks was flying around the sewer lagoons feeding on insects with many Common Nighthawks. Several times the nighthawk passed right overhead and gave me close up views. The flight pattern of the Common Nighthawks when feeding were different than this nighthawk.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Lesser Nighthawks have a different flight pattern, color pattern, wingbar placement and shape, wing tip and tail length than Common Nighthawks. These differences are not great but with experience they do become noticeable. I am the author of the article in the CFO Journal.
