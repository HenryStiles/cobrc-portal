Description of the Bird

A nightjar (white slash across primaries) with that white slash showing beyond the tip of the longest tertial when the bird was perched (see photo); in flight, the white slash was placed about halfway out the pp and did not extend to p6 (see photo); the secondaries were extensively spotted with buff (see photo)

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk is the only similar species of anything like expected occurrence in CO, with the LENI-like subspecies henryi being of possibly regular occurrence in se Colorado in migration. That form ruled out by the white pp patch showing at/beyond the tip of the longest tertial when perched and by the white pp patch being about halfway out the pp.
