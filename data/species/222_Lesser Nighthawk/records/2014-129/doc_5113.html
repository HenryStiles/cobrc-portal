Description of the Bird

We watched hundreds of Common Nighthawks flying over the sewage lagoons. After watching for awhile I was able to pick out the Lesser Nighthawk and distinguish it from the numerous Common Nighthawks when it came into view.  I would follow the bird in my binoculars for awhile before losing it, then pick it out from all the Common Nighthawks a few minutes later.  This pattern repeated itself 3-5 times during the evening.  

In flight the Lesser Nighthawk seemed a little smaller and had a small, buffy primary bar close to the wingtip as opposed to a much larger and more striking white wing bar across the primaries for the Common Nighthawks.  I watched the Lesser Nighthawk closely when it was in sight and on one occasion was able to pick out a middle primary feather protruding out farther than the other primary feathers on the wingtip.  It was not always easy to pick out, but on one occasion when the bird was closer I was able to see it clearly for a brief period.  It looked like a small finger protruding from the wing.

Similar Species Discussion

There were hundreds of Common Nighthawks in the area.  The Common Nighthawks had pointed wings, unlike the Lesser Nighthawk with the finger protruding from the wing.
Most striking was the larger white bar across the primaries for the Common as opposed to a smaller bar nearer the wingtips for the Lesser.  Also the bar on the Common Nighthawks was bright white as opposed to the smaller buffy bar on the Lesser Nighthawk.

Resources Used

I was standing next to Coen Dexter, who is a great resource.  Also referred to "The Sibley Guide to Birds" second edition for confirmation.

Previous Experience

I have seen large numbers of Common Nighthawks over the years.  I also observed Lesser Nighthawks at the Nucla Sewage Lagoons years ago.  On that date I recall Tony Leukering along with some other east slope birders present with Coen Dexter, Brenda Wright, and myself.  At that time we observed Lesser Nighthawks and pointed out the differences.
