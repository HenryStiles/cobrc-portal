Habitat

The location consists of 2 sewage treatment ponds plus additional still water bodies surrounded by cropland and riparian habitats . Topography = gentle rolling hills with lagoons located at the low point.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10 x 42 binoculars
Distance:  Varied from 20 - 150 yards
Light:  Dusk to dark. General conditions = still and clear. Lighting was good and allowed the bird to be viewed from all aspects of the compass as it flew around and from below and above.

Description of the Bird

Behavior - Aerial feeding generally over the sewage lagoons and surrounding periphery.
        Typical Nighthawk flight and feeding behavior.
        This bird was observed in the company of > 50 Common Nighthawks.

Auditory - No calls specific to this individual were noted.

Physical and Topographical observations - The general shape / feel was that of a "Nighthawk" - long winged, large headed, cryptically colored.
       
        I note the following characteristics and relative comparisons to the continuously surrounding Common Nighthawks.
        Size - Smaller in body size and wing span. Tail appeared longer relatively.
        Overall coloration - Lighter and buffier / browner in color vs. darker and greyer in Commons
        Specific coloration - A buffy hue was evident and obvious on the body, wings, and tail
        Wing shape - The wing had a more "rounded" appearance vs. the "pointier" wing of the Common.
                This is supported by the Lesser's last two primaries being of equal projection when extended, creating a "bobbed" apearance.
        Wing "bar" - The bird had a small buffy primary wing bar located, top and bottom, futher out on the wing and closer to the wing tip, than that of the white wing                  bar of the Commons.
        Wing beat - The cadence was a little quicker and shallower and easily discerned allowinng one to follow the bird in flight for extended periods of time.
        
        
        

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk is our most closely related species to the Lesser and the obvious species to exclude.

Common Nighthawks were closely, and in large #, foraging in the same space, presenting an ideal comparative situation during the hour the bird was viewed.

Reference "Description of the Bird" section for comparisons and conclusions.

Resources Used

I was advised by Coen Dexter that Lesser Nighthawks had been observed for an extended period of time, at least the prior few weeks, and that they should be obsevable the evening of June 11, 2014.

With that in mind and before visiting the Nucla Sewage Ponds site, i was able to carefully study Lesser and Common Nighthawks in; "The Sibley Guide to Birds", David Allen Sibley, 2000, & the "Smithsonian Field Guide to the Birds of North America", Ted Floyd, 2008.

In addition to the written literature, Coen Dexter and Mike Henwood shared their personal knowledge of the Lesser's field marks and direct comparison to those of the Common.

Previous Experience

My experience with Common Nighthawks is good and relatively frequent.
I had no previous experience viewing Lesser Nighthawks.
