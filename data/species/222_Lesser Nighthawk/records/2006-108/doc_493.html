Description of the Bird

The wing tips were rounded, not as pointed as a Common. The white in the wing tips was a slightly different shape than Common. Buffy spots on the upper and under wings and buff on the underside of the body.
Behaviors: They were with Common Nighthawks flying over some sewage ponds, good comparison.
Call: Didn't hear it call or sing.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk - Pointed wing tips. No buff in the wings. Gray or rufous on the upper side of the wings and back.
Antillean Nighthawk - Pointed wing tips. A little bit of buff in the wings. Buffy underside of the body.
Common Pauraque - Long rounded wings and a rounded tail. A lot of white in the tail.
Common Poorwill, Whip-poor-will, Chuck-wills-widow, and Buff-collared Nighjar - No white bars in the wings. Extremely rounded wings. A lot of white in the tail.
