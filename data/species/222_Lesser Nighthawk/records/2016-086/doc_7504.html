Description of the Bird

Nighthawks were flying and feeding on insects over the sewer ponds and surrounding marshes and wetlands. Both Common and Lesser Nighthawks were present. The Lesser Nighthawks were silent as were the Common until after the 10th of June when the Common Nighthawks began to call as they hawked insects.
The Lesser Nighthawks, when compared directly with Common Nighthawks, have quite a different overall look. Lessers have a sandy brown color while Commons are gray. The wing bar of the Lesser is much closer to the end of the wing and the wing is not bent as much at the wrist. The feeding flight is somewhat different as the Lesser feeds close to vegetation as well to the water with a fluttering flight.

Similar Species Discussion

Both Common and Lesser Nighthawks were present. Other nighthawk species should not be present in Colorado. Nightjars and poorwills do not feed in this fashion and do not have wing bars except Common Pauraque.
When comparing the two nighthawk species directly, the difference in the two species is quite striking. The experience we have had over the last decade studying the nighthawks at Nucla have made Lessers an easy call, verse Common Nighthawks.

Resources Used

We have a library of field guides and other resource materials that we have used to be certain of a correct ID. We have studied photos of the Nucla nighthawks and compared them with other photos taken around the southwest USA and Mexico by other birders.

Previous Experience

Brenda and I are world birders and have seen nearly all of the America's nighthawks, nightjars, etc. Over the last decade Lesser Nighthawks have been present each spring at the Nucla sewer lagoons and marshes. They come in late May are are present until at least mid-June. As summer heat comes on the nighthawks feed mostly after dark and it is not possible to know if the Lessers are still present. Another problem is the shear number of Common Nighthawks by mid-June, which makes it very difficult to pick out a few Lessers.
Our records would indicate that Lessers may be slightly more nocturnal than Commons.
