Description of the Bird

A very obvious nighthawk which flushed from low in woody growth and then made repeated forays swooping at and below the tops of the trees around Van's Grove.  It repeatedly settled into the trees before repeating this pattern and by doing so enabled our entire group to get solid views and some photos.

This was a long slender-winged agile flyer.  Undersides buffy brown with a bold white bar situated well out near the end of the wing.  Wingtip buffy brown in a equilateral triangle with the tip of the wing clearly rounded.

The bird was silent.

Most other nightjars eliminated by the white wing bars with the exception of Common Nighthawk which was eliminated by a combination of the buffy undersides, rounded wingtips and the placement of the white so far out on the wing.
