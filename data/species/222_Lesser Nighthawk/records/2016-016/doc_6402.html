Description of the Bird

Jeff and I were in the area and decided to check out the sewage lagoons hoping for the fun nighthawk show I'd been treated to the prior year.  The nighthawk show failed to materialize - we had no nighthawks over the lagoons that evening.  As we began giving up hope for any nighthawks, we noted a nighthawk flying towards us - and initially assumed it a CONI.

The bird flew directly overhead from the east in decent light and departed directly to the west.  It provided a nice steady view though its passage.

It immediately didn't feel right for CONI - Jeff looked at me and said, "THAT wasn't a Common Nighthawk!"

We both noted the wingtips were rounded as opposed to pointed and the overall warm brown coloration of the undersides of the bird.  The white wing-patch normally expected for CONI wasn't there, instead a smaller buffy wingpatch was visible closer to the wingtip.  The bird's flight was steady and lacking any of the snappiness exhibited by a CONI.

Bird was quiet - didn't peent at any time.  While not diagnostic, this it is consistent with LENI.

Rare, but regular at this location.
