Description of the Bird

A nightjar with buffy ground color, buffy spots on remiges and buffy bases to primaries, creating a buffy bars further out on wing than CONI.
Behaviors: Sitting horizontally on branches of deciduous trees, flying short distances when approached.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk was the only species to consider once we determined it was a nighthawk and not a differnt type of nightjar. The buffy overall color, buffy wing bars near the wing tips allowed us to eliminate CONI and others Antillean Nighthawk.
