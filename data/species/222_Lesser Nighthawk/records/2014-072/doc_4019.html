Description of the Bird

Bird 1; female. Seen at 8:25 pm or so. Came in from west, veered nearly overhead, dipped over water, swung low over field, and then flew back west. Sun had set, but lighting reasonable when bird close nearly overhead and then when low with water or hillsides as background. Closest approach 60 feet or so, maybe closer. I first noted this bird because of its different flight style, more rounded wingtips, and smaller size. Flight involved shorter glides, is more erratic, and wings less typically not raised as high as CONI, though on occasion would do so. When seen low over water, browner underparts in comparison with CONI could be seen pretty easily. Initially, could not see any mark on wing, though the white mark on each wing of the CONI it was trailing was pretty obvious. When the bird was nearly overhead, a buffy bar consisting of several large spots, could be seen near wingtip where a male LENI would show a white bar. The wingtips were distinctly more rounded than that of nearby CONI. Also, the LENI was distinctly smaller in wingspan, and length, when compared with the CONI it was trailing initially. Overall, the LENI flew lower for much of its viewing time, whereas the CONI tended to stay higher in sky. No vocalizations. Notably, Nick was looking at bird 2, which I never saw. Description same, including much of where/how it was flying, but at one point his bird turned sharply in a different direction than mine, so clearly different birds. He will likely be submitting bird 2
Bird 3; male. Seen by both Nick and I around 8:35 or 8:40. At this point, light pretty dim. The bird came in low, crossed the dam, and remained low. When turning, the white stripe on underwing (not visible from above) was apparent. It was far out on wing, seemingly 80% or so of distance from bend in wing to tip of wing. The shape of the white stripe was not noted, but it appeared narrower than that of a CONI. Its wings also appeared rather rounded when compared with that of the CONI present.

Similar Species Discussion

CONI: 
Bird 1- The buffy inconspicous wingbar and it position on the wing, the rounded wings, smaller size all eliminate CONI
Bird 2- The position of the wingbar on the wing, and the shape of the wing eliminate CONI. Additionally, the size of the wingbar is more typical of LENI as was the flight style. Finally, I wonder if the brightness of the wingbar from above might be useful in separating these two species
