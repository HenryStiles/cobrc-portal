Description of the Bird

This was a perched female, and possibly the same one from Chico Basin Ranch as seen on 25 May 2008.

Perched, I only look at the wing and saw three brownish spots on the folded wing, the key for separating this nighthawk from Common and Antillean.

I did see it fly briefly and it was buffy underneath, but that is really all that I noticed.
Behaviors: Mostly perced on horizontal limb.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk.

Neither male or female Common Nighthawks have the brownish patches on their folded primaries.
