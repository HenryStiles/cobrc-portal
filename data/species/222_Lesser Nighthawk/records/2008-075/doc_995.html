Description of the Bird

A very buffy Chordeiles nighthawk. Smaller in direct comparison to male Common Nighthawk (CONI). The wings were shorter and more rounded at the tip. Flight style was very different than the CONIs that were around. CONI was very jerky mechanical movement and the Lesser (LENI) was smoother and less jerky. When not flapping the LENI would glide with its wings in a fairly steep dihedral. The primary spot was very distal and small and buffy. The tail had even width bands down its length, alternating buffy and brown.
Behaviors: Flying around treetop level feeding on flying insects. Numerous CONIs were around too allowing for great comparisons.
Call: none noted
Plumage: Definitive adult female

Similar Species Discussion

The small buffy primary spots eliminate CONI as does the small buffy spots on primaries. The more rounded wing shape is good for LENI and not CONI.
