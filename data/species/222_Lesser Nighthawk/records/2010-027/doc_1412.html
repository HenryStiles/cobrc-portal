Description of the Bird

Bird was a road kill missing its head and tail. Rest of body was in poor condition, but wings were in good condition. This report is therefore based solely on color and shape of wings.

Wings had slight backward bend and nearly twice length of body, excluding missing head & tail. Primaries were dark slate gray with buffy primary bar, buffier on top than on bottom surface of wing. This patch was near midway between primary tip and base of primaries. Patch was somewhat tear drop in shape, wider toward the leading edge & becoming narrower toward trailing edge of wing.
Primary 9(P-9) was slightly longer than P-8 & P10 giving the wing tip a serrated somewhat rounded appearance.

Wings were speckled buff becoming a more richer cinnamon buff towards the body of the bird, especially in the tertials.
Behaviors: Not applicable--road killed dead bird

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk has primary bar further from wing tip, closer to base of primaries, and not as buffy; its wing speckles are not usually as buffy as Lesser. Common Nighthawk has longer wings with more pointed primary tips than Lesser.
