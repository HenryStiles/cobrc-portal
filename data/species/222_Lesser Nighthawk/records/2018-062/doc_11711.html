Description of the Bird

Bird flushed from about 30 feet in front of me. It flew low, with moderately long glides, and downward flaps, wings held above horizontal during glides. Base color of body below was brown. The underparts were barred with darker brown. Belly/vent as brown as chest. Wings appeared short and distinctly rounded when compared with those of Common Nighthawk. The white bar on the wing was placed more than 1/2, but probably not quite 2/3, of distance from "wrist" to tip of wing; it seemed to fully reach leading edge of wing (ie, outer web of outer primary). Chin did not have bright white mark(s) that I associate with adult males of either Lesser or Common Nighthawk. I did not have time to study tail well and was unable to discern spotting pattern on primaries. The wings, however, were not in molt.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk has more pointed wings with placement of white bar on wing closer to bend of wing or "wrist" than the Upper Queens Lesser. Common Nighthawks (except Cm henryi) are grayer and (except some Cm henryi) have vents paler than remainder of underparts; this bird was uniform brown below. The white mark on the wings seemed to extend all the way to leading edge of wing, which it does not on Common. Could not be absolutely certain about this though. Roosting on ground is typical of LENI and not of CONI

The flight, soaring low (just over tamarisk) with wings flicked downward and little-if-any raising of wing above glide position, is typical of Lesser Nighthawk; Common Nighthawk tends to fly higher and raise wings higher during flapping motions. Also, Common Nighthawk usually has a more erratic flight than Lesser, and than this bird. 

The age/sex is difficult. The white wing markings would suggest male in LENI. However, (most/all?) adult LENI should be in molt in August and they should have obviously white throats (or so I think; could not find direct commentary in either Pyle nor the "Nightjar book." However, the LENI at https://ebird.org/view/checklist/S47665931 (August, AZ) and https://www.flickr.com/photos/36088296@N08/9780554542 (August, Baja) are similar in lack of wing molt, white mark on wings, and limited pale on chin. Immature males? Perhaps, since LENI breed earlier than CONI, young of year already gone from juv to formative plumage by August.
