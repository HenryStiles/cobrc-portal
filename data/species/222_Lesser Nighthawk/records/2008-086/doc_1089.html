Description of the Bird

the bird was a nighthawk slightly smaller than nearby Common Nighthawks, but basically the same shape except for the differences discussed below. The wings were relatively shorter and broader than those of the Commons (accentuated by the fact that p10 was noticeably shorter than p9), and this give the bird a slightly more stocky and less lanky look.

The overall color of the upperparts were a darkish medium brown, with grayer patches on the crown and carpal bars. The bird had a broad, buffy colored bar near the tip of the wing, well over half-way from the "elbow" of the wing and the tip, noticeably closer to the tip than the white bars on the wings of Common Nighthawk.

The underparts were paler brown than the upperparts. The underside of the primaries and secondaries were densely spotted with buffy spots, so that the overall color of the underside of the wings looked entirely buffy from the pale bar crossing the primaries near the tip to the base.

I couldn't see the color of the throat for sure, but I believe that had it been white I would have been able to see it, and that it was buffy in color.
Behaviors: The bird was flying with a large flock of Common Nighthawks over the sewage ponds. It flew with more fluttery wing beats than those of Common Nighthawks, and on average was lower over the water. It never went into the deep "V" glide that Commons often do.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than nighthawks were ruled out by the distinctive shape and flight style. Common Nighthawk was ruled out by the buffy bars near the tips of the wings, by the extensive buffy spots on the undersides of the primaries and secondaries, by the shorter and more rounded wings, with p10 shorter than p9.
