Description of the Bird

At about 4:30 PM a nighthawk flushed from the ground where some short trees met barren desert-like ground. It flew towards but arcing around us, then past us, and around the far edge of the trees. Initially it was 100 or so feet in front of us, and when it disappeared it was 200 or so feet past us. At its closest approach it was 20 feet away at eye level. It never flew higher than 10 or so feet above the ground and never vocalized.
The nighthawk seemed immediately odd as it flew differently from typical Common Nighthawks. The flight was more direct and the wings did not appear to rise as high above the body as in Common Nighthawk. As it came more into side view, the rounded wingtips were quite evident; furthermore, the white slash on the primaries appeared to be 75-80% towards tip of wing from bend in wing (To me, on CONI, the slash appears about 50% of the way between bend and tip of wing). I was unable to ascertain the shape of the white primary bar, but the distal wing did have some warm brown spotting, in contrast to the typical solid dark appearance of the outer wing on a CONI. The body and wings appeared warm brown, at least below, without obvious gray hues. The throat was white. We were unable to relocate the bird.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk has less direct, floppier flight, seeming to hold wings higher above body at top of wingstroke; whether the wings truly are raised at a greater angle, or if this appearance is due to CONI longer wings or slower wingbeats is unclear, but I see 1000+ LENI per year, and the difference in flight style is pretty evident under normal conditions. More importantly, the wing shape and positioning of the white primary bar was typical of LENI and would be highly atypical of CONI. As CONI molt their primaries after southbound migration, a CONI in molt is ruled out. Other points in favor of LENI is that the bird took off, stayed low, and did not vocalize, behavior that would be unusual (though certainly not impossible) for CONI. Also, the warmer color and pale brown spotting on outer wing are more typical of LENI than CONI
