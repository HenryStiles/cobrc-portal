Description of the Bird

Observed the Nighthawk flying over the lake for about 20 minutes. Larger than the swallows. Noticed the white wing stripe was closer to the end of the wing. Was about 2/3 of the way toward the tip of the wing. Notice the photos show the outer primary feather is a little shorter than the next to last primary making the tip seem a little more rounded than a Common Nighthawk. Also, because the wing bar was close to the tip of the wing it was not a Common Nighthawk. I also noticed the mottled brownish coloration of this bird. The Common Nighthawk is more grayish. It never gave any sounds while feeding. The Common Nighthawk gives the peet call while feeding. This bird was ID'd by Luke Pheneger on May 5th. Greg Mihalik, Danielle Jernigan, Bruce Warren and Sally Waterhouse all saw the bird on the 5th of May. Greg Mihalik was with Ann Troth and I on the 6th of May when we observed the bird over the lake at dusk. Got several decent photos. Is still being seen as of May 8th. According to eBird this would be a first county record.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Nighthawk was considered. It was eliminated by the white wing bar being close to the wing tip. Also, the outer primary (P10) is shorter than (P9) making the wing tip look more rounded than the Common Nighthawk. The overall brownish mottling is indicative of Lesser Nighthawk. The Common Nighthawk is more gray and white mottled. The bird was silent while feeding which is true of the Lesser Nighthawk and not true of the Common Nighthawk.
