Description of the Bird

I was able to pick this bird out of a small flock of about 12 nighthawks by its significantly lighter coloring and more erratic flight. Its overall coloring on the under parts was much lighter, browner and more mottled with tan highlights compared to the adjacent Common Nighthawks that were quite dark and more uniform in coloration. The size was somewhat smaller, but difficult to ascertain with certainty. The wings were also distinctive. The light bar on the underwing was farther out toward the tip (more than half way out on the outer wing) and seemed cream colored rather than white, which made it much less distinctive. Also the wing tips were slightly rounded and not as sharply pointed as in a Common Nighthawk and the underside of the wings was considerably more mottled than those of a Common. The tail seemed long and was slightly forked and fairly uniform in coloring, but darker than the body. There was no white subterminal band on the tail. Finally I did not see any white or buffy throat patch. The lack of a throat patch, lack of tail bar, and the indistinct wing marking led me to conclude that this was a female Lesser. I only had very brief looks at the top of the bird which seemed browner and more mottled than those of the Commons.
Behaviors: Flight was erratic and fluttering at times, especially compared to the surrounding Commons.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

This bird appeared to be slightly smaller than a Common. However, the flight pattern, longer tail, and much lighter overall coloration were significantly more definitive in identifying this bird. It was a great help to have this bird with Commons, so that I could make direct comparisons on overall coloration (dark vs much lighter); wing shape; flight pattern; color, size and placement of wing bar.
It is extremely unlikely to have been an Antillean Nighthawk because it would be far out of recorded range at this location. Furthermore, bird would seem even smaller, tail shorter, wings more pointed, and underwings darker.
