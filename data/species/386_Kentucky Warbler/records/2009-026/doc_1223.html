Habitat

The area is a very small spring-fed pond lined with native willows and exotic Russian olives, and some large plains cottonwoods with large logs laying on the ground where the bird was mostly foraging.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  both Leica 10x42 Ultravid binoculars and a Leica 77mm scope with 32xx lens.
Distance:  as close as 30 feet.
Light:  Very good. Sun over my right shoulder, occasionally going into the shadows.

Description of the Bird

A mostly yellow bird with a thin warbler-like bill, this distinctive bird is easy to identify. Its yellow spectacles stand out because they are bordered by a black cap and black below and behind the eye in what sort of looks like a thin black mask. From chin to undertail, the plumage was yellow on the ventral surface and dorsally it was an olive-green color. The bill was black and I didn't look at the leg color but you can if you look at the excellent photo below.
Behaviors: This Kentucky Warbler spent the entire time foraging on the gound or up to one foot off the ground. It sometimes disappeared from view in 6-inch tall grasses. It stopped to pose for a few seconds now and again, but was mostly on the move.
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

There are no other yellow warblers with the yellow spectacle surrounded by black, but without a good look, it would be possible to confuse this bird with the other yellow warblers with black on their faces, such as Hooded Warbler, with a black hood, not the black placement as in KEWA, and more likely Canada Warbler which does have a yellow specatcle but with a bluish back and black-streaked breast, and without the posterior extension to the yellow spectacle that KEWA has. None of the other wood warblers come close to the head pattern of KEWA.
