Description of the Bird

A warbler shaped song bird, that was lemon yellow underneath, with dark feathers going under the eye down each side of the throat. The throat itself was bright yellow. The undertail coverts were also bright yellow. The area from above the bill going above the eye and around the eye was yellow on each side. The crown was grayish green/olive. The back and wings were the same color, of green/olive. The tail was the same color, of green/olive. The lower maniable of the bill was pinkish, the upper maniable of the bill was dark. The bill was pointed and thin. The legs were pink and then. The bird wasn't bright enough for a male I don't think.
Behaviors: Seen in some bushes in the open, I wish I had the camera.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Yellowthroat (male) -- has white eye stripe, not yellow.

Hooded Warbler (female) -- has yellow face and white in the tail. The face pattern is quite different.
