Habitat

-Kentucky Blue grass and Perennial Rye lawn with Locust trees, Pinion Pines, Junipers, some flower beds
-water feature in yard
some weedy patches along drive to south of house

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  camera lens with auto zooom
Distance:  at times only 2 feet on the other side of glass
Light:  excellent light

Description of the Bird

-overall appearance a little heavier than many warblers
-greenish olive on back and topside of wings without any distinct markings
-blackish crown and auriculars with bright yellow distinctive spectacles
-all bright yellow underneath from throat to undertail coverts
-pinkish legs
Behaviors: -behavior was atypical for a secretive warbler
-first feeding below rock walls in weeds on the side of the ensilage pit
-then openly feeding along edge of flower beds along sidewalk
-photos were taken through the glass on the door as it strolled down the sidewalk completely in the open
-if the door was open, he may have walked right in
-perfect opportunity for positive id
Call: silent
Plumage: not full adult male?

Similar Species Discussion

-obvious warbler size and shape
-distinctive head pattern set apart from other "yellow underneath" warblers such as Wilson's or Hooded
-also plumper appearance than either Hooded or Wilson's
-no white markings anywhere

Resources Used

-Sibley's Guide used after the sighting
-I recognized the head pattern without looking it up
-confirmed by David Leatherman

Previous Experience

-first time I've seen a Kentucky
-I've seen several Hooded and Wilson's
