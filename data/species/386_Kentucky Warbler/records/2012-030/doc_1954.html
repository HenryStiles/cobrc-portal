Description of the Bird

Structurally a warbler.

Very slightly larger than the several nearby Common Yellowthroats (COYE)

Olive-drab back color same as COYEs; extends up on to neck and the back of the head

Bright yellow throat color same as COYEs; however the yellow in the throat extended all the way down over the belly and through the vent area

Yellow eyebrow extending from behind the eye to the bill

Black on top of head which wraps around the yellow eyebrow and over the dark eye and also drops down towards the neck

Heavier bill than the COYEs
Behaviors: Skulking

I stood very quietly on the north side of that nasty Last Chance puddle hoping something good might come in for a drink. There were at least six COYE present in the area. I almost dismissed the KEWA as another COYE as it failed to stand out initially.

It stayed very low to the ground and skirted the south edges of the puddle disappearing into the undergrowth several times.
Call: N/A
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

COYE, already eliminated due to direct comparisons

Wilson's Warbler - should have no black through the eye

Hooded Warbler - no large yellow cheek patch, no black on chin.
