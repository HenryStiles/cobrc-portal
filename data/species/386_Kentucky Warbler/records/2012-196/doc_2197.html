Description of the Bird

A bright yellow warbler that I flushed twice from the ground before I finally saw it standing on the ground in good light. It was olive-green above from at least mid-crown through the tail tip and bright yellow from chin through undertail coverts, though with olive-green sides. There was some black on the forehead and forecrown, but I did not note the exact extent of such. There was also a strip of black between eye and bill that projected down and rearward, edging the yellow of the combined throat and lateral throat stripe area and which contrasted strongly with the yellow spectacles. The wings were the same olive-green of the upperparts. The eyes were dark, the bill was black, and the legs were bright pink.
Behaviors: Foraging, presumably, on the ground, but mostly seen flying away from me, and then standing on a beam lying on the ground.
