Description of the Bird

The bird a medium-sized warbler, about the size of nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers. The forecrown was black, with no visible traces of any other color. The back of the crown was black speckled with olive, and the back of the crown and the nape had a distinct grayish tinge over the olive green (more so than I have ever seen on this species before). The nape, back, rump, tail and wings were all a uniform olive green color without any other color except for the nape as noted above. The bird had bold black sideburns that divided the olive green of the nape and the yellow of the throat. The bird also had a bold yellow supercilium. The throat, chest, belly and undertail coverts were all a very bright yellow, with no white. The legs were pink. I did not note the color of the bill, though the bill was fairly heavy and long for a warbler, though still thin and pointed.
Behaviors: The bird foraged out in the open for about two minutes, and skulked in the dense brush for the rest of the viewing time, about three to four minutes in all. The bird remained on the ground or close to the ground for the duration of the observation.
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All similar species were ruled out by a combination of the bright yellow underparts, olive upperparts, and bold black sideburns. The behavior of the bird, spending most of it's time skulking on the ground, also ruled out most other warbler species.
