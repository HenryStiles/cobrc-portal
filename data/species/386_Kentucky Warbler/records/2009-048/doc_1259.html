Description of the Bird

A small,two-toned (green and yellow) woodland bird with a bright yellow breast, belly and vent and a greenish-dark gray back with no wing bars

The head and face marking with characteristic the green back darkened into a black cap with black "side-burns", a black eye-line through the eye but a yellow line above the eye and yellow around the eye.
Behaviors: Heard singing and then seen in song in the open branches of an elm tree. Later I heard it singing persistently about 200 yards away, but I could not see it at this second hearing as it seemed to be in dense ground cover growth.
Call: a loud tu-we, tu-we, tu-we, tu-we given several times per minute
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

I am not familiar with any other green and yellow bird with these facial markings and this song.
