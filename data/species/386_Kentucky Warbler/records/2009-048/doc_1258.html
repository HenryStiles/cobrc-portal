Description of the Bird

Greenish-backed warbler with yellow underparts and striking facial pattern. Back and belly essentially unmarked, most distinct patterning is on face. Facial pattern consists of bold yellow stripe above eye, black patch below eye widening into auriculars and lower side of neck. Cap black with tiny light spotting, paler towards back. Bill mostly dark, moderately long & thin as expected in Oporornis warblers. Legs & feet pale pink.
Behaviors: Bird was intermittently singing from heavy cover, unseen. Pishing agitated the bird, causing it to move closer and to go more quickly from perch to perch, calling, allowing it to be seen and photographed.
Call: Gave classic Kentucky Warbler song several times, consisting of two-syllable phrases repeated 4-5 times. Called vigorously in response to pishing.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Once in clear view, the unique facial pattern of the adult male Hooded Warbler ruled out other possibilities.
