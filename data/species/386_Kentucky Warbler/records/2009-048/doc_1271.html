Habitat

In dark, dense broadleaf understory of Gregory Canyon.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon 10X EDG binoculars. (But the point of this submission is aural, not visual.)
Distance:  Very close; as close as 18 feet.
Light:  Although it was sunny, the bird showed up briefly in the dark understory. It was easy to quickly determine the bird's plumage (adult male, alternate) in these conditions.

Description of the Bird

I saw the bird very briefly. This bird was a "stakeout," seen previously, and copiously photographed by other birders. For this submission, I am submitting only documentation pertaining to the bird's vocalizations.
Behaviors: The bird called frequently while it was skulking in dense understory vegetation. It responded to pishing, came out for a brief view, then disappeared.
Call: Called frequently. I am uploading two recordings of the bird calling. The call was a loud, hollow, rather "wooden" clucking sound.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Hooded Warbler, also reported in Gregory Canyon when the Kentucky Warbler was there, has a loud, penetrating call-note, like Kentucky. Kentucky's, though, is more wooden-sounding, and not as rich, as Hooded. Prior to seeing the bird, I wondered if it was going to be Hooded or Kentucky. I was leaning Kentucky (and, of course, got confirmation, on seeing the distinctive bird), but not positive. Other warblers that occur in Gregory Canyon (Virginia's, MacGillivray's, etc.) have weaker, less emphatic call notes.

Previous Experience

I was in Kentucky in late April 2009, and Kentucky Warblers were back in force at that time. I paid special attention to Kentucky Warbler call notes (also Hooded; also Cerulean, but never mind...) at the time, "just in case" I would go on to have an encounter in Colorado later on in 2009. Who'da thunk?... Previously, I have had extensive experience with Kentucky Warbler on the breeding grounds, especially in Pennsylvania and New Jersey, where I have lived and birded.
