Description of the Bird

Rather dark gray-greenish upper body--not remarkable in shade--but contrasting with much brighter yellow underbody, throat and especially supercilium. Log notes include "unique zigzaggy black mask going from general eye area toward shoulder & partwaydown side of throat." No white (eyering or wingbars for example) anywhere visible. Generally quite plain except for mask.

Size typical of parulidae: smaller than thrush family for example.

There was no time to note more refined details.
Behaviors: Skulky lurking in dense riparian undergrowth; periods of foraging, of singing (brief--a minute, then long silence of up to 5 or more minutes), of suspicious avoidance activity when others were on the trail. In this way, a thrush or a montane sparrow might have been suspected had not song & appearance been completely different.
Call: simple, resounding, even brilliant "double-syllable" upslurred notes, reminding me instantly of song quality of Carolina wren (with which I am much more familiar). Song notes sounded echo-ey due to bird's position, constantly in thick low underbrush.
Plumage: breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Hooded warbler is always a possibility here; the face of the Hooded is broadly yellow, with no "spectacle" effect from the yellow supercilium and black mask below eye. Song is markedly different. Furthermore there was no contrasting black on back of the head. Absence of other markings like eyerings eliminated species like MacGill warbler, of wingbars (the dendroicas), etc Behavior very different from (for example) YB chat.
