Description of the Bird

I did not get a look at the rump or see this bird in flight. Crown and ear patch pale tawny-brown with white head chin and throat. Sides also washed with tawny-brown. Slight tawny-brown breast band. Black mark on wing below shoulder, primaries black, large white wing patch, dark streaks on tawny-brown back, white underparts, dark eye, orange-yellow bill with very small amount of black at tip. Central rectrices black with white appearing in the outer tail feathers.
Behaviors: Associating with approx. 2,000 Horned Larks. Feeding on bare ground near a dirt pile on the edge of a corn stubble field. Cows present nearby. Roads, and other fields in area with much snow cover. This bird would fly with the Horned Larks when disturbed but came back to this location at least twice.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

McKay's Bunting would not show as much black in the retrices, the primaries would not show as much black and the mantle would not have the black and tawny-brown markings.
