Description of the Bird

Medium sized warbler with sturdy bill and tail cocked slightly up. Upper parts mostly a bright yellow green with very faint stripes on back. Distinct white eye ring and bill fairly heavy and lighter colored near base particularly on the lower mandible. Sides of face were a light gray which extended down sides of neck to flanks just below wings. Chin and throat were clean white which extended to belly, vent and undertail coverts. There were faint tones of yellow at the forward vent area. Wings were blackish to gray with two bold white wing bars. Upper tail was gray and whitish underneath. Legs were dark. Wings drooped slightly.
Behaviors: Found with a mixed flock of yellow-rumped warblers, sparrows, and kinglets. Actively foraging for food. Bright yellow green back was immediately visible and identified this bird as different from common area birds. Tail was frequently cocked slightly up and wings were also slightly drooped.
Call: not heard
Plumage: first fall

Similar Species Discussion

Bay-breasted and Blackpoll were faintly similar, but neither has the bright white eye-ring and extensive clean white underparts of this bird. Faces are not gray on either of these birds. Also, bright yellow green upperparts of this bird is striking compared to the other two.
