Description of the Bird

 

Small, all dark bird viewed outside my window,got my attention so I grabbed my binoculars and opened the back door. Originally I hoped for an Indigo Bunting, but even in the poor light I could see the bird had a red spot on the back of it's head.  Appeared to have a blue gray body with lighter blue head and rump. Gray bill with curved culmen, dark "mask" surrounded eyes and bill, large red spot on nape with various reddish plumage on back and belly. Bird was observed foraging in small willows along water ditch behind my house. The bird's vocalizations sounded like "chip" call which it repeated regularly. Unfortunately, due to the low light conditions, I didn't know what I had observed until I reviewed my photos, or I would have followed it longer. A later attempt to locate the bird failed.

Similar Species Discussion

At first view, the bird appeared very small and very dark so I initially hoped for Indigo Bunting, but picking up my binoculars I noticed the slightly curved culmen, and initially wondered if it might be a Brown-headed Cowbird, but the bird was too small. Due to very poor light conditions, the bird was basically a silhouette. l then saw a large reddish spot on it's head, which got me wondering if it was a Green-tailed Towhee, but again the bird seemed too small and dark.  I grabbed my camera and pushed the ISO up as I snapped photos.  The bird then flew farther up the ditch behind my neighbors' yard and I could no longer seem him, but I could hear him chipping.

Resources Used

After reviewing my underexposed photos, I knew I had a good bird; some type of bunting due to the dark mask. I confirmed the field marks for Varied Bunting using the National Geographic Field Guides to the Birds of North America, Seventh Edition and the Cornell Lab of Ornithology's website, AllAboutBirds.Org.  I then worked on increasing/lightening the exposue of my photographs in Photoshop Elements so I could see the addtional colors of a Varied Bunting.

Previous Experience

I originally thought this was a "life bird", but after reviewing my eBird entries, I had seen one at the famous Patagonia Rest Stop in Arizona in 2011 during a Western Field Ornithologists conference in Sierra Vista.  I've seen many Lazuli Buntings and several Indigo Buntings, but only one prior Varied Bunting.
