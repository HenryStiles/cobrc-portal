Habitat

Open water on Smith Reservoir. The birds were initially quite close to shore. I think the water is quite deep at the southwest corner (where I was), as this is a steeply cut boat-launch area.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  32X Leica scope.
Distance:  Initially, not much more than 100 feet.
Light:  Pretty poor, with the rising sun to the east. The birds were to the east, too. However, I was able to angle to the southwest, providing better viewing.

Description of the Bird

Two (possibly four) birds were noted. Two were quite close to the water's edge. The alternate bird had a black head with a very large golden band extending behind broadly behind the eye, apparently wrapping around the nape. Its neck was dark rufous, contrasting with the all-black lower regions of the head (or "face").

Its companion was a mess, plumage-wise, as it was in the (fairly early?) stages of the prealternate molt of its body feathers. It was just a gray and black blob. That said, its bill was perfect for Horned: bulbous and gently rounded above and below, with that telltale pale tip.

The two birds were about the same size and structure. Speaking of structure, the birds had that Horned Grebe structure about them: stouter and more-powerful looking than Eared, and much-less buoyant-looking. I find that Eareds practically float on top of the water, looking very puffy and feathery; Horneds, in contrast, tend to ride deeper in the water. These birds looked liked Horneds in that regard.

Two other birds, much farther out, might have been other Horned Grebes. I'm not sure. The light far out on the lake was very poor. They were Podiceps grebes, but that's all I'm willint to commit to.
Behaviors: Swimming (generally away from me), with 2 pronlonged dives. They kept quite close to each other.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1 alternate; 1 scuzzy prealternate

Similar Species Discussion

Eared Grebe, of course. See above. Focusing just on the alternate-plumage bird, the head and neck pattern is striking, and diagnostic. A good supporting detail (well noted especially on the icky bird in prealternate molt) was bill shape, structure, and pattern, described above. Overall body size and structure (described above) was also good.
