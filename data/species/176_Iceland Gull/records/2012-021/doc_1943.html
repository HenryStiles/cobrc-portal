Description of the Bird

At about 12:30 pm, at the northeast corner of Jackson Reservoir, a small group of gulls (all Ring-billeds plus one larger bird) flew past us to the north at a distance of about 75 feet at closest approach. The Iceland Gull was in view for 45 seconds, perhaps longer. The sun was mostly behind us (to the south) as we viewed the bird.

The upperwing looked pale gray, with a narrow white trailing edge to primaries. No secondary bar was seen, noting that the upperwing was visible, proximally, only to the mid-secondaries. The underwing was entirely white, except for some brownish smudging on the secondary coverts. The underparts were mostly white, excepting some pale brown barring on the undertail coverts and a bit of brown smudging on the belly. The bill was small and bicolored, with the basal third being an odd olive-like color, reminiscent of the color on the bill of some first winter Mew Gulls. The distal 2/3 or so was blackish.

The bird as a whole appeared relatively small, using the RBGUs for comparison: in other words, it looked smallish for a THGU or Herring Gull, and definitely too petite for Glaucous Gull. In the photographs, a pale iris is noted.

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull should have had dark on upper primaries and secondaries and been darker overall above (this bird appeared to be similar in color to a pale-end Glaucous Gull). Basal bill color is wrong for Thayer's Gull (and most other gulls). Pale eye would be rather atypical for Thayer's Gull. Glaucous Gull would have been much larger with pink basal half to bill.

Resources Used

None for ID. Aging baffled me at first, but plate 35A.40 on page 259 of The Gulls book is almost a dead-ringer for the bird we saw, excepting that it has more brown on underparts of body. The bird in 35A.39 resembles that which we saw on upperside, so 2nd year bird seems spot on.
