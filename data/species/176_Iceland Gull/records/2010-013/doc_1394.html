Description of the Bird

A pale gull, approximately equal in size to Thayer's Gull and small American Herring Gull. Head and breast mostly white, with dark eye and mostly dark bill with lighter base. Pale gray-brown markings on belly. Pinkish legs and feet. Pale gray mantle, somewhat mottled. Wings mottled very pale brownish-gray with darker outer primaries and secondaries. Almost white inner secondaries. Pale brown tail. Blocky head and fairly heavy bill for this species suggest male gender.
Behaviors: Flying, standing
Call: none
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Too small for Glaucous Gull. Too pale for Thayer's Gull although a faded THGU would look similar. THGU would not show an all white replaced P10 (unless leucistic) or all white inner primaries (see photos)
