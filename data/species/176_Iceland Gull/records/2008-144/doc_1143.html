Description of the Bird

While sorting through swimming and flying gulls chasing ducks around, I noted a very pale gull flying. It was obviously pale enough that it had to be either an Iceland Gull (ICGU) or a Glaucous-winged Gull (GWGU); in my mind, it was obviously not a Glaucous Gull (GLGU). When it landed on the water, it became obvious that it was a pale-end ICGU.

The whole plumage was whitish with individual feathers having variably brown markings, but with the overall impression of a very pale gull. The head was somewhat rounded, but large and blocky enough that I suspect that the bird was a male; it certainly wasn't the very dove-like head of a very small, thus female, ICGU. The bill was shorter and thinner than those of the nearby Herring Gulls (HERG) and nearly entirely black. The eyes were dark and the legs were bright pink.

The wings were quite long, with the wingtip projection longer than that of nearby HERGs. The outer primaries were the whitest part of the plumage, with only barely-darker subterminal anchors noticeable at short distance. The bird rode high in the water, with the back angled up from the neck to the wingtips; not as strongly angled as on the Ring-billed Gulls, but more obviously so than the nearby HERGs.
Behaviors: Flying; swimming; chasing ducks with fish; standing and roosting on tires
Call: None heard
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

GLGU ruled out by size, bill color and pattern, wing length; GWGU eliminated by size, wing length, outer primary color and pattern; Thayer's Gull (THGU) by overall coloration and this bird's very pale (whitish) tail, white tertials with a few brown marks, lack of dark trailing edge on wing, and very pale outer primaries
