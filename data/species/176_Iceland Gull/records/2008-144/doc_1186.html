Description of the Bird

A medium-large gull (larger than nearby Ring-billed, slightly smaller than nearby Herring), creamy white overall with sparse, very pale, weakly contrasting brown patterning. Bill moderate in length, nearly parallel-edged, very mild gonydeal angle. Distal third of bill black, but not cleanly demarked from pinkish base. The black on the mandible is a bit more extensive than on the maxilla, and extends inward along the center of the bill, giving the bill a bit of a messy appearance. The dark eye appears relatively large against the domed head. the primaries have moderately long extension beyond the tail, giving the bird an attenuated look on the rear end. The primaries are pale but have slightly darker outer webs. The feet and legs are pink. The bird had a bit of staining on the breast- perhaps oil or some other waterborne pollution.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding on dead fish (shad?) on the ice edge and swimming around on the pond. It made a few short flights around the pond, always returning during my observations. It associated with Ring-billed and Herring Gulls, but also swam among waterfowl including domestic strains of geese.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 1st-cycle, Kumlien's subspecies

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull generally not this white. Primaries typically a bit darker than body, but on this bird the primaries are about the whitest part of the bird. Glaucous Gull should be larger (even smallest females.) Wing projection on Glaucous Gull shorter, giving bird much stubbier appearance. Head blockier on Glaucous Gull, and eye looks relatively smaller. Bill larger & more robust on Glaucous Gull, and Glaucous Gulls this age have a more cleanly demarked dark bill tip than this birds show.
