Description of the Bird

Very pale gull, about Herring Gull sized, with small dark bill with a slight pink base. Uniform pale cream color and thin dark markings on upperparts (and bill color) consistent with 1st winter bird.

On closer views, faint tail band (see photos) and thin barring on the upperparts and upperwing, including coverts and scapulars. Primaries were nearly all white uniform with the rest of the bird, but with very faint shadows in the primaries. I'm not certain if the "Thayer's like" primary pattern apparent on some of the photos was caused by overlapping feathers or if the inner and outer webs of the primaries were indeed darker and lighter (all consistent with pale Kumlien's Iceland Gulls seen in western New York state over the past few winters).

See photos for other details.
Behaviors: Bird was first seen alone flying low over the Arkansas river below the Pueblo Reservoir dam. The bird flew up to the dam, circled a few times, and went over the dam and down out of sight.

The bird was refound out among large groups of Ring-billed, Herring, California and a couple Lesser Black-backed gulls feeding in large flocks out on the water in the middle of the south end of the reservoir. After spending 45 minutes (give or take half an hour??) or so out on the water, the bird flew to the south shore marina tires where it settled in (among a concentration of mostly Herring and 2 young GLAUCOUS GULLS) and rested until I left around 4:30pm.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: 1st cycle (pale)

Similar Species Discussion

Glaucous ruled out by size, bill structure and color, longer wings. Easy comparison with 2 Glaucous Gulls present near the resting Iceland Gull. Way to pale for Thayer's. Not a leucistic Herring or other similar sized bird by faint markings on upperparts and tail, bill and head structure, and overall coloration.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I've seen a dozen or so young "Kumlien's" Iceland Gulls (and Glaucous), most at very close range, in and near Ithaca, NY. Additional observations at Pueblo Reservoir, Niagara Falls area.
