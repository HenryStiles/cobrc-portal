Description of the Bird

Viewed for 20 minutes as bathed, preened, strolled on mudflat/adjacent water. Entirely pale biscuit color to white (at times bird appeared nearly white, at other times tinged uniformly brown), with long wing tips that were concolorous with body (as were tertials) when folded. Back appeared same color as wings. The bird, actually, appeared nearly white. Bill black with small pale base. Bill tiny, head moderate build. THE BIRD WAS ACTUALLY A BIT SMALLER THAN NEARBY ADULT CAGUs. Outstretched wing was uniform in color except slightly darker area on outermost two primries. No secondary bar. Tail pale and uniform in color, slightly brown. Legs pink. Plumage looked like 35A.16 on p. 254 of Howell and Dunn's gull book, though no dark patch on face. Bill pattern and size similar as well. Head a bit more "strong" in appearance.

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull would almost certainly be larger, have some dark on secondary bar, dark tail band. Also bill smaller than vast majority of THGU. Small size, tail pattern, and wing pattern above all c/w Iceland Gull and wrong for THGU or at very extreme of that species' appearance
