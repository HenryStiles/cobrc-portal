Description of the Bird

Uniformly colored gull, the color of a lightly browned biscuit. It stood among several hundred Ring-billed Gulls and a few Herring Gulls. Was roughly intermediate in size. Bird looked slim with relatively long wings. Wing coverts speckled in appearance. Tertials marbled/speckled throughout. Folded primaries same biscuit color, but broadly edged in white or near white. Head dove like in shape. Bill also proportionately delicate. About basal 1/5th black, a little more extensive on mandible than maxilla. Did not not details of pattern on rest of bird as Bald Eagle scared flock. In flight, uniformly colored tail, similar in color to back, noted. Wings totally uniform in color from above, without any dark on primaries nor any secondary bar. View from atop good, from beneath in bad lighting, but underwings appeared uniformly silvery. Bird circled off to west with most of other gulls.
