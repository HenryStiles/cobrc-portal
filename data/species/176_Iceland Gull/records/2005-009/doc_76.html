Description of the Bird

The bird was an obvious gull, smaller than Herring, larger than Ring-billed. The bird had a round head compared to Herring and smaller bill. Structurally, the bird looked similar to Thayer's in my experience including size and shape of bird and bill. The plumage was quite noticeably different. I spotted a very pale bird with a gray scapulars and whitish primaries from a distance. I thought it might be one of the many Glaucous gulls that had been frequenting the lake, until it swam next to a Ring-billed Gull. I immediately thought it is much too small for Glaucous Gull. I drove and walked close to observe and photograph the bird. The overall coloration was very pale, nearly white, but with some pale grayish-brown spotting in the wing coverts, tertials, flanks and sides of neck. The spotting was pale and limited, making these features resemble 1st or 2nd Winter Glaucous. The scapulars were light grey; giving me reason to believe it was a 2nd winter bird. The primaries were slightly paler than the rest of the bird, what I would describe as a shade darker than white. There were no notable mirrors, or primary tip color different from the rest of the primaries as seen in the sitting (floating) bird. The bird's head and face were very white, again with only limited spotting around the base of the sides of the neck. This gave the bird an overall pale (almost whitish) appearance including the head, neck, primaries, coverts, tertials, breast and under tail. The bill was flesh colored with a dark tip of about 1/3. The iris appeared dark.

The bird was too small for Glaucous. Also, only the scapulars were (adult grey), not the entire mantle. I do not believe Glaucous Gull ever shows this pattern. Thayer's gull was the bird I struggled with the hardest, due to its plumage characteristics and overall size and structure. However, this bird looked much too pale, paler than any THGU I have ever seen in person, photograph, or artist rendition. The bird fit very well the 2nd Winter Iceland Gull depicted in The Sibley Guide to Birds.
Behaviors: The bird floated and swam in water near shore, climbing up onto land with many other gulls. I was not able to see leg color when this took place as other birds obscured that feature.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 2nd cycle
