Description of the Bird

A pale gull larger than Ring-billed Gulls and obviously smaller than Herring Gulls in the vicinity, the bird had dark yellow eyes, a pinkish-gray bill with a distinct black tip accounting for about 40% of its length, and bright pink legs. The mantle was pale gray, paler than that of any of the Ring-billed and Herring gulls nearby. The forehead, face, chin, throat, and breast were white and unmarked. The white crown area around the eyes were very lightly streaked with pale brown, as was the nape. The rest of the underparts were smudgy pale brown.

The rump was white and contrasted with the gray mantle and the off-white-based, pale brown tail. The wing coverts and tertials were all immature-type feathers, being white with distinct pale-medium brown barring. The primaries on the folded wing were essentially white with only-just-visible pale brown centers. The tail tip fell between the tips of P7 and P8.
Behaviors: loafing
Call: none heard
Plumage: Second Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull of similar age is the only taxon of concern here, and that species is easily ruled out by the white primaries (second-cycle Thayer's Gulls have dark primaries) and the tertial pattern.

Previous Experience

I have seen >40 Iceland Gulls, but have only seen a second-cycle bird twice before. I have seen >35 similarly-aged Thayer's Gulls.
