Description of the Bird

Sean spotted a pale gray backed adult gull out on the ice and brought my attention to it. It was clearly smaller than a Herring Gull, probably nearer California Gull in size, though no direct comparison available. Its wings seemed proportionately long, longer than HERG. The back and wings were gray, paler than that of nearby adult HERGs. The tertials tips formed a fairly broad prominent white crescent. The primaries were white with a couple gray smudges distally on wing. The underparts and head were largely white, with modest dusky streaking on the nape sides of chest, and around eye. The bill was dinky: slim and short (and mostly dull yellow-green). That, combined with the rounded head, gave the bird an almost Mew Gull like jizz. Legs pink. Eye pale. In flight, wing tip pattern best fit types h or j in Howell and Dunn's Gull book.

Similar Species Discussion

Glaucous Gull larger, all white wingtips, stouter wings.
Thayer's Gull darker mantled, more dark on wing tip and that dark typically blacker than this birds light to medium gray. Light eye unusual in Thayer's Gull. Also, this bird was on the small side of ICGU, and thus would be at the very extreme for THGU, if not beyond.
