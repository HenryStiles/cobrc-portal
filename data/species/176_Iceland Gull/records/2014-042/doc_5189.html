Description of the Bird

I spotted the Iceland Gull on an ice shelf among other gulls on the north side of Aurora Reservoir, and then spent the next 20 minutes obtaining photos.  The following characteristics seen in the photos support the identification as adult, non-breeding Iceland (Kumlien's) Gull:  (1) size larger than Ring-billed Gulls and smaller than Herring Gulls, (2) light gray mantle similar that of Ring-billed Gulls, (3) folded primaries mostly white with some gray bands, (4) pink legs, (5) petite yellow bill, and (6) limited streaking on head.  The wingtip pattern in the photo of spread wings appears to be type "k" in the Howell and Mactavish (2003) classification.

Similar Species Discussion

The most similar adult gull species expected in this area would be Thayer's and Glaucous.  The primaries are too pale, and the mantle is too pale, for Thayer's.  The gray bands on the primaries rule out Glaucous.
