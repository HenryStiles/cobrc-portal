Description of the Bird

Small white-headed gull adult, about the size of nearby California Gulls. Head white with light gray smudgy marking around eyes. Neck white with smudgy pale gray streaks on nape extending onto upper chest almost forming a collar. Remainder of underparts white. Back and upperwings pearly gray, marginally paler than such of nearby Herring Gull, with white trailing edge to secondaries and primaries plus patterned primary tips as follows: Gray P10 with long white mirror extending to feather tip. P9 with limited gray on outer web and long white mirror extending to feather tip except for a very narrow gray band near terminus. P8 with dusky gray on inner and outer webs but with moderately extensive white on inner web and white tip to feather. P7 (still growing in) appears to have limited dusky gray on inner web, extenisve white on outer web and at tip. P6 with limited gray near feather tip and white spot at tip of feather. Tail all white. Bill dull ivory yellow proximal to gonys and brighter yellow from gonys to tip. Red spot on distal mandible. Feet and tarsi moderately bright pink. Iris brownish. Narrow pinkish oribital ring. Overall shape, including bill, delicate with dove-like aspect. Wingtip pattern closely resembling that of wingtip L or M, considered norm of Iceland Gulls from Newfoundland, as covered in Howell and Dunn. 

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull would have darker, and more extensive dark on primary tips, averages darker mantle and larger size. Eye color is more typical of THGU, but within range of ICGU per Howell and Dunn
