Description of the Bird

At about 9:45 am (2 hours and 45 minutes) after sunrise, I noticed that a small pallid gull had joined the group of gulls feeding upon dead fish at Prewitt Reservoir. Tim Smart and I had been watching gulls at this small bay at Prewitt for more than 30 minutes, putting the sun directly to our backs. Its uniform color and small size immediately put Iceland Gull in mind. The distance was about 150 feet, the viewing conditiions superb, unperturbed by heat waves or wind. I was using a Kowa 88mm scope with 20-60x eyepiece. Total viewing time 5+ minutes.

The Iceland Gull was petite and long-winged, smaller in body and bill than nearby immature Thayer?s Gulls (n=2). The head was somewhat rounded. The delicate bill was entirely yellow, except a blackish smudge near the bill tip, predominantly (or totally) on the lower mandible. The legs were pink. Just proximal to this, on the lower mandible, was a small reddish spot. When standing, the back and wings were entirely gray (except tertials and primaries), the shade a bit paler than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The tertials had a prominent white "tertial crescent." The primaries were gray, a shade darker than the mantle, with white crescents. The eye was yellow. The underparts, head, and neck were white, except dusky smudging on sides of chest, sides of neck, upper back, and lower hindneck plus some fine dusky streaking on the crown.

In flight, tail and rump white. Mantle evenly gray except a hint of slightly darker gray on outer 2 or 3 primaries with vague white "subapical mirrors" on those feathers. Underwing was completely white.
Behaviors: picking at dead fish at lake side
Call: not heard
Plumage: subadult

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull would have been darker backed, darker eyed, darker on primaries and would have shown far more gray/black on upperwings in flight. Likely would've been larger and larger billed as well.
