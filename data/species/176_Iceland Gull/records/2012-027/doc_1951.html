Description of the Bird

As Glenn Walbek and I searched for yonder Western Gull, an Iceland Gull made an appearance 50 or so yards away. The lighting was mediocre, but the bird was present for 30 minutes or so, walked around quite a bit, and thus gave us goodly views. The bird was pallid overall, paler than any 1st cycle Thayer's Gull seen that day. It was overall pale brown, though on the back, some pale gray feathers were coming in, and a few pale gray feathers were also noted on the median wing coverts. The greater coverts were entirely speckled brown. The tertials, though worn, still showed mottling/speckling throughout. The primaries were brown, similar in color to the rest of the bird, with moderate whitish edging. The legs were pink, the bill entirely black. Structure wise, the bird was long-winged and rather tiny compared to the nearby Herring Gull, though not that different in size from a nearby 1st cycle Thayer's Gull. The bird never flew whilst we were present.
