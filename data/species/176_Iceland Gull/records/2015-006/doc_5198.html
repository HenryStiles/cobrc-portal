Description of the Bird

Smallish gray-backed gull (similar in size to Cal Gull) initially found in presence of long-staying ICGU (already documented). Head and neck finally streaked in gray, slightly less so near bill and less on throat. Mantle pale gray, slightly paler than nearby RBGU and one ad nearby THGU. Tertials largely white. Folded primaries medium gray with white spots. Underparts white. Tail white. Bill ivory-yellow proximal to gonydeal angle. Distal to gonydeal angle was more yellow with small red spot at gonydeal angle. Bill with weak gonydeal angle, similar in size to nearby Cal Gulls. Spread wing. For spreadwing details, see photos. Match pattern "O" in Howell and Dunn's Gull book (and article by Mactavish and Howell). Eye pale but not white, within range of ICGU

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull usually has darker eye, darker mantle, darker primary tips with more extensive dark on primary tips. Also, this bird would be at the small end of THGU
