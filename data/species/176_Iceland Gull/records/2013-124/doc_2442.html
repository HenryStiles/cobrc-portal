Description of the Bird

Distinctly smaller than nearby HERG and longer winged. Bill and head proportionately small as well, imparting delicate look when bird relaxed. Smaller than pale THGU (also 2nd year) that was nearby. This bird seemed to be in 2nd cycle headed towards 3rd. The head, neck, and underparts were white (except perhaps some brown smudging near legs). The mantle pale gray, paler than that of a HERG. The wing coverts were quite worn and whitish. The tertials were had broad white edges and were mottled pale brownish-gray in center. The folded primaries were also quite pale brownish-gray, with moderately broad white edging, but also (oddly) had the appearance of having mirrors starting to appear. Can't explain this, but it looked this way in flight, too. In flight, the wing was totally white below. The top appeared very pale brown, with no secondary bar, and modestly darker brownish gray on outer three primaries, which had broad pale inner webs and what almost appeared to be sub-apical spots on outer webs (see 35A.38 on p. 258 of Howell and Dunn's gull book- the bird seen at Stewart's pond was much paler, and the number of primaries with dark more limited, but you can see that resemblance to having adult-like sub-apical spots.) The tail had a pale and diffuse narrow band. The legs were pink. The bill had a yellow-pink base, not nearly as pink as (that is, more yellow than) base of young THGU or HERG, and a small blackish tip. Eye whitish.
Behaviors: Perched at edge of pond with RB Gulls, Herring Gulls, a Thayer's Gull or two.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 2nd going into 3rd cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull is main confusion species. No secondary bar, limited and pale edges to primaries, smaller size and more delicate head + white eye all, in combo, eliminate THGU. Also, tail markings more c/w Iceland Gull and mottled tertials c/w Iceland not Thayer's Gull
