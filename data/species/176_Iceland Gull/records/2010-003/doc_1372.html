Description of the Bird

Moderately large gull- about Herring Gull- sized but with an overall more delicate build. Overall very pale in comparison with all other gulls present (Mostly Ring-billed and Herring.) Body nearly white, back very pale gray, primaries essentially concolor with back and rest of wings. Close inspection showed that primaries had pale gray centers and white fringes. Reasonable primary projection, similar to Herring Gull. Bill not as robust as Herring Gull, overall pink with clean black tip. Head rounded, very slight smudging around dark eye.
Behaviors: Preening & roosting with mixed flock of gulls on ice.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 2nd-Winter

Similar Species Discussion

Structure of head and bill and light gray tone precludes a leucistic 2nd-cycle Herring Gull, which I would expect to be very pale brown or even yellowish. Similar-aged Glaucous Gull should appear larger, with a blocky head and more massive bill. Additionally, Glaucous should be a more stubby-looking bird due to its shorter primary projection and the primaries should be all white, not with the pale gray centers. Thayer's of this age are typically browner and in the cases of birds I've seen in Colorado, darker overall. (I know there is a general problem with the continuum of birds from lighter Thayer's through darker Iceland.) Other largish gulls should be darker overall with distinctly darker primaries. Other white-winged gulls would be much smaller.
