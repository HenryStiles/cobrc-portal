Description of the Bird

I was birding at Jumbo Reservoir with Jessie Barry, Joey Kellner and Steve Stachowiak. I was scanning the lake with my binocular when I spotted a very pale gull that immediately struck me as a Kumlien's Gull or an extremely pale and worn Thayer's Gull. Upon looking through the scope I realized it was an Iceland Gull and drew everyone's attention to the bird. We were able to watch the bird for about twenty minutes at a distance of ca. 150 - 400 meters. During this time I was able to get a few very distant, but identifiable images. These photos show much what needs to be seen, but I was not able to get a photo of the upperside of the bird in flight so my description will focus on that.

Overall, similar to a Thayer's Gull but slightly smaller and appeared longer-winged when resting on water with 3 1/2 primaries extending past the tip of the tail. Bill also appeared smaller. Slimmer. Overall color pattern frostier, particularly on tertials when resting on water. head white. Upperparts extensively frosty in color. Underparts fairly pale, but more brownish on belly. Some mottling with whitish and pale brown but also somewhat worn making exact pattern difficult to determine. Scapulars and tertials pale with darker pale brown barring and white notches.

In flight this big appeared very pale. From above it showed only a very faint hint of a brown wash to the secondaries--not the dark brown secondary bar shown by Thayer's Gull. Darker outer webs to primaries restricted to P9, P8 and possibly to a lesser extent P7. Overall this seemed to be an average Kumlien's--not extremely frosty or worn, but also not on the darker end. Tail slightly darker than primaries with a bit of a darker smudge subterminally and white tail tip--fairly similar to Howell and Dunn photo 35a.35. Overall color pattern of tail blended smoothly into rump without much contrast. NOTE resting photo of bird on water, tail is shaded and looks darker than it appeared at most times in the field.

Eye dark. Bill dark with a bit of flesh color coming into base of bill. Leg color not seen/noted.
Behaviors: The bird was seen resting on the water alone. There were a few other gulls including two Glaucous Gulls (one appeared similar but slightly paler in overall color aspect, the other much whiter). They dwarfed this bird (but were proportionately shorter winged). Twice the bird got up, flew and circled. I was watching the bird closely in my scope the first time. The second time I got my camera and got a couple distant images. It did not interact with any other birds. On the water the bird rode slightly higher, with wings and tail held higher above water than nearby Herring Gulls (direct comparison).
Call: None
Plumage: first-cycle

Similar Species Discussion

See description for elimination of Thayer's Gull. Glaucous-winged Gull eliminated for similar reasons, and also that species is much larger, larger billed, shorter-winged, more even colored patter with less intricate markings to upperparts.

Previous Experience

I have been an extremely active birder since 1988 and have visited most of North America and several other continents to study birds. In my home state of Colorado I have been actively involved with birding and the Colorado Bird Records Committee, serving two terms while in college and graduate school. I served as regional editor for the Colorado / Wyoming region of North American birds. I have authored a variety of materials on bird identification including 70 accounts for the National Geographic Complete Birds of North America. Since 2000, I have served as a senior leader for the birdwatching tour company WINGS, where I have led tours throughout much of the Lower 48, Alaska, Mexico and the Caribbean. Since 2005, my primary work has been as Project Leader for eBird and the Avian Knowledge Network at the Cornell Lab of Ornithology.

I have seen a few hundred Kumlien's Iceland Gulls largely in New York, Ontario, Massachusetts, Pennsylvania, New Jersey and New Hampshire. I have also small numbers farther west to Minnesota, Wisconsin, Indiana, Ohio and Nebraska. I have only seen one other Kumlien's Gull in Colorado (along with a few Thayer's / Kumlien's, which have appeared intermediate in some respect).
