Description of the Bird

A gull observed at the edge of a lake, appearing intermediate in size between accompanying Ring-billed Gulls and Herring Gulls, with all white feathers on head and underparts. Upperparts pale gray (palere than accompanying gulls) on mantle with broad white tertial crescent and small white scapular crescent. Primaries mostly snow white, but each primary with a subterminal patch of pale gray. Tail white. Legs pink. Iris yellow. Bill pale yellow, with a small red spot at gonys. Bill smaller than that of Herring Gull, with weak gonydeal angle.
Behaviors: Walking in shallow water, standing, sleeping, preening.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing about the plumage suggests albinism, so the only similar species are other large "white-winged" gulls. Too small for Glaucous Gull or Glaucous-winged Gull. Yellow eye and lack of black in primaries and very pale gray mantle rule out Thayer's Gull. Gray in wingtips indicates the North American subspecies "Kumlieni".

Previous Experience

I have only seen adult Kumlien's Gull in Massachusetts where I spent more than 10 yrs birding. In the last 10 yrs in Colorado I have seen several adult Thayer's Gulls each winter, and a handful of adult Glaucous Gulls. I have seen adult Glaucous-winged Gulls on several trips to California.
