Description of the Bird

Smallish "large gull" (i.e., smaller than nearby HERGs). Back feathers fresh, pale gray, paler than those of HERG. Wing coverts and tertials worn; wing coverts essentially concolorous, grayish-brown or brownish-gray. Tertials nearly white with some speckling. Primaries with narrow pale brown centers and broad white edges. Beneath, white with some brown mottling. Eye darkish. Bill pink/yellow tipped in black; delicate. Head rounded, not quite as dove-like as some, but more gentle than most HERG present. Bill smaller than those of nearby HERG. Legs Pink.

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull: Since back feathers fresh, a THGU should have same color back, or slightly darker, than that of HERG. The speckled tertials are an ICGU mark, and argues against THGU. The color of the interior of the folded primaries was similar to that of the wing coverts, good for ICGU, and the brownish centers were narrow with broad white margins, typical of ICGU, not so for THGU.
