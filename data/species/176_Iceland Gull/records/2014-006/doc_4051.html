Description of the Bird

Bird size was slightly larger than the myriad of ring-billed gulls milling around, but smaller than the Herring Gulls and the Glaucous Gull seen in the vicinity.  Bill was completely dark.  Head and body had very faint brown pattern.  The bird almost appeared like a dirty white.  Primaries also appeared to have a very light brown coloring.  I compared the primary feather color of the bird to the observed glaucous primaries and the glaucous was a clear white while the iceland had a dirty white color.  Legs were pink.  I couldn't get a good view of the eye color or more detailed looks at feather color pattern on the wings given the distance I was viewing from.  The closest other possibility was a Thayer's which I eliminated based on the almost white coloring of the bird.  Glaucous-winged was an extremely remote possibility, but the smaller size, overall light coloring, and lack of a larger bill eliminated this option.

Similar Species Discussion

I used observed size to eliminate the larger gulls almost immediately.  Many Herring gulls were present to help distinguish that this bird was noticeably smaller.  The bird was larger but by a smaller margin than the ring-billed gulls.  What truly eliminated the ring-billed gull and most of the other gulls possible was the primaries that were a dirty white (very slight brown coloring).  Additionally the entire bird was almost free of brown while most other juvenile gulls have a clear brown coloring to them.  This really left Thayer's as the last possibility, but all my research on Thayers juv is that even a very light colored bird would still have reasonably mid brown primaries - nothing approaching white.  So I eliminated Thayer's and concluded it was likely a juv Iceland Gull.

Resources Used

I used Nat Geographic guide 4th edition, post-observation internet picture observation, and Sibley's 1st Ed guide to help sort out my observations.

Previous Experience

I have only seen a couple other Iceland Gulls, one of which was an adult - so my experience with the species is limited.  I have been viewing gulls for several years now and I find good comfort with adult ID - Juv ID is still tricky for all but more common species.
