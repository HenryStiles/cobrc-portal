Description of the Bird

Two birds were seen, here they are called A and B. Bird A was large, possibly as large as a small Herring Gull, and therefore was probably a male. B was smaller (presumably female), barely larger than Ring-billed Gull. The head for both A and B was more rounded than Herring Gull and the bills were smaller than that of Herring Gull. The bill was bicolored with the outer half being black and the inner half pinkish. On B, the bill was about the size of that of Ring-billed Gull. Both A and B had very pale off-white coloration all over body, lacking any crisp marbling, and therefore suggesting second-cycle plumage. Both birds had some pale gray coming in on the mantle and scapulars. Both birds had adult-like scapular crescents formed by the largest two scapulars on the middle of the saddle being gray with broad white tips, a pattern I have observed on other second-cycle Iceland Gulls. The wing-tips were the same pale glaucous coloration as the rest of the upperparts (mantle, scapulars, wing coverts), and they were long, extending well beyond the tail. The tail was seen on A in flight and appeared similarly pale off-white as the rest of the bird. The wings of both A and B appeared uniformly pale in flight. From photos, A had limited darker feathers in the outer primaries on the spread wing, and B had all of the outer primaries darker brown than the whitish inner primaries on the spread wing (Note that the digiscoped photos of B are underexposed causing the darker featehrs to appear darker than they really appeared in the field).

Similar Species Discussion

Other white-winged gulls (Glaucous and Glaucous-winged) would be larger than both of these birds. Glaucous would have larger, paler bill. Glaucous-winged would have larger, darker bill. Thayer's Gull would show darker brown wing tips and brown secondary bar and brown tail in flight and would be larger than bird B.
