Description of the Bird

Mark Peterson, Brad Steger, Lisa Edwards, and I were at Valmont Reservoir early in the morning of February 2, 2008. I had just spotted two Glaucous Gulls (a basic adult and first year), when I noticed a light winged gull, that looked like it was lacking any dark brown in the wings. I said to Mark, wow did I get see an Iceland Gull flying around out there. There were many gulls flying around, and I lost the bird. Then I refound it swimming in the water. The bird was light brown overall, with long wing tips that were the same color light brown as the rest of the bird (which elimnates Thayer's Gull). The bill was dark, I couldn't make out any other color, do to the distance of the bird. The size was smaller than the nearby Herring Gulls, and not the same size or bigger, which elimnates Glaucous-winged Gull. The bird had roundish head and bright pink legs. In flight, the wings and tail didn't have any darker brown feathers anywhere.
Behaviors: Flying and swimming and standing on the ice.
Call: none
Plumage: first year (juvenile)
