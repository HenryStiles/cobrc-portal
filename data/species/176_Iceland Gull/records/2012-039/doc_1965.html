Description of the Bird

On 29 March, Nick Moore and I pulled up to observe gulls feeding on dead fish along a cove of Prewitt Reservoir. As we were observing a cluster of about 20 birds (including Herring, Ring-billed, and Thayer's) a nearly adult gull came flying in from the main portion of the lake. We were immediately struck by the pale mantle and pale wingtips. I got my camera out in time to take a couple photos, only one of which showed any pertinent field marks. The bird then mulled among the flock, never allowing for further photography, but good for further field marks assessment.

This was a pale gray mantled bird, with dusky markings on the wingtips that best matches the "M" type primary pattern in Howell and Dunn's Gulls of the Americas. This wingtip type is considered within the "core" of Kumlien's Gull wingtips, representing of more than 50% of Kumlien's Gulls. Note that the primaries were quite worn, which was very noticeable when the bird was perched. Thus, the white tips to the outer primaries were most worn away. The underwings appeared totally white except for a hint of pale gray at the tips of two of the outermost primaries.

The uppertail coverts were white. The tail was mostly white, with some vague dusky markings distally on some of the outer rectrices. The underparts were white, except the sides of the upper chest, which were diffusely streaked with dusky. This streaking was also present on the nape with a few finer streaks on crown and auriculars.

The eye was pale, more so than the great majority of Thayer's Gulls, but not as pale as a Herring Gull. The bill was yellow, with a black tip, just proximal to which there was a small red-orange spot at the gonydeal angle. The orbital ring was pinky red, the legs pink.

The bird's size was quite small. It was smaller than the Thayer's Gull present, and appeared intermediate in size between Herring Gull and RB Gull.

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's GUll is the main concern. The bird's pale mantle and wingtip pattern should eliminate THGU, especially considering that 3rd cycle birds typically show more extensively dark wingtips than adults. The paler mantle, smaller size, and fairly pale eye also argue against Thayer's Gull.
