Description of the Bird

An immature gull somewhat similar to nearby First Basic Thayer's Gull, but much paler and without the darker primaries of that taxon. The bill was quite short and mostly black, but with a pale base, mostly on the mandible. The wings extended well past the tail tip with the tip of P7 falling just short of the tail. The primaries on the folded wing were fringed very widely with white, but because of the paleness of the primaries, the wide fringes made the primaries look white; they were very wide (much wider than found on Thayer's Gulls of the same age).

The tertials were white with a few brown bars and without a solid dark center. The legs were short and bright pink. The tail was darker than the rump, which was the palest part of the upperparts, being nearly white. The underparts were a creamy white with a variety of small darker splotches.
Behaviors: Foraging in landfill
Call: none heard
Plumage: First Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gulls of similar age are the most similar species, but details of the tertial pattern and the very pale coloration rule out that taxon.
