Description of the Bird

From the boat ramp on the east side of Barr Lake, I spotted though a scope the adult Iceland (Kumlien's) Gull on an ice shelf about 100 yards away, initially walking among the other gulls (Ring-billed and Herring) and then preening.  I changed vantage points a few times to get views from slightly different angles during the 15 minutes of observation, until an eagle scattered the gulls at 5:30 PM.  Light was good for scope viewing but not photography; nevertheless, I have attached one of the only photos I have, even though it doesn't show much in the way of identifiable features, to complement the detailed notes I made at the time of the sighting.

The Iceland Gull was only slightly larger than the Ring-billed Gulls in its company.  The pale gray back color was similar to that of the Ring-billed Gulls.  The head was small and round, and it had "dirty" smudges on it (also on the neck).  The bill was pale yellow with a red spot and was noticeably petite.  Eye color was not discernible in the cloudy evening lighting.  Legs appeared pink.  Folded primaries were distinctly pale -- 2/3 white with gray bands in between appearing medium/dark gray, not black.  The following photos of other adult Iceland Gulls in Colorado show primaries essentially identical in appearance to those of the Barr Lake Iceland Gull:

Anthem Ranch,  26 Jan 2013, by Beto Matheus:  www.flickr.com/photos/58821080@N07/8422931924/

Aurora Reservoir, 29 Jan 2014, by David Dowell:  www.flickr.com/photos/76753483@N08/12282449836/

Similar Species Discussion

Overall size, pink legs, small round head, and petite bill narrow down the most likely species to Thayer's Gull and Iceland Gull.  Iceland Gull was chosen based on the pale primaries.  Another strike against Thayer's Gull is that the mantle appeared pale gray, not the slightly darker gray typical of Thayer's.
