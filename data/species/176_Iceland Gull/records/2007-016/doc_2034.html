Description of the Bird

We observed two 1st cycle Iceland Gulls during the day. One was at the Weld County landfill on State Highway 14. We watched that gull for perhaps an hour before the gull headed west toward Black Hollow Reservoir. Upon arriving at Black Hollow, Tony Leukering spotted a second Iceland Gull of similar characteristics and we were able to observe both birds in the same field of view.

Both gulls were medium sized, being much larger than the Franklin’s Gulls present, larger than the Ring-billeds, somewhat similar in size to California Gulls, and noticeably smaller than nearby Herring Gulls. Their bills were relatively short, were broad through the gonys, and were black. The heads were white, although a few light brown flecks were interspersed throughout. The eye were dark. The upper parts were whitish throughout with interspersed light brown markings scattered across the mantle. The upper wings were whitish, although the outer webs of the primaries and secondaries, characteristic of the Kumlien’s group of the species. The primary and secondary coverts were tipped with light brown. The underwings were mainly whitish, although the coverts and axillaries were much browner than above. These mostly light brownish feathers were highlighted with brown mainly along the outer webs and tips. The necks of both birds were mostly white with splotchy brown markings. The breasts and bellies were splotchy brown underlain by white. The rumps and upper tail coverts were basally white with brown bands. The tails was also mostly white, except for thin light brown bands. These bands were strongest toward the center rectrices. The undertail coverts were white basally with strong, dark brown and relatively thick bars. The legs were a fleshy pink.
Behaviors: The Weld County Landfill bird was observed foraging in the dump as well as flying. The Black Hollow Reservoir bird was observed swimming, standing, and flying.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: 1st cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Although there is a continuum of color and pattern between Kumlien’s Iceland Gulls and Thayer’s Gulls, in general, 1st cycle Thayer’s are darker and browner overall with blackish brown to dark brown wingtips, This darker brown color is especially evident on the outer webs of primaries 6-10 and portray the classic “venetian blind” effect typical of young Thayer’s. 1st cycle Thayer’s also have a dark secondary bar which the subject birds and Kumlien’s don’t possess. The tail of a 1st cycle Thayer’s is also darker brown overall.

Glaucous Gulls are much larger and typically have a distinct bicolored bill with basal being pink and proximal being black. Glaucous-winged Gulls are also larger, are more blotchy gray-brown throughout, lack the outer web contrasts, and have a mostly dark grayish-lead colored tail.
