Description of the Bird

Tim Smart and I had returned to Prewitt Reservoir in the evening, to revisit the gull hotspot, a small bay the shore of which was covered by dead fish. At about 5:45 (an hour and fifteen minutes prior to sunset), a pale small gull flew in. It was rather petite, substantially smaller than the nearby Herring Gulls, but larger than the Ring-billeds. It was long-winged, round-headed, and had a small bicolored bill. The overall effect was that of a large Mew Gull. We watched the bird for 4-5 minutes before it flew off. Lighting was excellent. Optics a 88mm Kowa scope with 20-60x eyepiece.

The back was pale gray. The wing coverts and tertials were worn, mottled and freckled with grayish brown. The folded primaries were similar in darkness to that of remainder of wing, but solid grayish brown with fairly broad whitish edging. The head and chest were mostly white, but with some dusky smudging especially on sides of chest and neck. The remainder of the underparts was white, with diffuse scattered brown smudging (sorry, did not note this part of bird in detail). The eye was pale, but not as pale/bright as the adult seen earlier in day. The legs were pink. In flight, the upper wings were mostly evenly colored, excepting a hint of darker edge on outermost couple primaries and a faint secondary bar. The tail was entirely grayish brown and rump white with moderately dense barring/freckling. The bird?s upperparts in flight were very similar to the bird on p. 258 of "The Gulls" book, plate 35a.34. The underwings were entirely pale.
Behaviors: picking at dead fish with other gulls at lake shore
Call: not heard
Plumage: 2nd cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Second year Thayer's Gull would have darker primaries and secondary bar, darker tail. Also, this bird was at small end of ICGU, likely outside range of THGU. The weird color at base of bill is unlike that which I've seen on 2nd year THGU, which usually have pink base, like Herring Gull
