Description of the Bird

A gull slightly larger than accompanying Lesser Black-backed Gull (estimated 23 inches in length), but significantly smaller than accompanying immature Herring Gull. Head gray-brown, with darker smudge around eye, dark eye and smallish black bill. Mantle mottled brown and white, with a patch of gray feathers indicating freshly molted "first alternate" feathers. Underparts uniformly gray-brown like head. Wings heavily mottled brown and white. Tertials brown with several white bars across their center. Primaries concolorous with tertials (brown) with broad white edges. Pink legs and feet.
Behaviors: Sleeping on ice, standing, preening
Call: none
Plumage: immature first cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Plumage resembles dark first cycle "Kumlien's" Iceland Gull, differing from other taxa as follows:

1. "Glaucoides" Iceland Gull probably cannot be differentiated in this plumage, although typically paler with whiter primaries.

2. Thayer's Gull (L. thayeri) in first cycle has primaries that stand out as darker brown than tertials, and dark-centered tertials.

3. Herring Gull (L. argentatus) has blackish primaries and is larger than this bird.

4. California Gull (L. californicus) has blackish primaries and a solidly dark tail. Bill mostly pale rather than dark from early first cycle onward.

5. Hybrid. A hybrid is difficult to rule out. Thayer's X Glaucous-winged could result in a similar combination of plumage features, although one would expect any bird with Glaucous-winged genes to be larger.
