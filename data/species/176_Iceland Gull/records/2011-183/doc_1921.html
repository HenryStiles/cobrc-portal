Description of the Bird

All white winged gull , larger than adjacent Ring-billed but smaller than nearby Herring Gull . Bill somewhat slenderer than Herring , predominantly dark with small paler basal area. Floating on the water , primaries extended beyond tail , primaries same pale color as body and back. Mottling on scapulars , coverts and tertials . Pale white head , dark eye and pink legs . In flight upper wing uniform pale color , somewhat darker tail band contrasting with pale upper tail coverts.
Behaviors: Strong NW wind kept bird floating in water. Occaisonally taking short flights into the wind.
Call: None
Plumage: first cycle

Similar Species Discussion

A 1 st cycle Glaucous-winged Gull had been reported previously by Steve Mlodinow which we hoped to see but this can be ruled on size along as well marbling on the tertials and bill size too. A pale 1 st cycle Thayer's can be ruled out because of lack of dark brown secondary bar and uniform pale primaries .
