Description of the Bird

Bird was associating with 20-30 Ring-billed gulls that were in flight over cottonwood marsh, circling to gain altitude and then flying away to the southwest. Essentially all white with just a hint of dark gray on primaries.

Similar Species Discussion

Thayers would tend to show more gray in the primaries, but admitedly difficult to rule out definitively. Glaucous is ruled out by gray in primaries.

Resources Used

I took photos and studied Sibley and Howell. Then reached out to Floyd, Nunes, Gent, Mlodinow and Leukering. All were ok with ICGU with the exception of Leukering, who felt the photos alone were not definitive.

Previous Experience

I've observed ICGU more or less annually for the past ten years or so prior to moving to Colorado. I'm not a gull expert, but I've spent enough time to have a decent ability to ID in the field. This bird was challenging as it appeared all white, bringing Glaucous into the equasion. Fortunately I was able to take photos that provided the opportunity to study the bird closely.
