Description of the Bird

A pale gull the size of a small Herring Gull uniformly mottled pale gray-brown and off-white. Pink legs. Small blackish bill. Tertials pale gray-brown, barred with off-white. Primaries mostly off-white, giving the spread wing a white appearance.
Behaviors: Loafing and preening while standing on an ice shelf with Ring-billed Gulls. Flying.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull has dark-centered tertials, mostly brown primaries. Glaucous-winged and Glaucous Gulls are larger, heavier-billed, less mottled.
