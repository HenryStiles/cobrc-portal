Description of the Bird

First observed in flight, noting lack of noticeable dark markings on the underwing. Then, sitting (seen through a scope), I noticed intermediate size betweeen Ring-billed Gull and American Herring Gull, lack of brown streaking on head except for lower hindneck, gray of saddle paler than that of Ring-billed and Herring gulls; darker gray wingtips with large white terminal spots on primaries, such that on one side (the bird's right side) where the primaries were well-folded over each other, it was difficult to discern the darker gray of the wing-tip at all. On the bird's left side, several inches of darker gray of the outer webs of the outer primaries was evident below the large white tips of each of the outer primaries. The underparts and tail were entirely white. The bill was yellow with a greenish wash and a small red gonys spot. The size of the bill was not much bigger than the bills of nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The eye was yellow, but not as bright as that of Herring, Ring-billed or Lesser Black-backed Gull. It was paler than that of California Gulls, and most Thayer's Gulls. In flight the wingtips appeared completely white, although in photos the gray was apparent, along with large white mirrors on the outer 4 primaries.

Similar Species Discussion

Glaucous Gull is much larger and lacks gray on the wing-tips. Adult Glaucous-winged Gull is much larger and has much more extensive gray on wing-tips. Thayer's Gull shows blacker wing tips when sitting, and has slightly darker gray saddle than Ring-billed and Herring gulls. Thayer's usually has a darker eye than this bird (but not always), and heavier bill.
