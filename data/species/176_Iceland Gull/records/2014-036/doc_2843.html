Description of the Bird

Almost immediately upon arriving at Woods Lake, I note a very pale mid-sized gull. It was clearly smaller than the nearby Herring Gulls, almost (that is, almost, not truly) approaching in size some of the larger RBGUs. In any case, it was closer to RBGU in size than HERG. The back was paler than that of RBGU or HERG. The wing coverts were worn, whitish, with brown speckling/mottling. The tertials were similar, with mottling throughout, but somewhat faded in general. The primaries were long, whitish, with grayish centers and pretty much the same color as the rest of the wing. The underparts were mostly white, with some brownish mottling on chest (mostly lower). The head was pretty much white, but a few brownish streaks present. The bill was small and fine with a dull olive-grayish base and black distally. Pale base a bit more than 50% of bill. Legs bright pink. Eye yellow. 

In flight, the upperwing looked white with a line of pale gray or gray-brown on outer webs of outermost two primaries. Underside of primaries bright white. Tail uniformly pale gray, about same color as remainder of bird. Rump white with a few markings. If one were to use Howell's ICGU/THGU wing rating scale and apply it to an immature, this bird would've been a "B" or a "D" - see page 252 of the "Gulls" book. 

Similar Species Discussion

Smaller than any other "large" gull. Most closely resembles THGU, but even on small side for that species. Wings paler than that of THGU, with more limited dark on primaries and NO SECONDARY BAR (sorry to have skipped mentioning that specifically in description) plus mottled tertials. Bird really had that small billed, rounded head, almost dove like quality seen in smaller Iceland Gulls.
