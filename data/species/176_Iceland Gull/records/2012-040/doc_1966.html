Description of the Bird

On 1 April 2012, Tim Smart and I found two gulls actively circling over a pond at St Vrain State Park, apparently looking for prey. One was rather dark backed, a 2nd year or so Lesser Black-backed Gull. The other was very pale mantled, resembling a Glaucous Gull in this feature. However, the bird was a tad smaller than the LBBG and had a bit of dusky on the wingtips.

The mantle was pearly gray, appearing paler than that of a RBGU. The wingtips were most consistent with type "L" in Howell and Dunn's Gulls of the America's book, which is within the "typical" Kumlien's Gull range. The underwing showed very limited pale dusky markings at the tips of two or so outermost primaries. Otherwise, the underwings were totally white. The plumage seemed in excellent condition. The underparts, tail, uppertail coverts, and head appeared snowy white. The legs were bright pink. The bill medium in size and yellow, with a small orange-red gonydeal spot. The eyes were quite pale and whitish, though not quite the clear white of a Herring Gull. The orbital ring was rose-pink. The birds circled for about 5 minutes, then drifted of together to the southwest.

Similar Species Discussion

Thayer's Gull would have been darker backed with darker wingtips and more extensively marked wingtips. The eye color would've also been rather atypical for THGU.
