Description of the Bird

The bird was a gull of medium size (larger than ring-billed, smaller than herring) and uniform coloring. The difficulty with Iceland gull is, of course, the differentiation from Thayer's, which can be largely a matter of opinion on the part of the observer.

In my opinion,this bird falls within the spectrum of "Thiceland gull" sufficiently toward the Iceland end to qualify it as an Iceland gull. While I defer to local birders with more expertise in gulls than I have (several of whom also saw this bird), I think this is a fairly solid classification based on the uniform coloration of the bird. While it was a pale brownish color (as opposed to the white that would be expected of a more eastern bird), the color of the wings (the bird spread the wings a few times, giving good looks at this feature) was uniform down the length, without any of the contrasting darker primary tips that would be expected in a first cycle Thayer's gull.
Behaviors: Standing on ice- nothing definitive.
Call: None
Plumage: First cycle

Similar Species Discussion

The debate about this bird would be centered upon differentiating Iceland from Thayer's. As previously discussed, the uniform coloration along the entire length of the wing is my main criterion for classifying this bird as Iceland. Other observers may have more detailed ID support.
