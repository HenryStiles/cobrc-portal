Description of the Bird

I was walking alone around under the tall elm trees at the Chico Basin Ranch banding station, when I heard a song "bee buzz buzz buzz"! I quickly thought, that has to be a Golden-winged Warbler. After a couple of minutes, I was able to see the bird and indeed the bird was a Golden-winged Warbler. See my photos. Small warbler, with black throat, golden-yellow crown, black ear patch, grayish underparts, a golden/yellow wing panel on each wing. Black bill, black eyes, warbler shaped. Bill Maynard, appeared a little later, and he was also able to see the bird, and also get photos. The bird didn't have any hint of yellowish underparts, so I think the bird was a typical male Golden-winged Warbler.
Behavior: Staying pretty high in the elms mostly.
Call: heard giving normal "bee buzz buzz buzz song", and an alternate song, that was also buzzy.
Plumage: singing male
