Description of the Bird

face with black maks surrounded by white and yellow crown and black throat. Whitish breast and belly. Nape and back light gray with strongly contrasting broad yellow wingbars. No signs of green on back or on white of belly or other noticable signs of hybridization with Blue-winged Warbler.
Behavior: Foraged and sang mostly from mid- to upper levels of tall Siberian elms, often covered by foliage.
Call: song primary bee-buzz-buzz-buzz and secondary song
Plumage: adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

No other birds similar appearing, Blue-winged Warbler primary song is short version of GWWA.
