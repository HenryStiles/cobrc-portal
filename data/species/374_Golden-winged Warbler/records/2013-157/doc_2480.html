Description of the Bird

Somewhat stout warbler. Grayish on flanks, whiter mid-belly and chest. Black throat and black on auriculars. Throat and auriculars separated by broad white stripe. Fore and mid-crown yellow, separated from auriculars by white stripe. Yellow of mid-crown fading into gray hindcrown. Back and nape gray. Wings gray with solid yellow panel formed by median and greater wing coverts. Bill black
Behavior: Feeding in trees from mid to upper levels
Call: series of three thin, high-pitched buzzes
Plumage: male
