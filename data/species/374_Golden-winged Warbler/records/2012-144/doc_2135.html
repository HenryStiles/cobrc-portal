Description of the Bird

Warbler by bill and size of bird. Black bib, and black through eye, black lore with black extending onto auricular. Broad white separating black auricular and black bib. Lemon yellow crown. Unstreaked gray back. Gray tail and upper tail coverts. Bright yellow and broad wingbars. Upper breast just below the black bib whitish but with some light yellow feathers in it almost identical to the photo of GWWA on page 591 of The Stokes Field Guide to the Birds of North America, from May in Texas. Also some yellow feathers on the gray back.
Behaviors: Foraged as low as on the ground, in the sand sage at two feet off the ground and in the Russian olives and eventually was caught in a mist net and banded..
Call: mostly sang four high buzz song but had another song it sang too.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

No other warbler looks like this.

Memory

Additional Information

Comments: Because of the yellow breast and back feathers I thought this was a good example of introgression with BWWA. Tony Leukering sent my photographs to Michael O'Brien who said that is what GWWAs look like now in the east. It was called a GWWA by Nancy Gobris in here banding report to the Bird Banding Lab. There is a bird that looks a lot like it in the photos for GWWA in the Stokes Bird Guide. There has to be some BWWA introgression in its genotype and at least some in its phenotype but I think it should probably still be called a Golden-winged Warbler since F1 hybrids are distinct in their plumages and names.
