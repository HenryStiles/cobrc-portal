Description of the Bird

I was birding with Mark Peterson, Kara Carragher, and Dan Maynard, when Peterson spotted a Golden-winged Warbler. The bird was first in a juniper tree in plain view. I could see a black throat, grayish white under parts, black ear patch, golden yellow crown, gray nape, gray wings with a yellow/golden thick wing bar on each wing. The bird also had dark eyes, and grayish colored tail. The bill was thin and pointed, like all warblers. There was no hint of yellow underneath, so I believe this was a normal looking male Golden-winged Warbler. The bird was near Yellow-rumped Warblers at one point, and was a little smaller. The overall shape and size were typical for warblers.
Behavior: Staying low in the juniper tree, and then in a golden current.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing looks like a male Golden-winged Warbler. I don't feel like the bird had any noticeable signs of being a Blue-winged x Golden-winged Warbler hybrid, since there was no yellow color on the underparts.
