Habitat

Along the rocks and banks of running discharge water from a water treatment plant. Heavily wooded with cottonwoods.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski EL 8 X 42 binoculars and Leica Televid 77 APO scope with 20-60X zoom.
Distance:  Variable.
Light:  Initially seen and photographed late in the day, fairy dark conditions in snowy weather. Observed other dates in good light under clear skies.

Description of the Bird

Medium-sized bird with olive-brown back, streaked throat and breast. Slight yellowish tinge to breast and lower belly. Broad buffy supercilium, in this bird, widening behind eye. Post-ocular streaks and malar stripes. Darkish upper mandible, lighter, dusky lower mandible. Dark eye. Pinkish legs.
Behaviors: Usually was walking while foraging along the bank, constantly bobbing and swaying tail and body. Flew on several occasions when flushed.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was separated from Louisiana Waterthrush by its streaked throat (LOWA should have a clean throat,) buffy supercilium (LOWA should be white) yellowish tinge to chest and belly (LOWA should be white) and no contrast between flank color and rest of underparts. Darker, and more sharply defined streaks on chest than would be expected with LOWA. Smaller bill than LOWA.
