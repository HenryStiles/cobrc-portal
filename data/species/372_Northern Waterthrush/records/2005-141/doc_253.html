Description of the Bird

Small passerine foraging along a stream. Solid olive-brown upperparts; head had a thin, even width, buffy supercilium, olive-brown eyestripe, and splotchy cheek patch. Underparts were basally buffy-white, with bold distinct olive-brown streaks extending from chin to belly and along the flanks. Bill was short, pointed, and dark. Legs were dark with a bit of a fleshy-pink tone.
Behaviors: Observed foraging along a stream coming out of a wastewater treatment plant. Bird occasionally would teeter its tail, not its entire body like a Spotted Sandpiper. Mostly stayed along the water's edge, but did fly up onto the bank to forage around exposed tree roots on two occasions.
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Louisiana Waterthrush only other candidate is ruled out by this bird having an overall buffy wash to underparts, narrow and buffy supercilium, smallish bill, darkish legs, and teetering confined to tail rather than involving the body.
