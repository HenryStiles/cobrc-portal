Description of the Bird

Viewing Conditions: full-sun, at feeder, distance ~ 20 ft through 8 and 10 power binocs for 5-10 minutes and then 20 minutes when it returned.

First Impression: What just flew by my window with the distinct white wing patches?

Size: in yard with HOFIs and AMROs, size distinctly in between.

Bill: Distinctly grosbeak-large. Not bi-colored. Seen in excellent light in full sun at feeder for ~ 25-30 minutes. Looked at bill color as bird turned both profiles into view. Also seen briefly through a window at about 3 feet.

Breast: Pale pink just below neck.

Underwing coverts: distinctly light pink, no yellowish tinge. Saw the bird depart for flight through the bincoculars several times-only pink.

Head: Broad white stripe just above/at eye.
Behaviors: Eating black oil sunflower and safflower seeds from two feeders. In company of other seed eaters (HOFIs).

In flight: Conspicuous white wing patches drew immediate attention.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 1st winter male

Similar Species Discussion

streaked head and position of wing patches immediately ruled out Pine and Evening grosbeaks on the initial fly-by.

Pink on the breast and underwing coverts was noticed immediately.

I consulted Sibley's and noted the bicolored bill of the black-headed grosbeak and watched carefully for that feature when the bird returned. I saw the bird swivel its head in both directions so that I saw it in bright light and then good light but shaded by its own body. Bill was uniformly pale, not bicolored.
