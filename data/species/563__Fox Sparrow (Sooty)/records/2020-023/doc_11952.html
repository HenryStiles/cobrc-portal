Description of the Bird

I am documenting this bird because it was a good Sooty Fox Sparrow and this species may be split in the future. 

 

The Fox Sparrow was foraging in the leaf litter. Scratching and digging.

Head mostly brown with slight gray supercilium. Auriculars streaked with brown and gray-brown nape. Uniform brownish plumage with solid brownish back & with densely spotted breast. Dark flanks and undertail-coverts.Reddish on the wings and upper tail coverts and tail. Underparts were heavily marked with brown, quite dense and brown on the breast. Wings solid brown. No gray in the face. Area around the left eye damaged.

R/O Red Fox Sparrow: Upperparts not streaked rufous, no white tertial edges, no white wing bars.

R/O Slate-colored Fox Sparrow: Brown face not rusty or gray, no patterning of dark brown streaks on back.

R/O Thick-billed Fox Sparrow: Not thick-billed! Head brown, back brown not slate.

R/O altivagans: Face not rusty & gray. Very dense brown chest and belly markings, not rusty. No rusty and/or gray striping on the back, no wing bars and pale tan rather than gray rump.

I posted photos pf this bird to the FB Fox Sparrow Group (as did Christian Nunes). Steve Hampton commented as follows: “This looks like a standard northern Sooty to me, probably sinuosa (in the unalaschcensis group) with a gray wash to the nape.”
