Description of the Bird

the bird was a very large gull, about twice the size of adjacent Ring-billed Gulls. The overall shape was also bulkier than that of Ring-billed Gull, with a heftier body, thicker neck, relatively larger head and thicker bill, and relatively shorter wings. The bill was quite thick relative to it's length (for a gull), yellow in color, with a red spot on the strong gonydeal angle. The eye was relatively small for a gull, and pale colored. I was not close enough to see the orbital ring color.

The head, neck, and underparts were all clean white. The mantle and wings were very dark slate gray, nearly black. The wing tips were black. The bird had a thin white crescent between the mantle and wing tips. The legs were pale pink, and the tail was completely white.
Behaviors: The bird was observed loafing the ice with a flock of Ring-billed and Herring Gulls. Towards the end of the observation it flew to the south, but I was not really watching it as I was occupied with a different gull.
Call: none heard
Plumage: definitive basic

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of gull were ruled out by the very large size and very dark mantle. Lesser Black-backed Gull was ruled out by the darker mantle color, pale pink legs, larger bill, chunkier shape and relatively shorter wing tips, and unstreaked head. Slaty-backed Gull was ruled out by the larger size, chunkier shape, relatively shorter wing tips, pale pink legs, and unstreaked head. Western Gull was ruled out by the darker mantle, larger size, and chunkier shape. Kelp Gull was ruled out by the pale pink legs, larger size, chunkier shape, and relatively shorter wings.
