Marina at the mouth of Bogg's Creek on Pueblo Reservoir. Open water surrounded by a mix of short grass prairie and limestone bluffs with occasional stands of pinyon & juniper.

Optics:  65mm Swarovsky spotting scope.

Description of the Bird

See photo at http://www.flickr.com/photos/pauljhurtado/6411953279/in/photostream/

The gull was very large relative to nearby Herring and Ring-billed gulls, much too large for other dark backed gulls that could occur in Colorado including rarities like Western and Slaty-backed Gulls. Very dark mantle (almost as black as the primaries, which were tipped white), pink legs, heavy yellow bill with a small bit of red and black near the tip as is typical for the species. Head appeared clean white. Size, mantle color, leg color rule out other dark mantled gull species that have occured in or near Colorado including Kelp, Western and Slaty-backed Gull.
Behaviors: Resting on the tires just out from the marina.
Call: none
Plumage: winter adult

Similar Species Discussion

Kelp Gull (ruled out by pink legs);
Slaty-backed Gull (mantle too dark, too large, clean white head);
Western Gull (bill and head shape wrong, mantle too dark, too large when compared to nearby Herring Gulls);
Lesser Black-backed by mantle color, size and leg color.

Resources Used

Extensive experience observing this species in Colorado, New York (finger lakes region) and Ohio over the past decade. Previously I've used Olson & Larson, Howell & Dunn, and many of the more general field guides when identifying this species, as well as information gleaned from the video "The Large Gulls of North America".

Previous Experience

I've seen Western Gulls in California on multiple occasions, most recently in the summer of 2011. I've seen 2 Slaty-backed Gulls (adults) in NY -- one at Niagara and one in Ithaca. No experience with Kelp gull.
