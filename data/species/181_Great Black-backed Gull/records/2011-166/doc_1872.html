Description of the Bird

At about 7:30 AM, Christian Nunes and I were looking at gulls on floating tires at the south marina of Pueblo Reservoir. Christian found the Great Black-backed Gull and put me on it. The bird was about 150 yards away, with sunlight directly to our backs. It was a monstrously large gull in comparison with the nearby Herring Gulls. Its back and wings looked jet-black. Head, neck, underparts, and tail appeared all white. Legs were bright pink. I was unable to discern the eye color with certainty, but it was not Herring Gull white. The folded wingtips appeared concolorous with the mantle, but had several small white spots near/at the tips of the primaries. The bill was quite large, with a deep gonydeal angle. It was yellow with a red spot near the tip. We watched the bird for a minute or two, and the briefly a number of times thereafter for about 30 minutes. It was not seen in flight.

Similar Species Discussion

Pink legs eliminate Kelp Gull and Yellow-footed Gull. Western Gull and LBB Gull not as dark. LBB Gull has yellow legs and would be rather smaller. There were LBBG nearby for comparison
