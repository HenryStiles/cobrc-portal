Description of the Bird

the bird was a very large gull, about twice as large as nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The overall shape was quite heavy compared to that of Ring-billed Gull, with a relatively thick neck, heavy chested look, short wings, and large bill all contributing to this. The bill was fairly large and very thick. The gonydeal angle was quite strong. The color of the bill was all yellow except for a red spot on the gonys, and a black spot between the gonys and bill tip on the lower mandible. The eye was pale, and the orbital ring was red. The legs were a pale pink color.

The head of the bird was a clean white, as were the underparts. The back was a very dark gray, almost black. The bird had a thin white tertial crescent. The wingtips were black, with medium sized white spots on the tips of the primaries. The tail was a clean white.
Behaviors: The bird was loafing on the edge of the reservoir with other gulls, mostly Ring-billed Gulls.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Almost all other species of gull were ruled out by the large size, in combination with the very dark back, pale pink legs, and heavy set appearance.

Lesser Black-backed Gull was ruled out by the larger size, clean white head, pinkish legs, shorter wings creating a bulkier shape, darker shade of gray on the back, and larger bill.

Kelp Gull was ruled out by the paler gray on the back, not a inky black, the pale pink legs rather than yellowish legs, larger size, and shorter wingtips.

Yellow-footed Gull was ruled out by darker gray back, bulkier look, larger size, pink legs, and red orbital ring.

Slaty-backed Gull was ruled out by less pink legs, unstreaked head, and darker gray back.
