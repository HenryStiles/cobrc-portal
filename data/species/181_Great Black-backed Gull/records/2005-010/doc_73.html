Description of the Bird

A very large gull with quite a dark mantle and pink legs. The bird was bulkier and stood taller than all neighboring Herring Gulls (HERG) and was quite a bit larger (and darker-mantled) than a Lesser Black-backed Gull (LBBG) about 30 feet away. The head was whitish with a bit of brown streaking, but at distance, the bird just looked white-headed. The lower sides of the white neck had more and darker streaking and except for a few brown streaks on the sides the remainder of the underparts were unmarked white.

The back feathers, scapulars, and upperwing coverts were a mix of juvenile-like (dark brown to blackish with white fringes) and adult-like feathers (blackish without pale fringes). The back may have been comprised of as much as 50% adult-like feathers but the upper wings had only about 10-15% adult-like plumage. The rump was whitish, though I didn't see it well enough to determine whether there was streaking present or not.

The tail was white-based with a distinct blackish terminal tail band and at least one thin, subsidiary tail band proximal to the terminal tail band. The primaries were quite dark and, apparently, lacked adult-like features, such as white tips and mirrors. In flight, the remiges contrasted darker relative to the coverts, despite some blackish coverts.

The bill was substantial, being thicker (absolutely, not necessarily relatively) than those on the neighboring HERGs and was yellow-based with the distal half or so being black, but with no clear-cut separation of color as in young Glaucous Gulls. The legs were pink and much duller in color than those of the neighboring adult HERGs. The eyes, which appeared small relative to the eyes on the nearby HERGs, were obviously in the middle of changing from dark to yellow, but closer to dark.
Behaviors: Foraging with other gulls by chasing Common Mergansers surfacing with fish; loafing with other gulls on ice shelf on west side of reservoir.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Basic II

Similar Species Discussion

Most other gulls easily eliminated by mantle coloration and size; graellsii LBBG ruled out by size, mantle color, bill size, shape, and color; Slaty-backed Gull (SBGU) ruled out by size, upperparts pattern, and leg color; Kelp Gull (KEGU) eliminated by size, leg color, upperwing pattern
