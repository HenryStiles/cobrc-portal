Description of the Bird

A very large gull, dwarfing the Herring Gulls (Larus argentatus) that it was standing beside. This bird had a massive bi-colored bill, mostly pink with the distal 40% black; the head and underparts were white with dark flecking on the head (especially around the eye), nape, and along the flanks; the eye was a dark yellow; wings and mantle were a mixture of newly molted slate-gray and worn mottled brown/white older feathers; wingtips were black with a very thin white border to the primary tips (e.g., no mirrors) and extended well past the tail with at least three primaries beyond the tail tip; the tail was mostly black; the legs were bright pink.
Behaviors: Mostly stood on the ice along with the other gulls.
Call: none heard
Plumage: second winter

Similar Species Discussion

The only dark-mantled gull considerably larger than Herring Gull is Yellow-footed Gull (Larus livens), which is a three-year gull. In second-winter plumage, Yellow-footed is adult-like and would have yellow legs.
