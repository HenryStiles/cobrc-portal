Description of the Bird

A very large gull, larger than all others gulls nearby (including Herring Gulls (HERG)), the bird's head was white with just a suggestion of brown head streaking. The eyes were small and pale. The bill was large, being thick-based and fairly long, larger in both of these dimensions than were the bills of the nearby HERGs; it was yellow with a red gonydeal spot.

The neck and underparts were white (at least what we could see of them), the back and upper side of the wings were blackish, contrasting only slightly with the black wingtips. I could not see the tail as the bird was standing on the ice mostly facing us, but somewhat to our left.

When it stretched its left wing once, I could see the wingtip pattern: the white tip to p10 was very extensive, extending well up into the area on the feather that most large gulls sport a mirror. There was a sizable mirror on p9, and vague whitish tongues on pp5-7.
Behaviors: Standing on ice with Ring-billed Gulls and HERGs.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Only Kelp Gull (KEGU) and darker-mantled forms of Lesser Black-backed Gull (LBBG) are as dark-mantled as was our bird. Since I could not see the legs of our bird, separation from KEGU is based solely on the slight contrast from wingtip to wing coverts and the precise wingtip pattern of our bird so distinctive of Great Black-backed (GBBG). All LBBGs would be smaller than or of about the same size as smithsonianus HERGs, not noticeably larger, as was our bird. Additionally, our bird's wingtip pattern is even more different from that of LBBG than it is from KEGU.
