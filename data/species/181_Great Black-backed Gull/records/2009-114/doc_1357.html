Description of the Bird

First, my trip report, including a brief written description of the species, can be found here: http://tiny.cc/OXM2U

This was an easy ID. I first saw the bird in a group of 46 Herring Gulls, 1 adult Lesser Black-backed Gull, and 1 Thayer's Gull. The bird was substantially larger than the nearby Herring Gulls and even larger than the Lesser Black-backed Gull--which it was standing next to.

The bird had a very large black bill and pale pinkish legs. Its upperparts showed the characteristic and distinctive pattern of coarse white speckling on the dark mantle. The rest of the bird was off-white, somewhat paler on the head. Although there are average differences in size between the sexes, I am reluctant to try to sex this bird. Anyhow, it was a very large gull, notably larger than nearby Herring Gulls, with plumage that was perfectly consistent with a first-cycle Great Black-backed Gull.
Behaviors: The bird must have flown around a bit, as I saw a first-cycle Great Black-backed Gull at various locations on the ice at Valmont Reservoir. It is possible, I believe, that there were two first-cycle Great Black-backed Gulls out there; see my comments on the trip report referenced above. However, this submission refers only to the bird I saw initially and closest.
Call: not heard
Plumage: first-cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Discussed above. Briefly: Substantially larger than nearby Herring Gulls. Glaucous Gull, of similar heft, is much paler at the same age, being substantially white all over. The Great Black-backed had an almost dark bill, dark primary tips, and medium-length wings; Glaucous would have have a distinctively pale-based bill, with shorter wings having all-white primary tips
