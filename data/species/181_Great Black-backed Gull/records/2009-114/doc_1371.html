Description of the Bird

Very large, dark immature gull. Nearly a head taller than nearby Herring Gulls. Bill black and heavy. Head whitish, contrasted strongly with blackish mantle. Overall lack of brown tones, mantle dark blackish-gray with very clean checkering of white. Blackish primaries, relatively short primary projection. Pink legs.
Behaviors: Roosting with a mixed flock of gulls on ice.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 1st Winter

Similar Species Discussion

Large size, heaviness of bill, white head contrasting with black body tones and clean white body checkering eliminate other candidate 1st-cycle gulls.
