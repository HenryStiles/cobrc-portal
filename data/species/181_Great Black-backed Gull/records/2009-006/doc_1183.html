Habitat

McIntosh had a mix of open pools and ice cover. The bird was observed in the water and on the ice.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  80mm Pentax scope with 20-60x eyepiece
Distance:  100 yds estimate
Light:  Alternating sun and clouds. Sun at back.

Description of the Bird

Large, slaty black mantle and upper wing, head neck and under parts bright white. Flattish head and long heavy bill with bright red gonydeal spot. No noticable streaking on head or neck. Legs grayish. Eye color indiscernable.

Size: Larger than any neaby Herring Gull. Taller and heavier looking than Herring. Top of Ring-billed Gull's head even with top of back on this bird.

Color: Blackish upper parts but not as black as primaries.

Legs appeared gray when standing next to Herring Gull, whose legs were noticably pinkish, and next to Ring-billed Gull with obvious yellow legs.
Behaviors: nothing noted
Call: not noted
Plumage: Adult non-breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Lesser Black-backed Gull should appear a little smaller than Herring and have yellow legs and feet. This bird was larger than Herring with grayish looking legs and in the submitted pictures has pink feet.

Resources Used

At the time of sighting: Big Sibley and Peterson Field Guide to Birds of North America.

Later: Gulls of the Americas by Howell and Dunn.

Previous Experience

Thiis is my first experience with this species and I have not yet observed Lesser Black-backed.
