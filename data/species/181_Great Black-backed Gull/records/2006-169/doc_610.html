Description of the Bird

A very large gull, standing obviously taller than all other gulls nearby (California (CAGU), Lesser Black-backed (LBBG), Thayer's (THGU), and Herring (HERG)). The head was white with just the faintest vague gray-brown smudging on the crown. The bill was yellow with an obvious reddish gonydeal spot. The back and wing coverts were nearly black and contrasted only slightly with the black outer primaries.

The rump and tail were unmarked white, as were the underparts. The eyes were small and yellow and the legs were pink, with the tarsi having a gray cast to them.

All outer primaries were tipped strongly with white and the tip on p10 merged with the mirror on that feather to create an extensive white tip.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of my observation time standing on ice.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

All other similar species (Kelp, LBBG, various other dark-mantled gulls) can be eliminated by various of leg color, bill size and shape, wingtip pattern, upperparts coloration, size.
