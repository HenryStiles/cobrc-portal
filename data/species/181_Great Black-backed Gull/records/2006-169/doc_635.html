Description of the Bird

Very large gull (size of Glaucous Gull) with dark gray (almost black) mantle, black primaries with white tips, and white mirrors on outer two primaries (mirror almost at terminus of outer primary), broad white tips to secondaries and tertials; white tail and underparts; mostly white head with a few grayish streaks on nape; very heavy yellow bill with red and blackish gonydeal spot. Pale pinkish legs and feet.
Behaviors: Lounging with other gulls
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Pink legs rules out Yellow-footed, Slaty-backed and Kelp Gulls. Large size and blackish shade of mantle rule out Western Gull. Large size rules out Lesser Black-backed Gull.
