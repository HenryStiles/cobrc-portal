Description of the Bird

While scanning Erie Reservoir on January 3, 2010, I noticed a large gull towering over the Ring-billed Gulls that made up the bulk of the birds standing on the ice.

[Photo at http://www.flickr.com/photos/rudi_noodle/4243156220/ - Viewing conditions were heavily overcast and the quality of the photo reflects that.]

The large gull appeared to be about twice the size of the surrounding Ring-billeds. The bill on the large gull was large, thick and completely black. The head was generally white, with a dark shadow surrounding the eye, and a mich light shadow extending behind and above the eye. the back and coverts were dark grey with a checkered black appearance. Breast appeared whitish, with some light streaking toward the flanks. Primary tips on the folded wing appeared completely black with no white "mirrors." Legs were light pink.
Behaviors: This particular gull was not active at all during the time I viewed it.
Call: did not hear
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Let me preface this by saying that my gull identification skills are ... developing.

1st winter Herring Gull would be smaller and would not compare so favorably in size to the Ring-billeds. Also, the breast should have been darker and the bill thinner/lighter at the base. 1st winter Vega (unlikely, but considered) would be closer in plumage than the American Herring, but, again the bill did not fit. Thayer's , Iceland, Glaucous, Glaucous-winged would all be much lighter (and smaller in general). California, Western and Slaty-backed would all be much darker (and smaller in general).

The closest would be Lesser Black-backed, but the size differential between the gull in question and the surounding Ring-billeds eliminates LBBG. This left me with GBBG.
