Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x42 binoculars, 100-400mm lens on Canon Rebel XSI digital camera.
Distance:  couple hundred feet at closest.
Light:  Great - sun behind me, clear, cloudless sky.

Description of the Bird

Large, black-backed gull, mirrors on P9,P10, with the P10 mirror merged with the white tip to the primary. Eyes appearing pale brownish, heavy bill with prominent red spot on the gonys with some distal black smudging in the spot. Faint streaking around the eyes and back towards the nape. Pink legs, etc. See included photos.
Behaviors: Seen in flight, then circling the tires at the south shore marina at Pueblo Reservoir. Twice (out of 4+ attempts) it went after Ring-billed gulls, tackling them into the water and biting at their bodies and shoulders for a few seconds until giving up and letting them free. This seemed to me to look more like predatory behavior than kleptoparasitism.
Call: No vocalizations made
Plumage: Adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Kelp Gull ruled out be leg color, streaking on the head, large size, large white tips to the primaries. No other possible species.

Resources Used

Personal experience w/ the species in NY state, and Pueblo Reservoir is past years, and Sibley. Also web resources including "All About Birds" (www.cornell.edu/AllAboutBirds/) and the "Gull Identification Website" (http://www.geocities.com/rainforest/canopy/6181/gulls.htm) in ruling out Kelp Gull.

Previous Experience

Lots of experience with GBBG in Ithaca, NY during the past 6 years, the Pueblo Reservoir birds even longer. No experience w/ Kelp Gull.
