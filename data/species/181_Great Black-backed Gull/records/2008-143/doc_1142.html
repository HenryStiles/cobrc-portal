Description of the Bird

A very large gull, the bird was on the tires with about 1000 other gulls and dwarfed all of them, including, even, the Herring Gulls. It's head was white with a very small amount of vague brown streaks. The eyes were small and yellowish. The bill was very large (thick and long) and yellow and sported a red gonydeal spot.

The neck, underparts, tail, and rump were white. The legs were pale pink. The mantle and the upper side to the wings were blackish, contrasting just a bit with the black wingtips. The scapular and tertial crescents were medium-sized. The p10 mirror was conbined with the white tip to that feather to create a very long white tip. There was a sizable p9 mirror and vague whitish tongues on pp5-7.
Behaviors: Lying and standing on tires; flying
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Only Kelp Gull (KEGU) and darker-mantled forms of Lesser Black-backed Gull (LBBG) would be anywhere near as dark as this bird. Both are ruled out by the precise wingtip pattern, leg color, and proportions.
