Description of the Bird

Very large, dark-backed gull. The bird was larger than the Glaucous (Larus hyperboreus) and Herring (L. argentatus) gulls that were standing immediately next to it. The underparts were entirely bright white. The head was also white with faint streaking near the eyes and on the crown. The wings and back were slate-gray. The eye was yellow with a red orbital ring. The stout bill was yellow with a large red gonydeal spot. Its legs were a dull pink.
Behaviors: Standing on ice in the middle of a protected bay and eating a fish. Successfully defended its food from the other gulls.
Call: The bird gave a long-call several times, but I can't describe it.
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

The large size, very dark back, and pink legs eliminate other species.
