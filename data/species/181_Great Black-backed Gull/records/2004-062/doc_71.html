Description of the Bird

Very large, dark-backed gull. Wings and mantle were a very dark charcoal gray intermixed with brown first-year feathers; tertials had broad white tips; primary tips appeared all dark; underparts were mostly white except showing some dark streaking on the sides and chest; head was also mostly white except for dark streaking on the hindneck and around eyes; the bill was pinkish-yellow with a distinctly demarcated black tip. This individual was larger than Herring Gulls in close proximity.
Behaviors: It rested on the water the entire time.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: second winter

Similar Species Discussion

No other gull species approaches the size and dark charcoal gray mantle coloration exhibited by this bird.

Resources Used

Gulls of North America, Europe, and Asia (Olsen and Larsson)

Previous Experience

I have seen dozens of this species on the upper east coast, as well as the individual, "Murray", each year in Pueblo since 1999.
