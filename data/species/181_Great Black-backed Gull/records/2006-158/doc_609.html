Description of the Bird

A very large gull with nearly black mantle and wings. The head was white with indistinct gray-brown smudging on the crown. The yellow eyes appeard small. The bill was large and yellow with a distinct red spot on the gonys, which sported a fair angle.

The back and (folded) wings were all the same very dark gray (nearly black), except for the black outer primaries. There were neat and distinct white scapular and tertial crescents. The underparts were entirely white; the legs were a very dull, pale, grayish-pink. The tail was white.

In flight, the upper side of the wings looked nearly as black as the outer primaries (which were black). There was a medium-width white trailing edge. The outer three primaries were almost or wholly black except for the dark gray base to p9, the sharp white tips to p8-9, the relatively large white mirror on p9, and the connected white mirror and tip of p10.
Behaviors: The bird was loafing in the water, on a small island, and on tires for most of the time of observation. I saw it fly a few times.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Kelp Gull is blacker above and has yellow legs. Darker forms of Lesser Black-backed Gulls are smaller, longer-winged, yellow-legged, and with a different wingtip pattern. Other dark-mantled gulls are ruled out, variously, by leg color, wingtip pattern, size (this bird being larger than all), and head pattern.
