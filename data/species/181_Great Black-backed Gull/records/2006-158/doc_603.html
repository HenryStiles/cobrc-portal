Description of the Bird

This is still a rare bird in CO. Just to document its prescence at Lake Pueblo State Park for another winter.

Huge dark mantled gull. Undersides gleaming white. Head white with a few black streaks. Neck white with a few black streaks. Flat crown. Heavy bill with reddish gonys spot. Red orbital ring. Dark eye. Legs and feet pinkish. Mantle gray black with wings slightly more black. Trail edge to wings broadly white. Extensive white wing tips on p9 and p10.

Still
Behaviors: Seen mostly loafing bathing.
Call: none
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Kelp Gull - has greenish legs and narrow white mirror on p10 unlike Great Black-backed Gull and mantle is darker.

nominate Lesser Black-backed Gull - same mantle color or a little dark but longer winged and significantly smaller with proportionately shorter legs which are yellow and not pink. Eye is yellow and not dark.
