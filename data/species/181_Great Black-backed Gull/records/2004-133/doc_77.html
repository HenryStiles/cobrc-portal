Description of the Bird

Large, heavy billed, bulky gull. From a distance appears dark winged, pale headed. Upon closer inspection the bill reveals a small almost white tip followed by a clearly defined dark third or so and the remaining two thirds of the bill is pink. Head mostly white, with slight dark smudging behind the eye. The scapulars are mottled gray and black. The remaining wing feathers are mostly checkered dark grayish brown with white highlights, a white tertial crescent, and black primaries. The bird does not appear especially long winged. The bird was noticeably larger than nearby Herring Gulls.
Behaviors: The bird was associating with other gulls that were harassing a raft of Common Mergansers.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 2nd basic

Similar Species Discussion

Few other gulls are this large. Herring Gull ruled out by smaller size, warmer brown tones, etc. Although large, Glaucous Gull never this dark. Although similarly dark, Lesser Black-backed Gull is significantly smaller and has more uniformly dark gray scapulars at this age.
