Description of the Bird

Very large gull with white head and body and tail; Faint brown streaks on crown and nape. Dark gray back and wings except for white scapular crescent, white tertail crescent, and black primaries with small white tips. The outer primary had a larger terminal white "mirror". Legs and feet pale pink (although perhaps brighter pink than most GBBG). Eye (iris) ochre, with red orbital ring. Bill yellow with subterminal dark smudge, with some red on lower mandible. Gonydeal angle pronounced, with a thicker gonys than expected for GBBG.
Behaviors: Loafing on ice, swimming in water, bathing, stealing food from accompanying gulls, flying.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Sub adult probably 4th cycle

Similar Species Discussion

All other gull species are smaller. Some other large, black-backed Gulls in essentially adult plumage can be ruled out by leg color: Yellow-footed Gull, Lesser Black-backed Gull have yellow legs; Kelp Gull has greenish legs; Slaty-backed Gull has bright pink legs. Western Gull also has pale pink legs but has yellow orbital ring (not red) and paler gray mantle, and black terminus on outer primary.
