Description of the Bird

Almost immediately upon arriving at Rist Benson Reservoir, I picked the Great Black-backed Gull swimming. Soon thereafter, the bird swam to a pod of other gulls along the shore. Compared to nearby Ring-billed and Herring Gulls, the bird was noticeably larger in all aspects with a bulky body, thick neck, and very heavy bill. The upperparts were almost jet-black, though were a slight shade lighter than the primaries. The bill was mostly yellow, except for a red gonys and a small black edge to that gonys. The legs were a pale fleshy pink. The head, neck, breast, and underparts were clean white. The tertial and scapular crescents were white and the black primaries displayed large white mirrors.
Behaviors: Noted swimming and foraging along shore.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definitive alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Lesser Black-backed Gull is smaller and slimmer in all aspects, has longer primary projection, has a lighter shaded mantle, and has yellow legs. Western Gull is slightly smaller as well with a thicker bill. Yellow-footed Gull is smaller with yellow legs. Kelp Gull is also smaller with greenish-gray legs. Slaty-backed Gull is also smaller with wider tertial and scapular crescents.
