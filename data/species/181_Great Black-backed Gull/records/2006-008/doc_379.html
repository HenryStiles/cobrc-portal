Habitat

iced-over moderate-sized reservoir with patches of open water, in urban setting. US-34 and businesses to the south, and residential homes to north, east, and west.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Kowa TSN-824 spotting scope with 20-60x zoom eyepiece.
Distance:  250 ft (estimated)
Light:  early morning under clear skies, and excellent light. Bird was positioned due north of my observing spot, with sun relatively low in sky to the southeast.

Description of the Bird

A very large, dark-mantled gull.

Bird clearly as large or slightly larger than any other Herring Gull in the vicinity, and much larger than every Ring-billed Gull. Mantle color dark slaty gray, in sharp contrast with mantle color of every other gull in the area. Very clean white underparts and head, and only minimal smudginess on the nape. Bill yellow, with black 'ring' near tip, and small red gonys spot inward of it. Eye color dim yellow. Small white scapular crescent, actually more like a patch, obvious against the mantle plumage. Small white tips on otherwise black primaries when wings folded. Legs light pink when seen standing on ice edge.
Behaviors: Bird mostly swam and foraged in the open water alongside and near other gulls (Ring-billed and Herring) and waterfowl (mostly Common Goldeneye). These 'swim-bys' allowed numerous occasions for size comparisons between birds. Was not particularly aggressive toward other birds around it, even when it appeared they had found edible material.

For short intermittent periods (3-5 minutes) it left the water and stood on the ice, grooming and 'resting'. However, it never stayed out long before resuming its foraging. Mostly stayed in the same open water patch, that is, not exploring a couple sizable neighboring patches.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult winter

Similar Species Discussion

Lesser Black-backed Gull - bird was too large as seen in side-by-side comparisons with neighboring RBGU and HEGU. No head smudging. Also, leg color completely wrong for LBBG.

Yellow-footed Gull - leg color completely wrong for YFGU. Also, black ring around bill rules out adult winter YFGU

Slaty-backed Gull - head and neck of bird was very clean-plumaged, not smudgy like SBGU in winter. Iris color too dingy yellow. Also, black ring around bill would seem to rule it out.

Western Gull - mantle color too dark for WEGU, bird size rather large for WEGU, and existence of black bill ring for adult ruled it out.

Previous Experience

This is my second adult GBBG, the first being 4 years ago near Nantucket, MA. I've also seen first-winter GBBG last winter on Lake Loveland.

I observed adult LBBG on Dec 31, 2005, and on Jan 19, 2006 on same reservoir with Sol Miller.

I've not seen YFGU or SBGU, but I've seen numerous WEGU, having lived on Pacific Coast for 4 years.
