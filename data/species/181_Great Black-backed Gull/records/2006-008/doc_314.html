Description of the Bird

The largest and darkest mantled gull on the pond. The bird was very easy to pick out among the not-quite-as-big and much lighter mantled Herring Gulls it was with and completely dwarfed the Ring-billed Gulls too. This bird obviously had greater mass than even the largest of the nearby Herring Gulls, with a near black mantle and wings, solid white underparts and from what I could tell of the head it also appeared to be pure white (although the distance and lack of optical power would have prevented me from seeing light streaking). Also due to these hindrances, I could not discern wingtip pattern. However, it was not a problem to see the very large yellow bill with red gonydeal spot. I did not note the eye color and, since it was swimming, also missed the leg color.
Behaviors: Mostly swimming on the only open patch of water with Herring and Ring-billed gulls.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The incredible size and dark upperparts rule out most gulls. Slaty-backed, Yellow-footed, and Western gulls can be ruled out by the near black upperparts as compared to the more gray, slate-colored upperparts of those species. Also, none of them compare in size to this bird and would be more similar in size to Herring, which this bird was much larger than.
