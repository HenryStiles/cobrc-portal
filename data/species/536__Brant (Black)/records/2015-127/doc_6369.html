Description of the Bird

This Brant was a small Goose similar in size & shape to nearby Cackling Geese. Head, neck, breast, back, folded wings, eye & bill were solid black except for prominent white collar on the neck. Black flanks were mottled by prominent white feathers. Under & upper tail coverts were solid white, trimmed by small solid black tail. Since bird was swimming about, I did not see belly nor legs/feet. I never got a good straight on frontal view of bird's neck & could not determine if white collar met & circumvented the foreneck, a field mark usually(but not always) present on Black Brants. 

Bird was observed entirely while it swam about Lake Beckwith, Colorado City, SW Pueblo County. I heard no sounds from the bird & did not see it fly.

Similar Species Discussion

Cackling Geese have prominent white cheek patch lacking in this Brant. Even the darkest Cackling Geese are paler breasted with paler secondaries & backs than this Black Brant.

Altantic Brant subspecies(hrota) are also paler in the back & flanks than this Black Brant.

Gray-bellied & intermediate hybrids of Black x Atlantic Brants have grayer paler backs & paler flanks than Black Brants.
