Description of the Bird

Predominatly black in color, with slight white coloring in the shoulders, slight white colar on the upper neck. Black Bill, Black feet, black eyes. I would estimate the bird to be about 5 lbs. in size. The amount of white on the bird identified it as a "western race".
Behaviors: I spotted the bird on March 17, 2005 around 11:30 am. ,on the west side of Cherry Creek Reservoir, south of the marina. It appeared to be grazing on grass. Bird was grazing with 10 Greater Canadian Geese and one Greater White-Fronted Goose. The birds it was with appeared to be migratatory birds rather than the local geese, they were not displaying any early courtship behavior as the locals are. I stayed in my car and used binoculars, the bird came within 75 feet of me. The Brant was very alert to movement and the environment and stayed loosely with the flock.
Call: none given
Plumage: mature

Similar Species Discussion

I had my Golden Birds of North America identifaciton book with me. I noticed the bird immediately as being different from the Canadians, that's what got my attention. It did not have a white face of a barnacle goose, and the amount of white on it seperated it from an eastern race. Really no other choices.
