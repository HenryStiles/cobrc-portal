Description of the Bird

Blackish, medium-sized goose with white flanks, upper- and undertail covers, and neck collar.

The head, eyes, and short bill were entirely black; black extended down the neck onto the upper chest and lower belly, except for a thick white collar vertically striped with black on the upper neck that tapered on both sides to a point on the hind neck; the remaining dark plumage was a shade lighter than the black of the head/neck/underparts described above; the only light colored parts were the white flanks with vertical smudges of blackish extending upwards from the underparts, the pure white rump and vent regions that completely separated the black tail from the rest of the dark body feathers; the legs were entirely black; pale edges to the wing coverts indicate a bird in its first winter.
Behaviors: It calmly ate grass, allowing for close approach.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: first winter nearing full adult
