Description of the Bird

A dark goose smaller than nearby Canada Geese, it had entirely dark plumage on head and neck except for a white horizontal slash and some associated, smaller, vertical slashes, on the upper neck. The upperparts were dark gray-brown. The wings had distinct whitish fringes/tips the secondary coverts.

The underparts were dark, darkest on mid-belly, with an odd-shaped, yet distinctive, patch of white on the sides. The rump and uppertail coverts were bright white and the dark tail was quite short. The lower belly and vent region were also bright white.
Behaviors: Foraging on a golf course next to an industrial park.
Call: None heard
Plumage: First Basic

Similar Species Discussion

This was a Black Brant, easily separable from Atlantic Brant by the dark belly and from the taxon called Gray-bellied Brant by the strong white patch on the sides.

Previous Experience
