Description of the Bird

Small, squat, dark goose associating with larger Canada Geese (Branta canadensis). Head and neck sock, which formed a bib on the upperchest, were solid black, except for the large, black-and-white triangular patches on either side of the neck that connected on the throat but did not on the hindneck. The black neck sock contrasted with the dark brown under- and upperparts. The wing coverts were edged in dull white. The flanks were just beginning to show the large silvery patch shown on adults. Both the under- and uppertail coverts were pure white, completely surrounding the short, black tail. The bill was rather small and entirely black.
Behaviors: Associated with a foraging flock of Canada Geese eating grass on a golf course.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Atlantic Brant (B. b. hrota) would have much lighter underparts that dramatically contrasted with the black neck sock. No other waterfowl species has the combination of black neck, white triangular neck patches, white flanks, and dark belly.
