Description of the Bird

In size, the Brant was noticeably smaller (1/3rd) than adjacent Canada Geese. Overall impressions were of a very stocky, short-necked, stub-nosed, dark goose. The head, neck and upper breast were a dark black except for a white stripe/barring pattern on the throat and upper neck. Mantle and upper-wing coloration was a dark brown. The middle and lower breast was dark brown which separates the western race from the more eastern American race (hrota); hrota birds typically have a whitish breast and belly with numerous brown striations. The flanks of the subject bird did appear noticeably lighter (almost whitish) from that of the dark brown belly and breast. Remiges appeared black and the tail and upper-tail coverts were white.
Behaviors: Seen both dates swimming within a large group of Canada Geese.
Call: None heard
Plumage: 1st cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Except for Canada Goose, no other goose-like bird is similar in appearance to that of a Brant. In general, Canada Geese are larger, possess a white chin-strap and face mark, and have a lighter breast.
