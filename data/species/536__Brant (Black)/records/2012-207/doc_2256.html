Description of the Bird

Observed bird with numerous whitecheek geese making it easy for comparison. Bird was about much smaller that other geese present--maybe 25% smaller. My attention was drawn to it by the overall color. That being very black from tip of beak to the tail section. That contrasted with the almost white undertail and rear of the bird. I then checked by field guide and detgermined it was a Brant. Note the wing bars but an absence of white patch or neck collar thus indicating juvenile. Bird was grazing along with the other geese within the park. This area also contains large lake.
Behaviors: Bird was grazing much like the other geese in the area. Did not seem to be wary and did allow me to walk within 30 to 40 feet. Nothing else of note. No other simular birds noted.
Call: none
