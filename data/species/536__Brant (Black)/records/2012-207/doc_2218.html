Description of the Bird

A goose, distinctly smaller than nearby Canada Geese. From in front, appeared all black, except white collar that was complete across front of throat and almost met behind neck. More specifically, head and neck formed black "stocking", except for white throat collar. Bill black and proportionately small/narrow for a goose. Underparts, viewed from in front, showed blackish upper belly, with virtually no contrast between black chest and upper belly. From side, the dark on belly narrowed as white sides (scalloped with dusky) broadened, causing dark on belly to narrow considerably as it reached legs. Undertail coverts white. Back and wings very dark, as dark or nearly as dark as head/neck. Paled edging on wing coverts not seen, but both distance and my intent to try digiscoping may have caused me to miss this mark. Legs dark.
Behaviors: Feeding on golf course lawn, primarily with Lesser Canada Geese (B.c. parvipes)
Call: not heard
Plumage: likely adult

Similar Species Discussion

None. Bird was of nigricans subspecies, the expected race in CO. Too dark for "Atlantic" or "gray-bellied brant," and too much white on sides for Dark-bellied Brent Goose
