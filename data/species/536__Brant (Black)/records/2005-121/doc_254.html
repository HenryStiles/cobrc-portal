Description of the Bird

Small, dark, stocky goose associating with Cackling and Canada geese. The plumage was mostly blackish, including the chest and belly, except for the silvery-white flanks (with vertical dark striping), bold white triangular patches on sides of neck, and white upper- and undertail coverts. Bill, legs, and eyes were also black. The bird was a little smaller than the other geese in close proximity, which I thought were probably Lesser Canada Goose (C. b. parvipes). In flight, the short, black tail surrounded by white upper- and undertail coverts was obvious.
Behaviors: Mostly sat on ice in association with a large flock of "white-cheeked" geese, occasionally stood, and eventually flew off.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

None. This bird is the darkest Brant I can recall seeing in Colorado. Black underparts rule out B. b. hrota.
