Description of the Bird

It was a small goose. It had an all black head, and neck down to the breast. Also a small black bill. It also had black on the belly and on the edge of the tail. The back also has black on it, but it is not as dark of black as the rest of the bird. The rest of the tail, and upper and undertail coverts are white. It had a small line of white on the side of the bird and it has a small line of white on its neck. The reason I don't believe this is a full adult is becasue there should be more white on the neck and sides of the bird.
Behaviors: The bird was hanging out with Canada and Cackling Geese. It was in the water the whole time swimming around the small lake.
Call: Didn't hear it.
Plumage: Black close to adult

Similar Species Discussion

Pale-bellied Brant
Has more white on the belly and sides than Black Brant. The back is also lighter. The black and brownish colors between the neck and the rest of the body is noticably different.

Greater White-fronted Goose
It has pinkish bill and orange legs. Too much black on the tail. Not enough white on the sides and it is more of a brown goose than black.

Barnacle Goose
Has white on the face. Not enough black on the black, too much balck on the tail, and too much white on the sides.

Canada and Cackling Goose
Has white on the face, not the neck. Brown back and belly, not black. The black on the neck doesn't extend as far down the breast as a Brant does.

Emperor, Blue Snow, and Dark Ross's Geese
These juv. are more blue than black and does not have enough white on it, except Ross's. Juv. Ross's have white coverts. All these geese except juv. have white on the face.
