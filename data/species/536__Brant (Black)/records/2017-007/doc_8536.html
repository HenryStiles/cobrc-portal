Description of the Bird

Originally observed in flight with a small flock of Canada Geese; the Brant was noticeably smaller, blackish overall with a pure white underbelly and undertail coverts, mottled flank, and white necklace.  When found at the golf course, the bird was foraging with Canada Geese and a Greater White-fronted Goose - the Brant was the smallest goose in the flock.  The Brant was essentially tri-colored with black and white predominating, although it had a slightly brownish tinge to the dark charcoal gray breast and non-flight wing feathers that contrasted slightly with the black neck sock. Its primaries were be black contrasting slightly with the coverts and scapulars.  Just below the head on the neck was a white japanese handfan-shaped white patch with internal black stripes.  Its bill was short and entirely black. Black legs.  Its flanks were predominately white with charcoal gray-brownish smudging that gave its flanks a mottled appearance.  The underbelly, undertail coverts, and uppertail coverts were pure white; its ridiculously short tail entirely black. Wing coverts were distinctly tipped in white.  Based on the extensive white necklace, large flank patch, but white-tipped flight feather coverts, I assume this is a second-year bird and not yet a full adult.

Similar Species Discussion

None considered.  This was a straight-forward identification.  Atlantic Brant and Gray-bellied Brant eliminated by darkness of breast and upper belly that constrasted only slightly with the black neck sock.
