Description of the Bird

At 7:00 am, Mary Jane Black and I arrived at the north side of Expo Park. We parked approximately 50 feet from Alameda Avenue, near Lansing Street.

Thirty feet from the parking lot, we stopped walking to observe two groups of geese 50 feet from us. After viewing the gourp on the left, I noticed 2 Canada geese grazing slightly in front of a second group. Then, a third group just beyond the 2 Canada geese came into view. It was dark with a white band around its neck. I excitedly told Mary Jane who looked at it and exclaimed "It is a Brant!"

The goose was slightly smaller than the nearby Canada geese. It had a black neck and breast with a very distinct white band around its neck. Its head was also black with a dark back. It had very prominent whtie upper tail coverts.

The goose continued to graze during the 10 minutes we watched.

Mary Jane had seen many Brant during a trip to Jamaica Bay and in California. I had only seen one during a birding trip to Cherry Creek State Park on March 29, 2005.

We were so close, neither of us thought of the scope in the car! No camera available! Obviously, my birding skills are very limited but I was so excited and eagerly told Ann Bonnell of the sighting when we joined the group at Fairmount Cemetery.
