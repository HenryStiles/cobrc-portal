Description of the Bird

It was feeding on the ground with many Canada Geese.

It was at the small end of the size range in the flock of Canada Geese.

Its back was as dark as the darkest Canada Goose, or perhaps a bit darker. There were prominent white feather edgings on the wings, near where they met the sides of the breast (upper flanks).

The bill was dark, with a paler section near the base (this could have been on the face).

The entire head and neck, down to the upper breast, was solid black, except for a dingy white collar. This white collar was 1" - 2" wide.

The lower breast was blackish, paler than the neck.
Behaviors: feeding on the ground with many Canada Geese
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was of generally comparable size, shape, and behavior to nearby Canada Geese. I can therefore narrow the choice to various species of geese. It was too dark in color for even a dark juvenile Snow or Ross' Goose, and the head pattern was completely wrong for White-fronted Goose. I ruled out Canada Goose by the very dark color, especially on the upper breast, the lack of a white cheek patch, and the whitish collar.
