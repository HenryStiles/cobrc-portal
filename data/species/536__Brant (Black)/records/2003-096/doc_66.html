Description of the Bird

A small, dark goose about the size of a Lesser Canada Goose (B. canadensis parvipes).
The remiges, rectrices, head, neck, and extension from the neck onto the belly were very dark brown-black. The vent region was pure white as were the uppertail coverts. The remainder of the body was a dark brown, with lighter brown on the flanks. Greater coverts were edged in pale brown, the same color as the flanks. On both sides of the neck were obscure white v-shaped patches that connected in front. All soft parts were black.
Behaviors: Swimming with other waterfowl in pond.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: 1st basic

Similar Species Discussion

Eastern hrota is ruled out by the extensive dark underparts, resulting in little contrast between the black neck sock and the upper breast, pale brown edging to greater coverts, and white necklace connecting in front. Of the currently recognized Brant subspecies, only nigricans has a necklace that connects in front.
