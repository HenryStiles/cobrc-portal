Description of the Bird

This bird looked identical to the one observed 11/19/2002 at Walker Pit CBRC 2002-105.  It was smaller than a Common Loon but slightly larger than a Pacific Loon.  The bill was was stouter than a Pacific bill and held above horizontal with a dark tip on the upper mandible.  The top of the head down to the back of the neck was a pearly gray.The eye was just in the gray and it had a bit of a grayish white cheek patch.  From the patch down to the base of the neck was a distinct 50/50 line with gray in the back and white in the front.  A couple remanant stripes from its alternate plumage could just be seen along the line between gray and white. The neck seemed long. The forehead sloped down giving it a "blocky" look.  The back was mostly black with white specks.  The thin white flanks broadened to a distinctive white patch at the rump.

Similar Species Discussion

The most similar species is the Pacific Loon.  This bird had a stouter bill held more above horizontal, and seemed bigger and stockier.  Pacific does not have the distinctive white patch on the flank by the rump.

Previous Experience

I saw a bird that looked exactly like this one two days earlier at Walker Pit.
