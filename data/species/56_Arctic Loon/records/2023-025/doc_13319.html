Description of the Bird

Mark Peterson found this loon.  The gray on the back of head, a long bill, that was some what upturned, though not as much as Red-throated Loon.  The white flank patch on each side was very obvious.  The throat was dark.  The head seemed more blocky and not round. See photos. 

Similar Species Discussion

The gray head elinates Common and Yellow-billed Loon.  Red-throated Loon has thinner more upturned bill.  The bird was clearly either Pacific Loon or Arctic Loon.  The more blocky head, longer bill held slightly up turned and white patches all elimnate Pacific Loon.

Previous Experience

I've seen Arctic Loons in breeding plumage at farther distance then this bird, that was very distintive showing white flank patches, longer slightly up-turned bill near Nome, Alaska.  I saw the non-breeding plumage Arctic Loon in Franktown, Colorado (the first accepted record in Colorado).  I've seen breeding plumaged Pacific and Red-throated Loons mostly in Alaska.
