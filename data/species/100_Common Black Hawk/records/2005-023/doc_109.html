Description of the Bird

Large, chesty, broad-winged, hawk. Flight style and shape recalled Black Vulture. Wings short, reaching only to base of tail (about 1/3 of way to tip). Primary extension short (much shorter than on Zone-tailed Hawk), but fairly long for a Black-Hawk (longer than on Great Black-Hawk). Slaty-black overall, perhaps paler on upperparts than below. Eye dark. Bill dark, with yellow cere and some grayish bare skin in lores. Legs yellow. Tail black with prominent, band about 2" in width, centered on tail. Also, thin, crisp, white terminal band on tail tip. Underwings with some faint barring on rmiges, contrasting with dark wing linings and indistinct darker trailing edge. Ragged bulge to rear of wing indicated molt, which may suggest as third-year (TY) bird. See attached field notes and photos.
Behaviors: Soaring over canyon and perched in willows along stream (twice). On most occasions it soared with Turkey Vultures. See attached field notes and photos.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Zone-tailed Hawk easily eliminated by shape and flight stile, as well as having just two white tail bands. Great Black-Hawk differs in having an additional white band at the base of the tail, barred legging feathers, and more gray in the lores and cere than this bird showed. See photos.
