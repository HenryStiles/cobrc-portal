Description of the Bird

It was a very heavy-bodied hawk, circling lazily well above the tops of the trees.

Its upperparts were solid black, except for a broad white band in the tail.

Its bill was heavy and uniformly light, constrasting strongly with the dark body.

The underparts had a mix of patterns. The wing linings were a solid, dark, blackish color. Most of the rest of the undersurface of the wings was a mottled blackish pattern. Along the leading edge of the wings, most of the way to the outer end, was a small whitish patch on each side.

There was a broad white band in the tail, visible from above and below. Outside this white band was a broader black band, and then a thin terminal white band.
Behaviors: The bird was circling lazily well above the tops of tall cottonwood trees for the entire five minutes that I watched it. I presume that it was gliding on thermals.
Call: It was silent during the time that I observed it.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

I distinguished this bird from other buteos by two things:
1) unique color pattern
2) very heavy-bodied appearance (very different from a Red-tailed Hawk, Ferruginous Hawk, or Rough-legged Hawk)
