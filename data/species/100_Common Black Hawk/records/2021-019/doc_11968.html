Description of the Bird

While walking along the wooded area at Chico Basin, we spotted a large dark bird soaring in the distance. Jacqui (this reporter) first observed the bird through her 10X50 binoculars and noticed destict white banding on the tail of a very dark brid. Steve was able to photograph the bird with his 420mm Camera which led to confusion about what it may have been. After consulting other birders and the Merlin Bird ID application the concencous of Common Black Hawk was established. Once that ID was considered, there is no doubt about what the ID is. 

Similar Species Discussion

Harris's Hawk - Tail banding was not correct, and they are non-migratory 

Zone-Tailed Hawk - Tail banding was not correct

Broad-Winged Haw - Tail banding was not correct and morphology did not match

 

Resources Used

Merlin Bird ID application, Sibley's Bird ID application, and other knowledgeable birders 

Previous Experience

I have identified over 400 speicies in the ABA area. We were able to add a juvinile Common Black Hawk to our life and state list in 2020 which was origionally sighted in a near-by county. Although the mature and juvinile look very different, both were in the same area.
