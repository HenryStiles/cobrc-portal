Description of the Bird

I first took notice of this raptor because it was dark (black) and had the distinctive shape of a buteo as I was driving. The date, June 12th, would be an unusual date for a dark-morph Red-tailed Hawk in Colorado. The very broad wings also caught my attention even before I raised my binoculars for closer inspection. Once I did stop my car and get my binoculars on this bird I again realized that this was not a Red-tailed Hawk because of its shape (very broad wings and the impression of a short tail) and it was entirely black except for a wide white band at the base of its tail. I know that a narrow white terminal band is supposed to be evident on Common Black-Hawks; however, I could not see any white at the end of the tail possibly because of the distance. At one point I did see this hawk soaring with a Red-tailed Hawk and noted that it seemed to be about the same size as the Red-tailed Hawk.
Behaviors: This raptor was soaring the entire time I viewed it. I watched it go approximately one half mile from the middle of Hotchkiss to the west. I did not see it flap during the time I watched it.
Call: no vocalizations heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Zone-tailed Hawk and Dark Morph Red-tailed Hawk: this hawk had very broad wings and one wide white median band on the tail.
Great Black-Hawk: When I saw this hawk soaring with a Red-tailed Hawk it was approximately the same size as the Red-tailed Hawk.
