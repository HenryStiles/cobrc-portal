Description of the Bird

Black buteo with very broad wings. Tail appeared short because of broad wings. Thick white band visible at base of tail and thin white band at the trailing end of tail. The only white I could see on the hawk was in the tail. Hawk caught a thermal and quickly was high in the sky where I watched it soar for approximately 15 min.

Similar Species Discussion

Turkey and Black Vultures have no white in tail. Other dark Buteos do not have same tail pattern and wings on other buteo species are not this broad. 

Resources Used

I have seen Common Black-Hawks very near this location three other times (2009-2010) so I was confident in my identification and did not consult field guides.

Previous Experience

I have seen Common Black-Hawks several times (I would guess between 50-100 observations) in Arizona where I studied Bald Eagles nesting along the Verde and Salt Rivers near Phoenix. I have also seen this species several times in New Mexico (between 5-10 observations). This is my fourth sighting of this species in the Hotchkiss area. I submitted documentation of my first sighting to CBRC and it was accepted (observation was 12 June, 2009).
