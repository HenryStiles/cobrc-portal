Description of the Bird

Very dark overall, could see a slight brownish coloring on the wings when the bird was first found, deep in a grove of elm trees. When the bird flushed and flew directly over us, the overall dark apperance of the bird was noted along with the single white band on the tail, (excluding the narrow white stripe at the end of the tail).
Behaviors: The bird was first found in a thick grove of elm trees. It was perched about 4 feet below the top of the trees. When the bird moved it continued to do so by flying to below tree top into thick parts of trees. Most if not all of our more common resident hawks do not do this.
Call: NA
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion
