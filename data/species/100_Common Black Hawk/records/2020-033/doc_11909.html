Habitat

Hunting below the dam in the stream. It would fly down to hunt birds or other animals in the stream below the dam and then fly up to the trees or dam to observe.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Maven 9x45 B2's. Canon 7dMii w/ 400mm lens and 1.4x extender.
Distance:  20-50 ft.
Light:  Mid-morning light on a sunny day. Bird was lit very nicely from the east while in the trees and on the dam. Most of my observations came from the east side of the bird, so the lighting was perfect.

Description of the Bird

Medium sized hawk with brown tear dropped streaks throughout chest.  Back was primarily brown but had some white and the primary coverts (I think) had a rufous tinge to the edges.  Underside of tail had fine black and white barring.  Thick, dark malar stripe.  Dark patch behind the eye.  Light supercilium.

Similar Species Discussion

My first thought was that it was a Broad-winged hawk (or maybe a Red-shouldered Hawk) while I was observing it.  I wasn't able to make that work though.  I also considered a strange morph juvenile Red-tailed Hawk, but I couldn't make that work either.  I didn't get a positive id until I got home and got some help id'ing my photos.

Resources Used

Facebook Bird Id group was my first source.  Tony Leukering saw me submit this as on a temporary list as a "buteo sp." and quickly corrected me with COBH.  I got confirmation from Mark Peterson and Scott Schaum.  Scott was also the first to confirm the bird in-person.  David Tonneson also confirmed the bird in-person, among others.  The bird is also a really good match for the juvenile COBH in the National Geographic field guide.

Previous Experience

I've been birding in Colorado since 2016. I've seen all the expected Colorado raptors, plus a Zone-tailed Hawk.
