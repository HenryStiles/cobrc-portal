Description of the Bird

I got out of my car at the east end of the Osprey Picnic Area around 945am, when a dark large raptor (red-tailed hawk sized bird), circled above the cottonwood trees, I go the binoculars on it.  I was surprised to see a black raptor with a white band in the middle on a short tail.  The wing shape was more broad, than long like Zone-tailed and Turkey Vultuires, more the shape of Black Vulture.  I quickly realized it was an adult Common Black-Hawk.  The bird was all black overall, and a big raptor, I didn't really get any sort of look at the legs or bill.  I saw it circle over and behind the trees, though it quickly disappeared behind the cottonwood trees and I couldn't get anywhere to see it.  I didn't see the bird very long, less than 30 seconds.  I mainly ruled out other birds, by the color of the bird, tail band, size, and shape of the wings.  I wish I had seen the bird longer and could get photos, though since I've seen this species quite a few times in the south and a couple times in Colorado, I'm confient it was this species.

Similar Species Discussion

Zone-tailed Hawk - would have longer tail, a more bands.
Broad-winged Hawk - dark morph, much much smaller buteo, tail long, not short.
