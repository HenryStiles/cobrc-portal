Description of the Bird

Middle sized Hawk whose plumage was mostly black. The tail was seen well both in flight and when the bird lost its balance when perched. There was a large white band on the upper part of the tail which was very visible both from above and below; there was also a narrow white tail band at the end
of the tail. The legs were bright yellow, as was the cere on the bill. In flight, the wings were broad and round at the tips, the tail was rather short and rounded, and the flight looked slightly labored with slow wingbeats. The plumage of the upper side of the bird looked entirely black. I did not have time to notice the pattern of the underwings.
Behaviors: First seen perching on a dead branch in clear view about 100m away, and then seen flying across a road between the trees on the south side of the Arkansas River on either side of MacKenzie Avenue.
Call: None
Plumage: Summer

Similar Species Discussion

Zone-tailed Hawk. Short, not long, tail: short, broad, not long thin, wings; tail pattern with band at the end of tail; labored flight with slow wingbeats. The plumage does not fit the dark morph of any other buteo. Snail Kite. Wing shape was short and broad; feet and cere were yellow, not orange; tail pattern is different with terminal band.
