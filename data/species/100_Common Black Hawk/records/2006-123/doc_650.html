Habitat

Flowing river with mature cottonwood gallery forest on only one side, the south. North bank in this area was mostly barren, which may be why the bird did not spend much time in this immediate area.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon Fieldscope ED with 20-45x zoom eyepiece, Pentax 8x40 binoculars
Distance:  about 80 meters
Light:  Bird was rather strongly backlit for first sighting. Second sighting was about 90 degrees right of sun, but brief and in-flight.

Description of the Bird

Bird flew up to an exposed perch on the south side of the river, about 80 meters from the bridge. Bird was strongly backlit, but large yellow cere and completely black plumage were still visible. Black eye. The bird was there for about 6 to 8 seconds; got scope views. The bird 'lost its balance' for a second, flapping its wings a bit and flashing us a beautiful view of its tail, with a wide white median band, a black subterminal band of roughly equal width, and a very thin white terminal band. Then the bird took off and flew through the trees towards us, crossing the road directly south of us a few moments later. Got brief 1-2 second binocular views of it in flight as it crossed the road in good light. Completely blackish upperparts/upperwings and large white tail band visible again in flight. Tail quite short for a buteo.
Behaviors: See above.
Call: bird was silent
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Zone-tailed Hawk eliminated by short tail with single wide white band, non-Turkey-Vulture-like flight and behavior. All other buteos ruled out by combination of short tail with single wide white band, yellow cere, black eye, and all-black plumage above and below except for tail.
