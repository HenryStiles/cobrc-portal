Description of the Bird

I was completing a burrowing owl survey and paperwork at the corner of Rd 53 and H when I saw something from the corner of my eye on a wooden post. I glassed it and saw that it was a melanerpes. I observed yellow on nape and back of neck and a small spot of yellow above the base of the bill. I saw no orange or red anywhere on the head and determined it was a golden-fronted woodpecker. Subsequent photos show a black tail, not barred black and white, confirming the ID. The bird was first seen on a 4' high wooden post, acting like a woodpecker. I subsequently flew to a stubble field and bthen to the junipers. It repeated this behavior twice before I left.

Similar Species Discussion

Red-bellied woodpecker was eliminated due to yellow nape with no orange or red anywhere on the bird
