Habitat

It was in the shallow area where the inlet canal reaches the very large reservoir.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski Spotting Scope
Distance:  2800 feet
Light:  We saw it from several different angles. The best lighting was when we were standing directly west of it on a cliff and the sun was directly behind us.

Description of the Bird

An all white bird with long legs and a straight, long, thin heavy bill. It was quite a bit smaller than Great Blue Heron, but still appeared to be a fairly large bird. It had dark greenish gray legs and feet. It had a mostly dark bill. It also had dark lores.
Behaviors: We saw it both just standing around in shallow water and also watched it actively feeding. When it was actively feeding it would mostly be dancing around with its wings held out running around and then thrust at something in the water.
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: White morph Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The white body eliminates most of the herons, everything except Snowy Egret, Cattle Egret, Great Egret, Great White Heron, and juvenile Little Blue Heron. The dark bill eliminates Great White Heron, Cattle Egret, and Great Egret. The dark greenish gray legs eliminates Great Egret, Great White Heron, Snowy Egret, and juvenile Little Blue Heron. The dark lores eliminates Great Egret, Snowy Egret, Cattle Egret, and juvenile Little Blue Heron.

Previous Experience
