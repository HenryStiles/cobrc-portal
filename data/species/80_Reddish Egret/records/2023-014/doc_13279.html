Description of the Bird

Found by Linda Chittum and Ada Jones.

Adult bird first observed from some distance through binoculars and the slate gray coloration of the body immediately had me thinking Little Blue Heron.  I immediately grabbed my spotting scope and saw the rusty head and neck, pale base of the bill and legs that looked dark (grayish black).  The bird was walking in mowed grass at Clifton Sewage Treatment (closed access) and kept heading east and farther away from us.  It took flight heading east and we promptly lost sight of the bird but knew there was wetlands at Clifton Nature Park immediately to the east so we headed over there.

At Clifton Nature Park we began scanning the edge of the pond and Ada relocated the heron near a Snowy Egret and perched on a log at the shoreline.  We got the scope on the bird and confirmed the rust colored head and neck with a slate gray body.  Feathers of the nape and neck were long and not tight to the skin.  The bill was pale pinkish at the base with about the distal third of the bill being black.  The iris was yellow and the facial skin at the lores and around the eye was a pale chalky gray.  The legs were dark gray to black.  In direct comparison to the SNEG it was signifcantly larger.

The bird was never heard vocalizing. 

Similar Species Discussion

Initially Little Blue Heron was considered because while rare over here it seemed the most obvious candidate.

However, as soon as the pinkish and pale base to the bill and dark legs were seen we were led to the only remaining candidate - Reddish Egret.  A LBHE in breeding plumage would have a gray base to the bill, more purplish head and neck and the legs would be yellowish-green.
