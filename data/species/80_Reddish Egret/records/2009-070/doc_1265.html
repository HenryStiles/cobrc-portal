Description of the Bird

This was a wader by long legs, long neck and long bill. The bill was mostly dark but it did show pink at at the base and at the gape and was long and spear-like as in herons, egrets, and bitterns. The legs were dark gray. The irdes were yellowish, a good character for young Reddish Egrets (see photos). There was a thin whitish "V" that outlined the base of the bill. The crown, face, chin, throat, and hindneck was a reddish brown and it extended on both sides of the long neck most of the way to the body, but not all of the way. The lower neck, breast, and belly were creamy gray-white. The body was dull blue-gray with a few rufous feathers stuck in here and there. The legs were long and were blue gray in color.
Behaviors: At times this bird was an active feeder and at times used the herky-jerky style of feeding, but it mostly employed a slow walking style of searching for prey and it also just stood and watched us for long periods.
Call: none heard
Plumage: immature probably juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Great Blue Heron - Is much larger with longer legs and longer bill. Some were next to the REEG for comparison in size. GBHE has a whitish face, contrasting dark crown and yellowish bill, unlike the REEG.
Tricolored Heron - has brighter white belly and narrow white throat surrounded by bluish feathers and legs are, depending on age, yellow or reddish.
Little Blue Heron - dark phase can look a bit like this bird, but instead has greenish legs, darker blue body feathers, blue-based bill with a dark tip, and that would be for an adult. Young birds will be with white feathering and a pink-based bill.
