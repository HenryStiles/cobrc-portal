Description of the Bird

the bird was a large heron, smaller than a Great Blue, but larger than expected for Snowy Egret or Little Blue Heron (closest in size to Great Egret). It was also proportionally very tall, with long, stilt like legs. The bill was very long, straight, and thin, and completely very dark gray or black. There was a small patch of bare skin between the eye and the bill that was pale gray in color. The eyes were straw colored, and visible at a long distance. The legs were dark blue-gray or black.

The body of the bird was dark blue-gray, with no other colors apparent. The neck was a dark purplish-blue color, appearing slightly reddder in certain lights. There were some short shaggy plumes around the lower neck and chest, though these were hard to see unless the bird was in certain poses.
Behaviors: The bird would forage with a very distinctive style - it would "dance" around in the water, with jerky motions while flaring its wings every now and then. The overall behavior of the bird seemed less controlled than the foraging behavior of most heron species, and the bird seemed "awkward" while walking and foraging.
Call: none heard
Plumage: sub-adult

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of egrets and herons were ruled out by the completely dark plumage. Little Blue Heron was ruled out by the larger size of this bird, the redder neck, by the much longer legs in proportion to the size, by the longer and straighter bill, and by the feeding behavior. Western Reef-Heron was ruled out by the larger size of this bird, by the proportionally longer legs, by the redder neck, and by the lack of white in the face.
