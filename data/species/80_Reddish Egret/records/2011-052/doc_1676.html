Habitat

The Egret was on that portion of the Lake with little vegetation on an open sandy shore.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Kowa zoom scope
Distance:  150 yards when standing on the shore and about 75 yards as it flew by
Light:  Good-bird was east of observers when on the shore and north of us when it flew by.

Description of the Bird

A solid white heron excepting dark legs and feet, and dusky dagger shaped bill becoming darker at the tip, this bird was larger than nearby Little Blue Herons and smaller than nearby Great Blue Herons.
The bird was slender, appearing longer-necked and longer legged than Little Blue Herons. Due to distance we were not able to determine the exact colors of the bill.
Behaviors: Often doing dancing staggering side step with one leg crossing over the other like a tipsy reveler along a sandy lakeshore. Sometimes it jumped up & down with wings spread.
Call: none heard
Plumage: possibly 2nd cycle or adult

Similar Species Discussion

Snowy Egrets and 1st cycle Little Blue Herons are smaller than the Egret we observed which we were able to compare in size to nearby Little Blues. Little Blues have paler legs, and Snowy Egrets have yellow feet with younger Snowies having paler legs.

Great Blue Herons and Great Egrets are larger than the Egret we saw and able to compare in size to nearby Great Blues. Great Egrets also have bright carrot colored bills in contrast to the dull dusky bill of the Egret we saw.
