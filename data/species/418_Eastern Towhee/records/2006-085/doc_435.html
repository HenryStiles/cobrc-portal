Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 8x32BN Binoculars
Distance:  24, estimated
Light:  The sun at times was to our sides and other times it was to our backs. When we saw the bird it was always in good enough lighting to get a great look at it.

Description of the Bird

Brownish head and back. It had a white patch at the base of the primaries. No spots on the back. It had a red eye. Some white in the tail. Rufous on the sides. White belly.
Behaviors: It was visiting an area which had a few feeders at it and some dripping water out of a milk jug. There were quite a few bushes and a few trees in the area.
Call: Didn't hear it.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee - It has spots on the back of the bird. No white patch at the base of the primaries. Female has a grayish brown head, not the same color as an Eastern.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide to Birds after the observation.

Previous Experience

I have seen 3 other Eastern Towhees before this one and I have a lot of experience with Spotted Towhees. Plus I have also seen a few hybrids at Tamarack Ranch in Logan County.
