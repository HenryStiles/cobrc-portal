Description of the Bird

Brown head, back, and tail. Rufous sides and undertail covert. White patch on the edge of the wing (base of the primaries). Dull red eye. White belly.
Behaviors: First seen about half up a tree, then it flew across the dirt road. It then buried itself in a thicket giving usually just brief views.
Call: didn't hear it
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee - Isn't as brown as this bird was and it has spots on the back. No white spot at the base of the primaries
Hybrid Towhee - This bird had no signs of any spotting on the back, it had a white wing spot where it was suppose to, it was the right color of brown for a female Eastern Towhee. I could see no problems with this bird to call it a hybrid.
