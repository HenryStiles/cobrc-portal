Description of the Bird

Rufous-sided Towhee of brown with sienna (commonly called "milk chocolate" in field guides) hues. No spots on back or scapulars. Wings showed white "check box" at base of primaries and some white edges on folded primaries. Three tertials had white edging, barely visible in one. The tips of the primaries were worn, being a tan color that contrasted with remainder of wings. No spots on wing covert tips. Rufous orange sides showed some wear and faded to buff yellow on vent. Remainder of underparts white. Head showed (in photos; did not see this in field), some gray on face. Tail spots large, approaching tip of undertail coverts, consistent with Eastern Towhee, but not Eastern x montanus Spotted Towhee (which is the flavor of hybrid that I've seen in CO and is prominent at Tamarack). Head rounded, not peaked as in most Spotted Towhees. Bill smallish, not as large-based as typical of Spotted Towhee. Eyes dull red. See http://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/eastern_towhee/id for two photos of female Eastern Towhees that show some gray in face (and interestingly, similarly worn). Also http://www.howardsview.com/SpottedTowhee/SpottedTowhee.html shows such a bird below the photos of Spotted Towhee. I am presuming the bird is an immature due to wear and what seems to be a dullish eye color, but I could be wrong on the age

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted x Eastern Towhee: I have gained much experience with this cross, especially at Tamarack Ranch, where most seem to resemble Eastern Towhee more than Spotted. The combination of overall body color, no spots on back/scaps nor on wing coverts, presence of a white "check box" at base of primaries, and large tail spots - in combination - is something that I've never seen on a bird that called like an Spotted Towhee and are all marks that point towards Eastern and away from Spotted. Additionally, the shape of head and bill are that of Eastern vs Spotted. The odd nearly blackish markings at bottom of bib can be found on some photos of females on the internet from areas far away from the range of Spotted Towhee.
