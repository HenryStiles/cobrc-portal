Description of the Bird

I was pishing at willow clump, when a towhee flew away from me and tee'd up on a willow branch, fully in view. Initially, the view was almost strict profile; before the bird flew, it faced away, leaning a bit forward and cocking its tail up, giving me a good view of vent and undertail. The head, upper chest, and upperparts were an odd brown, not dissimilar to milk chocolate but with a warmer tone and a bit darker (I guess it partly depends on the brand of milk chocolate). In any case, a color that I've seen repeatedly in female Eastern Towhees. The wings were utterly plain (including tertials), except a square of white at the base of the primaries with a narrow white line extending down lower edge of folded primaries. The bill was relatively short c/w that of a Spotted Towhee and the head more rounded. The underparts below chest were white with orange-buff sides. The undertail coverts were a paler more yellow-buff. The tail had white "spots" that extended proximally almost to the tip of the undertail coverts.

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee and Spotted x Eastern Towhee. No Spotted Towhee features such as smaller tail spots, markings on scaps, wings, back. The square was fully sized and not reduced, as in many hybrids. The head and bill shape were typical of EATO and not SPTO. The color of the bird was not intermediate, but typical of EATO
