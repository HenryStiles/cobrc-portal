Description of the Bird

the bird was a very large sparrow, about a third again as large as the Juncoes it was near during part of the observation. The bird had a heavy but short black bill. The legs were dusky pink, and the eye dark brown.

The head, back, chest, wings, and tail were a warm dark brown, with no white markings on the back, wing coverts or chest. There was a bold white patch at the base of the otherwise brown primaries, and white edges to the outer primary feathers extending from the basal patch to the tips of the feathers. The tertials also had bold white edges. The tail had large and bold white tail corners that were visible in flight, and when viewed from below when perched it appeared that the outer half of the tail was white.

The bird had very large, bold, and bright rufous sides extending from the chest to the undertail coverts. The center of the belly was clean white. The undertail coverts were unmarked buffy.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of its time skulking in the brush, espcially during the earlier observation. Later in the day it spent about 15 minutes in a large but isolated bush hopping around a bit but mostly perching motionless.
Call: The bird was heard to call during the early morning observation, a upslurred "chu-wheet", with a slight whining quality.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All species of sparrow were ruled out by the larger size and heavier build, bold rufous sides, clean white belly, unmarked brown on upperparts, head, and chest, and large white outer tail corners.

Spotted Towhee was ruled out by the lack of any white spots on the upperparts and wing coverts, bold white patch at the base of the primaries, warmer brown color that was even throughout without any gray highlights, and by the call.
