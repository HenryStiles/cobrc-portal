Description of the Bird

A rufous-sided towhee lacking nearly all white on the upperparts. Head, nape, and upper back appeared a uniform chocolate brown color. The lower back appeared a slightly grayer shade of brown. On the upper breast, the dark hood came to a point on the white lower breast. The belly was also white. The sides were brick red. The wing coverts and flight feathers were dark brown with slightly lighter brown highlights. A white spot was formed at the base of the primaries, with white shafts running down the outer two or so primaries from the spot. The tertials appeared to have long white panels. The tail also appeared dark brown, with white corners on the outer tail feathers. The legs and feet were pink. The bill was dark gray, sparrow-like in structure. The iris appeared brown with just a tint of reddish warmth.
Behaviors: The bird was skulking in heavy brush and junipers.
# of Birds:
Call: Song not heard. Bird gave the distinctive EATO zhwink call several times, as well as thinner, more generic sounding chip call notes. The call note was verified in the field with an iPod and external speakers.
Plumage: possible HY female per Chris Wood

Similar Species Discussion

The more expected rufous-sided towhee, Spotted Towhee, would show much white spotting on the back, scapulars, and in the wing coverts. Additionally, SPTO should have a more gray-toned head and a redder eye. Finally, SPTO doesnt make the typical EATO call note that we heard.
