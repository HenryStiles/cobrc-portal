Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Eastern Towhee
First Date/Time:  12/22/2005
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  5 minutes
County:  Yuma
Specific Location:  On the south side of Bonny Reservoir. Drive into Bonny SP and then follow the road west along the reservoir until it ends. It was in the row of trees and bushes on the south west side of where the road ends.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Female
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

It was heavily vegetated. Some large trees, but most of the vegetation was understory. I think it was Russian Olive trees, but I am not sure.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Lieca 8X32 BN Binoculars
Distance:  Closest was probably about 6 ft.
Light:  Every time I got to see the bird the sun was behind my back. The last time I saw the bird I got to see it from every direction.

Description of the Bird

Brown head, back, and tail. A white spot at the base of the primaries and a little bit of white on the secondaries. Rufous color on the side of the body and the undertail covert. White belly. Red eye.
Behaviors: Very Towhee like. It stayed in thick cover most of the time while we were trying to find and while we were looking at it. Occasionally it would get in the open, but when it did it would usually fly to a new location.
Call: A nasal zewheek
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee
Female, has grayish brown head. Has well defined spots on the back. Doesn't have the white patch on the base of the primaries.

Resources Used

The Sibley Guide to Birds. Used after the identification.

Previous Experience

I have seen Eastern Towhee once before this and I have seen Spotted Towhee regularly.
