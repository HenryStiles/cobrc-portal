Habitat

Rural, natural yard. Lots of fallen leaves, stream 50 ft. from house, lots of natural brushy, fairly dense undergrowth.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  12X50 binoculars and 50mm to500 mm telephoto lens
Distance:  10 -15 ft.
Light:  Clear sunny morning. Lighting was good.

Description of the Bird

Plain warm brown on head, back and throat, white patch on primaries, rufous on sides, white underneath. Size approx. 8.5 inches. Shaped like a long-tailed sparrow
Behaviors: Was scratching at leaves under feeders with Spotted Towhees
Call: Did not hear
Plumage: plain warm brown/white patch on primarie

Similar Species Discussion

Was seen with Spotted Towhees (4 males)
