Description of the Bird

Odd brown-colored "Rufous-sided" Towhee (the color is not truly represented in photos. Kathy (Cathy?) Dunning, who first spotted the bird, commented that she had on oddly colored towhee lacking spots. The color was rather similar to the lowermost left-facing towhee in Crossley's book.) with no spotting or other white markings above other than white "box" at base of folded primaries, some white edging on folded primaries, and a slight hint of white edging on one or two tertials. The eye was dark, with no red to be seen. The white tail spots were quite long, consistent with Eastern Towhee or "arcticus" Spotted Towhee, but not other races of Spotted Towhee.
Behaviors: scratching on ground near base of junipers with Zonotrichia sparrows
Call: not heard
Plumage: immature (1st basic or formative)

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhees are, well, spotted. Given size of tail spots, hybrid would be with arcticus Spotted Towhee, and should show some spotting above.
