Description of the Bird

An obvious "Rufous-sided Towhee" lacking white spots on scapulars and wing coverts. The bird's head, chest, and upperparts were entirely blackish. The wings had black coverts and dark brown flight feathers (retained Juvenal) with a distinctive white patch at the base of the primaries that extended in a strip down the length of a couple of outer primaries. The sides were rufous and the center of the belly and the vent region were white. The tail was fairly long and blackish, with very large terminal white spots on the outer few pairs of rectrices. The R6 spots occupying more than half of the feathers.

The bill was stout, but not that long and mostly dark. The legs were dark pinkish-red.
Behaviors: First seen flying across a two-track road, then hiding in a wood pile, and finally flying out of the wood pile.
Call: I didn't hear; Mark heard it call once.
Plumage: First Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee is the only really similar species, once Black-headed Grosbeak is ruled out (my long tail, and not-too-thick beak). The bird entirely lacked white spotting on scapulars and wing coverts and had the basal-primary patch that Eastern sports and that Spotted lacks.
