Description of the Bird

Larry and I found the bird first by sound, hearing it scratching among dead leaves on the ground under some brush. The bird was exactly like our more common Spotted Towhee in size and shape, and superficially plumage-wise. Black completely enveloped the head and upperparts, while forming a sharply defined edge on the upperchest - creating what I consider a cowl. The black met with white in the center of the chest with the white extending to the vent region. Dark orange along the sides and flanks, gave the bird a tri-colored look. The undertails coverts and vent region were a dull orange-tan color. The wings were mostly the same black as the upperparts, except that the tertials were clearly edged in white and a large, white patch could be seen at the base of the outer 5-6 (roughly) primaries (even on the folded wing). The bird flew once directly in front of me, and the wing patch and bold white tips to the outer rectrices were noted.
Behaviors: We watched the bird foraging on the ground, where it would scratch using both feet simultaneously. The bird was seemingly unbothered by us, allowing us to get within about 15 feet of it.
Call: Extended single-noted call rising in pitch and ending sharply.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Most similar to our common Spotted Towhee, from which it is distinguished by complete lack of white spotting on the scapulars, complete lack of white wingbars, and having a large white patch at the base of the outer 5-6 (roughly) primaries. I looked carefully for indications of hybridization and did not find any.
