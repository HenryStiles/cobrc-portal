Description of the Bird

Larger than a song sparrow but smaller than a robin, perhaps about 8 inches in length. Head, upperparts and tail mostly black with some white patches noticeable in the flight feathers at rest. Belly white with bright rufous sides. Feet and bill black. Bill conical shaped and short, more like a sparrow than a blackbird. Eye dull red.
Behaviors: Sitting verticle atop a bush while calling. Hopping horizontally on a log with tail cocked at 45 degrees. On ground, scratching among leaves.
Call: Gave two calls. One was a long dranw out slurred zeeeooooweee starting on one pitch then dropping then rising. The other call was louder and shorter but I did not hear it well enough to adequately describe it.
Plumage: adult
