Description of the Bird

the bird was a very large sparrow, about a third to half again as large as the nearby Juncoes. The bird was quite chunky, more so than the Juncoes. The bill was black and quite heavy and short. The legs were a dark, dusky pink.

The bird had a black hood and upperparts, which were completely unmarked except for a bold white patch at the base of the primaries, and some white along the edges of the primaries. The sides of the bird were a warm brown with a rufous tinge. The belly was unmarked white. The undertail coverts were a pale buffy-cream color, also unmarked. The bird's tail was black, with very large white tail corners, so that when the tail was viewed from underneath when the bird was on the ground it appeared that the outer three quarters of the tail was white.
Behaviors: The bird was scratching around on the ground out in the open for a bit before heading into the thick brush.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of sparrow were ruled out by the large size, unmarked black upperparts and rufous-brown sides. Spotted Towhee was ruled out by the complete lack of white spots on the scapulars, lack of white wing bars, and the prescense of the white patch at the base of the primaries.
