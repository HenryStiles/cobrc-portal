Documentation of Eastern Towhee
2006-002

Observer Information

Reporter:  C W   
E-mail: 
Other Observers:  Nick Komar and Eric Defonso; I don't know who reported it, but it had been seen before we I saw it.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Eastern Towhee
First Date/Time:  2/3/2006
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  45 minutes
County:  El Paso
Specific Location:  Chico Basin Ranch, It is about 1 mile south of Hanover. Then there is an area with a lot of trees and Russian Olives and that is where it was hanging out.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

The bird was usually in dense Russian Olives.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski spotting scope and Lieca 8X32 BN Binoculars
Distance:  At closest we were about 10 feet from the bird
Light:  When we initially saw the bird. The bird was between us and the sun, but then we saw the bird where we were between the sun and the bird.

Description of the Bird

Black on the head, back, and tail. White patch on the base of the pimaries. A few white patches on the secondaries. Rufous on the sides of the birds and undertail covert. White belly. Red eye.
Behaviors: It acted towhee like. It usually stayed down low in dense brush. When we first saw it, it was sitting up in the top of some bushes. Then it flew off after about 5 minutes and stayed low in the Russian Olives for the rest of the times we were around that area, which was about 30 minutes. We would see it every once in a while during that 30 minutes when it decided to fly from one bush to another.
Call: A nasal zewheek
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee
The head of the SPTO looks darker than the body becasue the spots on the back make the back look lighter. The spotting on the SPTO is well defined. The SPTO doesn't have a white patch on the base of the primaries.
