Description of the Bird

A towhee type sparrow with its black hood and bib, white belly with chestnut flanks, and dark dorsal surface to its long tail. The back and wings were black with white-centered black tertials, and a white "flag" that shows prominently at the base of the primaries. The eye was red.
Behaviors: Sang from top of a short tree but moslty foraged actively on the ground moving from the ranch house at Chico HQ to the dam of little HQ pond to the front yard of the ranch house to the HQ parking lot to the big red barn to the north of HQ almost always on the ground.
Call: song was sung from the top of a short Siberian elm. It was noticably higher in pitch than the song of SPTO and it ended in a trill.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The only species in North American north of Mexicon that is similar is our Spotted Towhee. Spotted has similar plumage EXCEPT SPTO has a number of rows of white-edged primary coverts that appear as white spots on a dark back. In addition, SPTO has a white wing bar that does not show in an EATO. The other important part was the song, this one was noticeably different than SPTO song.
