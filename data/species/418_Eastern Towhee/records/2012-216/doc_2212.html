Description of the Bird

The bird had the features that make spotted and eastern towhees distinctive: black head, bill, breast and wings; red eye; orange flanks; and white belly. The bird had a single white patch (check mark) on the wing, characteristic of eastern towhee, as opposed to numerous spots on the wings of spotted towhee. I also understand from consultation with Steve Mlodinow that the observed long white undertail spot (extending from the tip of the tail to nearly the vent) is a good indicator of easter towhee.
Behaviors: The bird was perched and singing in a cottonwood tree for the minute or two that I observed it.
Call: An unfamiliar song (the "drink your teeeee" song that I learned about after the sighting) attracted my attention to this bird. I also heard the familiar song of spotted towhee (few short notes followed by trill), perhaps from this bird and/or from spotted towhees in the same area. I don't recall hearing the eastern towhee call, but I was unfamiliar with it at the time.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted towhees are similar. I also understand that very similar spotted x eastern towhees have been observed in Tamarack Ranch. This bird was identified as an eastern towhee primarily by the uniform black wing except for the white patch at the base of the primaries. The length of the white undertail spot also supports the identification as eastern towhee.
