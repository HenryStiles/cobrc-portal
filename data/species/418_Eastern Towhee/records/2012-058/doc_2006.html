Habitat

back yard with some dense shrubbery near water

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  binocs
Distance:  8' to 30'

Description of the Bird

-warm brown head, back and upper breast
-rusty orange sides
-white belly
-no spots on wings
-missing the typical one white wing spot
-reddish eye
-dark bill
-larger than a sparrow
-long tail
Behaviors: -first appeared at water feature
-scratched through debris under euonymous bush
-also foraged for something in grass
-assumed it was the "miller moths" which were abundant and many birds were eating them
Call: n/a
Plumage: maybe not quite full adult

Similar Species Discussion

-similar species is the Spotted Towhee
-coloring very very similar
-no white wing spots
