Description of the Bird

The Eastern Towhee and two Spotted Towhees were in a hedgerow in area 10 of the eastern section of Tamarack Ranch State Wildlife Area.  The Eastern Towhee had typical roufous-sided towhee characteristics in general, and the following specific characteristics:  (1) scapulars and wings mosty dark (black and brown) but with a white check mark at the base of the primaries and white edges on the tertials and (2) long undertail spots.  It called at least twice with the fast, sharp, rising "chewink" typical of this species.  The Spotted Towhees were also calling, with their typical slowly rising "zhreee", and the contrast in calls was obvious.

Similar Species Discussion

distinguished from similar Spotted Towhee by call, distinctive wing markings, and long undertail spots
