Description of the Bird

Moderately sized passerine (about 8 inches in length) with conical emberizine (sparrow-like) bill. Bright chocolate brown head, upper chest, and upperparts, with faint blackish streaking on back (suggestive of retained juvenal feathers). Bright brick-orange sides. Otherwise white below.
Behaviors: Perched on ground eating seeds, or perched in a low woody shrub or brush pile.
Call: None
Plumage: Hatch Year

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee would have white spots on scapulars, and white wingbars, and would lack the white marks on the primaries and seconds
