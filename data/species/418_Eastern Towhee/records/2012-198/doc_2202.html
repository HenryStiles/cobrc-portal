Description of the Bird

Spotted/Eastern type towhee, with black head, chest, and back. Wings black except two tertials narrowly tipped in white and a white "box" at base of folded primaries. Sides "rufous" and brightly colored, contrasting with bright white center to belly and cream-yellow undertail coverts. Narrow and abbreviated black line extending down from chest between rufous sides and white uppermost white belly. Eye red, bill black, white tail spots long, approaching tip of undertail coverts.
Behaviors: Singing atop tree
Call: Song: Djeenk chur tee-ee-ee-ee-ee
Call: Shrill metallic shureenk
Plumage: not determined

Similar Species Discussion

No stray spots on wings or scaps to indicate Spotted Towhee, tail spots typical of Eastern Towhee and far longer than local montanus race Spotted Towhees. Call and song typical of Eastern Towhee, without any evidence of Spotted Towhee influence
