Description of the Bird

Size and shape overall similar to that of Spotted Towhee (except subtleties of head/bill shape). Upperparts and head the peculiar brown color that is typical of Eastern Towhee (I hate 'milk chocolate' to describe that color). Orange sides. Belly and chest below hood white. Only white markings dorsally were on wings with one white tertial tip and a fully formed white 'box' at base of primaries with a thin line of white on lower primary edge. Head and bill shape that of EATO, being smaller billed and rounder headed than Spotted Towhee.

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee would have more spots above, lack "check box" at base of primaries, different color
Hybrid Spotted x Eastern Towhee should show at least a couple stray spots, vary in overall color, have different shaped head/bill. Hybrids often have diminished box at base of primaries as well
