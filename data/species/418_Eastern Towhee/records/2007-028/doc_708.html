Description of the Bird

Easily recognized as a towhee. Plumage on head and dorsally a rich warm brown. Flanks brightest part of bird, a rich warm rufous. Wings same coloration as nape and back, a warm brown. Noticeable white "flag" at base of primaries, contrasting strongly with wing coloration. Bill sparrow-like and dark gray, tail dark brown, throat same color as face forming a bib. Head, back and wings warm brown. Breast and belly white. Leg color appeared brownish.
Behaviors: This bird was in the tumbleweeds along the earthen dam at Rose Pond at Chico Basin Ranch. It eventually worked its way up into near the top of a small (20') tall Siberian elm. Bird called frequently. First seen on the ground. Not real shy although liked to stay partially hidden.
Call: call given was a loud rising "shhhwwink" quite different from Spotted Towhee.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee comes closest but female EATO totally different from female SPTO so really there are no species that resemble the female EATO. The closest in coloration but not in size is probably Red Fox Sparrow which is noticeably smaller with different shape crown (peaked) and with streaks on breast unlike EATO and significantly different in jizz, coloration, structure, and most other plumage characters. Really no other bird like a female EATO

Previous Experience

I grew up in Ohio where this was a yard bird. My grandmother taught me to call it a "Cheewink" and not a towhee, from its call.
