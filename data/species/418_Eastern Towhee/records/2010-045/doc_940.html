Description of the Bird

Towhee species with black head, nape and hood down to mid-chest, white chest and belly. Large, sharp black bill.

Iris: almost black with a hint of deep red.

Flanks: bright rufous fairly wide in size. A bit of white separation between the top of the rufous flank and the black bib. Along the central chest part of the rufous flanking there were some difuse black spots or smudges whose concentration diminished continuing down along toward the belly.

Undertail coverts were pale rufous. Underside of tail had grey panels than white panels toward the tip that were longer (2x?) than the gray panels.

Wings: One large white spot along the fore-edge of the primaries exactly as in a Black-throated Blue Warbler. Some narrow white edging along the primary extension beyond the spot. The primary extension beyond the secondaries was not as pure a black as the rest of the wing, slightly lighter. On the top of the wing on the rump side of the secondaries there were two creamier edges to the secondaries as if the outside tip of the secondary was cream colored. The rest of the wing was entirely black.
Behaviors: Seen in association with Spotted Towhees, White-throated Sparrow, Dark-eyed Juncos, Mountain and Black-capped Chickadees. Seen 20 feet up in a juniper, then along a stone wall with bird seed scattered on it at about 8 feet.
Call: n.a.
Plumage: adult
