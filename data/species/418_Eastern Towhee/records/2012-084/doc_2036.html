Description of the Bird

This bird was about same size & shape as nearby Ash-throated Flycatchers, but with a smaller more rounded head.

Head, throat, back, and wings were solid black except for small but well defined white patch at base of primaries on bottom of folded wing. This patch appeared nearly square shaped. I didn't see the usual wisps of white linings sometimes seen at close range nor any white in otherwise longish dark tail, probably because I was about 50 yards from the bird.

Since bird was facing away from me, I only saw its backside with glimpses of orange flanks when it occasionally turned slightly to the right.
Behaviors: Bird sat atop narrow-leaf cottonwood looking about and occasionally preening before flying off.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee often shows a few diffused white speckles on back with more dense spotting on wings often melding into fairly distinct serrated wingbars.

EasternxSpotted Towhee hybrids usually show a few white speckles on wings if not on the back. They are less speckled than "pure" Spotteds, but more speckled than the bird I saw.
