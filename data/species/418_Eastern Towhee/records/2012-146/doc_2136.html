Description of the Bird

Large sparrow-like. Brown head and throat. Orange flanks and whitish belly. Back brown without white spots. Wings with white showing in folded wing.Tail brown.
Behaviors: Heard first at edge of marsh but then flew and perched in a tall species of sunflower where it continued to give the ascending call notes.
Call: high upslured notes, but not the chewink call
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing else like female Eastern Towhee with its brown head and bib, and this color of orange on its flanks.
