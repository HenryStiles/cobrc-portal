Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Eastern Towhee
First Date/Time:  10/24/2012
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  10 seconds
County:  Pueblo
Specific Location:  Between the lower and upper twin ponds at Chico Basin Ranch.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Female
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Fairly flat area near a pond. There were a lot of thick dry weeds and some Russian Olive trees.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica Trinovid 10x42
Distance:  15 feet
Light:  Sunny. The photo indicates that the sun was to our backs causing the bird to be well lit.

Description of the Bird

Brown head, back and wings. Single white spot at the base of the primaries no other white spots visible. Rufous flanks and a white belly. Size and shape similar to a Spotted Towhee.
Behaviors: The bird was first seen on the ground in a weedy area and then flew up to a Russian Olive tree for a few seconds. It then left the area and we could not relocate it.
Call: none
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

This bird has very similar size, shape and coloration of a Spotted Towhee. Spotted Towhee was eliminated because this bird lacked white on the scapulars, coverts and tertials but had a white patch at the base of the primaries.

Resources Used

At the time of the sighting Ira Sanders and I both believed that this was an Eastern Towhee.

Previous Experience

I see Spotted Towhee in my yard several times a week. This was the second Eastern Towhee that I have seen.
