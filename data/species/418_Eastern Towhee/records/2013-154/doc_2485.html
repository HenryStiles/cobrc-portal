Description of the Bird

Towhee of the size and shape of the Spotted/Eastern pairing. Upperparts and head colored that peculiar warm brown that may be unique to Eastern Towhee females and rather different from Spotted Towhee. The back, wings, head were utterly unmarked except for a white square at base of primaries and a thin white line trailing backward along primary edge. No white marks even on tertial tips. Underparts white except orange-brown sides typical of both Spotted and Eastern Towhees fading to paler buffy undertail coverts. White undertail spots extensive, nearly reaching tip of undertail coverts.
Behavior: hopped up on bush, tail cocked, in response to pishing.
Call: shhhweeenk. metallic and shrill
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Spotted Towhee is spotted and lacks white box at base of primaries. Hybrids should show some spotting, smaller tail spots (at least the CO ones seem too), and very different call
