Description of the Bird

This bird came to a feeder in the company of Broad-tailed Hummingbirds. This made her large size quite obvious. The length of the bill and the thickness of the bill-base strongly contrasted with the bill length and thickness of the adjacent Broad-taileds’ bills. The bill was straight. She had a short, white post-ocular streak and dark auriculars. Below the auriculars was pale gray as was the speckled throat; the chest was a bit darker gray.

The top of head, back and tail were bronzy-green. The tail had small white corners.

We saw what we assume was the same bird earlier in the day while it was feeding facing us. The view showed a mottled chest and a bulky bird. The white tail corners were seen when she left.

Both views were brief
Behaviors: It came to hummingbird feeders at the same location a female Mag had been reported the last two years.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult female

Similar Species Discussion

We eliminated immature Anna’s and female Black-chinned hummingbirds based on size. Blue-throated Hummingbird was eliminated based on its shorter bill, wider auricular area, white stripes both above and below the auricular area and larger white tail corners. Given the plumage differences with other large hummers, no others were considered.
