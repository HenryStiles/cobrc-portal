Description of the Bird

The hummingbird faced me as he initially approached the hummingbird feeder.  I had enough time to notice that his length was about 5 inches and his bill was very long.   Since he perched on the back side of the feeder, I took the opportunity to study the underside of the tail.  The tail was forked and dark for most of it's length. The tips of the tail feathers had a very thin, gray edging that widened slightly toward the outer tail feathers.  (At least three or four of the outermost tail feathers had that gray edging on the tip; the gray tail tips could easily have been overlooked if I hadn't been intent on seeing that field mark.)  When the bird left the perch of the feeder, he flew much closer to me to perch on a dead oak branch.  The face had a distinct white, post-ocular stripe.  In addition, there was a second short, white stripe below the auricular, near the base of the bill.  The gorget appeared black from my perspective; I never got the angle I needed for seeing the iridescence of the gorget. The crown appeared black and almost crested due to placement of those feathers.  The underparts were mottled with green splotches.  I believe that the white stripes on the face in combination with the dark gorget indicate a second-year male Magnificent Hummingbird.     

Similar Species Discussion

The hummingbird was too large to be any of the expected species for the area.  A Black-chinned female would be about 1.5 or 2 inches shorter in length.  The Blue-throated Hummingbird would appear about the same size.  In fact, a male Blue-throated Hummingbird would be the closest contender for a similar species.  However, a Blue-throated Hummingbird's tail has a much larger white area on the outer tail tips.  (This white color on a Blue-throated Hummingbird's tail is also much whiter than the edging on the tail tips on the Magnificent Hummingbird.).  In addition, the overall tail shape for a Blue-throated Hummingbird is rounded rather than forked.    A male Blue-throated Hummingbird would have a more rounded head, a shorter bill, and two lengthy, white stripes on the face.
