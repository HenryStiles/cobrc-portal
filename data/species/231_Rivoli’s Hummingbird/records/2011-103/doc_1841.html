Description of the Bird

I arrived at the Tunnel Campground where Connie Kogler found & photographed a female Magnificent Hummingbird a few days before (& the summer before.) As I was driving in (afternoon- 4:30-ish) I noticed a lot of activity at the host's three feeders. I stopped the car and watched for a few minutes when a monster greenish-backed hummer with a long, fairly decurved bill and strong facial pattern arrived to feed. It was substantially larger than the mostly Broad-tailed Hummers and scattering of Rufous that were around and I had no doubt it was the Mag. I set up camp, put up a feeder, and got my camera ready but didn't see the bird again for the following two days of my visit. It didn't strike me as dark as a Blue-throated (either underparts or tail) and I think the bill was too long and curved for Blue-throated as well.
Behaviors: Using hummingbird feeders.
Call: Not heard.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Significantly larger size than "normal" CO hummingbirds rules out most choices. The other likely large hummingbird choice would be Blue-throated, but the long bill and greenish back, lacking any warmer tones on the rump, rule this out. I also didn't note the massive blackish tail with super prominent white outer tail corners which should have been obvious on a Blue-throated Hummingbird.
