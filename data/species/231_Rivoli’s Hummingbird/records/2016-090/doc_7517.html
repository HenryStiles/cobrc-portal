Description of the Bird

An obvious hummingbird but much larger then other hummingbirds accompanying it (Rufous and Broad-taile). Dark green above with no apparent pale edging to the feathers. Underparts grayish with a scaly appearance. Flanks slightly washed with green. Tail darker than back with black band and lighter tail corner (not white). Bill long, black and fairly straight. Face grayish with white postocular spot shped like a triangle and an indistinct postocular line. There were several hummingbirds in the area that had responded to a Screech-Owl mob tape. This bird was noticed because the wing beats were much slower and it was much larger than all of the otheres that responded. It perched briefly on a branch in full sun and then vanished.  

Similar Species Discussion

Blue-throated was the only other large hummer considered. This species was eliminated by the lack of black tail and lack of white tips to the outer tail featers. There was no real well defined white eye stripe. All other expected species were eliminated on size alone. The bird was seen within feet of several Rufous and Broad-tailed Hummingbirds

Resources Used

Consulted Sibley, National Geographic and Hummingbirds of North America after the fact just to confirm my observations since I don't see these larger species very often.

Previous Experience

I have seen these species (Magnificent and Blue-throated) on several occasions in Arizona. I have also seen Magnificent on two other occasions and Blue-throated once in Colorado.
