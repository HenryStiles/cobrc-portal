Description of the Bird

It was green above and pale below with white marks on the face as illustrated in the accompanying photographs. It was long-necked with a slight peak to the rear crown. It was shy and would not approach the feeder when I was in close proximity. It could not be approached closely while feeding, unlike the numerous and tame broad-tailed (often >12 at two feeders at dawn and dusk). It often seemed intimidated and chased off by a broad-tailed, then on other occasions it was not intimidated and feed in close proximity of one or more broad-tailed. Broad-tailed hummingbirds also intimidate each other and illustrate similar behavior depending on and sex and dominance ranking. It was too early in the season for the even more aggressive rufous hummingbird to be observed with the new visitor. The lack of complete or partially developed bright, metallic crown and/or gorget in colors other than the general body color ruled out adult or juvenile male birds.
