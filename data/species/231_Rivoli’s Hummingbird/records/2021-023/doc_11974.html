Description of the Bird

One adult male Rivoli's Hummingbird came to the feeder along with Broad-tailed Hummongbirds. Bird found by Meg Nyberg from inside her home looking through the window at the feeder, mid-afternoon. I went in the afternoon and the next morning and did not see the bird. Meg photographed the bird from inside her homel

Similar Species Discussion

Definitely not a Broad-tailed. All the features pointed only to Rivoli's. Meg Nyberg found the species in her bird book, took a photo, and reported it online at Next Door. I saw it very shortly afterward and confirmed her photo. I then visited in case the bird returned, which it did not.

Resources Used

 Megused her Peterson Bird Guide and found Magnificent, I identified it immediately and checked my current Sibley's Western

Previous Experience

I have seen Rivoli's Hummingbirds for years in southern Arizona, usually at Madera Canyon. Meg is new to following birds. She made a very good identification with little background. The bird was large, much larger than the Broad-tailed hummers, and had its extravagant colors. She saw it clearly.
