Description of the Bird

A large hummingbird found its way into net 8 at the Chico Basin Ranch banding station. I extracted it and thought, "Oh, my."

The bird was substantially larger than any regularly occurring hummingbird in CO. The bill was very long and nearly straight and black for the entire length, though there was some anomalous pinkish-red stuff at the base of the maxilla. The crown was slaty with darker streaking, except that the rear of the crown was a bit iridescent green, There was a distinct black eye line that diffused beyond the eye and curved downward, and above it behind the eye was a distinct rhomboidal white spot very nearly the size of the eye (which was dark).

The upperparts and upper wing coverts were bright metallic green. The wings were blackish. The tail was mostly medium-dark green -- and somewhat metallic -- except for grayish-white tips to the outer rectrices and submarginal bits of black separating those whitish tips from the green bases. The feathers on the chin and throat were brownish-gray with wide white fringes, with this area becoming gradually darker from bill base to chest. The sides and flanks were greenish -- from dull olive to bright metallic green. The vent region was whitish; the legs were black and very short.
Behaviors: Hanging in mist net, putting up with hand-held ogling and photos, flying away when released, never to be seen again.
Call: None heard
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Blue-throated Hummingbird is the species occurring in CO that can be most similar, but that species easily ruled out by lack of large, bright white rectrix tips, nares covered by forehead feathers, and by the r1s being green. No other species in the US is at all similar.
