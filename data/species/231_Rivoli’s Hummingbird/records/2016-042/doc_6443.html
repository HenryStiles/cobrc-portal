Description of the Bird

The bird appeared at a window hummingbird feeder in the AM of June 30. It fed briefly from the feeder then flew 3 meters to feed on yellow flowers of a honeysuckle bush. I had good, unobstructed views of the bird with binoculars during this time. The bird then flew out of sight west. The bird was large compared to the other hummingbirds at the feeder (Black-chinned, Rufous and Broad-tailed). The tail was noticeably large when spread and was a slate gray with white tips at the ends that were smaller toward the mid line. The breast appeared spotted in green dots on a whitish back ground. The back was a metallic green that continued from the crown onto the tail. A white, fairly bold supercilliary line started about the middle of the eye and continued to the back of the head. Under this line was a patch of black that faded onto the auricular area. A fainter white line separated the patch of black below from the chin/throat area. The wing primaries and secondaries were slate gray and the coverts emerald green. The bird was not chased nor did it interact with the other hummingbirds nearby.

Similar Species Discussion

The only similar species considered was the Blue-throated Hummingbird. The absence of a bronze rump patch eliminated the bird as this species.
