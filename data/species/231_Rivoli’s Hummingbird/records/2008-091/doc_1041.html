Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Pentax K100D digital SLR camera with Takumar 2.5, 135mm lens and Makinon 2x converter.
Distance:  Seven feet, indicated by the focus ring on the camera.
Light:  The sun was still above the mountains to our west and the skies were clear with a light breeze. The light conditions were good for visibility and for photographing the subject.

Description of the Bird

The bird was larger than the numerous Broad-tailed hummingbirds visiting our feeder. The bill was long (over 1 inch) and black, and the body was long and slender. The feet were dark, almost black. The top of the head and back were medium green in color and some iridescence was apparent. The throat was whitish with darker dotted lines from the base of the bill to the top of the breast. A white spot behind the eye was prominent and extended dimly toward the back of the head. The breast was whitish and covered with darker spots which were more rounded at the bottom than the top. The lower body underneath from the base of the legs to the end of the tail was whitish with several spots which appeared to be almost valentine-shaped.
Behaviors: The bird never visited the feeder when other species (Broad-tailed or Rufous hummingbirds) were present. He also seemed to wary of our presence if we were outside the trailer. (The feeder is hung from one of our awning supports.) The bird seemed to favor one plastic flower of the four on the small plastic feeder. When he came to feed, he mostly fed while flying, but did perch once. I noticed (and the photos bear this out) that he had to bend his larger body to reach the plastic flower that was designed for smaller hummingbirds. There are a number of other campsites in the park that have hummingbird feeders but have not reported seeing anything like this larger hummingbird.
Call: None Noted.
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

This is a large hummingbird! Only one other species is possible which fits this size, the Blue-throated. The references I used for determination note a prominant bronze rump on the Blue-throated which was not present on the bird in question. The Blue-throated also has a distinctive white line from the base of the bill extending back the lower cheek below the eye which also was not indicated on the bird in question.
