Description of the Bird

Brad Steger, who iives on the lower slopes of Pikes Peak, found a larger hummingbird coming to his feeders on Sunday, 6 July 2008.

The bird was noticeably larger than the common Broad-taileds with which it could be compared.

Age is based on the numerous buff- or white-tipped crown feathers, and light tips to three of the bird's secondaries (if I identified those feathers correctly) and many of its back feathers. The bird had no visible wear to its feahters, i.e. it was in fresh plumage, a characteristic of juvenile hummingbirds from this age group.

The bird was about an inch taller than a Broad-tailed. Its wings were noticeably longer.

The face was its most distinctive feature, with a dark auricular patch, surrounded on the top and bottom by narrow white stirpes. The post occular stripe was largest just behing the bird's eye. The bill was dark and more than twice the length of its head, or quite long. The back was green, but the individual feathers had light-colored tips. The breast was somewhat scaly, the scales being small green feathers. The breast also had a dull buffy white coloration where it wsa not green. Near the bend of the wing there were a few scattered small golden and green feathers, which continued a short distance down the bird's flanks.

When the bird flew, its wing-beats were slower than the nearby BTHUs, and somewhat moth-like.
Behaviors: Over two days ofviewing, I saw this bird about 10 times. It would come to its favorite feeder, one located on the second story of Brad's house, where it would perch on the top of the rod from which the feeder hung. The bird would feed at flower-shaped (Yellow) opening in the feeder and hover and on occassion perch on the rest below the "flowers".

It was also seen feeding at a sap well on a Limber Pine tree, the branches it used for a roost on at least four occassions.
Call: none heard
Plumage: hatch year

Similar Species Discussion

Blue-throated Hummingbird

Blue-throated was considered, but females of that species have longer tails than this bird, a proportionately shorter bill, and broader white-tips to the tail. Not mentioned in the description above, the tail of this bird was green with some bronze tones on the central rects, and small white tips to many of the rects, visible when perched and in the photos. The tail was much shorter with different white patterns to the tail tips than female Blue-throateds.
