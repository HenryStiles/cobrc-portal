Description of the Bird

A larger hummingbird, noticeably larger than the Broad-tailed Hummingbirds at this feeder. Quite a long dark bill. Dark eye with a white post-ocular stripe. Dark auricular patch with a small white stripe below. Some green and some golden-green white-tipped crown feathers. Some golden-green feathers on the nape as well as the side of the neck. The back was primarily green with buff tipped feathers. Strongly margined whitish tips on the outer rectrices and the white/buff tipped feathers all point towards a juvenile female. The bird's breast was spotted gray with a few golden/green feathers on the upper flank. The wings were dark (blackish in color) and almost reached the tip of the tail. Its flight was noticeable slower than other hummingbirds at the feeders.
Behaviors: Feeding at the hummingbird feeders, resting in various bushes and trees near the feeder.
Call: not heard
Plumage: juvenile
