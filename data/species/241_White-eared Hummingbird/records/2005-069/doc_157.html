Description of the Bird

A hummingbird similar in size to the Black-chinned and Broad-taileds that were also present. The most obvious field mark was the black mask and white eye stripe. The mask went from the base of the bill, below the eye and down onto the cheek. The bold eye stripe was right above this from the eye down to the neck. Both the mask and the eye stripe were wide, bold and the dominant face features. The bill was of medium length, straight and vaguely red, especially over the inner half. The top of the head, back of the neck, back and central tail were all a darkish green. The breast was considerably lighter, with green spots especially on the upper breast. The wings and outer tail looked very dark or black. The legs and feet also looked dark.
Behaviors: Mostly seen coming into three hummingbird feeders at a residence, but also seen feeding at a nearby blackberry bush.
Call: None noted.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other North American hummingbird has such a large, bold white eye line. The Broad-billed has a narrow eyeline, but its breast is plain gray, and not light with green spots. This bird was obviously much smaller than either Magnificent or Blue-throated. All other hummingbirds that have occured in Colorado do not have the bold white eye line of this bird.
