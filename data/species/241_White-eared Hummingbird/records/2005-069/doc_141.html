Small mountain community with lots of open Ponerosa Pine forrest and various species of scrub oak.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  80mm Leica APO spotting scope with a 32x wide angle lens. Brunton 7.5x43mm binoculars. Photographed with a Nikon Coolpix 995 camera.
Distance:  15 feet
Light:  Sky was clear but the sun did not shine on the feeder that this bird was coming to. Light was generally even all around preventing me from using a lower ISO rating and a higher shutter speed. :-)

Description of the Bird

Small hummingbird about the size of nearby Broad-tailed Hummingbirds. Back mostly blue-green with faint buffy edges to the lower back and uppertail coverts. The crown was dark gray. The lores and auriculars were black and was bordered on top by a very wide white supercillium. The throat whitish with small green "flecks" becoming larger and more abundant on the chest and sides. Center of belly less "flecked". Undertail coverts appeared white. Wings gray and primary length was even with the tail. Tail was blue-green. Eyes black. Bill mostly black with just a small flesh-colored base to the lower mandible (seen in some photos).
Behaviors: This bird was seen daily from 6am-8:30am and again in the evenings from about 5 or 6pm till 8:30pm.

On July 12th when I observed the bird it would show up and feed for about 30 seconds every 10 minutes. The times between feedings got longer and longer (it was 30 minutes at 8:30am when I left).

There were many Rufous (about 75), Broad-tailed (about 100), Black-chinned (about 30) and Calliope (about 3) hummingbirds all feeding at various times. Like the other hummingbirds this White-eared would fly in and patiently wait for a perch to open on the left most (western most) of five feeders placed in a row on the deck of Jim and Kim Fisher of Durango. No interactions between the White-eared and others were witnessed.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The only species that is this size with black auriculars and white supercillium is a Xantus's Hummingbird but that species has buffy underparts and rusty tail and lacks the green spotting of a White-eared.
