Description of the Bird

A medium-sized hummingbird with a striking facial pattern. Bill is straight, fairly short, dark, with a hint of red at the base of the mandible. The red is more apparent in in-hand photos than in profile. The face is dominated by a sweeping white line beginning above the eye and continuing down parallel to the back of the head, bordering the auricular to the rear and ending just above the shoulder. The auricular is dark gray. They eye sits mostly in this dark patch but is bordered on the top by the white supraloral. The crown is greenish-gray, appearing duller when not iridescing. The throat and breast are white with small iridescent green spots. The green spotting gets heavier on the upper flanks. The wing coverts also have iridescent green coloring that comes and goes as the light changes. The flight feathers are dark gray with a hint of bluish iridescence. The primaries appear wide and blunt-tipped, reaching the end of the tail. The bird had dark feet and displayed a band when I saw it from its 13 July capture.
Behaviors: The bird made two brief visits to a hummingbird feeder while I was there. The feeders were being frequented by hundreds of hummingbirds. Other species present were Broad-tailed, Rufous, Black-chinned, and Calliope Hummingbirds.
Call: Bird gave flat chip notes that contrasted with other hummingbird calls
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Xantus Hummingbird is similar but shows cinnamon underparts lacking green spots in adult plumages.
