Description of the Bird

A very strikingly patterned hummingbird roughly the same size as the Broad-tailed and Rufous hummingbirds also at the same feeder. The most noticeable feature was the broad white stripe that extended from the top of the eye back along the side of the head. Below the white stripe was a broad blackish, slightly mottled and broader than the white stripe, patch that went from just in front of the eye backwards adjacent to the white stripe above it. The underparts were basally white with metallic green flecking scattered throughout, the sparsely flecked throat is diagnostic for a female, but more concentrated along the sides. The upperparts were metallic green with the crown and back feathers edges in a coppery tone, hence the aging of this bird as a hatch-year. The dull brown wings extended to the tail tip. In the accompanying photo one can see that at least two primaries, probably P3-P4, were shed and had not been replaced by this date. The bill was mostly black, except for the lower mandible that showed a small amount of red at its base extending about one-fourth distally.
Behaviors: It came to a hummingbird feeder on the residence's balcony on a regular schedule of every 20 or so minutes.
Call: none heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Broad-billed Hummingbird - the white eye stripe is not as broad, nor does this species have the black eye patch. The green flecking on the underparts is less extensive as well.

Xantus's Hummingbird - this species has buff colored underparts and rufous in the tail.
