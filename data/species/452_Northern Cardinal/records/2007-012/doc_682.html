Description of the Bird

The bird, larger than a House Finch, smaller than an American Robin, was bright red, except for the black surrounding the red bill and encompassing the eyes. The legs were dark reddish, the eyes were dark. The head was topped by a tall, sharply-pointed crest. The tail was relatively long.
Behaviors: Responded quickly to playback of Northern Cardinal song and calls by flying in to area in front of us and singing.
Call: In response to playback, sang a bright, melodious, "whi-cheeer, whi-cheer, whi-cheer, whi-whi-whi-whi" a few times, with the number of introductory syllables and ending syllables varying during its response.
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

There's nothing really similar to a male Northern Cardinal; Pyrrhuloxia has different plumage coloration and bill shape and color; Vermilion Flycatcher much smaller, differently shaped and patterned, tanagers with different shapes, bill shapes and colors; all of the above have very different songs and probably don't respond to playback of Northern Cardinal song
