Description of the Bird

This is a medium-sized, mostly red bird with a bushy red crest, an orange bill and a black eye, bib, and mask. It is the state bird of my home state, Ohio, in addition to the state bird of six other states. It is difficult to misidentify this species, especially the male.
Behaviors: Singing from an exposed perch in a planted ornamental deciduous tree.
Call: yes, both singing and calling. First heard call notes then I encouraged it to sing and it did.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Vermilion Cardinal of southern South America looks a little like this species, but in North America, only Pyrrhuloxia male has some similar looking plumage. PYrr has a significant amount of gray on its flanks, back, and even on its crest plus its bill is more yellow than orange. There really is not another species that looks like the male Northern Cardinal.
