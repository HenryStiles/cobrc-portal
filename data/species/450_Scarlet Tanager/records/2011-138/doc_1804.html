Description of the Bird

Joe Roller and I were walking west from Valco Ponds parking, when I noticed a tanager land in a large cottonwood tree in the dead branches on top. I quickly noticed that the tanager didn't have any wing bars, also had dark wings, a small, thick and short bill. Also, the bird was greenish yellow overall. The wings and tail were the darker than the yellowish underparts and greenish yellow head. The size and shape of the bird was typical tanager like. Bigger than the small passerines, like warblers, and smaller than a robin. The dark wings with no wing bars and the small bill eliminated other tanagers.
Behaviors: Seen well high up in cottonwood.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Summer Tanager -- much bigger bill, more yellow, instead of yellowish green like this bird. Also, wings not dark like this bird.

Western Tanager -- has noticeable wing bars, which this bird didn't have.

Hepatic Tanager -- bigger gray bill, darker cheeks, wings not dark.

All orioles have thin curved bills and have wing bars.

Painted Bunting and all other buntings are smaller.

Previous Experience

I've seen a few female Scarlet Tanagers, and many female Summer, Western, and Hepatic Tanagers. I have no doubt that this bird was a Scarlet Tanager.
