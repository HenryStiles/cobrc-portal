Description of the Bird

Smaller than a robin, larger than a house sparrow, otherwise typical passerine shape; body completely scarlet with jet black wings and tail; black eye; horn-colored bill. Bill was typical for a "Piranga" tanager, stout like a sparrow, but long like a warbler.
Behaviors: Hopping around the cannopies of small trees while singing.
Call: song a slow burry warble, like a "hoarse robin" similar to Western Tanager
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Summer and Hepatic Tanagers are less brightly colored and lack black tail and wings. Vermillion Flycatcher has similar coloration but behaves differently, is smaller, shaped differently, and sings differently.
