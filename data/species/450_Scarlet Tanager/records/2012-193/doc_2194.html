Description of the Bird

To begin with these are the notes that I took a little less than 30 minutes after I saw the Tanager. I did not have anything with me at the time of observation to record what I saw. Being that I was walking along our canal and was behind the house that is several houses down from us, I could not get home really quickly. As soon as I got home, I recorded these observations. I will rearrange them at the end, so as to provide clarity.

As I was walking along our canal, I came to our neighbors house where there are a lot of grapes. For the past week or so, female Western Tanagers have hung out here feasting on the grapes. As I was watching them I noticed one that was different than all the rest. The beak was a gray color whereas all the others had pinkish bills. Its bill was the same length as the other Tanagers which were all Westerns. I never saw its back straight on, but the view I did get showed a greenish color not a gray like the others. But again, that was not from straight on, that was from a slight angle. The wings were one solid color. It had no wing bars. Across its chest was a grayish/yellow band of color while from there up it was bright yellow and from there down it was a grayish/yellow on the sides and bright yellow down its middle. It was not a Summer Tanager based on its beak size and the fact that its undertail was dark gray, almost a blackish. It was also just slightly smaller than the Western Tanagers which were sitting on the branches around it.

I am going to start rearranging the observations of what I saw. The bill of the bird was a grayish color and was smaller than that of a Summer Tanager. I did not note the color of the top of the head in my immediate notes, and the sighting was far too long ago to remember exact shades of color correctly or without a bias. From what I saw and recorded at the time, the back appeared to be a greenish color not a gray like the Western Tanagers there that day. What I do still remember is that I first saw the bird's front/underside. It was plucking grapes and was turned towards me. As it continued feeding, it turned to where I could see the wings and the side profile. Throughout the time that I watched the bird, it never turned to where I could see the back straight on. I did see it at an angle though. The wings were solidly black with no white or yellow on them at all. The underside of the tail was a grayish/black. This helped eliminate Summer Tanager. From its tail up the underside was yellow in the middle and a grayish/yellow on the sides. On the chest area, this color pattern changed slightly. There was a band going across the chest that was grayish/yellow and from here up was yellow.
Behaviors: It was feeding with other Western Tanagers. I really don't know what behaviors would separate Western Tanagers and Scarlet Tanagers. Overall, it seemed to act the same as the others to me.
Call: I didn't hear a call of a song.
Plumage: Can't quite tell with the time of y

Similar Species Discussion

The lack of wing bars, the grayish bill, and green back all eliminated Western Tanager. The small bill and dark gray underside of the tail eliminated Summer Tanager. The lack of red/orange on the bird eliminated Hepatic Tanager.
