Description of the Bird

The bird was generally the size and shape of a Western Tanager, but perhaps a bit plumper.

The bill was thick, dull orange in color.

The cap, nape, and mantle were a uniform, unstreaked greenish color.

The face and throat were unstreaked dull yellow, with a very narrow white eye ring.

The belly was whitish, and the undertail coverts were pale yellow.

The wings were dark and unmarked (no wingbars), with a large, uniform black patch on the shoulders, and dark gray below the patch.
Behaviors: It would perch in a tree or on a roof for a few minutes, then fly to a different tree or building. It had a roughly horizontal posture - not the vertical posture of a flycatcher.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult nonbreeding

Similar Species Discussion

The size, shape, and posture allows only tanagers, orioles, and perhaps some thrushes (e.g. Gray Catbird). The plumage allows only tanagers and perhaps some orioles, but the thick bill rules out orioles. Among tanagers, only the Scarlet has unmarked black wings on a uniform greenish body.
