Habitat

Open tree stands along the crow creek valley (not much of a valley). Sitting on the top of a Cottonwood tree (I believe that was the tree species).

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Bausch And Lomb 20x-60x Telescope 72mm, Nikon Coolpix 4500, Bausch and Lomb Discoverers (10x42)
Distance:  (estimated) 50ft
Light:  Sun was toward the ESE, while I was looking at the bird from the south. Conditions were clear making for good lighting on top of this tree.

Description of the Bird

The bird was the size of a western tanager, but lacked wingbars. Beak seemed larger than a western tanager by a little bit, but not to the extent that a Summer Tanager would be. The beak color was a yellowy-pale color. Complete eye-ring was evident. Tail was yellow as well as undertail. Back was a dull olivey-yellow that did not contrast much with yellow of the chest and head.
Behaviors: Only saw the bird long enough to snap two photos of it. It was high up in a tree, was not vocalizing, and flew off moments after the second photo was taken.
Call: Silent
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Western Tanager: This bird lacks wing bars which a western tanager would have in all ages. Back color is more olive than grayish, and it did not contrast with the head and chest. Beak was yellow not orangey. Complete eye-ring. The bird was not very contrasting in color on any part of its body. Beak was slightly thicker than expected for Western Tanager.

Summer Tanager: Bird was not large enough to be a summer tanager. Complete Eye-ring rules out summer tanager, as well as the shortness of the beak. Wings were more greenish to gray not yellowy like the rest of the body.
