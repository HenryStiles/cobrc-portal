Habitat

Private grove of trees, mostly elms, located in a very nice setting with many shrubs.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10 by 40 binocs
Distance:  50-100 feet as the bird spent most of its time in the elm canopy
Light:  Good as the bird was opposite the sun, sun southeast and the bird, northwest.

Description of the Bird

Did on a few occasions counter sing with a male Western Tanager which was also present in the same area. We actually we able to locate the bird by its song. We were there a few minutes before the Scarlet Tanager sang and then we were able to locate it.

Similar Species Discussion

Another rare species in our area that could be problematic is the Summer Tanager. This tanager, however, was totally bright red except for the black wings and tail. I remember studying the bill. Summer Tanagers have a large bill as does Hepatic Tanager which is even rarer in western Colorado. This tanager had a rather small bill, much like the bill of a Western Tanager

Resources Used

Most common field guides. Jason Beason was present at the time of viewing the bird and the three of us discussed the field marks.

Previous Experience

Brenda and I have traveled extensively in Central and South America and have seen most of the tanager species. We have seen Western Tanagers locally as well as in winter in Mexico. Hepatic Tanagers, in eastern Colorado and in many places to the south. We have seen a few Summer Tanagers locally and lots of them south.
