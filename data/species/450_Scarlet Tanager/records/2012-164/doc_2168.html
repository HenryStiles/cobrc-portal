Description of the Bird

The bird flushed out of a tree above me and over to a large shrub where it sat in full view for a short period of time. I pulled up my i-phone and snapped the attached picture. It was an adult male bird in almost full alternate plummage. It had an all black tail, all black wings and the rest of the bird was bright red with small bits of greenish/yellow flecking through out as it had not fully transition to breeding plummage. It soon flushed to a nearby tree.
Behaviors: It was a very active bird that was singing loudly. It was moving about quite a bit and did not stay perched in one place singing for extended periods.
Call: The bird was singing enthusiastically. I got out of my car at the Colorado Welcome Center rest area at the Julesburg exit to Hwy 385 off of I-76. The bird was singing non-stop in a tree directly over my head as I exited the car. I admit that I was debating whether I was hearing either Summer or Scarlet when after a minute the bird flew out of the tree and made the distinction for me.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Hepatic Tanager, Summer tanager and Northern Cardinal can be eliminated by lack of black tail and black wings.
