Description of the Bird

A tanager, mostly dusky yellow with charcoal grayish wings, appearing darker than the rest of the bird. Thin wing bar created by paler tips to greater coverts only. Pale edges on tertials. The bird had a greenish-yellow back which isolated the folded wings by color, unlike Western Tanager. Bill was smaller than Summer Tanager and dull.
Behaviors: Sitting and slowly foraging, while eluding 2 other observers.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st fall male possibly adult female

Similar Species Discussion

Western and Summer Tanager. Bill was not large enough and wings were too dark for Summer Tanager. Single narrow wing bar and greenish back eliminated Western Tanager. Concolorous head and auriculars among other marks, eliminates Hepatic Tanager
