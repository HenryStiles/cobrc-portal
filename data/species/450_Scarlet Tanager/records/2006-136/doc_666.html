Description of the Bird

Tanager by body size and body shape; very similar in size and shape to Western Tanager (Piranga ludoviciana). Tanager bill vice an orioles longer black, pointed bill. Back, neck and head all bright olive green; undersides (throat, breast, belly and undertail coverts) bright lemon yellow. Wings dark, not quite black and unmarked, no wingbar(s). Wings contrasted strongly with bright olive back and with the yellow underparts. Tail slightly paler than wings. Eyes black; bill pale.
Behaviors: Feeding in the mid- and upper branches of Plains Cottonwoods and willow.
Call: No vocalizations were heard.
Plumage: fall

Similar Species Discussion

Only tanagers in fall and first fall plumage are considered here.
Summer Tanager: wings do not strongly contrast with back and body. Back color is not greenish in fall like a Scarlet Tanagers
Hepatic Tanager: Back is grayish brown not green like this bird. Underparts not lemon yellow.
Western Tanager: one or more wingbars present. Back is grayish not green like a Scarlet Tanagers.
