Description of the Bird

The bird was the same size and shape as the Western Tanagers in the area, but bright red, with solid black wings and a pale bill.
Behaviors: The bird was foraging and occasionally calling and singing from exposed perches. It was seen flying after female Western Tanagers and fighting with male Western Tanagers, so it was territorial and apparently interested in mating.
Call: The bird sang a song almost indistinguishable from that of the Western Tanagers around it. It also gave the characteristic "tick-burr" call, which is much different than the classic Western Tanager call.
Plumage: alternate
