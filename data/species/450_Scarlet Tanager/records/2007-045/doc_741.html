Description of the Bird

This bird was strikingly red with black wings including wing coverts and tail. The wings had no signs of any wingbars. The bill appeared at times yellowish gray, but out of direct sunlight, the bill appeared medium gray. On the birds right side breast and belly, there were some yellow feathers mixed amongst the red ones. The legs were darkish gray. The eyes appeared large on the bird's head and they were dark. In my photos I noticed that this bird lacked the blackish eyebrows of a full alternate plumaged bird. The bird also had white underwings. The maxillae appears to have a distinct tooth, that is characteristic of this species.
Behaviors: This bird was actively defending its territory against a male Western Tanager and cavorting with a female Piranga Tanager. I am afraid that I didn't study the female tanager as much as I should, forgetting that hatch year and second year female Scarlet Tanagers can rarely show yellowish wingbars. I did notice the it had thin yellowish wingbars ( the female) but I didn't study the bill size or anything else that I should have looked at. The bird's territory seemed to be an area about 100 meters by 70 meters on the top of a hill with a drop off to a deep valley on one side of the hill. I saw the Scarlet Tanager chase the WETA at least 10 times and was seen with the female Piranga tanager about 6 times, occassionally entering a ponderosa pine together. This action was repeated about four times, perhaps indicating a future nest site location. This tree was directly on the trail just up and to the right of the second bridge along this Gregory Canyon Trail.

I also watched the bird preen for about 12 minutes, sitting near the top of a small diameter, but fairly tall and recently dead tree.

During the chasing sequences the red of the tanager was flashing like it was waving a red flag, noticeable from far away.
Call: yes, Robin-like but a lot rougher and shorter phrases, noticably different from the 3-5 note, abrupt burst given by Western Tanager.
Plumage: 2nd year alternate male

Similar Species Discussion

The other Piranga U.S. tanagers each have wingbars and do not have the solid black wings. The thick gray bill is totally unlike Vermilion Flycatcher's, which has somewhat similar coloration.

The only bird that comes close to this species, is the South American Vermilion Tanager, but that species has a dark blackish bib and more silvery beak. Summer Tanager has more ivory colored beak and more of an orangy red underparts, but its wings are red and not black. Hepatic Tanager is never as red as this bird, has some gray tones on its back and flanks. Its wings are never black like the Boulder bird. Also this bird has a distinctly smaller bill than the other U.S. Piranga species.
