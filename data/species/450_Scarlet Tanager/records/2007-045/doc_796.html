Description of the Bird

The bird was a typical Piranga and was strikingly bright red overall with all black wings and tail. The grayish bill was rather conical, but long and somewhat the mandible was somewhat bulged. The eyes and legs were also black.
Behaviors: Sang periodically from ponderosa pine and douglas-fir stands. Observed perched in trees and chasing nearby female Western Tanager.
Call: Similar to that of an American Robin,but with a hoarser quality.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Male Summer Tanagers are duller red, lack black on wings and tail, and have light colored bills. Western Tanagers have red heads only, with contrasting yellow neck, underparts, and uppertail coverts, and black wings with characteristic wing bar. Hepatic Tanagers are brick red overall, have grayish auriculars, darker bills, and lack black wings and tail.
