Description of the Bird

the bird was pretty much the exact size and shape of nearby Western Tanagers. The bill was fairly short, thick and tapering slightly to a blunt tip, and pale horned color. The legs were black.

The entire body of the bird, other than the wings and tail, were bright scarlet red. The wings and tail were contrasting black.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of it's time singing from the top of large trees; at one point it flew to the same tree from which a Western Tanager was singing, but the two did not seem terribly agitated by the other.
Call: The bird sang for most of the observation, as robin-like song made up of a five-six phrases somewhat more burry and faster than that of a robin, that was virtually indistinguishable from that of Western Tanager.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All similar species were ruled out by the tanager shape and the very distinctive plumage.
