Description of the Bird

All bright red with black tail and wings with some yellow under the wing and at the lower edge of wing on the body. Bill was gray and not thick and heavy and much like the bill of a Western Tanager. The tanager did sing on several occasions. The song was somewhat like a robin's song. On the 25th the tanager spend nearly the entire day in the yard.

Similar Species Discussion

Brenda and I have traveled extensively and have seen most of the tanagers (more than 150 species) in Central and South America. The only likely red tanager species that could occur in western Colorado is Summer and Hepatic. The Summer Tanager has a large, thick bill and red wings and tail. The Hepatic Tanager also lacks the black wings and tail and has some gray about its face. Brenda's photos will help eliminate these to tanager species as well as any other wild vagrant.

Resources Used

We have a good library of bird info and we did do some research on Scarlet Tanager as well as other possible tanager look-a-likes.

Previous Experience

Tanagers are one of our favorite species and in our travels have seen many Summer, Hepatic, and tropical tanagers. This is the 3rd Scarlet Tanager (6 total records) I have seen in western Colorado and they are now all reported to the record committee. Disclaimer!, I did not find any of them, however.
