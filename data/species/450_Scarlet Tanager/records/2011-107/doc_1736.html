Description of the Bird

This was a "tanager" based on bill shape and size. It was bright orange (not red) as described for an orange variant. The wings lacked any wingbars and were blackish. The bird's left wing had a few orange feathes on the median or lesser wing coverts. The back was also orange contrasting with a black lower back and rump. The underparts were also orange although less brightly colorored than the throat, head, and back. The color was dark.
Behaviors: The bird was busily hunting and gathering food. It was seen at times feeding a juvenile that had two wingbars and was therefore presumed to be the hybrid result from the male SCTA x female WETA. The bird also was seen preening after coming up from a small stream where it had been searching for food and feeding its young. The most interesting behavior was that it dove at a small terrier being walked next to the bush where the young bird was being fed. The SCTA flew two feet off the ground towards the dog and the dog nipped at the tanager. The SCTA flew to a nearby ponderosa pine and the juv followed and was fed there.
Call: none detected
Plumage: orange variant

Similar Species Discussion
