Description of the Bird

I found this tanager and thought it looked good for a Scarlet Tanager. I took a lot of photos, and asked for comments from other people who see them a lot.

"From Marshall Iliff":
I agree the bird looks weird, but I think that is mostly due to the wing markings. I have seen Scarlets once or twice with medium to prominent wingbars. I think that is what you have here.

I don't see the bill as large here (it looks thick, but not at all long) and I think the overall coloration (clear lemon yellow and bright green on back and rump) is all Scarlet. The bill size is OK to me and has a nice tooth--typical for Scarlet, and presumably (?) reduced in a hybrid?

Not sure what else to say, except that I don;t see much out of line with Scarlet, that the coloration and structure looks good to me, and that some Scarlets with wing bars can approach this bird, I think.

"From Tony Leukering"
Though some have opined Western x Summer as a solution, I find it hard to believe that a species with entirely yellow wing linings could hybridize with another species with partly-to-mostly yellow wing linings and produce progeny with apparently entirely white wing linings. However, the wing bars may be what send folks to the Western Tanager camp, but those wing bars don't bother me -- I have seen at least two SCTAs with such, and the dark topsides to the wings and the greeny-yellow appearance are also fine for SCTA. The bill is, decidedly, on the large size for SCTA and the size and coloration of the bill certainly suggest Summer Tanager. I would say that if the bird is a hybrid, then Summer x Scarlet makes more sense to me. I have one caveat, however: we only have one pic of the wing linings to work from, and that pic may have the wing linings blown out (overexposed). If that is the case, then wing lining color is much more problematic.
Behaviors: Seen in cottonwoods, also on the ground eating grasshoppers.
