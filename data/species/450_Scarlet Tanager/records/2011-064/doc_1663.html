Description of the Bird

This was an olive-backed bird with a tanager shape and bill. The olive on the back was dark in coloration. The wings were dark but not black and were WITHOUT WINGBARS and contrasted with the back. The wing feathers looked to be edged in green. The shoulder had an area of darker olive green. The tail was dark but not black. The bill, for a tanager was about the same size as the bill of a western. The bill color appeared light gray in my photos.
Behaviors: Fed on the ground near a male Blue Grosbeak. Flushed easily and flew short distances before disappearing.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult female likely

Similar Species Discussion

female Summer Tanager and female Hepatic Tanager come closest in plumage but, this bird had wings that were noticeably darker than the bird's body unlike in the other two mention tanagers. It was more slender and with a smaller bill than SUTA. HETA shows the tooth on the maxillae, not on this bird's bill and it would have dusky auriculars for a HETA that were not present on this bird.
