Description of the Bird

Unmistakable North American seabird, this adult Brown Pelican, with brown body (with dark underwings in flight), white on head and front of neck, oversized dark bill with obvious pouch and yellow eye.   We watched bird who at first was perched, do pouch stretching and then take off to fly above lake then do plunge diving to successfully catch numerous fish, which sometimes took multiple efforts to swallow, with the fish seen "flopping" in the bird's pouch.

Similar Species Discussion

Great Blue herons do not plunge dive. American White Pelican was excluded because the bird was not all white with an orangish bill and pouch.  Lake of gray excluded the improbable juvenile white pelican.  With apologies to Dixon Lanier Merritt, no other North American bird has a "bill that can hold more than his belly can".
