Description of the Bird

At about 9AM 4-5-16 a large bird flew over Lake Beckwith dam. It was larger than largest nearby Canada Geese. Upperparts, thick rounded wings, & short tail were solid gray, with obscure paler interior panels on underwing. Underparts were darker slaty gray.  Long foreneck was white, hind neck was dark brown becoming more chestnut toward nape with a small rufous tuft of feathers at top of nape and rear of crown. Rest of crown &  face were an off-whitish color. A fairly bright yellowish color marked the lores & surrounderd eye with a pale iris.

Large long boat shaped bill had a grayish upper mandible with yellow tip; lower mandible was mostly pale yellow. After catching fish, bird tipped its head skyward to swallow it, & resvealed a solid orange gullet which was prominent, fairly bright, but not as bright as illustration of Pacific form in Sibley 2nd ed.(2014)Guide to Birds. Legs were proportionately very short & blackish as were large webbed feet. The orange gullet, rufous tuft near top of nape suggest Pacific form, but the gray upper mandible more closely resembles Atlantic form. Even adult Brown Pelicans often show variable plumages & colors, so I'm not 100% sure if bird is the Pacific form. 

The bird uttered no sounds.

There are many photos of this bird on the ebird website for Pueblo County; I personally did not take photos. 

Similar Species Discussion

Juvenile White Pelicans often show dusky colors but never the prominent dark nape & underparts of Brown Pelicans. White Pelicans at any age show almost an entirely bright orange bill in contrast to the variable colored bills of Brown Pelicans.
