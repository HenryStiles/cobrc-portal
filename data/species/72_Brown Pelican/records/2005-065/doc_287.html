Description of the Bird

A large waterbird, I first found it flying while I was driving up to the pond at which it had been reported. It was larger than all other waterbirds (ducks, coots, cormorants) on the pond except for a few American White Pelicans (which were obviously larger than the Brown Pelican). It had a very large bill (easily 2.5 times the depth -- front to back -- of the head) with a grayish maxilla and a darker, grayish-olive mandible with a distinct pouch. The face was the same color as the pouch/mandible, the crown, auriculars, and chin were white, and the nape was a medium brown with a warm cast. The rest of the neck was the same color as the nape.

The back and rump were mottled blackish and whitish with the scapulars contrastingly brown. The underparts were dingy, mottled gray and whitish, with darker sides and undertail coverts. The wings were very long, with the inner lesser coverts mottled gray and white, but with most of the rest of the wing coverts brown. The remiges were blackish and the outer half (or so) of the primaries had white or whitish shafts. The tail and legs were dark.
Behaviors: Bird soared and flew around over the pond, occasionally diving head-first into the pond after, presumably, fish.
Call: None heard

Similar Species Discussion
