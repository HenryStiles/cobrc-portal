Description of the Bird

Lark, bulky bird with about a 6-foot wing-span; massive dark grayish bill about 18 inches long, deeper at the base. Belly of the bird was pale gray with a grainy appearance (probably due to very fine mottling). This color was a bit darker on the chest. In flight, the outer flight feathers (primaries) were much darker gray (blackish). The hind neck was chestnut-brown, with a frostly central stripe extending down from the crown; much of the head was bright white, with a lateral white stripe extending down each side of the neck. The throat was a golden yellow color, apparently bare skin, extending down almost half way down the length of the long neck. At the base of the bare skin the neck was entirely chestnut-brown (a ring about 2 inches thick). Below this there was a diamond-shaped off-white/yellowish patch at the top of the chest. The upperparts were pale gray-brown, fairly uniform in color.

Overall shape was typical of a pelican. Size was about 70% the length of adjacent American White Pelicans, and overall, 50-60% of the mass.

Webbed feet were blackish.
Behaviors: Soaring over water; floating on water; standing on a log.
Call: none
Plumage: subadult

Similar Species Discussion

Peruvian Pelican (Pelecanus thagus) would be similar morphologically but would be larger, closer in size to American White Pelican.
