Description of the Bird

Huge bird, shape of a pelican, but smaller then accompanying American White Pelican. Pale grayish brown body, with chestnut brown, long neck except for dark yellow skin on throat bordered by bright white. The white extended up the neck to the head and forecrown. Hind crown and nape was chestnut-brown like hindneck. Huge horn-colored bill.

In flight, flapped constantly but slowly, maintaining an altitude of about 5 feet over the lake water.
Behaviors: Loafing on a mud-spit then flew low over lake
Call: none
Plumage: subadult

Similar Species Discussion

Peruvian Pelican is larger, almost same size as American White Pelican; Great Blue Heron has longer legs, slimmer body and relatively shorter bill
