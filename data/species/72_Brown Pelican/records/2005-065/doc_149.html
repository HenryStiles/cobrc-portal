Documentation of Brown Pelican

Observer Information

Reporter:  C W   
E-mail: 
Other Observers:  None; Solomon (Sol) Miller, a half mile away from where I was. He is a birder and a Ranger at Fossil Creek Reservoir. I called him and was going to tell him that I had found Brown Pelican but he was already seeing it when he got my call.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Brown Pelican
First Date/Time:  6/13/2005
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  5 minutes
County:  Larimer
Specific Location:  Flying above Fossil Creek Reservoir, going from the West to the East along Fossil Creek Reservoir and finally disappeared when it flew far enough East that I couldn't see it anymore.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Flying above Fossil Creek Reservoir (a large body of water that usually has American White Pelicans around).

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 8X32 BN Binoculars
Distance:  estimated 500 feet
Light:  I was to the South of the pelican that was flying over Fossil Creek reservoir, and the sun was to the East of where I was.

Description of the Bird

A very brown bird, slightly smaller than the American White Pelicans it was flying with, had white on the belly and a white on the head, brown on the front and back of the neck, back of the head, back and wings, the tips of the under parts of the wings were slightly darker than the rest of the wing.
Behaviors: Flying (circling) above Fossil Creek Reservoir I never saw it on the ground or on the water. It flew, with 8 American White Pelicans, towards the East (Windsor).
Call: I didn't hear any sound.
Plumage: Alternate 2nd or 3rd year

Similar Species Discussion

American White Pelican: The Brown Pelican was smaller than the 8 American White Pelicans it was with, it had very little white compared to a White Pelican. The pouch on this bird was a darker color rather than orange.
Peruvian Pelican: Peruvian Pelicans are larger than the bird that I saw flying with the White Pelicans.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide to Birds after observation to check on age and subspecies.

Previous Experience

I have seen this species a number of times, but only once (the bird in Windsor) in Colorado. The Brown Pelican I saw today looked a lot like the Brown Pelican I saw in Windsor. I have also photographed adult and Juvenile birds in Texas and Florida. I have no experience with Peruvian Pelicans, just looked at pictures and read a little about them.
