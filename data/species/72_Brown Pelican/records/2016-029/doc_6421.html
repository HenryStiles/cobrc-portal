Description of the Bird

It was an obvious pelecanus species: relatively large bird with a pouch, brownish over most of body with a white head. White extended from proximal end of bill over the crown of the head and onto the nape. There was no visible orange and/or yellow coloration on the bird. The pelican had a dark eye and dark bill. The white head is similar to David Sibley's illustration of a 2nd year bird as shown in Sibley Guide to Birds, 2nd Edition, 2014.

The pelican was loosely associating with the American white pelicans in Cottonwood Marsh. It would swim off by itself for a time, then attempt to join in with the white pelicans as they did their typical circular group fishing. I did not observe the pelican doing any typical dive hunting as is often seen in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. I did not hear the bird vocalize.

Similar Species Discussion

The bird had a pouch and was thus a pelican and obviously not an American white pelican. I did not consider a bird like a Brown booby because it was an obvious pelican to me. I see Brown pelicans in Florida annually.

Resources Used

I used the Sibley Guide to Birds, 2nd edition, in checking on the age of the bird, but did not use any resource to first identify the bird. I did ask another birder who was also observing the bird if he had any doubts about it being a Brown pelican; he said no.

Previous Experience

I see Brown pelicans, Atlantic subspecies, annually in Florida along the Atlantic coast.
