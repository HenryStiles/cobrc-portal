Light:  Weather was light overcast and sun was to our left.

Description of the Bird

A very large dark brown bird with an unmistakable pelican bill and pouch. Crown of head was off-white that extended to back of crown and down the sides of face to just below the lower mandible. The rest of the bird was dark brown with minor shading differences. The hind neck was dark brown.

When flying in the upper wings were uniformly dark brown. The under wings were not observed, as I was above the bird and it guided down to the water surface.
Behaviors: Bird was first seen guiding into a landing on Totten Lake from the northwest. It landed with the feet spread and in front of it. After landing, it rested on the surface and did not feed while I was watching it.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

There are no 'similar' species except the American White Pelican which is all white except for black wing tips including secondaries.
