Description of the Bird

the bird was an obvious pelican, very large in size, with a chunky body, long neck, small head, and exceptionally long bill that was fairly wide. The bird was a little smaller than a nearby American White-Pelican. The bill was pale yellowish or flesh colored, with a gray throat pouch. The legs were dark.

The body of the bird was almost entirely dark brown, but the belly was white. This was mostly obvious in flight. The neck of the bird was the same color brown as the body, as was the entirety of the head except for a small white spot behind the eye. The wings were brown, with a thin but bold white stripe on the underwing, seen in flight.
Behaviors: The bird spent a little time loafing around the edge of the reservoir, and preening. It then flew SW and out of site. In flight it had slow but powerful wingbeats, and flew with it's neck curled against it's body.
Call: none heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than pelicans were ruled out by the very distinctive shape and size. American White-Pelican was ruled out by the brown coloration of the bird, as well as smaller size.
