Description of the Bird

Pelicans are comparatively unmistakable in size shape and flight pattern. Large mostly gray bird with paler almost white underparts. Typical long, flat, pointed pelican type bill, mostly gray tinged with lighter brown.
Behaviors: Lone individual, flying low, fairly fast, realtively flat trajectory. Made a couple of passes. On one pass it was it was followed closely by a Bald Eagle maybe 75-100 yards behind, same flight path and speed but did not attack. A pair of eagles were observed in the area earlier that day.

Finally it settled on the water for a brief period, then moved to the shore for preening. No feeding behaviour was observed.
Call: Silent
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

After having the opportunity to learn more about American White Pelicans earlier this month at the North Park Colony Watch, I reflected on the long ago observation of this bird and shared the photographs with some experts. Based upon the photographs, there is concurrence that this is a juvenile Brown Pelican.

Resources Used

Back in 2006, the reference, COLORADO BIRDS by Robert Andrews & Robert Righter, noted only 7 sightings through 1991, whereupon I convinced myself that it had to be a juvenile Aemerican White Pelican.

Mr. Charles Hundertmark leader of the North Park Colony watch has reviewed the photographs.
