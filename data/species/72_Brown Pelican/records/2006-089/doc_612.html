Description of the Bird

A large, brown bird, with an immense bill, though smaller than neighboring American White Pelicans (at MacIntosh Lake on 11/27). The bird was mostly brownish above, with a brownish neck and chest, and white belly. On the head, there was a distinctive patch of white feathers, mostly auriculars, that enabled me to be sure that the bird at MacIntosh Lake was the same spent much of the late fall in Larimer and Weld counties.

From above, the open wings had brownish coverts and very dark brown remiges. From below, the whitish greater coverts (both primary and secondary) contrasted with the brownish color of the rest of the wing linings and the dark brown remiges, creating a wing stripe extending from the axillars nearly to the leading edge of the wing.
Behaviors: Stretching and sleeping on a tire at Lake Loveland, standing on the beach and flying at MacIntosh Lake.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Juvenal molting to Basic I

Similar Species Discussion

No other bird in the world is at all similar, other than Peruvian Pelican (which is nearly the size of American White Pelican); American White Pelican easily eliminated by size and coloration.
