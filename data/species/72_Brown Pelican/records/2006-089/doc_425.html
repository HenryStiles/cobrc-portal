Description of the Bird

Clearly a pelican. Large bird, larger than a Giant Canada Goose, with an extremely long straight bill with a large skin pouch. Long, rounded, broad wings, though not as broad as the nearby soaring White Pelicans; very short tail; long neck, with bill length approx equal to neck length. Bird mostly dull dirty brown, but white below from lower breast through belly and onto undertail coverts. Tail, upperwing, back, upper breast and neck and head mostly unmarked dirty brown. Fairly large whitish patch right behind eye, possibly making this a second-year bird. Bill and pouch both gunmetal gray, with no contrast between upper and lower mandible or pouch, making this an Atlantic bird, according to Sibley. When soaring, a thin white stripe visible down the center of the wing, parallel to leading and trailing edges, roughly equidistant from them. Rest of underwing brown.
Behaviors: First spotted sitting on the lake. Swam around trying to avoid motorboats. Then took off, soaring over the lake in circles with occasional flaps, rising higher and higher until I lost it several minutes later as it headed SW.
Call: The bird was silent.
Plumage: juvenile or second year

Similar Species Discussion

American White Pelican is white, with black flight feathers and orange bill in all plumages.
