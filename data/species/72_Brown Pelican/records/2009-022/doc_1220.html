Description of the Bird

I first saw this bird flying. It was obviously a pelican based on its very large spatulate bill, ponderous flight, and top-heavy appearance.

The overall coloration of the pelican was brown, not white. The dorsal suface was brown, the bill was dark, not yellowish, the ventral surface of the wings were dark on leading and trailing edges with whitisn in the center.

The belly was gray, the head dark without any yellow as in an adult.
Behaviors: Fly and swim, but it did not plunge-dive or plow through the water with its bill open. When in flight, it was harrassed a couple of times by larger, not Franklin's Gulls.
Call: none
Plumage: Immature

Similar Species Discussion

The only other brown pelican in the Americas is Peruvian Pelican but they are larger than Brown Pelican. The only other expected pelican is the large, orange-bill, big white bird, American White Pelican. There were lots of those around for comparison. This bird was smaller and brown, not white.
