Description of the Bird

The bird had an obvious pelican body and bill.

The bird was substantially larger than adjacent Double-crested Cormorants.

It had a brown back and neck, with a pale belly and breast.

It had a brown cap.

It had a white face. The forehead was white, with flecks of brown.

It had dark eyes.

The bill was dark, but with a pale (dull yellowish) upper ridge.
Behaviors: standing on an island with Double-crested Cormorants
Call: none heard
Plumage: first year

Similar Species Discussion

This ID was quite easy. The large size of the bird (substantially larger than a Double-crested Cormorant) and huge bill quickly identified it as a pelican. The mostly brown body and dark bill ruled out White Pelican.
