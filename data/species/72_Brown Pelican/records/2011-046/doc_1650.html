Description of the Bird

Large brown bird first seen gliding in air with wide wings and large, thick bill. On water the bird was large with a gray-brown body similar in color to a Canada Goose. A long neck was held upright with a long wide bill as long as the neck held doubled down against the neck. The nape of the neck was a rich, dark russet-brown the crown and throat were all white with no yellow. The bill was a dark grayish/blue-black, wide and slightly splayed at the tip.
Behaviors: I first noticed the bird gliding into the only available body of water in the area, a small sewage lagoon only about 500 feet across. The bird rested on the surface for 10 to 15 minutes and was seen flying off about 15 minutes later.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Combination of field marks is unlike any other species: pelican bill rules out gannets, boobys etc.; brown color rules out American White Pelican.
