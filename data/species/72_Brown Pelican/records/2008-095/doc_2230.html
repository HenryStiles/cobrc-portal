Description of the Bird

An all brown pelican aside from underside which was whitish (never truly seen well due to distance). Lighter neck and head than body. Had darker brown flight feathers. Bill was bluish-brown all over. Black or blackish eye. Smaller than surrounding American White Pelicans. Thin neck, pelican-shaped scoop bill, minor hook on the end. Blackish to brownish legs. Short brown tail. Hunched back with peak being behind the legs.
Behaviors: Bird was preening while dipping beak in water then lifting up wings to use enormous beak to preen under the wing and on the body feathers. Stood with body sloping greatly upwards toward the head rather than more horizontal like say a resting duck.
Call: None noted.
Plumage: 1st Year

Similar Species Discussion

American White Pelican (AWPE): This bird was smaller than the surrounding AWPEs. Considered this being an oiled bird, but with its ability to fly and its consistent color plus lighter highlights made me believe this could only be a Brown Pelican.
