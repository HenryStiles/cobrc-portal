Description of the Bird

A very large bird; intermediate in size between a White Pelican and Double-crested Cormorant, that were sitting next it. Huge bill with a large pouch. Upperparts a uniform dark brown, including the wings. Dark eye. White lower chest and belly. Flew with the slow flaps and labored flight of a Pelican.
Behaviors: First seen flying over the water. Then standing on a sandbar, preening a little and resting. Finally, seen taking off and heafing east and then north.
Call: None
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

American White Pelican is almost entirely white, with black wingtips, and is considerably larger.
