Description of the Bird

I was birding alone at the Chico Basin Ranch banding station, when I was looking south into the Willow.  I was looking at some Swainson's Thrushes low to the ground, when I heard "cu cu cu" coming from higher up in the willow.  I quickly looked, and in the open I saw a cuckoo shaped bird, with a black bill and red eye ring face me, with white underparts, brown upperparts and a long tail.  Then I was trying to get my camera up and take a photo, when it flew south to the olives.  I didn't see the bird very long, though by hearing it and seeing important things, I know the bird was a Black-billed Cuckoo.  There were many other eastern migrants present in the area:  Magnolia, Hooded, Mourning Warblers, Red-eyed Vireo, Ovenbirds, Northern Waterthrush, American Redstarts, Least Flycatchers.
