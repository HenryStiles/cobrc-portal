Description of the Bird

This bird caused me quite a bit of angst at first as I have no experience with immature cuckoos. It was a typical Coccyzus cuckoo in size and shape. The underparts from chin to belly (at least) were white. I never got a look at the bird's ventral side and did not see the undertail pattern. Its upperparts were a rich brown color throughout, except for rusty tones about mid-shaft on the outer primaries. These rusty tones were more like what I would expect on a Yellow-billed Cuckoo, although, again, I have no experience with immature cuckoos. The uppertail pattern consisted of a nearly entirely brown coloration throughout except for small, off-white tips. The bill was small and entirely gray, with some bluish-gray tones to the lower mandible.
Behaviors: Perched on an exposed branch for several minutes before flying into understory never to reappear.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Immature

Similar Species Discussion

Others have told me that immature Yellow-billed Cuckoo would have a thinner, duller yellow eyering, and the bill should have some yellow tones to it by this time of year.

Additional Information

Comments: Black-billed cuckoo. I don't think rufous is unusual in hy. – Chris Wood, NY

I think it's a young Black-billed Cuckoo. The extensive pale edges to the wing coverts look better for a Black-billed. From what I understand very young Yellow-billeds can have gray bills but if the photo was taken recently I would expect to see yellow on the bill, if this was a Yellow-billed Cuckoo. Regarding the rufous in the wings, I believe young Black-billeds can show some rufous in the wings. – Bob Hughes, IL

Michael O'Brien believes it to be a BBCU – from Tony Leukering, NJ

Features pointing to BBCU include at least the following:

1) small bill (both length and depth);
2) gray base to mandible; and
3) white fringing to some upperparts feathers and covs.

However, I was very confused by the rufous pp, but then noticed in Pyle: "inner webs of pp warm brown with little or no rufous...." Then I looked at Sibley and noticed that his illustration of juv BBCU has pp more rufous than most of the rest of the wing. That lined things up for me. – Tony Leukering, NJ
