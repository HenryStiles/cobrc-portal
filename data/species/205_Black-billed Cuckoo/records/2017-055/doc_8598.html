Description of the Bird

The cuckoo first flew in apparently in response to my pishing. When it saw the source of the noise, it veered off about, passing me at about 50 feet, about 15 feet off the ground. At that point the sun was to my back, and I was viewing it thru Swarovski 12x50 binoculars. I could see the small black bill, the lack of rufous in the wing, and due to the lighting, the bright red eyering. The bird landed in one of two large cottonwoods about 100-150 feet away. I had the bird in view for 10-20 seconds. 

I approached the tree, eventually standing underneath the canopy near the trunk, before I relocated the cuckoo which was 20-30 feet above me. The underparts were dull whitish, the undertail was dark with two rows of white crescent-shaped "spots" (each row consisted of a one crescent on either side of tail). The small (compared to YBCU) curved all black bill was visible. The bird flew again, after about 15 seconds of good viewing time. I thought it gone.

However, 5-10 minutes later I saw several birds perched high in a dead tree. One was a WEKI, another was a MODO, and the third bird baffled me (facing away). It was long in appearance and its upperparts were dark brown. Its length was roughly that of a Mourning Dove, but it did seem larger overall. As I approached, the bird looked over its shoulder. The small, curved, black bill was again visible as was the red eyering. No rufous/rust was visible in wing. The bird flew once more, not to be seen again

Similar Species Discussion

YBCU - Juv might show limited rust in wing (but still should have some) and mostly (or even all) dark bill, but should still have larger tail spots and would lack red eyering.
