Description of the Bird

We heard the bird for two days before we saw it. Often sounded quite distant,  was sort of ventriloquil, low quick po po po po, pause, po po po po, pause, po po po po repeated over and over. This was the only call we heard it give while it was here. Quite distinct and different from Yellow-billed Cuckoos, which we have had in our yard for the past 4 years, but recogniziable as a cuckoo. 

We did use playback evening of the 10th of June and the bird came in quite close but was very secretive.  It eluded us until it lit in a box elder in front of the house. Slender, around 12" long, off white chin, breast and belly continuing to vent,  long tail with white spots...far less distinct spots than Yellow-billed Cuckoo. Back of bird and tail was overal grayish/tan/brown. Back of tail showed small white tips in flight. Could not distiguish any rufous in wing.  Definitely a cuckoo with slightly hunched posture, decurved ALL BLACK bill, RED ORBITAL eyering. 

Interestingly, on the morning of June 14th the Black-billed Cuckoo was calling from a little after 5:00 am persistently until 7:45 am. At around 9:00 am a Yellow-billed Cuckoo started calling, we were finally able to locate it. Nice to have both call the same morning for comparison.

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Cuckoos have been coming to our yard for the past 4 years. Distinguished by call first. We have heard Yellow-billed Cuckoo many times with many call variants, including:
Ku-ku-ku-ku-ku-kddowl-kddowl
to to to to to
tok tok tok
This was quite different, almost hollow sounding and continuously repeated:
po po po po, pause, po po po po, pause, po po po po...over and over

Yellow-billed cuckoo appears as slightly stockier and long tail has large white tips on black feathers. Yellow-billed has rather stout yellow bill and yellow orbital ring (sometimes appears almost black). Yellow-billed cuckoo has rufous on primaries.
This bird was slimmer, but with that distinct cuckoo shape with  small, distinct white tips to the long tail...less distinct than Yellow-billed. This bird (Black-billed) had a slender, blackish, partly grey bill and RED orbital ring around eye.  No rufous on primaries.

Previous Experience

We have no experience with Black-billed Cuckoo. We have had Yellow-billed Cuckoos coming to our yard for the past 4 years. We had been expecting them and watching,  listening for them. Calls are quite different and distinct.
