Description of the Bird

cuckoo shaped bird, pale brown on upperparts with black eye and bill, red iris, whitish on underparts with pale buffy throat and upper chest; black and white markings under the tail; in flight, a hint of brighter reddish-brown in some flight feathers.
Behaviors: Sitting quietly and motionless on tree limbs, but moving frequently between trees, sometimes flying low to perch on fenceline or tall weeds.
Call: none
Plumage: SY

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Cuckoo juv has a similar dark bill but has much more rufous in the wings and a greenish orbital ring; Mangrove Cuckoo has a bicolored bill, a yellow orbital, and a black mask, and more extensive buffy underparts. Dark-billed Cuckoo of South America lacks the red orbital.
