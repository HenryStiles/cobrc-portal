Habitat

I small riparian area with large cottonwoods, a small creek, with only a couple pools of water in it and open lands in all other directions.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica Ultravid Binoculars
Distance:  At closest about 20 feet
Light:  We saw it at several different angles and the sun was low towards the east

Description of the Bird

We saw the bird fly in. We noticed an off white belly and a brown back, tail, and wings. We didn’t notice any rufous on the wings. We got a good look as it sat for a long time. We saw a long tail with small spots on the underside. It had black legs and eye with a red orbital ring. It had a small bill with a black upper mandible and gray lower mandible.
Behaviors: It flew close to us from the south and sat in a tree for a while allowing for several photos, it then flew to a fence line and sat there for about 45 seconds, it then flew back south.
Call: I never heard it
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

In flight the lack of rufous in the wings eliminated Yellow-billed Cuckoo and the color of the belly eliminated Mangrove. While sitting the small dark bill eliminated both Yellow-billed and Mangrove Cuckoos.
