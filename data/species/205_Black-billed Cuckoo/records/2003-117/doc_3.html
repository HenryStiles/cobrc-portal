Description of the Bird

Wet and a bit bedraggled, the bird gave an overall slender appearance. Generally, a medium-sized passerine with a long, slender tail. The bird was mostly seen from underneath, and clean white underparts dominated the body. The long tail was dark gray underneath, with only minimal pale tips (juvenile trait or very worn) The feet of the bird were dark. The fairly long, gently curving bill was completely dark gray, darker on the maxilla. The throat was pale, with a gray cap dividing the head about in half. The bottom of the eye was about at the bottom of the dark cap. The iris was dark, with a red orbital ring surrounding it (adult feature perhaps this bird was in transitional plumage.) The upper parts visible looked dark brown or grayish brown (good looks at the back weren't possible.) No contrasting marks were noted in the wings. Probably everything but the white underparts looked darker on the soggy bird under those conditions.
Behaviors: During observation the bird was just sitting there, waiting out the rainstorm.
Call: n/a
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

For North American Cuckoos, both Mangrove and Yellow-billed Cuckoos would show yellow in themandibles. Also, both should show extensive white in the tail undersides, in all plumages. This bird had basically no white under thetail. Finally, neither Mangrove or Yellow-billed Cuckoos should have red orbital rings, as this bird did.
