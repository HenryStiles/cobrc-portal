Description of the Bird

Shape and size of bird indicated one of the Coccyzus cuckoos. Dark gray to black bill , white underparts and dark brown upperparts and wings . Long tail was in a position that could not see any of the lower or upper surfaces. Reviewing my photos later , shows a greenish ( not yellow or red ) orbital ring and slight rufous tinge to some of the secondary coverts but nothing as extensive that you would expect in YBCU.
Behaviors: Perched lengthwise on cottonwood branch. Then flew off into marsh .
Call: None heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Cuckoo has a yellow bill and rufous in primaries and yellow orbital ring. Mangrove Cuckoo has a black mask , yellow orbital ring , and buffy underparts.
