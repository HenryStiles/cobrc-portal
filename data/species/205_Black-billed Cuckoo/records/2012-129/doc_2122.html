Habitat

Cattail and bullrush marsh at edge of a small lake and also a narrow riparian area with mature plains cottonwoods and a few remaining Russian olives that produces a lot of fruits. Also, giant sunflowers, not the roadside one, but a species that grows in and at the edge of the marsh (12 feet tall) where most of the birds were foraging this cold day.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x42 Leica Ultravid bins
Distance:  150-200 feet and a little closer when it flew away
Light:  Fair, looking into a gray sky behind the bird. Overcast and cool.

Description of the Bird

I saw this bird first in flight coming OUT OF A MARSH. In flight it had the easily recongizable shape of a cuckoo. I yelled "cuckoo" to the group I was birding with.

when perched I looked for an saw the bill was two-toned, dark at the distal 1/2 and gray at the base. It appeared to be thin. I looked at the orbit and it appeared to be greenish and narrow. The eye was dark. The tail was very long. The wings were brown with hints of rufous and there were buff edges on the wing coverts. In flight I looked for rufous wing patches and did not see any.
Behaviors: First flying toward a grove of trees and then landing and then flying back to another tree grove over a 60 second time period.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st year

Similar Species Discussion

The only other N.A. north of MX, cuckoo to consider is Yellow-billed Cuckoo. Juvenille Yellow-billeds do have a grayish bill but that changes to yellow by August. Their bills are noticeably stouter than BBCU bills and in flight they show obvious large rufous patches on their wings, lacking in this bird. The large white tail patches of YBCU should also have been noticeable the two times this bird was observed in flight.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Very little with BBCU, I saw and adult in Ohio in May, but I have seen at least 50 YBCUs in a few states including CO, NM, and AZ.
