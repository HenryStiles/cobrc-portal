Description of the Bird

Bird was medium size, smaller than a Black-billed Magpie but similar shape. The bird had a medium long, all back bill that was curved downward. The back was all medium brown from head to tail. Underneath the bird was off white from the chin to the base of the tail with the chin and throat less white. The long tail was gray with alternating narrow light gray bars or spots and then wider dark gray bars. The eyes, in good light, were reddish brown and surrounded by a noticable orange-buff eyering. The legs and feet were dark. The bird was preening so good view of the wings both upper and lower, showed consistent brown color, somewhat lighter on the underside. The feathers on the back and wings were edged buff to whitish.
Behaviors: I first notice the bird sitting in some brush with its head sticking out. I looked at it with my binoculars and did not know for sure what it was. Then it pop up and flew across the road and landed in a Russian olive tree. As good luck would have it the tree was near the road the the bird did not leave the tree for the next 30 minutes. The bird preened and would eat an occasional berry. At this point we, Coen was with me, knew it was a cuckoo. All field marks pointed to a Black-billed Cuckoo. We needed to get photos so Coen walked back home 1/2 mile away to get the camera. The cuckoo was still in the same tree until Coen brought me the camera. As soon as I tried to take photos the cuckoo began moved to the other side of the tree and therefore, the photos are not that great. I did have about 1/2 hour to study the bird at close range while sitting along the road waiting for the camera. That gave me a great chance to study field marks and as soon as the bird flew on we discussed what we had seen. In a few minutes after returning home we put the photos on the computer and again talked about the photos and the bird.
Call: None heard
Plumage: First fall plumage

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Cuckoo has a yellow lower bill. The tail would have large white tail spots. Our cuckoo looked to be a hatch year bird so we needed more field marks. The eyering was buff orange which is right for a Black-billed Cuckoo. Also the buffy edging of the wings is a good clue which clearly shows in the photo. I saw the cuckoo fly three times and watched it preen. I never saw any rufous in the wings.

Resources Used

We have a library of bird books and consulted most of them plus we looked at many of the photos that can be found on the web. We even have the 16 volume set of Handbook of the Birds of the World. The reseach has been going on for the last 6 months every now and then when we think about the cuckoo.

Previous Experience

We have seen many cuckoos both BBCU and YBCU in Nebraska, along the Missouri and Mississippi rivers, in Minnesota and in Texas during migration. My last BBCU experience was at Puerto Maldonato in Peru near the Bolivian border on 30 November 2012.
