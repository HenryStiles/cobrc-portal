Description of the Bird

Cuckoo size or about the size of a Western Scrub-Jay which is a common species in Nucla. Slender bird with a long tail and medium to long bill which was down-curved. The bird was uniform brown for head to tail on the top side. The underneath side was white to off white from bill to undertail coverts. The underneath side of the tail was gray with indistinct markings. The eye was reddish, brown with a buffy, orange eyering. The bill was slim, down curved and completely dark brown.
Behaviors: The bird was sitting in a shrub with just its head sticking out. Then if flew accoss the road to a Russian olive tree where is perched for 30 minutes eating an occasional Russian olive berry. Brenda and I watch the bird from all angles. After a few minutes I decided we should get a camera and I walked home 1/2 mile and then drove back with Brenda's camera. Brenda studied the bird while I was gone as it stayed in the same tree. Brenda did get some photos before the bird flew on to another olive tree.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Cuckoo which does occur around Nucla is the only species that is problematic. Our bird has a slim, completely black bill, both mandibles. The tail spots were not distinct on the underneath side side which suggests a juvenile. The back and wings feathers were edged which is noticeable in the photos which points to a BBCU

Resources Used

I used our complete collection of bird info. It has been over 30 years since a BBCU has been reported from western Colorado. The other two westslope reports have incomplete info so this is record is important to us on the west side.

Previous Experience
