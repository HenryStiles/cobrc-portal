Habitat

The habitat here is the typical riparian found in Eastern Colorado with large cottonwoods dominating.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Bushnell 10 X 50 Binoculars
Distance:  as close as 30 ft
Light:  The sky was mostly sunny and the bird was intermittently in full sun and shade.

Description of the Bird

This bird was the overall size and shape of a Yellow-billed Cuckoo. The bird was about 12 in. long.
Upper parts: The crown, back, upper wings and upper tail were generally a uniform, grayish brown.
Bill: Noticeably curved, sharp and all black.
Face: Grayish with a red orbital ring clearly visible around the eye.
Under parts: Throat, breast and belly clean white.
Tail: Long and graduated. Grayish brown above slightly tipped in white (seen when tail was spread). Grayish brown below with small, white ends to the feathers
Behaviors: The bird was very curious at my whistled imitations of it’s call and came out into the open for some excellent views. Most of the time, however, it stayed in the lower branches of cottonwoods or in the under story.
Call: A repeated “cu cu cu” along with other occasional guttural croaking calls.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Cuckoo has rufous primaries, a yellow bill, a yellow orbital ring and a black under tail with large white spots. The call/song is mor gutteral and drawn out, not the short three cu cu cu notes of the Black-billed.
