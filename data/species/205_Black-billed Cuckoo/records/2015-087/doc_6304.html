Description of the Bird

I'm submitting this sighting per Tony Leukering's request after he saw it on eBird. Location was Prewitt Reservoir Inlet Canal.
First binocular sighting showed very little white under tail, almost all dark brownish/grayish with small white specs vs. four Yellow-billed Cuckoos viewed in immediate area and time frame, which had large white patches under their tail making it look basically white in flight. No rufous coloration on wings but I didn't realize in the field that this was an ID factor until I compared it to the four Yellow-billed Cuckoos which had prominent rufous coloration. My bird appeared slightly smaller/thinner than the Yellow-billed Cuckoos I saw, most importantly the bill was notably smaller and thinner than the Yellow-billed Cuckoos which appeared to have massive bills in comparison. Bill on my bird was all dark gray or all black, I called it all black in the field with the bird in the shade of the tree and then I was able to see it in better light and it appeared all dark gray to black. Bird was silent during my observation. At home with my Sibley book I ruled out juvenile Yellow-billed Cuckoo due to very little white under tail, no rufous on wings, and smaller thinner all dark gray/black bill. Based on undertail and bill size I would lean towards juvenile Black-billed Cuckoo but I'm not positive, it could have been an adult Black-billed Cuckoo, this was my first experience with this species. I described the bird to Steve Mlodinow via e-mail, he sent me a photo of a juvenile Yellow-billed Cuckoo to help me possibly identify my bird as same, or further rule out juvenile Yellow-billed Cuckoo. The comparison of his photo to what I saw helped me rule out juvenile Yellow-billed Cuckoo. I have one poor photo which I'll attach or embed if I can figure out how, otherwise I'll e-mail it to Tony per his suggestion.

I'm sure of my sighting however I'd make the point that being sure can be different than being accurate with an intermediate birder vs. a highly skilled one. I'm in my sixth year with 367 species identified and feel good about this ID but I've been corrected many times as I develop my ID skills so I'm glad Tony asked me to submit this observation.

Similar Species Discussion

Four Yellow-billed Cuckoo viewed immediately afterward. Massive yellow bills, prominent rufuous coloration on wings, very large white spots under tail, and appeared to be slightly larger/blockier overall.

The important question I asked myself was why wasn't my sighting a juvenile Yellow-billed Cuckoo? Even though juv YB Cuckoo has a smaller, thinner bill than adult, the photo I saw of a juv YB Cuckoo had a longer and thicker bill than the bird I saw. But the most important factor was very little white under the tail, almost appearing as small white spots or specks, ruled out juv YB Cuckoo to me.
