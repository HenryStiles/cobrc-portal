Description of the Bird

This was a medium sized bird, about the height of a Flicker but much thinner.  It had a warm brown colored back with a light or off white front.  It had a long tall and decurved, black bill.  It was obvious right away that it was a cuckoo.  The bird didn't call.  It perched for awhile on a bare branch and then flew off to another tree maybe thirty or forty feet away and I was not able to relocate it. It had a black/gray bill and what appeared to be a yellowish green or greenish yellow orbital ring.  I also detected a bit of rufous in the wings.  I never got a look or photograph of the underside of the tail. 
It is definitely a young bird, as an adult Yellow-billed would have a yellow bill and the adult Black-billed would have a red orbital ring.
Young Black-billed Cuckoos will have the hint of rufous in the wings and the blackish gray bill.  Also the orbital ring is the right color for Black-billed.  The throat is also quite buffy, as seen in the photographs.

Similar Species Discussion

Main differences of this bird from a Yellow-billed Cuckoo would be the buffy throat and lack of a clear delineation of color between the throat and the nape.  On a YBCU, there would be a stark and clear contrast between a white throat and the brown of the nape.
The black bill is only seen on very young YBCUs and the orbital ring is the right color for BBCU.  The rufous in the wings fits in with a young Black-billed, as well.  Just for kicks, you can rule out Mangrove Cuckoo due to the all dark bill and lack of buff on the belly of the bird.
