Habitat

Bird was in the taller cottonwoods on the far side of the marsh south of the road. It may have been calling from the mature cottonwood forest with little underbrush, or from the second-growth cottonwood edge, which was denser and closer.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  N/A
Distance:  probably 100 feet
Light:  Mostly sunny.

Description of the Bird

Bird was heard only.
Behaviors: Bird was heard only.
Call: Bird--presumably an unpaired male--was singing the primary song, a low-pitched "cu-cu-cu."
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Least Bittern sounds somewhat similar, and was my original thought, seeing as how there was a marsh in between me and the cottonwoods. However, I had heard and recorded a Least Bittern at Walden Ponds in Boulder the day before, and soon realized that this was a different sound: less regular in rhythm and length; softer; different in tone, without the hoarser, deeper noise components of Least Bittern.

Resources Used

I recorded the sound as soon as I realized what I was hearing, then immediately played it back for myself and compared it to the tracks of Least Bittern and Black-billed Cuckoo on the Peterson Western CD.

Previous Experience

I had heard and recorded a Least Bittern the day before in Boulder. I had heard Black-billed Cuckoo once or twice in Northern Minnesota, but not for several years.
