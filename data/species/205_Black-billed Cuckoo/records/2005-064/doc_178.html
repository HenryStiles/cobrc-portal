Description of the Bird

I had been birding Bonny Reservoir State Park and the adjacent state wildlife area during the early morning of July 9, 2005. I stopped along the gravel road within the state wildlife area roughly 0.5 miles west of Foster Grove Campground. The stop was fruitful as I picked up Great Crested Flycatcher and Red-bellied Woodpecker calling from the cottonwoods to the south of the road. Abruptly, I heard the distinctive song (call) of a Black-billed Cuckoo, a series of roughly five same-pitched, deep notes of cu-cu-cu-cu-cu with a brief pause between each set. Having spent a lot of time listening to Black-billed Cuckoos, I immediately thought of that species, but wanted to make sure. I walked into the cottonwoods and after a few minutes of trailing after the call, I found the bird perched in a cottonwood sapling approximately 60 feet away. The bird was longer than an American Robin and had a long, graduated tail. The bird had a slender appearance. The bill was short and slightly decurved and was all black. The upperparts were brown, which extended through the bottom of the eye posteriorally through the wings and tail. Though the eye appeared black, there was a noticeable red orbital ring surrounding it. The underparts were plain white. As the bird was facing me, I noted that the tail from beneath was mostly a dark brown, except that each rectrice had a small black-and-white tip to them. After observing the bird for a short bit, the bird took flight towards the south. In flight, the upperwings appeared all brown and lacked any cinnamon-rufous tones.

It was following posting the observation of this bird that I heard from Nathan Pieplow that he also had a Black-billed Cuckoo at this location a few weeks prior to my observation.
Behaviors: Bird was calling occasionally from a small cottonwood tree. It was perched in a quite vertical stance. I also observed it in flight.
Call: A series of roughly five same-pitched, deep notes of cu-cu-cu-cu-cu with a brief pause between each set.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Cuckoo is the closest congener. Yellow-billed Cuckoo differs by usually having a yellow maxilla (though juveniles can appear all dark in that region) a yellow orbital ring, has cinnamon-rufous tones to the primaries, and has broad white tips to the undersides of black rectrices. The song of a Yellow-billed Cuckoo also differs significantly, which generally starts as a dry rattle that slows to a ka ka ka ka ka ka
