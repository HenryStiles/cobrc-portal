Description of the Bird

I am submitting this observation at the request of Bill Schmoker following a record I submitted to eBird. Details as follows.

Observation date: Sep 12, 2012
Location: RMNP, Trail Ridge Road, Alpine Visitor Center, Larimer, US-CO
Location: 40.4411335,-105.7550383

I was walking on the trail from the alpine visitor's center with some colleagues. The trail is at ca. 12,000 ft and in an alpine habitats. We observed a soaring broad-winged raptor coming along the ridge, and I observed the bird for 5 to 10 minutes at ca. 200 m with Zeiss 10x40 binoculars. The bird periodically stopped to hover along the ridge in a light wind. My overall impression of size and shape was relatively narrow wings and tail. I only saw the underside of the bird, and observed black patches at wrists on underside of wing, and patterning on the breast. I did not see the dorsal surface of the bird and was unable to confirm whether the white patch on the rump.

The bird was too small and the wing beats were too fast for it to be a golden eagle or a bald eagle. I have seen red-tailed hawks hover in strong winds but the shape of the bird was more slender than what I would expect for a red-tail. Based on the combination of hovering, black wrists, and slender Buteo form, I identified the bird as a Rough-legged Hawk. I'm familiar with the species, see it regularly in Kansas in the winter and at my field sites in western Alaska in the spring.

I regret that I do not have any photos for documentation, I have not birded in Colorado before and did not recognize the sighting would be unusual. Early records of Rough-legs in Kansas include 20 September according to Thompson and Ely 1989 Birds in Kansas.
Behaviors: Soaring broad-winged Buteo that frequently switched back and forth from soaring to hovering.
Call: No vocalizations
