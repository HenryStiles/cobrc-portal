Description of the Bird

The bird had the shape of a medium sized Tern: larger than Forster's or Common, but smaller than Caspian or Royal. It had a complete black cap, and black eyes and legs. The bill was quite long: mostly black but with a distinctly yellow tip. The face, neck, breast and underparts were white. The back and wings were a very light shade of gray. The outer few primaries were dark/blackish, which was much more extensive on the upper wing than seen from below. The bird had a very strongly forked and white tail.
Behavior: Flying over ponds and a small reservoir. Diving into the surface of the water, and often catching fish in a typical tern fashion.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other medium to large North American terns have red (possibly orange/red) bills in alternate plumage.
Bird was too large to be one of smaller Sterna terns.
