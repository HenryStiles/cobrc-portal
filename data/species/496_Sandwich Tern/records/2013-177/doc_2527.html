Description of the Bird

Largish Sterna tern whose main diagnostic feature as a long, thin black bill with prominent yellow tip. Otherwise, as with other Sterna terns, body plumage basically white with light gray upperparts and wings. The tern sported a solid black cap that extended from the base of the upper mandible, in line with the eye, to the hindcrown. I observed the bird only in flight, so did not notice whether it had a small crest or not. Secondaries and inner primaries were bright white. Outer primaries were dusky gray with blackish edging to the outermost five primaries (as counted in the occupying photo).
Behavior: Observed foraging at beaver pond, plunge diving a couple times otherwise flying with powerful wingstrokes modestly high above the water.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Prominent yellow tip to black bill rules out all other species.
