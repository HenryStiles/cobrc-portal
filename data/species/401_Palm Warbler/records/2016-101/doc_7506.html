Description of the Bird

Small, yellowish, long legged, long tailed warbler. Yellowish all over, dullest on the back, brightest on the undertail coverts. Slight streaking on the breast. Thin (and difficult to see) yellowish wingbars. Long yellow supercilium, yellow throat, dark eyeline, brown cheeks, and a hint of rufous on the cap. The underside of the tail had diagnostic white square on the end and black base. Bobbed tail often. Spent most of its time foraging on a large bare patch of ground (it had snowed the day before) with juncos. Otherwise, it foraged in low bushes/trees. Often called its loud Chink! call. 

Similar Species Discussion

Identified to warbler by thin bill, yellow all over, active habits, different posture, small size, and loud chink! call. Told from most warblers by distinctive facial pattern (long supercilium, dark eyeline, etc) pumping tail habits, foraging behavior (foraging on the ground), and yellow undertail coverts. Told from Pine Warbler by yellow undertail coverts, dull wingbars, and different face pattern. from Prairie Warbler by long supercilium, bright yellow undertail, and different tail pattern. From Yellow-rumped by yellow all over, tail pumping, and different call.

Resources Used

The Sibley guide to birds: Second edition, The Warbler Guide, and National Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America: sixth edition.

Previous Experience

This was a new bird for me, but I could immediately tell because of the tail pumping behavior and long supercilium, as well as other minor details. When I first heard the call I didn't recognized it, but knew it was someting rare. And when it landed on the ground I knew it was a Palm Warbler. I watched it over the next 80 minutes with field guides and pictures, so I'm certain of the Id.
