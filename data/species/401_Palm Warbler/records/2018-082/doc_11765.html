Description of the Bird

An obvious Palm Warbler -- brown crown, pale supercilium, dark eyeline, thin white eye arcs, dark legs, bright yellow under-tail covs, large white tail spots on the r6s giving a suggestion of white tail corners; tail was pumped frequently -- that was extensively yellow below from bill to tail.

Similar Species Discussion

A few yellow species of warblers might be superficially similar, but plumage features and tail-pumping behavior rule all such out. The only real ID concern is that to subspecies.  The bird was not as bright yellow below as the limits imposed by other birders.  However, my extensive experience with the form in New York, New Jersey, and Florida lead me to believe that this bird (pix provided) fits comfortably within hypochrysea, given the wide variation in plumage appearance accorded to the nominate subspecies.  While I understand that an intergrade may well be more likely in Colorado than is hypochrysea, this report fits quite well in the established occurrence pattern in Colorado.  Links to Macaulay Library pix of Yellow Palm Warbler from Nova Scotia (where Yellow is much more likely than is Western):

Resources Used

I immediately identified the bird as a Yellow Palm in the field.  A few days later, I studied many pix of YPWA in the Macaulay Library archive.

Previous Experience

I have seen >3000 Yellow Palm Warblers in various Eastern Seaboard states, particularly during spring and fall in Cape May.  I have seen >20,000 Western Palm Warblers in a variety of US states, particularly Florida, Michigan, and New Jersey.
