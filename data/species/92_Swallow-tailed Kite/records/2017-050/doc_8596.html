Description of the Bird

First seen by Bill Maynard from the Lamar swim pool parking lot briefly in flight looking north , appeared to land in a tree along 3rd street . Group walked north along 3rd street where it was spotted resting in a bare tree . Many photographs taken in the course of the hour of observation , spent much of the time preening . Was not disturbed by lawn mowing , weed whacking in the yard adjacent to the tree , nor the many vehicles passing by or our walk past on the other side of the street .The bird was still in the same tree preening when the group walked away at 9:15 am .Obvious Swallow-tailed Kite by profile , see photos . The buffy streaking on head and upper breast indicates that this a juvenile born this year , Sibley indicates that this juvenile buffy streaking is present for only 9 weeks after fledgling .
