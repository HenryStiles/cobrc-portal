Habitat

bird was moving around in an area of about 100m x 300m (7 acres), which is in agreement with a citation in the BNA account (Tenney, 1997) of spacing for singing BCSP (4-10 acres). Site description: sandy/gravelly wash at elevation ca. 4950 ft, in a canyon 150m wide, rimmed by sandstone formations. Vegetation in the area includes sage species, scattered juniper, four-wing saltbush, serviceberry, greasewood, rabbitbrush, squawbush, prickly pear, Mormon tea, single leaf ash (bird was often perched up at 10-15 ft singing in the larger of these that were present), willow species lower in the wash, some pinon and Fremont cottonwoods were nearby on canyon slopes, lots of interesting native bunch grasses. Observers on a couple of different dates noted a second BCSP.
Area is located in a BBA II priority block (per Coen). CNM personnel are aware of the bird’s presence and are interested in our observations (possible breeding, in particular).

Description of the Bird

BCSP description – Devils Kitchen Trail at Colorado National Monument, Mesa County CO, 09 April 2012

One singing bird was encountered 09 Apr 2012 on the same territory as last year (09 Apr – 18 Jun 2011, m.ob, photos, accepted by CBRC). Bird was out in the open about ten feet off the ground in a tallish shrub (no leaves yet), moving to eye level at times, all gray head and breast, not streaky, a small amount of black around the base of the bill and downward onto the throat area (but the most recent time i saw it, wil Joe Mammoser, there appeared to be a larger area of black on the face), back was brown and streaky. Superficially junco-like in appearance but no obviously white belly, and NO white on the outer rectrices when it moved around (eliminating all junco plumages). Smallish bill was not gray or black but I am color-challenged so can not name the bill color without a close-up view in a spotting scope. The habitat (geography and vegetation) of this area were described last year, and is available on request. NOTE - one bird was seen again near this same area, on GVAS Spring Count by a very exoerienced birder, Brett Walker. One bird was seen on 09 Apr, but two birds were seen on two other occasions since then, 3 observers on one occasion, 2 observers on another occasion. I know of at least 14 observers, including a group of Brits with Dick Filby. First known sighting this year was 09 Apr, and most recent was 12 May. I have two fairly good quality photos to share with CBRC if i can figure out how to inclde them. i might need to simply forward to Faulkner or somebody?
Behaviors: fairly shy, except when singing or chasing or being chased by BTSP, JUTI, etc
Call: same as all of my recordings, very clear, bouncing ball (accelerating)... Song was conspicuous, as bird was only 15m away and easily viewable with sun almost directly behind me. Song consisted of high sharp, slurred notes accelerating into a rapid trill not unlike a Field Sparrow, but was higher and more mechanical than the latter, a “bouncing Ping-Pong ball” sort of song.
Plumage: breeding
