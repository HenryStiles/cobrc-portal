Description of the Bird

Size and shape indicated a sparrow with a long tail . Had a dark gray head and upper breast tapering into a whitish belly , black throat and black just above the small pink bill . Brownish back streaked black and brownish wings.
Behaviors: Singing from exposed perches on juniper or sage bushes. Would disappear for 10 minutes or so before returning to sing. Moved 100 to 150 yards from song post to song post . Did observe feeding seeds from grasses at base of dead juniper bush and then carry and place dead grass into base of an adjacent sage bush ( confirmed breeding , NB ).
Call: Song had some similarities to a Field Sparrow ,but not as musical. Song matched the song in Bird-jam ( Stokes ) on my I-phone.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other sparrow has dark gray head and body with black on chin. Dark-eyed Junco has extensive white in tail which this bird did not show in flight.
