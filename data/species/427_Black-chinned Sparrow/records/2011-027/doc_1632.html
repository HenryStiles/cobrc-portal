Habitat

A dry ravine with scattered cottonwood and ash in the gullies, mostly sagebrush and juniper on the slopes, with many large boulders and cliff faces in the immediate vicinity. This ravine is relative flat-bottomed and open, with more sage and less juniper than the surrounding hillsides.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Eagle Optics binoculars
Distance:  approximately 30 feet at closest approach
Light:  It was mostly sunny, and the bird was observed in direct sunlight from various angles.

Description of the Bird

The bird was a sparrow, about the size and shape of a Chipping Sparrow, with a notably long tail that had a shallow fork. It had a small, conical bill. Its face was black on a grayish background that covered the head, the sides of the underparts, and the rump. The back was brown; I could not see if it was streaked, and I did not see the wings well. The center of the belly was the lightest part of the underparts, nearly white. The bill was bright pink, contrasting with the black face. The border between black face and gray head was kind of diffuse rather than crisp.
Behaviors: The bird would tee up and sing from exposed perches at the top of short juniper or single-leaf ash trees for several minutes at a time, then fly to another perch and sing some more.
Call: The bird sang frequently. It had two songtypes, like most Black-chinned Sparrows. Both were accelerating series that started with slow, clear, slurred whistles and ended with a toneless buzz with the elements repeated way too fast to count. The song is extremely distinctive; only a few other birds in North America sing accelerating trills (notable Field Sparrow, Rufous-winged Sparrow, and Wrentit), and none of them start musical and end with a mechanical buzz.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Black-throated Sparrows were in the same area for direct comparision, but they have bold white stripes on the face and a black bib that contrasts with white breast. They also have an entirely different song. Black-throated Sparrow more closely resembles a junco -- and indeed I saw a Pink-sided Junco at the same location -- but the song again is diagnostic, and no junco has a black facial patch.
