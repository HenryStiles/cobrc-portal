Description of the Bird

This was a smallish sparrow, very similar in size to the Black-throated Sparrows that were also singing. The head, neck and breast were uniformly gray, except for the black on the throat which extended thinly all around the bill. The bill was a bright pink, which contrasted strongly with the dark head of the bird. The eye was dark. The wings were uniformly brown with small whitish edgings, but there were no wing bars at all. The tail was uniformly dark. The legs were not black, but were not bright either.
Behaviors: We found the bird as it was singing from the top of a bush. It sang periodically, but not continuously. It seemed to be foraging a little when not singing. At one point, it landed in a bush where a Black-throated Sparrow was singing. The B-t Sparrow chased the B-c Sparrow out of the bush. After about 10am, the bird stopped singing, and I didn't see it after that.
Call: The bird sang a song that was the same as we had just listened to on a recording as we left the car. This is how we were alerted to the bird's presence.
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

There is really no other Sparrow like this: it is not really light brown anywhere, and it has no red or white on it at all. Black-throated Sparrow has a large black patch and white stripes. Harris's Sparrow looks a little similar, but in breeding plumage has more extensive black, white wing bars and a white breast.

Resources Used

Listened to a tape recording of song when left the car. Did not use a field guide when looking at bird.

Previous Experience

I have seen this species only a few times before first in New Mexico Sandia Mtns and then in Arizona. I have seen all Colorado's sparrows very frequently.
