Light:  Sunny, light winds. The bird was observed at many diffent angles and positions relative to the sun.

Description of the Bird

The bird was a dark sparrow type bird of similar size to the near by Black-throated sparrows. Its head ,nape, neck and chest were a medium gray. The gray of the belly continued to lighten into the white of the undertail coverts. The lores and throat were black as well as the eyes. the bill was conical and pale (pinkish-orange). The mantle feathers were light brown with central dark brown stripes. The feathers of the wings and tail were dark brown with pale buff edges. The rump was gray. The legs were dark.
Behaviors: The bird was observed mutiple times perched on top of various types of vegetation (junipers, rabbit brush, gambel oak) singing repeatly. At one point the bird was observed foraging on the ground for seeds/insects near a prickly pear cactus.
Call: The bird was first located by its song, which consisted of 2 or 3 clear notes followed by a desending accelerating trill.
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Distinctive, The lack of white in the tail rules out DE Junco (gray headed race). Song also dictinctive. Field Sp ruled out watching the bird sing.
