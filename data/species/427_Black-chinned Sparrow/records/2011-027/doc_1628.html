Description of the Bird

This bird had a sparrow shaped (seed eating) pink bill, similar to some junco subspecies with black immediately behind the bill causing the bill to stand out. The crown and nape were medium gray with a rich gold, brown and dark gray back contrasting strongly with the rest of the bird. The belly was about the same color gray as the crown and nape and the tail appeared very long for the size of the bird. I did not check the leg color.
Behaviors: At 5 P.M. it was heard giving soft chips, skulking low in big sage mostly. It twice perched on top of tall shrubs and sang where it became completely visible for about 20 seconds each time and then disappeared into dense sage again where it moved around a bit but always in cover.
Call: heard both singing, bouncing ball type call similar to Field Sparrow, but I think faster with a buzzier ending. The call was soft, single noted, thin, spizella-like.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Song similar to Field Sparrow but coloration not even close.

Pink-sided Junco comes closest in appearance and there were two nearby for comparison (but not present at the same time. DEJU was lighter bellied, much redder back without the intricate pattern on the back of the BCSP which is colored differently with a combination of rich colors unlike the solid rufous tones of the junco, plus song of DEJUs of any subspecies is nothing like the bouncing ball rich song of this bird.
