Description of the Bird

Long-tailed, overall gray sparrow with brownish upperparts & pink bill offset by black front of face & black throat. Very distinctive plumage and very close looks- unmistakeable ID.
Behaviors: Bird 1 was singing from prominent perches. At one point this bird flew to a bush where another BCSP was present. The interaction didn't seem too aggressive and the other bird wasn't hear singing- perhaps a female?
Call: at least one of the two birds was a male- it was singing the species' typical accelerating, descending trill with an upslurred end. Video including several repetitions of the song here: http://tinyurl.com/BCCH-20May2011
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Plumage pattern and song rules out any other species.
