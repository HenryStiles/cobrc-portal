Description of the Bird

Two adults birds with a singing male and a female in the area feeding mainly in Mock Orange. Brenda first heard the male singing as we had stopped at the pullout to read the signs. After seaching the shrubs we located the male below us. The female mostly stayed in the Mock Orange but did give us a few quick views.

The male Black-chinned Sparrow mostly gray with a black face and chin, small pink bill, and dark eye. The back and tail were streaked and brown.The female looked much like the male except lacked the black about the face and head.

If the Black-chinned Sparrow was not singing it could be mistaken for a Dark-eyed Junco. We had been aware of reports over the last three years of Black-chinned Sparrows on Mesa Verde National Park and were searching for this species on this occasion. We stopped at pullouts and would search for the sparrows in the shrubs. Mike Thompson had reported a singing Black-chinned Sparrow on a BBS survey a few weeks earlier south of our location in a different canyon complex on the opposite side of the road. This may suggest there could be other locations where Black-chinned Sparrows could be found in Mesa Verde National Park as well as other locations in southwest Colorado.

Similar Species Discussion

Dark-eyed Junco had to be eliminated but was easy because the male was singing the bouncing thrill song. No white in tail during flight, small bird with all gray belly and black chin.

 

Previous Experience

Black-chinned Sparrows can be found in Arizona northeast of Globe along US 60. We would stop at a known location returning from leading birding trips to see this sparrow.

Also doing transects for RMBO in southwestern Utah I had many points where I heard Black-chinned Sparrows singing.

We have also seen this species near Monterrey, Mexico and Grand Junction, Colorado.
