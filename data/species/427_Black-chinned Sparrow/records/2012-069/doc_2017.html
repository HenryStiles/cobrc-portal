Description of the Bird

Size was slightly smaller than a junco. Distinctive medium gray (head, nape, breast and belly). Back reddish with black streaks. Bill small and pale. No white tail flash apparent when the bird flew. No black chin spot.
Behaviors: The bird appeared to be foraging in xeric habitat on a south-facing slope.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

First impression was a Slate-colored Junco, but the reddish, streaked back didn't fit. The gray was a little too dark for a Gray-headed junco, which has a solid red back without streaking. The gray on the breast continued all the way down the breast, unlike the mid-point cutoff on a junco's breast. I looked for a chin-spot, but there was none. The bill was small and pale. When the bird flew, the tail was all dark without the white outer rectrices that a junco would exhibit.
