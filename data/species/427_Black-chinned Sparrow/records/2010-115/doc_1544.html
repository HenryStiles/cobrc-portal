Habitat

Mostly sage with scattered Utah serviceberries and a few small junipers. Terrain sloped up to rocky ridges with Gambel's Oak and some ponderosas nearby.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss 8x40 and Kowa 830 30x.
Distance:  30 ft closest; usually 50 yards
Light:  Excellent. Most observations were during full sunlight and the sun was generally behind me.

Description of the Bird

These were slim spizella sparrows that appeared long-tailed in flight. The body of the birds was medium gray. The underparts were slightly lighter gray. The wings were sparrow-like brownish with faint wing-bars. Some brownish extended onto the back. The males had black surrounding the bill above and below. The bill was pinkish-orange. The female had a more extensive black chin and lacked black above the bill. The bill was pinkish-orange and somewhat darker tipped. The legs were black.
Behaviors: The males sang from the top of shrubs with exposed dead snags, then flew long distances (100yards) and typically disappeared from sight for long periods. The flight was generally low.

The birds were hard to approach for close looks. Even when singing. Pattern of movement was not predictable, although the males did return to the same singing perches with some frequency although the interval could be one to two hours.
Call: Three or four clear beginning notes transitioning into a descending thrill. To me it was similar to a Field Sparrow
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

There is nothing similar to this spizella sparrow in my opinion.
