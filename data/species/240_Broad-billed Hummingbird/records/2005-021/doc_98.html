Habitat

Farm headquarters for cattle operation and winter wheat farming operation; sighted in both front yard and back yard of the farm house; photos taken of the bird on a honeysuckle bush and at a hummingbird feeder; also viewed in a flowering crabapple tree (our headquarters are on a hill and have the only protection...trees, shrubs, lawn, water...for several miles in all directions)

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  none - just camera
Distance:  3' to 10'
Light:  evening of the 17th very low dim sunlight;
morning of the 18th very bright daylight, clear and sunny
viewed bird in excellent light from several directions as it moved around the yard

Description of the Bird

about 4" in length; long curved broad bill; small white patch behind eye; grayish patch on auriculars; green supercillium and crown; bright green back; dark throat to bright blue collar on breast; greenish sides and belly; white undertail coverts; grayish primary coverts; broad notched dark tail with grayish tips in layers
Behaviors: typical hummingbird flight - close enough to see it was a hummingbird - but not experienced enough to identify without photos
Call: not noted
Plumage: bright adult male
