Description of the Bird

Large gull with pale head and chest with some dark feathers around eyes, otherwise fairly uniform light brown mantle, primaries and tail. All black heavy bill with strong gonydeal angle. Flat headed appearance.  With wings spread, pale tips to primaries. Pinkish legs. 

Similar Species Discussion

Glaucous ruled out (3 were nearby) based on pigmentation and bill color and shape.  Not Iceland based on size.  Young Herring gulls had similar pale heads, but had dark primaries and distinct bill shape differences. Ring-bills were tiny in comparison, Lesser Black-backed juveniles would be significantly darker mantled.

Resources Used

Had reviewed photos of this bird at Aurora Reservoir on E-bird, as well as photos of the juve GWGU in Pueblo (possibly the same bird). When home, reviewed National Geographic (7th ed., Dunn and Alderfer), The Sibley Guide to Birds (2nd ed., Sibley), and Gulls of the Americas (2007, Howell and Dunn).

Previous Experience

Travel to California in fall/winter (pelagic trips) and to coastal Alaska, as well as Adak, AK, with a few previous juvenile sightings in Colorado.
