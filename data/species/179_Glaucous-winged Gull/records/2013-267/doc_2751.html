Description of the Bird

Gulls were continuously flying into Windsor Reservoir as I stood along its bank (see position noted on map; accessed via private property). I was rather surprised to see a large chunky pale biscuit-colored gull sitting on the water amongst Herring and RB Gulls. The bird was larger in bulk, a tad longer in length than HERG. The wings, as projecting beyond tail, were short, much more so than in HERG. The bird's color was nearly uniform throughout as it sat on water, being a tad paler on the head. Its bill was entirely black, and stouter than that of a HERG. In flight, the upper wing was uniform in color, with no darker (or lighter) markings on primaries and no darker secondary bar. The undersurface of primaries was uniformly silvery pale, as typical of GW Gull. The rump was barred mildly contrasting light and dark. Tail solid dark, same color as back. I observed the bird with the sun to my back, minimal heatwaves, no wind. I probably saw the bird in flight for 30-60 seconds, and on the water for a goodly 10 minutes off and on, maybe more as I kept looking at it more closely as it swam slowly closer.

Similar Species Discussion

Glaucous Gull or GLGU x GWGU hybrids should show some pink at base of bill and some whitish edging to primaries (or white primaries in pure GLGU).
GW x HERG or GW x WEGU should have dark on primaries.
Size and shape totally typical of GWGU, which would also help eliminate ICGU or very pallid THGU
