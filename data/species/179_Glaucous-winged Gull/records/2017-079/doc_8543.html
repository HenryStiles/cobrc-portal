Description of the Bird

Large gull, larger than nearby Herring Gulls, with thick-chested, short-winged stout appearance. Stood taller and looked bulkier than its neighboring HERGs. Plumage uniformly grayish brown, moderately pale (somewhat paler than first cycle HERGs nearby), with overall smudgy aspect to plumage. Back, wing-coverts, tertials, and primaries all appeared to be of the same color, as were the head, chest, and underparts. -- more like juvenile than a full-on first-winter plumage GWGU.  Bill all black. Description written within 5 minutes of seeing bird

Similar Species Discussion

Uniform color should eliminate all other species other than ICGU or, perhaps, GLGU. This bird was too large for ICGU and darker than typical of GLGU and lacked that species bicolored bill. Similarly, GWGU x GLGU should have a bicolored bill and, usually, somewhat paler primaries. 

GWGU x HERG might look like this, but should show some structural differences (smaller head and/or bill, longer wings) and darker primaries.
