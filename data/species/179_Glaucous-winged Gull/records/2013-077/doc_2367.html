Description of the Bird

Modestly larger than HERG with short primary projection and a distinct secondary skirt. Pale biscuit color bird, rather uniformly colored, except head and neck a shade paler, hardly detectable. Wing overall uniform in color (primaries same color as wing), with whitish-brown ends to tertials and similarly colored narrow edging on folded primaries. Tail same color as body, rump white. Upper side of wing uniformly colored during flight, underside uniform pale silvery, without dark tips to primaries. Bill entirely black, or nearly so, no visible gonydeal angle, moderately thick.
Behaviors: Sitting on ice with mixed flock of gulls about to go to roost
Call: not heard
Plumage: first cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Hybrids with Western or Herring Gull would have darker wingtips. Hybrid with Herring would also typically be less beefy, longer winged, smaller billed. Hybrid with Western would typically also be darker bodied. Hybrid with Glaucous Gull typically paler primaries, more patterned wing coverts, paler body, and often, obvious pink at base of bill
