Description of the Bird

I was scoping gulls at Pueblo Reservoir near the South Shore Marina, with other birders, when I spotted a large tan gull standing on the shore with other gulls.  The gull flock included mostly Ring-billed Gull, with a few Herring Gulls.  This tan gull, was big, at least the size of the Herring Gulls, though looked a bit larger.  The bird was first year bird, because it had a very large and thick, all black bill.  The head was mostly tan colored, the wings were tan, the wing tips were tan, the tail was tan, the belly and underparts were also tan, the back and wings while standing were all tan as well.  The eyes were black and the legs were pink.  The head was somewhat more blocky, instead of round, like a juvenile Thayer's Gull.  The bird flapped a few times, and I wasn't seeing any darker feathers in the wing tips, tail, or anywhere in the wings.  My feeling was the bird was a Glaucous-winged Gull.  It wouldn't fly, so we couldn't all see, if there was any darker feather, which might indicate a hybrid.  We decided to leave the gull for a while.  We came back to the South Shore Marina tires at Pueblo Reservoir, later on, and, re-found the gull, as it flew right by us.  We all looked at the wings looking for darker feather, and none were there.  The tail shape was good for Glaucous-winged Gull as well, as it was wider than a squared tail, that Thayer's Gulls have.  The lack of any dark feathers, the size, shape, and tan overall color, made the identifaction as a first year Glaucous-winged Gull.  I've seen many Glaucous-winged Gulls in CA and AK, and a few in Colorado, and this bird looked perfect.

Similar Species Discussion

Hybrid Glaucous-winged x Herring or Western, would have darker feathers in wing tips, or darker feathers in the tail, or darker feathers in the wings in flight.  None of the bird, had any darker feathers.

Juvenile Thayer's Gulls, are smaller than Herring Gulls (this bird was big), the bill is small (not large and thick), the head is more rounder as well (the heed on this bird was larger, not as round).

Glaucous and Iceland Gulls have lighter wing tips, that the body (the wing tips were the same color, not darker or lighter).

First year Slaty-backed Gull, which I haven't for sure seen, has darker wing tips.
