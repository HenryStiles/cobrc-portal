Description of the Bird

I was on my own, walking back across the ice, looking at the array of gulls before me, sun to back. I'd picked out two GW x HERG and then saw another similar bird, but this bird had wingtips that were concolorous with the remainder of the wing. As I lifted my camera and aimed, it took off, so I only have flight shots. The bird was a stout gull, shorter winged and thicker billed than a HERG. It was nearly entirely brown, uniform in darkness, when perched, lighter than HERGs nearby. The bill was mostly black, with a tad of pink at very base. For flight, see photos which accurately depict the bird. 

Similar Species Discussion

GW x Western or GWxHERG should have darker wing tips; GW x Western, typically somewhat darker bodied as well
THGU should have been longer winged, slimmer, more delicate bill, smaller body. Same for ICGU.
