Description of the Bird

First seen in flight. A pale biscuit colored gull, uniformly colored above, with primaries same color as remainder of wing. Underwing appeared uniform silvery white. Rump barred brown and white. Tail solid, same biscuit color. Underparts brown (vent not well seen). Head with some darker brown around eye. Bill large, with prominent gonydeal angle, and black (with vague area, a bit paler, near base). Scaps same biscuit color with some white freckling at feather tips. Median wing coverts mottled biscuit brown with white mottling. Greater coverts same basal color, with sparse white mottling. Tertials same color with whitish at terminus. Plumage overall smudgy in appearance.

Similar Species Discussion

GW x Herring Gull. Should have darker primaries, more slender build (body and bill), sometimes with some white barring on outer tail feathers. This bird had typical short-winged (while perched, broad winged (in flight), hefty bodied appearance of GW Gull, with sturdy bill. Color paler than most GW x HERG.

Glaucous Gull. Among other things, should have pink base to bill
