Description of the Bird

The bird was large and bulky, similar in size to nearby AHGUs and noticeably larger then CAGUs swimming nearby. It had a very thick bill especially toward the tip, maybe slightly shorter then AHGU next to it. Overall the head and upper chest were very streaky. The bird also had distinctly dark eyes. The mantle was pale gray and the primaries seemed to be just a bit darker then the mantle. While flapping the primaries also appeared just slightly darker then the back.

In addition to my pictures both Jason and Glenn got open wing shots which I did not get. These pictures can be seen at: https://ebird.org/checklist/S283189538 and https://ebird.org/checklist/S283206310

 

Similar Species Discussion

The only other similar species is a pale Iceland Gull. This species can be eliminated by overall size, bill shape and the extensive streaking on the head and upper chest. 


The big concern with this bird is possible hybridization with either WEGU or AHGU. The bird is defenetly mostly GWGU but since the birds primaries are slightly darker then the back it does raise some red flags about possible hybridization. However nothing else about this bird strikes me as hybrid traits. Eye color, back color, bill size and shape, and general shape of the bird all seem good for GWGU. Thus I think this bird is within the range of pure GWGU.

Previous Experience

I have some experinece with GWGU in the Pacific Northwest as well as Olympic Gull although I do not feel fully confident with the range of variation of pure GWGU. Also, I have seen several GWGUs in Colorado. Specifically an adult bird in Larimer County in March of 2020 which is very similar to this individual in primary color specifically.
