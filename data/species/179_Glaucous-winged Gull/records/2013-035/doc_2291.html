Description of the Bird

About 3:30 PM, Paul Hurtado pointed out a GW Gull or GW x ?? Gull hybrid that was, at the time, partly obscured by nearby other gulls. I looked through his scope quickly and then got the bird in my scope. The sun was directly behind us, offering near perfect lighting, and the gull (on the tires at N Marina, Pueblo Res) was about 100-200 meters. During time of observation, the gull walked about giving views from different angles, and on a couple occasions, spreading wings so that such could be seen well dorsally and ventrally. There were a number of us present, and we watched the gull for 15 minutes or more.

It was a stout gull, perhaps not longer than the nearby Herring Gulls, but definitely bulkier, with short primary extension and a more prominent secondary skirt than that of a Herring Gull. Overall, the bird was medium to pale brownish and relatively evenly colored. The head showed no sign of the mask seen on WEGUs and many GWGU x WEGUs. The bill was stout and black, excepting a tiny bit of pink at the very base. There was not a distinct gonydeal angle, as is shown by most Herring Gulls but lacking on many GW Gulls.

The back was mottled with some gray (1st winter plumage coming in) but overall was mottled the same color as the underparts. The wing coverts were similarly colored, but each feather had some pale edging. The tertials were unpatterned and of the same color as the general body color. The primaries were the same color as the tertials, wing coverts, and body, but had significant pale gray edging.

When wing spread, the underwing was totally pale (silvery) beneath, without any dark on underside of primaries, including tips of outer primaries. The upper wing was uniform in color (no secondary bar) excepting that the outermost primaries were slightly darker than rest of wing.

The tail was uniform dusky. The rump whitish but details not noted. Legs pink.
Behaviors: Roosting on tires with hundreds of other gulls, mostly RBGU but a fair number of Herrings
Call: Not heard
Plumage: 1st cycle

Similar Species Discussion

The above description fairly well eliminates all other species, but hybrids with other species remains a concern. GWGU x HERG should have darker primaries, slimmer bill, longer primaries, less distinct secondary skirt.. or at least a couple of these features. GW x WEGU should be distinctly darker and darker primaried. Also should show some dark secondary bar.
