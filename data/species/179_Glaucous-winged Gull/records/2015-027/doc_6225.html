Description of the Bird

Massive biscuit colored gull first seent in flight. Pallid and uniform plumage combined with size and stout build (particularly broad wings) made this bird stand out fro the hundreds of gulls (including Herrings) it was with. More speciifally, wings uniform with no dark on primaries, no secondary bar. When perched, the bird's wings looked short compared with those of HERGs and THGU nearby. Bill all or nearly all dark and thicker proportionately than that of HERGs. Legs pink. Head not appreciably paler than rest of bird. Plumage appeared smudgy overall, without the patterning that I could, at least intermittently, make out on THGU. I watched this bird for at least 10 straight minutes 

Similar Species Discussion

GWGU x HERG should have darker tail often with some pale at base of tail on outer tail feathers (this bird's tail uniform colored without and pale at base of outer tail feathers that I could discern; tail same color as back, rump a tad paler) and dark on wingtips, which I avidly searched for. Also, shape typical of GWGU (which some hybrids can show). 

THGU/ICGU should be smaller, finer build, smaller billed, often with some dark on outer primaries
