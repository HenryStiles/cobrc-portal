Description of the Bird

Notes from first day: larger than neighboring HERGs, bigger chested, thicker billed, shorter winged. Mantle/wings were uniform in "lightness" as seen from a lateral view while perched. The primaries were no darker than the tertials nor the body. Paler overall than nearby first-cycle HERGs, but not by much. Back was medium gray, similar in darkness to brown belly and wings.  The head, neck and chest were paler than remainder of bird, but heavily mottled with smudgy brown. The patterning seemed smudgy on bird as a whole. When the bird flapped, the upper wings appeared evenly colored, without any darkness to primaries, secondaries. Bill stout and black, larger than that of nearby HERGs

Notes from second day (written within 20 minutes of seeing bird): Seen from 1:55 to 2:15. A hefty gull with short wings and a distinct secondary skirt. Heftier than nearby HERGs and shorter winged. Wings worn pale brown including coverts, tertials, and primaries which were all of the same color. The back was grayish washed with brown. Tail muddy gray-brown contrasting with whiter rump. Head and chest mostly white, but mottled with some brown. Belly was brown. Bill thick and black. Legs pink. Wings uniform above when flapped, without darker markings on primaries or secondaries. The bird was similar in overall color to nearby first cycle HERGs, but a bit paler. 

Though these descriptions do not perfectly match, I felt as if I was looking at the same bird. If the BRC wishes to vote on the sightings separately, that is okay.

Similar Species Discussion

Too large, short-winged for THGU, ICGU. Black bill, smudgy medium-colored plumage wrong for GLGU
Real issue is GWGU x HERG. There was no trait to suggest this hybrid. No dark on primaries or secondaries, bill and head appropriately large, bird short-winged, plumage characteristically (for GWGU) smudgy. No pale at base of bill.
