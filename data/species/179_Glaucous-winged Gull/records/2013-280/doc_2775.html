Description of the Bird

Large gull with short wings. Larger than nearby Herring Gulls with heftier bill. Plumage rather worn. Back gray with some brownish hues, perhaps due to wear. Back color a bit darker than that if nearby Herring Gull. Greater coverts worn, and thus whitish near base, but more brownish distally. Base of terials similarly pallid, and gray-brown distally. Folded primaries (note that in photos, wings are drooped a bit, so that base of primaries more visible. This description pertains to when wings fully folded) brown centered with fairly broad pale edging. Bill hefty but relatively little gonydeal angle. Bill pink with irregular dark band near tip and small pink tip. Eye dark. Observed in flight with bright light nearly directly behind. Underside of flight feathers pearly white, with mottled underside to wing coverts. Upper wings pale gray brown with some grayish brown on outermost two or three primaries. Tail pale grayish- brown with paler rump. Head and chest white, mottled with some brown, belly more heavily mottled with brown. Legs pink.

Similar Species Discussion

GW x WEGU - would be darker backed and with darker wingtips.
Glaucous Gull- should lack any markings in primaries
GW x GLGU- Should be paler backed, paler wings... paler overall, and probably with some pale in eye
GW x HERG- Should have longer wings, finer bill, more dark on wingtips, smaller overall size. 

The age of this bird is a bit baffling. The bill is more like a second year bird going into third winter, though I've certainly seen second year birds (and even the occasional first year bird) with pink on base of bill. The worn plumage makes sorting out gray from brown when it comes to individual feathers. The smudgy gray-brown color does match GWGU 33.37 in Howell's book on gulls, a second-cycle photographed in CO in April, and I suspect that this bird is a worn bird molting into second winter plumage... raising the possibility that it is the same individual present at same location last winter as a first winter bird.

Given that at least one challenge to this bird's ID was thrown my way (nobody on the BRC or who would be on BRC), I decided to ask for some outside input. Charlie Wright, Brad Waggoner, and Bill Tweit are all WBRC members.

TWEIT: Hey Steve,
Nice photos.  I considered both GW X GL and GW X HE, but do not see any reason to think this bird is an intergrade with either.    The tail pattern, lack of secondary bar, general 'muddiness' of plumage pattern are all consistent with typical GWGU.  Best, Bill

WAGGONER: Hello Steve,
I don't think I would have even given this gull much attention if it was mixed in a flock of gulls here, other than the bill perhaps grabbing me momentarily. It looks like what I would classify as a Glaucous-winged Gull.
Hope you are staying warm.
Brad

WRIGHT: Looks okay to me -- though the bill gave me momentary pause.
