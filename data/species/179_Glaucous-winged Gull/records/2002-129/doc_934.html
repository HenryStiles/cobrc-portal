Description of the Bird

the bird was a fairly large gull, about the same size or slightly larger than nearby Herring Gulls and much larger than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The bill was relatively large for a gull, and noticeably "blob-tipped". It was black, with a shadow of pinkish color at the base. The legs were pink, about the same shade as nearby Herring Gulls.

The entire plumage of the bird was a pale brown, paler and much more uniform than the brown of Herring Gull. The bird had a faintly darker helmet of color on the head, contrasting with the slightly paler color of the nape and neck. The wingtips were the same color as the body, and were both short and blunt. The tail was slightly but noticeably darker.

The overall shape of the bird appeared bulky, accentuated by a relatively thick neck, short and broad wings, and large and blob-tipped bill.
Behaviors: the bird was seen loafing around on the ice-edge for the most part, but also actively feeding on the water and on the ice
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species of gull were ruled out by the uniform pale brown plumage. Thayer's Gull was ruled out by the paler wings that did not contrast with the rest of the body, by the shorter and broader wings, thicker neck and overall bulkier proportions. Iceland Gull was ruled out by the same combination of characteristics.

Potential hyrbids were ruled out by the fact that the wingtips were not darker at all than the tertials and body plumage.

Resources Used

various sources were consulted to rule out the possibility of hybrid combinations

Previous Experience

I have seen many Glaucous-winged Gulls on the west coast, as well as many Glaucous-winged x Western hybrids. I am less familiar with Glaucous-winged x Herring hybrids, but have seen many Thayer's and Iceland Gulls.
