Description of the Bird

Large (distinctly larger than Herring Gulls nearby) pale gull, essentially uniform in color. Color biscuit brown. Wing-coverts moderately mottled throughout. Tertials solid brown of same base color of upperparts, with pale terminal fringe. Primaries same color as tertials and wing-coverts, with frosty pale brown edging. Scapulars also mottled pale brown. Back with some gray not contrasting in lightness/darkness with remainder of upperparts. Head somewhat paler around face and forecrown. Vague slightly darker area around auriculars. Bill hefty, even in direct comparison with 2 adult Herring Gulls. Moderately strong gonydeal angle. In flight, wings looked uniform above, excepting slightly darker on proximal secondaries and limited darker tips (only) to outermost primaries. Underwing looked whitish on flight feathers, without evidence of darker coloration on flight feathers. Tail appeared uniformly brown of same color as rest of upperparts. Rump not solid like tail, but barred. Underparts same overall color as upperparts, but unpatterned and smudgy, except vent, which had some darker barring. Bill entirely black. Feet/legs pinkish.
Behaviors: Stood with other gulls (Largely Ring-billeds, scattering of Herring Gulls, one Thayer's) on ice shelf in wind-protected portion of reservoir
Call: not heard
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Much larger and heavier billed than Iceland Gull.
GW x Glaucous Gull hybrid should have some pink on bill and paler primary tips. GW x Western Gull should be darker overall with more extensive dark on flight feathers. GW x Herring Gull should have more petite bill and more dark on primaries. Glaucous x Herring Gull should have paler inner primaries, darker outer ones and a pink-based bill

Previous Experience
