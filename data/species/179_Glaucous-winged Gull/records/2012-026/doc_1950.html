Description of the Bird

This bird was seen several times during the course of the morning whilst looking for Western Gull. It came as close as 50 yards or so, and the lighting varied from fair to quite good. It was seen with Glenn Walbek, and on my own. I believe Glenn photographed it.

In flight, the bird looked uniformly biscuit colored above: darker than the color of most (all at this time of year?) Glaucous Gulls but paler than Herring or Thayer?s Gull. In flight, the bird did have slightly darker secondaries, forming a weak secondary bar. The outer 2-4 primaries were modestly darker brown, seemingly limited to outer webs, but I can't be totally certain. At times, the darker color on outer primaries could not be discerned at all. The underwings showed some dusky markings on coverts, but flight feathers were entirely whitish. The tail was uniformly brown, similar in darkness to back. The rump showed little contrast with either back or tail. The underparts were uniformly brown. The head and neck were a bit paler, with the transition from body to head gradual. The folded primaries were brown centered with moderately broad pale edging. The bill was somewhat hefty and entirely black. The bird looked bulky on water, with relatively short wings.

Total viewing time was in excess of five minutes, about evenly split in flight and on water.

Similar Species Discussion

GW x Western or GW x Herring Gull should show more dark on wings/tail. The latter should have been finer in structure. Iceland Gull would be much smaller.
