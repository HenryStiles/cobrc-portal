Description of the Bird

I first noticed the bird in flight due to it's almost pure white underwing (the primaries in particular) with a hint of pattern visible.  The gull was on the large end for Herring gull and when it was close enough I could see the dark eye and larger bill. All together it looked too big and light-winged for Thayer's or Kumlien's Iceland Gull. On the ground, it had wingtips that were only a shade or two darker than the rest of the upperparts (not at all as dark as the primaries on Glaucous-winged hybrids with Herring or Western). The primary coverts were also showing some darker gray, perhaps suggesting it's a sub-adult bird (see photos). Legs pink, bill yellow with a small red dot and a smudge of black out near the gonys. Overall, everything lined up for a Glaucous-winged Gull, or at least bird that is phenotypically indistinguishable from one! ;-) 

Similar Species Discussion

Most gulls were ruled out by this adult or near-adult bird's dark eyes, and pale gray (not black) wingtips, which narrowed it down to Iceland (sensu lato), Glaucous-winged or Glaucous hybrids, or Glaucous-winged Gull.

Kumlien's and Thayer's were easily ruled out by the size, bill structure, and (most importantly) wingtip pattern. Eye color, structure, and plumage details ruled out Glaucous and Glaucous hybrids. 

Glaucous-winged x Herring hybrid was my next main concern, as these have shown up on multiple occasions in CO (although usually as 1st cycle birds). In this case, the bird is on the petite end of the Glacuous-winged Spectrum, but the dark eyes and pale wingtip pattern rule out a Glaucous-winged x Herring or Glaucous-winged x Western hybrid, except perhaps a backcross with only very little Herring ancestry. Nothing jumped out at me as an obvious sign of hybrid ancestry, as everything looks within the range of Glaucous-winged, and the structure of the bird is clearly not suggestive of Glaucous-winged x Western, leaving Glaucous-winged as the only option left for the ID.

P.S. Please note the photos were shot it bright sun, and are a bit underexposed (e.g., see the Herring Gull colors in the flight shot!) which has enhanced the apparent darkness of the wingtips relative to how they looked in the field.

Previous Experience

I've seen Glaucous-winged and hybrids in California and Washington, and I've seen at least one 1st cycle Glaucous-winged Herring in Pueblo, Co., CO.  I have seen multiple Nelson's Gulls (Glaucous x Herring) of all ages in Ohio and New York, and have seen many Kumlien's and Thayer's gulls in CO, NY, OH, NV, and CA. With respect to adult Glaucous-winged and hybrids, I've never seen adult hybrids away from the Pacific coast.
