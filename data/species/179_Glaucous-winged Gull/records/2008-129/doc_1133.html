Description of the Bird

the bird was a large gull, larger than any other gull out on the reservoir. It was slightly larger than nearby Herring Gulls. The overall shape was bulkier than that of Herring Gull, with a more chesty look, thicker neck, shorter and wider wings, and larger bill adding to the perception of bulkiness. The bill was large and thick, for a gull, with a strong gonydeal angle giving it a "blob-tipped" look. It was entirely black. The legs were medium pink in color, and relatively shorter than those of Herring Gull. The bird was too far away to observe the color of the eye.

The plumage of the bird was evenly colored all over the body, and was a light tan colored, significantly paler than the brown of juvenile Herring Gulls. The only parts of the bird that were darker was the tail, which was a couple of shades darker, but still significantly paler than that of Herring Gull, and a very slightly darker "helmet" on the head.
Behaviors: The bird was observed out on the middle of Antero Reservoir. During the first observation it was observed flying around over the water and then landing on the far shore next to a California Gull. During the second observation it was observed going from water hole to water hole on the mostly frozen lake, usually in the company of a few Herring Gulls.
Call: none heard
Plumage: first basic

Similar Species Discussion

Many species of gulls were ruled out by the large size of the bird, slightly larger than a Herring Gull. California Gull was ruled out by the much paler color of the bird for this plumage, by the much bulkier shape, and by the larger size. Herring Gull was ruled out by the uniform plumage that was much paler, by the bulkier shape and shorter wings, and by the larger and blob-tipped bill. Glaucous Gull was ruled out by the darker coloration, by the all black bill, and by the shorter and wider wingtips. Iceland Gull was ruled out by the larger size, more uniform plumage, by the wider and shorter wingtips, by the larger, thicker, and blob-tipped bill, and by the bulkier shape. Thayer's Gull was ruled out by the large size, bulkier shape, more uniform plumage and paler wingtips, by the shorter and wider wingtips, and by the larger and blob-tipped bill.

This bird did not show any apparent signs of hybridization. A Glaucous-winged x Western Gull was ruled out by the more uniform and relatively unpatterned plumage, and wingtips that did not contrast with the body. Glaucous-winged x Glaucous was ruled out by wingtips that were not paler than the body, by the low contrast plumage, and by the large and blob-tipped bill. Glaucous-winged x Herring Gull was ruled out by the short and broad wingtips, by the low contrast plumage with wingtips the same color as the body, by the bulky shape overall, and by the larger and more blob-tipped bill.
