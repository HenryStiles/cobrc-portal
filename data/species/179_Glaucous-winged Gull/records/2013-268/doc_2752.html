Description of the Bird

David spotted a gull and told me that he thought he had a "real" GW Gull (we'd had a GW x Herring earlier in day at same location). The bird stood out among the surround Herring, Cal and RB Gulls. It was uniformly pale muddy grayish brown throughout, including back wings (through primaries), head and neck. The tertials and primaries were the same color as the remainder of the wing as the bird sat on the water. It was larger and stouter that then neraby HERGs with a stouter all black bill. The wings barely extended beyond the primaries, so proportionately shorter winged that the HERGs. Bill all black. When bird spread its wings, as it did a few times, flapping, the spread wing was noted to be uniform, with no darker color on primaries nor secondaries.

Similar Species Discussion

GW x HERG or GW x Western should show dark on primaries. GW x HERG should have finer bill, longer wings
GW x GLGU should have paler wing tips, some pink at bill base. GLGU, very much pink at base of bill, paler primaries.
