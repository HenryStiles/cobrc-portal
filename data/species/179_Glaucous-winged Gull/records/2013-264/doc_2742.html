Description of the Bird

The glaucous-winged gull was the only large gull at the lake during the observation period.  At times, it was by itself on the water.  At other times, it was next to ring-billed and Franklin's gulls, providing a good size comparison.  The glaucous-winged gull was much larger than the ring-billed gulls and had a bulky appearance.  The head was lightish colored, and the eyes were dark.  The bill was light colored near the base and near the tip, but was otherwise dark.  The breast and belly had some off-white mottling / streaking.  The upperparts were a medium shade of gray, transitioning to brownish gray in the rear median / greater coverts and light colored in the side median / greater coverts.  Primaries were quite pale on both the upper and lower sides, as seen both at rest and in flight.  The underwing was mostly pale, but some darker color was seen near the forward side.  Overall, the plumage has a second summer appearance.  Legs were pink.

I do not recall hearing any calls that I could attribute specifically to the glaucous-winged gull.

Similar Species Discussion

Other gulls with pale wingtips include Iceland, Thayer's, and Glaucous.  The gull was large and had a thick bill, unlike Iceland and Thayer's.  Both the upperparts and bill were too dark for Glaucous.
