Description of the Bird

The first-year Glaucous-winged Gull was seen resting on a sand bar, bathing in the nearby shallow water, and flying low over the lake.  It was approximately the same size as the nearby Herring Gulls but had a shorter primary projection.  The gull had a stout appearance.  Plumage was rather uniformly biscuit colored.  Some fine, low-contrast patterning could be seen on the upperparts.  Some streaking could be seen on the neck, and the head was somewhat paler than the rest of the upperparts.  The folded primaries were the same color as the back, with paler edges.  Tertials were plain (not patterned).  Wing color also appeared uniform when the bird spread its wings in the water and in flight.  A light brown tail band could be seen on the bird in flight.  The bill was all black and very thick.  Legs were pink.   

Similar Species Discussion

Iceland Gull:  ruled out by large overall size, short primary projection, and thick bill
Glaucous Gull:  rule out by lack of bicolored bill
