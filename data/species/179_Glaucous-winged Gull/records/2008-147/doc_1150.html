Description of the Bird

the bird was a large gull, slightly larger than nearby Herring Gulls. The overall shape was also more bulky than that of a Thayer's Gull; the chest appeared larger compared to the head, giving the bird a chestier and smaller headed look. The wings were proportionally broader at the base, and the outer part of the wing appeared a bit proportionally shorter. When perched on the water the wingtips fell not too far beyond the end of the tail; the bird was too far away to count which primary fell at the tail tip, but the wings appeared proportionally shorter compared to the tail than Herring Gulls perched nearby. When perched the wingtips also appeared proportionally broader, with wider primaries. The bill was fairly thick for a gull, with a strong gonydeal angle creating a "blob-tipped" look. The bill was also completely black in color. The legs were fleshy pink colored.

The head of the bird was pale brown, with a darker patch on the face behind the eye. The entirety of the rest of the bird was evenly pale brown, with no contrast anywhere in the body except for a slightly darker tail in certain lights (but it did not appear darker in others). When perched on the water the tertials also could appear a tiny bit darker in certain lights, but mostly they also didn't contrast.
Behaviors: The bird was first observed flying over the large duck flock on the reservoir. It would occasionally land on the lack amid ducks and other gulls, where it was fairly aggressive and would snap at other gulls and steal food from both them and the ducks. It was last seen flying over the ducks. We started looking at other birds, and when we looked for the Glaucous-winged again we were unable to find it.
Call: none heard
Plumage: first cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of gulls were ruled out by the overall pale coloration and lack of darker wingtips. Glaucous and Iceland Gulls were ruled out by the darker overall coloration of the bird; Iceland Gull was also ruled out by the lack of patterning and bulkier shape and larger size. Thayer's Gull was ruled out by the large size, bulkier shape and broader wings, lack of patterning on the wings and back, and larger bill with stronger gonydeal angle.

Hybrid gulls were ruled out by the uniform and pale coloration. Glaucous-winged x Western Gull was ruled out by the lack of any dark on the wingtips or tail and muddy overall look. Glaucous-winged x Herring Gull was also ruled out by the lack of any dark coloration on the wing, as well as a lack of Herring Gull shape characteristics. Glaucous-winged x Glaucous Gull was ruled out by the lack of Glaucous Gull characteristics, including all dark bill, uniform brown wings, and overall evenly brown color.
