Description of the Bird

I have little to add beyond what the photos show. In flight, the upper tail seemed about 1/2 white, 1/2 pale rust. No barring was seen. But the look was a bit distant and for about 20 seconds... not the longest. I went for pics after that.
Behaviors: Soaring. Then apparently chased by another RTHA, which it later landed near, almost like a pair. They could never be refound
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The key here is what one considers Krider's RTHA, and where one draws boundary of what is or is not a Krider's. Since this is well out of my arena of expertise, I consulted Jerry Ligouri who said:

Krider's tail type for sure. And that is all that we need to know...speculating on whether it is a variation of Krider's, Krider's x Eastern, or Krider's x Western (which would probably be impossible to say and doesn't appear to be likely for this bird) is less of an issue. Brian and I call any paler borealis with a white tail base essentially a Krider's.

I'm not surprised, what I'm more surprised about is that these sightings weren't documented sooner. After we got back from our Krider's study and realized that Krider's is breeding just outside of Billings, MT, we wondered how far south of there individuals would be.
