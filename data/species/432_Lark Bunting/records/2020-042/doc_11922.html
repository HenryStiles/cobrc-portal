Description of the Bird

From the side view

I believe this bird to be a non-breeding male or possible a November juvenile. A chunky compact bunting, shorter tail, with a seed bill, gray upper and silver lower mandible. Larger in weight and size than the mixed flock of White-crowned and House Sparrows. The secondary feathers, primary and greater coverts were black, edged with rich tawny showing a bright wing bar look. The folded primaries were black. The crown and mantle were muted brown and tawny modeling. The flanks were white changing to tawny with muted black spots. The head had a plain brown crown and auricular patch with white surrounding the lower edge extending into the submoustachial area. It had black surrounding and bill with a white supraloral stripe and tawny thin eye ring. Muted tawny supercilium behind eye to nape.

From the front view

Heavy black malar stripe and surrounding bill including chin. White with smudgy black stripes thinning to belly. Obvious white submoustachial and supraloral stripes. 

Similar Species Discussion

Chestnut-collared Longspur – lacks the following: blue/black bill vs pinkish, white or tawny wing and covert edges vs browns, black around bill and chin vs browns, heavy malar stripe vs none and obvious white and black stripes/spots on throat and chest vs mixed browns.

McCown’s Longspur – lacks the following: blue/black bill vs pink and gray, black malar stripe vs browns, black and tawny median coverts vs rich chestnut, chest is white with smudgy black stripes vs dirty tan/gray with no stripes or spots.

Harris’s Sparrow – lacks the following: large blue/black bill vs bright orange, black surrounding bill vs black crown, chin and throat, short tail vs a long tail.
