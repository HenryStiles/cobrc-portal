Description of the Bird

Petrochelidon swallow hunting low over mud/sandflats with a goodly number (50-100) Cliff Swallows and a similar number of Bank Swallows. Bird first seen when flying straight towards me, making plain orange throat apparent, at which time I drew my companions' attention to the bird.
Shape much like that of Cliff Swallow. Head/neck: Small orange forehead patch that was considerably darker than that of adult (Colorado) Cliff Swallow, but probably within range of young Cliff Swallows. Size of forehead patch consistent with immature of either species. Throat was a medium orange that was distinctly paler than that of any Cliff Swallow present and was unmarred by dark (or light) markings. This orange went up onto auriculars and bled into collar which was grayish on hindneck. The demarcation between color and back was fairly crisp. Crown dull dark glossy blue, barely extending down to eye and curving sharply upwards posterior to eye. Upperparts: Back and wings dull glossy dark blue, without white streaking. Rump orange. Tail dark and square. Underparts: Chest white. Sides orange-buff mixed with dusky. Undertail coverts washed in orange-buff and with very limited markings, seemingly limited a couple narrow dusky smudges on outermost undertail covert feathers, and those only posteriorly.
Note that small area of yellow at gape noted.
Behavior: See Description
Call: Not heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Cliff Swallow, even juv/imm, should have dark, not orange, auriculars. Fresh juv CLiffs can have white and dark mottling nearly anywhere on head, but by this plumage, throat should have distinct dark markings, resembling (or starting to resemble) that of adults. The cap should be more extensive, particularly behind the eye, on Cliff Swallow. The throat should be a darker orange. The border between nape and back is said to be less distinct on Cliff, though this mark I've little experience in assessing. Finally, the less marked undertail coverts on this bird was different than that of the Cliff Swallows (adults and young) flying about.

Additional Information

Comments: Notes taken immediately after bird departed;
Note that in the photos, the bird's throat appears darker orange than in field. Reason for this is unclear, but probably due to angle of lighting. The paler throat was what first caught my attention and was noted by all three observers independently. Also, forehead patch is essentially invisible in photos, but present (albeit small) on the bird.
Finally, I'd like to note that we had superb views of this bird, with multiple close up views in good light.
