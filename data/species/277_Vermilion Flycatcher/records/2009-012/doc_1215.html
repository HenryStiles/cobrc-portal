Description of the Bird

The bird was a smallish flycatcher with a somewhat peaked crown and short bill. The crown, throat, sides of the neck, and all underparts were a brilliant scarlet red. The bill was black. There was a black mask through the eye and rear face. The nape, upperparts and tail were black, with a bit of white fringing along the coverts. The legs were black.
Behaviors: The bird would perch at the end of tree branches, then sally out after moths and then land at the tip of another tree branch.
Call: At one time it gave a soft 'pit pit pit, pedyear'.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The only other bird with a color combination similar to an adult Vermilion Flycatcher would be Scarlet Tanager, which lacks black upperparts and mask and has a much larger bill.
