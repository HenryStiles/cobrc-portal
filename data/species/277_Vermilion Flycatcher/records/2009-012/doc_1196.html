Description of the Bird

The bird had essentially two colors - red and brown. The bird's crown, forehead, and entire underparts were a vibrant bright red. A medium brown, thinnish eyepatch extended from the bird's eye to connect with the same medium brown color of the hindcrown and remainder of the upperparts and tail. The bird's eyes, bill, and legs were dark colored. The bill was fairly short, thin, and sharply pointed.
Behaviors: Actively hunting (flycatching in the air, as well as dropping to ground to pick items from within the grass) from mullein in an open grassland and from branches of several small trees.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: adult
