Description of the Bird

the bird was a small flycatcher, notcieably smaller than nearby Mountain Bluebirds. The overall shape was Phoebe like, but more stocky, mostly due to a shorter tail. The bird had a slight crest. The bill was very small, short and thin, and black. The legs and eye were black.

The bird's head was bright crimson red, except for a very dark gray eye line that got increasingly wider behind the eye and connected with a nape and upperparts the same color. The underparts were entirely crimson red, though there was a small patch on the chest that seemed to be a bit paler. The tail was slightly forked, and the same dark gray/black color as the upperparts.
Behaviors: the bird was flycatching from the tops of mullens out in a field, near a large flock of Mountain Bluebirds.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than Scarlet Tanager were ruled out by the entirely two colors of the bird, and the Tanager was ruled out by the radically different shape, and black on the head, as well as smaller size and behavior.
