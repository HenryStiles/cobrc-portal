Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Vermilion Flycatcher
First Date/Time:  3/19/2006
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  15 minutes
County:  El Paso
Specific Location:  It was at the Banding Station at Chico Basin Ranch.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

It usually stayed in the open and it was usually on the tops of bushes. There were a few large trees in around where the bird was.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski Spotting Scope
Distance:  150 feet
Light:  The sun had just come out from behind the clouds and the sun was to the left of us.

Description of the Bird

Bright red head, chest, belly, and undertail covert. Black on the eye strips, back of the head, back, and tail. It also had a small black bill
Behaviors: It was bug catching and it kept landing on the tops of bushes. It usually stayed in the opening.
Call: Didn't hear it
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Northern Cardinal - It is an all red bird with a black face. It has a crest on the head and a thick red bill.
Scarlet Tanager - All red bird except black wings and tail and it has a gray bill.
Summer Tanager - Larger bird and it is all red with a large bill.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have seen a few Vermillion Flycatchers in Arizona and Mexico
