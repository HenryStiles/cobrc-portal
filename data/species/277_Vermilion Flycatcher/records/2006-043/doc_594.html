Habitat

Rocky and course sand "spit" of land "jutting" out into the reservoir. Sparsely populated with grasses, knapweed, and young cottonwoods.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8.5x44 Brunton Epoch binoculars
Distance:  Varied, approximately 20-60 feet
Light:  light overcast provided even lighting with no harsh shadows.

Description of the Bird

Small flycatcher with dark eyes and a thin, black bill. Crown, auriculars, nape, wings and tail all a uniform medium brown color. Back was gray-brown. Thin white supercillium noted from the beak to behind the eye. Faint black eye-stripe present. Chin and throat white; breast white with faint brown streaks. Belly and flanks salmon colored. Wings brown with two faint white wingbars. Legs black.
Behaviors: Flycatching for insects from small cottonwoods and from the shoreline.
Call: No vocalizations were heard
Plumage: adult female

Similar Species Discussion

No other bird has this combination of white throat, white streaked breast and salmon colored belly with a thin bill, flycatching.

Resources Used

None needed.

Previous Experience

I have seen 6 others actually at Chatfield over the last 17 years (4 were females, 2 were males). Have seen this bird several times in SE Colorado where they nested for several years as well as several times in Arizona.
