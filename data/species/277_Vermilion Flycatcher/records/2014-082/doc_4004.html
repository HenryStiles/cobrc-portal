Description of the Bird

This bird was reported to me from Ann Cummins who resides at this place. I went out with Lisa and Jerry and I spotted the bird in some willows near the edge of a meadow. It was about the size of a House Sparrow, about 6 inches in length. Its face, neck and undersides were bright red. Crown red. Wings, back and tail were brown. Bill was black and thin. Eye was black. Eye has a brown stripe through it. It would sally off its perch, catch a bug and return to its perch or another perch. I took a number of photographs as we watched it.

Similar Species Discussion

There is really no similar bird like this. Was not any finch because it lacked a thick finch bill. Not a cardinal for same reason...lacked thick bill. Not any other finch due to bill differences.
