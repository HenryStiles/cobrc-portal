Description of the Bird

A smallish Phoebe like Flycatcher with thin dark bill, dark back with a slightly darker tail and two faint wing-bars. The bird had a vaguely defined eye-line, a clean white throat, and a white breast with gray streaking. The bird's flanks and belly were salmon pink with the pink on the flanks reaching further towards the breast compared to the pink near the dorsal mid-line. The contrast between the streaked breast and the pink flanks and belly was distinct and not diffuse.
Behaviors: The bird was sallying out from exposed perches flycatching around the edges of Sawhill Ponds 13 and 5. The bird infrequently wagged it's tail. In addition, the bird was aware of our presence and was fairly skittish although it approached to nearly 25 ft. For the first 30 minutes of observation, the bird was clearly patrolling a "territory" utilizing the same perches along the west side of Sawhill Pond 13. Before other observers arrived, the bird slowly made it's way across the northern edge of pond 13 and over to pond 5.
Call: None
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Say's Phoebe is the most likely species to cause confusion with a female Vermilion Flycatcher. Say's Phoebe is a larger bird overall with a generally stouter appearance than Vermilion Flycatcher. The presence of a clean white throat and a whitish streaked chest as well as the more distinct transition between pink belly and white chest indicate the bird I observed was a female Vermilion Flycatcher. The belly color of Say's Phoebe is more of a yellow-orange and not the vibrant pink of the bird I observed. All of these features indicate that the bird I observed is a Vermilion Flycatcher.
