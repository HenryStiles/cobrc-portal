Description of the Bird

The bird was a smallish flycatcher with a somewhat peaked crown, short bill, and moderate tail that sallied from perch to perch while chasing insects. The crown, nape, and upperparts were gray-brown. A white supercilium was present above the eye. The lores were black and there was a grayish-brown face mask posterior to the eye. The throat and breast were white, although the white breast was vertically streaked with gray-brown. The lower belly and undertail coverts were a rich reddish-peach. The wings were dark with thin white wing bars. The tail was blackish with white outer rectrices.
Behaviors: The bird would perch at the outer end of branches of trees and then sally out over the fields after insects, returning to a different perch.
Call: None heard
