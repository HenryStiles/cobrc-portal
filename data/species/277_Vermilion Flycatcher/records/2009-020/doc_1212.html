Description of the Bird

This was a small bird bigger than a kinglet/goldfinch or most warblers/vireos; about the size of a housefinch. It had a fairly long and pointy black fill generally brownish gray on the back with the tail being darker. The chest was streak with difuse gray/brown streaks and the entire belly and vent was a bright, "house finch" pink.
Behaviors: Flycatching from low branches down to the ground.
Call: NA
Plumage: adult female

Similar Species Discussion

I'm not sure there is any similar North American speices. Say's Phoebe is larger, the vent color is a much duller orange-brown and the chest is generally dark rather than white with brown streaks. House Finch was ruled out because the pink was on the bottom not the top--otherwise I would have to go on bill shape.
