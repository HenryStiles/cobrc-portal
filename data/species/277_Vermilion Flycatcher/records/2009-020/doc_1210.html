Habitat

Bird stayed near the water, perching on overhanging limbs, upright poles and sticks, and sometimes cattails.

Light:  It was overcast and raining during the entire observation.

Description of the Bird

The bird was clearly a flycatcher, with an upright posture; it had a short straight black bill. It was smaller than a phoebe, with a shorter tail. The bird was uniformly medium brown above, with two faint pale wingbars and some pale edgings to the flight feathers. Its tail looked uniformly brown, without paler edging or tip. The bird had a thin darker mask through its eye into the upper part of the auriculars. Below this mask, on the throat and chest, the bird was very pale, nearly white; there was strong and sharp contrast between this white and the bird's browner back. Towards the bottom of the white area on the bird's breast there started to be some fine black streaking. Then there was a sharp transition into a reddish-pink color that covered the bird's flanks and belly. This color came farther up the sides of the bird than it did in the middle of the chest.
Behaviors: The bird was very active, hawking insects in midair and returning to open perches 1-10 feet off the ground, usually near the water. It flicked its tail down quite frequently.
Call: Bird was silent.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Say's Phoebe is the primary species to rule out. It was eliminated by size and shape--the Vermilion was a significantly smaller bird with a shorter tail--and by plumage, as the Vermilion had a much rosier tone to the belly, a much whiter breast, and much sharper breast/upperparts contrast than a Say's Phoebe would ever have.
