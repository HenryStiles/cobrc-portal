Description of the Bird

A small red-and-brown flycatcher (smaller and lankier than a nearby Say's Phoebe), the bird had been present since 25 May when found by Mark Peterson. The bird was bright red on crown and on underparts from bill base to tail base. It had dark brown extending from the base of the bill through the loral and auricular regions, connecting to the dark brown on the nape and upperparts. The wings were the same dark brown coloration as the upperparts, except that the remiges were paler brown, almost certainly retained juvenal feathers.
Behaviors: Primarily sallying from cottonwoods after insects, often to ground; occasionally perched on barbed-wire fence. Also occasionally performed typical fluttering flight display with song (described above).
Call: a series of high-pitched notes accelerating into a higher-pitched trill heard>12x
Plumage: first alternate

Similar Species Discussion
