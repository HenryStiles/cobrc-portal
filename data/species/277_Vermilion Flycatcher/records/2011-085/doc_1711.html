Description of the Bird

I first noticed the small passerine, roughly empid or wood-pewee in size, as it fluttered above a 30-foot cottonwood tree. The bird was bi-colored - a bright red on crown and its entire underparts from chin to belly, and an earthy brown on wings, back, tail, and large ocular stripe on the side of its head.
Behaviors: I first noticed it briefly fluttering just above the treetop of a 30-foot cottonwood as if it were displaying. The flycatcher landed on the tree top for less than a minute before flying out of site into a grove of trees. I did not see or hear the bird again during my visit.
