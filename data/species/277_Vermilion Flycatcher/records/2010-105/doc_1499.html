Habitat

Farmstead, with wind row of elms.

Description of the Bird

Male was in the classic brilliant vermilion plumage on the crest andunderparts; upperparts and tail a brown color. Female had a whiter breast, with a very light pink wash on the lower belly; upper parts and tail a slightly lighter color of brown than the male.
Behaviors: 5/31/2010-Male was fly catching from various Elm trees at the homesite. Eventually found the female perched near the male and then saw her settle on to the nest
Call: none observed
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

I am not sure there is any other bird that is similar to the Vermilion Flycatcher. The coloration of this bird is unique to it in the US.
