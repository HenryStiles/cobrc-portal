Description of the Bird

I was along the Canon City Riverwalk, looking north to a green field, looking for the male Vermilion Flycatcher that Rich Miller saw eariler in the day. I had been here for 40 minutes or so, when the male Vermilion Flycatcher came flying in and landed in the top of a tree on the north side of the Arkansas River. The bird was bright red on top the head, and below the brownish cheek area, throughout the entire throat, breast, belly, and undertail coverts. The eyes were dark, and were within the brownish cheek area. Below the bright red top of the head, was all brown, the nape, and wings, and tail. The bird had a thin dark bill, similar to all flycatchers. The shape and size of the bird was like a phoebe, though a little small. Certainly larger that empidonax flycatchers, though smaller than kingbirds.
Behaviors: As mentioned above, only seen as it flew up into the tree across the bird. Looked at the bird through the scope on top of a tree.
