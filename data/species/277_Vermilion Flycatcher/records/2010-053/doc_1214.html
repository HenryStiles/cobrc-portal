Habitat

The bird was perched in an exposed spot in some shrubs. The shrubs were along side a driveway close to a wooden bridge over Boulder Creek. On the opposite side of the drive was a wooden fence and a small corral

Description of the Bird

This was a small bird bigger than a kinglet/goldfinch or most warblers/vireos; about the size of a housefinch. It had a fairly long and pointy black fill generally brownish gray on the back with the tail being darker. The chest was white but streaked with difuse gray/brown streaks and the entire belly and vent was a bright, "house finch" pink.
Behaviors: perched and not very active in a small bush
Call: NA
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Much more extensive red on the belly than a Red-whiskered Bulbul, plus this bird had streaking across most of the chest. Much smaller and wrong bill shape for a Crissal Thrasher. White, streaked breast rules out birds like Painted and Slate-throated Redstart and Gray Catbird. Lacked long tail of Scissor-tailed Flycatcher.
