Description of the Bird

I observed the bird perched on a phone line, next to a Cassin's Kingbird.

It was much shorter than the kingbird (maybe 60% as long), with a much more compact structure.

The upperparts were medium brown, with no markings.

The face was pale brown, with a large, bold, dark brown eye. The bill was thin and dark.

The throat was whitish, unstreaked.

The breast was light gray, with bold, coarse streaking running down to the belly.

The belly and undertail coverts, except for the central (median) third of the belly, were a dull pinkish (maybe rosy with a tinge of orange). The median part of the belly was off-white.

The sides of the tail had narrow strips of off-white color.

The upper surface of the tail was darker than the back.
Behaviors: It was perched on a phone line, with a posture typical for a flycatcher.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

For a medium-sized, compact flycatcher, perched quietly on a phone line in an open area, the only other obvious choices are phoebes. The rich color of the underparts rules out all but Say's Phoebes (and Vermillion Flycatcher). The whitish throat and breast, streaking on the breast, pink color of the belly, and light outer tail feathers, rules out Say's Phoebe.
