Description of the Bird

A gull most similar to Ring-billed Gull but obviously smaller-bodied, -headed and -billed. White head was heavily smudged gray, appearing "hooded", but with some patchiness suggesting active molt to alternate plumage; dark eye; yellow bill with hint of a dark smudge near the tip of the lower mandible; pure white underparts and tail. Upperparts gray, close in shade to California Gull, but with prominent white scapular and tertial crescents, and broad white tips to secondaries visible in flight. The pattern of black and white on the primaries is best described by the supporting photographic documentation itself. P1-5 are gray with prominent white tips. P6-8 have small white tips, each with a subterminal black spot and a prominent white spot contiguous with the black, with the white spots forming a "string of pearls" effect. P9 is all black with a small white mirror on the outer web near the tip. P10 is all black with a large white mirror on the outer web near the tip.
Behaviors: Loafing on shore and swimming in water, and flying over water in close association with a flock of dozens of Ring-billed Gulls.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull is about 10% larger, with heavier, ringed bill, paler gray mantle, less prominent white tips to scapulars, tertials and secondaries, and more extensive black in wing tips. Laughing Gull lacks string-of-pearls pattern on wing-tips; Franklin's Gull has a different pattern of white on wingtips, and lacks the large white mirrors on P9 and P10; California Gull is much larger among other differences. "Common" Mew Gull from Europe has more extensive black on wingtips. "Kamchatka" Mew Gull from Siberia is about 10% larger, and also with more black on wing-tips.
