Description of the Bird

Adult gray-mantled gull, similar in size to nearby Ring-billed Gulls, distinctly smaller than Herring Gulls. Yellow legs, mantle slightly darker than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. Prominent wide tertial crescent, white tips also on innermost scapulars(? or rear-most mantle feathers.) Black primaries with clean white tipping. All white tail and body. Head heavily smudged, darkest smudging around eye. Eye dark brown. Bill small, droopy, and yellow, lacking heavy black ring.
Behaviors: Loafing on ice edge near open water with a few hundred Ring-billed gulls and a smattering of Herring, Thayer's, California, Lesser Black-backed, and Glaucous Gulls.
Call: Not Heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Size rules out California Gull or larger. Dark mantle color, wide tertial crescent, and bill pattern on an adult-plumaged bird rules out Ring-billed Gull.
