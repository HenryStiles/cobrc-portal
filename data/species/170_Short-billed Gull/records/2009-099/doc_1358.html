Description of the Bird

First, my trip report, including a brief written description of the species, can be found here: http://tiny.cc/OXM2U

Note that there were 2 Mew Gulls out there. This report does NOT concern the adult, which I believe has been previously documented--with photographs--by Bill Schmoker. This report concerns only the first-cycle individual, which has not been previously reported to my knowledge.

The bird was small and notably petite in contrast with Ring-billed Gulls sitting and standing immediately next to it. It was dusky all over, with a notably dainty bill. The flight feathers of the wing, as seen on the sitting bird, were darker than the rest of the upperparts; however, the overall appearance of the bird was one of low-contrast, extensive duskiness.
Behaviors: I saw it sitting and standing.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: first-cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull is the ID contender here. The Mew Gull was somewhat smaller and--especially--daintier than Ring-billed Gulls in its immediate vicinity. The bill was characteristically tiny. The dark, dusky, low-contrast, rather uniform-appearing plumage was unlike the higher-contrast plumage of a first-cycle Ring-billed Gull. The bird was too distant to discern eye-color or size.

I note that this bird was of the expected "American" or "Short-billed" subspecies (brachyrhynchus).
