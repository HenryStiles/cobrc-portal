Description of the Bird

The subject bird was a medium-sized gull, slightly smaller overall than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The bill was all yellow, thin, and slightly drooped. No evidence of any black smudging or ring was visible. The head was rounded, and somewhat dove-like. Most of the head and neck was basally white, though the lower nape and hindneck were streaked blurrily with dark brown. The eyes appeared dark. The breast, belly, undertail coverts, and tail were white. The mantle was gray, not as gray as some Mew Gull's I have seen, but noticeably a shade darker than that of nearby Ring-billed Gulls. Obvious, and a key separation from Ring-billed Gull, was a distinct, white scapular crescent. The white tertial crescents were also very broad, compared to Ring-billeds. The primaries were long, black, and narrow, with at least five visible white primary mirrors. When preening (see one of attached pictures), the mirror of P10 was quite extensive, very much longer than Ring-billeds.
Behaviors: Swimming on open water lake. Preened a bit as it swam.
