Description of the Bird

A gull smaller and darker-mantled than neighboring Ring-billed Gulls (RBGU) swimming on lake. The rounded, white head had relatively extensive brown blotching with a particular concentration of streaking on the nape. The bill was short, thin, entirely yellow, and appeared to lack a pronounced gonydeal angle. The eyes were dark. Except for some brown on the neck, the underparts were unmarked white; the tail was white. The mantle was a medium gray, obviously darker than that of the RBGUs. The bird had large and well-defined white scapular and tertial crescents and a large white mirror on P10

We never saw the bird get off the water, so cannot describe the wingtip pattern (other than that already mentioned) or leg color.
Behaviors: Swimming and, briefly, preening on the water's surface
Call: none heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

RBGU is readily eliminated by size, head pattern, mantle coloration, and well-defined scapular and tertial crescents; Common and Kamchatka gulls poorly ruled out by mantle coloration and size
