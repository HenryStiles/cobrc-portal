Description of the Bird

A small gull, about 10% smaller than adjacent Ring-billed Gulls (RBGU), the bird's head was light brown with a whitish aspect, with this color blending into darker brown on the neck and the rest of the body that we could see. The bill was quite short and, at the distance seen, appeared nearly all dark; there was at least a bit of the base that was pale. The bill was about 2/3 to 3/4 the length of the bill's of the nearby RBGUs, was considerably thinner than those of the RBGUs, and lacked a gonydeal angle. The eyes were dark.

As stated above, the upperparts, chest, and wings (except for the primaries) were all the same medium brown in color and had no warm aspect. The greater coverts were just slightly paler and the primaries and tail were blackish. I did not see/note the belly color.

The bird rode high in the water, appearing even more buoyant than did the RBGUs, with the back angled up from neck to wingtip, and the wingtips crossed near the tip of the tail with the wingtip projection being quite long (longer than that on the RBGUs).

When the bird jumped up and dove into the water after a fish (I presume), I could see that the tail was entirely dark and that the rump was a bit paler brown than the back. As it jumped facing me, I still didn't really see the belly.
Behaviors: Swimming and, once, leaping up and diving into water after prey
Call: None heard
Plumage: Formative

Similar Species Discussion

RBGU ruled out by size, bill size and shape, overall coloration, tail pattern and color; Laughing Gull (LAGU) eliminated by bill size and shape, our bird's small, rounded head with no suggestion of blackish hood or mask, and overall size; no other gull would be that small and that brown
