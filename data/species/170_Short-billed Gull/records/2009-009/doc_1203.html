Description of the Bird

A very brown bird. Brown back, brown belly, most of the head is brown (with some white on the head), white and brown chest, white and brown undertail coverts, dark brown to black primaries. Dark eye. Bicolored bill, base of the bill is pink and the outer half is black. The bill is also long and thin. Light pink legs. Very small bird, smaller than all the other birds that it was by. When I first saw it while I was driving by I could see all the brown and new it could be nothing else but a Mew Gull.
Behaviors: Sitting on the ice with all the other gulls. Occasionally being flushed (by raptors and cars driving by), but then landing back on the ice.
Call: Didn't knowingly hear it
Plumage: 1st year

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull - It was smaller than any of the RBGUs. Wings are darker than the bird that I saw. They are starting to get gray on the mantle. More pink on the bill and it is a thicker and slightly longer bill. It has dark centers on coverts, but the bird that I saw didn't have that. They usually have a black tail band.
black headed gulls - They all have dark wings A little to a lot of black on the head, clean white bellies, not much, if any brown on the back, and no brown on the head.
Large white headed gulls - These gulls are larger than RBGUs, they have large, thick bills.
