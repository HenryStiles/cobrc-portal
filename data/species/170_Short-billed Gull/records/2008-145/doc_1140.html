Description of the Bird

A small gull, smaller than neighboring Ring-billed Gulls (RBGU), the bird was very well marked. It's head was heavily smudged with medium brown, forming a well-demarcated hood. The head was small and quite rounded. The eyes were dark and the bill was short, thin, and entirely yellow, but for a tiny smudge near the tip.

The mantle and upperside to the wings were a medium gray, obviously a shade or two darker than those of the nearby RBGUs, but just a bit paler than those of a nearby californica California Gull (CAGU). The belly was white.

The bird's upper surface while swimming was angled up from the neck to the wingtips, more so than even were the RBGUs, creating a more-buoyant appearance than presented by the RBGUs. The wingtip projection was quite long and the wingtips crossed at about the tip of the tail.
Behaviors: Swimming with other gulls (RBGU, CAGU, Herring Gull, Lesser Black-backed Gull)
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

RBGU lack such prominently-brown heads, have pale eyes, an obviously ringed bill that is longer, thicker, and sports a strong gonydeal angle (unlike that of the bird in question), and is overall larger. CAGU is much larger (typically), with a slightly darker (in californica) or paler (in albertaensis) mantle than our bird had. That species also has a much larger bill with black and/or red at and near the gonys.
