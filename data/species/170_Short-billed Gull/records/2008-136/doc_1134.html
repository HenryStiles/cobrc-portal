Description of the Bird

the bird was a small gull, larger than Bonaparte's Gull, but noticeably smaller than adjacent Ring-billed Gulls. The overall shape was very similar to that of Ring-billed Gull, except the wings were proportionally longer, and the head was more rounded and dove-like, accentuated by the shorter and thinner bill. The bill was short for a gull, and thin; in both dimensions relatively smaller than that of Ring-billed Gull. It was yellow with a dusky gray ring near the tip. The irides were dark brown, barely paler than the pupil. The legs were yellow.

The head of the bird was white, and extensively covered with brown streaks. These streaks were a bit warmer toned than those of Ring-billed Gull, as well as a tad bit "blurrier". The continued down the sides of the neck and chest, and were strongest on the lower sides of the neck and upper parts of the chest, creating a faint "collared" look to the bird. The remainder of the underparts were white, with a few faint brown bars on the belly.

The mantle of the bird was medium gray, a couple of shades darker than Ring-billed Gull and about the same shade as California Gull. The bird had a large white tertial crescent and a small white scapular crescent. The wingtips were black, with small white tips on the folded primaries. In flight, there was a large white mirror on p10 and a smaller white mirror on p9. There was also a "string of pearls" affect created by small white spots between the gray of the outer primary bases and the black of the outer primary tips.
Behaviors: The bird was first found as we were chumming the gull flock in for a closer look. It came quite close, and spent it's time on the water at our feet, occasionally flying around and snagging a piece of bread. Eventually it lost interest and flew off to points unknown.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of gulls were ruled out by the small size of this bird in comparison to Ring-billed Gull. Hooded gulls were ruled out by the brown streaking of the bird, as well as the wing pattern more typical of a larger gull. Ring-billed Gull was ruled out by the smaller size, darker mantle, larger tertial crescent, smaller and thinner bill without bold black ring, more dove-like head shape, dark eye, and large mirror on p10. Common Gull was ruled out by the "string of pearls" pattern on the spread wing, and the darker coloration of the mantle.
