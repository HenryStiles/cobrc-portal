Description of the Bird

Small gull with all-yellow, short and thin bill. Mantle slightly darker gray than RBGUs and bird slightly smaller that that species. Neck heavily streaked. Prominent white tertial and scapular crescents.
Dark wingtips with at least two white mirrors on outermost primaries.
Behaviors: Loafing with hundreds of RBGUs and scores of CAGUs.
Call: none heard
Plumage: winter adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull comes closest in size. At this age RBGU has a subterminal dark band on its yellowish bill, much thicker bill, less streaks on its nape, less distinct tertial and scapular crescents. Eyes yellow. Both species had yellow legs.
