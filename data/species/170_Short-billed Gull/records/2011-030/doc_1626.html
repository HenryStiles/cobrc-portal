Description of the Bird

Note: I saw this bird on 2 dates. This description is based on my sighting on the 2nd day of viewing, because I saw the bird better that day.

The bird was a straightforward basic adult Mew Gull. Seen in the general vicinity of Ring-billed Gulls, it was smaller, daintier, and darker-mantled. It had a light to moderate (for a basic Mew Gull) brown streaking on the nape. Its dainty bill looked to be entirely yellow. Its staring black eye stood out on its small, rounded head. Its long wing tips were mainly black, with large white spots.
Behaviors: Standing on ice. It was generally in front of a large mass of Ring-billed Gulls, although a few Ring-billeds were standing to its left and right, i.e., the same distance from me.

At one point, it flew off, although I did not see this happen.
Call: none heard.
Plumage: Basic adult
