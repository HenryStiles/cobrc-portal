Description of the Bird

The bird was a small gull, a bit smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. Its head was white with extensive gray-brown streaking on the nape and gray-brown smudging on the rest of the head. The eyes were dark brownish-yellow and the bill was thin, parallel-sided, short, and yellow with an indistinct blackish ring at the gonys. The underparts were white, except for a bit of gray-brown smudging on the sides of the breast (extension of the nape streaking). The back was medium gray, somewhat darker than that of the nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The bird had distinct and obvious white tertial and scapular crescents that contrasted strongly with the color of the back and wings.

The folded wings were the same color as the back, except, of course, for the blackish outer five (or so) primaries and the corresponding bright white tips to those. The tip of the tail was just beyond the tip of p7 -- thus quite a long wing.

In flight, pp 9 and 10 were nearly all black, except for large white mirrors and the aforementioned white tips; pp 6-8 (at least) had white tongues and small black straps. The effect of these markings was to give the bird a fairly pale-tipped wing, as there was relatively little black in the wingtip. In addition, the white tips to the secondaries were fairly wide giving the wing a wide trailing edge.

The legs were medium-bright yellow. The tail was white. Though others noted a faint tail band, this was not something that I noted in the short time that I saw the bird in flight.
Behaviors: The bird perched on the tires for most of the time of observation; at the end, it and the Ring-billed Gulls flew a bit to the other end of the tires.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull eliminated by head pattern; eye color; bill size, shape, and markings; upperparts coloration; wingtip pattern, well-defined and obvious scapular and tertial crescents. California Gull ruled out by size; bill size, shape, and markings; and wingtip pattern.
