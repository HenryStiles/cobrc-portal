Description of the Bird

This is a small-billed, round-headed gull with yellow legs about the size of or a little smaller than a Ring-billed Gull.

The very thin yellow bill with a very thin grayish mark at the gonys, small brownish eye was evident. The bird was seen in the water and flying. On the water the mantle color was noticeably darker than nearby Ring-billed Gulls, the tertial and scapular white crescents stood out, especially the significant width of the tertail crescent. Gray spotting was dense and confined to the crown and nape, abscent from the sides of the face. The bill was distinctly yellowish with only a small dark gash running up from the gonys. Bill was shorter than that of RBGU.

In flight the most noticeable feature was the two large white mirrors on P9 and P10. A faint "string of pearls" could be seen contrasting with the otherwise dark (except the white mirrors) wingtips. These "pearls" extended along the primaries from about p10 through p5 and then quite visible was a wide white trailing edge to the flight feathers. A heavily spotted and darkish hindneck stood out from the rest of the head and the tail appeared all-white.

The head and neck were less densly spotted than RBGUs and the head appeared shorted and quite rounded (See photo)
Behaviors: Mostly sitting on the water. When in flight, made erractic tacks to the wind dropping to the water three times and then taking flight again after each descent to the water.
Call: none
Plumage: adult winter

Similar Species Discussion

Common Gull - the European counterpart has a longer bill than this bird and usually a distinct blackish 'Z" above the gonys. Common gull has more robust bill and it's bill is usually longer in length. It's head is usually less round and overall a bit bigger. It's bill is less tapered than on Mew Gull.

Ring-billed Gull. Mantle is less dark than this bird with distinctly thicker bill with more dark marking on the distall end causing a dark ring, not found on this bird or other MEGUs. Ring-billed less rounded head and less petite look. Much more head markings on RBGU than on this bird. Because the mantle is lighter gray the tertial and scapular crescents are not distinct on RBGU and the width of the white on RBGU appears much less.
