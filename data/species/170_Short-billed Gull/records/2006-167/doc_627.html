Description of the Bird

This is a small gull, slightly smaller than a Ring-billed Gull with which it associated.

Distincive features were mantle coloration, about two shades darker than nearby RBGU, bill thickness, thinner than RBGU, all yellow and short, except an distinctive black mark which makes this bird unique in appearance. The tertial crescents are broad white and contrast nicely with the mantle. The feet were yellowish. The neck streaking was distinctive with thick, closely spaced smudges on the hindneck, also noticeable different from RBGU.
Behaviors: Sat on a tire the entire time I observed on both days.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull is similar but a bit bigger, lighter mantled without the prominent neck streaking, without the mostly thin yellow bill (RBGU has a distinct distal ring at this age), without the prominent tertial crescents of MEGU. See photo for more comparisons.
