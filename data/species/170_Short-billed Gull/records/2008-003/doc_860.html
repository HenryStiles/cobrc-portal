Description of the Bird

This was a small gull, slightly smaller than the nearby Ring-billed Gulls. This bird had a medium gray mantle a few shades darker than Ringers, thin unmarked dull yellow bill, rounded head, dark brown eye, large white tertial crescents, and a overall petit look. The head was heavily streaked with brown. Feet were not visible.
Behaviors: Loafing in open waters of Pueblo Reservoir.
Call: non heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull - Ring-billed Gull always has a wider, ringed bill at this age. Mantle of RBGU is less gray with noticably smaller white tertial crescents. Head usually not this marked on RBGU adult winter.

Common Gull looks quite similar, not recognized yet by AOU, and almost always has a dark mark near the bill tip, unlike this Mew Gull

Previous Experience

I have worked in Alaska for 5 summers and seen many Mew Gulls. In CO RBGU is the most common gull, and I have never seen a Common Gull.
