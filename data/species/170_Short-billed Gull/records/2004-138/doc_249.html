Description of the Bird

Adult gull similar to Ring-billed gull but slighlty (5-10%) smaller, with slightly darker gray mantle with broad white tertial crescent and white scapular crescent, wings with black tips, each black primary tipped with white (when sitting); white head and underparts, with head heavily smudged with gray-brown streaks, especially on the nape; dark eye; small yellowish bill with dark smudge near tip. Thin yellowish legs.
Behaviors: Sitting on ice.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic aka adult winter

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull is slightly larger, lighter gray, larger-billed, paler-eyed, and lacks obvious scapular crescent. California Gull is larger, larger-billed, larger-headed, with blue-green legs. Heading smudging on nape extending down on to chest is more typical of "short-billed" Mew Gull (L. c. brychyrhynchos) rather than "European" Mew Gull, or "Common Gull", L.c. canus, and dark eye is atypical of "Kamchatka Gull", L.c.kamtchatchensis. Franklin's Gull would have black bill, black legs and more hooded look.
