Description of the Bird

While looking at gulls off West Fisherman's Point at Pueblo Reservoir around 11am, I spotted an adult basic plumaged Mew Gull. The gull was a little bit smaller than the nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The things I noticed were the darker gray mantle, all thin yellow bill, dark eyes, and in flight, a bigger white spot in the wing tips. The bird had a whitish head, with some brown streaking on neck area, and all white belly and under tail. The bird showed the white cresent, below the mantle, which Ring-billed Gull doesn't have. The bird was an adult bird. I couldn't see leg color, as the bird was mostly on the water swimming with other gulls. The wing tips in flight showed a bigger white area, bigger than in Ring-billed Gulls.
Behaviors: Swimming and flying with other gulls at Pueblo Reservoir.
Call: none
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull: this species which was right next to the bird, is bigger, light gray mantle, ring around the yellow bill, no cresent, and smaller white spot in the wing tips.
