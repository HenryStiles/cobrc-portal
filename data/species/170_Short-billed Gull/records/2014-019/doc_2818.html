Description of the Bird

A small gull among Ring-billed and California gulls that flew past me undetected except that I unknowingly took one picture of it flying by.  It came into a pack of other gulls loafing and bathing in the SE corner of the reservoir, where I later found it swimming.  It was smaller than the nearby Ring-billeds, with a mantle about as dark as those of the Californias and sported dark amber eyes; a small, thin bill with a vague darker band near the gonydeal angle; large mirrors on both p9 and p10; and thick and well-defined scapular and tertial crescents.

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull is larger, with paler mantle, paler eyes, thicker and longer bill with obvious and well-defined black band at gonydeal angle, thin and ill-defined scapular crescents, and thin tertials crescents.  California Gull is much larger with much darker eyes and a larger bill with a different pattern (including at least some red at the gonydeal spot).
