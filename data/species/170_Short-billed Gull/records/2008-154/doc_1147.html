Description of the Bird

This was a small gull about the size of a RBGU, but with a shade or two darker mantle. It had an unmarked thin and short yellow bill. One the mantle were both wide whitish scapular and tertial crescents.

The leg color was yellow. The head was densely streaked dark brown. This bird is unique in that the bird's left foot appears broken or bent at an odd angle.
Behaviors: Loafing in and out of the water. When out, it was on the ice.
Call: non heard
Plumage: basic adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-billed Gull is a look-alike species, however this adult MEGU had a very thin bill without any dark distal ring. It's mantle was a few shades darker than the RBGUs nearby and the wide white scapular and tertial crescents were noticeably wider than those of the RBGUs.
