Description of the Bird

the bird was a fairly small gull, slightly but noticeable smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The overall shape was similar to Ring-billed Gull, but with a more delicate look to it, possibly due to the small bill. The bill was shorter and more delicate than those on nearby Ring-billed Gulls, and was entirely yellow. The legs were yellow. The irides was dark.

The bird's head was extensively marked with dark streaks, concentrated as a large collar, and around the eye, giving the bird a "big-eyed" look. The mantle was dark medium gray, a couple of shades darker than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The bird had a large and bold tertial crescent, and a small white crescent further up the mantle. The wingtips were black with noticeable white mirrors on the folded wing. I did not see the bird in flight, so cannot comment on the size of the P10 mirror. The underparts and tail were clean white.
Behaviors: the bird was loafing on the edge of the ice fairly close to the shore with a mixed species gull flock.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species of gull were ruled out by small size. Yellow legs ruled out California Gull, as did smaller size in comparison to Ring-billed Gull, smaller and unmarked yellow bill, and pattern of the streaking on the head. Most small gulls species were ruled out by the straking on the head and lack of any black present in hooded gulls. Ring-billed Gull was ruled out by the darker mantle in direct comparison to this bird, more streaked head, dark iris, smaller and unmarked bill, larger white tertial crescent, and more delicate look.
