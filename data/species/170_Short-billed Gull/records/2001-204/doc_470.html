Description of the Bird

the bird was a small gull, slightly but noticeable smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The shape of the gull was similar to that of the Ring-billed Gulls as well. The bill was all unmarked yellow, and shorter and a little thinner than that of the Ring-billed. The iris was dark.

The head had a lot of streaking, concentrated in an area on the nape and sides of the neck, creating a slightly "collared" look. The streaking was also stronger on the back of the head and crown, and sparsest on on the throat. The area around the eye had a concentration of streaking, giving the bird a "big-eyed" appearance.

The underparts were unmared white. The mantle was a medium slate-gray, a few shaded darker than nearby Ring-billed. The white tertial crescent was large and bold. In flight we could see very large outer primary mirrors. The tail was unmarked white.
Behaviors: The bird was first observed perched on the water below the platform. It then flew with some other gulls to the other side of the lake, at which point we lost track of it.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Herring and Thayer's Gulls were ruled out by small size, unmared yellow bill that was dainty compared to those species, and darker shade of the mantle. California Gull was ruled out by small size, unmarked and dainty yellow bill, large outer primary mirrors, and "collar" and "big-eyed" pattern to head streaking.

Ring-billed Gull was ruled out by slightly smaller size, daintier and unmarked bill, noticeable darker shade of the mantle, large tertial crescent, and "collar" and "big-eyed" appearance to the streaking.
