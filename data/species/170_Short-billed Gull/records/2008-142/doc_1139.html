Description of the Bird

It was a small gull standing with Ring-billed Gulls (RBGU) on ice, the bird was obviously smaller and much browner than the nearby RBGUs. As it was facing away and to our right, I could not discern all details that I would have preferred, but the bird was more than distinctive enough to ID with the view provided.

The bird's head was small and rounded (though not as tiny as the head of the Mew Gull (MEGU) seen the previous day at JMR), being obviously smaller than that of the neighboring RBGUs. The bill was shorter and thinner than those of the RBGUs, and lacked an obvious gonydeal angle. It was mostly black with about the basal 1/4 of the bill pale. The eyes were dark (or, at least the right one was).

The head, neck, chest, back, and wings (except for the primaries) were the same medium brown. The wingtips were black, as was all of the tail that I could see. The bird had a long wingtip projection.
Behaviors: Standing on ice

Plumage: Formative

Similar Species Discussion

RBGUs are and were larger, larger-headed, larger-billed, and are never as brown as was our bird. Laughing Gull (LAGU) would be slightly larger than our bird, but would have very different head and bill shapes and would not be as brown as our bird at this time of year.
