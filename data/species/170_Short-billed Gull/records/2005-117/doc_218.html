Description of the Bird

Typical adult white-headed gull in winter, that is white underparts and head with gray-brown smudging on nape and crown; gray mantle and wings, with black and white wing tips, white tail. Superficially similar to accompanying adult Ring-billed Gulls, but overall about 10% smaller, with rounder head, apparently darker eye, smaller bill with only a dark smudge rather than a well-defined ring; thinner legs; darker gray wings and mantle; large white scapular crescent; larger white mirror on tenth primary.
Behaviors: Preening and loafing on rocky sandbar in a small lake derived from an old gravel pit.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Mantle too dark for Ring-billed Gull which also lacks large white scapular crescent; head shape too round for RBGU; bill too small.

Amount of black in wingtip as seen in flight too limited on 8th primary for "Common Gull", Larus canus canus/heinei (see flight photo) and "Kamchatka Gull", Larus canus kamstchatschensis. Overall size too small, head too round, scapular crescent too large, and bill too small for "Kamchatka Gull".

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have carefully observed about a dozen Mew Gulls in Colorado in the last 6 years or so. I have observed numerous Common Gulls (without careful observation) in England in 1985. I have no experience with Kamchatka Gull.
