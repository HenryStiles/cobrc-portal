Description of the Bird

This gull-shaped bird stood out in the front of a flock of several hundred, mainly adult Ring-billed and Herring Gulls. The bird was slightly smaller than the Ring-billed Gulls next to it. In addition it had a noticably smaller more delicate bill which appeared to be almost entirely dark.

The overall plumage of the bird was a warm, dark brown with the tail and primaries being more black than brown. The mantle still seemed more brown than dark gray, and even the head and chest remained fairly dark brown. Leg color not noted.
Behaviors: The bird was standing in idly in a large flock of resting gulls.
Call: silent
Plumage: First winter or juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Smaller than a Ring-billed Gull, with a much more delicate bill and darker plumaged. Hooded gulls would be even smaller, and only something like Laughing Gull (which might be the right size, actually) would have this brown plumage. LAGU, however, would have a more prominent bill.
