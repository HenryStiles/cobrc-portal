Description of the Bird

A small gull, the bird landed in front of the juv. Iceland Gull that I was studying at the time. Almost immediately, it move to the end of the tires and sat nicely for photographs.

It was obviously smaller than all nearby Ring-billed Gulls (RBGU) and was quite brown. The bird's head was small and rounded, unlike the more severe shape of the heads of RBGU. The bill was quite short, very thin, and lacked an obvious gonydeal angle. In all dimensions, the bill was smaller than that of the RBGUs. The eyes were dark and the legs were pink.

The head, neck, chest, belly, back, and wings were the same medium brown, except for the black wingtips and the obviously paler greater coverts. The tail was entirely blackish.

This bird was obviously different from both of the other formative-plumaged Mew Gulls (MEGU) that I saw this week, as it lacked the whiter head of the Bent Co. bird (16 Dec) and it had obviously pale greater coverts that the Crowley Co. bird (17 Dec) lacked.
Behaviors: Flying in and landing with other gulls on tires
Call: None heard
Plumage: Formative

Similar Species Discussion

RBGUs are larger, larger-headed, larger-billed, and never as brown as the bird in question. California Gulls (CAGU) are much larger and larger-billed. Laughing Gulls (LAGU) are longer-legged, black-legged, have very different head and bill shapes, and would not be as brown as our bird at this time of year.
