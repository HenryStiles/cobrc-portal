Description of the Bird

The birds were about the size of a Western Tanager, with the same heavy bill and build (compared to a warbler). The bill was blackish or silvery (depending on the light), and the legs were dark.

The male bird was almost entirely a bright red color, about the same shade as a Summer Tanager, but perhaps a bit deeper. It was brightest red on the throat, cap, and rump. The flanks and lower belly had a mixing of grayish-red feathers, giving it a slightly contrastingly duller look to the brighter throat and chest. The grayish flanks and and lower belly were harder to see on this bird than most other Hepatic Tanagers I've seen, and this made this bird appear particularly bright. The back also had some grayish feathers. The bird had a contrasting grayish-red auricular patch that was obvious in most lights. The wings and tail were the same red as the rest of the bird.

The female bird was an intense yellow below, with a strong orangish tinge, especially on the throat and belly. This bird had a contrasting grayish auricular that only had a very slight yellowish tinge. The cap was yellowish, though less intense than the underparts. The upperparts were also yellowish, though with a slight greenish tinge rather than an orange one. The wings and tail were about this same color, though with a grayish color, especially on the underside of the tail.
Behaviors: Both the male and female were seen bringing food into a nest that I found in a pine tree, where they were feeding two older nestlings. They would come in about every three minutes. They were both calling almost continuously, and we found the birds by listening for this call. A third bird, probably a male, was heard calling from a different part of the ponderosa grove.
Call: The bird continuously gave a emphatic "chik" note, not very metallic but not too flat sounding either.
Plumage: adult male and female

Similar Species Discussion

Almost all other species were ruled out by the bright red color of the male. Northern Cardinal was ruled out by the very different shape without a finch-like bill, lack of a crest, lack of a black facial mask, black bill, very different behavior, etc.

Western Tanager was ruled out by the mostly red color of the male, lack of wingbars, etc. Scarlet Tanager was ruled out by the lack of a contrasting black wings and tail, dark bill, less intense red color, habitat, etc.

Summer Tanager was ruled out by the contrasting grayish auricular and flanks, slightly deeper red color, dark bill, grayish coloring on the back, and habitat.
