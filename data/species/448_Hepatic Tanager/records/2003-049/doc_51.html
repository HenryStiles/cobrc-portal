Description of the Bird

I observed the bird for one minute as it perched near the top of a snag, in excellent light.

Its underparts were a uniform, unstreaked brick red.

The back was more grayish than red.

The head was red, with a prominent gray eye patch, narrow in front of the eye, and flaring widely behind the eye.

The bill was thick and grayish.
Behaviors: It flew up to near the top of a snag, perched there for a minute, and then flew off. It did not act like a flycatcher.
Call: none heard
Plumage: breeding plumage

Similar Species Discussion

First, I will add (from memory, alas) one small item that is missing from my field notes. The bird was somewhat smaller than an American Robin, and much more slender.

The color, size, and shape of the bird narrows the choice to a tanager or possibly an oriole. The thick bill and red face rules out any North American oriole. The red color indicates a male tanager, and rules out Western Tanager. The lack of black wings rules out Scarlet Tanager, and the choice is between Hepatic and Summer Tanager. The brick red color, grayish back, and gray eye patch is only consistent with Hepatic Tanager.
