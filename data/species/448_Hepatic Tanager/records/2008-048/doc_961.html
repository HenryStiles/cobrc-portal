Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized passerine, noticeably larger than nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers, but smaller than an American Robin. The overall shape was similar to that of a warbler, but heavier in build. The bill was dark gray, fairly long and heavily built, with a broad base tapering slightly to a blunt tip.

The crown, throat, and upper chest were yellow with a noticeable orange tinge, strongest on the throat, and tapering at the lower end of the chest into a line of yellow down the center of the belly. The cheek and nape were grayish brown, as was th back, flanks, and tail. The wings were the same brownish color, with no wingbars, and had a slight yellow tinge, as did the tail.
Behaviors: the bird was part of a mixed flock, staying high up in a tree and moving about lethargically. It first came in to a mobbing tape, and sat still for a few minutes before moving around a little and then flying out. It was not seen again
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than tanagers were ruled out by the distinctive shape, larger than a warbler but smaller than a robin. Western Tanager was ruled out by a lack of wingbars. Scarlet tanager was ruled out by the browner plumage and brown wings that did not contrast with the rest of the body. Summer Tanager was ruled out by the smaller bill, and extensively brownish gray plumage, especially in the cheeks and flank.
