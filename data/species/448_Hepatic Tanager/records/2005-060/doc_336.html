Description of the Bird

The bird was a brick-red tanager, without strong markings anywhere. The bill was dark and shorter than I'd expect on Summer Tanager; the cheek area had a sooty wash on top of the red color. Size and shape were similar to Western Tanagers in the same area.
Behaviors: The bird was singing from the tops of junipers, especially dead parts of live junipers, on a slope well uphill from the road. It was only occasionally singing from places where it could be seen. I thought at certain points that there could have been two males singing, but I was not able to confirm this, and the presence of singing male Black-headed Grosbeaks could be a spoiler on that point.
Call: The bird was located by its song, which was surprisingly similar to that of Black-headed Grosbeak, and rather unlike that of other Piranga tanagers. The presence of singing Black-headed Grosbeaks on the same hillside complicated ID, at least until I was able to get a visual on the singing male HepTan.
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Summer Tanager presents the biggest ID challenge. It was eliminated by vocalizations, dirty cheek patch, and dark bill shorter than I'd expect on a SUTA. Cardinal was eliminated by vocalizations, lack of black face mask and crest, and bill. Scarlet Tanager has black wings and tail. All Carpodacus finches were eliminated by large size, tanager shape, tanager bill, vocalizations, uniformly reddish plumage and lack of streaking.

Previous Experience

I have seen many Hepatic Tanagers in Arizona, Texas, and several Mexican states. However, I was not particularly familiar with their song and had to familiarize myself via tapes. This was my first HepTan in Colorado.
