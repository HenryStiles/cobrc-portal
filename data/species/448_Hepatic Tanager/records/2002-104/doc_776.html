Description of the Bird

Mike Henwood found an apparently adult male Hepatic Tanager near the Casita and some of us at the Chico Basin Ranch for the Monitoring Colorado's Birds training session got to see the bird that evening, but all of us saw it the next morning when it was around the banding station -- eventually captures in net 8.

The bird was an obvious male Piranga (bright red body plumage, tomial notch on bill) that in the hand showed some feathers that may indicate that it wasn't fully adult. However, the bird could not have been in its first plumage cycle, as males of that age in May should show an extensive amount of yellow feathers remaining from their first basic plumage.

The plumage was nearly all red with dark shadowing on the auriculars, a quite dark loral area, and with the crown and underparts brighter and paler than were the upperparts. The wings, too, were red, though the flight feathers were dark with strong red fringes. The bill was dark, but with a paler, bluer, base to the mandible. The eyes were dark brown and the legs were black.

The yellow, yellow-orange, and yellow-green feathers in the face should not, according to Pyle (1997), necessarily indicate that the bird was in its second calendar year (see appropriate account in that reference). However, the fact that the inner two primaries had greeny-yellow to yellow-orange edges confuses me as to the age of the bird. Perhaps it was in its second spring (or third calendar year).
Behaviors: Foraging at medium and low-medium levels in woodlot; also hanging briefly in net and posing for hand-held photos.
Call: None heard
Plumage: possibly Definitive Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Summer Tanager is similar, but lacks the dark area on the face and is generally brighter red. No other species is particularly similar.
