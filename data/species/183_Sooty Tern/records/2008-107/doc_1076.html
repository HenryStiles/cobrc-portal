Description of the Bird

A large tern roughly the size of nearby Ring-billed Gulls, larger than nearby Forster's Terns and nearly twice as larger as nearby Black Terns. The bird was very dark above, ink black, and white-ish below including underwing coverts. The flight feathers (Primaries and Secondaries) below were completely dark, all the way up to the coverts, giving the bird a dark trailing edge to the wing as seen from below. This dark edge became broader towards the wing tip as more primaries were exposed from the coverts than secondaries. The bird had a dark cap, white face and forehead with dark line extending into the lores, seperating the white forehead from the white face below the eyeline. A long, dark, forked tail with white undertail coverts. White outside edge of tail was not discernable in the field.
Behaviors: Flying along the shore of Lake Meredith, occasionally dipping it's bill into the water, while in flight.
Call: none detected
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Bridled Tern - bird was ink black above, with completely dark primaries, not dark tipped primaries of Bridled Tern

Black Tern - much larger and darker than the many Black Terns in the vicinity. Also had a long forked tail, unlike Black Tern
