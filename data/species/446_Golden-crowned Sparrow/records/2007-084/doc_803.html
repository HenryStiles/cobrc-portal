Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized sparrow, about the same size as nearby White-crowned Sparrows. The shape was similar to those species as well, somewhat bulky, though less so than Fox Sparrow. The bill was bicolored, with the upper mandible gray and the lower pinkish.

The sides of the bird's crown were black, broader than the black crown stripes of White-crowned Sparrow. There was a broad stripe of yellow down the center of the crown, which was bordered on either side by the black sides of the crown. One side had the black intermixed with a few brown feathers, likely indicating that it was still molting into alternate plumage.

The face was a clean gray, and the underparts were paler gray, with no markings. The back was medium brown with bold black stripes. The wings were also brown, with two thin white wing bars. The tail was the same color brown as the back and wings.
Behaviors: the bird was associating with a small flock of White-crowned Sparrrows, spending some of it's time in a small tree, and the rest in a shrubby ravine. The bird was not terribly shy, though some of the time it was hard to see as it skulked in the brush.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of sparrows, including White-crowned, were ruled out by the head pattern, with bold and wide yellow stripe down the center of the crown bordered on the sides by broad black stripes.
