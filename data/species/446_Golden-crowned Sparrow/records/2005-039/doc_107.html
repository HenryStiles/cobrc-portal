Habitat

Most of the surrounding terrain is Pinyon Juniper habitat. The state park and campground area has been altered a lot, including a nearby golf course. The bird was staying in or next to a gully with running water, with lots of willows and cottonwood trees in the gully. The banks of the gully had other shrubs including rabbitbrush that the bird would use for cover at times as it was foraging out of the gully.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica Ultravid 8X42, Nikon D70 at 900mm equivalent
Distance:  About 8 meters at closest
Light:  Good light coming from behind my position.

Description of the Bird

A blocky, large sparrow. Bill mostly dull yellowish orange, mandible perhaps a shade paler, culmen dark gray. Black cap above mid eye level, eye is half in black cap and half in the mostly unmarked gray of the rest of the face and throat. Lower half of eye has thin white border. Eye is dark. The front half of the wide median crown stripe is golden yellow. The rear half abruptly turns pale grayish white. The belly is mostly unmarked gray, transitioning to browner tones on the flanks. Feet and legs are orangeish pink. Wings are mostly dark edged brown, with thin, broken wing bars formed in the tips of the median and greater coverts. Tail is mostly featureless dark grayish brown.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding amongst catkins on a budding cottonwood tree when first found. Later it was seen foraging on the ground amongst rabbitbrush plants and at the base of a juniper. Inbetween sightings it appeared to retreat into heavy brushy willows and other dense vegetation in a small gully with flowing water.
Call: I heard a few call notes but could not separate them from White crowned Sparrow with certainty.
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

White crowned Sparrow has a completely white median crown stripe, and the black cap is broken by a white supercilium, both of which this bird lacked. White throated Sparrows also have a pale supercilium. To my knowledge no other sparrow has the combination of dark cap and wide golden yellow forecrown seen on this bird.
