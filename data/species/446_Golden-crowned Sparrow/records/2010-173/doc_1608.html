Habitat

Coyote willow (Salix exigua) thicket mostly, with wild sunflowers, kochia weed, other weedy lower plants, perhaps a golden currant here and there, cottonwoods nearby, shortgrass prairie not far away.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8X42 B&L Elites (close focus was helpful).
Distance:  3 feet to 25 yards
Light:  Sunny day. Bird was usually in the weeds or bushes and not seen super clearly.

Description of the Bird

Large sparrow, long-tailed, similar in size and overall shape to nearby White-crowned Sparrows. Head rather flat-crowned in side profile with a slight "ruffle" at the rear crown (sort of like Steve Lavin, coach of St. John's (see ESPN) on a bad day when his jell is not holding in the back). Side of face rather plain, buffy tan. Complete, not overly conspicuous white eyering. Eye black. Rear of auricular area somewhat darker gray-brown than main part, similar to that of winter Lapland Longspur. Bill thick and bi-colored: upper mandible grayish yellow, lower bright yellow. In front-on view, crown dark brown and gray striped (2 stripes?) on the sides, and yellow and brown striped in the center. Yellow not all that evident except for perfect straight ahead, close views. Bird had a light gray "collared" look from below the auriculars on the side of the lower head around to the throat. Lower throat, breast and belly light gray brown and unstriped. Back (mantle) black and buffy-tan striped. Rump grayish tan. Scapulars not seen all that well. Greater coverts with broad, pale (tan fading to white) margins, entirely black centers. Secondaries and primaries similar to greater coverts, with tips of primaries being mostly black with white margins. Under tail coverts appeared mostly white (and unstriped?). Tail mostly blackish on top, long. (see photos)
Behaviors: Very unwilling to give good views. Stayed in sunflowers and coyote willows, once sat on fence wire briefly, skulky, influenced by hyper White-crown flock which, likewise, seemed paranoid. Rachel Hopper's photos submitted with this report show it atop a fence post, somewhat obscured by willow foliage. This area was north of the campground on the edge of a temporary wetland and the open prairie.
Call: not heard that I know of (bird was mixed in with a large flock of White-crowned Sparrows and I did not note it making vocalizations distinguishable from the others)
Plumage: first-winter

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was obviously an immature Zonotrichia sparrow based on its large size and longish tail. Bill color immediately separated it from Harris's, and the other candidates, really. Plain face without a strong eyeline behind the eye gave it a different look from the nearby immature White-crowns. The crown was more heavily striped on the sides than immature White-crowns and the yellow (when rarely seen) in the center of the crown was richer (more bright yellow than just "creamy" yellow) than that which shows on immature White-crowns. Lack of strong supercillium and conspicuous white throat, bill color, overall lack of rusty coloration separated this bird from all plumages of White-throated Sparrow. Overall pale, unmarked lower parts (no stickpin, for example), lack of rusty crown, eliminated bicolored-billed American Tree Sparrow.
