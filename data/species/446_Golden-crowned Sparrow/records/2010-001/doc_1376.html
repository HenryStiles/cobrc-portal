Description of the Bird

Large, bulky sparrow (seen with Dark-eyed Juncos and a Chipping Sparrow for size comparison.) Tail fairly long, proportions typical of genus Zonotrhichia. Wing projection somewhat short, with tips only reaching rump and thus accentuating long-tailed look. Wings dark with white tippin in covert tracts and lighter brown leading edges on most wing feathers. Body mostly unmarked light grayish brown, lighter towards vent. Upper back boldly striped black on brownish gray. Lower back and rump mostly unmarked brownish gray. Tail darker than body, nearly black-looking. Legs dull pinkish gray. Face quite faintly marked with weak lateral throat stripe and paler throat. Bill yellowish-pink with darker culmen, triangular-shaped. Crown darker due to fine black striping. Yellow wash on crown strongest on forehead, appears more yellow when seen from above and nearly disappears from many side views. Lores darker, appearing nearly black in some views.
Behaviors: Skulking in grape vine and dashing out to feed on seed scattered on the ground.
Call: not heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Unstreaked body rules out many sparrows, as does large size (when directly compared to Dark-eyed Juncos) and long tail. Other immature Zonatrichia similar, but White-throated has bolder head pattern, gray bill, and lack darker culmen. White-crowned have bolder head pattern, brighter bill, and lack yellow forecrown. Harris's Sparrow has bolder throat pattern and some upper breast spotting forming a necklace, and are much brighter underneath.
