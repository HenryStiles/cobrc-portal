Description of the Bird

Chunky sparrow-sized bird darker on back and lighter on front. Chin area lighter than breast. Pointy beak appeared dark. Tail approximately medium length as far as sparrows go. Yellow spot on top front of head alerted observers that is was different species than White-crowned Sparrows it was associating with.
Behaviors: Bird was discovered in a large flock of White-crowned Sparrows (WCSP). Habitat was brushy area (many shrubs in an area without overstory) along road. Bird perched up in Russian olive with other WCSP when flushed by our approaching car.
Call: not heard at all
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Bird was perched with WCSP and juncos. WCSP are most similar to this species, but lack any yellow on top of head.
