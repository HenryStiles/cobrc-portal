Habitat

Dry hillside residential lot in primarily Pinyon/Juniper habitat. In immediate proximity of multiple bird feeders and water sources.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10X binoculars and Variable power telescope.
Distance:  > 20 feet
Light:  Sunny and clear. As indicated, bird was seen in both the morning and afternoon, both in shaded area and in open sunlight.

Description of the Bird

Darker-appearing sparrow with dark bill among White-crowned sparrows foraging/feeding on the ground under hanging feeder. Golden crown and very dark supercilium clearly visible. Hint of mustachial stripe. Unmarked breast. Back shows dark stripes similar to White-crowned sparrows. Size similar to White-crowned sparrows.
Behaviors: Foraging/feeding under feeder.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Not likely to be confused with any other sparrow. Observed with numerous White-crowned sparrows, offering comparison to immature and adult White-crowned sparrows.

Resources Used

Peterson field guide and National Geographic field guide both consulted.

Previous Experience

I have seen and heard Golden-crowned sparrows in Alaska in past years.
