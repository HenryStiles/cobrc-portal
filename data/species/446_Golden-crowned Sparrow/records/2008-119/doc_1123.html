Description of the Bird

A large sparrow of Zonotrichia size. Clear breast, distinct lower wing bar and partial upper wing bar. Overall brownish-red color with heavily streaked upper back/shoulder area.

The most important area is the crown, with a yellow/gold patch at the forecrown, just above the bill and between the eyes, and the streaked rearcrown. These factors, coupled with the lack of streaking on the breast and flanks, suggest that this is a hatch-year bird which has mostly undergone its pre-basic molt.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding on the ground with a variety of white-crowned sparrows. It was the same size as the white-crowns, but lacked the distinctive head pattern of either the juvenile or adult white-crowns, both of which were present.
Call: Silent
Plumage: Hatch year basic maybe

Similar Species Discussion

The only other likely alternative is white-crowned sparrow, and the differences in crown markings are definitive, as previously described.
