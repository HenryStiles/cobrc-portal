Habitat

The feeders behind the Red Rocks Trading Post are in a tiered garden like setting under two large apple trees. There is a platform feeder, but most of the seed is spread along the ground. The feeders are bordered by thick brush with a seep. The GCSP spends most of the time in the thick brush coming out to feed on the ground occasionally and then scurrying back to the thick cover.

Description of the Bird

Fairly large sparrow with lemon yellow forehead bordered by heavy black borders. On top of the head and hind neck the yellow becomes light gray; lores and ear coverts both gray; nape brownish gray. Bill is dark with the upper mandible darker than the lower mandible. The iris is brown. Back and scapulars broadly streaked with brownish-black. Rump is grayish brown with long,brown, slightly notched tail. The wings are brown with chestnut brown on the primaries and two narrow white wingbars. The throat, side of neck and breast are gray with buffy tones on the sides and flanks. The feet are pale pinkish brown.
Behaviors: The Golden-crowned Sparrow came to the feeders behind the Red Rocks Trading Post. The sparrow would come out from the dense brush and undergrowth to feed on the ground in a tiered garden like setting with two large apple trees providing a canopy covering.
Call: did not sing
Plumage: non-breeding plumage

Similar Species Discussion

There is an adult White-crowned Sparrow that also comes into the feeders and on occasion a first year White-crowned shows up. The Golden-crowned Sparrow has a rather dull, unmarked face while the adult White-crowned shows a clean cut black and white pattern on the head. The first year White-crowned Sparrow has brown lateral crown stripes with a buffy median crown stripe. The bill on the White-crowned is pinkish/yellow and not as dark as the Golden-crowned.

I have seen this bird for several winters at the Red Rocks feeders as well as a few other occasions in Colorado and have observed the birds in Sequim, WA, where they are somewhat common.
