Description of the Bird

While scanning and attempting to count the large number of WCSPs, search for HASP and WTSP as well as locate a LISP for photos that I had heard, a WCSP size/shaped bird popped up in a small sapling in front of me.  A quick look told me it was not a WCSP as it was missing the stripes over the top of the head (brown or black with a white median stripe).  Instead I detected a golden hue to the top of the head along with a very indistinct dark border and knew I was looking at a GCSP.

It was clearly a largish sparrow.  Golden crown, bordered with a thin dark stripe.  Lighter gray-brown face getting paler towards the malar area.  The bird had a very pale malar stripe which bordered a throat that was whiter than the breast which had a smudgy gray look.

I never saw much of the back, other than it was brown when the bird dropped down into the nearby thicket.  The bird had two thin white wingbars.

Legs were pinky.

Bill color not noted, so nothing bright and hard to detect in the picture.

No other species were ever entertained as no other expected sparrows sport that golden crown bordered by dark.  WCSP has no golden, WTSP and SAVS's gold would be limited to the lores. Sizewise, the bird was far too large for Ammos and Spizellas.
