Description of the Bird

The bird was an obvious Zonotrichia sparrow, similar in size and shape to White-crowned Sparrow, but with no White-crowns nearby for comparison. When I first saw it, I noticed the plain face, unlike other Zonotrichias. The crown was a mixture of colors and patterns: the forehead was black, which was connected to the beginnings of lateral crown stripes; the center of the forecrown was variably bright yellow to golden; the rear crown was comprised of feathers with buffy edges and wide, dark central shaft streaks. The nape was similar, but the dark bits were browner.

The face was gray with a vague and thin dark eyeline and pale eye arcs, the bottom of which was whiter, longer, and more noticeable than the upper. The eyes were dark and the bill was vaguely bicolored, darker above, lighter below. There were vague blackish lateral throat stripes bordering the whiter chin and throat.

The underparts ranged from gray to brown, with more brown on the sides/flanks. The back was strongly streaked gray and blackish/very dark brown. The folded wings appeared brown, due to strong warm brown fringes to all remiges and greater coverts, but the real basal color of much of the visible folded wing was blackish. The tail was brown and the legs were pinkish.
Behaviors: Foraging on the ground under a stand of Gambel Oak, along with one Song Sparrow and three male Spotted Towhees
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic I

Similar Species Discussion

White-crowned Sparrow is the most similar, but that species does not have distinct black and yellow on crown in Basic I and has a more obvious face pattern.
