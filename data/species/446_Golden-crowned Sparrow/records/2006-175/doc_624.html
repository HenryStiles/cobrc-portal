Description of the Bird

Body & bill morphology with predominantly brown coloring clearly a sparrow. Most obvious feature a dull yellow crown. Crown pure yellow only on most forward part. Moving back on the crown the yellow becomes duller and interspersed with thin dark brown stripes. Bold dark brown supraloral borders the yellow crown. Supercillium has a yellow wash, as does the upper eyelid. Lower eyelid white. These last two features gives the bird a bit of a thin eye-ring, broken at the corners. Very thin, dark upper border of auricular creates a postocular stripe. Rest of face unfeatured brownish-gray. Throat slightly paler gray. Eye dark brown. Bill Yellow overall, with broad brown wash on culmen and upper sides of maxilla.

Body largely same brownish gray as face. Flanks a bit browner. Mantle pale brown with dark brown streaking. Wings darker brown, with pale outer edges of remiges. Fairly short primary projection. Greater coverts tipped with white forming single wing bar. Tail medium-long, brown.

Legs orangish-pink, feet slightly darker brownish-pink.
Behaviors: Bird was foraging on ground under grape vines and shrubs. Surrounding areas snow covered. Associating with Song Sparrows and Spotted Towhees.
Call: Very short, high, thin, slightly upturned, clean call note heard several times.
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

No other sparrow species comes to mind with an extensive yellow crown.
