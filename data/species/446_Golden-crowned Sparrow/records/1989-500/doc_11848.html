Description of the Bird

Sparrow would only feed from the ground feeder where seeds were spread on the grown. Sparrow was the same size as the White-crowned Sparrows it was with. Song Sparrows and Juncos were also present. I first noticed the bill was dark and not yellow. The the face lacked the strong eye-line due to a grayer overall face color then the White-crowns. When the bird faced me I could clearly see a wide space between the brown head stripes and a gold/yellow patch at the leading edge. The rest of the field marks pretty much matched the first winter White-crowned Sparrows. The Golden-crowned was always easy to pick out among the ground feeders even though its differences were not bold. We never hear the bird call.

Similar Species Discussion

Sub-adult White-crowned Sparrow were present so we had a direct comparisom which made it fairly routine. White-throated Sparrows have a very white throat and narrow head stripes.

Resources Used

Field guides and many birding friends who came to see the sparrow.

Previous Experience

Brenda and I have seen 1/2 of the world species and have been birding for 35 years. A good deal of our time has been spent birding locally. We have birded California several times and have seen Golden-crowned Sparrows from there to Alaska. We know the two local subspecies of White-crowned Sparrows very well; our winter birds from the north and our breeding summer birds.
