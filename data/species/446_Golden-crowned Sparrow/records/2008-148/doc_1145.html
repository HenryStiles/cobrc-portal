Description of the Bird

The sparrow was associated with White-crowned Sparrows, which allowed for easy comparison. The bird displayed classic Zonotrichia traits such as longish, squared tail, long tertials, lack of ventral streaking, and diagnostic bold head striping. The top of the head had a broad, median stripe of yellow that seemed to end dorsally at the top of the crown that was bordered on each side of the head laterally by a thick, black stripe. The bill was mostly a dark gray, unlike the pinkish/yellow bill of a White-crowned. The back and scapulars were an olive brown, broadly streaked with blackish brown. The rump was a plain olive brown. The wings were brown and contained two white wing bars and the tail was brown. The sides of the head and neck, the throat, and breast were a grayish brown. The flanks were brown and the belly was whitish.
Behaviors: The bird foraged along the ground and perched in some small shrubs. Also observed it in flight.

Similar Species Discussion

White-crowned Sparrows differ by having multiple lateral head stripes, lack bright gold forehead and crown, and have pinkish-yellow bills. White-throated Sparrows have bright white throats, yellow lores, and have multiple lateral head stripes.
