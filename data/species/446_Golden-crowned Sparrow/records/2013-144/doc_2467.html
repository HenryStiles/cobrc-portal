Description of the Bird

While birding at the Durango Hatchery Pond, I found several White-crowned Sparrows and a sparrow I did not recognize. The bird was similar to the White-crowned Sparrows in size and seemed comfortable in their group. I noticed the head was gray and very plain. The breast was also a plain gray and the wings were brown with a noticeable white wing bar. I chased the bird around until I finally got a head-on view of it which revealed a gold/yellow patch on the front of the crown. At that point I realized it must be a Golden-crowned Sparrow.
Behavior: The bird was feeding in rabbit brush along the edge of the pond and usually stayed in a flock with several White-crowned Sparrows. Sometimes it was on the ground and other times in the brush a few feet off the ground.
Call: No vocalization
Plumage: 1st Winter

Similar Species Discussion

The bird had no eye stripe which eliminated White-crowned or White-throated Sparrow. The gold spot on the forehead also helped eliminate these two species and other sparrows.
