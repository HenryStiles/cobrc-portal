Description of the Bird

This was a large and chunky Zonotrichia sparrow with a longish tail, partial golend crown (without solid dark on crown, rusty edges to tertials and greater coverts, bi-colored bill with upper "mandible" appearing grayer than "lower mandible" which was lighter gray with pinkish tones. Legs appeared dull pink.
Behaviors: Mostly a skulker, coming on to the edge of an open deck for food, the retreating quickly into the dense vegetation adjacent to the feeder. Bird always walked to food and back into cover.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Probably 1st winter, unsure after Pyle

Similar Species Discussion

There really isn't any other sparrows with gold crowns, except the juvenile White-crowns which were also present, but have brownish, not blackish crown stripes, and tan-colored, not gold-colored median crown stripe. Plus, check out the photo.
