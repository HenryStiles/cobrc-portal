Documentation of Golden-crowned Sparrow
2008-018

Observer Information

Reporter:  Marty Wolf   
E-mail: 
Other Observers:  After posting my sighting of 2/23 on COBirds, four others came by and successfully viewed it: Bill Maynard, Mark Peterson, Brad Steger, and Saraiya Ruano.; I am the original finder and identifier.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Golden-crowned Sparrow
First Date/Time:  2/23/2008
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  5-10 seconds per appearance
County:  El Paso
Specific Location:  Backyard deck of my house, 2220 Capra Way, 80919, in NW CO Spgs.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Mixed scrub. Backyard is natural/native vegetation, with sprawling, thick juniper and lilac plantings along back of deck where bird is sheltered between forays onto edge of deck.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Audubon Equinox 10 X 42 binoculars (me). Not sure what others had.
Distance:  18-30 feet, depending on where on deck it was. Measured with pace later.
Light:  It has been typically cloudy to mostly cloudy when the bird shows. Sun sometimes is shining, but not on the bird, which is in shadow of house. Sun is behind/south of the observers, while the bird is north of us. Through binoculars (10X) the quality of observation is excellent.

Description of the Bird

Same size as white-crowned sparrows & juncos feeding with it. Crown distinctly yellow, with two-toned bill--clearly different from the immature white-crowns present with it.
Behaviors: Feeding on seed spilled from elevated platform feeder on the edge of my deck. Much more furtive & skittish than the white-crowns; makes only 5-second appearances typically, max of 10 seconds. Skitters off deck into shrubs immediately at any movement inside house (as I took pictures).
Only appears on days with light snow, or mornings after nights with light snow. Does not show on warm/sunny days.
Call: N.A.
Plumage: 1st-winter

Similar Species Discussion

See above under description. Yellow crown and two-tone bill distinguish it from immature white-crowned sparrows, which it otherwise resembles.

Resources Used

Sibley's Field Guide to Birds of Western North America; Natl. Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America (3rd Edition); Golden Guide to Birds of North America (1966 Edition). Guides were consulted immediately after my first sighting of the bird, when I knew it was not a white-crowned sparrow.

Previous Experience

This is my first sighting of a golden-crowned sparrow. Am very familiar with white-crowns and white-throated sparrows.
