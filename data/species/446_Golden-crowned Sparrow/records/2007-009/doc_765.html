Description of the Bird

A large sparrow similar in size and proportions to nearby White-crowned Sparrows. The bird was present at the site all winter and I finally got around to seeing it in March.

The bird had a mix of yellow and dark on the crown (as it was molting head feathers during its first pre-alternate molt). Its face was fairly plain, with very thin white eye arcs and an indistinct dark brown eye line. The back was medium brown with darker brown streaks all with very thin white highlights. The tail was long, rounded, and dark brown. The underparts were pale, grayish on the chest and a vague buffy-brown on the flanks. The brown wings were marked with thin, but distinct, white wing bars. The bill was bicolored, with a mostly dark maxilla and a pinkish-yellow mandible. Finally, the bird sported faint dark lateral throat stripes.
Behaviors: Foraging on ground with large flock of White-crowned Sparrows.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic I molting to Alternate I

Similar Species Discussion

Other Zonotrichia sparrows can be somewhat similar, but the bird's incoming yellow crown and its very plain face rule out all other contenders.
