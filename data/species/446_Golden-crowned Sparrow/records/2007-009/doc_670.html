Description of the Bird

This was a large plump brownish sparrow with pinkish legs, dull gray breast, GRAYISH BILL, dark eye, and a GOLDEN color on the forecrown only. The remainder of the crown color was a rusty brown in color. The wings were warm brown with two rows of whitish wingbars, the upper wingbar being faint. The tail was gray brown and long. The flanks showed a bit of gray brown as was the lower back and rump. The back was dull brown with darker brown vertical streaking.
Behaviors: This bird moved with a flock of about15-20 White-crowned Sparrows, 1st winter birds and adult gambelii. It stayed mostly hidden in skunkbush (Rhus trilobata) as did the majority of the White-crowns. It is possible that it was foraging on the ground under the bushes. I did see it for about 8 seconds teed up on a skunkbush before it moved just into the bush, partly obscured from view.
Call: none detected
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Most like immature White-crowned Sparrow but that species, WCSP, has a golden crown with rusty lateral stripes, and an orange-pink bill, not an all- grayish bill of the GCSP. The crown of the two species are quite different with GCSP having the proximal quarter of its crown golden, and the distal three quarters rusty brown. GCSP is a slightly larger bird and the observation of the comparisons between the two species was possible because of the proximity of the two, i.e. side by side. 1st year White-throated Sparrow has a noticeable white thoruat and a whitish or tan supercillium unlike this bird.
