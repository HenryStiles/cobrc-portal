Description of the Bird

Similarly sized and shaped to the White-crowned Sparrows around it. Noted as different (even without seeing the head pattern) due to its grayer overall plumage coloration. Showed darker gray on the nape and auriculars. Head pattern was distinctive with a yellow forecrown and black lores, eyestripe and supraloral feathers. Noted no white in the crown pattern even though this is noted in field guides. Face of this bird seemed dirtier than photos I have seen online almost like the bird was significantly worn. Yellow on the forecrown was not as bright as in field guides almost showing more of a dull dandelion or goldenrod color instead of bright like a buttercup (I guess I have flowers on the mind). Rest of body looked like a White-crowned Sparrow aside from the darker overall cast. Beak was dark gray to black depending on lighting, but didn't show the bi-colored expected in field guides. At no point did the beak look yellow, pink, or orange like a White-crowned Sparrow.
Behaviors: Worked the roadside and weedy patches around the trail by hopping along with White-crowned Sparrows. Occasionally would post up after being flushed, but wouldn't remain up for very long.
Call: None noted
Plumage: worn adult

Similar Species Discussion

White-crowned sparrow never shows a golden crown like this bird did and the black above the eye and toward the crown is never this thick in White-crowneds. Beak was the wrong color as well being all black or dark gray. Overall grayer tones to the body and head made this bird stick out like a sore thumb.

It is hard to say this bird isn't a White-crowned x Golden-crowned Sparrow. No easy way to tell, but saw no signs that would indicate this has White-crowned Sparrow genes in it. No extra white in or around the head and eyes, no hint of extra coloration in the beak.
