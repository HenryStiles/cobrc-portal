Description of the Bird

Among the birds in close proximity, the Golden-Crowned Sparrow was smaller than an American Robin and of comparable size to a Harris's Sparrow and several White-Crowned Sparrows. The Golden-Crowned Sparrow was more plump than the other zonotrichia. Most notably, it had a yellow forecrown outlined in black. The thick black band behind the eye was nearly complete, and thus the bird was nearly in breeding plumage. Otherwise, it had dull gray undersides, tan flanks, a checkered mantle, and multi-colored wings with white-tipped coverts.
Behavior: The Golden-Crowned Sparrow was foraging in weeds together with other zonotrichia: one Harris's Sparrow and several White-Crowned Sparrows.
Call: none heard
Plumage: nearly in breeding plumage

Similar Species Discussion

The distinctive yellow forecrown eliminates other sparrows.

Previous Experience

I have observed Golden-Crowned Sparrows at two locations: this one (approximately a dozen times) and at Red Rocks Park near Morrison, Colorado (once). Otherwise, I have observed zonotrichia (white-throated, white-crowned, and Harris's sparrows) numerous times at many locations during the past two years.
