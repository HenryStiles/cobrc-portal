Description of the Bird

This small-to medium sized bird - male - had bright blue upperparts from the head to the tail including all of the back. Throat and upper chest were orange and the orange throat wrapped around to the sides of the neck. The belly and undertail coverts were white. Bill and legs were black, although base of bill looked lighter. Its mate was similar, but much more subdued in overall coloring - female.
Behaviors: Male was seen peerched on a fence line and post and surrounding shrubs. Male seemed to be bringing food to female or possibly young. Female emerged from nest box briefly.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: adult male and female

Similar Species Discussion

This bird's orange throat and sides of neck distinguishes it from Western. Also, no orangish-red on scapulars. Blue was not as electric as Western.
