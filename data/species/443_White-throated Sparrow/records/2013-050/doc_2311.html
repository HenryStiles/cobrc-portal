Description of the Bird

A large sparrow, with dark brown lateral crown stripes and eyeline; buffy-tan central crown stripe and superciliary; dull yellow patch in supraloral area; brownish-gray auriculars; white chin; thin, black lateral throat stripes; white malar stripes; back streaked with dark brown and buffy-tan; chest grayish with vague darker streaking; sides/flanks with medium brown streaks; wings rufous-brown with white tips to median and greater coverts forming wing bars; tail long and rounded
Behaviors: foraging under willows and then under feeders
Call: none heard
Plumage: Formative

Similar Species Discussion

Song and White-crowned sparrows lack yellow suparloral and distinctly white throat contrasting to slightly-streaked chest
