Description of the Bird

The initial bird was discovered on May 16th by Chris Nunes while doing survey work on the soon-to-be Fishers Peak State Park, he was camped near Pearl Spring up the McBride Creek drainage at the south end of the the property and heard a whip-poor-will singing that he immediately recognized as a MWPW (based partially on hearing the Oak Creek Grade bird the prior night).

A single bird was reported by Jason Beason and Greg Levandoski while doing nocturnal survey work as contractors for CP&W on May 21.

I arrived at this location to do volunteer survey work for CP&W on May 31.  Shortly after sunset, I heard one singing a bit upstream of Pearl Spring, then bird then worked its way downstream to join another singing bird.

This behavior was repeated on June 2nd.

On June 5th, with Tony Leukering and Chris Nunes, we heard the birds begin singing quite close to each other a bit downstream of Pearl Spring.

These birds were identified only by voice - a "whip poor will" like song, but too rough and trilled sounding for EWPW.
