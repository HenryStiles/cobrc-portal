Description of the Bird

eBird checklist with recordings here:

https://ebird.org/checklist/S112979497#

 

The bird was found a few days prior by Rebecca Laroche, who alerted me to its presence. I aranged to have my DFO field trip try for it before sunrise on 6/13/22. It washeard that morning by Rebecca, but my field trip group ended up too far away to hear it calling then. We tried again after sunset on 9/14. 

 the bird began calling at 8:51 with 'whip' notes only for several times and a few minutes, then it began the full 3-part call about 8:55. We played a recording at intervals and the bird continued to call until 9:12. It called from positions for a minute or two and then moved to another nearby spot and resumed calling. We saw it fly over against the night sky once.

Similar Species Discussion
