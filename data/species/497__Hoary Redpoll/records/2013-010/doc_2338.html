Description of the Bird

12/23/12 This redpoll had a very white breast and was obvious to spot wiithout binocs when came in. Undertail coverts white with no streaking (two other birders confirmed). Flanks, belly and breast were all white (may have had some pinkish on breast, but I could not discern any (hard color for me to see). Its side streaks were barely visible, but had some (hazy looking). Black bib was surrounded by mostly white with little streaking or color on the ear patch or chin - almost all white. When it flew, I finally could see its rump patch, but just for a flash. It was all white as best as I could tell, but I was not convinced since it was just a flash.

12/27/12 Four days later saw same redpoll again. Easily found because all white. This time I was able to see all white undertail coverts again, but got great views of rump patch - all white with no streaking, and a gray back with black streaking that was frosted. The edges of the back on the sides had some brownish flecking. For some reason I did not pay that much attention to beak, but as I recall was intermediate (nothing special - not short and stubby, but not as long as most of the commons - my big oops). However, this bird was so bright white, you could not miss it. I have not seen a picture of it yet, but it looks like the one on American Birds except the face did not appear as steep and beak did not have quite that stubby appearence.
Behaviors: 12/23/12 The bird came in by itself, but about 50 redpolls were aaround. It landed on the back block feeder and was feeding on it as were a couple of Commons. It stayed there until something spooked the entire flock. It flew onto a "chain" patio barrier about 25 feet away by itself. It flew towards the other flock as they all left the area. (time 10:50)

12/27/12 Came into a feeder where at least up to five other Common Redpolls were feeding (thistle sock). A kestrel came flying over and all the birds, about 50-70 Common Redpolls and an assortment of other birds) took off. It flew by itself to a ornamental fir to the north while all the other birds flew west. Went to get a pic in the tree, but it and two other redpolls flew off west.
Call: None heard among the throng of Common Redpolls
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Common Redpoll - There were several pale Common Redpolls at feeders for comparison. Undertail coverts of these all had streaking - at least 4 streaks (this one had none). Rumps had streaks on white patch (this one had none). Coloration of these more brownish - especially on back whereas this one was gray. Streaking on flanks not as crisp - little streaking. Bibs on oa lot these had some streaking - not as white chested. I looked at beaks of these and they were variable and it was at least in the mid range if not smaller than the Commons.
