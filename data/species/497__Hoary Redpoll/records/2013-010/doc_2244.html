Description of the Bird

Small pale finch associating with Common Redpolls.

Pale head and sides of neck. Overall much paler than Common Redpolls with the bird. Smallish yellow bill. Limited black mask, limited red "poll". Mostly white underparts with pale reddish pink on upper breast and upper flanks. Paler upper and underparts than Common Redpolls bird was associating with. Flanks with slight streaking, sparse and narrow. Streaking was more gray in color and noticeably lighter than the blackish streaking on the Common Redpolls associating with the bird. Undertail coverts clean white except for one dark streak on longest undertail covert. Rump appeared clean and white through binoculars and photos suggest it was white without streaking or spots. Frosty white edges to scapulars.
Behaviors: Feeding at nyjer feeders and tray feeder.
Call: Cannot be sure I heard this specific bird. Many redpolls in the area
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Redpoll is only similar species. See description above and photos. Common Redpoll eliminated by this bird's overall pale appearance, limited, light and sparse side streaking on flanks which is good for HORE, only 1 undertail streak (good for HORE), clean rump (good for HORE). Limited black mask, small red "poll."
