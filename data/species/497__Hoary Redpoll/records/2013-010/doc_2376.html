Description of the Bird

A mostly gray and white bird with flecks of brown on sides of back and black streaks. Completely white undertail coverts and rump with no streaks. Flanks had heavier, more noticeable streaks of black than males that went up to chest. Ear patchs were colored kind of a brown with the black streaks from flanks ending just below - the cheek patches seemed to have a mix of light brown and dark brown/black, but appeared more buffy. The middle breast and underbelly were white.
Behaviors: This bird came in by itself to a feeder about 35 feet from me. Several feeders had about 50 Common Redpolls at them already. The flock was skittish and after 5 minutes, it flew with the Common Redpolls all together to trees across the bike path west of the Science Center's feeders.
Call: not heard
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Common Redpolls - This one looked grayer overall, had no streaking on undertail coverts and rump. This bird hjad a shorter beak and more slo-ed forehead, but was not as exaggerated as seen in some photos. Beak length seemed to be shorter than most Common Redpolls I have seen.
