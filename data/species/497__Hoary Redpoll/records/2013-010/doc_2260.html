Light:  Excellent winter morning light. The sun was behind me, thus putting the bird in very nice light.

Description of the Bird

Views through a high-powered, high-quality scope of bird in good light in a tree to the west of the Discovery Museum feeders.

A colder looking, more black/white/gray redpoll lacking the warm tones of the nearby COREs.

Snowy-white undertail coverts lacking any streaks.

Flanks had faint/pale gray streaks.

Head: red cap was small surrounded by frosty gray with very little black on the face (no goatee).

Breast had a pale pink wash which was only on the upper breast and didn't extend onto the throat or the belly.

Unfortunately the bird failed too cooperate and provide views of its rump
Behaviors: Nothing notable. Was sitting on a branch catching some sun.
Call: None detected
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Only similar species with the little red beanie is a CORE.

The COREs generally had a warmer/browner tone than this bird.

The COREs flank streaking appeared more like a rivulet and was generally pronounced. This bird had very pale, fine grayish streaking. The COREs streaking was much darker.

The red cap was smaller and surrounded by gray when compared with the COREs whose caps were surrounded by more of a brownish tone.

The pinkness on the chest was very pale and soft looking whereas the pink on the breast of the COREs was more extensive and much more red.

I could generally see anywhere from several undertail covert streaks to a whole bunch on the COREs, but couldn't see any undertail covert streak on this bird, even though I had outstanding views of this particular part of the birds anatomy.
