Description of the Bird

Pale head and sides of neck. Overall much paler than Common Redpolls with the bird. Smallish yellow bill. Limited black mask, limited red "poll". Nasal bristles extending down onto bill. White underparts. Paler upper and underparts than Common Redpolls at this location. Flanks with sparse and narrow streaking. Streaking was more gray in color and lighter than the blackish streaking on the Common Redpolls at this location. Undertail coverts clean white except for one dark streak on longest undertail covert. Rump was clean and white through binoculars and photos show it white without streaking or spots. Frosty white edges to scapulars.
Behaviors: Feeding at nyjer feeders and tray feeder and flying to nearby Cottonwood trees.
Call: Birds were calling. Cannot be sure if I heard this particular bird or not
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Redpoll is only similar species. See description above and photos. Common Redpoll eliminated by this bird's overall pale appearance, limited, light and sparse side streaking on flanks which is good for HORE, only 1 undertail streak (good for HORE), clean rump (good for HORE). Limited black mask, small red "poll."

Resources Used
