Description of the Bird

At Ft Collins Museum of Discovery on 6 January 2013. The undertail covert was limited to one short streak, the rump unstreaked. The side streaking was paler than that of most (all?) male CORE present, but the extent of streaking was only modestly limited in c/w CORE males. The red on crown was more limited in extent than that of male CORE and the black on face more restricted as well, and not as black. The bill was smaller in all dimensions than that of most CORE, and thus not proportionately shorter, so that the "pushed-in" look is not apparent. This bird scored 15.5 on Sibley's scale, putting it well into the Hoary range.
Behaviors: At feeders with other redpolls

FROM THEDE TOBISH
Hey Steve, thanks for sending your redpoll photos my way. Sounds like quite the excitement, especially for January. Per your bird in question, it was good to see it in context with Schmoker’s earlier individual. Both birds are reasonably similar, although his photos show more suffusion of pink tones on the rump and upper end of the underparts. I’d have to agree with you that the photos of your bird show a Hoary type, differentiated by the bright white and basically completely unstreaked rump, the single thin dark undertail streak, the very pale fringes and overall whitish tone to the light shades on the dorsum. Additional marks that point to Hoary include the pale areas on the sides of the neck and auricular, the smaller area of red on the “poll”, and a “smallish” bill. Your bird’s bill is somewhat larger overall than most typical Hoarys, but of course bill shape and size is variable and alone is not the most reliable. The flank streaking is hard to see for sure, but it looks restricted to a couple short rows, fairly thin, and towards the vent only. Your bird’s white fringes on the tertials and greater coverts are as extensive or even more so than on Bill’s individual and more Hoary-like. So, in all, except for the bill, your bird displays basic features of a Hoary. I think I see a hint of pink on the rump but the photos do not allow me to see pink shades on the underparts otherwise. I sent your shots to Dan Gibson and we talked about the photos. He agreed it was likely a Hoary but he agreed the bill was less than ideal for what we would consider a classic Hoary. Bill size and shape is variable and may not prove as reliable or definitive as some might suggest. Your bird certainly seems good for Hoary.
