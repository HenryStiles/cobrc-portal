Description of the Bird

Adult maie. Scored 15 on Sibley's scoring scale using rump, undertail coverts, and side streaking. Rump unmarked, entirely or very nearly so. Side streaking avg for Hoary. Longest undertail covert with streak, otherwise no undertail covert streaking. Smallest billed bird present, though not as small billed as some Hoaries, distinctly within range. Upperparts base color grayish, not brown, with whiter lower scaps in photo. Black on face not as dark as male Commons present. Red not as dark/extensive below as 3 male CORE present. Call different from CORE that I've heard. Sounded similar to the call on 105853 in Macaulay Library, recorded in Alaska during June 1998. Specifically I refer to the chew chew chew call, different from the dji-dji-dji harsher/rougher sounding call of the CORE. Not sure if call truly of significance, but ...
Behaviors: Sitting up in tree, awaiting opportunity to go to feeder
Call: see below
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Redpoll should have streaked rump, typically more streaking on undertail coverts, larger red "poll" and darker overall plumage. Also, this bird did appear small billed, though probably not outside range of smallest billed Commons
