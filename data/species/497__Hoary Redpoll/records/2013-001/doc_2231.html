Description of the Bird

This was a male Redpoll: See the attached photos. It had a small bill and a weak black facial mask. The undertail coverts had one medium length dark streak. The rump had no streaks that I saw through binocs, but it was somewhat pink and not pure white. The rump pink was not as bright as the pink on the sides of the breast. The back was gray and white, and not brown and white, and it did not show any white lines down the upper back, as seen on many Commons. Other features of the bird were like Common Redpoll.
Behaviors: Eating thistle seed from a feeder and perching nearby.
Call: none heard
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Common Redpoll. The undertail coverts pattern, the unstreaked rump and small bill all suggest Hoary.
