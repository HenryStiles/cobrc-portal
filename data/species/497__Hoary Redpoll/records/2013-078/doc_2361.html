Description of the Bird

By this date, most Common Redpolls had gotten rather darker than they were in January. This bird stood out as almost ghostly in comparison. The background color to the upperparts was a rather pale buff. The auriculars looked white in field, perhaps with faint buff wash in photos. The underparts were white, with streaking lighter than the male COREs. The bill was short and stubby, the head big and "buffled" accentuating the smallness of the bill. The black chin was limited and a bit pale. The red poll quite small. The undertail coverts bore a single long thin streak. The rump had an unstreaked patch of moderate size and the bird scored 12 by Sibley's scale per my reckoning.
Behaviors: Feeding on ground with other redpolls. Perching up in low vegetation
Call: not heard, specifically, but calls like those uttered by Willie's bird were heard from this flock.
Plumage: undetermined

Similar Species Discussion

Sibley score well within range for female Hoary. Common Redpoll should have more streaking on rump/undertail coverts, typically larger billed, darker overall, more side streaking, etc..
