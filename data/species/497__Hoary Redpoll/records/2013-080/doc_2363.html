Description of the Bird

Saw redpoll with glaring pale pink rump sitting on log, rump without markings. Pulled out camera and ripped off series of photos, then watched bird sitting in low trees (above eye level), before it flew off. Seen again, but not photographed again. Birds upper parts were paler than those of COREs about, though not as dramatically so as other male and female reported from that day. Moderate to large unstreaked pale pink rump patch. One moderate length very thin undertail streak. Side streaking diffuse and thin. Bill size within range of both species. Large white greater wingcovert bars and moderately large white tertial edges. Pink beneath on chest and upper to mid sides, far paler than that of CORE present.
Behaviors: Feeding on ground, low weeds. Perching up in low trees.
Call: Hoary type (like that from Scmoker's bird) heard from flock... which individual uttering call..?
Plumage: not determined; 1st year male?

Similar Species Discussion

Scored 15-16 on Sibley's scale, though notably, looked not as obviously like a HORE as other male present that scored lower. Rump and undertail covert pattern main marks. Paleness of pink, light side markings, broad white wing edges contribute to ID from CORE
