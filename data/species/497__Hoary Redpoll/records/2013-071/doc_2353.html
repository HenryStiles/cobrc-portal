Description of the Bird

This bird showed up at my feeders on Jan 21 amidst lots of siskins and goldfinch. It has a frosty white appearance compared to the yellow/brown hues of siskins. Its body is more round and stocky compared to slender siskins, and thick neck. It has a light pink wash to its chest, and white belly. The undertail coverts are white with a hint of one, possibly two tiny streaks. Flanks have some brown streaks, faint to average. The back is brown/white streaked. The rump has an obvious white patch. It has a large white edge to secondaries. The forehead has a red patch edged above bill and eye with white. There is a very tiny pink tinge to the malar adjacent to the bill and on the lower rump - these are only seen in close photos, not with the naked eye. Black encircles the bill and extends to the eye. The black below the bill extends down the same length as the vertical depth of the lower mandible. The tiny conical bill is mostly yellow, dark only on the dorsal surface. The primaries and tail feathers at brown with fine edging of white. The folded tail is slightly notched, the primaries extend a bit farther down the tail than the undertail coverts.
Behaviors: This bird feeds primarily at a thistle/
sunflower chip feeder and rarely at whole sunflower feeder. It arrives early morning and feeds fairly regularily throughout the day. It may disappear for an hour or afternoon then return to feed again. It has been seen almost daily. It behaves similar to the siskins and finches at the feeder except a bit less skittish. He often does not immediately fly away when startled as the others do, instead sits and looks around before flying away. Which makes it seem a bit more independent than a follower in the flock. He defends his perch at a feeder a little stronger than the others do, often not being chased away but holding position or taking theirs.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The bird is white and brown, not a warm brown. It's chest is a light pink wash. The two most defining characteristics are the undertail coverts are white with only one, maybe two tiny streaks, and there is a definite white patch on the rump that appears unstreaked.
