Description of the Bird

This bird was about the size of a Yellow-rumped Warbler . It was gray above and white below with bright yellow throat sharply contrasting with white chest. It had a solid white supercilium suggesting albilora race. A bold black triangle marked auriculars with elongated bottom angle which melded into black spotted flanks. White crescent below eye contrasted with black auricular triangle. Two bold white wing bars contrasted with slaty gray wings. Legs were dark as was bill which was slim but long for a wood warbler.
Behaviors: The bird sang in tree tops; fortunately some trees were not tall. I saw it do little but sing occasionally flying from tree to tree.
Call: Song was similar to Yellow-rumped Warbler but louder and sweeter: "deedle, deedle, deedle, deedle, sweet, sweet" with last 2 notes slightly higher than rest of song.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Grace's Warbler has almost solid yellow supercilium, shorter bill, and lacks bold black auricular triangle. Grace's song is softer with a much more rapid trill.
