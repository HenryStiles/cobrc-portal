Description of the Bird

A small bird that spent time gleaning insects in trees that were just leafing out. The bird had a bright yellow throat and chest with a black border on either side of the bib that turned into dustings broad black stripes and dashes against stark white flanks. The belly and vents were white and the back was “dendroica” blue-gray with white wingbars. The tail seemed fairly short to me.

The face had a black pattern offset by a white eye line, ear patch and half eye-ring. I believe that the yellow bib extended up to the bill, but do not recall yellow lores.
Behaviors: The bird gleaned insects in cottonwoods along and nearby a creek. It did not seem as active as near-by Yellow-rumped warblers and it did not fly as far in feeding forays.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Not as much yellow nor was the yellow as diffused as the Yellow-throated Vireo down the stream. Black face pattern separated it from Grace’s Warbler.
