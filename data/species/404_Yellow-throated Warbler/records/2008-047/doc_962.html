Description of the Bird

The bird was a medium sized warbler, slightly smaller than nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers. The bill was long, thin, and black, relatively long for a warbler, and longer than that on Yellow-rumped Warbler. The legs were black.

The crown was dark gray/black. The bird had a white supercilium, contrasting boldly with the black auriculars. There was a thin white crescent on the underside of the eye, and a white patch behind the auriculars. The black auriculars merged at their lower end into bold black streaks down the flanks. The throat and uppermost chest was bright yellow. The lower chest and belly were white. The nape and back were medium gray; the wings were the same gray, with blacker highlights in the coverts and flight feathers, and two bold white wingbars. The upper tail was dark gray, the undertail was mostly white, with small dark gray tail corners.
Behaviors: the bird was first found low down on an isolated snag, and flew down to the ground, and then high up into a tree. It flitted around the tree for a little while, and then disappeared.
Call: The bird was heard to sing a couple of times, a song made up of rich, loud, sliding notes that descended slightly in scale, before ending in two more emphatic, lower notes.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of warbler were ruled out by a combination of the bold black and white face pattern, yellow throat, white underparts with bold black streaks down the flanks, and gray upperparts. Grace's Warbler was ruled out by the thinner and longer white supercilium, the white patch behind the auriculars, the blacker face, and the very different song.
