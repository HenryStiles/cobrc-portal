Description of the Bird

A warbler by bill shape and bright coloration. Bill longish. Chin and throat bright yellow. Black surrounding eye except white eye arcs, extending down to form a triangle of black on anterior part of auricular. White behind the black of face. White supercilium. Crown and back medium gray with two whitish wingbars. Belly white, and so are flanks but with broad dark verticle streaking.
Behaviors: Slow foraging bird that explored limbs and trunk for insects. When it flew it moved only about 12 feet or less except when flock moved to another tree. Extracted unknown insect or mold from tree bark crack.
Call: none
Plumage: 1st year

Similar Species Discussion

Grace's Warbler comes closest. GRWA has yellow supercillium and tiny black triangular auricular patch and is lacking the white area behind the dark auricular patch. Yellow on throat is darker and does not extend down towards breast as far as it does on YTWA.
