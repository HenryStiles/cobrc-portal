Description of the Bird

A small Dendroica warbler. Gray backed with a blackish tail and 2 white wingbars. White supercilium, black face with a white ear patch. Striking yellow throat. White underparts with blackish streaking on the side.
Behaviors: This bird was observed foraging on the ground, on tree trunks and large branches and in the smaller canopy branches of elms, cottonwoods, willows and Russian olives.
Call: call a sharp chip
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Grace's Warbler is the only similar species and they never show any black on the face and they don't have a white supercilium
