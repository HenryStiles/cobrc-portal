Description of the Bird

This is a long-billed warbler with yellow throat, black auricular, pointing down to distal sides of yellow throat, white supercillium, white eye arc below the eye, white breast with dark broad vertical breast streaks. Bill dark as were feet. Back gray with two broad white wingbars. White neck spot behind black auricular patch.
Behaviors: Very sluggish, creeping about on large branch and trunk in nuthatch-like fashion, gleaning insects.
Call: None heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Grace's Warbler comes closest, but yellow breast extends up to form a yellow supercillium and a yellow lower eye arc on that species. Grace's does not have the white neck spot of YTWA.
