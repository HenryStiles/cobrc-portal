Description of the Bird

This was a slow moving warbler that had a very long dark bill. The crown back and wings with blue gray and two prominent white wingbars were visible. The face pattern was distinctive with a wedge of black tapering towards the eye withthe pont of black at the base of the bill. Inside the black wedge was a white arc below the dark eye. The was a white supercilium that extended posterior to the eye. The chin throat and upper breast was yellow. The belly and undertail coverts were gleaming white with two rows of dark flank streaks. There was a distinct patch of white just behind wedge of black on the face about where the auriculars should be located.
Behaviors: Foraged while calling in a few narrowleaf cottonwoods, always with about 3 Yellow-rumped Warblers. Moved fairly slowly perching for brief periods before moving on. Mostly in mid-storey but occassionally higher.
Call: chips heard that was soft and descending in quality
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Grace's Warbler is the only species with which YTWA should be confused. GRWA lacks the white auricular and has a broad yellow supraloral stripe on its face. It also lacks the black wedge of black descrbed above for YTWA.
