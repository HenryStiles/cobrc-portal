Description of the Bird

Gavin Beiber and I were leading a WINGS trip on our final day scanning for waterfowl at Bonny Reservoir. A woman stopped by to say that she has seen a Yellow-throated Warbler at the end of the road. Unfortunately, she left before I was able to get her name. We went to that area, pished and in flew a Yellow-throated Warbler callling. Slightly smaller (less bulky) than Yellow-rumped Warbler with noticeably longer bill. Dark gray above. Darker wings with bold wing bars formed by white tips to greater and median coverts. Black auriculars bordered in rear by large white patch. White supercilium (including above black lores). White arc below eye. Bright yellow throat bordered on sides with black. Underparts otherwise white. Bold black streaking on sides of breast and flanks. Legs, bill and eye dark.
Behaviors: n/a
Call: I hear the bird give a couple call notes. Difficult to describe but sharper than Yellow Warbler with a bit of a Swamp Sparrow quality.
Plumage: albilora

Similar Species Discussion

Face pattern unmistakable. Grace's Warbler and Audubon's Warbler have dark cheeks.

Previous Experience

I have been an extremely active birder since 1988 and have visited most of North America and several other continents to study birds. In my home state of Colorado I have been actively involved with birding and the Colorado Bird Records Committee, serving two terms while in college and graduate school. I served as regional editor for the Colorado / Wyoming region of North American birds. I have authored a variety of materials on bird identification including 70 accounts for the National Geographic Complete Birds of North America. Since 2000, I have served as a senior leader for the birdwatching tour company WINGS, where I have led tours throughout much of the Lower 48, Alaska, Mexico and the Caribbean. Since 2005, my primary work has been as Project Leader for eBird and the Avian Knowledge Network at the Cornell Lab of Ornithology.

I have seen and studied thousands of Yellow-throated Warblers in much of the US and Mexico. I have only seen two other Yellow-throated Warblers in Colorado.
