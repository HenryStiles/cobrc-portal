Description of the Bird

the bird was Yellow Warbler sized and about the same shape, though with a longer bill. The bill was thin and pointed, and along with the legs black in color.

The bird's crown was black, contrasting with the strong white superciliary, which in turn contrasted with the bold black triangular auricular patch. The lower corner of this patch merged into bold black streaks down the sides of the breast. There was a fairly large white patch behind the auricular patch. The nape and back of the bird were clean slate blue-gray. The wings were also gray, with two bold white wingbars. The bird's throat was bright yellow, and the remainder of the underparts were clean white. The uppertail was gray, the undertail mostly white with a small gray area on the base and in the corners.
Behaviors: The bird spent all of it's time high in the pine trees, singing about half the time and just foraging the other half. I watched one extended bout of preening, at which time I took the attached pictures.
Call: The song was about two seconds long, accelerating and increasing in volume. It was made up of about eight slightly slurred notes that descended slightly in pitched, and ended in an emphatic note.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of warbler other than Grace's were ruled out by the combination of face pattern and bright yellow throat. Grace's Warbler was ruled out by the bold white patch behind the auricular, bolder black face pattern, and white supercilium, as well as song.
