Description of the Bird

The bird was feeding and singing a little above head height in a Cottonwood at the North end of Huck Finn Pond (the last big one there).  It eventually flew up a bit higher to feed for a bit before flying off to the west toward the neighborhood behind/near the hatchery.

We compared it with the Grace's Warbler and yellow-rumped, but the markings of the (adult in breeding plumage) bird were just so distinct, and we were only about 8 feet away from it without any interference between us and the bird.

It had a black crown, very distinct white eyebrow, white lower eyering, and white cheeks with black malar region.  Its sides and flanks were striped black.  It's bill was quite long (as far a warblers go) and its throat was brilliantly yellow. 

I would suggest getting to know its call, as that was what caused us to stop and take a second look at it and not just brush it off as a yellow-rump. 

It was just singing its little heart out.  The call matched the description in the book (as unlikely as that often is).

Similar Species Discussion
