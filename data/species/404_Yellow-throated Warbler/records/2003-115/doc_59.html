Description of the Bird

It was a short-tailed warbler, moderately active.

The upperparts were a uniform gray, with white wingbars and some white feather edgings.

It had a large black face patch, with a prominent white supercilium separating black below from gray above.

It had a large, white ear patch behind the black face patch.

A broken white eye ring appeared to be more prominent behind the eye.

The throat was a bright, unstreaked yellow, changing abruptly to white at the breast.

The rest of the underparts were white, with dingy streaking at the sides. This streaking was most prominent on the upper breast, and to a lesser extent on the undertail coverts.

It was giving a single call note, and was foraging in scotch pine trees.
Behaviors: Foraging fairly actively in pine trees.
Call: single call note, given repeatedly
Plumage: uncertain

Similar Species Discussion

A Yellow-throated Warbler is quite distinctive. Perhaps the closest species in appearance are:
Grace's Warbler
Magnolia Warbler
eastern (Myrtle) Yellow-rumped Warbler
The white ear patch and white supercilium rule out Grace's Warbler. The white breast and the bold black and white patches on the face rule out Magnolia Warbler. The clean yellow throat, plus the lack of any black (except side streaking) on the underparts, rule out Myrtle Warbler.

I believe that this bird was a female, based on my observation of "dingy" streaking on the flanks. However, I have not had experience in identifying Yellow-throated Warblers to sex.
