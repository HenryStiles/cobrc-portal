Description of the Bird

The main issue was to differentiate this bird from other phalaropes. The bird was distant but I spent a great deal of time looking for what I considered to be definitive field marks.
From the expected species, Wilson's: a much shorter, heavier, more blunt bill. Clearly seen.
From Red-necked: pale uniform unmarked gray back, and much thinner bill.
Behaviors: Floating out on open water, and the bird was by itself.
Call: N/A
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

This was covered under field identification. The only two species with which this bird could be confused are Wilson's and Red-necked Phalaropes.
