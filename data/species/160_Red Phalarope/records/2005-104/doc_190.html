Description of the Bird

The bird was a small shorebird, about 25 percent smaller than a Killdeer. The legs were short, and as far as I could tell, black. The overall shape was "dumpy", partly due to the short legs, and short, thick neck. The bill was black, and short (about the length of the head), and as far as I could tell, black. The bill was also fairly thick, about as thick as the eye, and blunt tipped.

The face was white, except for a bold rectangular black mark through the eye, that extended back almost to the nape. The nape was white with a thin line of gray extending up to a darker hindcrown. The forecrown was white, at about the halfway mark on the crown.

The back was completely a medium gray (a bit darker than usual for REPH, in my experience), unmarked, with a little of the gray bleeding into the sides of the chest. The wing coverts were the same color gray, but there were a couple of black feathers mixed in. The underparts, except for where the gray bleeded onto the chest, were completely white.

The rump, seen in flight, was white with a black line down the center. The tail was gray. The underwings, seen quite well in flight, were bright white with no markings. The wings above were gray with blackish flight feathers, and a very bold white wings stripe.
Behaviors: The bird would spend much of its time on a small sandbar, foraging like a normal shorebird. Sometimes it would also swim in the water a little ways off the sandbar and act more like a phalarope. I only saw it land way out on the water once.
Call: we heard some twittering sounds that may have come from this bird, but I couldn't swear to that.
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

All shorebirds other than Red-necked Phalarope were ruled out by the black rectangular mark through the eye, as well as the combination of very short legs, clean gray back, white rump with black stripe through it, half gray, half black cap, and swimmin behavior

Red-necked Phalarope was ruled out by the thick, blunt bill, clean gray back with no white stripes, clean white underwings without gray markings, shorter legs, heavier build, and overall paler look.
