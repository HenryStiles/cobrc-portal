Description of the Bird

After hearing of a possible Red Phalarope and other interesting birds (3 loon species), Mark Peterson and I traveled to Cherry Creek Reservoir to look for them. After searching most of the reservoir's surface waters, we found ourselves in the far northeast corner as light was fading.

Mark spotted the bird, on the shoreline, close to us.

The bird was a large white-and-gray phalarope. The legs wiere grayish and seemed a bit short, but maybe that was because it was seen walking.

The face was distinctive, dark gray crown, wide dark mask, completely covering its eye, stoutly-based bill with a hint of yellow at the base on the mandible. The breast, throat, chin, and belly was white, with no other colors showing. The back, uppertail coverts, and dorsal surface of the tail was light gray, darkening on the hind neck and crown. The forehead was whitish.

We focused on the mask. It was broad towards its hindneck, tapering in width towards the bill.

The bird did flush onece, and I could see a wide white wing stripe that contrasted strongly with the rest of the gray wing. In flight the back appeared pure gray.
Behaviors: It wattled on the shore in an side-to-side gate, occasionally gleaning "stuff" off the shoreline sand.
Call: non heard
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

The only phalarope that needs to be separated from this species is Redj-necked Phalarope. RNPH has a fine longer bill, without the enlarged yellowish base to the mandible. Although RNPH can walk on shore, it usually does not. Mask on RNPH usually droops downward on the auricular unlike that of REPH. Bill on RNPH is finer and slightly longer.

Timing out so that's all.
