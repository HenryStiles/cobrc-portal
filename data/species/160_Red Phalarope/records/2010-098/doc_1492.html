Description of the Bird

2 birds were noticed nearby larger Wilson's Phalaropes. We also noticed these birds had much shorter & thicker bills than Wilson's.

Bills were short, blunt tipped, and appeared solid black. Yellow base of lower mandible not seen but often lacking in basic plumaged birds. We also did not see legs, tail, nor wing pattern because birds were swimming near middle of lake & did not fly.

Head and neck were solid white except for dark smudge mostly towards rear of crown, which melded into pale gray nape and back of neck. There was also a darker black smudge surrounding dark eye.

Back was uniform pale whitish gray and unstreaked, about same shade as rear of neck and slightly darker than white foreneck.
Behaviors: Birds swam about near middle of lake sometimes swimming in fairly tight circles with the typical phalarope spinning.
Call: None heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Birds were similar in color to Wilson's Phalarope's except for dark smudge around eye, darker crowns, much shorter blunter tipped bills and noticeably smaller in overall size compared to Wilson's.

Red-necked Phalaropes have darker backs with noticeable streaks. Our Red Phalaropes had very pale gray untreaked backs. Red-necks also have slimmer bills with pointed tips in contrast to thicker blunt tipped bills of Red Phalaropes.
