Description of the Bird

the bird was a small shorebird, smaller than any other bird out on the water except for a single Red-necked Phalarope which showed up late in the day. It was very slightly larger than this bird. The shape was typical of a Phalarope, with a fairly short neck (for a shorebird), long, straight, and pointed bill, longish legs (though short for a shorebird), etc. The bill was entirely black, except for a very small area of flesh color at the base, and fairly thick for a phalarope (about the same thickness as the eye). It was about as long as the head, and blunt tipped. The legs were black, and somewhat short, giving the bird a weird shape when on land.

The bird had a white forecrown, and a black hindcrown that split over the eyes. The face was white except for a black patch around the eye and a black patch behind the eye. Unlike any other Red Phalarope I've seen before, the mark on the face of this bird was not a continuous patch of black, but rather two seperated patches. The underparts were white, except for some clean pale gray on the flanks. The back was clean pale gray. The flight feathers were dark. In flight the bird had a bold white wingstripe above, and below had mostly white underwings.
Behaviors: The bird was first seen walking around on the shore of a small island. Later it was seen far out in the water, and then closer to shore but still in the water.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than Red-neckd and Wilson's Phalarope were ruled out by the distinctive shape, black marks on the white face, habits, etc. Red-necked Phalarope was ruled out by the clean gray back, bulkier shape, thicker bill, and whiter underwings. Wilson's Phalarope was ruled out by the different shape, bolder black mark on the face, shorter and thinner bill, etc.
