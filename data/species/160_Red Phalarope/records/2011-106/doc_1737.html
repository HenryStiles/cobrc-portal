Description of the Bird

Size: about 7-8 in long

Shape: phalarope-like, based on previous experience with Wilson's phalarope.

Bill: short, fairly thick (not like photos of red-necked I have seen); mainly dark but yellowish at base.

Plumage: rufous/reddish on flanks and neck; brownish back; blackish on head. But none of the colors were uniform, as the bird appeared to be transitioning to winter plumage.

I have a picture of the bird, which I will try to upload.
Behaviors: Observed only for a very short time before it flew away. It was resting on the water at Empire reservoir, near Wiggins, CO.
Call: not heard
Plumage: transitioning from breeding to winter

Similar Species Discussion

I am familiar with Wilson's phalarope; this one did not look like Wilson's, but it did look like a phalarope. I consulted Sibley's, which was more or less consistent red phalarope. I finally consulted a colleague from work who is an experienced birder (Peter Gent, of Boulder, CO), who identified it as a red phalarope and suggested I submit it to CBRC.
