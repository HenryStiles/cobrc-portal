Description of the Bird

Adult female phalarope. Prounounced black and white streaking on the back. Black crown with white patch around eye. Thickish yellow bill with black tip. Bird was not completely reddish brown below...believe it to be transitioning into non-breeding plumage.

Bird was very quiet and intensely foraging...spinning in the water and making short forays to shore where it also foraged and probed the shoreline. Would then fly...sometimes far out on the lake only to return and forage again.

Similar Species Discussion

Red-necked Phalarope and Wilson's were both eliminated due to coloration and bill color and shape. As the bird was behaving like a phalarope did not consider other species.


***Would like to note: this bird is being submitted as this is an unusual time of year for this bird and this may be a first county record for Huerfano County. This is very rare for our area of the state.
