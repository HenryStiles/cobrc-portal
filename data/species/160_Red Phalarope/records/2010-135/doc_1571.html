Description of the Bird

When I saw the bird it was very white bird with a gray back swimming circles close to the shore. Then I saw a black cap with a white forehead. Also saw a thick eyeline. The way it was holding the short thick bill was very distinct also, with the head up high and the bill turned downward. The bill was mostly black with a little bit of yellow on the lower mandible. It also had greenish legs.
Behaviors: It was mostly on the muddy shoreline and swimming right next to the shore.
Call: Never heard it
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

A shorebird swimming around in circles eliminates all but phalaropes. With the way it was holding its head and bill eliminates both phalaropes. The color of the back eliminates Red-necked Phalarope. The length of the bill eliminates Wilson’s Phalarope and the thickness eliminates both Wilson’s and Red-necked. The leg color also eliminates both Wilson’s and Red-necked.
