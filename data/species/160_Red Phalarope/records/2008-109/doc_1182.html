Habitat

DeWeese Res. is a 240 acre, manmade impoundment located in the Wet Mountain Valley of Custer Co. It is surrounded mostly by open grassland. The bird was seen on the north shore of the res. The shoreline was mudflats exposed by the retreating water level.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x42 Binoculars
Distance:  15 to 100 feet
Light:  Mid-afternoon sunshine. Direct sunshine; no shade.

Description of the Bird

See photographs. No detailed observations were made.
Behaviors: The bird was wading on the shoreline or swimming in the water just off the shore.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Non breeding, some molt plumage on wings

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was determined to be a non-breeding phalarope by the size (about 8”), gray back and unstreaked white breast, straight bill and swimming behavior. The dark ear patch eliminated the Wilson Phalarope. The short, thick bill eliminated the Red-necked Phalarope.

Resources Used

National Geographic’s Birds of North America was referred to on site. Later, Sibley and Peterson.

Previous Experience

The Red Phalarope was a lifer. I’ve seen scads of Wilson Phalaropes and numerous Red-necked Phalaropes.
