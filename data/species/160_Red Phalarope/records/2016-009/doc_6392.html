Description of the Bird

I was at the North Shore Marina at Pueblo Reservoir, looking at birds in the cove, when I looked down and there was a small bird swimming on the water, very close to shore.  I quickly said, "Red Phalarope", Bill Maynard, Richard Bunn, and Jan Albright were all birding with me, and quickly saw the bird.  The small white bird, had a gray back, thick black line through eyes, and pink color to the thickish black bill.  All of these things were correct for a Red Phalarope and not Red-necked Phalarope.  See photos.

Similar Species Discussion

Red-necked Phalarope has thin bill, all black, more lines in back.

Previous Experience

I saw a Red Phalarope back on January 7, 2016 while scoping the ocean in San Diego, CA.  It was in this same plumage.  I've seen lots of Red Phalaropes in breeding plumage in Alaska, and also in winter plumage in CA and WA, while on boats in the Ocean.  I've seen just a few winter plumaged birds in Colorado.  Never one in the middle of the Winter.
