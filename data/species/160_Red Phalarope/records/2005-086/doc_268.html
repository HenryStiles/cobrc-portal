Description of the Bird

A small, overall white and gray pelagic shorebird. The bill was stout and straight, appearing to be mostly black. The face was mostly white, with a nearly black postoccular spot not extending to the nape. The bird also had a dark gray crown with some pale mottling. Its back was mostly gray, with the gray feathers appearing quite new with crisp pale fringing. A darker line of retained alternate back feathers appear above the new, pale tipped gray scapulars, and there are dark retained median and greater coverts and tertials. The primaries also appear to be worn, dark retained basic feathers with buffy fringes. The body and neck feathers have molted and are mostly bright white, with a little gray near the sides of the upper breast. The legs appear to be dark gray.
Behaviors: Unexpectedly close to shore, the bird was mostly resting in shallow water or probing the mud with other shorebirds.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult molting into basic

Similar Species Discussion

Basic Wilsons Phalarope would have a much thinner, needle like bill and would be less expected out in the middle of a large lake. It would also lack the dark postoccular line and crown. Basic Red necked Phalarope would show a darker, more streaked back and darker gray scapulars as well as more gray on the flanks, creating a darker overall appearance. Red-necked Phalarope would also have a thinner bill.
