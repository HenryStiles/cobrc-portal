Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Red Phalarope
First Date/Time:  10/3/2010
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  25 min.
County:  Mesa
Specific Location:  It was in a pond directly south of Redlands Parkway, just east of the bridge which crosses the Colorado River. It is known as Redlands Parkway South Pond.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

a wooded pond- tamarisk and small cottonwoods.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski 65 mm scope with 20-60X zoom eyepiece.
Distance:  as close as 20 ft.
Light:  At the original sighting the sun was at a low angle, and I was looking toward it at an angle, thus with a partial glare. I subsequently saw the bird on each of the days of its presence and sometimes had excellent lighting from behind.

Description of the Bird

Size: swam past hen Mallard which was more than twice its size.
Bill black, straight, about as long as the head, heavy at base, blunt tip. Back and wings gray; no white seen in scapulars. Dark primaries. Black ear patch, drooping to rear behind eye. Forehead white.
There was a faint reddish wash in upper breast and throat which could be seen only in certain lighting.
Behaviors: It was swimming in a small pond. the only other bird on the pond- a hen Mallard. It was actively foraging, gleaning insects from the water surface. It turned one way, then the other, but I didn't see it spinning as phalaropes often do. I did not see the bird fly.
Call: none heard
Plumage: winter (basic) adult

Similar Species Discussion

Red-necked Phalarope. This bird had an unstreaked gray back as opposed to the streaking on the Red-necked and a much stouter bill, compared to the thin tapered bill of the Red-necked.

Previous Experience

I have seen many of these birds from pelagic trips off California to almost annual sightings in ponds in Arizona from 1974 to 1999, also in Alaska. I have seen many Red-necked Phalaropes annually.
