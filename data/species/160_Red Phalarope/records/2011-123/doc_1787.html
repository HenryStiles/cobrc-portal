Description of the Bird

This was a phalarope by coloration, behavior, and bill shape and length.

A gray phalorope with the remanent patches on throat and sides of neck of buffy juvenile plumage. The bill, for a phalarope, was thick at the base and blunt-tipped and dark and about the same length as the width of the head. The face was white with a black mask and black on the crown. The nape was white with just a little remaining buffy coloration from juvenile plumage. The light gray back was without any dark streaks. The rump (photo) was whitish. The gray wing has a very wide white streak especially wide on the secondaries (photo). The ventral surface was whitish except for a noticeably buffy throat patch. The bird rode high in the water.
Behaviors: Rapidly feeding on hatching midges fairly close to shore. Picked midges from the surface but also from the air. Spread wings once but did not fly.
Call: non heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Wilson's Phalarope - This species with its very long needle like bill is distinctive.

Red-necked Phalarope. Head of this species usually seen as rectangular compared to blocky square head of REPH. This species has dark patterend streaks on its back unlike REPH's all gray back and RNPH sits noticeably lower in the water and I think they show quicker jerky motions when they feed. At close range the bill is thinner compared to the thicker based REPH bill.
