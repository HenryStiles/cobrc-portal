Description of the Bird

A phalarope based on size, shape and behavior in mostly basic plumage. Plain (unstreaked) pale gray mantle, white bellow with white belly, breast, throat and face. Face with a black trianglular mark behind the eye. Gray mantle color extended up the back of the neck, nape and cown. Forehead was white. Bill was short and thick, relative to RNPH. Head and neck were thick.
Behaviors: Swimming in circular patterns while picking up morsels from the water. Stayed mostly in the middle of the lake. No other phalaropes were present at the time of this observation.
Call: none
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Red necked Phalarope ... very similar in basic plumage, though short blunt bill and plain gray mantle of REPH eliminates this species from consideration.
Wilsons Phalarope ... this species has no large black mark behind eye in basic plumage. Bill of WIPH is ling and needle like, much different than subject bird.
