Description of the Bird

the bird was a fairly small shorebird, with a body about the size of nearby Dunlin, though it appeared smaller since it was swimming on the water. The bird's bill was black, and about the length of the head, and thick, about the width of the eye. It ended in a blunt tip not much thinner than the base of the bill. There was a tiny and almost invisible patch of pale coloration at the base of the lower mandible that was only visible upon inspection of the photos. The neck appeared fairly thick for a Phalarope, the bird overall appeared fairly stocky, partially because of the thick bill and neck.

The bird had a black crown, bifurcated by a thick white crown stripe connecting to the white fore-crown. There was a very thin strip of black running down the back of the neck, which also connected to the black crown. On an otherwise white face there was a large rectangular patch of black, running from the eye almost to the stripe of black on the back of the neck, but seperated from that by an area of white. The eye was black.

The bird's neck, chest, undertail coverts, and sides were white, with some faint gray markings on the sides. The back was clean, pale gray, with some black feathers mixed in where the neck stripe connected to the back, but otherwise unmarked. The flight feathers were black, with some thin white edging. The wingtips were about as long as the tail. I did not see the tail.
Behaviors: the bird was swimming and spinning on the water at absolutely amazingly close range, and seemed unconcerned by our presence
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

All other species other than Red-necked Phalarope were ruled out by the white face with black "phalarope mark", as well as clean gray back, white neck, chest and sides, and black cap.

Red-necked Phalarope was ruled out by the thick bill with a blunt tip, clean gray back without white markings and heavier build.
