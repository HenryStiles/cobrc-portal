Description of the Bird

the bird was a small shorebird, with a body noticeably smaller than that of a nearby Killdeer. It swam on the surface of the water, and the legs were never visible. The overall shape was somewhat chunky, with a short and "fat" neck, for a shorebird. The bill was short, a tiny bit shorter than the length of the head. It was also thin, but not needlelike, about the thickness of the eye. The color was pale orange, with a small black tip.

The cap was black, extending to in front of the eye and around the base of the bill. The bird had a bold white cheek patch, extending from just in front of the eye to almost the nape. The nape itself was black, an extension of the black cap. The area from below the white cheek patch was bright brick red, including the entirety of the neck, chest and underparts. The back and wings were black, with bold pale brown stripes down the length of the scapulars and on the wing coverts.

The wings were about as long as the tail. The underwings, seen when the bird briefly flew the length of the pond, were bright white. The tail was gray, with black central tail feathers. The rump was the same color red as the underparts, with a strip of black from the tail to the back. The wings had a bold white stripe down the base of the primaries on the upperside.
Behaviors: The bird spent all it's time on a small playa, partly on shore, and partly swimming on the surface of the water. It was seen eating some large fish or invertebrates.
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All similar species were ruled out by the umistakable pattern of red over most the body, white cheek, black cap, and yellow bill.
