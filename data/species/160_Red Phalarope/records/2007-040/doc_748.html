Description of the Bird

When I arrived at the playa, I noticed a small to medium-sized shorebird swimming along the edge in typical pigeon-necking phalarope fashion. The bird was solidly brick-red on the underparts from the throat to the vent. The back and wings were dark with light streaking. The bird's facial pattern was very distinctive, with a large white ear patch that extended above and below the eye while the remainder of the head was dark colored. The bird's bill was dull yellow with a slight darkish tip. The bird flew a short distance once, and flapped a couple other times while swimming, and each time I noticed gray upperwings with a white wing stripe extending along the base of all the flight feathers. The underwings were mostly white.
Behaviors: The phalarope swam along the edge of the playa, foraging among some emergent vegetation - apparently picking prey items off of the water surface. It flew once for a short distance, but stayed within the playa.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Other reddish shorebirds, like Hudsonian Godwit, Red Knot, and Curlew Sandpiper are ruled out by the bird's white facial patch, yellow bill, and swimming/foraging behavior.
