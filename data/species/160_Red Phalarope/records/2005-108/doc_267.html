Description of the Bird

A small, overall white and gray pelagic shorebird. The bill was stout and straight, appearing to be mostly black. The face was mostly white, with a nearly black postoccular line extending to a dark gray nape. The bird also had a nearly black crown. It had a clean, pale gray back but the lower fringes of the folded wings were pretty dark and I thought I detected a faint rufous wash along the line between the dark nape and the white neck & throat. I thought it might be an adult retaining a little alternate plumage, but after looking at my crappy, distant digiscoped shots and comparing them to Paulson's Shorebirds of North America, The Photographic Guide, I think it matches what he is calling an immature. I interpret this as a 1st winter bird. In particular, that plumage shows a more extensive dark crown that extends above the eye instead of farther back in an adult, and has the dark median and greater coverts creating the dark fringe above the white flanks.
Behaviors: Swimming, feeding, and preening in lake. Its swimming behavior was active with many quick turns, as expected of a phalarope.
Call: not heard
Plumage: 1st winter per Paulson

Similar Species Discussion

Basic Wilsons Phalarope would have a much thinner, needle like bill and would be less expected out in the middle of a large lake. It would also lack the dark postoccular line and crown. Basic Red necked Phalarope would show a darker, more streaked back and more gray on the flanks, creating a darker overall appearance, and would have a thinner bill.
