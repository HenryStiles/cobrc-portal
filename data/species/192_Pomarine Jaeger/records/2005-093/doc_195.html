Description of the Bird

The bird was a little larger than a Ring-billed Gull, and stockier in build, overall a more powerful bird. I was the typical jaeger shape, with broad wings, somewhat wedge-shape tail, and heavier build than gulls. The overall color was meduim to dark brown, darkest on the head, and palest on the undertail coverts and underwings.

The bird had a dark head, chest, upper belly, and back, fairly uniform in color. The color was a fairly cold brown, though not grayish, and in certain lights there appeared to be a slight tawny tone to the edges of the back feathers. This was hard to see, though, and I was only able to discern it at a few limited times.

The lower flanks had some very, very faint checkering, very hard to see except with a close look. Towards the undetail coverts this became much more obvious, and the undetail coverts themselves were boldly barred brown and white. The uppertail coverts were also barred brown and white, but here the white predominated, making the uppertail coverts appear white at a distance.

The tail was fairly short (for a Jaeger), and dark brown. The tail had two very short, blunt central retrices that barely extended past the tip of the tail, but this was hard to see except with a close look.

The underwings of the bird were boldly checkered brown and white, but here the brown predominated slightly. At a distance, though, the wings appeared very pale underneath, and only with a close look the they appear darker. The base of the primaries had a very large and bold white flash underneath, with the primary tips being blackish. There was then a "double flash", created by the white edge of some of the primary coverts underneath, and seperated from the white primary flash by a row of dark coverts, that was very obvious on this individual, even at a distance. The secondaries were dark.

The upperwings were dark, except for a white flash at the base of the primaries, created by white primary shafts, most obvious on the outer two (and at a distance these were all that was visible, approaching Long-tailed Jaeger), but with a close look white primary shafts could be seen on at least the outer 6-7 primaries.

The bird had a fairly large bill, visible on the bird even at a great distance, and the base was bluish-gray, and the outer third or so was black. I did not get a good enough look at this feature to comment on bill stucture.

The wings were very broad based, and the overall build very bulky, even for a Jaeger. The flight style was very powerful, with strong wingbeats, and a direct flight.
Behaviors: when not sitting on the water adjacent to the dam, the bird was flying circuits around the reservoir, and chasing and harrasing gulls very agressively.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal dark or intermediate morph

Similar Species Discussion

All species of gull were ruled out by the build of this bird, with very broad wings and stocky shape, as well as behavior (chasing other gulls very aggressively), and proportions (longer-winged that gulls). The very dark color also helped rule out almost all species of gull.

Ruling out the other two Jaegers was much harder. Parasitic was ruled out by the lack of the bright tawny edges to the upperpart feathers expected for this form, by the larger size when compared to RBGUs than expected, by the blunt tipped central retrices, by the prominent "double-flash" on the underwings, by the strongly barred undertail coverts, and by the lack of a strong flash of white at the base of the primaries above.

Long-tailed Jaeger was ruled out by the larger size when compared to RBGUs, by the shorter tail when compared to the body, especially in the central retrices, by the prominent "double flash" on the base of the primaries below, by the warmer brown overall color than expected for that species, by the slight tawny edges to the upperpart feathers, by the larger and heavier bill, by the broader based wings, by the more powerful flight-style, and by the habit of persistently harrasing even large gulls.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have seen a number of Jaegers of all three species off both coasts, mostly Poms, but also Parasitics and a few Long-taileds. I have only been able to study well a few Jaegers, including dark morphs of both Parasitic and Long-tailed, but this was my first close encounter with a dark-morph Pom.
