Description of the Bird

A dark jaeger, with bulky body and head. Chocolate brown mantle in resting bird with no apparent cinnamon tones or barring visible. Head color was medium to light brown contrasting with mantle in resting bird. Base of primaries white, seen well in underwing of flying bird. White at primary shafts of outer few primaries above, seen in flying bird. White at the base of the underwing primary coverts giving a prominent double white flash in the flying bird. Bird appear bulky compared to PAJA and LTJA. A slight central tail feather protrusion was detected.
Behaviors: Harassing gulls, while flying from one end of lake to the other, Occasionally resting on water.
Call: none noted
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The other 2 North America jaegers are extremely similar in juvenile plumage. Bulkiness of this bird as well as the pale bases to it's underwing primary coverts made me think Pomarine Jaeger. Lack of cinammon or grayish tones lead me away from other 2 species.
