Description of the Bird

This bird was a jaeger, identified by its mostly chocolate brown plumage with barred undertail coverts, bill thinner than a gull's bill, and white primary tips which were more pointed than that shown on any of the gull species.

This is was what I believe is a dark intermediate morph.

Bill was strongly bicolored, gray with black tip to distall 1/4 of bill.

Head was blocky with NO contrasting hindnecked coloration.
Mantle was barred with rusty edges
Base of tail (dorsal surface) was barred out half way to its tip
Undertail was heavily barred and this feature was the most distinctive feature at rest and while in flight
Pot-bellied look, looked like it was not centered properly to be able to fly, big-ass chest, wide too
Flew with powerful but SLOW wingbeats, often banking and landing in the H20
Wingtips with outer three (at least) primaries very contrasty whitish with remainder of dark primaries.
Underwing very mottled and quite noticeable in flight.
Arm of bird very wide, compared to rapidly tapering hand with ponted tips.

Tail short. Only on occasion did small rounded tail projections become just barely visible
Behaviors: Fed alone, did not chase any gulls or other birds.
Appeared attracted to boat wakes, often flying a short ways and landing in the wake, sometimes picking "stuff" off of the water surface.

Strong flier.

Seemed to prefer resting in water in area of south bank where point sticks into reservoir.
Call: none given
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

I really DON'T want to do this so I am going to be brief

Both Long-tailed and Arctic (Skuas) Jaeger jueveniles HAVE PALE NAPES CONTRASTING WITH HEAD AND BACK COLOR - this bird, the POM does not

Parasitic has a little dinky head. This bird has a substantial big blocky head. Slower flight than other two species. Very noticeable as this bird flew around a LOT and in quite close. Very difficult to see tail projections which at this range would have been quite visible on other two species. I always loved the quote in Olsen and Larsson's Skuas and Jaegers "Pommies go for the player, Arcitc for the ball". In this case there was NO chasing either player or ball. Barred uppertail coverts eliminates Parasitic. Extremely bull-necked, (see photos) never found on Long-tailed. This bird, I forgot to mention has dark lores that contrasted with the dark, but lighter than the lores, head. (see photos)
Bill thickness (quite thick, see photos) much thicker than Parasitic, thus eliminating it. Long-tailed also eliminated as that species would show at least some rounded tail projections, up to 6 inches worth and this bird just barely showed any.
