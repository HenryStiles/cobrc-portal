Description of the Bird

A large adult light-morph jaeger (brown upperparts, whitish underparts, white flash in primaries, long R1s) that was an even medium brown above. The black cap extended below the eye and below the gape. The bill was thick-based and fairly long and looked mostly or all black. Yes, the species is supposed to have a bicolored bill, but I didn't see the bill all that well (due to not paying it much attention and not noting the feature in my notes).

The tail was dark brown and the R1s extended beyond the rest of the rectrices at least as long as the length of the R2s, probably a bit longer. The extensions were very wide-tipped and twisted 90 deg or so from the horizontal.

The underwing coverts were brown, except that the bases of the greater primary coverts were whitish, creating a second white flash, separate from the flash created by the white bases to the primaries. There was a wide and obvious dark chest band, but not sharply defined as is typical of Parasitic Jaeger. The undertail coverts and vent region were dark, but not as brown, as the upperparts.

The bird was quite pot-bellied, with the center of the bird's weight falling under the wings (Long-tailed's falls in front of the wings and Parasitic's near the leading edge of the wing, though it's quite variable for that species).
Behaviors: The bird would swim by itself for periods of time, interspersed with aggressive chases of Franklin's Gulls in the air.
Call: None
Plumage: Definitive Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

No other species at all similar except for the other two jaeger species. Both of those lack the double-white flash on the underwing, the shape and twistiness of the R1s, and the size and bulk of the bird, and are also eliminated by cap shape.
