Description of the Bird

a jeager by size and hooked bill compared with gulls. Bill two-toned with dark tip, rather thick. Body barred brown and white. Wings on dorsal side with rufous bars and underneath with two white "wing flashes" (see photos). tail with two central rects extending just past tips of others. Size larger than Ring-billed Gull it was chasing. Slow, but purposeful flight. Wings bowed when flying. Head color brown, darker between eye and bill. Barred uppertail coverts. Entire underwing barred (see photo).
Behaviors: Spent most of time loafing. Saw it fly four times, once chasing a Ring-billed Gull (photo).
Other flights were lower about 30 feet above the water. Last observed flight high out over dam at Chatfield and then directly overhead.
Call: none heard
Plumage: junvenile intermediate morph

Similar Species Discussion

Long-tailed Jaeger - smaller, more rapid flight, cold appearance without rufous edgings on any feathers; without double wing flash. Usually shows 2-3 primary shaft streaks not 5-7 like this bird.

Parasitic Jager - slimmer head with triangular shape without double wing flash on ventral wing surface, not as barrel-chested as this bird
