Viewing Conditions

Light:  Overcast light conditions with high clouds made for great viewing conditions.

Description of the Bird

Dark bird with a paler brown rump, and white bases to the primaries and greater primary coverts giving the distinctive "double flash" of pale on the underwing. Bill was stout with a black tip, feet were black, legs pale gray. Brown underparts were finely barred, including belly, undertail/vent and wing linings. Slightly smaller than a Herring Gull but had a proportionally heavier body and proportionally smaller head. Tail slightly wedge shaped due to protruding central tail feathers.
Behaviors: Seen circling the reservoir, twice giving chase to Ring-billed Gulls. It never got a meal from either of them (nor did it make a meal of either of them) as far as I could tell.
Call: none
Plumage: light intermediate immature

Similar Species Discussion

Long-tailed and Parasitic Jaegers were both ruled out by the bill size and shape, the size and bulk of this individual relative to Ring-Billed Gulls, the plumage details described above (particularly the barred underparts, pattern of pale markings on the underside of the wing, and pale rump).

Resources Used

No resources were used at the time. One week prior, I had great looks at a very similar individual on Lake Erie just north of Cleveland during a "pelagic" boat trip. Resources used then (prior to observing the CO bird) included Sibley & Kauffman guides, and later "Skuas and Jaegers: A Guide to the Skuas and Jaegers of the World " by Olsen & Larsson.

Previous Experience

Similarly plumaged individual was seen well the previous week in Ohio (see "Resources Used for ID"). The bulk of my experience with Jaegers before that was from reading field guides, an adult light morph seen in June at Pueblo Reservoir a decade or so ago (found by Dave Silverman) and a few distant "jaeger sp." seen of the Atlantic coast between 3-6 years ago.
