Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized larid, a bit larger than Ring-billed Gulls, closer in size to California Gull. The overall shape was similar to that of a California Gull, but bulkier, with proportionally broader wings and a bulkier torso. The tail was a bit longer than that of a gull, and slightly wedge shaped, with the two central tail feathers projecting slightly beyond the rest of the tail. The bill was quite heavy, with a strong gonydeal angle about 2/3 of the way from the base of the bill to the tip. The base of the bill was pinkish gray in color, the nail was black.

The overall color of the bird was warm, choclatey
brown. The upperwings had a broad white flash at the base of the primaries. The uppertail coverts were mostly brown, with some small white checks. The underwings were boldly checked with white. The underside of the primaries had a bold white base; the primary coverts on the underwing were also white, creating a "double flash" when combined with the white undersides of the primaries. The undertail coverts were heavily barred black and white.
Behaviors: The bird was first observed flying around about 30 feet over the water. It eventually landed near the middle of the lake and spent the rest of the observation swimming out there.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal intermediate morph

Similar Species Discussion

Gulls were ruled out by the heavy build of the bird, with broad wings, hefty body, large head, and large bill. Gulls were also ruled out by the wedge shaped tail with elongated central tail feathers, white checking on the underwing, white flash at the base of the primaries of the underwing, and overall color.

Long-tailed Jaeger was ruled out by the large size, very hefty build, large bill with gonydeal angle nearer the tip than in LTJA, double flash on the underside of the primaries, warm brown color, and more than two white primary shafts on the upper wing.

Parasitic Jaeger was ruled out by the white double flash on the underside of the primaries, large size, hefty shape, broader wings, and lack of rufous feather edges above.
