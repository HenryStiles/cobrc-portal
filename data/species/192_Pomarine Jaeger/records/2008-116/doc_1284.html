Description of the Bird

Large bull-necked jaeger species baiscally dark brown throughout but with extensive white at the base of the primaries and rectrices.; underwing and axillaries were barred. The back and rump showed some barring; thick, black tipped gray bill and gray legs; dark eye.

The central rectrices showed a stubby extension.

The bird was large enough to take a Franklin's Gull out of the sky, kill it and consume it.
Behaviors: The bird alternated between sitting on the water and foraging past other birds, occasionally giving chase to smaller gulls.

Call: none
Plumage: probable sub adult dark or barred phase

Similar Species Discussion

Large size for a jaeger relative to Franklin's Gull and the ability to take a Franklin's Gull, plus extensive white at the base of the primaries and stubby tail feathers ruled out Parasitic and Long-tailed Jaeger. I think Skuas would show even more white at the base of the primaries, perhaps none in the tail and a mono-toned bill.
