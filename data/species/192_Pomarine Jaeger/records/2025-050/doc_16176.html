Description of the Bird

Juvenile light/intermediate morph jaeger, first spotted by me on the water.  Took several flights, pursuing Ring-billed Gulls aggressively, and allowing for good views and photographs by Ethan.

Field identification was largely based on GISS impressions -- the bird's head shape seemed gull-like, with a stout sharply bicolored bill.  The bird's wings were broad with signficiant underwing flash.  In direct comparison to Ring-billed Gulls it pursued, it seemed to be slightly larger than the gull.  On the water, the undertail coverts appeared strongly barred and white-based, not buffy.  Its behavior was aggressive and its flight was powerful.  When the bird tired of harassing gulls, it would ride thermals and soar quite high, behavior I have never seen of a jaeger on a pelagic or seawatch but I did see with the Pomarine at Chatfield in October.

I reviewed photographs taken by Ethan Cleveland, Cole Sage, and Isaac Boardman to solidify the ID.  The photographs provide a similar GISS impression of a broad-winged, bulky bird with a gull-shaped head, a thick neck, and a stout bill.  In addition, the photographs show the following plumage features, which together suggest Pomarine Jaeger to me:

- White bases to the underwing primary coverts, forming a "double flash"

- Neatly barred undertail coverts, appearing white especially in Cole's photos, and uppertail coverts strongly barred as well

- 5ish white primary shafts

- Underwing coverts, especially the axilliaries, are neatly barred, not wavy

- Central tail feathers seem short and blunt, especially in Isaac's photos

- Head generally smooth brown in color; although it has a paler nape "Golden Eagle" effect especially in my poor digiscopes, it doesn't have a pale collar or a dark-capped appearance

- Overall coloration is gray-brownish, without rusty tones.  Isaac's photos taken in late afternoon light seem buffier because of the lighting but not cinnamon or rusty

- I believe I see eye arcs in Isaac's best photo, which is generally seen in Pom/Long-tailed but not Parasitic.

 

The only photos I took were poor digiscopes and are not helpful for ID, so I won't upload them here.  Instead I will link to eBird checklists by Ethan Cleveland, Cole Sage, and Isaac Boardman, where good-quality photos of the bird in flight are available:

Similar Species Discussion

Long-tailed Jaeger was eliminated based on flight style and GISS (especially overall size compared to the Ring-billed Gulls).

Parasitic Jaeger is always difficult to eliminate in juvenile jaegers, but the combination of features described on the previous page suggests Pomarine to me over Parasitic.  In particular, I would expect Parasitic to have rustier/cinnamon tones, wavier underwing coverts, less zebra-striped under and upper-tail coverts, a more triangular head, a paler collar giving a capped effect, pointier central tail feathers, and streakier patterning on the head.

Previous Experience

I have seen many jaegers of all three species on pelagics off the West Coast, where ID is generally confirmed by expert spotters.  I have seen many Long-taileds and a few Parastics in Alaska.  I have seen a few Parasitics while seawatching Puget Sound, where Parasitic is the only expected species but others are possible.  This is only the third jaeger I have ever seen at a reservoir inland -- the Long-tailed at Cherry Creek SP in August and the Pomarine at Chatfield in October.
