Description of the Bird

A dark jaeger, with bulky body and head. Chocolate brown mantle in resting bird with no apparent cinnamon tones or barring visible. Head color was medium to light brown contrasting with mantle in resting bird. Base of primaries white, seen well in underwing of flying bird. White at primary shafts of outer few primaries above, seen in flying bird. Occasionally an isolated brown spot was seen in underwing at base of primaries / ends of primary coverts suggesting lighter primary covert bases. Bird appear thick-winged and bulky compared to PAJA and LTJA. No central tail feather protrusions were detected.
Behaviors: Occasionally harassing gulls (California) then resting in the water near the middle of the lake. Would fly low across the lake to a group of resting gulls, make them fly, and isolate one gull to chase.
Call: none
Plumage: Intermediate or Light Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

LTJA and PAJA are very similar in this plumage. The overal size, shape and color of the bird as well as several photos showing a "double flash" in the underwing supported POJA.
