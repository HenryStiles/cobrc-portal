Description of the Bird

Unique plump shape of this shorebird with long straight bill indicates that this was of the Scolopax genus , and the buffy orange ( not barred ) underparts that this was an American Woodcock not Eurasian. Head had three broad black transverse bands on crown ,dark centers with white edging to the upper scapulars , very short tail upper surfaces lightly barred, buffy orange underparts make this an easy call.
Behaviors: Spent most of the time resting on ground sleeping. Did get up once and walked a few inches , and did do a bobbing and rocking motion as it moved forward .
Call: No calls heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Eurasian Woodcock has grey barred underparts ,not bufy orange , and complex black ,orange and white feathering on wing scapulars and coverts. Eurasian Woodcock is also somewhat larger.
