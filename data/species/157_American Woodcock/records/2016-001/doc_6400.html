Description of the Bird

Observed these two birds in an area directly across from the Park Rangers residence at this Ft. Collins openspace area.  Was part of a group of Denver Field Ornithologist that were on a Larimer/Weld county field trip.  This bird has been seen at multiple times at this location within the last few months.  A bird had been seen last year at this same location.  These birds were down on the banks and in shallow water in a stream that flows through this area.  This area is heavily wooded with cottonwoods and other wetland shrubs.  These birds were observed foraging the muddy banks for some food source.  They moved about very slowly and did not make an audible sounds that we could here.

Similar Species Discussion

These birds were in classic plumage and they apperaed to look exactly as photos of them in All About Birds (Cornell University).  They were much smaller than a Wilson's Snipe and were definitely not Killdeer or Rails.
