Description of the Bird

Bird was along the edge of a small creek. It was very well camouflaged. It had a constant bobbing motion as it foraged and walked. I did not hear the call.

Similar Species Discussion

No other species were considered. It was widely posted by reliable members of the birding community that the bird was an American Woodcock and it conforms to the pictures in our guides.

Resources Used

Sibley's Second Edition and Singer, Robins & Zimm Golden Guide. In addition, the expertise of the other birders who were present viewing the Woodcock.

Previous Experience

This is the first time I have seen an American Woodcock. I saw a common Snipe in 2002. I would say that I have next to no experience. Having 45 minutes to observe this bird was enough to create a good impression of the look and behavior of the bird.
