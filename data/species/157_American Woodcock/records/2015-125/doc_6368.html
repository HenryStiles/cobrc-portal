Description of the Bird

The bird was flushed 3 times when I was around (I think it was flushed 3 times in the morning).  Two of those were around noon and the last one was around 4 PM.  The first time, Dave Leatherman and I just flushed a brown bird with rounded wings that made a tiwttering sound with its wings.  The second time, I flushed it alone from next to an apartment building (3 stories?) and the bird continued to fly (struggling) over the building.  This time I noted the bird was buffy underneath with a long bill, small round head, and gray/brown camoflauge pattern on the wings/back.  The final time the bird was flushed, it came from some dense vegetation as Austin Hess, John Shenot, and I were walking along a row of trees about 250 yards from the original sightings.  Again, I noted the long beak, small round head with a dark eye, clean buffy lower parts and brown/gray patterend upper parts.  Overall, I probably saw the bird for less than 30 seconds.

All three times the bird was flushed it flew straight up first and made a twittering sound with its wings. 

Similar Species Discussion

Wilson's Snipe: not found in forested habitat, coloration not buffy underneath and more brown/black than brown/gray, snipe usually give a different low pitched call when flushed.
