Description of the Bird

When first observed the woodcock was actively feeding using two methods. 1st it walked slowly, randomly probing unfrozen ground in and at edge of stream. 2nd foraging observation was the bird standing in one location and rocking back and forth like a rocking chair, almost quivering and then inserting bill and usually extracting an earthworm which it rapidly consumed.  Also seen at rest in an upright position as compared to the foraging posture. At rest only, one eye stayed open as it was cryptically concealed and almost invisible without knowing where it was.

Bird was a large shorebird by overall shape and longish bill and foraging techniques.  The long bill was light gray and maybe 2x the length of its head.  Its eye was farther back on the head than what I have observed in other shorebirds. The breast was tawny with some orangish tones.  The back was mottled mix of grays and browns of different hues giving it the cryptic coloration.  The crown was dark brown with some a 2-3 light horizonal thin bars.   The forehead was buff.  The overall appearance was a big chunky shorebird in the woods.

Similar Species Discussion

The only species that comes close is Wilson's Snipe for species that occur in CO.  WISN is small, slimmer, with a longer bill, and a mostly white breast and belly.  Two were in the area for comparison.  The WISN gave a distinctive call when flushed.  This bird was bigger, chunkier, foraged using a much different technique and was under a complete canopy unlike the snipe that liked more open areas along the same stream.

Previous Experience

I grew up in Ohio where there was a spot I could go to to mostly hear them peenting at dusk.  I have seen a few in Maine and again later in life at MaGee Marsh in Ohio two years ago, but for sure more heard than seen well.  Also heard and briefly saw the one displaying in Rocky Ford State Wildlife Area a long time ago.
