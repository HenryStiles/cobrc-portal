Habitat

In a fairly developed area with many homes nearby. Was spending most of its time around a feeder. The time I was there the bird was spending most of its time in a tree which I was not able to identify. About 300 feet away was a gulch with cottonwoods along it. About 100 feet away is a rail for public transportation. A pretty busy area for this bird.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon 200-500mm f5.6
Distance:  40 feet
Light:  Direct sunlight, sunny, no overcast at the moment

Description of the Bird

Was providing a short chip call for the duration of my visit. The bird was not shy and would travel back and forth between two yards. One with a tree it liked to perch the other yard had the feeder it was visiting. I was able to see the bird coming back to the tree in the other yard while still having seeds in the mouth. Was not interacting much with the nearby House Finches, Song Sparrow, Black-capped Chickadees, and Starlings. It kept to itself only making breif trips to get seed from the feeder.

Similar Species Discussion

The only species remotley similar would be a Northern Cardinal. The squised and bugly beak were very distinct. The amount of red on the face and chest is what makes me believe it is a male.

Resources Used

Past experience with Pyrrhuloxia in the Southwesrn USA. Also many other birders were there and agreed with the identification.

Previous Experience

I have seen many Pyrrhuloxia in the Southwestern USA. There I have become familiar with their calls and behavior. The Northern Cardinal which could sound somewhat similar I have come to know well when spending time in the Eastern USA.
