Description of the Bird

Behavior - Bird was landing in a leafless bush and ornamental tree when it would arrive back in the area. It would then fly on or near the ground looking for seeds or hiding. I couldn’t see it through the vegetation when it dropped down so not 100% sure what it did. One time it flew two houses away to their back yard bird seed feeder. That is where we all got good looks and photos without going into the yard.

Plumage - Mostly medium gray cardinal shaped bird with a crest. The following areas had red including crest tip, around bill and eyes, outer tail feathers, primary edges, and tibial feathers. There was a light thin reddish/pink wash down the center of the chest and belly but not as prominent as in breeding season. That is why I thought it might be a juvenile male verses an adult male but I’m not sure about that. The bill was short, stubby, thick, and yellow.

Vocalizations – As I was arriving in the area, I wasn’t sure I was in the right place. No other birders were there. The first bird I heard vocalizing was the loud metallic chip call note of the Pyrrhuloxia. I didn’t find it right away but could hear it. I soon found it. I did record an audio clip that isn’t very good as everyone at the time was talking excitedly about seeing the bird. Oh well! I never heard a song, only the call notes.

Similar Species Discussion

Northern Cardinal female was eliminated due to yellow verses a red bill. Other items were also different but that is an easy way to eliminate it.

Gray Catbird was eliminated due to lack of crest, thin bill, rufous vent and wrong shape.

I really can’t think of any other bird to eliminate.
