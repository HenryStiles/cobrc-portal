Description of the Bird

The bird resembled a grey cardinal with a red belly, yellow beak, prominent crest, and long tail. First observed with a number of house finches and black-capped chickadees perched on the fence next to the bird feeder during a snow storm. After a moment on the fence, the bird made use of the feeder and then flew off. The door was closed, so no calls were observed.

Similar Species Discussion

I initially thought that it could be a female Northern Cardinal, but ruled that out due to the rounded, yellow beak.

Resources Used

After looking through likely birds found in Denver, I began looking online at birds that resemble a Cardinal, where I was able to identify the bird as a Pyrrhuloxia. I sent the photos I had taken to my sister, Chase McCabe, who is an avid birder based in Connecticut. She agreed with my identification and forwarded the photos to Aidan Kiley, eBird reviewer for Connecticut, to verify that it was possible for Pyrrhuloxia to be in Colorado and that it was extremely rare.

Previous Experience

Having grown up in Connecticut, I am familiar with Northern Cardinals. I had not previously seen a Pyrrhuloxia.
