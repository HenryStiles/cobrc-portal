Description of the Bird

I was notified on June 8th, 2011 that a Pyrrhuloxia was seen by Cole and Sue Foster, at their feeders where they live 5 miles south of Alamosa. I immediately went to the residence and looked unsuccessfully for 45 minutes. Later that day, Cole called and said the bird was still there. So I immediately went over and found the bird at their feeder area. I knew immediately that it was a Pyrrhuloxia by the tall crest feathers, pale cone-shaped bill and grayish color. The bird was about 8 inches long. It was larger than nearby House Sparrows and smaller than nearby Mourning Dove. It was grayish overall with wings being slightly more brownish gray than the gray body. The bird had a tall crest with a slight touch of red on the tip. Face was gray, eyes dark. Back, breast, tail and undertail gray. Wings had a slight show of red on toward the primaries. Bill was large cone-shaped, and pale colored with a distinct downturn of the mouth portion of the mandible. Feet and legs were dark gray. I went back there on June 10th, 2011 and got additional photos with a camera and Swift spotting scope. They are included in this report.
Behaviors: Bird was seen flying down from trees, feeding on a mix of henscratch and bird seed on the ground. I did not hear any songs or calls. It was a flighty bird and would flush when all the other birds around the area would flush. There were at least 2 cats observed near the feeders at various times. After being in cover for a while, the bird would then return to the ground and feed.
Call: None
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Cardinal: Has much more red on male and female. Cardinal lacks the pale cone shaped bill. Cardinal has a red or dark colored bill and lacks the sharp curvature downward of the mouth portion of the bill.
