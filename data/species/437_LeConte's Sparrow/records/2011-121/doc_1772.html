Description of the Bird

The ratty looking bird had a brightly colored face pattern with orangy tones above and below the dark eye and a black tick behind a faint gray auricular patch. It had an orangy nape. The back with freshly molted adult feathers had dark centered feathers with light edgings creating a dark and white striped look. The tail was orangy, short and spiky. The breast was light in coloration with strong dark flank streaks. The bill was light colored and small for an Ammodramus sparrow. It was IDed to Ammodramus based on its relatively flat crown, but mostly its short spiky tail and secretive nature in a wet sedge meadow.
Behaviors: The bird flushed out from in front of me while I was walking in a wet sedge meadow where it perched less than 1 foot above the sedges on a dead shrub. It moved later when I tried to approach too closely to the base of a Russian olive where it stayed partially hidden for a minute until it flew back into the sedge meadow where it dove in and disppeared.
Call: none detected
Plumage: juvenile molting to adult

Similar Species Discussion

Henslow's Sparrow - Has a simlar crown colorationg, but slightly different pattern on its back (less white) and it has a very large bill unlike the small bill of LCSP. It also has a malar and moustachial streak that is lacking in LCSP and this bird.

Grasshopper Sparrow. Lacks the orangy nape and doesn't have the complex back pattern (less white) of this LCSP and it lacks the orangy nape.

Nelson's Sparrow - has somewhat similar face pattern but has a more pronounced auricular patch that is darker and not light gray like LCSP. Its nape is gray and not orangy.

Baird's Sparrow - has duller less bright back pattern, different face pattern with moustachial and malar streaks, and different ventral streaking with streaks confined to a necklace on upper breat, no flank streaks like the LCSP.
