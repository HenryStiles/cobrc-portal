Description of the Bird

An Ammodramus type sparrow flushed from my feet while walking in a wet sedge meadow at Chico Basin Ranch . The buffy orange face pattern immeadiately got my attention and after being flushed into a Russian Olive tree was able to note a pale whitish crown median stripe , blackish lateral crown stripe , a wide orangy supercilium , a grayish auriculars , grayish nape finely streaked reddish brown , upper breast buffy with fine streaks and a short spiky tail.The long blackish flank streaks indicated that this was an adult LCSP.
Behaviors: When flushed from its initial position in the sedge meadow , flew a few yards low over grasses and disappeared onto the ground where I got a brief glimpse of the upper part . The Aiken Audubon group that I was leading subsequently surrounded the bird and it was flushed to a Russian Olive where it stayed for several minutes permitting photographing and close observation.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Only other ammodramus sparrow with extensive orange face , Nelson's , has a dark gray crown median stripe , unstreaked gray nape , sharp demarkation between buffy breast and white belly , whitish braces and more diffuse flank streaking.
