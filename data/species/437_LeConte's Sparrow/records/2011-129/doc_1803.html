Description of the Bird

small sparrow with a yellow face when flushed and short spiky tail. Perched, it had a broad yellow/orange eye stripe, a contrasty gray auricular with orangish/yellow wash below. Face with a thin dark line runing behind the eye ending in a dark triangle. Crown with a thin white central crown stipe surrounded by two broader blackish ones. Nape light grayish with some very thin parallel browns stipes in it. Back richly patterned with alternating wide rows of browns and whites, also noticeable in flight. Breast with an orange wash with thin brownish steaks continuing to flanks. Over all beautiful bird.
Behaviors: Very skulky with very short low flight just over the grassland habitat. On the ground was curiouis but always kept some vegetation between it and the observers.
Call: call distinctive from other sparrows in the area but I don't remember it well enough to describe
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Nelson's Sparrow is closest, especially the expected interior population members, but that species has an unstreaked nape and different, gray median stripe on its crown and different back pattern.

The most expected Ammodramous sparrow, Grasshopper, has a less bold face pattern, dull colored, and its back pattern, though brightly patterned, is less bold. It usually shows a prominent yellow lore that is the brightest part of its face. It breast and flanks are unstreaked.

Baird's Sparrow has a face pattern with bold mustachial and malar streaks and the two dark spots behind the auricular and different coloration on the crown.
