Description of the Bird

This was a small sparrow that flew out from a wet sedge meadow, seen on two different days from the same area. When it first flew I noticed a short spiky tail, light colorful back with whitis verticle stripes. It landed and perced momentarily about 1.5 feet above the veg. End of 1st sighting.

A few days later the bird was flushed into a dead Russian olive surrounded by marsh sedges. The face pattern was distinctive dull orange surrounding a light gray auricular patch. Behind the auricular was a darker anchor-shaped (widest to the rear) streak. The crown was alternating orangish and dark streaks. The breast was light warm orangish with darker flank streaks. The tail was light gray, short and with spiky points. The back was quite colorful and it was here and the auriculars that looked ratty with both juvenile and adult feathers (from photos). The back had alternating rows of dark and whitish very broad stripes, the dark feathers with some white egdes. the legs were pinkish. The bill was substantial and pinkish gray.
Behaviors: Skulker, with short very low flights. When perched remained stationary until four of us moved slightly closer and then it moved about slowly in the same tree and eventually disappeared into taller bullrushes nearby.
Call: none heard
Plumage: suspended pre-formative

Similar Species Discussion

Nelson's Sparrow - lacks the strongly patterned back of LCSP - also has well-defined breast band and solid crown stripe, plus overall not as colorful.

Baird's Sparrow and Henslow's Sparrow have different face pattern with the strong mustachial and malar streaks.

Grasshopper Sparrow. Similar back pattern except the nape but without the flank streaks and usually with a bright yellow lore lacking in LCSP.
