Description of the Bird

An Ammodramus sparrow flushed up from my feet in a wet sedge meadow and perched about 10 feet away briefly in a low bush before flying higher to a Russian Olive where it stayed for may be 30 seconds . Description is taken from photos . A flat headed sparrow with a faded orangy buff supercilium , gray auriculars , dark streaky lateral crown stripe , relatively stubby bill , broad buffy and dark back streaks , and short spiky tail. Was not able to get a frontal view on this bird .
Behaviors: On flushing flew very low over sedge to small bush . Perched briefly in Russian Olive , mainly back views obtained with head turned around. Very nervous behaviour.
Call: None heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The only other Ammo with buffy orange supercilium , Nelson's has an unstreaked gray nape,shorter bill and less extensive streaking on back.
