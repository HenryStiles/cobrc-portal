Description of the Bird

The crown of the bird was streaked gray and reddish. Around the sides and the back of the neck was an area of plain gray feathers. This feature reminded me of a similar pattern on the back of the neck of a Chipping Sparrow. Down the back of the Fox Sparrow were thick gray and red stripes. Its wings were a reddish color overall. The primaries were a darker red morphing into a black color. The lower back of the bird was gray. The rump was a vivid reddish/orangish. The entire top of the tail was the same shade of red with a few noticeable spots of reddish/black on the outer edges. The underside of the tail was a grayish. The undertail coverts were white with a few spots of darker color. The legs were orange. The belly was white with reddish/brownish streaks. These streaks sort of converged in the middle of the chest, creating a reddish patch. The throat was white. It had a reddish malar stripe. The malar area was white. The auriculars were mostly red with some gray mixed in. Around the eye was gray. The eye itself was black. The bill was yellow on the lower part. The very top of the bill was gray.
Behaviors: The entire time that I saw the bird, it was digging in the leaves just as a Dark-eyed Junco would, except that the Fox Sparrow was a lot more animated and violent in its digging. As I was watching it, I thought to myself that if the bird did not stop digging in the same spot it would soon dissapear into the ground entirely!
Call: No call or song heard.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Song Sparrow can be similar under certain viewing conditions. However, the nape of a Song Sparrow tends to have more patterning. The bill of a Song Sparrow is gray while the bill of the Fox Sparrow was almost all yellow. The Fox Sparrow's bill was also fatter than a Song Sparrow that was feeding a few feet away. The red subspecies of Fox Sparrow can also be close to other subspecies of Fox Sparrow. This Fox Sparrow's coloration was dominated by a red color. Two other features that made this Fox Sparrow a red subspecies, were the back and the head. This Fox Sparrow had a gray back that was streaked with red. Other subspecies of Fox Sparrow do not have any red on their backs and they do not have streaks on their backs. The crown of the Fox Sparrow that I saw had a red color on it. Other subspecies of Fox Sparrow do not have red on their heads.
