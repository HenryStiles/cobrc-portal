Description of the Bird

A large (even larger than adjacent Song Sparrows), boldly patterned sparrow. Thick triangular bill with yellowish base transitioning to gray tip, culmen a darker gray. Facial pattern distinct with thick reddish brown auricular border and crown contrasting with gray nape and supercillium. Thick dark gray lateral throat stripe contrasts with white malar and throat. Back with thick alternating gray and reddish brown stripes. Wings and tail primarily same reddish brown, white tips to median and greater coverts forming thin wing bars. Vent area white, flanks with bold, thick nearly black stripes. Breast has bold arrow-shaped nearly black spots on a white background. Legs and feet pinkish.
Behaviors: Coming to feeder, associating mainly with Song Sparrows but also a Harris's Sparrow, Juncos, Tree Sparrows, & House Finches.
Call: Mostly silent, occasional smacking call note heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Bulk, size, & striping most similar to Song Sparrow but abundant reddish tones, facial pattern, triangular breast spots eliminate that. Other Fox Sparrow subspecies eliminated by abundant reddish coloration & facial pattern.
