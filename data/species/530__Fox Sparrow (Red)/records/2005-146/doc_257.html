Description of the Bird

the bird was a large sparrow, about 12 percent larger than nearby juncoes, with a heavier build. The bill was very heavy but short, and yellow. I did not see the leg color.

The bird overall had a bright rufous look to it. The rufous was brightest on the uppertail coverts and tail. The back was rufous with bold gray streaks running down the length of the back. The wings were bright rufous with some darker markings on the edges, though the lenght of the observation did not permit me to determine where on the wing these were. The head was a mix of rufous and gray, and some white in the throat/malar area, with a rufous auricular patch with some gray in the center. The underparts were very boldy streaked with rufous spots that were triangularly shaped, coalescing into a large spot on the center of the chest. The spots faded out on the belly, which was mostly white.
Behaviors: the bird spent most of its time skulking in the brush, though at one point it did perch at mid-height in a tree in the open.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult eastern race

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of sparrow were ruled out by the striking bold rufous color overall, as well as the large size, heavy yellow bill, and bold rufous streaking below.

Other races of Fox Sparrow were also ruled out by the bold rufous color overall.
