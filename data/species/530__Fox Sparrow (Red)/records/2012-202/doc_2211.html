Description of the Bird

large sparrow with a large yellow bill, brightly-colored rufous and gray head (distinguishing this bird from slate-colored fox sparrow), rufous wings with faint white wing bars, rufous caret-shaped spots on otherwise white breast and flanks
Behaviors: This fox sparrow had been reported by John Breitsch on Nov. 21 (http://birding.aba.org/message.php?mesid=287486&MLID=CO01&MLNM=Colorado), and I found it (presumably the same bird) where it had been reported previously. The bird was in thick junipers, and I located it by hearing it moving. It mostly stayed out of sight and silent deep within the junipers. As a hiker walked by on the opposite side of the junipers, the sparrow briefly peered out on the side where I was standing, in time for me to get a brief look through binoculars and one photo. It then resumed hiding within the junipers.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was identified as a fox sparrow from the large size and the dense coverage of thick, caret-shaped spots on the breast and flanks. The rufous wings narrow down the subspecies to either slate-colored or red. The rufous and gray pattern on the head indicates red rather than slate-colored.
