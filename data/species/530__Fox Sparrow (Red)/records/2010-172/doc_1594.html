Description of the Bird

This was the largest bird in the flock that was feeding in some weeds by the edge of a narrow canal with water.

The bird was overall reddish in coloration. The tail was red, the back was gray with with some red streaks. The face had a large red auricular patch. The bill was yellowish. The breast was white with wide reddish streaks extending to the flanks. There was a wide reddish stickpin on the chest. The wings were reddish. The supercilium was wide and gray. The breast and flank streaks were orderly and looked like arrowhheads, largest on the flanks. The nape was gray.
Behaviors: Fed with mixed-species flock on the ground, flying up to perch when the group got close.
Call: distinctive call alerted us to the bird
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Red Song Sparrow is similar but it doesn't have the red auricular patch, no gray supercilium, fewer and thinner red breast and flank streaks, less bright red tail, bill not as yellow as this bird.
