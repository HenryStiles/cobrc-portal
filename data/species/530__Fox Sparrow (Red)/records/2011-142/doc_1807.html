Description of the Bird

Fox Sparrow (streaked sparrow, medium to long tailed, larger than Song Sparrow). Underparts white with moderately heavy rusty streaking across chest and down sides to flanks. No confluence of streaking on side/flanks. Vague rusty central chest spot.

Cheek patch moderately well-defined, with some rusty streaking inside auriculars. Bordered above and below by pale supercilium and "moustachial" stripe. Supercilium broad and gray. Moustachial narrow and buff-tinged. Crown dull brown-gray, contrasting modestly but distinctly with supercilium (more obvious in field than in photos). Below buff-tinged moustachial was flared, broad rusty submoustachial or whisker. Gray collar behind auricular patch.

Back streaked gray and rusty with streaks (of each color) roughly even in width. Wings rusty with fairly broad, white wing-bars. Rump rusty, tail rusty.
Behaviors: Called repeatedly from brushy thicket, not flying whilst White-crowned Sparrows bolted in every direction. Finally, when I entered brush, the bird flew to hillside where it was kind enough to "tee-up" for distant photos.
Call: A loud chup-chup, always given in couplets. Quality quite similar to that of Sooty Fox Sparrow, though Sooties typically do not call in couplets. The call did seem a bit more emphatic and of slightly different quality from Sooty Fox Sparrows. To me, SC Fox Sparrows are less prone to call in response to pishing than Sooty or Red Fox Sparrows, and their call is slightly weaker than that of Sooty.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Song Sparrow smaller, stronger facial pattern, not as rusty, etc. SC Fox Sparrow should not be as rusty nor have as prominent wingbars, and SC Fox Sparrows should have unstreaked backs. Sooty Fox lacks wingbars, has confluence of streaking on sides, and no streaking on back. Altivagans should not be as heavily streaked on back and wingbars narrower. Also, I believe, they are not as rusty throughout as this bird
