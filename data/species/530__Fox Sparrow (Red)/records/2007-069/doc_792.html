Description of the Bird

The bird was a large, stocky sparrow with conical bill and relatively short tail. The bill was dusky above and yellowish below. The upper portion of the head was gray, with diffuse rust toward the hind crown. The auriculars, portions of the throat, wings, and tail were rusty in coloration, with that color being brightest on the wings, tail, and upper coverts. The mantle had gray undertones with rusty streaking above. A narrow white wing bar was present along the terminal edge of the outer secondary coverts. The underparts were white with broad stripes, blotches, and arrow-shaped spots of rusty and chestnut on sides of throat, breast, sides, and flanks. The legs were flesh colored.
Behaviors: Skulked beneath dense shrub layer. Very difficult to photograph as it remained low to the ground within tangles. Observed scratching for food on ground in flight within the shrubs, and perched on twigs.
Call: None noted
Plumage: Possilby juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

All other rust colored birds that could occur in Colorado differ in overall size, bill shape, tail length, degree of rustiness overall and breast spotting pattern (Brown Thrasher, Wood Thrush, Veery, etc.). The extreme rufousness of the bird eliminated Slate-colored Fox Sparrow (P. i. schistacea), the Colorado breeding subspecies of Fox Sparrow, as a potential candidate.
