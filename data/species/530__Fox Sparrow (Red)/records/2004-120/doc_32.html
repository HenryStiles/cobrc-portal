Description of the Bird

Large sparrow, considerably larger than the Song Sparrow that was near it. The bird was generally a mixture of light gray, rust, and white coloration. The head was mostly light gray, rusty on the crown and auriculars. The back was also this mixture with alternating gray and rust streaking vertically down the back. In flight, as the bird flew away from me, I noted that the tail was solidly rust colored. I didn't note much on the wings, other than they were the same rust color as other parts of the body, as the bird was pretty much facing me, before turning and flying away. When the bird flew away, from that perspective, I got the impression that the rust coloration dominated the overall appearance of the bird's upperparts from head to tail. The bird's underparts were basally white, with rust-colored vertical streaking (really looked like a series of connecting splotches than truly streaks) that extended from the throat to the flanks. The belly was pure white. The thick bill was a dingy yellow.
Behaviors: Saw only briefly while foraging on the ground under a conifer, and in flight.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Adult
