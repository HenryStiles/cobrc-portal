Description of the Bird

This is submitted as a record of a unusual (for CO) race of Red Fox Sparrow. subspecies unknown

Face with rufous auriculars on grayish background
Tail and upper tail coverts rufous
Lower rump rufous, upper rump medium gray
Back - rufous on light gray background
Wings - dark gray with rufous and faint whitish wingbars

Legs - pinkish

Bill - deep based pinkish, not yellow
Behaviors: skulker flushed into open low perch then retreated into cattail marsh edge
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Red races of Song Sparrow eliminated, SOSP less round-headed and less stocky. SOSPs lack red rump. This was a big bird compared to SOSPs which were present in numbers.

From other subspecies (speices) of Fox Sparrow by only red fox sparrow has red auriculars, rusty on back of head and reddish streaks on back.

Additional Information

Comments: This is a very interesting subspecies of Red Fox Sparrow. I would be very interested if someone can come up with a name for it. Rising suggests that there are only two subspecies for Red Fox Sparrow and if true, then this is most likely the Yukon fox Sparrow (P.i. zaboria) "which tend to be grayer, especially in the head" which was what it showed in low light, often looking more gray than reddish on various body parts.
