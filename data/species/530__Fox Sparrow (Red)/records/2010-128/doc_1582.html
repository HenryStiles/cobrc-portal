Description of the Bird

A large & bulky sparrow dominated by by rufous tones & boldly marked underparts. The underparts have a white background marked by rows of heavy dark reddish-brown stripes along flanks & breast, thinning towards belly.The wings, tail, & back were dominated by rufous-red coloration, contrasting with a gray nape. The head had a rufous crown & gray supercillium. Gray was also around the eye and on the front part of the auricular, with a triangular rufous facial frame formed by an eye-line and the rear & lower auricular. A white malar contrasted with a rufous lateral throat-stripe, which in turn contrasted with a whitish throat. The bill was bright yellow.
Behaviors: The bird flew into a willow tree and skulked before flying off.
Call: I didn't note any vocalization.
Plumage: Red Fox Sparrow

Similar Species Discussion

Large size, bulky build, & bold patterning on underparts rule out most other sparrows. Extensive reddish coloration and yellow bill quite distinctive, red on face and crown rule out other Fox Sparrow subspecies.

Previous Experience

I've seen a few Red Fox Sparrows in Eastern States. I've seen other Fox Sparrow subspecies in the west, including many Slate-colored in Colorado.
