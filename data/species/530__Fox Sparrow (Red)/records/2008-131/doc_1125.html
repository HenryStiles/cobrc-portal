Description of the Bird

Red or Taiga Fox Sparrow

Rare in El Paso County

A large sparrow with an all red tail and reddish stripes on the breast with a large reddish "stick pin". The wigs and wing coverts were reddish except darker gray wing tips. Flanks with broad rusty streaks. Head was red and gray, the auricualar red, broad gray supercillium and rustly crown. The nape and upper back had a medium gray back with broad rusty verticle streaks. The bill was stout and yellow based on the lower and upper mandibles with dusky towards the tip and along the culmen. The legs were thick and pink.
Behaviors: Fed under a feeder in a surburban yard. Double-jumped, scratching in the stones to uncover provided food of millet, and sunflower seeds.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult?

Similar Species Discussion

Red Song Sparrow - much smaller bird, stick pin, and bill with different face markings, especially without the broad rusty auricular.

Adult female Gray Bunting - bill much smaller with more yellow. without big rusty stickpin

Rustic Bunting - all ages with blackish markings on face and without big rusty stickpin on breast

Pine Bunting - does't have rufous tail
