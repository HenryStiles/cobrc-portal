Description of the Bird

The bird was an obvious Passerella type sparrow, being slightly larger than the near by House finches. The bright rufous color of the tail and wings were what first caught my attention. The head was gray with a rufous crown and a trianglar shaped rufous auriculars, dark eye with a white eye ring, a yelowish bill with a black upper maxcilla.
The throat , breast and belly was white with heavy rufous blotches/streaks more concentrated in the throat and breast and fading in the belly, but continuing on to the flanks.The wings were rufous with a faint upper wing bar and a noticable lower wing bar. The tertials were tipped with white and the primaries were dark. The tail had bright rufous outer feathers with the inner feathers being dark. The was streaked rufous and gray. The legs and feet were a dull pinkish color.
Behaviors: The bird was observed feeding on the ground below the feeders.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Red race

Similar Species Discussion

The rufous head pattern and back streaking rule out all other sparrows (including slate-colored Fox sp.) except for the unlikely SW race of the Song sparrow which has a slightly different auricular pattern and a dark bill.
