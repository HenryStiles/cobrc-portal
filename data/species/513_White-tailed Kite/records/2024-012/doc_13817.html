Description of the Bird

First spotted by Mike Thompson on March 30, 2024.  Observed by us at same location on March 31, 2024.  Small white hawk.  White body with dark shoulders, and dark area around eye.  Grey back and primaries while perched.  Long tail.  Viewed bird in perched position for over 30 minutes.  Two Common Ravens mobbed the kite causing it to take flight and fly for a while.  Bird was smaller than the two Common Ravens.  Observed long white tail and dark wing tips.  Saw overall dihedral wing shape with ends of wings slightly more upturned forming an overall shallow "U" shaped dihedral.  Bird hovered multiple times dropping lower each time until landing again.

Similar Species Discussion

Considered American Kestrel.  American Kestrel was eliminated due to the bird being observed having predominantly white plumage with dark shoulders, and dark area around eye.  This coloration does not match plumage of the American Kestrel.  American Kestrel was also eliminated due to the size of the bird being observed, it was noticeably larger than nearby Red-winged Blackbirds, which are about an inch smaller than an American Kestrel.

Ring-billed Gull was considered and eliminated due to Ring-billed Gull lacking the black shoulder patch, lacking dark patch around eye.  Bill did not match that of RBGU, and tail on RBGU is smaller.  RBGU was further eliminated because the bird observed perched on dead branches.

Northern Harrier was also considered and eliminated because a Northern Harrier was observed at the same time in the same location.  The Harrier had darker coloration on back, tops of wings, and tail, creating the distrinctive white patch on the rump.
