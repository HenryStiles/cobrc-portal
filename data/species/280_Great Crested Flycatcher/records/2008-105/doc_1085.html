Description of the Bird

A Myiarchus flycatcher that was dark olive above and bright yellow below. The most disticntive feature was the dark gray face and the bright yellow underparts. The flight feathers were rufous-edged and the three Tertials were broadly whitish-edged. The inner web of the outer tail feathers was rufous as well.
Behaviors: Feeding lethargically from weedstalks and within a Russian Olive
Call: none noted
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Dusky-capped has no rufous in tail.
Ash-throated Flycatcher is much paler dorsally, on the face and throat and has just a hint of lemon yellow on the belly. This bird had bright yellow from the lower breast to the undertail area
