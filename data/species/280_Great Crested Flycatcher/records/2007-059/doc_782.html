Description of the Bird

This bird was a fairly large flycatcher almost the size of a Western Kingbird, but with a more slender body shape. Its head and back were grayish, with gray crown coming to a point. Throat was a slightly lighter shade of whitish gray. Its belly was bright yellow. Long tail was bright rufous, with color especially visible in flight. Each wing had two creamy colored wing bars with rufous in the primaries.

Bare parts: I did not see legs well but eye and beak were dark; beak was fairly robust somewhat shrike shaped but a bit thinner.
Behaviors: Bird sat atop a scrub oak fairly still with no noticeable behavior pattern to describe.
Call: none given
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ash-throated Flycatcher is smaller with browner crest and cream (not bright yellow) colored belly.

Brown-crested Flycatcher is also browner crested.

Dusky-capped Flycatcher is noticeably smaller than Kingbirds & Crested Flycatcher. It also has browner crest and face than Crested Flycatcher.
