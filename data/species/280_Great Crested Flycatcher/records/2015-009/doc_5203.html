Description of the Bird

An obvious large Myiarchus (noticeably larger than Western Wood-Pewee seen shortly before) with bill quite thick for relatively short length and with a little pale coloration at base (though not visible in accompanying pictures); dark gray hood; bright yellow belly; obvious white leading edges of all three tertials; little rufous in tail.  Bird perched for minutes at a time turning its head this way and that searching air space in its vicinity, then changed perches.  It did not flick either wings or tail.

Similar Species Discussion

Ash-throated and Nutting's flycatchers both easily ruled out by bird's large size and robustness, thick bill, dark hood including all of throat and chest, bright yellow belly, bright edges to tertials.  Dusky-capped Flycatcher also eliminated on size and on bill pattern and thickness.  Most importantly, Brown-crested Flycatcher ruled out by bright white edges to tertials, bill shape, and bright yellow belly.

Resources Used

None at time of sighting; referred to Sibley (2014) to refresh my memory of Dusky-capped Flycatcher appearance and to my pictures and brief field notes written about an hour after the sighting.

Previous Experience

I have seen many hundreds of both Great Crested and Ash-throated flycatchers, many tens of Dusky-capped Flycatchers (though none in quite a while), a smattering of Brown-crested Flycatchers in the US, Mexico, and Costa Rica, and just a few Nutting's Flycatchers two decades ago in Costa Rica.  I have also seen the very-unlikely-to-occur-in-Colorado Panama Flycatcher once.
