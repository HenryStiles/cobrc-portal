Habitat

A fairly thick area of cottonwood and Russian olive extending along a major irrigation supply ditch running through agricultural land and pasture.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10X42, Swarovski 10X42 binoculars
Distance:  20 feet
Light:  Bright sun overhead.

Description of the Bird

A little smaller and much more slender than a robin. A bright yellow belly and a solid gray throat and chest. Large, black thick bill. Grayish-green back. Two pale white wingbars, and a tail that showed considerable red when it flew.
Behaviors: We observed it perched with an insect in its mouth, and we watched it fly three different times when it was disturbed by our presence.
Call: Call heard by Nancy Bell: a clear, single rising syllable. Same as Great Crested Flycatcher calls heard many times a month ago while birding in South Carolina.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Because we were so impressed by the bright yellow belly and the dark gray throat and chest when we saw the bird perched, we eliminated the Ash-throated Flycatcher. Also, the large size of the bill was distinctive especially when it held the insect. The call was not a sharp, single note, as described for the Ash-throated in the field guides.

Resources Used

We used National Geographic and Sibley, when we reached home about an hour after our sighting.

Previous Experience

We bird South Carolina annualy, usually in the spring, and we were just there for a week in May. We had several sightings of Great Crested Flycatcher this year around Columbia and Myrtle Beach, including hearing its distinctive call enough times to remember it well.
