Description of the Bird

A large dark Myiarchus flycatcher with a bright yeloow lower breast, belly and undertail. This bird had a dark olive back and rufous-edged flight feathers. The head was dark gray especially the cheek area. The throat was gray.
Behaviors: came in to investigate my screech-owl call. It mostly sat on Russian Olive branches bobbing its head while looking for the owl (which was really me).
Call: none noted
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Dusky-capped Flycatcher has no rufous in the tail. The rufous inner web of the tail feathers is visible in the photo. Ash-throated Flycatcher is a much paler bird. ATFL has much less yellow below and what is there, on an ATFL, is much paler. ATFL throat is paler too.
