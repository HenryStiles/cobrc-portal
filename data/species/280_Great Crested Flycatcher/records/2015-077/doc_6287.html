Description of the Bird

Larger flycatcher with relatively bright yellow belly up onto upper chest; light gray throat and upper chest; rufous tail with rufous extending to tip of tail; relatively large bill; bright edges to tertial feathers.

In the trees along the Poudre River, the bird often flew short distances, perching briefly to look for insects/prey.  The bird was silent the whole time of my observation.

Similar Species Discussion

I considered both Ash-throated and Brown-crested Flycatchers.  I eliminated Ash-throated because the yellow seemed too bright and extended too far up the chest, the bill seemed too big, the rufous extended to the tip of the tail, and the edges of the tertial feathers seemed too contrasting.  I eliminated Brown-crested because the bill did not seem large enough, and the yellow extended too far up the chest.

Previous Experience

I have seen numerous Great Crested Flycatchers (over several years) in Florida and Missouri.  I had never seen one in Colorado.  I have seen Ash-throated Flycatcher a couple of times.  I reported a Brown-crested Flycatcher to CBRC last fall, and this sighting (as a comparison) actually strengthens for my that ID, seeing the comparison, especially of the bills of the birds.
