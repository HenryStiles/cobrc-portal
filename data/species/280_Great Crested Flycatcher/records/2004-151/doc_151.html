Description of the Bird

This was a large myiarchus flycatcher with dark olive brown on the back and crown. The face and sides of the neck were similar in color to the crown but looked a bit more olive-gray. The bird had a dark gray throat and upper breast. Below the gray chest the sides and belly were fairly bright yellow as were the undertail coverts. There was an obvious contrast between the dark gray chest and the yellow belly. Heavy dark upper mandible, lower mandible also dark but with a pale base. Wings were darkish brown with two pale wing bars and pale edged inner tertials. The lower part of the primaries were rufous. Brownish-olive tail with rufous inner webs of the outer tail feathers. This was noticeable to me when the bird was facing me. When I was close to the bird it did raise its crown-feathers to form an obvious crest.
Behaviors: This bird was very skittish and did not stay in one location for any length
of time. The first time I saw it, it flew between two cottonwoods. I then
found it sitting on an exposed lower branch of a cottonwood in a dense
thicket. It did "flycatch" one time at this location but then disappeared.
It took me over an hour to find it again and then it re-appeared on the same
exposed branch where I had seen it previously. Again, it sat for enough time
that I had excellent close views. It then flew away and a short time later,
appeared again at the same location. Although each of the three observations
was brief, the total time I saw it was probably about 15 minutes. After it
disappeared the third time and although I believe it stayed in the same
thicket, I was never able to find it again.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ash-throated flycatcher: Has lighter brown back and crown, a paler throat
and breast and is without the contrast between breast and belly of the Great
Crested Flycatcher. Has a paler yellow belly. Ash-throated has a dark
slighter bill with no pale area to the base. Brown-crested Flycatcher: Great
Crested has a darker gray throat and breast. Brown-crested Flycatcher's face
shows more contrast with dark crown. Also has grayer upperparts with more
contrast between crown and back. The lower mandible is usually all black not
showing the pale area at the base as does the bill of the Great Crested
Flycatcher. Dusky-capped Flycatcher: Smaller size than Great Crested.
Dusky-capped usually shows marked contrast between dark-brown or black crown
and olive-brown back and has less contrast between chest and belly than is
shown in Great Crested. Great Crested Flycatcher also has a longer bill.
