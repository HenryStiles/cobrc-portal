Habitat

Tops of isolated trees with no understory, in a cemetary.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swift Audubon 8.5x44 binoculars. I also saw the bird through other viewers' spotting scopes.
Distance:  Approximately 75 to 100 feet.
Light:  Excellent early morning light. The bird was observed under a variety of light angles, many of which were very good.

Description of the Bird

The bird was generally light below and blue-gray above. It had a blue-gray head with a black area around the eye, and white wing bars. The throat and belly were yellow, with a diffuse orange patch in the breast area.
Behaviors: The bird was high in the treetops the entire time we were observing it. It flew from tree to tree several times, always staying near the tops of the trees.
Call: A steady flat buzzing. My hearing is not the best, and others no doubt heard it much better.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The bird could be confused with a Northern Parula, but lacked the Northern's white eye-arcs and dark breastband.

Resources Used

Sibley and National Geograpic guides were used after the sighting.

Previous Experience

I have seen Tropical Parula in the Lower Rio Grande Valley of Texas. I have seen many Northern Parulas in a wide variety of locations.
