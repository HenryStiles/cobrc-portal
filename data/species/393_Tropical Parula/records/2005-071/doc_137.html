Description of the Bird

The bird was small, rather plump with a short tail and long tapered sharp bright yellow/orange bill. This characters were emphasized by direct comparison while in flight to Black-capped Chickadees flying in the same specific area. The underside showed white undertail with vague windows. The undertail coverts, vent, and belly were all white. The lower chest exhibited a splotchy yellow/orange on white, gradually moving to a solid yellow/orange on the upper breast and throat. There was no evidence of a dark breastband as in N. Parula. There was a central glowing orange "medallion" on the breast. The hood and mask were a slate blue/gray solid with no evidence of eye cresents or loral markings. The loral area, in fact, was very dark/black.
The upperside was a general dark slate blue/gray with a mantle of yellow/green/olive. The wings exhibited two wing bars, one obvious, the other not as clear.
Behaviors: The individual was alternately perching in one location for 5 to 10 minutes and then moving about feeding for 5 to 10 minutes, all the while singing. It visited 5 or 6 species of trees, both deciduous and coniferous, demonstrating a preference for the upper third of each tree. The individual spent the majority of the 2-plus hours of observation in locust trees. The bird, when moving and feeding, was very active, moving about in mainly dense foliage. It both crept along smaller branches and hopped from twig to twig, inspecting leaves with sometimes very acrobatic, extended positions, providing good views of the back from 30 feet below. The flights between trees were generally short (30 yds or less). The individual aggressively chased a house finch.
Call: Individual was consistently singing at regular intervals of approximately 20 sec. to 1 min. for vast majority of over 2 hours of observation time. The song was the familiar main song consisting of a buzzy extended section punctuated with an emphasized exclamation point at the end.
Plumage: full Adult male

Similar Species Discussion

There was no evidence of a dark breastband as in N. Parula. Also no evidence of eye crescents, or any loral, supraloral, or supercilliary white markings.
