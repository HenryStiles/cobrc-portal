Description of the Bird

A very small, short-tailed warbler, smaller than Red-breasted Nuthatch seen once with it, usually staying high in trees, with a blue head, black between the dark eyes and the bill and a yellow throat. There was no trace of eye arcs. The bill was bicolored, with a black maxilla and a yellow mandible.The back was greenish. I never saw the rump well enough to determine its color, but it was not pale.

The chest had an ill-defined patch of orangish that was only detectable at certain angles; otherwise, it was the same bright yellow of the throat, as was the upper belly. The flanks were grayish and the undertail coverts/vent area was white. The wings were bluish-black with bright white wingbars. The tail was dark with distinct white spots on the inner webs of the outermost pair of rectrices; from underneath, these white patches accounted for much of the color on the folded tail.
Behaviors: The bird tended to stay high in the trees, seeming to prefer deciduous trees, though I did see it foraging in a Blue Spruce at one point. It would be silent for stretches, interrupted by bouts of singing (10-20 songs).
Call: Sang often; a buzzy song rising in pitch virtually throughout
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Northern Parula is really the only strong candidate, but that species can be ruled out by the lack of white eye arcs, the lack of a darker chest/throat band, the lack of blackish lateral throat stripes, and the yellow sides.
