Light:  The first time I saw the bird the sun was to my back (south) and the bird was to the north. I saw the bird on both days in every direction.

Description of the Bird

Had the shape of the other warblers, with a short, sharp bill. A yellow breast and throat with a light line of orange on the breast. Pale lower mandible and a dark upper mandible. A dark mask on the face (no white eye ring). Two white wing bars, a blue body and head. A dark under side of the tail. White under tail covert and belly.
Behaviors: I the tops of trees fluttering around for a while and then sitting in a single spot that has a lot of foliage. Sometimes it would fly from tree to tree before stopping to rest.
Call: a buzzy rising trill ending in a buzzy downslur
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

North Parula - Has white eye-arcs, a breastband that is black and rufous, and occasionally has rufous flanks. The last note of the call isn't as buzzy and it rises, not falls, in pitch.
