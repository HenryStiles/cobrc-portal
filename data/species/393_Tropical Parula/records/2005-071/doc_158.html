Habitat

Very old cemetery with well-established mature trees; the warbler preferred the locust trees in one particular location

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss 7x42 binocular
Distance:  about 30 feet at closest distance
Light:  Partly cloudy. The bird sometimes perched in the open so that it was "lit up" quite nicely.

Description of the Bird

A small passerine similar in size and shape of Northern Parula. Underparts were mostly lemon yellow from chin to upper belly, blending into white on the belly with white vent region, and with a flame orange chest; upper mandible dark, lower mandible flesh colored; upperparts were basally dark bluish-gray; head also had a solid black eye mask extending from back of eye forward to bill base and down to the yellow throat; the mantle was a solid olive green patch; and the wings had two white wing bars; I did not note any markings or spots on the tail; the eye was dark.
Behaviors: Mostly sang from tree tops, occasionally foraging within the tree canopy.
Call: very much like Northern Parula, except without the ending sharp note
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Northern Parula (NOPA) is very similar; however, this bird lacked the white eye ring and bluish breast band typical of NOPA. Also, this bird showed yellow on the underparts extending to the lower belly and a black eye mask, neither characteristics of NOPA.

Resources Used

I looked at references before seeing the bird: National Geographic Birds of North America (1999), and Dunn and Garrett's (1997) Peterson Field Guide to Warblers.

Previous Experience

I have seen Tropical Parula in Mexico about a dozen times and Northern Parula in fair numbers annually.
