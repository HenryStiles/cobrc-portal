Description of the Bird

This bird was sitting with a few other Cackling Geese (at least a couple of which were typical Richardson's Cacklers) at the edge of an enormous Snow Goose flock. The bird stood out due to its dark bronzy chest. It was a bit larger than the nearby nominate Cackling Geese. Its bill was relatively long and slender. The forehead was steeper than that of taverneri Cackling Geese, but not as abrupt as typical of nominate Cacklers. The forehead curved smoothly into a relatively flat crown. The rear crown was rounded. This head shape was held throughout our observation, with the bird sometimes at rest, sometime alert; the shape was typical in my experience for Aleutian Cackling Goose (B.h. leucopareia). The cheek patch was narrow and unindented. The bird had a moderate black gular stripe. A hint of white feathering could be seen at base of black neck when neck stretched out. Otherwise, the bird had the share characters of white-cheeked geese, see photos.

Similar Species Discussion

Lesser Canada Goose. Larger body. Longer and narrower bill. Paler chested. 
Dusky Canada Goose. Far larger body (nearly size of Big Uglies), flatter forehead.
Taverner's Cackling Goose. Can be dark chested, but typically dark gray not bronzy. Bill proportionately shorter and stouter. Less often has full gular stripe. Forehead not as steep, crown more rounded.
Richardson's Cackling Goose. Paler breast (some can be relatively dark, but rarely if ever so richly colored). Steeper forehead, flatter crown, peaked towards rear. This bird was larger than surrounding Richardson's. Bill shape typical for Aleut, rarely approximated in Richardson's. 
minima Cackling Goose. Smaller. Smaller billed. Rounder head. Glossier breast.

ID from Richardson's and Taverner's truly rests on multiple marks. The black gular stripe is present on 99%+ of Aleuts, but is rare in Richardson's. The head shape can vary with the bird's position, but this bird had a typical Aleut head shape over a long period of observation, and that head shape is not consistent with the ID of either Richardson's or Taverner's. The bill shape was typical of Aleut, not compatible with Tav, and would be rarely seen in Richy, if ever. Also, the breast color is rare in Richy or Tav (I am not sure if I've ever seen a Richy with this bronzy a chest).
