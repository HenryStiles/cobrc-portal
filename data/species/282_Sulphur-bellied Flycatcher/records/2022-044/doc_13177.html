Description of the Bird

The bird briefly flew to the top of the thick grove of Russian Olives from the grasslands.  It was not actively hunting or displaying any typical flycatcher behavior, nor did it give any calls.  The bird was originally found by Colin Woolley and participants of the Bird Conservancy of the Rockies field trip.  No other birds were seen associating with the flycatcher.

Similar Species Discussion

The closest I can think of would be a female red-winged blackbird, but this bird was larger, red tail, yellow belly, thick long bill, striped head, and streaking down flanks. We've seen this bird in Arizona many times and we recognized it immediately.

Resources Used

We were alerted to the presence of the bird, so we were on the lookout.  Photo evidence supports the id.

Previous Experience

 We have been to Arizona multiple times over the years and have become familiar with this species, most recently in Madera Canyon in June 2022.
