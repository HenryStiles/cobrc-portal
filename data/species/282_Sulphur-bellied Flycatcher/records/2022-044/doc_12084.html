Description of the Bird

I discovered the bird while leading a trip on behalf of Bird Conservancy of the Rockies - 15 individuals.

The bird was perched in the top of a Russian Olive in close proximity to a Western Tanager. Once I identified the bird, I immediately photographed it, though unfortunately using only a 70mm lens. A cropped version of this photo showing both the Western Tanager and the Sulphur-bellied Flycatcher is included.  Also included is a photo of the bird perched in the meadow taken by a tour participant.

 

Description:

Robin-sized bird with two bold white stripes on the head above and below the eye, streaked breast with pale yellow belly, dark brownish gray back with white highlights on the wings, bright rufous tail. The bird did not vocalize. After flushing from the Russian Olive, it flew low and fast south into the adjacent meadow where it perched in a low, thorny, leafless bush in the company of several Sage Thrashers before flying out of sight heading roughly southeast.

Similar Species Discussion

I've observed SBFL in Mexico and Arizona where they are often quite noisy and boistrous. This bird appeared to be hunkered down on a cold, misty morning and did not vocalize. Piratic Flycatcher can be ruled out because it lacks the bright rufous tail and the streaking on the breast is less distinct. Variegated Flycatcher is somewhat similar in appearance but would have a longer tail that is not as bright, blurry rather than fine streaks on the breast and a shorter bill. Finally, Streaked Flycatcher would have a longer, stronger bill and bold black mask on the face with little to no yellow in the breast, shorter primary extension and somewhat duller rufous tail.
