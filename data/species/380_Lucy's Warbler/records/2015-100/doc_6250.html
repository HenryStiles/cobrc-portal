Description of the Bird

I noticed a very small, dull gray bird flying away from me and followed it.  It was slightly darker gray on the upper parts and almost white on the underparts and my first impression was that it might be a Virginia's Warbler.  However, upon further study, the bird showed no yellow under the rump or on the throat. I also noticed a small patch of maroon/red on the top of the head.  The eye ring was faint and not bold as I would expect with Virginia's Warbler.  The bill was dark and very sharp looking.  The bird moved to a taller aspen tree at which time I passed my binoculars to my wife so she could view the bird. She quickly stated that she thought it was a Lucy's Warbler because she had seen the maroon/red patch on the rump.  I took back the binos and viewed the bird for a bit longer seeing the edge of the red rump patch before the bird took off into a dense grove of trees where we were unable to refind it. 

Similar Species Discussion

Virginia's Warbler was eliminated by lack of eye ring and lack of yellow patches on the throat and under the rump.

Gray Vireo was eliminated because the bill was too pointed and the overall color too light.

Bushtit was eliminated because the tail was not long enough in relation to the body and overall color was too light.

Blue-gray Gnatcatcher was eliminated because the tail was not long enough and not black on the upper side with white outers and underside.
