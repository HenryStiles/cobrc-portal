Habitat

All sightings were in riparian habitat along the Animas River within about 1/4 mile of each other. Bird was in hawthorn on 4/24/15, and in Gambel's Oak on 5/21/15.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski 8.5 X 42 binoculars
Distance:  20 or 25 feet
Light:  Lighting was good

Description of the Bird

    The warbler was very small - much smaller than the Audubon's Warblers that were in the same shrubs.  The bird was mostly gray.  The upperparts, crown and wings were gray.   The gray wings were without wing bars.  The upperside of the tail was gray, and I never managed to see the rump patch of this bird.  The bill of the bird was dark and pointed.  The face of the bird was a very pale gray.  Consequently, a white eye-ring was evident, but not very conspicuous.   The bird's dark eye and dark bill appeared very prominent due to the light-colored face.  The gray crown of the bird was darker that the face of the bird.  The crown patch of the bird was always concealed when I viewed the crown.  The throat, chest, belly, sides, and undertail coverts were fairly white in color.  The throat and underparts of the bird were unmarked and without any streaks.       

     This bird was only seen by me on the 2 dates listed, even though I walked this area a number of times during the 4 weeks in between sightings.  On 4/24/15, I didn't hear any vocalizations from the bird.  On 5/21/15, the bird was singing. (Listing the bird as a male was based on the fact that I heard his song.)  I am familiar with the song.  It seems somewhat like a Yellow Warbler song to me, but much faster.  (While the Lucy's Warbler sang, a couple of nearby Orange-crowned Warblers were singing louder and with a very similar cadence.)  The Lucy's Warbler ended its rapid song with two or three distinct notes with more separation between those last notes.    The Lucy's Warbler was very difficult to keep in view due to it's small size and pale color.  There were times when a Ruby-crowned Kinglet in the vicinity drew my attention because the Lucy's Warbler was also very small in size.                    

Similar Species Discussion

A Virginia's Warbler would also be mostly gray.  On a Virginia's Warbler, the white eye ring is more conspicuous.  The Virginia's Warbler has very conspicuous yellow undertail coverts.  On the Lucy's Warbler, the undertail coverts are white (as I noted on this bird).      

Resources Used

I didn't really require any references for identification.  It's a familiar bird to me. 

Previous Experience

I've seen the bird often, mostly in New Mexico and Arizona.  I've also seen the Lucy's Warblers quite a few times in Yellow Jacket Canyon in Montezuma County, Colorado.
