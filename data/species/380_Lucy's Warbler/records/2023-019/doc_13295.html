Description of the Bird

Painfully, the bird was originally reported by Steve Barlow on 5/3/23, while Curt and I were birding SE CO, but we made an evening visit to Long Lake once we had returned.  About 50 mins in, we found a Black-throated Gray Warbler (which Steve also had on 5/3) and I said to Curt...if that thing is still here then the Lucy's might be too!.  Just as soon as I said that, I spotted a smaller, very pale gray warbler in the buckthorn(?) thicket on the side of the trail.  I am familiar with the species and knew when I saw zero yellows or greens on it that it was the Lucy's.  It was at first forraging on the top/edges of the low bramble with a pair of gnatcatchers and then sometimes into medium-sized "emergent" willows nearby.  We were able to watch it as it fed, sometimes hover-gleaning small insects.  It was in one of these hovers that I was able to get a photo showing the deep-rosy band on the rump.  No reddish crown patch was noticed in our observation nor were any vocalizations.  In the end, I was able to notify my friend Rob Raker who was nearby and he saw and photographed the bird as well.  

Similar Species Discussion

The white below and gray above ruled out all other wood-warblers immediately.  Tennessee could be a contender but our bird had a gray back.  I suppose the gnatcatchers that it was with could be similar and it was a nice comparison....shorter tail and no white outer-tail feathers.  

Resources Used

 The fact that somebody had reported a Lucy's at this spot recently on eBird and my own familiarilty with this species outside of the Front Range. 

Previous Experience

I've got 12 checklists in eBird for Lucy's in  AZ, CA and NV and have also seen them down in the known area of Yellowjacket Canyon, CO (didn't have an eBird list).
