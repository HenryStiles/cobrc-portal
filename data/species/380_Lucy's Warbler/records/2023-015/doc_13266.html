Description of the Bird

Gnatcatcher-sized drab warbler, gray above, white below, rusty cap and rump.

While watching a group of yellow-rumped warblers, one bird stood out as being smaller and moving more. The bird was a drab gray warbler with a rusty cap, making me assume orange-crowned warbler at first but that didn't feel right. I considered Lucy's warbler and after reading about the differences went back to get a better look. I found the bird again and by this time had Joan Grant on the bird as well. It finally turned and we could see the rusty rump. Later, Kara Laszyk and Shawn McCormick were able to get photos. Kara's checklist: https://ebird.org/checklist/S133889854

Similar Species Discussion

Orange-crowned Warbler : cap rusty and large compared to the typically unseen orange cap of OCWA. While OCWAs are relatively drab for a warbler, the bird in question had no yellow.

Blue-gray Gnatcatcher : size, movement, and basic coloration very similar, but rusty camp and rump eliminates BGGN.
