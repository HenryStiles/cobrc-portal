Description of the Bird

A small, mostly plain gray warbler, whitish belly, faint eye ring, with a rufous rump and crown patch. It appeared to be an adult male in breeding plumage. The bird was very active, following a group of Yellow-rumped Warblers. No vocalizations were heard. They were moving through the trees near the creek next to the regional trail. Those present observed the bird high in the trees as well as on or near the ground on both sides of the trail. Jessica Miller first reported seening the bird in the area earlier that day. When I arrived at about 2:50PM, Jessica Miller, Joan Grant, and Shawn McCormick were looking for it. Jessica eventually had to leave, but Joan, Shawn, and myself continued to look for the bird. After about an hour we spotted it with the little flock of about 8 Yellow-rumped Warblers on the west side of the trail moving through the tree tops. We lost track of the flock for a brief period, then saw the bird again foraging among the grasses/shrubs on the west side of the trail. It flew to the east side of the trail near the narrow portion of "Rice's Pond" where it perched briefly on a low hanging branch then flew off. That brief moment of perching is when I was able to capture two photos.

Similar Species Discussion

The only other bird that I am aware of that is similar to a Lucy's Warbler is the Virginia's Warbler. However, this bird completely lacked any yellow. The rufous crown and rump patch were very distinct.

Previous Experience

This is the first time that I have observed this species in the wild. I had only seen photographs up to that point.
