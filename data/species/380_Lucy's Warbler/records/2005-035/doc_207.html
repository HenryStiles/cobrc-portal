Description of the Bird

Bird was small verivora warbler with very drab gray coloring. Bill was dark, thin and short to medium in length for size of bird. head and upperparts were medium gray. Dark eye was surrounded by indistinct, off-white eyering on otherwise plain face. There were no wing bars. Underparts were lighter and somewhat buffy. Undertail coverts were white. Tail was short and dark. Overall appearance of bird was similar to a kinglet, but no wing flicking. Bird stayed fairly high in cottonwoods and was very active. As a result I did not observe the rump or crown. I also saw the female which was quite similar, but slightly less colorful.
Behaviors: Birds were very actively feeding and flying around an area about 300 to 500 feet along the creek. They generally stayed at the mid-height or higher in the cottonwoods with only short observation periods. I examined the 2004 nest site, but birds were apparently not using it this year. They seemed to be using an area slightly farther downstream.
Call: Both heard. Song was fairly loud, sweet, rapid, and fairly long, but pitch did not change much. Flight call was a soft tset.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Did not have yellow of Virginia's and song very wrong. Too small for Colima and way out of range. Some very drab Yellow Warblers could be similar, but bill too sharp, body shape more kinglet like, undertail coverts white, not yellowish, and no tail spots.
