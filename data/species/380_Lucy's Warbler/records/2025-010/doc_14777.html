Description of the Bird

Small pale gray warbler with subtle yellow wash to the breast, white eyering, rusty patch on crown and rusty patch on the rump (the rump patch usually being hidden under the wings). Heard it sing once, otherwise silent. Foraging rather frenetically in poplars, willows and Russian-olive. Prey suspected as being midges but might have been getting other smaller insects, especially those attracted to poplar catkins.
