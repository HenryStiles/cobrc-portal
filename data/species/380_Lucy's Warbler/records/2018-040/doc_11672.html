Description of the Bird

Dull gray warbler with broad poorly defined white eyering. Wings dull gray without wingbars. Crown with small rufous-brown oval. Belly dull white. Bobbed tail once or twice. Rump rufous but often not visible but see submitted image.

Similar Species Discussion

Virginia's Warbler is larger with eyering crisply defined and faint to more prominent yellowish wash to breast. 
Orange-crowned Warbler always with greenish wash.
Tennessee Warbler with greenish back and whitish eyestripe. 
Brewster's Warbler has yellowish wingbars of varying width.
female CSWA has wingbars and greenish wash on dorsal parts

Resources Used

Already identified by finder, David Tonnessen who texted me he was looking at a Lucy's Warbler, which he was.  He pointed it out when it was about 20 feet above ground in a mostly small dead tree on the first day across from the main stream bed south of Sinton Pond.

Previous Experience

I used to live in SE Arizona where I saw a few.  Saw one in Yellowjacket Canyon twice in SW CO and the one found by Brandon Percival at Chico Basin Ranch at the El Paso County banding station there.
