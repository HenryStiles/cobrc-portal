Description of the Bird

Very small, mostly gray warbler.  Needlelike bill.  Short tail.  Pale eyering, whiter than overall gray color.  Basically pale gray all over with upper parts (head, back and wings) being slightly darker gray than underparts.  No wingbars of note.  Maroon undertail coverts visible when bird in flight, especially when landing.  Hint of maroon on crown.  Eyes, bill and legs dark gray to blackish.  Lores white with a hint of a whitish eyeline behind eye.

This bird spent almost all of its time in hackberries, American elms and plains cottonwoods, with the overwhelming % of its time in hackberry.  Since this tree is known for its gall-making psyllid activity at this time of year, presumably adult psyllids and maybe newly laid eggs on buds/freshly-emerged leaves were targets for this insectivore.  I heard it sing more (which is why I guess it was a male) when in tall cottonwoods than while in hackberry.

Similar Species Discussion

Virginia's Warbler was the first consideration.  This species always has yellow undertail coverts.  The Lucy's Warbler had white undertail area.  Reddish on crown was OK for either, but if a male Virginia's, it would have had yellowish patch on breast.  Virginia's is also a bit longer-tailed than this bird was.

Nashville Warbler, which is similar structure, always has considerable yellow on the underparts, which this bird did not have. 

Colima Warbler is much bigger, and has yellow undertail.

Immature Yellow Warbler on the gray end of the species spectrum would have yellowish undertail, longer tail and longer bill.

Tennessee Warblers would show at least a strong hint of green in the back, and would not have maroon undertail and crown.

Ruby-crowned Kinglet is similar size, but has white and black wingbars, broken eyering, holds bill slightly upturned (this bird's bill was held straight out, not at an angle to the face), vocalizations completely different, crown coloration bright red in kinglet, maroon and reduced on this bird.  Kinglet does not have maroon undertail coverts.

Bushtit does not have maroon undertail or maroon patch on crown, has very long tail, different vocalizations.

Bill not right for any of our sparrows.

Resources Used

Sibley field guide.

Previous Experience

I have seen several individuals of all the lookalike species mentioned in previous section, and have seen at least 30 Lucy's Warblers within their expected range in the Southwestern U.S.
