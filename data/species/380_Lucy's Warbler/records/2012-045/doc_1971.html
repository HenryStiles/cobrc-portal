Description of the Bird

This was a tiny, actively flitting warbler that was all whitish underneath, or with a little buffy tones on the upper breast. The head was very dull whitish gray with about half the crown collored a dull rust that was visible when the bird bend down to feed which it did regularly. It appeared to have a tiny, beady dark eye that was surrounded by a narrow whitish eyering. The short spiky bill was black. I didn't get great views of the dorsal surface and I never did see a rusty rump patch.
This was mostly a warbler without field marks, the best was the crown patch and the eyering and overall small size.
Behaviors: Flighty, foraging for insects in the developing seeds clusters on plains cottonwoods, unfortunately foraging mostly just under the canopy so great views were lacking. It would hang upside down a few times searching also emerging leaves for insects.
Call: similar to Virginia Warbler, one note, fairly loud, partially ventriliqual
Plumage: adult - probably male

Similar Species Discussion

Virginia's Warbler - is larger and would always show yellow undertail coverts and at least a little yellow wash on its breast

A female, immature gundlachi subsp of Yellow Warbler could actually look similar but would always be larger and the wings would show yellowish edges

Tennessee Warbler - the dark eyeline would have been visible as would its contrasting gray cap

Verdin juvenile would look similar but should have a brownish not gray back and it doesn't forage as actively

Bushtit - longer tail than LUWA and calls very noticeably different
