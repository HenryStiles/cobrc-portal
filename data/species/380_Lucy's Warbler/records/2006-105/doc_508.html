Description of the Bird

the birds were small warblers, noticeably smaller than Yellow Warblers in the same tree. Their shape was similar but slightly more slender than the nearby Yellow Warblers, but with a slighter bill that was almost spike-like. The bill and legs were black.

The heads on the birds were pale gray, with a paler loral area. The back and wings were the same color on two of the birds, and on one bird (the juvie) the wings had two buffy wingbars. The underparts on all three bird was a paler gray, almost white, but with even paler (white) undertail coverts. I saw a brick-reddish rump on one of the birds, and on the juvie saw a buffy rump, but on the third bird did not see the rump well enough to tell color. The tails on all three birds was the same gray color as the back. I never saw any red coloring on the crown of any of the birds.
Behaviors: The birds appeared to be a family group, and were foraging fairly high in a cottonwood along the creek. All three were somewhat hard to see.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species of bird were ruled out by the overall pale gray color, combined with the very small size, and warbler shape. Virginia's Warbler was ruled out by the lack of yellow anywhere on the plumage, pale loral area but lack of a distinct eye-ring, smaller size, etc. Very drab young Nashville, Yellow, and Orange-crowned Warblers were ruled out by the complete lack of yellow, red rump on at least one of the birds, smaller size, smaller bill, etc.

Bushtit was ruled out by the gray color with no hint of a brown component, warbler-like shape, red rump on at least one of the birds, etc. Young Verdin was ruled out by the thinner bill, red rump on at least one of the birds, more slender shape more indicative of a warbler, and more obvious pale loral area.
