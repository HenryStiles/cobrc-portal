Description of the Bird

Adult male and female birds were observed with a third heard singing. The male was a small bird with pale gray above and whitish underparts. Whitish undertail coverts. Reddish or rusty crown was clearly observed. Very thin and pointed bill. The female was smaller than the male and as above except for a gray crown rather than rusty. Both birds had gray wings, dark legs and dark eyes.
Behaviors: The male was observed singing from various perches. The female was observed flying into a tree nest hole with food and then coming out again. The female's feeding behavior was observed several times.
Call: Call and song heard as described in Nation Geo.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

There is no other warbler of this description nesting in this habitat in Colorado.
