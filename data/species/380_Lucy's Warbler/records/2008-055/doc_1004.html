Habitat

Birds were foraging in low deciduous vegetation (young cottonwoods, willows, tamarisk) along a permanent watercourse. Sagebrush in immediate vicinity (away from the stream).

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Eagle Optics 8x42 binoculars
Distance:  12 feet at closest approach
Light:  Sun was out; birds were mostly at right angles to it or more-or-less "down-sun."

Description of the Bird

Birds were slender warblers, with short thin dark pointed bills and plumage that was pretty much entirely pearly gray, without yellowish tones. I got an excellent look at the reddish rump of one of the birds while it was preening, facing away from me in the tree.
Behaviors: The birds were foraging actively in the lower deciduous vegetation along the watercourse. They appeared to be paired, as one apparently begged food from the other; it may have received a feeding, but I couldn't confirm this as the birds were behind a branch at the most critical moment--behavior was consistent with a feeding, however.
Call: Birds were calling frequently, giving a high-pitched thin sharp unmusical "chip".
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The pearly gray plumage on a small bird with a reddish rump rules out all other North American birds. Virginia's Warbler does not have the reddish rump, and should show (at least) yellowish on the undertail coverts. Gnatcatchers and kinglets are the wrong color and shape.
