Description of the Bird

A very small, gray warbler with a dark eye and black bill. It had a partially concealed rusty cap, about 10mm long, and rust color over 5mm in length on the cap feathers, and a bright rusty rump patch.

It was white on the underside, with light buff tinges, white undertail coverts, no yellow anywhere, and light supercillium and lores. The eye-ring was insignificant.

Overall the bird was pretty worn. The outer rectrices, R6,  showed a prominent white patch, but the rects were mixed fresh and dark with old light-colred and worn feathers. The wing appeared worn, but mostly even in color between primary coverts and greater coverts, and there was gray edging on the primary coverts. From this, and the status of the cap I surmised that this was an adult male, but very worn.

The bird was netted, banded, photographed, and released. No calls were heard (I'm deaf to high-frequency sounds anyhow), and no naural behaviors were seen. It was not refound after release.

Similar Species Discussion

Virginia's Warbler - eliminated by lack of yellow, its larger eye-ring, and overall size was bigger than the bird in the hand. I've banded many of these, and had photographs published.

Orange-crowned Warbler - eliminated also by lack of yellow, and cap color.

Blue-gray Gnatcatcher - eliminated by it having a longer bill, more blue color, and the lack of any rusty feathers. Although of similar size, the tail on the unknown bird was too short by comparison.

This was the first Lucy's Warbler I have banded, or had in the hand, but I have observed them numerous times in SE Arizona over the years.
