Description of the Bird

Finder was Cole Sage

Bird was VERY active and would not stay still (difficult to photograph as it flitted, feeding primarily in leaf-less elm trees (I could not tell what the bird was feeding on...it must have be tiny).  The bird produced some call notes that Frank Farrell and I heard that were different from what we normally hear (and that led us to the bird on two occasions).

Small warbler with sharply pointed bill.  Underparts were all clean whitish and all upper parts a pale gray.  The exception to this was a small dark red patch I saw on ONE occasion on the top of the head and on the rump and tail coverts (above the tail).  Black eyes and bill.  Otherwise, plumage was somewhat "unremarkable."

eBird checklist with my photos showing the red rump is located at: https://ebird.org/checklist/S134108606

 

Similar Species Discussion

Virginia's Warbler shows yellow undertail coverts.  Male Virginia's Warblers show a yellow wash mid-breast and has a very distinct eye-ring.  This Lucy's Warbler DID show a faint "eye-ring", but NOT very distictive.

Colima Warbler ruled out due to darker overall color, distinct eyering and yellow undertail coverts.

Nashville Warbler ruled out due to the overall yellow underparts that Nashville has compared to the plain whitish underparts of this Lucy's Warbler

Previous Experience

Lucy's Warbler - I have seen perhaps 10-15 in SW Colorado north of McElmo Creek over the last 20 years.  I have seen multiples of this species on most of my birding and herping trips to SE Arizona on ~15 visits.

 Virginia's Warbler - Seen MANY in Colorado (mostly spring and fall) over 30+ years of birding in Colorado

Nashville Warler - I have seen 1-5 each year in Colorado, as migrants over 30+ years of birding in Colorado

Colima Warbler - No experience.
