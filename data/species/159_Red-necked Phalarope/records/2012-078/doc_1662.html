Description of the Bird

Phalarope-build. Long, thin, black bill. White throat. Rufous-red nape. Top of head dark. Chest gray. Back/wings rufous and gray. Flanks white with darker streaks. Undertail coverts clean white.
Behaviors: During the time we watched this bird, all it did was feed in the middle of the pond, twirling about in typical phalarope fashion.
Call: Did not vocalize.
Plumage: adult, alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The other phalaropes (Wilson's and Red) are the only other species taken into consideration (phalaropes are quite distinctive...).

Wilson's Phalarope has gray top of head (not black), black down side of nape with white stripe in back (not rufous down side with gray in back), salmon on throat (not gray/rufous), belly/flanks clean white (without dark streaks).

Red Phalarope has white cheeks (not dark gray), yellow bill (not black), and rufous throat, side of neck, and underparts (not like RNPH).
