Description of the Bird

This bird appeared to be a small, long-tailed, Spizella sparrow with an unstreaked gray breast. It was similar in size and shape to the Chipping and Clay-colored sparrows present. This bird had a pink bill, complete white eye-ring, a rather plain gray face with a bit of a rufous cap and a rufous post-ocular eye line. It had faint wing bars. I didn't observe the rump color.
Behaviors: The bird perched briefly and then flew away.
Call: none
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

The pink bill, white eye ring and rufous trim eliminate all other Spizellas: Am. Tree has a bicolored bill and a breast spot; Black-chinned has no rufous trim; ditto for Brewer's; Chipping and Clay-colored have more elaborate facial patterns, lack the eye ring and pink bill.

Going further afield, Rufous-winged and Rufous-crowned Sparrows both have dark lateral throat stripes and would not be expected in this area.
