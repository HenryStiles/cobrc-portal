Habitat

Mixed agricultural and residential use. Bird was closest to pinon/juniper woodland but pasture land and homes were nearby. No cropped land.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x25 binocs, point & shoot digital camera
Distance:  approx 20 feet
Light:  Bird was easier to observe than photograph. Sun was high, but sky was hazy and white. Photo is somewhat enhanced to show features that were in shadow.

Description of the Bird

Approximately 10-12". Long neck. Brown bars on buff. Darker bars on wings and tail. Lower breast and belly largely unmarked. Yellow legs. Yellow bill, slight downward curve at tip. Reddish-brown crown. White eye ring, maybe just partial. Tail dark under tip.

(Yellow cast to feathers in photo is not accurate. The hazy sky was really bright behind this bird, and I was definitely not prepared with the proper equipment.)
Behaviors: Sat on fence pipe for duration of observation. Turned head occasionally, ducked head once, but mainly ignored observer. Did not startle, fly or move along pipe. Did not call or sing.
Call: Did not hear.
Plumage: Summer, mature

Similar Species Discussion

Initially thought bird was a greater yellowlegs, but bill was yellow, had downward curve and was shorter than yellowlegs'. Combination of yellow legs and yellow bill was instructive.

Resources Used

Sibley and Kaufman at time of sighting. Several other field guides and Internet sources at home later. Consultation with other birders, including Jim Beatty, Jim and Dennis Garrison.

Previous Experience

Upland sandpipers were common in north-central Kansas, where I grew up. Yellowlegs are occasionally sighted here. Other large sandpipers are unusual here, although we get dowitchers, willets, phalaropes and other shorebirds.
