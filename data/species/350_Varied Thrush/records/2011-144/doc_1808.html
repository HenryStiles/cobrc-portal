Description of the Bird

Mark Peterson and I walked up to the pond below Two Buttes Reservoir dam, and saw a Varied Thrush fly up from the water into the trees. We saw the bird briefly, though saw the orange wing stripe on a robin sized bird. We also saw the short tail, eliminating Townsend's Solitaire. The bird was near American Robins, and was quite similar in size and shape. We looked for a quite a while and couldn't re-find the bird. Photos taken earlier in the day by Doug Kibbe are included in this report.
Behaviors: Flew up from the pond into the trees, near American Robins.
Call: none
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

No other robin sized and shaped bird would have the orange wing stripe with a short tail.
