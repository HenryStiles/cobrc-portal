Description of the Bird

This bird was nearly robin sized with slightly shorter tail. It had a slaty gray back and fairly bright orange underparts with dusky grayish speckles on lower belly. A complete slate gray breast band crossed the orange breast and was slightly darker than gray back. Throat was orange. Face,nape,and crown were slate gray with orange supercilium.

Wings were a mixture of gray and orange with orangish wing bars, tertials, various orange & gray coverts, and blackish primary tips.

Bill and eye were dark.
Behaviors: Foraged in shallow slow moving creek.
Call: None given
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Speckled young robins come closest in appearance to Varied Thrush. But robins in any plumage lack orange supercilium, orange in wings, and a distinct dark breast band.
