Description of the Bird

A presumed adult female Varied thrush was seen from ~15:30-16:30 at 6987 Olde Stage Rd (residence), Boulder county on 1/6/10. The bird originally hopped onto a feeding platform (~3ft above the ground), and immediately noted as being something different. The most obvious characteristics were: prominent buffy-orange supercilium and brownish-gray upper chest band. The bird was noted as being Am. robin sized. Also noted were 2 prominent buffy-orange wing bars, buffy-orange throat and chest (except for the previously noted chest band), brownish-gray cap and back, scalloped flanks (buffy tending toward gray)
Behaviors: The bird would ground feed on sunflower seeds for ~5 - 10 min. then fly back to a nearby thicket (~10 ft away). This happened ~4-5 times over the course of 1hr. Other birds feeding in the same vicinity were House finches and Dark-eyed juncos. The bird finally flew away from the area ~16:30.
