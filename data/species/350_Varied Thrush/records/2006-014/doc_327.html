Description of the Bird

I was walking north on the south side of the Lamar Community College grove, walking toward some Blue Jays, when I looked up and a medium sized bird with orange in the wings flew over me and back to the south. I though, wow, that looked like a Varied Thrush. I called over to Brad Steger to see if he saw the bird, and he was looking another direction. I alerted Mark Peterson on the radio, that I may have just seen a Varied Thrush fly over me. Then just minutes later, Mark spotted the thrush in the cottonwood trees. The bird was mostly showing the back and wings. The back was blue/gray, with two orange wing bars on dark wings. The rump was and tail were also blue/gray. Finally the bird turned around, so we could see the blue/gray head, with orange eyeline going back from the eye. Also, we could see the orange chin and throat, with a black chest band, and more orange color below the black chest band going through the belly. The bird had orange legs, and mostly dark bill, with a bit of lighter color on the base of the lower maniable. The bird was thrush sized, though looked more pump than an American Robin. The bird wasn't really with other birds, except for a Northern Flicker in the same tree.
Behaviors: The bird stayed in the cottonwoods, until we lost the bird, and didn't see it again.
Call: none
