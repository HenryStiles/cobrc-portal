Description of the Bird

Robin-sized and shaped. Orange on throat and upper chest, and mid-belly. Buff-colored eye-brow; black eye and black bill; bill longer and more slender than American Robin. Head otherwise gray-brown. Crown and nape browner; mantle gray but browner in the center. Wings dark gray with buffy-orange tips to greater and median coverts, forming two wing-bars; also broad buff-orange tips to primary covers and secondaries; hint of scaling on sides; extensive gray on lower flanks.
Behaviors: Hopping on ground like American Robin.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

American Robin lacks gray breast band and wing markings and eyebrow stripe
