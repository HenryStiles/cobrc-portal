Description of the Bird

I first observed the bird perched on a chair back on our back patio. It flew to our back fence and into some oak trees. It flew off and returned several times, drinking at our waterfall/bird bath and foraging on the ground under our bird feeders.

Similar Species Discussion

It was so distinct that I never seriously thought it might be another species, but rather confirmed the sighting by looking at my Sibley Guide and comparing that to the pictures I took.

Resources Used

Again, Sibley guide to the birds of North America.

Previous Experience

Zero. This is a lifer for me. Of course the closest comparison would be the American Robin which is a common backyard bird for us.
