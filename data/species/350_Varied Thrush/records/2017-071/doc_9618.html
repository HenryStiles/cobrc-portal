Description of the Bird

This Varied Thrush originially flew by me and landed in a nearby tree in the thick branches.  I noticed the orange color and stripe on it's head and knew immeadiatly that this was a different bird for the area due to coloration.  It was a difficult bird to photograph as it was not very patient and continued to fly away.  It eventually flew to the west side of the river and into a wooded area where I could no longer view it.

Similar Species Discussion

I understood this bird to be a thrush and thought of the American Robin due to the orange coloration.  Identifying this bird was fairly easy.

Resources Used

I used both Cornell University Ornithology and Sibley's West for positive ID.

Previous Experience

This is the very first time seeing this bird and is a life lister, other similar species that I have seen is the Swainson's Thrush along with the American Robin.
