Light:  Bright sunny morning, bird moved from shade to sunlight

Description of the Bird

Approximatly 9.5 inches. Gray back and head with orange supercilium, gray mask on face, gray breasband, orange breast, with boldy patterned wings.
Bird flew into cottonwood, then to the ground where it foraged for a few minutes before flying off.

Similar Species Discussion

Would say the bird was unmistakable. The only thing even close would be American Robin. That was quickly eliminated due to orange supercilium, gray breastband and boldly patterned wings (robin does not have),  and lack of white eye ring (which Robin has).
