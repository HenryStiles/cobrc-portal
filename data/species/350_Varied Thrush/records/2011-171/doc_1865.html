Description of the Bird

It was very similar in structure/size to the many nearby American Robins (three of which were in the same binocular view)

Seen well in good light from the side only.

Dark gray-brown on the back, wings, tail and face patch which ran through the eye with a very bold rusty-orange eye-brow stripe.

Breast and throat rusty-orange with a gray-brown band across the upper chest.

Rusty wingbars noted.

Bill dark and structurally similar to the robins.
Behaviors: The Varied Thrush (VATH) seemed quite shy and as the other birders approached in evaporated into the thicket with the associated AMROs and was not re-found.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Superficially like an American Robin. AMRO does not have that bold facial pattern with the dark patch over the eye much like a mask, nor does it have the dark band across the chest of the VATH.
