Description of the Bird

This bird was feeding on Russian Olive fruit in the company of one American Robin. It was slightly smaller than the robin, but similarly shaped. It had a bright orange supercilium, throat and chest with a gray chestband and auriculars. It had 2 orange wing-bars and ornge at the bas of the primaries and secondaries. The rest of the bird including top of head, back, rump and tail were gray with a slight brownish tint. It is distinct from American Robin by its bold orange pattern on face and wings, and the gray breastband.
Behaviors: It was feeding on Russian Olive fruit and in the company of another member of the same family.
Call: silent
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

American Robin is the most similar species (see description). Other common US thrushes have spotted breasts which this bird did not.
