Description of the Bird

This bird was feeding on Russian Olive fruit in the company of one American robin. It was slightly smaller than the robin, but similarly shaped. It had a bright orange supercilium, throat and chest with a gray chestband and auriculars. It had 2 orange wing bars with orange at the base of the primaries and secondaries. The rest of the bird including top of head, back, rump and tail were gray with a slight brownish tint. It is distinct from American Robin by its bold orange pattern on face and wings and gray breastband
Behaviors: The bird was feeding on Russian Olive fruit.
