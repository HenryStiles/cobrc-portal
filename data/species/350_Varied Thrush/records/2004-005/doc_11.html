Description of the Bird

Approximately American Robin (Turdus migratorius) shape and size. The bird was basically gray, orange, and black. The orange dominated the throat and breast fading into white on the lower belly and vent region. The bird also had an orange supercilium extending from just in front of the eye to the hindneck in a shallow curve. Several wing feather tracts also had orange, producing two wingbars, and orange patches on the closed wing (result of orange base to remiges and primary coverts). The black included a complete, thick, breastband, a facial mask from base of bill, around eyes, to the cheek, and also non-orange parts of the wing. The blue-gray parts included the crown, mantle, lesser coverts, rump, and rectrices. The eye and bill were both black. The legs were orange.
Behaviors: The bird visited a feeding station at a private residence. It remained solitary, staying low in brush near the house.
Call: None heard.
