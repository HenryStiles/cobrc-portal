Description of the Bird

as before
Behaviors: This bird was first seen on Mon. Jan. 29th, and last seen on Sun. March 11th. We did not see it from Feb. 1st - 8th, but Don Fronk, a neighbor, thought that he saw it once during this period. While here, it seemed to be roosting within the crown of a blue spruce, and feeding on the ground beneath the spruce tree. It was feeding on a mix of crushed peanuts, sunflower hearts, millet and nyjer. We think that peanuts were the attractant, because once it was seen poking at a peanut in the shell, and it would eat peanut butter offered on a spruce cone. Seen further afield within back yard toward end of period. Bathed at length in pan 30' from spruce tree on March 10th.
