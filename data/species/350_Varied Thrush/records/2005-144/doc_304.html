Description of the Bird

Overall size: thrush sized, same as American Robin

First seen in flight - diagnostic orange fringing wing pattern clearly observed and first indicator of species id.

Perched - broad orange supercillium bordered by bluish gray cap and black face stripe; black breast band across orange breast and throat; orange wing bars; bluish-gray back, cap and upper tail.
Behaviors: Behavior: first flew in and perched at eye level in response to pishing; then, when being pursued for better looks, it perched in the tops of taller trees and acted rather shy. Eventually flew several hundred yards to private property - thrush like flight.
Call: quiet "thuck" while perched high in tree, repeated several times
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Initial sighting of wing pattern in flight alerted me to the species. Closer, perched observations eliminated AMRO based on plumage description above
