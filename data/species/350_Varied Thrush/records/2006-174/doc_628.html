Habitat

Rural residential yard with lots of trees. Ground snow covered with small clearing beneath seed feeder hanging from apple tree. House and outbuildings nearby. Bird observed in apple tree, nearby mature cottonwood or Siberian elms or ground feeding beneath seed feeder.

Other birds in close proximity or at feeder include: Juncos, four species of sparrows, Northern Flickers, Bluejays, magpies, Downy Woodpeckers, occasional Cedar Waxwings, robins, Black-capped Chickadees. Also fox squirrels and cottontails.

Description of the Bird

Robin like in size and shape with shorter tail; bill dark, robin-shaped with upper surface of upper mandible decurved; crown, mantle, upper tail surface all dark gray; under tail mixed black & white with small amount of orange; orange eye stripe above eye originating directly above eye and extending to rear with no stripe forward of eye; breast, sides, & belly orange; flanks generally orange with unpatterned light grey spots transitioning rearward to light grey femoral tract; vent white; black chest stripe and black cheeks; upper wings same dark grey as mantle with orange wing bars; under wings grey with white stripe paralleling anterior and posterior wing edges. This differs from Sibley picture showing single buff stripe on under wing. Please see accompanying documentary photos with birds wings outstretched about to take flight; legs pinkish orange.
Behaviors: Bird would enter yard alone, often coming from the east, & land in a mature apple tree with a sunflower seed feeder hanging from a lower branch. Bird would perch for several minutes then drop to the ground beneath the feeder where sunflower seeds cover the snow-covered ground. Bird would feed on sunflower seeds by walking very short distances and rapidly picking up seeds; bird would feed for up to two minutes but was timid and easily disturbed taking flight at the slightest noise or disturbance. Bird would usually wait to drop to ground feeding when few other birds were present; often saw it feeding with several Bluejays, Dark-eyed Juncos, several sparrow species, and several Mourning Doves (I sprinkle millet, ground corn, etc on the ground for the ground-feeding species). If disturbed, bird would return to perch in the apple tree & wait for varying time periods before ground feeding again, or it would fly off to perch in Siberian elms that border the eastern property boundary or nearby old large Plain Cottonwoods, or would leave the property & return at sporadic times.

Bird is always alone. There are numerous mature Russian olive trees on site that attract large numbers of robins & occasional small flocks of Cedar Waxwings; I never observed the subject bird feeding on the olives or joining the robins or Waxwings.
Call: Unheard. Always observed from indoors through kitchen window.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Bullocks Oriole. Exhibits similar colored breast, black eye stripe, no black chest band, white wing bars, black upper body surface

Robin. Exhibits similar colored breast, no black chest band, no wing bars, yellow bill. I have never observed robins at the sunflower feeder.

Black-Headed Grosbeak. Exhibits similar colored breast, grosbeak type bill, white wing bars.
