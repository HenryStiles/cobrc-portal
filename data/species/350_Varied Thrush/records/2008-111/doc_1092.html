Description of the Bird

Not sure how much this will help being that the photo shows a VATH. Nevertheless, A robin shape and sized bird perched low in a bush as I was viewing a group of 15 TOSOs near the NW corner of the enclosed campground. Orange ventrally outside of the faded blue breast collar and white belly. The scalloping on the breast and white belly indicates an immature. The strength of color and pattern indicates probably a male also the overall darkness of the bird when perched. Bird was viewed only for about 7 seconds before it flew off and was not relocated. Stocky body with square tail. Dark dorsally!
Behaviors: Came out of some cottonwoods and perched low for about 7-10 seconds not moving at all. Then flew up and back into the cottonwoods!
Call: I heard one high pitched "tip," that came from the area of the bird....presumably the thrush. It was hard to determine at that point. Otherwise the bird was primarily silent!
Plumage: Imm.
