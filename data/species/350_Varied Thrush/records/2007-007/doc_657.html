Habitat

There are lots of tall old spruce trees that hang low to the ground. It is on the location of a fish hatchery so there is a constant moving water source. There are bird feeders out that he cleans up under.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon 8X36 binoculars
Distance:  Least about 10 feet, most about 50 feet
Light:  It was a sunny day, but the sun was going down. Still plenty to daylight to see the bird, and we were able to get to within 10 feet of it at the closest.

Description of the Bird

Head black with orange supercilium. Throat orange with black breast band. Approximately 9 inches in length. Dark blue-gray mantle. Undertail coverts white. Median and greater coverts dark blue-gray on top, orange at tips. Wings orange and black. Tail dark blue-gray.
Behaviors: Hopped around like an American Robin on the ground, picking up seeds. Very wary of any human activity. Flies into surrounding spruce trees when scared, and stays there until it feels safe.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Adult male

Similar Species Discussion

We had heard that there may have been one sighted across the river the previous week. When I saw it, I thought at first that it was a strange colored American Robin, because of the way that he hopped around. Then I was able to get a better look, and saw the black breast band and orange (rather than red of a Robin) and knew what it was.
