Description of the Bird

the bird was about the size of an American Robin, and similar in shape. The bill was fairly short and thin, but blunt-tipped. It was black, with a small pale area at the base of the lower mandible. The eye was dark. The legs were a fleshy-pink color.

The bird had a grayish-brown cap, which connected in from of the eye with a grayish auricular area. The bird had an eyeline from behind the eye to the nape that was a burnt orange. The throat of the bird was also orange. There was a grayish chest band that seperated the orange throat from extensively orange underparts. The underparts were somewhat mottled in color, and with gray feathers in the flanks. The belly was white, and the undertail coverts white with some darker feathers.

The upperparts were gray with a sligth bluish tinge (almost plumbeous). The wings were dark, with two bold burnt orange wing bars and a complex pattern of orange patches in the flight feathers. The rump was grayish, as was the tail.
Behaviors: the bird was first found when it was on the ground near a wet area, foraging for food. It then flew up to an exposed branch, where it sat almost motionless for quite a while. It then flew away to parts unknown.
Call: none heard
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Almost all species were ruled out by the robin-like shape and size, including all thrushes other than Robin (which are smaller). American Robin was ruled out by the chest band, brighter orange color on the underparts, much more complex face and wing pattern, etc.
