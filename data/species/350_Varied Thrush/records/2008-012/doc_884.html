Habitat

Small creek riparian zone with cottonwoods, small willow and dogwood shrubs. Feeder station is basically a narrow deck and rail with seed scattered on rail.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10X42 binoculars, 400mm camera lens
Distance:  about 30 feet
Light:  The sun was behind the bird but on a very cloudy day. Light conditions were adequate for color, shape, and observing behavior.

Description of the Bird

This robin sized bird was obviously different from normal feeder birds found in Steamboat Springs in the winter. Observations were at 20-40 feet, in good light with and without binoculars.

The yellow-orange throat, grey chest band, and pink legs were the first features observed. The back was grey with some streaking, breast dull orange to white near legs, head grey with prominent dull white eye stripe, dark long (thrush like) bill. There appeared to be 1 1/2 orange tinted wing bars.
Behaviors: Posture was much like a robin when perched, fed by picking through seeds available at feeder station, and flew with wing beat similar to a robin.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Am. robin would be only similar species in appearance. The eye stripe, and chest band of this bird were very different from a winter plumage robin. Also, the yellow/orange of the throat was diagnostic.
