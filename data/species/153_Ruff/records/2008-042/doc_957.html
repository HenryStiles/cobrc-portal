Description of the Bird

Bright, luminescent orange legs; sturdy dark bill, fairly thick at the base and then slightly down-curved; overall very brown in appearance, especially compared to a very gray Lesser Yellowlegs standing nearby; about the same size as the Lesser Yellowlegs, but very, very stocky in comparison with a humpy back that made the head seem fairly small; fairly plain head; scapulars were all black with a uniform light brown edge; breast was heavily marked with billowing brown; white vents with dark smudges starting at about the legs; wings reached just barely past the tip of the tail; did I say bright orange legs?
Behaviors: Feeding along muddy reservoir shore line in the snow.
Call: n.a.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Stockier, browner and brighter legs than a Lesser Yellowlegs; legs not red enough and not black enough for a Spotted Redshank; Spotted Greenshank's bill would be upturned.
