Description of the Bird

A fairly chunky shorebird approximately the same height as a Lesser Yellowlegs it accompanied, but much larger bodied. One of the most obvious characteristics on this bird were its long, bright orange legs. The bill was entirely black as far as I could tell, of medium length and depth, and faintly drooped towards the blunt tip. Structurally, the bird's forehead was nearly straight vertical like nothing I have seen on any other species of shorebird. The front of the crown was higher than the rest of the head, as from mid-crown back the head sloped sharply down. The head was basally golden-brown with short, dark streaks or spotting on the crown and face. The bird had a very unusual off-white patch just above the bill that extended around to the chin and blended into the the sides of the neck and throat. Its underparts were brownish-white on the chest, upper belly, and flanks, blending to pure white on the lower belly and especially the undertail coverts. Irregularly sized black patches mottled the underparts. The upperpart feathering was very sharply patterned with all of the feathers thickly edged golden-brown. Most of the feathers were centered dark brown or black, while the lower scapulars had a very peculiar pattern such that the dark centers were V or tulip-shaped. The primaries were strongly barred brown and black, with the black barring thicker than the brown. The wings extended slightly past the brown and black barred tail. In flight, the bird showed large, white outer uppertail coverts, roughly oval-shaped. Dark brown central coverts split the two white ovals. The underwings were mostly white and the upperwing pattern showed a long, tan wing stripe that extended through the secondaries and most of the primaries. The legs extended beyond the tail in flight.
Behaviors: Mostly foraging in leg deep or shallower water along the reservoir's edge.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: alternate
