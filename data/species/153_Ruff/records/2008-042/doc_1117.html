Description of the Bird

Medium-sized shorebird similar in size to Lesser Yellowlegs, mottled golden-brown upperparts, white underparts with grayish mottling on breast, gray-brown head, moderately long orange legs, moderately long thin dark bill; in flight, white underwings, thin white wing stripe on upper wing, white rump with thick brown central stripe. Orange feet extend beyond brown tail.
Behaviors: Foraging in sandy shoreline of lake; walking, standing, flying short distances.
Call: none
Plumage: non-breeding alternate

Similar Species Discussion

No other North American sandpiper has orange legs except for oystercatchers which are much larger and turnstones which are smaller; bill too heavy and legs too short for a redshank.
