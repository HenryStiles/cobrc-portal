Description of the Bird

Medium-sized shorebird with bright orange legs, oddly small-headed and pot-bellied look, and boldly patterned upperparts. Bird was overall about the size of a nearby Lesser Yellowlegs but more bulky. Bill all dark, moderately long and thin, slightly downturned. Head mostly buffy brown, a bit darker around the eye giving it a slightly masked look. Head disproportionately small looking compared to body. Legs fairly long, bright orange. Upperpart feathers mostly dark centered (nearly black), with wide buffy margins. Breast white, with uneven buffy and nearly black splotching on the upper parts but mostly white lower, transitioning to nearly all white on the undertail coverts. Primaries about even with end of tail. Tertiaries nearly black with wide bold buffy barring
Behaviors: In my two observations the bird alternated between feeding and sleeping. It fed anywhere between the water's edge to belly-deep water. It associated with a Lesser Yellowlegs much of the time.
Call: not heard
Plumage: probably faeder-type male

Similar Species Discussion

Many similarly-sized shorbirds ruled out by their black legs. Among similarly-sized shorbirds with yellowish legs, Lesser Yellowlegs ruled out by direct comparison, and their legs aren't this orange. Greater Yellowlegs would be noticeably larger, with a slightly upturned bill, and their legs aren't this orange. Solitary Sandpiper darker, not as boldly patterned, and their legs aren't this orange. Spotted Sandpiper (also seen that day) much smaller, not as patterned, and their legs aren't this orange. Upland Sandpiper quite larger, with a pale bill, thinner neck, and their legs aren't this orange. Pectoral Sandpiper has a cleaner bib, lacks bold buffy barring on the tertials, and their legs aren't this orange. Sharp-tailed Sandpiper shows rufous cap, lacks bold buffy barring on the tertials, and their legs aren't this orange. Stilt Sandpiper darker, has more obviously decurved bill, lacks bold buffy barring on the tertials, and their legs aren't this orange. Dowitchers have obviously longer bills and their legs aren't this orange. Buff-breasted Sandpiper has shorter bill, lacks bold buffy barring on the tertials, and their legs aren't this orange. Wilson's Phalarope has more needly bill, very different coloration, lacks bold buffy barring on the tertials, and their legs aren't this orange.
