Description of the Bird

This was a medium sized shorebird, and was somewhat larger than the Lesser Yellowlegs that was also present, especially in body volume. To me, the feature I noticed first was the very bright orange legs, brighter than any shorebird I can remember. The bill was fairly short, black, and slightly downcurved, and the eye was also dark. The head was a uniform light brown, but appeared brighter than that description conveys. The entire back was also bright, and many feathers had a dark center, fringed with lighter brown or off-white, which made for a very striking pattern. The throat and upper breast were also mottled with either light brown or dark patches, and this pattern continued down the flanks. The belly and further back, including the under tail coverts, were white. I did not see the bird flying, but other people present said it had the diagnostic white patches at the base of the tail.
Behaviors: The bird was foraging along the muddy shore of Boulder reservoir, probing for food in the mud.
Call: None given.
Plumage: Satellite Male

Similar Species Discussion

The only other shorebirds with very bright legs like this bird are Ruddy Turnstone and Black-necked Stilt in breeding plumage. The body pattern rules out these species. Other shorebirds of this approximate size are :=
Yellowlegs - yellow legs, longer bills, darker body plumage
Upland Sandpiper - yellow legs and bill, long thin neck
Dowitchers - very long bills, yellow legs, darker body
Wilson's Snipe - very long bill, much darker body
