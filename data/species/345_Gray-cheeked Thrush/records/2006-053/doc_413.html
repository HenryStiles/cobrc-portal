Description of the Bird

American Robin-like passerine bird; about 25% smaller than a robin; typical size and shape of Catharus species thrush.

Upperparts (tail, rump, wings, back, nape) olive-gray-brown. Crown browner. Dark eye with pale gray orbital. Dark gray auriculars. White throat with black malar stripes/spotting, more spotted toward upper chest. Upper chest dotted with distinct black spots, about 1-2 mm in diameter, becoming gray on the lower chest. Chest background color white, extending to belly and vent. Flanks gray, same color as gray spots on lower chest.

Legs pale pinkish-yellowish. Bill dark except for basal half of lower mandible, which was yellow. Bill typical length of Catharus thrushes.

Underwing with pale buff stripe noticeable in flight.
Behaviors: Hopping on ground, perching in small trees near water source.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush has distinctly reddish brown tail. Swainson's Thrush has buff-colored orbital, and buff-tinged auriculars, malar, throat and chest, and paler gray flanks.

Resources Used
