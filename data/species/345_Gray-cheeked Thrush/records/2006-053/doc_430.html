Description of the Bird

The bird had an overall gray color, including the tail. The head was even a little darker gray than the body. It had gray on the cheeks. It had extensive spotting on the breast. It had dark gray flanks. It had a small eye ring. The front of the lower mandible was black, while the rest of the lower mandible was yellow. All the other birds looked the same.
Behaviors: The first bird wsa on the ground most of the time by a dripping water fountain. It would occasionally fly up to the trees in the area and stay there for a few minutes, then it would drop back down to the ground.
Call: Didn't hear it.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Swainson's Thrush - It has a cream colored eye ring and cream color face. Not as much spotting on the breast. The back is about the same color as the head. Olive colored flanks. Lower mandible is almost all yellow.
Hermit Thrush - It has a white eye ring. The head and back is gray. Brown wings and a rufous tail. It has a mostly pink lower mandible. Not as much streaking on the breast. It also has a white breast. It doesn't have very dark flanks.
Bicknell's Thrush - It looks a little more browner. Usually a little smaller. All measurements over lap with Gray-cheeked Thrush. It has a distinct reddish tail. It averages a shorter primary projection.
Veery - It is weakly spotted. A pale eye ring. Pale flanks. Head, back, and tail is a reddish color.

Previous Experience

I have seen quite a few Swainson's and Hermit Thrushes and Veerys also. The first Gray-cheeked Thrush at Crow Valley was the first one that I had ever seen. I have yet to see a Bicknell's Thrush.
