Description of the Bird

Thrush about size and shape of Swainson's Thrush, though seemed a bit bulkier. Dorsally, including wings, color was flat dull gray-brown. Beneath, moderately heavily spotted blackish-brown on chest without any buffy washing or other warm hues. Flanks dull gray, not the bright clean gray of Veery. Cheek dull grayish, with dull gray eyering, most noticeable above and behind eye, blending into (and thus disappearing) gray cheek, so that eyering incomplete. Remainder of underparts whitish
Behavior: Hopping on ground under dense low trees in fairly bright shade. Hopped into lower branches of trees and onto log as well
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Swainson's Thrushes have buffy wash on face and chest, more prominent eyering. Even Olive-backed Thrush has more colorful back. Hermit Thrush has rusty tail, which this bird distinctly lacked. Veery much much less spotting beneath, brighter above
