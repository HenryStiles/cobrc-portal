Habitat

Prewitt Inlet Canal with its hodgepodge of downed timber and mud and mosquitoes. The bird was right along the edge of the canal.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon Binoculars
Distance:  12'
Light:  Overcast conditions. Lighting not harsh and not too dark.

Description of the Bird

Size/shape identical to the many Swainson's Thrushes in the area. Cheek had an silvery-gray fan of feathers. No warm tones in the face/cheek. Tail was very slightly redder than the back of the bird. Chest white with dark brownish-gray spots. No warm tones in chest. Back and wings all the same flat gray-brown tone.
Behaviors: Behaving much like the Swainson's Thrushes in the area. Staying low and largely hidden along the inlet grove.
Call: N/A
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

SWTH: Lacking warm tones in the face. Very slightly reddish tail not correct.

HETH: Lacking a notable eyering, what it has is gray. Not red enough tail.
