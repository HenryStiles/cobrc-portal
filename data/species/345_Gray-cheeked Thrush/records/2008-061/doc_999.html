Description of the Bird

A brownish-olive backed Catharus thrush. Very plain gray face with a slight eyering mostly posteriorly. The breast was heavily spotted with blackish spots on a pale grayish-white breast.

The basal half of the mandible was a yellow-flesh color
Behaviors: Hopping on the ground and in low shrubs
Call: A burry Weeer call and a soft gurgling rich thrush song, impossible to put into words.
Plumage: Adult Definitive

Similar Species Discussion

The lack of a rufous tail contrasting with the olive back eliminates Hermit Thrush. The only other North American Thrush with a gray cheek area is Veery. The Veery has a bright russet back, this bird did not show that trait.
