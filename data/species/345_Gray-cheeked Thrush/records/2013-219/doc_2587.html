Description of the Bird

Large thrush, about size of Montane Hermit Thrush and larger than Pac NW Hermit Thrush, both of which were present nearby. Upperparts grayish brown without markings or wingbars. Tail and wings uniform with back color. Face with fairly distinct gray patch. Weak eyering posteriorly only. Moderately heavy black spotting on chest and upper belly against whitish background fading into weaker spots and then gray sides. Bill fairly hefty for thrush, with some pale at base. No buff or rust hues on face, chest, underparts, tail, wings (or anywhere on bird)

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush would've had rust on folded primaries and tail, bolder eyering, typically would've pumped tail under circumstances (came in to pishing), often flicking wings. Montane Hermit Thrush often grayer on back. Pac NW browner. Swainson's Thrush with buff around face, olive-brown upper parts, bolder and buff eyering.
