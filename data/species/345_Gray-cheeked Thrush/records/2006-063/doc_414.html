Description of the Bird

American Robin-like passerine birds; about 25% smaller than robins; typical size and shape of Catharus species thrush.

Upperparts (tail, rump, wings, back, nape) olive-gray-brown. A browner crown was observed in one bird (see photos). Dark eye with pale gray orbital. Dark gray auriculars. White throat with black malar stripes/spotting, more spotted toward upper chest. Upper chest dotted with distinct black spots, about 1-2 mm in diameter, becoming gray on the lower chest. Chest background color white, extending to belly and vent. Flanks gray, same color as gray spots on lower chest.

Legs pale. Bill dark except for basal half of lower mandible, which was yellowish. Bill typical length of Catharus thrushes.

Underwing with pale buff stripe noticeable in flight.

The degree of spotting on the breast, heaviness of the malar stripe and brightness of the yellow base of the lower mandible was variable among the individuals observed.
Behaviors: Foraging on ground, perched in trees usually within 15 feet of the ground.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush has distinctly reddish brown tail. Swainson's Thrush has buff-colored orbital, and buff-tinged auriculars, malar, throat and chest, and paler gray flanks.
