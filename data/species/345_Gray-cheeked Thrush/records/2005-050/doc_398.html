Description of the Bird

Medium-sized bird. Upperparts grayish-brown (more gray) with some olive. Grayish tail. Grayish face with some whitish streaks on ear-coverts, grayish lores. Very narrow pale eye ring only present along upper and posterior border of eye. Dark lines at sides of throat. Triangular brownish-black spots on lower throat and breast. Belly and sides also marked with spots but oval shaped and paler than breast spots. Underparts white with grayish wash on flanks. Upper mandible blackish, distal half of lower mandible blackish, proximal half of lower mandible yellowish. Legs fleshy colored.
Behaviors: Hopping and foraging on ground, probing for food. When approached would fly to low perch.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Bicknells Thrush should have more extensive buffy wash on breast and chestnut-tinged tail and wings. Mandible with more and brighter yellow. Hermit Thrush is generally much brighter rufous on tail, warmer brown on upperparts, has more extensively spotted breast and shows a complete eye ring. Swainsons Thrush has pale lores, broad buffy eye ring, and buff wash on face and breast.
