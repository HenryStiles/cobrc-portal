Description of the Bird

When initially sighted, the bird was obviously a thrush.  The typical thrush here would be Swainson's, but upon closer inspection the bird lacked the buffy eye-ring of a Swainson's.  The overall dull coloration ruled out Hermit Thrush.  Finally, good quality looks at the gray on the face confirmed the identification of Gray-cheeked Thrush.

Similar Species Discussion

Swainson's- lack of buffy eye-ring and dull body coloration.  Gray face.
Hermit- Dull body coloration with no red on the tail.  Gray face.
