Description of the Bird

This was the plainest faced GC Thrush that I saw during this spring's mini-invasion. The cheek was flat gray (I couldn't see a hint of internal streaking as on others) and there was no eyering visible. The back was plain dull grayish brown. The streaking on chest was heavy and blackish brown, more diffuse on upper belly. Flanks grayish. Remainder of underparts paler. The tail was visible and concolorous with back and wings. No buff wash anywhere.
Behavior: Sitting about 4 feet off ground at edge of bush in clear view after having flushed off ground
Call: unheard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush would have had rusty tail and an eyering. Swainson's Thrush would've had buffy wash and more olive back and an eyering.
