Description of the Bird

I was at the south end of the Last Chance Rest Stop "hole," where there is still vegetation. I was standing above this area, pishing, when a thrush popped into view at about 15 feet, maybe less. It was so close, that I dare not call out for David. I watched it for about a minute as it gazed up at me and shifted about some. then it flew, David and I could both see it. It was in very bright shade, about 30-40 feet from me. It remained there, frozen on a low branch, for about 30 seconds before scuttling off into dense cover from which it could not be retrieved.
The upperparts, including wings and tail, were uniform dull grayish brown. The cheeks were dull gray with a weak (almost non-existent) and partial eye ring. Moderately spotted blackish below. No hint of buff or warm color to face or chest. Sides dull grayish. I know, sounds like other descriptions, but they aren't that different from each other
