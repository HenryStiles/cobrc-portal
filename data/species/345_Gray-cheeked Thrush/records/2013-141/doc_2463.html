Description of the Bird

At about 6 AM, I was at the head of a group of about 8 birders working a flock of thrushes through moderately thick undergrowth. Mark had called out GC Thrush. In pursuit of that, a Veery and a Russet-backed Thrush had popped out. After seeing the RB Thrush, we split up a bit, and the Gray-cheeked popped up in front of me, about 15 feet.
It was a dull gray-brown thrush, dorsally, including the with tail, which was concolorous with the back. The cheek was dull gray, the eyering was weak, gray, and incomplete, seemingly disappearing into the gray cheek in front and below. The chest was moderately spotted dark, a bit heavier than that of Olive-backed Thrush but not nearly as spotted as Wood Thrush. The flanks appeared dull grayish (not as clean gray as those of a Veery). The bird seemed a bit bulkier than OB Thrush.
