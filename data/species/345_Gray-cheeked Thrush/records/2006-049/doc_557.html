Description of the Bird

A Catharus thrush, grayish brown above, whitish below with dark spots on breast. Little buff ground color to breast, with no warm buff to auriculars. Face was cold grayish with very slight grayish eyring. Auriculars looked more fan-like than other Catharus thrushes.
Behaviors: Acting thrush-like, spending time on the gound, picking through leaf litter, mostly in shaded areas, occasionally flying up to perch in low limbs in nearby trees, and remaining nearly still when perched.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All Catharus Thrushes are similar. Bird did not possess a bold white eyring nor contrasting rufous tail of HETH, nor the warm buffy tones in the face and breast of a SWTH. I don't know that BITH can be truly eliminated except by range.
