Description of the Bird

A browish-olive backed Catharus thrush. The tail was concolor with the rest of the bird's back. The breast was grayish-white and had bold blackish spotting. the face was grayish with a little bit of an eyering.
Behaviors: none as the bird was handheld during banding
Call: none
Plumage: Adult definitive

Similar Species Discussion

The lack of the rufous tail eliminates Hermit Thrush. The only other gray-cheekd Thrush in North America is the Veery. This bird had an olive-brown back not the bright russet back of Veery
