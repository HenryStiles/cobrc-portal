Description of the Bird

Bird was observed in Greenhorn Meadows Park, Colorado City, SW Pueblo County. Bird made no sounds nor call notes.

This thrush was smaller than nearby robins & larger than nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers. It was somewhat pot-bellied in shape with a smaller but robin shaped dark bill with some yellow on the basal half of lower mandible.

Upperparts were solid dull brown from crown & nape thru back & tail. Brown tail showed no contrast with brown back. No rufous tones were seen anywhere on bird. Underparts were white excepting dusky flanks & heavily black spotted breast. No eyering was observed on a grayish colored face & auriculars.

Bird briefly perched in narrowleaf cottonwood at my eye level & jumped to ground for a few seconds before disappearing into dense riparian habitat.

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush has more distinct eyering & rufous tail which it frequently & slowly raises when perched. Bird I saw had dull brown tail with no contrast with brown back. This bird also did not raise its tail during observation.

Swainson's Thrush has bold buffy eyering and breast. Sibley(2000)Guide to Birds illustrates some buff on G-c Thrush's breast, but I did not find this to be true on all photos I examined in J.Farrand's photos Audubon Society Master Bird Guide(1983) & his Audubon Handbook of Western Birds(1988) + many photos in internet photo albums. All photos showed spotted white(not buffy) breasts like the bird I saw.
 
Bicknell'sThrush shows some rufous in tail & wings which was lacking in bird I saw. In my 15 second look I went for most important field marks distinguishing G-c Thrush from other thrushes but had insufficient time to observe very fine & subtle distinctions.
