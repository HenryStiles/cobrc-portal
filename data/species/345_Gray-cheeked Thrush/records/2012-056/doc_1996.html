Description of the Bird

After previously having seen 5 or 6 Swainson's Thrush the same morning for comparison , I observed this thrush with a completely cold gray upperparts including tail with no contrast between back and upper tail , a narrower eye ring than adjacent SWTH , gray cheecks , no buffy tones at all around lores or neck , breast heavily spotted , did not get very much on the flank coloration unfortunately.
Behaviors: Skulking ,kept low in willows close to the edge of Rose Pond , did not flick wings or cock tail which is a characteristic of Hermit Thrush.
Call: No call or song heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other thrush has this combination of gray upperparts , small eye ring , gray cheeks , heavy breast spotting except Bicknell's , which I consider impossible to tell apart except by location or song. This bird did not sing or call. But the chances of a finding a Bicknell's in CO must be exceedingly slim in view of the wintering and summer breeding locations and their typical migration pattern through the Eastern seabord states .
