Description of the Bird

At about 7:45 am, Nick Moore called my attention to a thrush he had just flushed up. I walked in his direction, and together we watched the bird in dappled sunlight for about 90 seconds at about 20 feet. This was a largish thrush, with dull gray auriculars (not as well demarcated as that of Veery) that lacked any buff hues. The bird had an incomplete dull grayish-white eyering, essentially absent anteriorly and inferiorly, boldest towards rear and also present above eye. Upperparts uniform in coloration: flat dull brown without warm brown or olive cast. Tail same color as back. Throat white. Chest whitish with moderately heavy, darkish brown spotting. Spotting appeared more prominent than that of typical of Swainson’s Thrush. Flanks gray. Bill color and shape not noted.
Behaviors: Flew from ground, sat on low branch, flew into dense undergrowth from which we could not extract it.
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush eliminated by dull brown tail. Swainson’s Thrush would have some buff, and even if that were not seen, eyering shape would be different. Veery not nearly as spotted beneath.
