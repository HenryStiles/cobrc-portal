Description of the Bird

At around eight in the morning I saw a small thrush perched not moving low in a tree. This was the first thrush I had seen this year. I was immediately struck by the fact that I could seen no buffy tones in the face and the lack of reddish color in the tail. Mostly due to the lack of red in the tail I initially thought Swainson's Thrush. The bird was observed for around 45 seconds from under 15 feet away and I managed to get a few photos of the bird. I was not happy with my initial identification of the bird but moved on and shortly found a Swainson's Thrush As soon as I saw the face of the bird I went back to my camera to review the photos I took. The photos showed a white eye-ring with little to no buffy tones in the face. The eye ring looks more pronounced in one of the photos however it fades as it approaches the front of the eye. This combination of field lead to the identification of a Gray-cheeked Thrush.
Behaviors: The bird perched silently low in a tree until it dropped into the more dense vegetation below.
Call: The bird was silent.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

As discussed in the description white incomplete eye ring is a very good mark for GCTH as well as extensive spotting below. As an ebird editor Ted Floyd remarked that in the photo the bird held its tail in the "same plane" as a good mark to help eliminate HETH it is also important to note that observations in the field noted a lack of rust in the tail.
