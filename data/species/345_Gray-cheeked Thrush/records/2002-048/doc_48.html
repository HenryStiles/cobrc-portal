Description of the Bird

The bird had a similar size, shape, and posture to a Yellow-rumped Warbler (seen at the same time).

It had a very prominent white iris, with a bold, broad yellow lore. The neck and throat were gray.

It had prominent yellow flanks.

Its back was a dull greenish color, with two white, moderately broad wingbars.

(My field notes did not include any mention of the bill characteristics)
Behaviors: The bird was foraging slowly in trees, about 20 feet above the ground.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ignoring the white iris for the moment, the gray neck and throat, yellow flanks, and bold white wingbars leaves these possibilities:
Cassin's Vireo
Blue-headed Vireo
perhaps Pine Warbler
In fact, none of these alternative species would be expected to have such bright yellow flanks. The addition of the white iris and yellow lores leaves only White-eyed Vireo as a possibility.
