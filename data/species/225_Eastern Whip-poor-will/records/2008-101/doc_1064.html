Description of the Bird

A dark long thin-winged nightjar with a long tail. The tail had prominent white tips to the outermost feathers. The bird was completely dark chocolate brown. It had a tiny bill.
Behaviors: Flushed from the exact smae spot two times. Each time it flew up to a tree limb which allowed for good to excellent looks
Call: none
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Common & Lesser Nighthawks, Common Poorwill, Chuck-wills-widow. CONI & LENI have big white to buffy wing spots in the primaries. A mark that I would have seen easily if it had been present. COPO is smaller, paler and grayer with a much shorter tail. CWWI is larger, not nearly as much white is visible in flight on the tail corners. CWWI is much more richly colored reddish brown. The bird I saw was cold dark brown with a few buffy-cinnamon spots on the wing coverts and head.
