Description of the Bird

I was going to play recordings of Chuck-Will's-Widow and Norn was cueing up Eastern Whip-Poor-Will just for grins (hey, you never know).  Norm beat me and started the Whip-Poor-Will recording.  He played it for about 15 seconds and then stopped.  A responding Whip-Poor-Will was calling back from the dry creekbed area!!  We were all staring at each other, open mouthed in utter disbelief!  The bird called several phrases for about 10-12 seconds then stopped.  We got setup to record the bird with Merlin and Norm played the recording again, but alas, we could not elicit another song reponse.  About 15-20 minutes later we heard a "Quip" call further east, but was not sure it was the Whip-Poor-Will or a Common Poorwill.

Bird called a rapidly repeating three parted "whip" "poor" "will" that  is diagnostic for Whip-poor-wills. This bird did NOT have the burry,  "stuttered" call of a Mexican Whip-poor-will.

Similar Species Discussion

Other than Mexican Whip-Poor-Will, no other species was even considered.  Mexican was ruled out as this bird did NOT have the burry, "stuttered" call distictive of Mexican (and not Eastern Whips)
