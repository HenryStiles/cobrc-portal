Description of the Bird

I saw it for a short period of time. It had a long tail and long wings. It had a lot of white on the outer tail. Dark upper body and rufous wings.
Behaviors: It was calling from a tree and then when we saw it, it flew towards the west and landed in a tree not far from the one it flew from.
Call: It gave a repeated whistled whip-poor-will.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common Poorwill - Smaller than this bird. Not as long of wings or tail and not as much white in the tail. Song is just a whistly 2 part, poor-will repeated over and over.
Chuck-wills-widow - Larger than this bird. Longer and broader tailed and longer wings. No white in tail. More rufous overall. Song is a 3 part chuck-wills-widow, repeated.
Buff-collared Nightjar - Looks a lot like a whip, but has a buffy collar and not as much white in the tail. More neatly mottled back.
Other Goatsuckers - They all have white bars in the wings. Nighthawks have more pointed wings and forked tail.
Mexican Whip-poor-will - More rough and blurry song. Very distinguishable.

Previous Experience

This is my first eastern Whip-poor-will and I have only heard a few Mexican Whip-poor-wills. I have seen a lot and heard many more Common Poorwills. I heard 2 and saw 1 Buff-collared Nightjar. I have never heard or seen a Chuck-will's-widow. I have seen and heard Common and Lesser Nighthawks and heard a Common Pauraque.
