Description of the Bird

Typical warbler-sized bird with contrasting yellow and black plumage for the most part. The "face", throat, and underparts were basally yellow with black facial markings and black streaks along the sides and flanks. The black facial markings consisted of a straight line from base of the bill through the eye and connecting to the olive-colored auriculars, as well as a shallow "U" shape crescent below the eye but separated from the eye by a patch of yellow. The crown, hindneck, auriculars, and upperparts were olive.
Behaviors: I heard the bird singing as I was driving along the highway adjacent to the park. It continued to sing and forage among the tree leaves, apparently gleaning insects from the leaf surface, while I observed it.
Call: Short burst of "zeet" notes that rose in pitch as song progressed, song lasted approximately 2 seconds
Plumage: Adult
