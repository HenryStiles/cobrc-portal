Description of the Bird

A mostly yellow warbler with a long dark bill. Underneath all yellow with a few rows of blackish streaks on the flanks. Face with a dark eyeline and a dark loop attached to the dark lore and bending below the eye but not connected like a full adult would show. Crown, back, and nape a dull olive green. Back with a few parallel rows of dull reddish streaks (seen at close range).
Behaviors: Singing and moving at mid and low elevations in a riparian area in Ponderosa pines but mostly in shrubby willows. Foraging between singing.
Call: sang a loud rising series of zee-zee-zee-zee notes.
Plumage: possibly 2nd yr

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow Warbler without black flank streaks and without dark loop below its eye.
Townsend's Warbler - has prominent wingbars and dark auricular patch
Black-throated Green Warbler - immature female can have similar face pattern but would have 2 broad white wingbars and it would not sing a PRAW song and would be without reddish streaks on its back.
