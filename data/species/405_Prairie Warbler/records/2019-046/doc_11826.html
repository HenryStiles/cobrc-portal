Description of the Bird

Small yellow warbler (smaller than adjacent chickadees) with faint streaking in flanks, yellow supercillium, and darker semicircle under eye. Fully yellow below up to throat with weaker yellow on undertail coverts. Yellowsh back and head (not as bright as below) with darker wings.Foraging in cottonwoods at mid-height with Black-capped Chickadees and White-breasted Nuthatches.

Photos from Donna Stumpp here: https://ebird.org/view/checklist/S59606844

Similar Species Discussion

Wilson's, Yellow, Townsend's Orange-crowned, Nashville, Tennessee, Pine Warbler, Palm, Cape May, Blackpoll Warbler, Magnolia, Common Yellowthroat:

The extent and brightness of yellow plumage on underside rules out Orange-crowned, Tennessee, Common Yellowthroat, Cape May, Palm, and fall Blackpoll. Townsend's generally only yellow on chest with whiter belly and undertail coverts. Magnolia and Pine also has white under tail and undertail coverts. Pine also will usually show noticeable white wingbars and is a little bit bigger and bulkier (like Yellow-rumped). Extent of darkish, smudgy streaking on sides rules out Wilson's, Yellow (which have become relatively sparse in the area over the last week), and Nashville, as well as some of the other species previously ruled out by lack of bright extenive yellow below.

Resources Used

Experience with this species and all similar species in both breeding and non-breeding plumage allowed me to instantly recognize this is a Prairie Warbler. Sibley app used after sighting to confirm field marks and rule out other species.
