Description of the Bird

 Bird first observed and identified by Joey Kellner, eventually seen by a total of 11 birders (see previous page).  Bright yellow warbler from below with pale undertail coverts. Back and cap brown, with similarly colored flank streaks, eyeline and undereye arc.  Faint pale wing bars. Fed and preened during observation period.

Similar Species Discussion

Not yellow warbler as facial markings prominent, and streaks were brownish, not red. Pattern of facial markings and lack of white wing bars excluded Pine; while lack of white eye ring also excluded female Magnolia. Not palm warbler, as lacked contrasting cap and incorrect tail pattern, no persistent tail pumping.

Resources Used

Experience with this species by many members of the party of the birders present.  After we all saw the bird, I reviewed available resources on hand in our car, including The Sibley Guide to Birds (2nd edition) and The Warbler Guide (Stephenson and Whittle).   A bird most of us have seen either in expected breeding habitat in southeast US, or on a more limited basis out of range in migration.

Previous Experience

Previously seen this species in Colorado in migration (male bird, singing in Belmar park, May 2014), also had seen several of this species while traveling in Tennessee in April 2016, have multiple observations in Florida dating back to 2010.
