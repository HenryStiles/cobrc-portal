Description of the Bird

Mark Peterson found the bird in the biggest tree, just below the dam, at Lake DeWeese. The bird gave good views and was pumping the tail up and down. The bird was a warbler and was with other warblers, mostly Yellow-rumpeds. The bird was a little smaller than the Yellow-rumpeds. The head was grayish, with white eye arches above each eye. Grayish ear patch, contrasting with the yellow chin and throat. The underparts were bright yellow, lacking noticeable streaking, the back was greenish, the wings were similar in color as the back, without any noticable wing bars. The undertail was yellowish, though not as bright as the underparts. The tail was also greenish/yellow. The bill and legs with dark. The bird was overall dull, it appeared be a first fall female.
Behaviors: Feeding with other warblers in a tall tree below the dam at Lake DeWeese.
Call: none
Plumage: first fall

Similar Species Discussion

Not too many warbler pump the tail all the time, like this bird.

Palm Warblers would have dark line through each eye.

Magnolia Warbler probably looks most similar, though has white wing bars, very white undertail coverts, doesn't pump the tail.

Kirtland's Warbler not likely in Colorado, has streaking on the sides, grayish above, not greenish
