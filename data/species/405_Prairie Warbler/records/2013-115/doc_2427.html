Description of the Bird

A beautiful singing adult male warbler. Bright yellow chin, throat, belly, with bold black streaks on the sides. The face was yellow, and had a thin black line going from the bill through the eye, and black going down toward the ear patch. The back of the head was yellowish green, with red streaks on the back. Greenish yellow wings, with faint yellowish wing bars. The bird pumped the greenish tail, and the undertail coverts were mostly white with a touch of yellow. The bill was dark, small, thin, pointed. The legs were dark.
Behaviors: Singing male, in the scrub-oak.
Call: Singing distinctive song, buzzy rising song
Plumage: adult
