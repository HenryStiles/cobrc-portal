Habitat

Willows along a creek in wooded canyon with mixed confier moist north and east slopes, scrub oak and Ponderosa pines in south facing slopes. Creek bottom contained willows (near the water), cottonwoods with some scrub oak and juniper.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss Victory FL, 8x32
Distance:  as close as 7 feet
Light:  partly cloudy

Description of the Bird

A dendroica warbler, yellow below, greenish-yellow above with blackish, broken streaking down the sides and flanks and a dark spot on the side of the neck near the top of the blackish streaks. Olive green wings with thin, yellow wingbars. A dark, eyeline with yellow crescents above and below the eye, and a dark olive colored mustacial crescent, becoming wider and paler towards the rear, eventually blending with the greenish-yellow nape and head color. No red streaking was noted on the back during observation, however a photo shows a possible hint of pale red streaks.
Behaviors: Flitting in the willows, staying mostly out of sight, responsing briefly to pishing.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Pine Warbler was the only other species I felt compelled to rule out. This was done by facial marks of bird, particularly dark mustacial crescent, bolder streaks on flanks, and lack of white to the underparts. Also, wingbars were very thin and yellowish, not nearly as bold as PIWA.
