Description of the Bird

Warbler sized bird. Yellow below with faint brown streaking from side of breast down flank.

Facial pattern: Yellow supercilium, cheek and chin. Dark eye line, and a brown streak from base of bill across cheek, ear and to the nape.

Not a good look at upper, but generally soft gray/brown.
Behaviors: Silently caring what appeared to be cottonwood seeding to line nest. It would return to the nest about every 2-3 minutes.
Call: silent
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was a little bit of a challenge, it did not look like a other female Prairies I've seen before. Little green above, more sandy. The face pattern was also interesting - more yellow like a male, but streaks were too faint for a male.

Female Cape May has some similiar markings, but this bird did not have the yellow extending up the side of the neck toward the nape.
