Description of the Bird

As I was walking around our property trail, I noticed a bird feeding in our tree. I was very close to the tree, only a few feet away, when I saw a small warbler, about 4.5-5.0 inches long. It had yellow underparts and an olive upper side. Face was yellow and I noticed a distinct dark eyeline and a black moustacial streak coming from near the bill that looped under the eye. Crown was olive-yellow. Bill was narrow and warbler-like and dark. Neck, throat, breast and undersides were yellow and I noticed the very distinct bold, dark streaks on the sides and flanks of the bird. Tail and wings were brownish. Wingbars were indistinct. The bird was gleaning small insects attracted to the flowers of the tree.
Behaviors: Gleaning insects within the tree interior. It was 82 degrees and so the bird seemed to stay in the interior of the 15 foot tall tree.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Not a Yellow Warbler. This bird had distinct eyestripe and moustachial stripe, and heavy black streaks on sides and flanks. Not a Townsends because this bird had completely yellow underparts. Lacked any black on the breast. Not Canada due to distinct black eyeline and moustache on yellow background. Not Kirtland's due to yellow face. Not Grace's or Yellow-throated due to completely yellow underparts and yellow face. Not Orange-crowned or Pine due to distinct bold black moustacial and eyeline stripes.
