Description of the Bird

The bird was actively feeding in the mature willows, mostly in the same small group of trees. It feed mostly from 6 feet high to the trees tops (about 35 feet here).

Characteristic frequent tail twitching or flicking revealing white outer rectrices. Bright yellow underneath with dark streaks at sides and flanks, whitish vent and undertail coverts, greenish yellow crown and upperparts generally (did not get a good study of back), yellow supercilum arcing over eye, dark eye line, yellow crescent below eye, dark mark at side of throat or shoulder, indistinct yellowish wing bars. Did not note any calls during any of the 4 times I observed it over 2 days.

Similar Species Discussion

These features were distinctive and together eliminated other warblers:


Tail twitching and flicking, arched yellow super, broad yellow crescent below eye, extensive yellow underparts with dark streaks but pale vent and undertail covers, faint yellow wingbars. The face pattern is distinctive and unlike other warblers
