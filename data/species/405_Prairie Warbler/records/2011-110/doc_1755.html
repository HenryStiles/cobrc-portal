Description of the Bird

This individual was captured in a mist net.
It was a small (wing cord = 55 mm) yellow bird with a longish tail (= 50 mm) and a thin bill.
It had an olive back with large chestnut centers in the feathers on the back and faint chestnut spots/streaks on the side, and faint yellow wingbars.
There was a dusky eye line splitting the yellowish supercillium from the light yellow "crescent" below the eye, which was bordered by a thickerer olive-gray crescent line. It had faint olive-gray auriclars. There was a olive/gray slash on the "shoulder" or lower part of the "neck" near the nape.
It had a pale yellow breast and belly, with yellowish on the vent and undertail coverts.
The tail was longish (50 mm) with long white patches in the retrices.
The bird was aged and sexed by the criteria in Pyle, Vol 1, page 387

Similar Species Discussion

Pine Warbler is much larger, larger thicker bill, does not have crescent below the eyes, lacks dark spot on sides of throat, greener back and has white wing bars.
Magnolia Warbler has complete, distinct eye ring, yellow rump, white undertail coverts and white band across the tail.
Kirtalnd's Warbler (yeah, right) is much larger, no subocular crescent and has white undertail coverts
