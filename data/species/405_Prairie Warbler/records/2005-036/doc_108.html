Description of the Bird

A very small, bright yellow warbler. The bird was mostly observed from below as it foraged in the canopy of willow trees. The bill is dark, thin, and delicate. The center of breast is bright yellow and without markings. This yellow extends down through the belly but fades into pale yellow undertail coverts. The flanks have bold black stripes running up to the upper side of the breast. At the junction of the side of the upper breast and lower cheek a bold black bar cuts perpendicularly across the previously mentioned streaks. A bold black eye line runs through both sides of the eye, shorter behind the eye than in front. The auriculars have a bold black arc forming the lower border, with a bright yellow interior. This arc joins with the eye line just behind the bill. The eye line behind the eye and the auricular border both end abruptly in greenish-yellow nape feathers. The eye is dark. A yellow supercilium and supraloral enhance the facial pattern above the eye, and the greenish yellow nape coloration extends onto the crown. The feet and legs appear to be grayish pink. The underside of the tail is mostly pale grayish white, but the outer edges are dark gray, thickening near the end of the tail. The wings are darker than the underparts, mostly greenish yellow but with dark edging and pale yellow wing bars at the tips of the greater and median coverts. Most of the patterning on the upperparts was not visible as the bird was always above me. I briefly saw the rufous streaks on the back of the bird but I was unable to photograph this feature well.
Behaviors: The bird was actively foraging in the canopy of newly leafed out willow trees.
Call: The bird was heard singing 3 or 4 times. It was a very thin, very high rising call of several short syllables ending with a little longer, more drawn out syllable. I listened to the recorded song on Stokes Eastern Bird Songs guide and found the match to be excellent. The bird also called a few times, which was higher and had a bit more tone than the husky check note of nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers.
Plumage: Adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The lack of white wing bars eliminates many other primarily yellowish warblers with strong facial markings and flank streaks. For example, Magnolia Warbler has similar flank streaking but lacks the yellow below the eye. Blackburnian Warbler is more orange on the upper breast and below the eye. Townsend's Warbler abruptly transitions to white belly and undertail coverts, in addition to its white wing bars. All and all this is a very distinctive bird that would be hard to mistake. The song seals the ID deal.
