Description of the Bird

the bird was a tiny goose, much smaller (at most half the size) of nearby Greater Canada Geese. The overall shape was more stocky, mostly due to a relatively shorter neck. The head shape was somwhat blocky, though the head also looked relatively smaller in comparison to Canada Goose. The bill was black, and relatively smaller in comparison to the head than nearby geese. The eye was dark, as were the legs.

The bird's plumage was similar to nearby Canada Geese, with the exception of a thin white neck band at the base of the neck, and overall a much darker color. This was especially evident on the breast, which was extremely dark, almost a glossy dark brownish-purple.
Behaviors: the bird was hanging out with a flock of Canada Geese on a cemetery lawn, and was very shy, much more so than the Canada Geese
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Canada Goose was ruled out by the much smaller size of this bird, as well as proportions, with a short neck, small bill, etc.

Other subspecies of Cackling Goose were ruled out by the very dark breast, with glossy brownish-purple color, by the thin neck band, and by the especially small size of this bird.
