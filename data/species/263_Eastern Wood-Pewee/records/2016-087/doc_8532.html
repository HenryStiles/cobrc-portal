Description of the Bird

Bird repeatedly singing from medium to high regions of cottonwoods. Same location as last two years, more or less. Medium-small flycatcher (larger than Empid, smaller than Myiarchus/Kingbird). Olive-green above. Generally washed lightly in yellow below, with olive wash to sides of chest, but not sides of belly. No markings/smudging on vent/undertail coverts. Throat off-white, washed lightly in yellow. Auriculars and crown colored same as back. Two distinct wingbars, not seen well enough to comment in detail. Underside of bill entirely pinkish. Bill length and size typical of wood-pewee. Long primaries, typical of wood-pewee. Sang and called repeatedly. Peee-ooo-weeee. And Pee-weeeeee. Clear, without any burriness. This is same bird, presumably, heard and seen at same location by many others over ensuing weeks. 

Similar Species Discussion

Empids. Smaller, shorter primaries, different vocalizations
Western Wood-Pewee. Call and song differences definitive. The lack of markings on undertail/vent, the limited dusky on chest, the all pink underside of bill point away from Western and towards Eastern WP
