Description of the Bird

Heard singing for 10-15 minutes total. See-ooo-weeee. Seee-weee. See-oooo. Clean whistle, no burriness.. The bird was a pewee with a relatively bright green back and pale underparts with very limited dusky on sides of chest on no dark markings on vent/undertail coverts. There was a hint of an eyering seen in the field, posteriorly. The bill was entirely orange below. The bird appeared to be paired with a typical looking WEWP and they had two young (assuming the EAWP was really the father)

Similar Species Discussion

Western Wood-Pewee primarily eliminated by vocalization, though the underpart pattern, green back, and pale mandible support the ID of EAWP.
