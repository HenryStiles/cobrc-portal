Description of the Bird

This is additonal information about the same bird as reported on 15 August 2007.

The Eastern Wood-Pewee was observed lower and was photographed two days after I first discovered it.

It now prefers to forage a low elevations and perches more frequently below mid-canopy level. Some additonal things that I noticed about the bird, especially as compared to the number Western Wood-Pewees is the overall coloration is much duller than the darker Westerns. I thought that the crest on the Eastern was more rounded and less crested than the Westerns. The wingbars, which Sibley describes as being broad, are narrow on this bird. I thought that the bill color was a yellower shade of organe than the Westerns. Having said this, it is still just a sample size of one. It sang less frequently today, I didn't hear it at 0715 when I arrived and although I wasn't always in its territory, I didn't hear it until about 0830 and then only frequently until about 0930 when it became more vocal, and thus easier to find. It took me about 40 minutes before I actually could view it perched and singing. I watched it catch a large drgonfly which it quickly dispatched and consumed.

As the photograph shows the underside of the mandible has mostly orange with only the distal tip being dusky. If you were to use the key shown in Pyle's Indentification Guide to North American birds, this would make it an Eastern Wood-Pewee, but see comments below and Pyle does mention that more work is needed in this measurement.

Additional Information

Comments: I wanted to add photographs to this observation, thus the new report. Althought there are some slight differences between the two look alike species, Dr. Van Remsen told me last year that he has hundreds of unidentified wood-pewees in the LSU lab and he is convinced that neither species has unique characters 100% of the time, but that each can overlap the other species in some or most of its characters, therefore an I.D. by voice alone can be used to positively I.D. the two sibling species contra Leukering and Wood.
