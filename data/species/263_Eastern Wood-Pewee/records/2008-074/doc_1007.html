Description of the Bird

Bird was slightly larger than "empids".
Head was brown with dark eye & obscure incomplete eyering.
Bill was narrower than a finch & slightly longer than half the bird's head width. Bill had dark upper mandible & solid yellow appearing lower mandible.
Back had noticeably olive greenish tones.
Wings were brown but darker than head & with prominent white wingbars. Tertials were also dark with fairly crisp white edges.
Underparts white with obscure off-white vest & vague off-white breast band appearing incomplete.
Legs and feet were dark.
Behaviors: Bird perched atop or near top of low growing shrubs and small trees giving about three call notes in 15 minutes of observation. It occasionally flew from one perch to another or grabbed an insect & returned to original perch.
Bird did not flick tail or wings.
Call: A sparingly given but loud clear high-pitched
"peeeeeee" or "peeeeeei"
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Western Wood Pewee often has "dirtier" grayish underparts with more distinct vest & breastband.
It also has a browner back lacking noticeable greenish olive dorsal tones. Its bill often lacks the solid yellow appearing lower mandible of Eastern Wood Pewee.
Western Wood Pewees do not often give the high pitched single whistles of Easterns.

Willow Flycatchers are smaller & often flick tail & wings especially after alighting on perch. Bird I saw did neither. Willows do not have call notes like pewees.
