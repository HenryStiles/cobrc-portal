Description of the Bird

First noted with plaintive, whistled song: "pee dee dee, pee-a-wee" repeated over and over (male assumed). Finally observed the pewee in the top of a Cottonwood Tree on a small, dead branch. It had many similarities to a Western Wood-Pewee. It seemed perhaps a touch larger than a Western, less dark over all and slightly more noticeable wing bars. Head seemed slightly crested in comparison to the Westerns I've seen, but this could've been just an individual difference. Main identifying feature was the distinctive song. Starlings would occasionally give this bird a hard time. Well observed. Seen and heard from main Poudre River trail at a "pullout" witha sign just 200 feet past the Spring Creek bridge. Many thanks to the birder(s) who found and reported this bird!

Similar Species Discussion

Western Wood-Pewee. Song eliminated this species. Also, Western Wood-Pewees are very similar with most I've seen slightly darker and more obscured wing bars than this bird.
