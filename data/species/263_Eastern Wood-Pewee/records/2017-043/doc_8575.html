Description of the Bird

First heard bird singing clear two tone song ("pee-yer"). When visualized, bird was clearly Contopus  type flycatcher, with slightly raised crest and long primary projection.  From below, somewhat of a darker band across breast, but flanks and body appeared "cleaner" than more smdugy appearance, which would be expected in Western Wood-Pewee.  Lower mandible orangish-yellow, with no dark.

First reported this year by Glenn Walbek on Jun 2.  Per E-bird, this area has hosted a singing EAWP seasonally since first discovered by Kaempfer and Severs in Aug 2014, then by Mlodinov et al July 19-20 of 2015, m.ob spanning 6/3 to 8/6 of 2016. 

Similar Species Discussion

Western wood pewee has huskier, burry song, tends toward darker underparts, and darker lower mandible.  Obviously a pewee, based on shape and size, long wing projection and song ruled out Empidonax type flycatcher.

Resources Used

Warmed up listening to EAWP song (Stokes) on the way to the site.  Bird was extremely cooperative in terms of visibillty and spontaneous song, photographing and recording confirmed our initial impressions.

Previous Experience

Originally from the east coast, return there regularly when birds are on territory.  Lots of experience with Western Wood-Pewee in Colorado, very common summer visitor in Denver area and at our property on Fremont/Custer border.
