Description of the Bird

First located due to song. General pewee size and structure (Eastern/Western Wood Pewee, that is).
Watched bird for about 10 minutes, sang about 10 times. Pink mandible from beneath with small black tip. Upperparts grayish washed in green, paler and brighter than typical Western WP.
Wing bars near white, brighter/whiter than those of Western WP. Underparts with reduced and paler vest on sides of chest compared with Western WP. In field, vest seemed not to connect centrally, separated by narrow line of yellowish wash. In photos, vest seems to just cross chest. Remainder of belly and chest washed in pale yellow. Undertail coverts plain or vaguely smudged with gray. Throat at times whitish, at other times appeared lightly washed in yellow.
Behavior: Flycatching from high up in medium to large trees
Call: pee-EE-oo-whee was usual vocalization, Also pee-ooo (with 2nd syllable trailing off). No buzzy quality to songs.
Plumage: not determined

Similar Species Discussion

Western Wood-Pewee eliminated by song. Visual characters also more consistent with Eastern, rather than Western, Wood-Pewee
