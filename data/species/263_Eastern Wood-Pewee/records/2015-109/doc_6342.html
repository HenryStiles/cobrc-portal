Description of the Bird

Originally heard giving diagnostic upwardly slurred "peeeurr" call repeatedly. Followed sound to riparian strip along canal where it was flycatching and perching in some bare branches. Backlighting made appreciation of field marks extremely difficult. The silhouette showed a small flycatcher with long wing extension consistent with a pewee. A brief view of the underparts revealed a lemon wash on the whitish belly, and a whitish throat contrasting weakly with a slightly darker breast. Moderately sized, boat-shaped bill.

Similar Species Discussion

Western Wood Pewee has darker throat and breast, and a more burry quality to call note. Yellow-bellied Flycatcher has darker throat, shorter wings and more obvious upward slur to call note. Least, Willow and Alder Flycatchers have shorter wings and much different call notes. Hammond's Flycatcher has different call note, and much smaller bill.
