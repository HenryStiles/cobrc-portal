Description of the Bird

I walked up to a very small creek with small and medium sized trees on sides/leaning over the creek. at about eye level was a pewee facing me (very long primaries, no eyering, larger than Empid). It had a pale lemon wash on vent and lower belly. No dusky markings on undertail coverts. The chest was whitish with very limited olive wash sides (on chest only, not extending posteriorly). The throat was whitish. The bird went after some prey and returned to same perch. Saw that it had rather green upperparts. It had two white wingbars, rather bright, and nearly equal in size/intensity. There was white edging to tertials. Lower mandible was entirely pink. I had in view for 45 seconds. Sun was mostly to my back. Gave a pu-weee call several times, second note upslurred. Clear. Not even vaguely "buzzy" or "hoarse"

Similar Species Discussion

Western Wood-Pewee. Call eliminates WEWP. Also, visually, all marks align with Eastern (wingbars, no vent markings, green back, all pinkish mandible)
