Description of the Bird

Pewee by size, long primaries. Paler and greener above than nearby WEWP and WEWP in general. Pale beneath washed very lightly in yellow. Very limited olive sides to chest. Wingbars equal and appeared white. Bill all pink below. Hint of eyering, particularly posteriorly. No markings on undertail coverts. Similar size to nearby WEWP. After they squabbled and settled 50 or so feet apart EAWP gave thin whistled peee-ee. Clear no burry quality Also short pu-wee, again, without any burry qualities. The bird looked startingly like pewee number 2 at http://www.alanmurphyphotography.com/tax%20list/tyrant%20flycatchers/eastern%20wood%20pewee.htm 

excepting the eyering, which looked like pewee number 5.

Similar Species Discussion

The calls were the only "marks" that one would consider definitive. However, the suite of marks - pale beneath with very limited olive, greenish backed, white wingbars, hint of eyering - when all present, is very suggestive of Eastern Wood-Pewee
