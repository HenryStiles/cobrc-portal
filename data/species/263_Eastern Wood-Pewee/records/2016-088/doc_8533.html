Description of the Bird

We first heard the bird giving a Pee-oo-weee clear song typical of Eastern Wood-Pewee. After hearing the song several times, we set out in pursuit and saw a flycatcher, small-medium in size (larger than Empid, smaller than Myiarchus) with fairly bright olive-green back, crown, auriculars. No eyering. Primary projection long. Two moderate white wingbars of equal intensity. Bill shaped like that of a wood-pewee, entirely orange-pink below. Underparts mostly a fairly clean pale wash of lemon yellow with some dusky olive wash on sides of chest only. Undertail coverts unmarked. Bird gave at least one song while being watched, then flew off. We heard song and Peee-ooo call a couple more times. Bird did not respond to either Eastern or Western WP recordings (nor pishing, etc)

Similar Species Discussion

Underpart pattern, underbill color, wingbars = and relatively bright white all point towards Eastern WP. Song definitive. Back color also suggestive of Eastern
