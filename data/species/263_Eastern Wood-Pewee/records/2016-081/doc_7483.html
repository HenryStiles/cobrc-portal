Description of the Bird

I stopped to look at a pewee on a wire alongside the road. By pewee, I mean smallish but not Empid-small flycatcher, with long wings and overall olive-greenish coloration. The belly was pale yellowish green fading to whitish chest and throat. Slight olive smudging on sides of chest. Mandiple entirely pinkish-orange. No markings noted on vent/undertail coverts. Maxilla black. Bill relatively long and narrow. Wingbars moderate intensity and white, with upper and lower fairly equal in intensity. No eyering. Back moderately green. The bird was initially in poor light, when it was heard to call peee-weeee, without any burriness. Later, it sang PEE-oo-weee twice, again clear, "thin" notes without any burriness. No tail wagging or wing flicking. 

Similar Species Discussion

Vocalizations eliminate all other species. Lack of eyering, as well, eliminates Empids excepting "Traill's". Bird larger, longer billed, longer winged than Traill's. Eastern Phoebe different bill, darker head, no wingbars (or trace). Western Wood-Pewee has distinctly burrier song, and even the relatively clear call is more burry than what this bird uttered. Western Wood-Pewees tend to be duller above, more olive below, have markings on undertail coverts, some black on mandible, weaker wingbars with upper wingbar dull compared to lower.
