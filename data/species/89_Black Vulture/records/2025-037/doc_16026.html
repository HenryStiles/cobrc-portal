Description of the Bird

Vulture with all dark head and reduced nostrils vs the beak with large nostrils of a TUVU. Adult plumage.

Similar Species Discussion

Initially assumed to be and was identified as first year TUVU until further inspection of the photo that illustrates the reduced nostrils.  

Previous Experience

 BLVUs were actually my spark bird in New Jersey! They regularly roosted in my backyard, and occasionally visited the back deck. I volunteer with a raptor organization doing educational outreach and work with a TUVU there. Young TUVUs also have a dark head and are often confused for BLVU, so my expectation was that this was a first year TUVU in the short glimpse period. As you will see from the media, the vantage point was difficult to determine a BLVU rather than a first year TUVU and the bird flew off without me getting a look at the posture difference or the wing markings to show the silvery patch. This took careful beak review and a sanity check with the Raptor ID group.
