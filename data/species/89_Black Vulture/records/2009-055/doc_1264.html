Description of the Bird

The bird was found roosting in a pine tree with seven other Turkey Vultures. I located the bird because of the short tail relative to the Turkey Vultures. The bird's head was gray/dark. The distal half of the bill was light compared to the proximal half. The rest of the bird's plumage was black except for the lighter gray legs and the the lighter/whitish bases of the first few primary feathers. The black, short, square tail was barely visible beyond the thickness of the branch it was roosting on. One or two primaries were noticed extending beyond the tail at rest.
Behaviors: The bird was observed roosting and preening the entire time.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Definative basic

Similar Species Discussion
