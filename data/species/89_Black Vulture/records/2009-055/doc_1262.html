Description of the Bird

The bird was clearly a vulture, flying with nearby Turkey Vultures. It was quite distant, so the first clue to its identity was the rapid wing flapping with stiff wings, quite unlike the lazy flapping of Turkey Vulture. The bird was all black, except for large white patches at the base of the primaries. The tail was very noticeably shorter than the tails on Turkey Vultures, and the wings were shorter and broader.
The head was dark and not red; the color of the Turkey Vulture heads could be seen at this distance.
Behaviors: The bird was seen flapping and soaring in the company of Turkey Vultures as it rose in the sky.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The only other NA vulture is Turkey Vulture. This bird was shaped differently with a short tail and broad wings. It had prominent white patches at the base of the primaries, and no silver wing linings. The head was dark, not red. It also flapped quickly on very stiff wings, as opposed to the lazy flapping of the Turkey Vultures present.
