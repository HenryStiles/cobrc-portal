Habitat

Riparian, mostly deciduous urban park with trees and bushes.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikin 10X42 binoculars
Distance:  Approximately 12-25 feet; the bird was active and moved often.
Light:  Partly cloudy.
The bird was in fairly good light in spite of partially overcast skies. Light mostly from west, behind me at times , and at times came from side.

Description of the Bird

Gray above with broad bright yellow wingbar;
Black eye-line;
Large bright yellow cap;
White/very light gray throat
Light gray below;
Yellow patch on chest
Behaviors: Small, active bird, gleaning, flitting from branch to branch in deciduous trees. Seemed to be picking insects.
Call: Breezz-breez; heard calling one, in flight from one tree to another.
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Golden-winged Warbler was eliminated because this bird did not have a the dark ear patch; it did not have a black throat (had a white/pale gray throat).
Blue-winged Warbler was eliminated because this bird was whitte/pale gray below; it had broad bright yellow wingbar and a yellow patch on white chest.
