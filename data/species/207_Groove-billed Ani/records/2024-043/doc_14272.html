Description of the Bird

Found by me, Lorraine Lanning. I initially found the bird when I rounded the SE corner of the lake, perched on top of a willow. Followed it along the willows, up on a fence, then along the trail, taking phone photos for documentation. Stayed with the bird while I got the info out, then ran home for my camera, went back, other birders arrived, bird was refound.

Foraging in and out of willows along trail mainly, eating grasshoppers. Another birder played the call & it responded. Not particularly skittish, frequently within feet of m.o.b.

Similar Species Discussion

Knew it wasn't a Black-billed Magpie, no white. Also considered Common Grackle briefly, of which there were many, but knew it wasn't because it was larger & the tail was longer. Seeing the distinctive forehead slope into the large bill made ID pretty quick.

Previous Experience

I've only seen one before, the one in Adams County a few years ago and those weren't good looks or photos. We frequently visit TX so I'm aware of them and on the lookout but haven't seen one there yet.
