Description of the Bird

An all-black grackle-like bird, at least as large as GTGR, with very long, graduated tail with only 10 rects. Black head had bare, dark gray skin around, particularly behind, eyes. Bill was short and deep with at least two grooves near culmen. Wings were somewhat short and quite rounded. Bird was obviously partway through its prebasic molt, with both new and old remiges and rectrices. As flight-feather molt in anis (and lots of other cuckoos) is fairly eccentric (see Pyle 1997), this bird's state of feather replacement is not odd.


Pyle, P. 1997. Identification Guide to North American Birds, part I. Slate Creek Press, Bolinas, CA.

Similar Species Discussion

The only other real ID contender is Smooth-billed Ani, which is exceedingly unlikely to occur in CO. However, that species can easily be ruled out by the bird's the obvious grooves in the maxilla. If the bird is a youngster, then overall bill shape may not be all that useful in differentiation.
