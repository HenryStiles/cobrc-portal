Description of the Bird

Went looking for reported Herring Gull. Found this instead. Yellow legs and feet, dark back, red gonys. A bit larger than adjacent RBGUs and similar in size to 3 CAGU nearby. Asked Tony Leukering for advice because everything fit LBBG except the head color - not dark around eye with minimal streaking on head and neck. He mentioned it could be entering pre-alternate molt. May be first county record, hence this report.

Similar Species Discussion

The size of the bird elimates larger dark-mantled gulls. The mantle is too dark for Herring Gull. 

Resources Used

Sibley book and phone apps, also spoke with Tony Leukering because the bird's head color was lighter than I'd expect in mid winter. 

Previous Experience

Not much in recent years, since SW Colorado has less gull diversity than anywhere i've ever lived.
