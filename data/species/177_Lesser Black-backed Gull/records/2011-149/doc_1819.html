Description of the Bird

This bird was a darker mantled gull which immediately separated it from the nearby Herring and Ring-billed Gulls. The bird was about 20 inches long. It was smaller than the nearby Herring Gulls and larger than the nearby Ring-billed Gulls. It had a white head with a few dusky spots developing near the nape and neck. This is why I believe the bird was in transition to the winter adult plumage. Breast, belly and tail white. Mantle was dark gray. Wings dark grayish brown with a white spot near the ends of the folded primaries. Bill was relatively long compared to the head. It was yellow with a red spot on the lower mandible. Feet and legs were yellow. Eye yellow.

In flight, we again observed the dark mantle and wings upper sides.
Behaviors: Standing, sleeping, walking, flying. On occasion, a larger Herring Gull would pick at it as a sign of dominance.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Adult transition to winter

Similar Species Discussion

Herring Gull, California Gull, Ring-billed and others all have lighter mantles. Greater Black backed is darker mantled and much bigger. Slaty-backed Gull is much larger. Laughing Gull is smaller and has black feet. Yellow-footed Gull and Western Gull are much larger than this bird.
