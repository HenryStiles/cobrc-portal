Description of the Bird

This bird was first seen by me at the RMBO banding station at Chico Basin Ranch. It foraged on the ground at the edge of the Russian olives and plantation of mature Siberian elms. At first I was convinced that it was a Veery because of the reddish coloration of its back. Because I had no photos of a Veery, I tried to obtain some. When I looked at the bird in my scope, I realized that it was not a Veery and was not sure which Catharus thrush it was.

Here is what I saw (see all three photos)

Back, wings and tail rufous brown. Not contrast between tail color and back color. Face browinsh with complete light buffy eyering and buffy spectacle, less distinct than on other races of SWTH. Undertail coverts ligthly washed with buff. Breast washed light buff with distinct small medium brown spots. Flanks gray buff, but not gray as in Veery. Base of primarys show the most rufous and there is a dark bar there. Bill is short, with a pinkish base to the mandible and a black tip.
Behaviors: Feeds on ground with no pumping of wings or tail. Perches in cover when i approach it.
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush - eliminated after looking on a thrush website that showed C.g.faxoni race with rufous back but with bright orange tail. Also this bird never flicked it wings or pumped its tail.

Veery - eliminated by bird having distinct eyering and lacing the distinct gray flanks of Veery. Breast was not warm enough for Veery

C. u. swainsoni and C.u.almae eliminated on back color, the above two subspecies having olive colored backs

C.u. oedicus, the other "russet-backed" thrush has less rich rufous on back and is therfore paler or grayer on the dorsal parts.
