Description of the Bird

Nick Moore, Jane, and I had been circling Jane’s property chasing a small herd of thrushes amongst other birds. Predominantly, the thrushes were of the expected Olive-backed (Swainson’s) type. We had kicked up a single Veery that perched in the open for 15 or 20 seconds before diving back into cover. On one circuit, a thrush landed close to me, fairly low in a tree. It was obviously a Swainson’s (buffy face/eyering) but I could also see that it had buff-brown flanks, quite different from the distinctly pure gray flanks of the Olive-backeds. The bird then dropped into the upper branches of a shrub. The back was obviously warmly colored, more like that of a Veery than an Olive-backed Thrush. I called out to Nick and Jane who were unable to see the bird until it flew. When the bird flew, it went in such a direction that the sun was directly behind us. It went into a bush that several Olive-backed Thrushes had just flown into at a similar angle. The difference between the rust-brown back of the Russet-backed and the dull olive-brown backs of the Olive-backeds was rather obvious. I watched the bird at about 15 feet for about 60 seconds.

The bird was of general size and structure an Olive-backed Thrush. Its back was rusty, somewhat reminiscent of a Veery. The bird had a complete eyering that was buffy, as were the auriculars. The chest spotting was blurrier and paler than that of most Olive-backed Thrushes and less extensive. The suffusion of buffy on chest was more prominent than that of typical OB Thrushes. The flanks were distinctly buffy-brown rather than mousy-gray. Overall, this caused the Russet-backed Thrush to look far more uniform than an Olive-backed, which to me looks like an olive-gray bird with distinctly contrasting buff auriculars and suffusion of buff on upper chest.

Similar Species Discussion

These taxa have a very limited hybridization zone, with different calls and songs. I had the fortune of being able to study both in WA, and this bird appeared typical of Russet-backed Thrush. Olive-backed Thrush should have gray flanks, duller colored back, and usually more intense spotting beneath
