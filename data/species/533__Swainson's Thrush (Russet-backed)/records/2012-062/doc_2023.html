Documentation of Swainson's Thrush (Russet-backed)
2012-062

Observer Information

Reporter:  Jane Stulp  24247 Hwy 287,   LAMAR, CO  81052-9707
E-mail:  jane@stulpfarms.com
Other Observers:  Steve Mlodinow first identified the bird, and both Nick Moore and I observed it on the 13th; I had seen a reddish Swainson's but Steve positively identified it for me

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Swainson's Thrush (Russet-backed)
First Date/Time:  5/15/2012
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  1 hour 30 minutes
County:  Prowers
Specific Location:  back yard of the farm house at 24247 Hwy 287, Lamar,CO
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

-pinion pines, landscaping rocks, locust trees, water feature, and grass -yard has the only water for miles around

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10X42 binocs
Distance:  10 to 30 feet
Light:  good light but the bird liked to stay in the shadows and under the trees

Description of the Bird

-Swainson's Thrush with the typical spectacles
-had rufous flanks instead of the olive flanks
-rufous tinged back instead of the grayish back
Behaviors: foraged under the pines, around the rock walls, and occassionally out in the lawn
Call: did not hear
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The Veery was considered because of the rufous coloring, but it had the definite face pattern and spectacles of the Swainson's, and had the rufous colored flanks instead of pale gray

Resources Used

Sibley's and Steve Mlodinow at the time of the observation

Previous Experience

first time observation of the Russet-backed, but have observed olive-backed Swainson's Thrushes, Veerys, and Hermit Thrushes

Notes

Notes made DURING observation

Additional Information

Comments: Most likely two birds were present, but I could not confirm both with a photo nor did I see both at the same time

Elevation: 3800'
