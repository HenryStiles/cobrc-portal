Description of the Bird

David Dowell and I were departing Tempel Grove (to the north) when a bird flew right to left in front of us. Initially thought it was a Wood Thrush as it appeared rather bright rust and of robust size for a thrush. Fortunately, the bird perched on a log about 30 feet from road in bright shade, which provided excellent lighting (sun to our backs). The upperparts were uniform and rusty, not as bright as that of a Wood Thrush, but appeared to be as bright as a Veery or nearly so. No olive hues noted. The bird had weak buffy spectacles, a lightly spotted buff-washed chest and buffy flanks. This gave the bird an overall somewhat uniform appearance. It sat in the open, seemingly watching us, changing position once or twice, for nearly a minute, allowing for David to locate the bird and also have a goodly view. Then it flew off into the deeper brush.

Similar Species Discussion

Veery would lack buff on face, chest and flanks, have heavier spotting. Olive-backed Thrush would lack rusty back, have brighter eyering, more dense spotting beneath, grayish flanks.
