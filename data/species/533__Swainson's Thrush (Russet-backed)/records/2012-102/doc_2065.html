Description of the Bird

I was walking at Andrick Ponds SWA and, amongst dozens of Olive-backed (Swainson's) Thrushes noted a bird that I thought might be a Veery due to its rusty back. I lifted my camera, and the bird flew TOWARDS me, landing above me and at about 20 feet. The lighting was fair, in bright shade. The flanks looked buffy, spotting weakish for a Swainson's Thrush but heavier than such on a Veery, and the face was buffy, typical of Swainson's Thrushes (both subspecies groups). I snapped a few photos, and the bird flew off and could not be relocated. I had it in view for about 30 seconds.
Behaviors: See above. Originally feeding on ground, in shade, with OB Thrushes (and there was also a Veery in the area that I'd flushed up shortly beforehand).
Call: not heard

Similar Species Discussion

A buffy cheeked thrush with no rust on tail (this bird's tail was not contrastingly rusty as in Hermit) is pretty much a Swainson's Thrush. Olive-backed Thrush is eliminated by the rusty back color, buffy flanks, and lighter chest spotting. The intergrade zone is very narrow, but intergrades do occur, and a seemingly excellent example of such can be seen in my Flicker page. Sorry, but this website won't let me enter the URL.
