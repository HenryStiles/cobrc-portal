Description of the Bird

Swainson's Thrushes were everywhere at Crow Valley that morning, and among them, several Veery. David Dowell and I flushed up a bird that we thought was a Veery as it flew away based on it rusty upperpart color (in distinct contrast with two Olive-backed Thrushes that flushed up with it). We saw the bird land, and to our surprise, it had a buffy face with a weak buffy eyering. The spotting on the chest was more restricted and diffuse than that of Olive-backed Thrush, with a healthy wash of buff across chest. The sides had a buff wash to them.
Behavior: As with other thrushes present
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Buffy face and wash across chest eliminates all other taxa of Catharus thrushes except Olive-backed (Swainson's) Thrush which has grayish flanks and olive-brown, not rusty brown, upperparts. Veery eliminated by buffy cheek/eyering, buffy flanks
