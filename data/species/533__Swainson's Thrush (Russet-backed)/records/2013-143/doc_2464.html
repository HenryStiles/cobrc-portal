Description of the Bird

Mark Peterson and I were at the head of a group of about 8 people. He had seen a GC Thrush (already submitted), and we were working a mixed flock of thrushes trying to track it down, separated to some extent. I had flushed up a Veery, which we were also now also searching for. A rusty backed thrush popped up in front of me at about 15 feet, in clear view, and excellent light. I initially assumed it was going to be the Veery, but saw the bright buffy flanks, and realized I was wrong. I called out "Russet-backed Thrush," and Mark Peterson and Dan Maynard, who were close to me, were quickly on the bird. We watched it for 30 seconds. It shifted positions once or twice.
The bird was rusty dorsally with the color concolorous from crown through tail. The rust was similar to that of a bright western Veery. The face was buff and the upper chest was washed in buff. There was a weak buffy eyering. The flanks were buffy. The chest spotting was stronger than that of a Veery, but more limited and diffuse than that of a Olive-backed Thrush. The build was similar to that of an Olive-backed Thrush.
