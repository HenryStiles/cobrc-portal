Description of the Bird

Proportioned like many buteos, perhaps on the smallish side (but difficult to ascertain without direct comparison with another buteo.) Overall brownish tones on back with some scattered whitish gray feather tipping. Tail with wider brown bands interspersed with thinner grayish bands. Primaries fall well short of the end of the tail on the perched bird. Belly white with medium-heavy brownish spotting, growing heavier on breast (but not a solid bib.) Head mostly brownish with pale gray supercillium. Eye pale brown, with yellowish cere and moderately heavy bill (not as stout as Ferruginous but not as delicate-looking as Rough-legged.) Tarsi bare, yellowish. In flight, a pale cinnamon wash pervades the wing lining as seen from below, and a pale crescent crosses at the base of the primaries on the upperwing. The pale crescent grades from whitish on the bases of the outer primaries to more pale cinnamon on the inner primary bases.
Behaviors: The bird was flying from perch to perch along the Poudre River, spending a few minutes on each perch (tall cottonwoods.)
Call: Heard calling in response to Red-tailed Hawk flying overhead. Richer, distinctive descending calls contrasted with more shrill Red-tail call I often hear.
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Very similar to several other juvenile Buteos including Red-tailed when perched but shorter winged, longer tailed, and juv. Red-tailed have paler eyes. In flight, peachy wash to wing lining and pale crescent at bases of primaries seen on upperwing diagnostic of this species.
