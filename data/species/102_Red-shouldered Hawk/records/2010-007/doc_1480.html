Description of the Bird

The bird was a medium-sized Buteo, intermediate in size between Red-tailed and Broad-winged Hawks. It was seen briefly in flight and its’ wings were a bit longish, longer than that of a Broad-winged. When perched, the bird looked less stocky than a Red-tailed and it had a longer-appearing tail. The upperparts were a dark brown overall, except for scattered white feathers across the scapulats and coverts. The primaries were a dark brown and the secondaries were brown interrupted with tan barring. The primary tips extended to near the tip of the tail, but the tail was still longer. The tail was rectangular when perched and displayed 4-5 dark brown bands separated by light tan-whitish bars. The terminal tip of the tail was whitish. The head was rounded and dark brown with a thin, light supercilum and the bill was relatively short and deeply hooked on the upper mandible. The irides were yellow. From the front, the bird was basally whitish-cream with dark, tear-dropped shaped streaking throughout.
Behaviors: The bird was noted briefly in flight flying low through the cottonwoods. It then perched along the side of a cottonwood for the remainging length of my observation.
Call: None heard
Plumage: 1st cycle (Second Year)

Similar Species Discussion

Differed in size between Red-tailed and Broad-winged Hawks. Juvenile Red-taileds generally show a more extensive white V across the scapulars, have a gelling of dark brown across the belly, and lack the even streaking across the breast and belly of a Red-shouldered Hawk. Juvenile Broad-wings have an alldark head, have a mostly white throat, chest, and belly except for a few thick streaks across the upper side breast. Juvenile Broad-wings also have shorter wings when folded and have less diagnostic barring across the tail.
