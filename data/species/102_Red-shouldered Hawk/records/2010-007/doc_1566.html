Description of the Bird

The bird was overall fairly small, at least for our usual Buteos. It had a yellow eye. Brown back with white spots and a hint of reddish feathers on the shoulder. The head was mostly brown with a bright white supercillium with a little bit of white spotting on the top and sides of the head. It had even brown streaks on the breast and belly with fairly white undertail coverts. The medium length tail with many bars that was colored with light brown and dark brown. The bill was mostly black with some yellow by the lores. In flight the bird showed buffy crescents on the outer edge of the wings.
Behaviors: Most of the time I saw it just sitting in a tree. We did eventually see it fly through the woods and over the river and land in another tree.
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The small size of the bird eliminates everything except Broad-winged Hawk, Short-tailed Hawk, and Cooper’s Hawk. The length of the tail eliminate Accipiters. The brown back with white spots eliminate everything except juvenile Swainson’s, Red-tailed, and Rough-legged Hawks. The reddish feathers on the shoulder eliminate all other hawks. The white supercillium eliminate all other Buteos. The crescents on the wings eliminate all other hawks.
