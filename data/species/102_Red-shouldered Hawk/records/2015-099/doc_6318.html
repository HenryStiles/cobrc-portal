Description of the Bird


Observations occurred on a CFO field trip to NE Colorado.  Nine observers were present and all saw the bird.  The group was crossing a foot-bridge over the North Sterling Reservoir inlet canal when the bird was first seen flying to and perching on a dead tree stump 50 feet up stream from the bridge.  The bird then flew further into cover.  We approached it from both sides of the canal seeing it flush two additional times into trees until it finally flew over open ground to a more distant wood lot.

In its initial perch, the bird presented a long profile consistent with a bird of prey having a tail longer than folded wings. The bird seemed to be fairly slender (the long profile) and medium sized for a raptor—that is neither eagle nor kite/sharp-shinned hawk sized.

The tail of the perched bird was distinctly barred with narrow black and tan horizontal bars.  Over all the bird was mottled brown with blotchy streaking on the breast that became less dense on the lower under portions.  In flight the bird showed long wings that were distinctly marked with tawny crescents just inside the dark tips of the wings.  The flight pattern was like that of a buteo, not kite, harrier or accipiter.

Photos by Chuck Hundertmark show a bird with no patagial bar, slight reddish wash on the leading edge of the wings between the wrist and body (i.e. the “shoulders”), very dark primary tips with a contrasting tawny crescent more toward the base of the primaries

Similar Species Discussion

Red-tailed Hawk immature--this bird lacked patagial bar.


Mississippi Kite juvenal--this bird lacked extensive black over primaries on under wing, underparts were paler.


Broad-winged Hawk immature--this bird had longer, more slender wings and more contrasting tan and black bars on tail
