Description of the Bird

The following are relevant excerpts from the original Dec 11, 2025 eBird list (https://ebird.org/checklist/S288202947): First view while driving was of a medium sized buteo hunting from powerline directly above the road staring straight down, about to dive on prey. Its moderate size, relatively lanky legs and dark color led me to make an immediate stop on the tight road shoulder about 40 yds away. The hawk then dove on its prey, concealed by rabbitbrush adjacent to the road. I walked up the road shoulder and it flushed when an ATV drove by. Resulting flight photos are going away from me with close shrubs partially blurring foreground and causing overall discoloration to photos. The bird disappeared into cottonwoods to devoir its meal. I believe photos show just enough for positive ID: 4 distinct pale thin tail bands; dark brown uppersides with a hint of rufous shoulders; tawny/buffy undersides; and pure white crescents near wingtips. When photos are viewed in composite the tail width, proportionally large head, and shorter wingspan also all appear correct for RSHA (and not matching RTHA or BWHA).

I observed the hawk in question from both sides, the front and undersides before it dove. It was perched on the powerline directly over the road. This is a low voltage, low clearance powerline, crossing the road diagonally, ~25 feet high at the south shoulder. I observed the hawk as I drove towards it and continuously as I drove under it, the closest view was from about 30 feet. It was out of my view after I drove past it for about 40 yards to pull over. Then I observed it again on the powerline in binoculars when I exited my vehicle for just a moment before it dove on its prey into the south shoulder shrubs.

From all angles the hawk’s body appeared darker brown and more solid toned than our typical buteos with the front densely mottled giving a semi-solid very dark appearance. Examples of front and back matched these typical immature elegans: https://macaulaylibrary.org/asset/611886176 and https://macaulaylibrary.org/asset/385504001.

Similar Species Discussion

At this location, RSHA is most easily confused with RTHA which is a larger buteo with shorter legs, longer wings, shorter tail, and has different plumage patterns.

Also could be confused with an out of range BHWA, which is similar sized though a bit stockier, has different plumage patterns, and lacks the pale crescent near wingtips.

Hybridization with RTHA was hypothesized from obscure views of initial photo sequence on Dec 11. However, hybridization was easily ruled out from subsequent photos displaying no RTHA characterstics.
