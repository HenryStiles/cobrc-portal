Description of the Bird

I was sitting deeply under trees on the south end of my viewing area.  The bird flew in FROM the south and landed about 30 yards North of me on dead limbs of a tree hanging over a spot in the creek that widens to a tiny pond.  He did nothing but look around in all directions.  He clearly saw me and took off- not panicing, but still very quickly  I managed to get about 7 frames.  

Similar Species Discussion

This bird is rare for my area, so at the sight I considered Red-tail juvenile, and Broad-winged Hawk.  I briefly entertained Coopers - but the bird was too big and bulky...  I decided it had to be a Buteo.  After uploading images, I briefly considered Red-shouldered, due to the slight reddish shoulder, but rejected it due to the range!  I posted on Facebook Raptor ID and it was confirmed to be a Red-shouldered!

Previous Experience

I have seen Adult Red-shouldered Hawks in Both Florida and South Texas - but never in my home county (Prowers) Colorado
