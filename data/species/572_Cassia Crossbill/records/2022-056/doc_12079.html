Description of the Bird

Maggie Boswell and I met Duane Nelson and John Drummond, and Duane took us to the spot where they had heard and seen Cassia Crossbills.  Duane left after about 30 minutes during which time we had heard and seen many (mainly type 2) Red Crossbills.  About 10 minutes later, Maggie recorded on the Merlin app a group of crossbills that sounded quite different (sharper and faster calls) that Merlin identified as Cassia.  This recording is attached to this report.  I saw the birds making the sounds as they flew in, and it looked like a group of 4 birds and I definitely saw a female and a juvenile bird through my binoculars.  At that distance, I couldn't see any features on the birds that would distinguish them from Red Crossbills. 

Similar Species Discussion

Red Crossbills were eliminated by the different calls that the Merlin app identified as Cassia.  The birds did not have white in their wings, so they were not White-winged Crossbills.

Resources Used

Maggie and I used our ears to hear the different calls, and the Merlin sound app at the site.  On ebird, I compared the spectrograph of the sound recording with those of other Cassia Crossbills, and they looked identical to me. https://ebird.org/checklist/S117165010

Previous Experience

Cassia Crossbill was a new species for me.  I have seen and heard Red Crossbills many times in CO, and White-winged a few times.
