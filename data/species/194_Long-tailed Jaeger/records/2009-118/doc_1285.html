Description of the Bird

Small jaeger with all-dark, gray wings and white belly. Long tail streamers noted.
Behaviors: A large (Pomarine) jaeger finished off a meal of Franklin's Gull and flew off to clean itself further off shore. As it flew away and landed, it flushed a previously unobserved (you don't look away often from a jaeger that is 15 feet away as the Pomarine was while eating) second jaeger. The second bird was much smaller, showed long tail streamers and a much more bouyant flight.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

smaller with more bouyant flight than the large jaeger that caused it to take flight along with long tail streamers (visible in photos) made this an exceptionally easy jaeger call

photos, Nat Geo

Previous Experience

3-4 recent observations of LTJA, but none in this plumage. Adults seen on breeding ground in Alaska 30 years ago.
