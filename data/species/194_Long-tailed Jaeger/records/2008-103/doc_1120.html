Description of the Bird

Dark brown, long-winged, gull-like bird about the size of a Ring-billed Gull, with strongly barred vent, scaly appearance to upperparts due to pale edges of scapulars, gray legs and feet, pale gray bill with black outer half; in flight, whitish spots at base of rectrces and primaries, elongated central rectrices, white outer webs of outermost two primaries in flight, strong white stripe across base of primaries in the underwing.
Behaviors: Sitting on water, flying short distances
Call: none
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Both Parasitic and Pomarine Jeagers (and skuas) have more extensive white in primaries
