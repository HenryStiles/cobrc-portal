Description of the Bird

the bird was slightly but noticeably smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls, with a similar shape, but slightly more slender. This was accentuated by the relatively longer and thinner wings and the longer tail. The bird had a relatively small head for a jaeger. The bill quite short and relatively thicker than expected for a gull but thinner that expected for the larger jaegers. The basal half of the bill was pale bluish-gray in color, the distal half was black. The bird had a shallow but noticeable gonydeal angle slightly closer than half way to the tip of the bill. The irides were dark.

The upperparts of the bird were on the dark end of medium brown, with a slight but noticeable gray tinge, especially on the head and neck. The head and neck were also slightly paler than the back, especially the nape (at least in certain lights, which gave the appearance of a "helmet" of darker brown on the head). The feathers on the mantle were lightly tipped in white. There was a paler patch of feathers on the uppertail coverts, though I didn't see this feature well since I only saw the bird in flight briefly. The tail was dark, with the two central tail feathers noticeably longer, but blunt tipper. The outer two most primaries had bright white feather shafts; the next four or so inward from those had a bit of white at the base of the shaft, but not nearly so bright or as obvious as those on the outer two feathers. The feathers themselves were the same medium-dark brown as the rest of the wing.

The undersides of the wing were boldly checked with small white patches on the otherwise dark brown wing. The base of the outer primaries had a bold white patch near their base.

The throat and upper chest of the bird were the same medium brown color of the head, which gave the bird a hooded look when contrasted with the noticeably pale brown of the belly. The undertail coverts were boldly and neatly barred with dark brown on the otherwise pale coverts.
Behaviors: The bird spent pretty much all of its time on the water quite close to the observers; I only saw it stretch its wings once and briefly take flight one other time.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile intermediate morph

Similar Species Discussion

Species other than Jaegers were ruled out by the distinctive shape - gull like but more slender, with relatively longer and thinner wings, as well as the thick bill with a pale blue base and bulbous dark tip, the elongated central tail feathers, boldly barred undertail, boldly marked underwing, etc.

Parasitic Jaeger was ruled out by the grayish tones of the brown on the bird, without any hint of the buffy to rufous tones expected on a juvenile Parasitic. In addition, the smaller bill, relatively smaller and more rounded head, the gonydeal angle near the middle of the bill, the boldly barred undertail coverts, the two bright white outer primary shafts and no patch of white on the upper surface at the base of the outer primaries, and the overall more slender shape ruled out this species.

Pomarine Jaeger was ruled out by the smaller size, by the lack of a white patch at the upper surface of the outer primaries, by the two white primary shafts on the outer primaries, by the overall grayish tone to the bird, by the smaller bill with gonydeal angle near the middle, and by the boldly barred undertail coverts.
