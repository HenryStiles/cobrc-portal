Description of the Bird

A dark looking jaeger, juvenile plummage from the lack of tail extensions, grayish-brown mottled color overall, with no clear cap. Whitish fringes to the mantle feathers, 2 visible white primary shafts seen in the opened upper-wing, dar and white checkered tail coverts and underwing coverts, short bill with half black tip and nail, and gonydeal angle at mid-point point of the lower mandible.
Behaviors: floating in a lake, riding high in the water, occasionally flying short distances
Call: none detected
Plumage: intermediate juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

small size, short bill and 2 visible white primary shafts as well as buoyant flight help eliminate Pomarine Jaeger

white edges to mantle feathers, no rufous tones, bill size and porportion and 2 visible white primary shafts help to eliminate Parasitic Jaeger

Additional Information

Comments: Comments from Leukering, I feel are important considering his experience with jaegers...

"I don't see any probs with this being a LTJA. A PAJA of this overall coloration would almost certainly have cinnamon or, at least, buffy fringes, rather than the white to whitish-gray of the bird pictured. Additionally, the primary tips are fringed (another LT point), the bill is, as you say, perfect, and it rides very high in the water. I can also detect only two truly white primary shafts on either wing -- beware that many of the other primary shafts appear whitish, but the color is off-white at best and doesn't extend so far down the shaft as it does on the outer two."
