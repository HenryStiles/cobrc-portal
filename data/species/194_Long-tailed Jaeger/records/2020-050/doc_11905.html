Description of the Bird

My brother and I first saw this bird while walking on the exposed shoreline on the north side of the reservoir. It was resting on the water about 150 feet offshore. Immediately, we realized it was a jaeger. After a few minutes the bird was flushed by a motorboat and took off. It proceeded to fly around the NE corner of the reservoir with periodic rests on the water during the remainder of our visit. This was a medium-sized bird reminiscent of a tern or jaeger. It had a relatively short bill. The tail was impressively long, even when sitting on the water. It had a cream-colored neck and a pale grayish back contrasting with slightly darker wings. In flight, we noted two whitish shafts in the primaries.

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was separate from Parasitic and Pomarine Jaegers because of its relatively dainty, almost tern-like appearance and flight style (definitely not the powerful wingbeats of other jaeger species), well-defined black cap, grayish back with slightly darker wings, and very long tail. 

Resources Used

We consulted several online resources as well as field guides to confirm our initial suspicion that this was a Long-tailed.  

Previous Experience

We have limited experience with jaegers in Colorado and off the Panamanian coast.
