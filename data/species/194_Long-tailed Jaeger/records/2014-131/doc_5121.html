Description of the Bird


I was birding alone in the morning of Sept 5th, at Sailboard launching area, when I spotted a jaeger flying around way out on the reservoir.  Since I could tell it was some juvenile plumaged jaeger, I informed cobirds and other birders, who might be able to come and try to see the bird, and help figure out what it is.  The bird stayed way out, when other people came to look at it.  Margie Joy and Bill Maynard were first able to see it, way out from the South Shore Marina.  Later Bill Maynard and I went over to North Picnic Road and got lucky, when the bird flew in close enough for Bill to get a couple of good photos showing the upper wing.  A little later John Drummond, Joe Roller, and David Gillihan were able to see the bird from a distance.  After they left, I met up with Bill Maynard and Lynne Miller, who were watching the bird from a good distance away.  I went home, and downloaded my photos, and then Bill Maynard sent his around, and they were good enough to tell that the bird was an Intermediate juvenile Long-tailed Jaeger, confirmed by Tony Leukering.  Mark Peterson and Glenn Walbek were coming to see the bird, so I went to back out to North Picnic Road, and waited for them.  The jaeger came very close at this time, after 5pm, and great photos were taken by all of us.  The wing pattern, bill size, etc. all point to this bird being a Long-tailed Jaeger, please see photos.

Similar Species Discussion

The jaeger was smallish, so, Pomarine seemed ruled out quite easily.  Parasitic has a different upper wing pattern.

Resources Used

I looked at Sibley's Guide later on, to see the difference between all three juvenile jaegers.

Previous Experience

I saw all three species of jaegers in June in Alaska, though these were adult birds.  Juvenile jaegers are hard, so I'm happy good photos were obtained.
