Description of the Bird

the bird was a larid a bit smaller than a Ring-billed Gull. The overall shape was similar to a Ring-billed Gull, but with proportionally longer and more slender wings. The tail was also a bit longer, and the overall shape was more slender than that typical of a gull. The bill appeared quite small, though given the distance of the observation this was hard to discern for certain.

The overall color of the bird was dark choclatey brown. The underwings had some white checking and a large white flash at the base of the primaries. I could not see any white at all on the upperwing; given the distance I would not expect to be able to discern white primary shafts.

The uppertail coverts had some white checking; given the distance I think the white checking must have been at least somewhat extensive to be visible. The tail was slightly wedge shaped, and the two central tail feathers stuck out a little bit.
Behaviors: The bird flew with a buoyant, tern like flight; the overall impression of the bird, both on the water and in the air, was of a slender, small jaeger.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal dark phase

Similar Species Discussion

Larid species other than jaegers were ruled out by the overall dark brown choclatey color, the elongated central tail feathers, the white flash and white checkering in the underwing, the long, slender wings, and the overall more slender shape than most gulls.

Pomarine Jaeger was ruled out by the small size, the more slender look, the smaller bill, the lack of a double flash below, the tern-like flight, the lack of a white flash in the upper wings, and overall more evenly dark coloration.

Parasitic Jaeger was ruled out by the small size, slender shape, tern-like flight, the white uppertail coverts (which a Parasitic juvenal this dark shouldn't show), and lack of a white flash on the upperwing.

Resources Used

Sibley was consulted after the observation for some of the specifics on how to rule out Parasitic Jaeger.

Previous Experience

I have seen a number of Long-tailed Jaegers before, including the bird at Prewitt in pretty much the same plumage as this one. I have seen a number of Parasitic and Pomarine Jaegers.
