Description of the Bird

I saw this bird from a distance perched and in flight but I didn't have a chance to study it before it flew. I asked Mark Peterson who found the bird and digiscoped it if I could submit his photograph as most of the record. Therefore the information provided here is mostly what I see in the photograph plus a little of what I saw when the bird flew.

Small, slim, short-bodied jeager, pale morph. Cold coloration. Head very light gray contrasting with slightly darker breast band and paler belly. Dorsally two-toned with gray back and darker gray primaires (seen in flight and perched). Bill two-toned on a small head. Strong barring on distal third of ventral surface.

In flight wings were slender. I did not see any wing flashes but I was using binoculars and not a spotting scope.
Behaviors: Perched in very shallow water where midges were hatching and upon which it was feeding. In flight it flew directly away at a fast clip and disappeared in the direction of the Antero Reservoir Dam.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Both Parasitic and Pomerine jaegers are larger with warm tones. Parasitic has a wider, less compressed hind body. Pomerine and Parasitic juveniles show orangish tones and Pomerine is massive in comparison to this slender Long-tailed Jaeger.
