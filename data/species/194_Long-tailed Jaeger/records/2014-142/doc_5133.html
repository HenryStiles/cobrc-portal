Description of the Bird

Dark gray mantle with pale gray fringing. Head and chest solid gray. Belly heavily barred in dark and white, dark more prominent down to vent where white stripes became a bit broader. Bill smallish. Mostly dark silvery gray with small black tip. In flight. White along two primary shafts only. In flight, chest and head dark and belly more evenly barred (that is, less apparent contrast between belly and vent in flight than when on water). Blackish secondary bar contrasting with gray upperwing. Bird smaller than RB Gull, with slim appearance. However, when it flew by gulls, the RB Gulls as well as Franklin's panicked, but returned swiftly when jaeger angled away. Also, in flight, short central tail projection noted, but shape not discerned. Underwing with modest pale flash, but white only along outer two primary shafts

Similar Species Discussion

Parasitic tend toward warm brown hues, more primary shafts with white, bulkier, lacks contrasting darker secondaries.
Pomarine huge by comparison, more primary shafts with white, lacks darker secondaries.
