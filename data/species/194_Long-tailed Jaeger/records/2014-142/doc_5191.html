Description of the Bird

The bird, which we immediately recognized as a jaeger, had a slender build, was smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls, and had cold, brownish-gray coloration overall.  It floated high on the water, leaning forward, with a dainty profile much like that of a Sabine's Gull.  The bill was shortish and black tipped.  The head and neck were smoothly colored, with the head being slightly darker than the neck.  The back, scapulars, and wing coverts were all pale-tipped, giving the bird a well-patterned look.  The folded primaries were black with very thin pale tips that could only be seen at very close range.  The undertail coverts were whitish with dark bars.

One white primary shaft and a second fainter shaft could be seen on the uppersides of the wings when the bird flew.  Otherwise, the uppersides of the flight feathers were dark.  A single "white flash" could be seen on the undersides of the wings.  The belly was the palest part of the undercarriage, contrasting significantly with the darker head and chest.  The rump was whitish with dark speckling, contrasting sharply with a black tail band.  A significant projection of central feathers can be seen in photos but was not easily seen in the field.

The above characteristics support our identification as intermediate juvenile Long-tailed Jaeger.

The jaeger was observed on the water most of the time, but it did fly loops around the north and east sides of the lake a few times.  It briefly chased a Forter's Tern but was otherwise solitary.  The jaeger was silent the entire time we observed it.

Similar Species Discussion

Parisitic Jaeger, the most similar species, was eliminated based on the overall build (slender instead of bulky), the coloration of the head and neck (smooth and cold rather than streaked and colorful), the barring on the back / scapulars / coverts (whitish rather than buffy), and the tail projection (substantial rather than short).  Pomarine Jaeger was eliminated based on size (small and slender rather than large and bulky), bill size (slight rather than heavy), and the lack of a double "white flash" on the undersides of the wings.
