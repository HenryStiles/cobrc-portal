Description of the Bird

A smallish and lanky jaeger, the bird was overall medium-dark brown, with a strong gray cast. The face and crown were gray-brown, contrasting with a paler, unstreaked nape; the throat was also slightly paler. The rest of the underparts were uniform in color, except for the undertail coverts, which were strongly barred in black and white, with the bars extending across (from side to side) of that feather tract. The uppertail coverts were quite similar in pattern and coloration to the undertail coverts. The back feathers, scapulars, and upperside wing coverts were all tipped and/or fringed with a pale color that varied from very pale buff to grayish-white depending upon the lighting.

The black primaries were only barely tipped with whitish, not the pattern of buff- or rufous-fringed tips typical of Parasitic Jaeger. The secondaries contrasted strongly with with the upperwing coverts forming a distinct black trailing edge when in flight. The upperwing virtually lacked a white flash at the base of the primaries -- I could just detect a hint of white there. I could not count the number of white primary shafts on the bird, but could certainly tell that it was a relatively small number (<5).

The underwing coverts were tipped/fringed with whitish and the axillar region was barred with black and white. The underside of the outermost two primaries were nearly all dark, creating a dark leading edge to the pale patch created by the paler middle primaries. The rectrices were black and the R1s extended beyond the others a distance about equal to 1/6 to 1/8 of the length of the R2s. The R1s came to a quite rounded point, not the sharp point typical of Parasitic Jaeger.

The bill was fairly short (about equal in length to the distance between the bill base and the eye), relatively thick, and with the distal 40-45% being black, contrasting with the pale base of indeterminate color.
Behaviors: Most of the time that the bird was in view, it was walking/running amongst the smartweed (Polygonum sp.) chasing grasshoppers and other insects. We saw it fly once.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

The only considerations are the other two species of jaeger. Parasitic ruled out by strong gray cast to plumage, color and pattern of under- and uppertail coverts, color of axillars, near lack of primary fringing, near lack of upperwing flash, strong contrast from upperwing coverts to secondaries, R1 shape, and bill shape, length, and pattern. Pomarine ruled out by bill length and shape, lack of white tips to underwing primary coverts forming a second white bar, and the extensive darkness of both P9 and P10.
