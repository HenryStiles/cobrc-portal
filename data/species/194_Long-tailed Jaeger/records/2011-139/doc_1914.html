Description of the Bird

I was on a house boat at Pueblo Reservoir with birders Kelly Shipe and Jeannie Mitchell when I spotted a gull like bird on the water ahead of us. I asked the captain to head toward the bird slowly so we could look at it. When we got closer I thought the bird was probably a jaeger. Closer looks revealed a whitish head, smallish bill, and gray tones to the back, all of which made me think the bird was a Long-tailed Jaeger. See photos from the first date from the boat.
Behaviors: Swimming and flying around on the large Pueblo Reservoir.
Call: none
Plumage: light morph juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Other jaegers are more brown or dark brown, not grayish brown.

Resources Used

National Geographic Birds of North America, later on to make sure we all agreed it was a Long-tailed Jaeger and not one of the others.
