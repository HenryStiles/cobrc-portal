Description of the Bird

Dorsally brownish jaeger with hooked bill and webbed feet. Head noticeably light gray colored contrasting with a broad browinsh throat band. Tail somewhat broad and rounded projecting past folded wingtips (see photo). Breast and belly pale whitish. Wings dark grayish with 2-3 pale primary shafts (see flight photo). Overall cold coloration without any warm rufous colors on any parts. Very broad and and noticeable dark barring on undertail. Back dark with noticeable lighter bars or feather edges creating somewhat of a two-toned appearance (see flight photo). Nothing nearby for comparison but it looked small. Bil with a dark tip, maybe 1/3 of the length.
Behaviors: Loafed on water and flew with buoyant flight circling a large part of a very large reservoir. Said to have been eating small shad (Brandon Percival), but I did not observe this.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Both Parasitic and Pomerine jaegers are larger and always with warmer tones. Pomerine is very large with deep chest and more than 2-3 silvery shafts on the outer primary feathers. Juv Parasitic has noticeable neck streaks that this bird did not have. Light juv Parasitics never have the light head and neck. Parasitic always shows a small head look, described as noticeably triangular in appearance.
