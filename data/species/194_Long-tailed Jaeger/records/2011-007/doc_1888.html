Description of the Bird

The bird was gull-like in appearance about the size of a small Ring-billed Gull. When in flight the wings were brown both above and below and the color of the back. I did not see any white wing patches. Flight was somewhat tern like. The belly was light gray and graded into a very light chest and neck color. The bird had a dark gray cap. In flight a very light chest band was visible only at the shoulders. Black and white barring was visible on the undertail coverts and slightly visible under the wings. The tail had short, rounded, central feathers the were visible in flight. The bill was not stout, dark and somewhat long. The jaeger was a light phase bird.
Behaviors: The jaeger would fly around and them float on the water for a rest. It would then fly around some more.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Adult nonbreeding

Similar Species Discussion

The are three possible jaeger species possible in Colorado. Long-tailed Jaeger is the smallest and most tern like in flight. This jaeger lacks noticable white in outer wings, did not have complete breast band, had unmarked and very light chest and neck and a thin bill.
