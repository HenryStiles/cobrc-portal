Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8x42 bins, 30x WA Kowa scope
Distance:  50 - 500 feet while circling, 100-200 yds while sitting.
Light:  Clear, sunny. As the bird was circling low and close on the north side of us, we had excellent viewing conditions. It landed on a sandy beach a couple hundred yards to our north, again providing good scope views.

Description of the Bird

The bird was clearly of jaeger shape. It was generally dark but heavy patterning and barring was evident on its flanks and interior underwing. Its size was close to or smaller than a Ring-billed Gull. The central retrices extensions were long and rounded and the upper wing showed limited white on the outer primaries.
Behaviors: The bird circled the observers area at close range several times then flew off a couple hundred yards to shore where it landed close to a fisherman. It was not seen harassing gulls or otherwise feeding during the approximate 5 minutes it was seen.
Call: N/A
Plumage: Dark morph juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The bird appeared essentially identical to those photos of this species posted on Cobirds by T. Leukering taken on Sept. 10, 2005 at Prewitt Reservoir.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide To Birds. Used at time of sighting. Was also prepared to watch for the key ID marks ahead of time, knowing the bird was a possibility.

Previous Experience
