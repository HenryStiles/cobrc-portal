Description of the Bird

It was a dark bird, sitting on the water.

Its proportions looked similar to those of a Sabine's Gull, with a petite head and bill.

It had a smooth pattern to its neck (both front and back) and breast, with no streaking.

Its flanks had clean, bold black and white barring.

By direct comparison, it appeared to be 5% smaller than an adjacent Franklin's Gull, while both were sitting on the water.
Behaviors: Sitting on the water.
Call: none heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

The all dark appearance, slender head and neck, and barred undertail coverts ruled out birds other than jaegers.

I ruled out Pomarine Jaeger by the slender head and neck, and especially by the small size (slightly smaller than a Franklin's Gull).

I ruled out Parasitic Jaeger by the bold, clean black and white barring on the undertail coverts, and by the small size (comparison with the Franklin's Gull).

Previous Experience

I had seen numerous Long-tailed Jaegers on California pelagic trips and on their nesting grounds in Alaska. However, I had never studied their immature plumage before.
