Description of the Bird

CBRC Chairman, Mark Peterson posted on cobirds that he had found three jaegers (two long-tailed and a parsitic) at Pueblo Reservoir on the morning of 20 September 2018.  When I arrived in the SE corner of Pueblo Reservoir, afternoon time, Jerry DeBoer and his wife were looking at two jaegers, a lighter smaller one, and a darker bigger one.  The light one, appeared to be a Long-tailed Jaeger.  My photos of it are included. 

Jerry's e-bird description is also included here "Continuing bird. This bird cooperated quite well during my approx. 1 hour stay with some nice close fly-bys as well as on the water within perhaps 150 feet. Several other birders were there capturing images during these fly-by's including Brandon Percival, who I believe was the observer who initially found the birds. Observed through binoculars and 25x scope. In comparison to 2nd jaeger present (Parasitic), this bird was lighter in color. Noticeably smaller and "slighter" in build and profile. The bill had a dark tip comprising nearly half the bill. At times, they swam within several feet of each other so build and size comparison was easy. In flight, this bird had rather long central tail feathers... perhaps an inch and a quarter long."
