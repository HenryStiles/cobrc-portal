Description of the Bird

A jaeger by size and hooked bill plus unique flight. Barred undertail coverts distinctive and extend up towards the flanks. Pale white head = "pale type", dark wing tips from underneath view. Central tail feathers slightly elongated and possibly rounded. Cold coloration, gray, of body, never showing any warm coloration or rusty plumage parts. Breast pale. Primaries with only two silver shafts seen in flight and fairly close. to where I flushed the bird. In flight wings looked bared and not uniformly colored because of pale/dark barring. Chest band, or almost a hind collar seen in flight and separated the light head from the light underparts. Buoyant on water with tail raised. A noticeable blue (possibly plastic) was seen on the upper area of one leg only. Top of wings in flight grayish with darker wing tips. Bill bicolored. Breast was puffed out a bit.
Behaviors: spent a lot of time sitting on the water but it flew, maybe 6 times for up to a minute at a time where it covered a lot of ground. Caused ducks and some gulls to flush but the jaeger did not seem to chase anything.
Call: none heard
Plumage: First winter or juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Parasitic Jager and Pomerine jagers are both warmer in coloration and both usually show a double wing flash and more than two silvery primary shafts unlike this bird. both, but especially POJA very deep chested, aggressive and large. PAJA has different, triangular shaped head and different bill proportions and does not have the white head color phase.
