Description of the Bird

Medium-sized brownish larid. Capped appearance, distinct central tail feather projection, and undertail covert barring typical of young jaeger. About the size of a Ring-billed Gull. Small (for a jaeger) bill, distal half black, proximal half gray. Dark eye completely in brown cap that runs from upper nape to gape. Cheek to lower nape creamy pale brown. Upperparts grayish brown, back feathers tipped with pale brown giving barred appearance. Outer two primary shafts quite white, the rest more uniform dark gray with paler inner webs. Upper- and undertail coverts strongly barred. Spread tail diamond-shaped, with extended inner two recs. Throat mostly same creamy pale brown as nape and cheek, transitioning to darker bib on upper breast. Bib had vertically striped appearance, likely due to oiling or other pollutant rather than actual vertical striping of the feathers. Wing projection long, about even but slightly overhanging tail tip.
Behaviors: Mostly swimming around, picking food from surface (quite similar behavior to a feeding Sabine's Gull.) Occasionally seen on short flights, only to land again and resume feeding. No harassment of gulls seen while I was there.
Call: not heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Smaller than other jaeger species. Bill shorter than in other jaegers. Feeding behavior most typical of Long-tailed. Fewer white primary shafts than other jaegers. Undertail covert barring stronger than seen in Parasitic Jaeger. Less purposeful flight than other jaegers. Overall more slender body than other jaegers. Head appears more rounded than in other jaegers. Relatively longer primary extension than other jaegers.
