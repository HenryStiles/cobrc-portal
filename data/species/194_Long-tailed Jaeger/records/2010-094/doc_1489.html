Description of the Bird

A mottled brown and gray gull-like bird with long wings, slender body, the size of a medium-sized gull, larger than a Laughing Gull but smaller than a Ring-billed Gull. Dark cold-gray upper chest and head; dark brown mantle and dorsal surface of wings (except for bright white centers around the shafts of the outermost one or two primaries; tail blackish with a whitish base; central two rectrices were pointed and extended about an inch beyond other rectrices. Belly pale but not white. Vent and undertail coverts barred black and white. Underwings finely patterned by black and white barring except for a bright white flash at the base of the uniformly blackish primaries. The white shaft of the outer primary visible from below. Bill pale bluish gray with a dark gray tip. Dark eye.
Behaviors: Circling, gliding and flying powerfully over and around a large lake.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Pomarine and Parasitic Jaegers (and all Skuas) would have a much larger white flash at the base of the primaries and more than two white primary shafts visible from above. Pomarine Jaeger and Skuas (South Polar and Great) are much broader winged and heavier-bodied. All gulls lack the bright white flash of the base of the primaries which is a characteristic feature of jaegers.
