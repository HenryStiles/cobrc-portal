Habitat

Medium size lake with 1.4 miles circumference. Some fairly dense riparian stands of trees near lake shore but mostly open park like habitat with walking trail around lake & extensive nearby residential subdivisions. The Lake is heavily used by walkers/joggers/dogs which could explain why bird didn't stay long.

Description of the Bird

Overall structure: Long torpedo shaped body with small head & long rear end extension, and long narrow angular wings, pointed at tips. Shape suggested large Arctic Tern.

Head was proportionately small and was whitish. Did not see bill well but it looked dark in contrast to whitish head.

Upper mantle was steely gray with contrasting dark primaries and solid dark trailing edge to wings. At certain angles a white line was visible on outer primary but this line was not always visible. The line was probably one but not more than two primary shaft(s).

Underparts were whitish with obscure diffuse light gray breast band. I did not see undertail coverts well. Underwings were heavily barred to primaries which were dark but centered with a prominent
white flash.

The tail was narrow with a projection of central tail feathers of between 1-2 inches. The tips of these central tail feathers appeared blunt.
Behaviors: Flight was erratic and buoyant with bird frequently changing directions amid mostly flapping but occasionally soaring flight pattern. It landed on water twice during observation, but was down for no more than a few seconds before taking flight again.
Call: None heard
Plumage: light morph juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Parasitic Jaegers tend to be browner and not gray like Long-tailed. Parasitics often show rufous or rusty tinges to brown feathers. Parasitic also have pointed not blunt tail projection. They also tend to show more white in upper wing primaries. The bird I saw had dark primaries with any white barely and not often visible.

Pomarine Jaegers appear thicker bodied, broader winged, larger headed than Long-tails. They also tend to have the boldest primary flash on upper wing surfaces of all Jaegers. Pomarines also have a double white flash on underside of primaries.

The whitish head, I observed, and underparts combined with a pale breast band of observed bird is much more typical of juvenile Long-tailed Jaeger than other Jaegers. Also the gray mantle and dark trailing edge of wings is typical of Long-tailed in all plumages.
