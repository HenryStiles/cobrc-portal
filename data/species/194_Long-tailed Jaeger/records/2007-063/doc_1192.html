Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Mead ETX Cat scope (80 mm) with 26 mm eyepiece
Distance:  About 50-75 yards
Light:  Full morning sun behind my observation point (looking west).

Description of the Bird

Overall: a sleek looking white breasted bird with a white neck/throat and a black cap, black bill, and a very long darkish tail. Wings and back looked brownish to chocoloate color. The overall shape was "slim lined" especially as compared to the nearby gulls and pelicans. I, at first casual look, thought it was a Franklin's Gull (white with black head) as there had been previously a good population hanging out here. But quickly realized that it's look on the water was different. Taking up the scope showed immediately and clearly a long central tail sloping up and continuing the line of the tertials. Also, the bill was not colored and in the scope clearly had a "hook" on the proximal end. Specifically, I noticed that the black "cap" extended down below the eyeline and that the tertials were very dark to black...they really stood out. I did not see the bird fly (in or out), I was busy checking to see if I got a photo, looking up and it was gone.
Behaviors: Waryily swimming just near a group of loafing Ring-billed Gulls. Seemed to be "eyeing" them back and forth as swam east to west between the gulls and the center of the lake (keeping gulls between subject and shore). My impression at the time it was "antsy".
Call: N/A
Plumage: Adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

After confirming it was not a Franklin's Gull and in fact not a gull/tern at all (bill length and type/shape), the long tail made the remainder easy.
