Description of the Bird

Smaller gull-like bird first seen sitting on the water. Sat high in the water and rocked gently back/forth as it paddled.

Head and neck off-white with faint grayish streaking on the crown forming a faint cap. 

Bill sturdy, but not terribly large, silvery/gray in color and darker or black at the tip. Eye dark.

Back gray with narrow white edging to the feather tips giving the back a scaly appearance.

Wingtips dark gray.

End of tail showed the two dark gray central tail feathers.  Visible in photo with upturned wings.

Flanks off-white with smudgy gray barring.

Under-tail coverts barred off-white and gray.

When the bird flew it was on distinctively downward bowed wings.

In flight, the top of the tail showed barring with a dark terminal band.

The underside of the wings appeared gray with white primary shafts creating a triangle of white in the outer portion of the wing.

The upper wing was gray with two white-shafted primaries.

The other two jaegers eliminated primarily by date with immature of both species being later arrivals and by the white head. In addition, the other jaegers should have buffy feather edging and warmer tones as opposed to the cold gray tones of this bird.
