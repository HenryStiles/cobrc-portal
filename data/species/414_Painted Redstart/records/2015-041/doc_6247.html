Description of the Bird

The Painted Redstart was repeatedly making single-syllable chirps while moving its body.  It would turn back and forth with each chirp, and move its tail and wings at the same time.  This is how I noticed it because it was deep in the shrubbery, but constantly moving.  When I first noticed it, several Stellar's Jays seemed interested in it and had flown to the same shrubbery, but were not outright harassing it.  The jays left, and the Painted Redstart continued on with its movement and calling.  After several minutes it began to fly around in the shrubbery. After approximately 15 minutes the Painted Redstart flew further away and out of sight. 

Similar Species Discussion

I initially thought it was a Painted Redstart because I have seen them before in New Mexico, however I did not think they were in the area.  When I checked the Black Canyon National Park's bird list, I did not see Painted Redstarts.  Since I am not fully knowledgeable of Western birds, I browsed the bird list and looked up several species I did not know of on the bird list and reviewed their information/pictures in the iBird Pro app.  None of the birds remotely matched and when I pulled up pictures of Painted Redstarts, I realized the bird was a distinct match.

Resources Used

I used binoculars to better view the bird.  I then checked the Black Canyon National Park's bird list, which proved fruitless.  I also referred to the iBird Pro app which provided me with pictures of the species.

Previous Experience

I have seen Painted Redstarts once before in New Mexico.  I am an amateur birder from the East Coast, and have general knowledge of bird species throughout the United States.
