Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized warbler, about the same size as nearby Yellow-rumped Warblers. Overall, the shape was similar to the Yellow-rumped Warblers as well, but slightly thinner overall, with a propotionally longer tail. The bill was typical of a warbler - thin, pointed, and fairly short. The bill and legs were black.

The head, back, wings, flanks, and sides of the chest were a slightly shiny black. The wing coverts were white, creating a solid white patch on the otherwise black wings. The bird had a bold, bright patch of red running from the chest to the belly, contrasting with the black upperparts and flanks. The lower belly and undertail coverts were dusky white. The tail was black with the outermost tail feather completely white, and the next two outmost partially white.
Behaviors: The bird was flitting around actively throughout the observation, often clinging to the sides of trunks, and fanning it's tail.
Call: The bird called often, a sharp, loud, unwarbler-like call note somewhat reminiscent of a note from a vireo song.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All other species were ruled out by the warbler size, striking red, black, and white plumage, tail pattern, etc. Slate-throated Redstart was ruled out by the shiny black upperparts rather than dark gray, among a host of other field marks.
