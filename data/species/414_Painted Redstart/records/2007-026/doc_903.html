Description of the Bird

A distinctive and bright, active warbler, the bird was black on the head, upperparts, chin, throat, chest, and sides with bright scarlet belly and grayish-white vent and undertail coverts. The black tail had virtually all-white R6s, easily visible from underneath.
Behaviors: The bird was actively foraging among the leafing-out cottonwoods and other trees, often cocking up and fanning its tail in its efforts to flush insects. It sat still for only very short periods of time (generally <10 seconds).
Call: Song was a sprightly, somewhat high-pitched warbling
Plumage: unknown
