Description of the Bird

Large warbler, indentified by thin bill, thing legs, nervous foraging and genral gizz of a warbler.

Head, chin, throat, face, nape and back slate gray. Wings also gray but with large white wing patches, sometimes flashed while foraging. Dorsal surface of tail concolorus with back, EXCEPT at least three rectrices on each side white (outer two) or mostly white (third one in from outermost). Tail flashed open rarely, but seen at least twice. Beautiful reddish lower breast and belly contrasting strongly with gray areas described above, and large white wing patches. Noticeable white eye arcs directly below eye.
Behaviors: Mostly nervous forager. Forgaging strategy was almost entirely gleaning from twigs, flowers, and from large stems, and even spent some time gleaning insects on tree trunks. (No hover gleans observed) Although known to forage at lower levels, this CO bird fed actively at all heights including the top of trees, in emerging flowers for insects. Called frequently while foraging. Very active, almost never stopping to rest.
Call: no song, but loud calls low in pitch, somewhat explosive, emitted while foraging at varied intervals with long pauses between calls, but when calling did so repeatedly.
Plumage: probably 1st summer

Similar Species Discussion

Slate-throated Redstart (called Slate-throated Whitestart in most of Latin America) is similar.

STRE is without bold white wing patch, lacks the white outer rectrices, although it does have a reduce amount of white in the terminal third of its outer rects, and STRE lacks the white eye arcs of PARE. It is also a different shade of gray on its back, paler.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I lived in SE Arizona for two years. I have seen hundreds of Painted Redstarts, and I have seen 15-20 Slate-throated Redstarts in Mexico and further south.
