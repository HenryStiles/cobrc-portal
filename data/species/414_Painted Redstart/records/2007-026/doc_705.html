Description of the Bird

Medium-sized warbler with the following characteristics: black upperparts except for a small white crescent on the lower edge of the eye and a very large white wing patch (greater and median coverts); throat and upperchest black extending along the flanks below the wings, otherwise dull red belly and lower chest patch fading to mottled black and white at the crissum; the long tail showed black central feathers with at least the outer 3 rectrices entirely or near-entirely white.
Behaviors: The redstart actively foraged in the mid-canopy of several cottonwood trees for the most part.
Call: Called several times, each time a long, hollow "chuw".
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Slate-throated Redstart is eliminated by the bird's black plumage (gray in Slate-throated); very large white wing patch (completely dark wing in Slate-throated); and lack of a rufous cap.
