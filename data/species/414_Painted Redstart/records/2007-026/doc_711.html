Description of the Bird

Small, warbler-type bird. The head and all of the upper body was black, except for very conspicuous, large white wing patches and white outer tail feathers. The upper breast and flanks were also black, but the central and lower breast was a bright, deep red.The under tail was white, and the under tail coverts were barred. The legs and feet were also black. A few feathers at the back of the head were sticking up, and the base of the red patch was slightly less red, which some observers present thought made this a one year old individual.
Behaviors: This bird was actively feeding in the trees, and didn't stay still for very long at all. It also made sallies away from the trees to catch passing flies.
Call: The bird made a whistled call a few times
Plumage: Alternate or Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Red-faced Warbler has red on the face, and not on the breast. Slate-throated Redstart doesn't have the large white wing patches.
