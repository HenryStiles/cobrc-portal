Habitat

Mid elevation riparian surounded by ponderosa, Pinon / Juniper and rural mountain housing.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss Victory FL 8 X 32
Distance:  60 - 100 feet
Light:  Sun rising over mountain and illuminating the side of the east side of the house where the bird was seen.

Description of the Bird

A black wood warbler with white wing patches and a bright red oval patch from belly to mid-breast. White crescent below each eye, some white in vent and under-tail and white outer retrices.
Behaviors: The birds was clinging to siding and other porus vertical serfaces of a house while searching for insects.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

From a birders standpoint, none. Rose-brested Grosbeak and Slate-throated Redstart were considered as possible species the home owner may have seen, however the sighting and subsequent photos eliminate those as possibilities.

Resources Used

Sibley's Guide to Birds was referenced for age and sex.

Previous Experience

I have seen this species in Arizona on several visits.
