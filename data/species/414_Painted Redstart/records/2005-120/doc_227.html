Description of the Bird

the bird was a small (slightly larger than a chickadee) warbler with a long tail, and short, thin bill. The bill and legs were black.

The head was a shiny black color, with a bold white eye arc on the bottom of the eye. The upperparts, tail, and wings were this same color, except for a very large and bold white wing patch on the coverts, with some streaks of white running down the flight feather edges, and two or three white outer tail feathers on each side of the tail. The throat and sides of the chest were also shiny black.

There was a very striking and large patch of bright red running from the lower throat to the belly. The flanks and lower belly of the bird were a very dark gray, paler than the shiny black of the upperparts and head.
Behaviors: during the entire observation the bird crawled around on the eaves and chimney of the house almost like a nuthatch, looking for insects, until it flew to the south with a flock of chickadees and juncos.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All other species were ruled out by a combination of the small size, shiny black color with striking red patch on the underparts, and white wing patch.
