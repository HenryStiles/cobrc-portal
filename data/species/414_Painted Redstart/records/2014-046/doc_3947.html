Description of the Bird

Small warbler shaped bird with completely black head, small thin bill, with thin incomplete white eye-ring.  Black coloring extended to back, wings, and central tail feathers.  Thick white wingbar present.  Throat was black.  Breast and belly were crimson red.  Flanks and undertail coverts were black.  Tail was long compared to where its primaries ended.  When the bird flew its outer tail feathers showed flashes of white.  Behavior was very flittery and bouncy.  It rarely stayed in the same spot.

Similar Species Discussion

Only bird with similar markings in the area would have been the lark bunting, but the lark bunting does not have a red breast or belly.  The beak of the Lark Bunting is also thick and stubby - a very sharp contrast to the thin, small, short bill of the bird observed.  Also the body position of the redstart was more horizontal compared to the more upright stature of the lark bunting.  American Redstart would not be confused with the Painted redstart because it has orange on the chest, wing, and tail feathering. Despite the highly unlikely chance of a slate-throated redstart we can eliminate it because this observed bird has the thick white wingbar or patch.  This patch is absent on the Slate-throated redstart.
