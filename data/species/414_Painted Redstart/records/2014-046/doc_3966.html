Description of the Bird

I observed a bird consistent in size and shape with a warbler.  It was black on the head, back and tail, with a bright red belly, bold white wing bars and white outer tail feathers.  The bird showed a half ark of white under the eye.  Vent area showed dark feathers over a pale gray base.  Bird flew around between a variety of trees but allowed for good observations in sunlight before moving to an area where it was backlit.  The bird did not call while I observed it.  It appeared to be foraging on bugs in the trees and on leaves.  It was in the company of a mixed flock of Audubon's and Myrtle Yellow-rumped Warblers (plus a female Pine Warbler).

Similar Species Discussion

This species is pretty distinct and not easily confused.

Slate-throated Redstart can be eliminated by the half-arc under the eye and white wing bar of this bird.   

American Redstart shows a different color pattern on body and tail.

At a stretch the Towhee complex is much larger and has a white belly and different wing color pattern.

I have seen one Painted Redstart in Arizona last year.  I do not have much experience with the species, however it is distinct enough I was confident on my ID.
