Description of the Bird

Adults were very large long-legged birds with long necks and feathery rumps typical of cranes. Overall coloration of body was cinnamon-brown (color of tannic acid-stained water) with some gray. Forecrown was crimson red. Long, sharp bills.

Chick was covered in yellow down.
Behaviors: Foraging in wet meadow as a family group (two adults, one chick)
Call: Did not vocalize
Plumage: Adults and fledgling

Similar Species Discussion

Great Blue Heron is a large, long-legged crane-like bird but lacks crane body shape, and is smaller, and lacks red forecrown.

Whooping Cranes are white, not brown.

Common Crane are gray with black throat.

Other old-world cranes were not considered for this identification.

Additional Information

Comments: Greater Sandhill Cranes nest regularly in northwest Colorado, mainly in Routt County, and are considered a Threatened Species by the Colorado Division of Wildlife.

The Colorado Breeding Bird Atlas (H.E. Kingery, 1998) failed to document Sandhill Crane beeding anywhere in the Eastern Plains of Colorado.

A status review for this species in Colorado did not discover any breeding or summer records from the eastern plains of Colorado (R. Andrews & R. Righter, Colorado Birds, 1992).

From 2000-2004, at least one pair of Sandhill Cranes were observed annually during late June or early July in northwest Larimer County. One - two chicks were produced in at least three of those years (N. Komar, 2005. Sandhill Crane Nesting in Larimer County. Colorado Birds, 39(1):15-16.) The mountin region of Larimer County where these cranes nested would be the nearest documented nesting location to LaSalle, Weld County, and is about 80 miles west by northwest.
