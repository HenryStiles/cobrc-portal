Description of the Bird

the bird was a large dove, slightly larger than a Mourning Dove, with a much bulkier shape, mostly due to a relatively larger head, and a shorter but wider tail. The bill was a typical dove bill, and black. The legs were dark flesh colored

The overall color of the dove was medium grayish, with a slight pinkish tinge on the underparts and head, and a brownish tinge on the mantle and wings. The bird had a bold but thin black collar, bordered by a very thin area of white. The underside of the tail was white, with black on the base of the outer primaries, on the outer webs.
Behaviors: the birds were seen on the ground near a bird feeder, and one bird spent a good deal of time perched on a wire
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Almost all similar dove species were ruled out by the overall gray color with bold black collar. Ringed Turtle-Dove was ruled out by much darker overall coloration, and by the black patches on the base of the outer retrices below, as well as gray undertail coverts.

Resources Used

Sibley was consulted to rule out Ringed Turtle-Dove

Previous Experience

I have seen many EUCDs, and am very familiar with that species.

Additional Information

Comments: report made from photos taken of the bird, and from memory. At the time this bird was found it was a first county record. Since then I have seen the birds in 2005 and 2006, though never in large numbers. I suspect that they are not doing as well in Walden as they are in many other towns in Colorado.
