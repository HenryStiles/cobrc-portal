Description of the Bird

A light colored Dove, not as light as Ringed Turtle-Dove. It had a black band on the back of the neck. It had dark primaries and a light brownish wash on the back. The undertail coverts were gray and it had dark feathers going down the sides of the undersides of the tail for about an inch or two in length. It had a square shaped tail. It was a heavy looking dove. I never saw it fly.
Behaviors: It was sitting in a tree next to Sandy Fliniau's House in Walden. It looked cold (feathers fluffed) since it was a cold December day.
Call: Didn't hear it
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ringed Turtle-Dove - It is lighter than EUCD. It also has pale primaries and black, on the outer parts of the under side of the tail, that does not extend past or even close to the of the undertail coverts. The primaries were to dark to be this species.
There is no other dove in the United States that has a black ring on the back on the neck.

Resources Used
