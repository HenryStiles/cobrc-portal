Description of the Bird

All eight birds looked very similar, so I here describe them as one. A large, plump dove, it was basically soft brown above and pale pinkish-brown below. It had a distinct black half-collar, bordered above and below by thin white bands, on the back of the neck. The primaries were the darkest part of the perched bird, except for the blackish base to the tail. That black tail base extended only to the tips of the undertail coverts, except for the outer web of the R6s, where the black extended a bit beyond the undertail coverts.

In flight, the wings were tri-colored -- soft brown interior (lesser coverts), pale gray middle (median and greater coverts), and blackish outer (primaries). The bill was black, the eyes were red, and the feet were pinkish-red.
Behaviors: Perched on power line and flying
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Various other Streptopelia doves (including Ringed Turtle-Dove) are ruled out by the combination of wing and tail patterns. White-winged Dove is ruled out by the same and by the lack of a blue orbital ring, among many other features.
