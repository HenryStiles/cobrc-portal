Description of the Bird

This was a cormorant by length of neck and plumage coloration, brown.

It was flying with 4 other brown cormorants. Its tail vs. head/neck were equal in length. The description is based on comparison with DCCOs, so it will go here. In flight the DCCO all had blocky heads larger than their long necks. Compared with the length of their tails, the head/neck was much longer than their tails. THe NECO had a mush thinner neck, shorter bill, and smaller head. As mentioned head/neck equaled tail length.

Color of breast was light brown making it a juvenile. The gular comparisons were not visible to me.
Behaviors: Flying with 4 DCCOs circling Big Johnson Reservoir
Call: none
Plumage: juvenile
