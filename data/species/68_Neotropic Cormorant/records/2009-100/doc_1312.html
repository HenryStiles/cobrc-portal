Description of the Bird

In comparison to a juvenile Double Crested Comorant within 2 to 3 feet proximity , I observed the smaller head and shorter bill , a longer proportionall tail , dark brown chest versus a pale chest for the DCCO , darl lores versus the orange lores for the DCCO , gray upper mandible versus an all orange bill with the DCCO and a more V shaped border at the gape.
Behaviors: Bird was first seen perched on rocks below the dam wall alongside the juvenile Double crested Comorant where the smaller size was very noticeable. Later observed diving for fish again in the company of DCCO where the head shape and shorter bill could be compared.
Call: Not Applicable
Plumage: juvenile
