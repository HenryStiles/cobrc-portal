Description of the Bird

All black plumaged cormorant perched on log. No nearby cormorants for direct size comparison but waterfowl that swam past gave distinct impression that this cormorant was small. It also looked long-tailed and proportionately more delicate in bill size than DCCO. More importantly, due to excellent lighting conditions, with minimal (if any) heat waves and light winds, we were able to clearly see the head pattern. The gular patch was small, yellowish orange, and distinctly pointed towards rear, with a thin but obvious white border. Additionally, the lores were dark, utterly without any yellow or orange. The light was such, that even small amounts of yellow/orange in lores (as displayed by some immature DCCO present) were easy to see.
Behavior: Sitting on log in pond
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The gular patch size and shape, edged by white, plus dark lores eliminates DCCO. The tail length and bill size also supportive
