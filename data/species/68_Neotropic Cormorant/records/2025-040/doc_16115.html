Description of the Bird

The bird was a cormorant, which was significantly smaller than the Double-crested Cormorants that were nearby. It had a much smaller body and bill, but also showed a quite long tail: as least as long as those on the D-c Cormorants.  It had a small white area at the back of the bill, which extended down a very small distance below the bill. It had a conspicuous green eye. The neck showed some brown feathers and was not totally black, which is why I think it was an immature bird that hadn't quite reached adult plumage. 

Similar Species Discussion

The only real alternative in Colorado is Double-crested Cormorant, and it was significant smaller than that species in both body and bill size. Would be really small compared to a Great Cormorant, and showed features not consistent with the West Coast Cormorants.

Previous Experience

I see many hundreds of D-c Cormorants every year, and have seen many hundreds of Neotropic Cormorants in the tropics.
