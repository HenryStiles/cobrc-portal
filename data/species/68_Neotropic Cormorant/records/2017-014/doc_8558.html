Description of the Bird

The Neotropic Cormorant was perched on a shoreline next to 30 or so DCCO, most which were adults. The Neotrop was much smaller overall than its companions, and its head appeared proportionately narrower as did its bill. The tail was distinctly proportionately longer than those of the nearby DCCOs. The difference in head/bill proportions were distinct but not dramatic. The tail length difference was more pronounced. I looked at it for quite a while, and its size difference seemed not an artifact of posture or viewing angle.

The bird was all dark brown to blackish with bronzy hues, which were particularly evident on chest and upper belly. I stared at it for quite a while to get several decent views of the face/bill. The bill was dull yellow morphing to yellow-orange at the gular patch. The gular patch was also small and pointed posteriorly, bordered with dull feathering white along its lower edge. The lores were black(ish), with no yellow or orange -- the colored lores being obvious on all DCCO present in same grouping

Similar Species Discussion

Double-crested Cormorant and NECO x DCCO. Gular pouch, size, proportions (particularly tail length) were not those of DCCO. The color was more typical of NECO than DCCO. There were no marks to suggest any DCCO influence in this bird's appearance, thus ruling out (as much as one can) hybrid.
