Description of the Bird

The bird was an obvious cormorant, much like the Double-crested Cormorant it was next to, with differences discussed below.

The bird was about 75 percent the size of the Double-crested Cormorant it was next to, with a slimmer body shape enhanced by the much longer tail. The overall plumage was black, with no other discernable color, perhaps due to the distance. The tail was significantly longer than the tail of the nearby Double-crested Cormorant, by at least 50-75 percent. The neck appeared a little thinner, and the bill a little smaller, but due to distance it was hard to confirm these features.
Behaviors: the bird was on a stick on the middle of the reservoir, preening and just loafing around.
Call: none heard
Plumage: maybe adult

Similar Species Discussion

Double-crested Cormorant was ruled out significantly smaller size, much longer tail, and slimmer shape.

Pelagic Cormorant (hey, it could happen!) was ruled out by thinner neck and lack of a "snake-like" look, as well as heavier build, as well as extreme unlikeliness!
