Description of the Bird

On 29 July, at Neegronda Reservoir

Adult seen around 7:20 before fog drifted in. Description pertains to notes from far longer observation from about 9 am to nearly 10 am.

Adult all black cormorant. Orange gular patch small, pointed towards rear and bordered by white, more thickly below than above. Tail long (tail length at least 1/2 distance from distal tip of upper/undertail coverts to chest/neck juncture). Bill black. Body smaller than DCCO  and bill seemed thinner/more delicate than that of a DCCO (a DCCO drifted by, not close, but not too far away from the Neotrops -- for about 1 minute)
Immature. Dark brown turning deep bronze-brown on chest, neck, head. Gular patch yellow-orange, small, pointed towards rear. Narrow white border detected only along bottom edge. Basal portion of mandible (approx 1/2 length) yellowish. Remainder of bill black. Similar in proportions/size to adult. 
Both viewed for 30 minutes or so of nearly 60 minute visit to Neegronda.

Two NECO were found and observed for 30+ minutes at small pond adjacent to se. corner of Neenoshe Reservoir on 4 August, where seen from about 2:30 to 3:20. Seen in bright sun, fair angle. Description much as above, though gular pouch of adult did not seem bordered by white above. Immature seemed both a bit larger and longer tailed than adult, perhaps in part due to heavy wear of tail in adult. Also, bill of adult seemed dark gray, that of immature more extensively yellow. Lores still dark, etc. I believe immature to be a different bird from that at Neegronda as the pale of chest and neck (of Neenoshe bird) was grayer and more extensive (ie, going onto upper belly) than that of Neegronda individual. It is impossible for me to say if adult same or different. Same two birds (or what appeared to be same two birds) were watched by Dave Ely and I for about 30 minutes at same location at Neenoshe on 11 August BUT WERE WITH AN APPARENT NECO X DCCO (see https://ebird.org/view/checklist/S47793002). Photos from both 4 and 11 August are included. On 18 August, a lone adult was at same location, seen for only 1-2 minutes. All black small (compared to DCCO) cormorant with small yellow-orange gular pouch, pointed posteriorly and bordered by white below and behind. Long tail, c/w NECO, seen in flight

How many NECO were present during July/Augutst in Kiowa. Hard to say. At least 2 immatures and 1 adult. It is quite possible, perhaps likely, that 4+ individuals were involved

Similar Species Discussion

DCCO and DCCO x NECO
Gular pouch size and shape typical of NECO as was white border(s)
Tail length typical of NECO, too long for DCCO
Body size too small for DCCO
Bill finer than that of DCCO

All marks looked typical for NECO, nothing to suggest hybrid
