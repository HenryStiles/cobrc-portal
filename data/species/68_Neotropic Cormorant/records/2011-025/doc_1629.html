Description of the Bird

The bird was clearly a cormorant -- a predominantly dark, medium-sized waterbird with a long neck, long, hooked bill, and long tail. It was far smaller than the Double-crested Cormorants that were perched on either side of it -- when both the Double-crests and the Neotropic were sleeping, with their heads tucked under their wings, the Neotropic looked only half the size of the Double-crests. Its body had about the same bulk as those of the Ring-billed Gulls perched right beside it. However, in a direct comparison of the tail lengths of the two cormorant species, the Neotropic's looked slightly longer in actual length, meaning it was a great deal longer proportionately. The neck of the Neotropic was much more slender than that of the Double-crests and its head was more dainty. Its facial skin appeared gunmetal gray, with zero orange. There was a thin border of white feathers around the base of the facial skin below the eye, but this was difficult to see except with good views. The end of the facial skin just posterior to the gape appeared to come to a rather sharp point, as opposed to the more rounded base of the facial skin of the Double-crests. The Neotropic's breast and the anterior portion of its long neck were smooth warm brown, without the pure white feathers on the adjacent juvenile Double-crests, and without the mottling shown by most of the members of that species. The rest of the bird's feathers (head, back of the neck, wings, back, tail, vent) were dark glossy black. The legs were dark; they were both shorter and thinner than those of the adjacent Double-crests.
Behaviors: The bird was sleeping on a log at the edge of the marina when I arrived. Later it woke up and began looking around, preening occasionally. It eventually hopped into the water and began swimming and diving. Then I saw it hop back onto the log and go back to sleep.
Call: Bird was silent.
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Double-crested Cormorants were available in the same scope view for easy comparison. The Neotropic's tiny size, richer and smoother brown coloration, lack of orange facial skin, thin white border to the facial skin, and far longer tail were diagnostic.
