Description of the Bird

Continuing juvenile bird that has been in the area for weeks. This bird was on a snag to the south of the island where most of the other DCCOs and CAGU were located. Next to it was a DCCO, so the size comparison was easy to see. 30% smaller than nearby adult DCCO. Proportionally longer tail than several nearby DCCOs. Uniformly brownish neck without the light color of a nearby Juvenile DCCO on the island. Faint outline of triangular-shaped gape extending to rear of eye. Can be seen in photo. No color above eye.  

Similar Species Discussion

DCCO was considered.  This bird was smaller and was the only cormorant present with a uniformly brown neck and no color in the bill or gular.  Triangular gape was noted and was more easily seen in the scope than through attached photos.
