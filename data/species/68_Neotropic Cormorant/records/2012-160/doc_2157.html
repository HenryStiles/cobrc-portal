Description of the Bird

Lisa Edwards and I were birding at Lake Holbrook, and I started looking at the cormorants perched in the trees, and quickly noticed a smaller cormorant will a longer tail sitting in the trees in the middle of the lake, with bigger Double-crested Cormorants. I asked Lisa to look at the cormorant in my scope, and she also saw the smaller cormorant, and agreed that it was a Neotropic Cormorant. Besides for the size of the bird, being smaller than the other cormorants, also the bird had longer tail, thinner neck, and smaller bill. I've included a photo of the bird, taken by Cole Wild. I saw the bird again with Mark Peterson on July 15th.
Behaviors: Sitting in dead trees with other cormorants.
Call: none
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Double-crested Cormorant which were next to the bird, is much larger, shorter tail, thicker neck, and thicker bill.
