Description of the Bird

I viewed a large black bird about the size of a smaller heron or egret. The bird was almost totally black except around the bill.

The bird had a long tail that was flaired, fanned or rounded; delicate, thin but longish bill that contrasted sharply to nearby Black-crowned Night Herons; and thin neck.

The head was rounded and lacked any crests or white plumes. The face was marked by a small yellow patch below the bill that did not extend markedly down on the throat (however, no white border was observed). Above the bill the bird showed dark lores.
Behaviors: The bird was perched in a dead tree on the side of a small island in a large pond. It appeared to be sleeping or resting with its head hidden at first, but when a handful of Black-crowned Night-herons flew up into the dead tree it became agitated and threatening; perhaps hissing at the night-herons. The bird in question was about the same length as the night herons.
Call: none
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was obviously a cormorant. Double-crested Cormorant was ruled out by the bare-skin pattern around the bill, the thin bill and long tail and the overall size compared to nearby Black-crowned Night-herons.
