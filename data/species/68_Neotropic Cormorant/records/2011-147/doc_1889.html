Description of the Bird

Three NECOs were seen among Double-crested Cormorants on DFO trip to Cherry Creek Reservoir, Arapahoe Co., CO on 22 Oct 2011 led by Buzz Schaumberg. Two NECOs were adults, 1 juv. I concentrated on the NECO sillouetted against nearby swimming DCCOs that I photographed with my Canon 60D w/ 100-400 mm IS Canon lens. Smaller, thinner than DCCO with smaller head and bill. Black plumage, longer tail than DCCO. White V between bill and face. This NECO perched on protruding old tree stump in Cherry Creek Res. Then it turned to perch the opposite direction on same stump. I took many photos, 3 of which are on my website www.kayniyo.com.
Behaviors: This particular NECO was swimming with many DCCOs and climbed up on the stump protruding from the reservoir to perch in the sun. It was watchful and turned around to perch the other dircection providing another view for my lens.
Call: None noted
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

This NECO is smaller, slimmer, thinner with longer tail and smaller head and bill than the nearby DCCOs. It also had a distinct white V between the face and bill. Had a bluish eye.
