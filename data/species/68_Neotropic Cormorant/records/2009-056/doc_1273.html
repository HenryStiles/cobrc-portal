Description of the Bird

This was a cormorant by the dark plumage, long neck and tail and by habit of perching next to the water on exposed limbs sticking out of the water.

The cormorant was distant but the silhouette showed a smallish rounded head, dark back and breast, and a longish tail. The face pattern, especially the gular pouch shape and color was not visible at this distance.
Behaviors: Perched on stick over the water.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Double-creseted Cormorant. Luckily there were as many as 20 DCCOs close to the NECO.

The biggest comparative difference was in size and overall shape. The DCCOs were bulky, large-headed and large-billed with thicker necks and proportionately shorter tails. The NECO, was noticeably shorter, thinner-necked, rounder headed with proportionately longer tail. The gular pouch comparisons could not be made at this distance.

Previous Experience

I have seen 1000s of DCCOs in various plumages. I have seem 100s or maybe 1000s of NECOs in varioius plumages mostly in South America but also in South Texas and in New Mexico. I have studied the differences in the past and have been looking for a NECO in CO, this being my first.
