Description of the Bird

I was scanning the "new" pond along Weld County Road 7 (actually part St Vrain State Park) when I saw a dark immature cormorant that looked small and with limited color on the face. I followed it for about 10 minutes in fairly good light as it swam about. It was side-by-side with 2 immature DCCO for about 60 seconds, all birds at similar angles, same distance, similar postures repeatedly. The Neotropical Cormorant was about 2/3 the size of the DCCOs. It was darker than either, with its visible body plumage entirely a dark brown, including chest or neck. The bill dull yellow, duller than that of the immature DCCOs. The gular pouch was yellow, small, and pointed posteriorly. This was noticably different than the various immature DCCOs in the area (about 15). The lores dark, lacking the yellow or orange present in this location on DCCOs (and was easily visible in the DCCOs present under same lighting conditions/distance. Bill slimmer than DCCO, and neck seemed slimmer, though hard to know how much of this is due to the bird itself being smaller, but I did get sense that the Neotrop was a proportionately smaller billed.
Behaviors: see description

Similar Species Discussion

Separated from immature Double-crested Cormorant by the following:
1- lack of yellow/orange in loral/supraloral area
2- small pointed gular pouch
3- small size in direct comparison with DCCOs at same distance, same posture in water (perched birds, with their differing angles and elevation can be far trickier for me)
4- overall darkness supportive, but not definitive
