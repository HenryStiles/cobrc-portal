Description of the Bird

All black, swimming with mergansers, a large flock (>300 birds) most of which were RBME, but about 1/4 of which were COME. This bird appeared about same size as COME (perhaps a tad longer, but also less bulky, so I'd have guessed the bird would have weighed less); The gular pouch was some mixture of yellow and orange, not as bright as that of the adult DCCOs I'd been seeing that day. The gular pouch was small and pointed towards the rear. The lower edge was bordered narrowly in white. The loral area was blackish, without any yellow or orange. The bill was narrow and slim, without a bulge near tip as is the norm in DCCO. The tail was long, about (rough estimate) 50% longer (proportionately) than that of a DCCO. Also of note, head not as bulky as that of DCCO. Too far away to make out shape of scaps. 

Similar Species Discussion

DCCO and DCCO x NECO. The bird appeared the size and shape of a NECO along with appropriately sized and shaped gular pouch (some DCCO will show pointed gular pouch, but typically it is larger than that of NECO). Loral pattern as in NECO. No evidence for DCCO influence in size, bill/tail/head shape, loral pattern, gular pouch.
