Description of the Bird

Small cormorant with features typical of Neotropic (see photos); orange facial skin lacking from lores.
Behaviors: Loafing with cormorants, swimming.
Call: none
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Double-crested Cormorant is larger with orange on lores, and different shape of orange throat pouch. No other North American cormorant species has orange or yellow/orange throat color. Note that nestling DCCO have yellow throat with dark lore and small size. However, such birds are never seen away from nesting colony, have all dark bill and black plumage on neck and head.
