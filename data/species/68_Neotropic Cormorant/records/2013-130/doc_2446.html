Description of the Bird

Two large dark birds were seen flying towards us fighting a heavy head wind, slowing their speed (in relation to ground) considerably, allowing for lengthy look. The difference in size between two individuals was apparent upon first glance. We watched the birds, huddled (ourselves) out of wind) for about 60 seconds, with the pair passing as close as 100 feet, at which time they were essentially in profile. The Neotropic was about 2/3 the length of the Double-crested. The tail of the Neotropic was proportionately noticeably longer than that of the DCCO, giving an almost "balanced" look between head/neck extension forward and tail to rear. The DCCO was clearly "front heavy." Some of the face pattern could be made out, and no yellow in lores could be seen, but given look, I'd not put much stake in this
