Description of the Bird

A small cormorant, about 1/2 of body size of adjacent Double-crested Cormorants (DCCO). Its height on the water was about 2/3 that of DCCO. Appeared much slimmer-necked, rounder-headed(lacking the crest of DCCO), and smaller billed (both shorter and more slender) than DCCO. Tail length was not observed. The bright prange gular area, obvious on all DCCO (even immatures), was not observed on this bird. The color of the gular area could not be discerned due to distance, but must have been dull. The bill was horn colored (bills of adjacent DCCOs appeared dark). The orange supraloral area, visible (with some difficulty) on nearby DCCO was not visible on the Neotropic. A narrow white border was visible to the lower edge of the gular area, typical of breeding condition Neotropic Cormorants. The plumage was entirely black, indicating an adult.

See field notes and photos.
Behaviors: Hundreds of cormorants flushed off a roosting island (that also had ducks, gulls, and Am. White Pelicans). It was only after they flushed that I noticed the Neotropic Cormorant swimming among the DCCO. I only saw it swimming, and never saw it dive or fly.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Comparisons with DCCO discussed above: smaller size, rounder head, no crest, slimmer bill, slimmer neck, lacked orange supraloral, lacked bright orange gular, narrow white border to gular pouch
