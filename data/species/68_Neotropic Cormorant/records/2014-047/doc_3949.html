Description of the Bird

Small to medium sized upright wading/perching type water bird.  Light to medium brown in color...with whitish neutral bill that has yellowish orange color at part of beak closest to face.  The first time I observed I was not as close however the bird sat quietly perched on a small rock for at least 45 minutes grooming itself et. However it never flared its wings or put them out like I think book describes for the double crested.  By grooming itself I mean it looked like it might have been picking bugs off of itself.   I never heard it make any noise or call.  It sat there just lookin around and in same spot.  I did not have camera that day and was unable to determine what it was...correctly. I had looked several times for it again. Did not notice it again until the 29th.  Went to town for errand and stopped by lake just to check and surprisingly found it again through binoculars at same spot to the far south side of Denny like perched on same rock.  However by the time I walked closer to the area it had quietly disappeared and two Canada geese were instead on the rock.  Knowing I had for sure seen the bird I had previously needed to identify I just kept walking around the lake hoping to find it again.  I was able to find it perched on a stick towards the north east side of the lake and get some photos.   It then flew off and was over near the dock and I walked around and took more photos.  Each time the bird was alone and I did not see any other cormorants other than the one.  It seemed quiet and shy.  The beak or bill was not as long as herons. I have a photo which shows more accurate detail.  The bird was not dark enough in color or large enough in my observation to be a double crested unless it is a juvenile.  The photo also shows up a whitish area to face more consistent with the neotropic and also tufts of hair on head area that I believe I read is more consistent with neotropic.
