Description of the Bird

Slim dark brown cormorant.  Neck and tail were proportionally long.  Body slim and head appeared small. Tail extended well beyong wings and body.  Bill was gray on upper mandibile and orange on lower mandibile, as was orange facial skin below and behind bill.  Lores were dark.  Feet were black.  Tail looked shaggy with feathers somewhat separated.  Back was very dark brown with very indistinct patterning - probably from the feathers.

Bird perched a small island the entire time I observed it - both early morning and mid-afternoon.

Exact size was difficult to determine.  Body was slightly longer than a coot's body.  Total length of bird appeared to be about twice the length of a coot or less, if both were stretched out.

Bird did not call or raise wings.

Similar Species Discussion

Only other likely cormorant is Double-crested, which I saw later that day.  This bird was much slimmer in build; the neck and tail were noticeably longer; and the head seemed smaller ass did the bill.  Also, an immature(first year) DCCO would have a paler tan breast and would have much more extensive orange at the lores and around the eye.

Resources Used

I checked field marks with the Sibley Guide to Birds after viewing it.

I had received an e-mail with photo of bird before searching for it.  Tony Leukering and Mark Peterson both encouraged me to check the bird which had been tentatively identified as Neotropic Cormorant.

After viewing the bird, I agreed.

Previous Experience

I have seen this bird in Colorado once and several times in Texas.  I have seen all of the North American cormorants and most of them many times while birding both coasts and Alaska.  The DDCO is regular in SW COlorado.
