Description of the Bird

I arrived at Lower Latham and found viewing conditions suprisingly good: little to no wind or heat waves. I was on the road directly east of the reservoir; close to the near shore there was a flock of Western Grebes plus two cormorants of different size, about 100 meters away from the grebes. Over time, the cormorants swam towards the grebes, into the flock, and then flew off to the far side of the reservoir. 

The two cormorants were black and without crests. The larger one had a sizable orange gular pouch, squarish in shape. The smaller cormorant had a narrower paler gular pouch that was relatively small and narrow. Any edging would have been impossible to see at distance of observation. The smaller cormorant was also proportionately a bit more delicate than the larger, with narrower head and slimmer bill. When mixing with the grebes, the larger (Double-crested) cormorant was considerably larger than the Western Grebes, while the smaller (Neotropic) cormorant was about the size of a Western Grebe. When the two cormorants took flight, the Neotropic had a proportionately longer tail than the DCCO, with the tail being roughly the same length as neck and head (probably a tad shorter), while the DCCO's tail seemed far shorter than its head/neck
