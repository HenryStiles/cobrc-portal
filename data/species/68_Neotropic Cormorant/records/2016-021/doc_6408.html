Description of the Bird

Cormorant was sitting in the sun drying off. We did see it fly and swim also. We have downloaded 4 photos which will show the bird preening, swimming and flying. Brenda's photos are very good so will be somewhat brief in writing a description. The head and bill was cormorant shaped. The eye was located in feathers and not in the skin (yellow) as in Double-crested Cormorant. The bill was gray with only a little yellow at the base. The gape behing the bill came to a narrow v shape. The back was dark with some green/blue color when light reflected just right. The feet were very black as you can see in one photo. There was not a white border at the base of the bill so it was not in breeding plumage. The bird lacked any crest.
In flight the tail was very long and equal in lenth to the neck (see photos). The neck had a very noticeable crook in flight which is easy to see in the photo.
Overall the cormorant was quite small. I was driving by and noticed the bird perched on a stick. I did recognize it as a cormorant but the small sized puzzled me. I turned around and walked around the lake for a better look and then realize that that the cormorant was not a Double-crested.

Similar Species Discussion

Double-crested is larger, has a shorter tail, has a yellow area above the dark lores and a more rounded shape at the gape. DCCO also has a shorter tail, bigger bill. Double-crested should have a crest by this time of year. See photos!
