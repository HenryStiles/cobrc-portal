Habitat

Description of the Bird

Describe habitat: Ponderosa pine forest with 2 dozen bird feeders in yard; a few, small, scrub oaks growing below them.
 
Describe behavior: Kathy Dressel called to say they had seen his bird in their yard, 5 minutes before they departed for the airport.
 
I saw it perched vertically  on tree trunks, seemed to probe for food. Maintained a certain space between it  & me.
 
Field marks observed: size, color, pattern, shape, bill, etc.:  Larger than Hairy, maybe size of flicker.
Face: white forehead, red crown extending back towards nape. Black nape & neck. Conspicuous, yellowish eye ring. Very very pale yellow chin and cheek, black throat;
Large black chest patch, with streaks leading down as if the ink dripped down from the patch. White belly with a few stripes on side.
Black back and tail. White wing patches when it flew.
 
 
 
Bird's call or song: Silent
 
Field marks not seen: White rump in flight.
 
Similar species and how eliminated: Nobody looks as clown-like as this distinctive woodpecker.
