Description of the Bird

Photos are definitive.

Smallish woodpecker (larger than Downy, smaller than Hairy), with features often described as "clownish":

+ bright white eye surrounded by black mask that tapered back to shiny blackish back and wings, with white rump and white primary coverts

+ Thick and prominent red cap/crown, unbroken from forehead to back of head (vs. added partial black cap/strap in female)

+ Off-white forehead, off-white face and throat, and black breast down to black-and-white-streaked belly

No discernible calls given during its 25 total minutes in the yard. Initially flew in about 8:32 a.m. from black walnut tree 20 feet west of backyard feeder station (at my home at 1475 Eudora St. ,Denver, CO 80220). Lingered for about 10 minutes, browsing in hanging platform feeder (seed mix including black sunflower) and separate bowl feeder above with dried mealworms. Appeared at ease with other birds at feeders (starlings, Blue Jays, collared dove). Left the feeders and went to trees when a crow came by, returned to feeders about 5 minutes later and remained another 10-15 minutes before departing and not returning
