Description of the Bird

While traveling along the Highway of Legends west of Gulnare, I noticed a woodpecker at the top of a dead tree that looked "interesting." I stopped and got out and lo and behold, 'twas a male Acorn Woodpecker. I got out my scope and digiscoping camera and took pix of it.

The bird was a woodpecker of about the size of Hairy Woodpecker (no nearby birds for comparison, though), with black forehead, red crown (from even with front of eyes to top of nape), a wide black patch through the face (including the eye), black upperparts, white chin and throat, a raggedly black chest, and white belly. The tail and wings were black and, seen in flight, there were distinct white patches created by wide white bases to the primaries. The eyes were very pale yellowish or white. The bill and legs were black.
Behaviors: The bird mostly perched in dead tree tops, calling occasionally (3x during observation on 29 Jun). Flew twice during observation on 29 Jun, never traveling more than a 100 meters or so.
Call: loud, ringing "wekka-wekka-wekka" with a gravelly aspect
Plumage: basic
