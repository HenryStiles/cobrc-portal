Habitat

hilly neighborhood with scattered Ponderosa pines and many mature non-native trees and shrubs. Scrub oak nearby on hillsides along with a row of wooden power poles. Open space nearby.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x42 Leica Ultravids
Distance:  as close as 30 feet at one time
Light:  good, see photo, backlit some of the time but the bird was close.

Description of the Bird

Medium sized woodpecker. Black back with distincive face. Yellow eye with dark pupil oon dark auricular. Dark area above and below bill set off by white behind the black areas posterior to the bill that bleeds down the face to the throat. Red on crown but not as far forward as the eye. Breast dark and a white belly with some dark thin veritcle streaks. Bill Black.
Behaviors: Snuck in to tree above feeder and probed bark. Also observed at a verticle hanging seed feeder that had millet and sunflower seeds in it. Most of the time was spent on dead areas of live and dead trees foraging under the bark and on occassion pecking.
Call: gave an occasional "wacka" call
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

There is no other woodpecker with the "clown" face.
