Habitat

Open ponderosa with oak near buildings in Pueblo Mountain Park.

Description of the Bird

Basically a black woodpecker with flashing white in wings and white rump shown while in flight. White iris, red cap (black and red in female) and white face all distinctive. Black bib with black streaking down the white chest, belly and vents.
Behaviors: Flew to and from dead Ponderosa Pine snag with occasional pecking at the bark. Visited and entered a nest hole from which I could hear young begging.
Call: loud yacking
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other woodpecker species in the US has a white iris and face.
