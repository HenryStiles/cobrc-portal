Description of the Bird

Both sexes had all black backs with no streaks or patterns, a unique face pattern with white iris surrounded by black that sweeps back to the back of the head and contrast with a white throat. The upper breast had a dark area the became streaked lower down. the tail was black.

The male had a red crown with a white forehead and the female had a crown with three colors, a smaller red area of the hindcrown, then a small blackish are and then a white area in front of the bill.
Behaviors: First saw a female foraging on a large lower branch of a large Ponderosa pine snag by a parking area. Both individuals disappeared from time to time downslope in a ravine with mixed conifers. The male was seen outside what has been descibed as a nest hole although I personally did not hear young in the nest. Watched birds come and go from the nest about four times in an hour of watching but I didn't see them actually feed young or enter cavity becuase of my viewing angle.
Call: yes, wacka-wacka, wacka and other undescribable squock
Plumage: adult
