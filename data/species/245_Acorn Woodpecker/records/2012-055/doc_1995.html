Description of the Bird

Dark-backed medium-sized woodpecker with three-colored crown with white behind the bill then black and then red on the rear 1/3 of the crown. Face with yellowish eye surrounded by black and then with while between the black auricular patch and the bill extending down onto the sides of the neck aond throat with black chin. In flight I saw large white wing patches. Breast was whitish with black vertical streaks.
Behaviors: Foraged for about 20 minutes on the larger limbs of a few Gambel's oaks, the oaks in a large clump and about 25 feet tall. Foraged in the bark fissures but I did not see what it found there to eat.
Call: I heard one loud note somewhat similar to a flicker call
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

White-headed Woodpecker - has significantly more white on its head
