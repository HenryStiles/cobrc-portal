Description of the Bird

Mid-size woodpecker, black upperparts with bright white upper tail coverts, white patch at base of primaries, crimson hindcrown; whitish belly with gray spots on flanks, bright white undertail coverts; whitish face on otherwise black head (except for crimson hindcrown). White eye.
Behaviors: Perched vertically on a pine tree trunk.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: adult?

Similar Species Discussion

Red-headed Woodpecker has entirely red head (adult) or gray-brown head (imm.) and more extensive white on wings. Williamson's Sapsucker is yellow on belly, with much less white on entirely black head, and red of head restricted to throat.
