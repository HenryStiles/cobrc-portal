Habitat

Gambel's Oak with a variety of trees including two large silver maples and some Douglas-fir. Ponderosa pines about 120 yards away but I did not see the bird in that species, only in the silver maples.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10x42 Ultravid bins
Distance:  maybe 120 feet
Light:  In the shade mostly.

Description of the Bird

Woodpecker by chisel-like bill. All black on back. Face with characteristic white iris surrounded by a black mask that extends to nape. Top of head with three colors making it a female. Black behind bill base, then wide band of black and then a smaller area of red before becoming black on the nape and back. Breast black with white belly with thin verticle streaks. Throat black surrounded by white.
Behaviors: 1) foraged for over 15 minutes at on the trunck of an old silver maple at a large area near where an old branch might have been. It was pecking and one photo shows the bird's tongue extended indicating that is was searcing for under bark goodies.

2) Also, I observed it fly to a wooden suet feeder with 2" diameter holes filled with a suet/seed mixture. The bird fed there for about 3 minutes.
Call: heard a "woka-woka-woka" on two occasions otherwise silent
Plumage: presumably adult

Similar Species Discussion

There are no other Melanerpes woodpeckers with which this species could be confused.
