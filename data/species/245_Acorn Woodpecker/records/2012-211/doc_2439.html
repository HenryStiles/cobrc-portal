Description of the Bird

I first noticed a flash of white in my peripheral vision. Approximately the size of a RHWO, a species I see regularly. At rest noted glossy black wings and body, red skull cap that did not reach the forehead. The top of the head was black which surrounded the eye and formed a point at the beak. The forehead and throat area were an off-white. At this point the bird flew exposing a white patch at the base of the outer primaries and a white rump.the tail was solid black. The bird landed briefly and then flew out of view.I did not see the chest.
Behaviors: The short answer is, it acted like a woodpecker. Landed vertically on the main trunk. It probed a few spots, found nothing to its liking and moved to the next tree. Flight was typical woodpecker flight, flapping and torpedoing, creating an undulating flight path
Call: No call heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The only other solid glossy black- backed woodpecker with flashy white wing patches is the red-headed woodpecker. I immediately ruled this out by the head color
