Specific Location:  17355 County Road 96 (AKA: Platte River Road). Hyway 285 west from Morrison Co. to Conifer, south east on Foxton Road 12 miles to Platte River Road. Left on Platte River Road drive 3.9 miles to a hard left turn onto dirt drive into a village of old log cabins. The yard of 17355 Platte river road is one your right and up the hill. Acron Woodpecker has been feeding at feeders in yard.
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Transition Zone. Foothills with pine and native trees & schrubs. North fork of Platte River runs in front of cabin with dense willow and native schrubs along river.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Martin - Nikon 10x32
Carrier - Nikon 10x42
Distance:  20 - 50 feet at different times. 20 feet to poles - I placed poles.
Light:  Cloudy day but bright and goos visibility. Our sun room looks out on our pine pole feeders. Poles are 20 - 25 feet from house. No obstructions to viewing.

Description of the Bird

8 inch woodpecker shape. Black body with white belly. White cheeks and forehead. Red cap to white forehead. Black breast. White rump and white wing patch. White eye.
Behaviors: Flys into feeding station to land on one of the two 10 foot pine poles (poles 10 feet apart with feeders hanging from wire attached to the poles). Feeds from a feeder and flys to a pole. Drums on the poles and uses holes on the poles to place sunflower seeds. Flys across the Platte River to dead pines and returns later to drum poles and feed at feeders.
Call: did not hear
Plumage: Black with white belly,cheeks, forehead
