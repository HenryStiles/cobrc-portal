Description of the Bird

Bill, foot, and body structure all indicative of a woodpecker. Habit of using tail as a prop when clinging to vertical feeder also a woodpecker trait. Clown-like facial markings (Black bill and black feathering around bill, whitish face, red crown and nape, black around and behind whitish eyes) unmistakable. Back, tail, and wings mostly black, with white base of primaries obvious in flight and visible as white patch mid-wing when perches. Solid black bib transitions into dark streaking on upper belly, other underparts white along with white uppertail coverts.
Behaviors: The bird flew in once near a peanut feeder and then flew away towards mature cottonwoods along a ditch. It later flew in and fed at the peanut feeder for a few minutes. At one point it battled two Common Grackles intent on the peanuts, emerging victorious. When done feeding it carried a peanut towards the ditch as it left.
Call: Not Heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

All black back eliminates many N.A. woodpeckers. Unique facial pattern rules out other woodpeckers. Lack of black band over crown rules out female.
