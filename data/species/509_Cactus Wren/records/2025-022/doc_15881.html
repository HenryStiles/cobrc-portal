Description of the Bird

Picture(s) are worth a thousand words (see attached).

Large wren with long, thin, slightly decurved, dark bill.

Dark brown cap; white supercillium starting at the top of the bill and running back (widening slightly as it went) to almost meeting behind the head.  White cheeks "speckled" with brown.

Nape and back were brown, with generous amounts of white speckling.

Throat and upper chest were white with more boldly brown specking, turning to streaking at the upper chest.  Breast and belly were white with sparse brown streaking.

Tail was long-ish and brown, but otherwise not really noted.

Bird was found by its song (recording attached). 

Similar Species Discussion

No other species were considered as I recognized the song and later visually recognized the bird as a Cactus Wren.
