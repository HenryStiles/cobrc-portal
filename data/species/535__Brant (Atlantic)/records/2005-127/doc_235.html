Description of the Bird

Small goose, about the size of "Richardson's" Cackling Goose, with all black head, neck, breast; one one side of neck (right side I believe) a faint hint of a white crescent shaped mark was visible near the throat; small black bill; dark legs; pale, mottled brown sides, bright white lower belly and vent (seen from the side); dark gray-brown back and wings with thin white terminal bars on the secondary coverts of the wings noticeable upon studying the photo images.
Behaviors: Walking on shoreline and swimming in shallow water.
Call: not heard
Plumage: juvenal or subadult

Similar Species Discussion

This pale-bellied bird appear to be a juvenal of the Atlantic race "bernicla" (considered by some a separate species Branta horota) due to the lack of a noticeable white mark on the collar and the white terminal edges to the wing coverts. Black Brant would be much darker on the sides. No other goose has a completely black head, neck and breast. Size, shape and plumage are all consistent with Brant rather than a melanistic Cackling Goose or some sort of a hybrid.
