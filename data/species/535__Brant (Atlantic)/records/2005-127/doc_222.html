Description of the Bird

an obvious goose, about the same size as nearby Richardson's Cackling Geese, with a slight shorter neck, and appearing a little dumpier in shape.

The neck of the bird, as far as I was able to tell during the observation, was completely black. The chest of the bird was black, contrasting sharply with the very pale gray/whitish belly and sides, lightest on the flanks, with some darker striations. The undertail coverts and lowermost belly were pure white.

The upperparts of the bird was meduim to dark gray, darkest on the wings, especially the flight feathers. The underwings were the same dark gray color as the upperwings.

The tail was short. It was mostly white, with black tail corners and a small black spot on the central uppertail giving the white a "U" shape, even though the tail appeared mostly white.

The bill was black, as were the feet.
Behaviors: When I saw the bird it was flying with many thousands of Canada and Cackling Geese. I never saw it on the water, though I got a good look of the bird in flight.
Call: none heard
Plumage: atlantic race probable juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Canada and Cackling Geese were ruled out by the black neck with no white cheek patch, black chest, and whitish/gray underparts, as well as the dramatically different tail pattern.

Greater White-fronted Goose was rule out by black neck neck and chest contrasing with paler belly, more white in the tail, black legs, etc.

Young blue phase white geese were rule out by dark underwings and pale belly, etc.
