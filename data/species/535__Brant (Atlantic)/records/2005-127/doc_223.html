Light:  The sun was behind us and a little to the east of us. There were little to no heat waves. Great birding conditions.

Description of the Bird

It looked and acted like a goose. It was a little smaller than the Richardson's Cackling Goose, which the Brant was with. It had a short neck, and bulky.
It had a small bill. It had a black face, including cheek, and neck. One side of the neck had a very small white stripe. It also had very light sides, which contrasted sharply with the dark breast and neck. The back was gray with white edges to the coverts and dark primaries. It had a white undertail covert. I never saw the belly.
Behaviors: When I saw it it was swimming along the far shore of the reservoir for about 15 minutes. It then had disappeared for some time and I refound it about 5 minutes before it went into a cove and I never saw it again.
Call: didn't hear it
Plumage: atlantic probable juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Canada and Cackling Geese - They have white cheeks, where this bird had an all black face. Brant also has a darker back and grayer sides. Brant also has a black chest, but Canada and cackling have light / pale breasts.

Greater White-fronted Goose - This bird has a pinkish bill, white on the front of the face, except juveniles, and a dark brown face. The bird that I saw had a black bill and all black face. Brant has a dark, black breast while Greater White-fronted has lighter, brown chests.

Dark phase Ross's and Snow - The bird that I saw had a white undertail covert and blue Snow has a dark gray undertail covert. The sides of the bird that I saw had a sharp contrast between the breast and the sides. It was also black instead of the dark gray of Ross's and Snow Geese. The adult Snow and Ross's also have white heads and the Brant has a Black head.

All Other Sub-species of Brant - They were all ruled out by sharp contrasting sides, black breast to light sides. It did not have a broad white necklace.

Previous Experience
