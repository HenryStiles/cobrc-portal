Description of the Bird

A dark-backed and dark-headed goose. Bill dark, neck with white necklace that does NOT meet in the front or in the back. Breast dark gray like face color, contrasting strongly with light tan (with some darker brown feathers) breast and contrasting with white udertail. Upper tail coverts white. Rear of tan belly with darker brown feathers forming horizontal bars. Back browish.
Behaviors: Feeding on shore where cattail marsh emptied into Chatfield Reservoir. Seemed to select taller dried grasses. Also seen floating just offshore with Canada Geese. Also seen in flight when the two Brant flew south right past where I was standing.
Call: none heard

Previous Experience

I have seen quite a few "Black Brant" in Alaska, off Washington in early spring, and some Atlantic Brant in late fall in Chincoteague NWR, Virginia. Only a handful seen in CO, all blacks until this pair arrived.

Memory

Additional Information

Comments: There are four currently recognized subspecies of Brant, "Gray-bellied" not being one of them. Of the recognized subspecies, the bird appears in plumage to be attributable to the hrota subspecies, "Atlantic Brant". I sent photographs showing molt on the median wing coverts to Tony Leukering and he agreed the bird was older than a juvenile. He sent the photos to Michael O'Brien who agreed that the bird was as described as the subspecies, hrota, and not as described for the currently undescribed "subspecies", "gray-bellied" Brant.
