Description of the Bird

Two birds, acting as a mated pair. Size identical. Shape identical except one (male) had a larger head. The description of the male is as follows: A small goose not much larger than Mallards, with black legs and feet, very small black bill, black head and neck (except for bright white crescent-shaped mark on each side of neck), well-defined black breast. Lower breast and mid-belly a mixture of gray-brown to off-white. Sides pale gray broadly marked whitish. Lower belly and vent bright white. White tail with small black corners barely noticeable Cold gray-brown upperparts with blackish flight feathers folded neatly above the rump. The female plumage was identical to the male except as noted below: the white mark on teh side of the neck not as bright. The whitish wash to the sides less obvious, giving the underparts a slightly darker look. Border of the black upper breast less sharp.
Behaviors: Eating grass on shore with American Wigeons and Mallards. When flushed off the shoreline into the water, the pair floated among a large group of Richardson's Cackling Geese.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Other small geese in North America have extensive white on head, including Cackling and Barnacle and dark-phase Ross's (white cheeks), white-phase Ross and Snow and Emperor (white heads). Juvenile Emperor Goose is all dark on belly. Plumage matches Brant perfectly so unlikely to be an odd-plumaged goose or a hybrid. Identified as "Atlantic" form due to extensive paleness on sides, and gray rather than black on lower breast and mid-belly. Also, throat is black (white collar marks are disjunt - they connect under the chin for Black Brant).
