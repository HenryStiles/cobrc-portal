Description of the Bird

Observation:   Initially heard quietly tapping deep in a mature juniper tree (? sp.).  When located could only make out a dark silhouette, but recognized it was clearly a sapsucker species (Sphyrapicus).  After a couple of minutes of obscured viewing at ~5m (15ft) with binoculars (8X40 Nikon), the bird moved to an exposed perch in an adjacent locust (? sp.) for an excellent clear view at ~6m (20ft) in bright overcast light.  Total observation lasted about 10 minutes.

 Description:    Medium sized, well proportion woodpecker with a medium weight and length, sharp, and slightly upturned bill.  Basically sapsucker size and shape.

Plumage:  Primarily solid glossy black overall with lemon yellow belly and large bright white wing patches.

Head: Head and upper chest black with the exception of bright white malar and post auricular stripes on the face, and a small, deep red chin and throat patch (entirely surrounded by black).

Upperparts: Back entirely solid black. Large bright white patches on the upper wing, visible when perched.  White rump.

Underparts: Upper chest black and sharply demarcated from lemon yellow lower chest.  Lower belly paler lemon yellow with scalloped greyish yellow flanks.

Tail: Solid black.

Bill / Feet / Eye Color:  Bill about ½ to ¼ head length, medium weight, sharp, and slightly upturned.  Eyes, bill, and feet all dark 

                           Structure:  Well proportioned, medium sized woodpecker with round head and medium length tail.

Similar Species Discussion

Other Sphyrapicus woodpeckers (aka supsuckers) obvious candidates.  All other species, at all ages and sexes, eliminated by solid black back and tail.

Other “black-backed” woodpeckers (ie; Black-backed & White-headed) eliminated by lemon yellow belly and red chin/throat patch.
