Description of the Bird

The two birds were similar enough that I describe them as one bird here. A small banded plover, the bird was pale-backed (in direct contrast to a darker-backed Semipalmated Plover) with a distinct blackish chest band. The bird was pale grayish-brown above and whitish below. The bill was orange-based and black-tipped. The forehead was white and contrasted with a black bar above the forehead. The rest of the crown and much of the face was pale grayish-brown. The head color was separated from the same color on the back by a thin white collar extending up from the shoulders and wrapping around below the nape. Adjacent to the white collar was a complete black ring that included the black chest band - this collar was widest at the shoulders.

The wings and tail were the same color as the upperparts. The underparts were entirely whitish, except for the black chest band. The legs were bright orange.
Behaviors: Foraging on mud in typical start-and-stop plover fashion. The two Piping Plovers got near each other at one point and faced off a bit, but otherwise did not much interact.
Call: none
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Piping Plover has darker legs and bill (and smaller and thinner bill) and usually does not have a complete chest band. Semipalmated Plover is quite similar, only much darker-backed.
