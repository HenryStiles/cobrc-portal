Description of the Bird

the bird was a small plover, significantly smaller than nearby Killdeer. The overall shape was quite rotund, with short legs, very short neck, round body shape, and very small bill contributing to this look. The bill was tiny, quite short and thin but blunt-tipped, and black. The eye was dark. The legs were quite short for a shorebird, and orange.

The bird had a pale sandy colored "helmet". The throat was white and there was a white color that seperated the sandy colored helmet and the sandy colored upperparts. The upperparts were uniform and the same color as the head. The bird had a half-collar that was also the same color as the upperparts. The remainder of the underparts were clean white.
Behaviors: the bird was foraging on mudflats, and did not seem to be in association with any nearby shorebirds
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown but suspect a juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Almost all species of shorebirds were ruled out by the combination of small size, very dumpy shape, very shore neck, small bill, etc.

Snowy Plover was ruled out by the more rotund shape, paler upperparts, shorter and smaller bill, and shorter and orange legs. Semipalmated Plover was ruled out by the much pale upperparts, shorter legs, more rotund shape, and lack of any darker markings on the head.
