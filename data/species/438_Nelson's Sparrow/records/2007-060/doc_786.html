Description of the Bird

Quite small sparrow, noticeably smaller than nearby Vesper Sparrows. Top-heavy, large-headed, flat-crowned, short-tailed structure of ammodramus sparrows. The most distinctive feature, seen as the bird perched facing me for several seconds, was a clean-cut orange breast with very faint dark streaking and a horizontal lower border separating it from a clean white belly. One member of our group, unfamiliar with this species, compared this to a Pectoral Sandpiper's breast (referring to the clean, abrupt lower border.) Orange was also visible on the face, with a gray auricular and dark eye-line, and dark crown creating the facial pattern when the bird turned its head sideways. The most anterior part of the blue-gray nape was visible as its head was briefly turned sideways. The bill was moderately long and fairly sharp for a sparrow. I didn't get a clear side view, but when seen at close range flying away I had the brief impression of a bluish-gray nape, dark back, orange-brown rump, and short, wiry tail. I had this view about 4 times.
Behaviors: The bird popped up onto the top of a weed stalk as our large group approached, facing us. It flushed into thicker grass and weeds, where it held tight as we circled and slowly closed in on it, flushing only as someone got within a few meters of it. It repeated this about three more times before flying to much taller, more dense grass along the floodplain of the Arikaree river where we could not pursue it.
Call: Not Heard
Plumage: Adult nelsoni

Similar Species Discussion

Oddly top-heavy, large-headed, short-tailed look plus small size and spiky spread tail rules out non-ammodramus sparrows. Seaside Sparrow too large, lacks orange tone to breast, shows dark body and contrasting white throat. Baird's Sparrow lacks orange breast and has distinct dark streaks on breast. Henslow's Sparrow is yellowish-buffy on breast, not orange, shows darker streaking, and lacks gray nape. Additionally, Henslow's shows more of a rufous tone on back and on wing coverts when seen in flight from above. Le Conte's Sparrow buffy-yellow below but doesn't show orange breast. In flight from above, Le Conte's shows straw-yellow nape and rump and more obvious streaking on back. I saw what was probably a Grasshopper Sparrow flush a few times earlier in the morning and was left with the impression of a lighter-backed bird than this one. Grasshopper Sparrow buffy-breasted but not this orange, and breast patch not as cleanly broken from white belly. Breast streaking too fine for Saltmarsh Sharp-tailed Sparrow, which also has less complete orange breast and weaker boundary between breast and belly.
