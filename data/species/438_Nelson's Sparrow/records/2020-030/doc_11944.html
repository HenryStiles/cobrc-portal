Description of the Bird

We were standing about 5 feet away from the willows at Pelican Point scanning for gulls. Something feeding low in the willows caught my eye and I turned around to get my binoculars on it. Immediately I noticed the orange hues on the face and the gray under the eye. I alerted Mitchel and Kathy to turn around very slowly as to not flush the bird. At that point I was not sure what it was but knew it was important to get photos and document. In my head I was running through possible sparrows and landed on either LCSP or NESP. It continued to feed low in the willows and would hop out onto the mudflat and on the ground in the willows as it fed. We sat down as it continued this behavior and took plenty of photos. 

Key identification marks noted in the field and after review of photos were: 

Structure: Small size about the size of a LISP, short tailed, front heavy appearance

Plumage: Head; Crown with black strips and gray medial crown stripe, ending at clean gray nape. Orange extremely noticable above eye, no marking in superlores or line in front of eye, black eye line extending behind the eye ending at clean gray nape. Below the eye is a clean gray area lined by orange. Throat clean light orange. Body: orange in breast and extending down the flanks all the way to the undertail, clean white belly. Nape mentioned but clean gray. Back with 4 noticable white stripes on. Wings: no wing bars or noticable markings on wings, rufous in color. Tail: some orange/rufous noticable in tail but not prominent.

  

Similar Species Discussion

Similar species would be mostly those of ammospiza but other more common similar species treated as well.

SALS: By range, habitat requirments, and amount of orange in breast and flanks

LCSP: Oringinally thought we had found a LCSP, When I opened the Sibleys app to compare the two the first plate on NESP is for the atlantic sub-species which is draber overall and lacks the distinct streaking on the flanks and breast of the Interior sub-species. As I continued to look at my photos and compare our bird to the plate I was focused in on the clean gray nape of our bird which was not matching LCSP. I sent the photo to Scott S for a confirmation and he confirmed it was a NESP and not a LCSP. I scrolld down on my phone and found the plate for the interior sub-species of NESP that matched our bird. Other seperating ID marks; white stripes on back rather then patterened; clean gray nape mentioned, lack of white edging on teritals (although this is a field mark in sibleys, I had a hard time finding it represented in photos in Macauly); oranger wash vs more yellow buff color, more prominent gray patch below eye. 

HESP: White stripes on back and no scaling, no white eye ring, orange face and in breast and flanks.

LISP: Orange wash extremely noticable and gray patch under eye ruling out this similar sized and shaped sparrow.

GRSP: overall size and shape; smaller bill; streaking in breast and flanks; orange wash.

Previous Experience

My experience with this species is limited. I have heard them singing (albeit queitly) on their breeding grounds in Maine, but only saw them at a great distance. I have no experience with LCSP.
