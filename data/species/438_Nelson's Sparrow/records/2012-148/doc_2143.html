Description of the Bird

This well-seen sparrow had an orangish face with a dull gray contrasting auricular. The crown had a broad gray central stripe surrounded on each side by a broad orangish supercilium. The nape was unstreaked and gray. The back was brown with strongly contrasting white vertical streaks. The breast and flanks were orange. The breast was finely streaked with dark gray as were the orangy flanks.
Behaviors: Skulking in bulrushes, mostly at the edge of a marsh where they had been accidentaly been sprayed and killed during the eradication of the nearby Russian olives. This bird was mostly responding to 23 people trying to see it so its behavior doesn't mean too much.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

LeConte's Sparrow is closest, and the differences are on the unstreaked gray nape in NESP vs. streaked gray nape in LCSP, the streaking on the orange breast and flanks in NESP, no streaks on LCSP breast. The center stripe on the crown of this bird, NESP, was solid gray, vs. the orange central crown stripe on the LCSP. The NESP was overall a darker bird and it had a thinner bill than the LCSP which I have seen three times during the week.
