Description of the Bird

A medium sized sparrow. Bright, thick orange eyeline and malar region continuing round to the area behind ear. This orange completely surrounded a gray auricular patch. The median crown stripe was gray, and the back of the neck was gray and unstreaked. The upper breast was orange with streaks, and a very sharp dividing line between the upper breast and the very white breast and underparts. The flanks were also streaked orange. The back was darkish brown, but with two bright white streaks running down the upper back on each side. The wing coverts were dark, but noticeably edged with orange. The eye was dark with a light, thin eyering. The bill was average sized and light, and the legs were light.
Behaviors: Flitting around in quite tall marsh plants at the edge of a small pond.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Closest species is Saltmarsh Sharp-tailed Sparrow. The bill on this bird looked smaller than shown in field guides for Saltmarsh. Also, the habitat is very far from any saltmarsh.
From Le Conte's Sparrow by gray (not light) median crown stripe, and gray unstreaked back of neck.
From other Ammodramus by the bright orange face.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Have seen this species at Brandon Marsh west of Winnipeg where it breeds once. Have seen it three times near High Island Texas on spring trips. Have seen Le Conte's also at Brandon Marsh, and three to four times in Colorado mostly at John Martin.
