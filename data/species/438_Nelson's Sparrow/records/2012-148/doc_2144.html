Habitat

The bird was in bullrushes on the edge of a medium sized pond.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Binoculars

Light:  Sunny conditions. The sun was behind me as I faced the bird.

Description of the Bird

A small flat-headed sparrow with orange-yellow on the supercillium, supraloral, throat, side of neck, breast and sides. It had a dark lateral crown stripe with a light gray median crown stripe. It had a dark postocular stripe and moustachial stripe. It was gray on the upper back and on the ear coverts and a narrow strip of gray on the sides. It had a cleanly separated white belly. It had feint dark streaks on the sides and breast.
Behaviors: The bird was hidden in the bullrushes on the edge of the pond. When I approached it popped up into plain view for a few seconds and then flew about 15 feet and remained out of sight. A few minutes later when I returned to the area it repeated this behavior. I never heard it make a sound.
Call: bird was silent
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

At first I thought that this was a Le Conte's sparrow that had been seen in the area. When others viewed my photos they said it was a Nelson's sparrow.

Resources Used

Several members of the COBirds email group helped confirm the identification.

Previous Experience

Prior to this sighting I have never seen Nelson's, Le Contes, or Henslow's sparrows.
