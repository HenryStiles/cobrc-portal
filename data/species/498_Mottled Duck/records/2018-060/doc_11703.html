Description of the Bird

Shape and size similar to that of Mallard. Size, as judged by size compared with nearby Mallards, consistent with a female bird. When first seen, bird seemed to possibly associate with a group of young, Mallard-like, 1/2 grown ducks. When seen second time (and between my visits, by Peterson and Walbek), the bird did not associate with any other bird.

Body blackish. On body (chest and sides) dull buff feather edging visible. No edging or internal markings could be seen on folded wings or back, which appeared uniformly black. Good views of underparts were observed on first visit, when bird tipped to dabble; vent same color as remainder of underparts (blackish, that is) and sides. Tail without white, appeared concolorous and same color as that of body.

Neck and head contrasted sharply with body, neck and face a warmish tan, buff-tinged in some lights on chin extending onto lower face. More specifically:
Crown black from bill extending to rear crown, but not nape. Nape and hind neck with very narrow blackish stripe visible only from directly behind. Some diffuse dusky spreading a bit more laterally onto hind neck. From side view, hind neck would have been judged pale, same color as neck. Eyeline black, thickest in front of eye, and extending about as far behind eye as in front of eye (ie, eyeline extended backwards about same distance as that from eye to bill). Some fine streaking present on sides of neck and extending onto face just below eye. Chin and lower half of auriculars unstreaked, tan to buff-tinged, pale brown. Bill dusky, showing hints of olive and perhaps even a bit of orange when in bright light. There was a small black spot at gape that was difficult to see due, in part, to lack of contrast with bill. After 55 minutes of viewing on second visit, I was able to see a blue speculum with a very narrow white fringe behind and no white along anterior edge. 

Similar Species Discussion

1) Mallard. Very much too dark in body and tail for this species, even in its darker summer garb
2) Mexican Duck/Mexican x Mottled Duck. Eliminated, in part, by all traits consistent with Mottled Duck. More specifically, plumage too dark for female Mexican Duck, speculum pattern wrong for Mexican Duck, lack of streaking on chin/lower face wrong for Mexican Duck. Female Mexican Duck should show some pale edging to back feathers.
