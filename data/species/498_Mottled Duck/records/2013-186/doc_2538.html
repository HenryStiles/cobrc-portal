Description of the Bird

A Mallard-type duck, darker-bodied than any Mexican Duck I've seen. Entire body blackish-brown, including vent. Head and neck contrastingly straw colored, particularly pale on chin and cheek. Eyestripe fairly faint and crown not as dark as that of Mexican Duck or Am Black Duck. Speculum blue-ish in color, with barely visible pale rear-border to the speculum (seen only if you squint at a couple of the photos). Underwing coverts bright white and unmarked. The bill color looks olive in some photos, and at least partly orange in others, which is confusing.... I also think that I can see the dark spot near the gape in some photos, but this could be artifact. Size and shape similar to that of Mallards. Tail as dark as body.
Behavior: Loafing with Mallards, then flying off with them
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

American Black Duck should have more purple speculum, some speckling on underwing coverts, darker crown and eyestripe, duskier face (due to fine streaking). Mexican Duck should be paler bodied, and in my experience, the breast in good color (of male) always shows some warmer hues. Also, Mexican Duck has more prominent posterior border to speculum and obvious pale anterior border to speculum.
