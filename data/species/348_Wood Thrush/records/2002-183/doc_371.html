Description of the Bird

Plump, medium-sized songbird - shape and spotting distinctive for thrushes. Rufous upperparts brightest on crown and nape, browner on wings and tail. Dark eye, white eye-ring, black-and-white streaked auriculars. Underparts white with heavy black spotting extending from throat onto flanks and belly. Unmarked white undertail coverts.
Behaviors: N/A
Call: none
Plumage: first basic

Similar Species Discussion

Larger size, bright rufous upperparts, and extensive black spotting of underparts eliminated Catharus thrushes. Shorter bill and tail, spotting rather than streaking of underparts eliminated the similarly colored Brown Thrasher.
