Description of the Bird

Just slightly smaller than American Robin with "reddish-brown" upperparts and black spotting on the white underparts.
Behaviors: Sitting still in a tree for a long period of time and then going to the ground to forage.
Call: N/A
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

All other thrushes were ruled out by the brightly colored "reddish-brown" back and heavy dark complete round spots on the underparts. Really not any other species that would be likely found in Colorado that look like this bird. Hermit, Gray-cheeked and Swainson's Thrush do not have solid black, large, complete spots on their breast down to near the undertail coverts and don't really approach the same upperpart coloring. Veery comes the closest in upperparts coloring but facial pattern and breast spotting is nothing like Wood Thrush. Brown and Long-billed Thrashers have much longer tails and bills, no bold white eye-ring. The Thrashers also show wing bars.
