Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 8x32BN Binoculars and a Swarovski Spotting Scope
Distance:  We got the best looks at it from about 90 feet away.
Light:  The sun was out and to the left of us when we saw the bird sitting on a branch.

Description of the Bird

Light brownish orange head, neck, back, and tail. Large white eye ring. Cheek darker than the head and body. Dark eye. Bright white belly and breast with large dark spots. Short tail. Pink legs.
Behaviors: It usually stayed low in the bushes. When I got to see it the first time it was thrashing around on the ground under a tree. Then it flew out of the bushes into a tree where it didnt move for a few minutes. Then it flew into a different tree and sat there for about ten minutes. Then it flew and gave the call into another tree and I never saw or heard it after that.
Call: It gave a pit pit pit call when it was in flight and a few times after it landed in a tree. It also gave a series of gurgling phrases. It was very distinctive and something I had never heard before this bird. I knew it was nothing I was familiar with.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Brown Thrasher Bright brownish orange head, back, and tail. Gray cheek. Long curved bill. Off white belly and breast with dark spotting. Two dark wing bars. Yellow eye. Long tail.

Hermit Thrush Gray olive head, face, and back. Rufous tail. Heavy spotting on the breast. White belly.

Swainsons Thrush Small cream colored eye ring. Gray olive back and head. Cream face and throat. Cream colored breast with dark spots. White belly.

Veery A reddish brown head, back, and tail. It has a cream colored breast and throat with light brown spotting. White belly. Pink legs. Not much of an eye ring.
