Description of the Bird

I caught this bird at the Barr Lake banding station. It was a large, brown thrush, with distinctly rufous upperparts, particularly the crown and nape, a white eye ring, distinctive black-and-white streaked auriculars, white underparts with bold, distinct, black spotting on the chest and sides. The belly was white. The tail was shorter than the wing chord, the legs were pink, and the eyes were dark.

I determined the ossification of the skull, which was complete, so the bird was a full adult (at least 1.25 yrs of age).
Behaviors: caught in mist net
Call: none heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Brown Thrasher ruled out by shorter bill, dark eyes, pink legs, tail shorter than wing chord. The Catharus thrushes ruled out by larger size, chunkiness, the distinct black spots on the white chest, and the distinctive auriculars.
