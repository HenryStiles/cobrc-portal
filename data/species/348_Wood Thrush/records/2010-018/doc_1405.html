Description of the Bird

Pink-legged thrush with strongly rufous tail, back, and richest on the crown of head. Underparts white with bold, somewhat arrowhead-shaped dark spots on breast and flanks. Bold white eyering, bill bicolored, pale yellowish at base and dark gray distally. Face grisled with tightly marked, dark grayish streaks.
Behaviors: Spent most of the time on or very near the ground foraging in the litter and damp soil. When flushed, flew low and only a short distance. In same are on three days that I saw it.
Call: nothing heard by me
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The only thrush that comes close to the WOTH is Veery. The easternmost subspecies of Veery has a very rufous back and although it can have a hint of an eyering it is never as wide or as white as in WOTH. Also the spotting on Veery never is as bold and never comes down on the breast and flanks like on the WOTH.
