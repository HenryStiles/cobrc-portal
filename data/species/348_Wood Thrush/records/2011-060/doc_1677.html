Description of the Bird

I did not see this bird well so the ID is solely based on its song described above. It was very close in size to nearby Evening Grosbeaks and had a thinner bill than the Grosbeaks.
Behaviors: The bird sang atop a tall cottonwood and only moved when flying away as a Cooper's Hawk flew by.
Call: A flute like "ee-o-lay" sometimes preceded by a low soft husky "tuk-tuk-tuk." Variant songs were "ee-o-lay" immediately followed by a rising "ee-yeeyeeyee" or a lower "yer" note.
Plumage: Assumed adult

Similar Species Discussion

The most similar sounding thrush to Wood is probably the Swainson's. Its call rises but with shorter slurred & less distinct notes.
Hermit Thrush call is introduced by a long drawling note then much shorter slurred notes in contrast to the distinct "ee-o-lay" notes in all the variant calls I heard from this Wood Thrush.
