Description of the Bird

Obvious thrush species from shape and behaviour with orange brown nape and back , very heavily spotted black down to almost the vent over a white breast. Appeared to be smaller than an adjacent American Robin.See photo.
Behaviors: Feeding on a grass lawn , taking a few hops , turning head to listen for insects and then quickly extracting a worm .Where there was leaf litter , it would turn over leaves looking for food.
Call: No calls heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other thrush has this combination of orangy brown upperparts and very heavy black spotting of underparts.
