Description of the Bird

I caught this bird at the banding station. It was a large, brown thrush, with distint rufous tones to the upperparts, particularly the crown and nape. The bill was dark with a pink-based mandible. There was a distinct white eye ring and distinctive black-and-white streaked auriculars. The underparts were white with bold black spots on the chest, sides, and upper belly. The legs were pink and the tail was shorter than the wing chord.

I skulled the bird and determined that it was not completely ossified, thus an immature. This, then, agreed with the age that I assessed from noting the distinct orange tips to all of the greater and median coverts. This feature alone enabled positive separation from the adult that I had caught and banded only the day before!
