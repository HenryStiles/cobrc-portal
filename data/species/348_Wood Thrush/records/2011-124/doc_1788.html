Description of the Bird

Spotted thrush with extensive rufous dorsally. The dark eye was surrounded by a broad white eyering. The auricular seemed streaked with dark. The crown, nape, back, tail, and wings were bright rufous. The legs were pinkish. The white ventral side was boldly spotted with black.
Behaviors: Skulking on the ground and once on a log in the darkest shadows mostly. Flew once low and out of site in forest of Russian olives.
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing comes too close.

Veery is smaller and without the extensive large spotting.

Ovenbird - there was one in the same area and it is superficially similar but with a different bill (warbler) and breast streaks, not spots, and not rufous on the back
