Description of the Bird

This was a large thrush with rich cinnamon upperparts, bright white underparts, and large dark spots on the breast and flanks. It had large eyes and white eye rings.

see photos
Behaviors: Not much observed except for foraging in dead leaves and broken branches in darker shaded areas beneath large cottonwoods and Russian Olives. It was silent.
Call: None
Plumage: Hatch-year

Similar Species Discussion

Rejected Swainson's Thrush by size, lack of buffiness in the face and eye ring, and cinnamon over all upper parts- unlike russet backed SWTH individuals, and large spots.

Rejected Hermit Thrush by size, richness of color, and large, black spots, and entire cinnamon upperparts.

Rejected Veery by size, richness of color and contrast of the breast spots - these were much more distinct.

Previous Experience

I have seen one other Wood Thrush at CBR in Spring 2010 (also found by Bill Maynard), and have seen several in the field in Eastern Seaboard states and Ohio. I'm not an expert, but this bird was spectacular, and at close range in great lighting.
