Description of the Bird

I initially noted the Wood Thrush as it flew west in the Arickaree River bed on the Walk’s Camp side of the Thompson Ranch property. It had a thrush-like flight, smaller than an American Robin. Its body structure was very thrush-like, a bit bulkier than a Hermit or Swainson’s Thrush. The bird stayed low to the ground and was somewhat secretive (less so than the WOTH I’ve encountered in the Northeast). On this date, it seemed to prefer the dry streambed.

The bird was rusty-red on the back with a sharply contrasting white breast with very bold black spots beginning at the throat and extending all the way down to and including its belly. A somewhat dull eye-ring was noted and the bird was somewhat smudgy looking below the eye.
Behaviors: Skulking and shy. It stayed low to the ground and seemed to like to be out of sight.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

As I initially viewed the bird, my first thought was the more common Veery due to the rust color the bird presented as it flew away from me. Once it landed, it turned and faced me with the bold black spots. That eliminated Veery and the rust back eliminated the other thrushes.
