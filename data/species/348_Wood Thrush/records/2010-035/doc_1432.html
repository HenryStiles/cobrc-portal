Description of the Bird

Bird was noticeably smaller than American Robin but larger than White-crowned Sparrow.

Upperparts were brownish rufous becoming a brighter cinnamon rufous on nape & crown.
Underparts were white with large black spots on breast and belly. Undertail coverts were white. Auriculars were heavily streaked with broader dark streak separting auriculars from whitish throat. Tail much shorter than Brown Thrasher's.

Eye was dark with large white eye ring, legs and bill were flesh colored especially lower mandible.
Behaviors: Bird mostly sang high in 20'+ scrub oaks, occasionally foraging on ground where it hopped about in leaf litter.
Call: Song built around a flute like three note "eee-o--lay!" Variants "eee-o-lay-yeeyeeyeeer" with last syllable highest or "eee-o-lay--yer" with last note lower. This song was often preceded by low husky "tuk-tuk-tuck-tuk" of 4 to 6 notes. Also a loud "pit-weet! pit-weet-weet-weet-weet!" not unlike a Curve-billed Thrasher.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Hermit Thrush has rufous tail but a dull brown nape & crown. Belly is not usually as heavily speckled as Wood Thrush. Hermit's song usually introduced by by one long flute like note instead of the 3 note introduction of Wood Thrush song.
