Habitat

riparian with non-native shrubs and trees and eastern cottonwoods and crack willows, plus planted large ponderosa pines, englemann spruces here and there.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10x42 bins
Distance:  about 30 feet both times.
Light:  Light was good with sun behind me.

Description of the Bird

bright rufous thrush with large spots on breast and flanks. I saw the bird twice and it was flying away from me both times, but I did see the completely rufous head, back, and tail.
Behaviors: Secretive, flushed twice from the ground near dense cover.
Call: none heard
Plumage: breeding

Similar Species Discussion

The only other rufous-backed thrush is a Catharus thrush, Veery, with a totally different structure and more slender build.
