Description of the Bird

This is a large spotted thrush with bright orange-brown crown and nape a large complete whitish eyering, and white breast with bold dark black spots. The legs were pinkish. The wings and tail mostly medium brown. The tail was comparatively short. The bird had a chunky look especially compared to Catharus thrushes. The eye was dark, the face with dark auriculars and the bill was relatively short and light colored.
Behaviors: First seen on the ground at AMEN corner, the bend at Rose Pond where most migrants are seen. It perched for about a minute on an open branch, uncharacteristically. Later in the day it became much more secretive and it flew to near the top of a taller tree where it remained motionless.
Call: silent
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

There are no other look alike species in North America. All of the Catharus thrushes have smaller spotting that stops before the breast.
