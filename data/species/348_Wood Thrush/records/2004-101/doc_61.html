Description of the Bird

An obvious brown thrush, it was larger than a Swainson's Thrush in the area and much redder. Though I saw it only briefly, the features that I did see were enough to convince me of the ID.

The crown, nape, and back were bright orangish. The dark eye was surrounded by a bold white eyering. The auriculars were streaked black and white. The underparts were white with large, bold black spots.
Behaviors: Skulking in dense cover in the drainage north of the pond.
Call: No vocalizations heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

AMRO is eliminated by color of underparts and upperparts; all Catharus thrushes eliminated by bold white eye ring and bold black spotting on white underparts; BRTH eliminated by head pattern, eye color, and that the bird had too short a tail
