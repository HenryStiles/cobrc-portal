Description of the Bird

This bird dwarfs the broad-tailed hummingbirds that frequent the feeder with noticably slower wing beats. She has a bronze rump, obvious long white supercilium. Dark tail with large white corners on the upper and underside. Overall, dull, uniform gray below, dull green above fading into bronze rump which then fades to black tail w/ white corners.
Behaviors: Bird returns to feeder about every 30-40 minutes every day for the past few weeks. Takes a drink, then flies to the same Ponderosa branch not far from the feeder to briefly preen, stretch and wipe bill on branch, then flies off.
Call: none
Plumage: summer
