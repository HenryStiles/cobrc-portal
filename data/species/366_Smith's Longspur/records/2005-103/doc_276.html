Habitat

Dry upland grassland/agricultural. The bird was in short grass along the edge of the road between the road and a wheat stubble field

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x50 Bushnell Binoculars and a 15x to 45x scope
Distance:  about 50ft. at closest
Light:  I was looking West with the sun to my back. The sky was partly cloudy. Conditions were good.

Description of the Bird

Overall the bird was brown above and quite buffy below. It was about six inches long, slightly smaller than the many Horned Larks in the area.
Bill: Cnonical with a thin appearance
Crown: Brown and finely streaked
Face: Grayish auricular area with a distinct cheek outline broken at the rear and a buffy supercillium. There was a faint hint of a pale eye ring.
Wings: Overall the wings were brown. The primary projection was long. There were two white wing bars the most prominent being the shoulder with a faint but noticable bar below.
Back: Uniformly brown and streaked, the same color as the wings.
Underparts: The breast and belly were completely buffy with faint streaking and the chin was pale.
Tail: Only the outer two rectrices were white and seen only when the bird banked in flight.
Behaviors: The bird was seen foraging in the short grass along the side of the road occasionally coming right to the edge of the vegetation where it was easily seen. It was not vocal while on the ground, the call was heard only in flight.
Call: A dry rattle heard in flight
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Lapland Longspur has less white in the tail and shows a contrasting chestnut wing patch, it is also a grayer bird. McCown's (which were present in good numbers in the area) and Chestnut-collared show much more white in the tail and have a much shorter primary projection and are also grayer birds
