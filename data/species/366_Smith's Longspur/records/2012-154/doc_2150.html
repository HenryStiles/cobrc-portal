Description of the Bird

These birds were identified after the fact, see comments.

Buffy underneath. Buff tan head. Long wings in flight. Tail with white tips on corners visible on photo. Lesser coverts white contasting with rest of wing, the character to look for in longspurs in flight to separate SMLO along with the tail pattern if seen from other longspur species. Rufous visible on inner primaries.
Behaviors: I flushed these birds along with a couple others and they flew a good distance before coming down. I went to try to get a photograph of them perched but I didnt see them again.
Call: They called but see comments as to why I don't remember the call.
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Both CCLO and MCLO have very distinctive tail patterns in flight. I was chasing CCLOs at the time and have a good photo of their tails with lots of white and the dark inverted T. MCLO also shows a lot of white unlike these birds. Both of these species have much shorter wings.

Lapland Longspur - I haven't submitted this for a long time because I wasn't sure it I could separate these birds 100% from LALO based only on the tail, but LALOs never show the white coverts in flight. They shouldn't be as buffy brown underneath (see photo).

Resources Used

I looked at every field guide I own, especially The Sparrows of the United States and Canada by James Rising. I procrastinated but finally asked for Dr. Rising's e-mail address and asked him if he agreed that these were SMLOs. Here is the question I sent to him and his response.

The tail pattern looks more like Smith's to me. Don't think that it is a C-c Thanks for sending the photo.

Additional Information

Comments: Report actually made from the one photo I was able to take of the birds in flight. When I took the photograph, I was trying to photograph a flock of CCLOs in the same general area, about 4 birds that landed near these two. I incorrectly assumed these two birds were the same species and unfortunately at the time I didn't pick out the noticeably different call notes of SMLO because I thought by association that the birds were CCLOs. It wasn't until about 6 months later when Tony Leukering asked me for photographs of my CCLOs to document them for Park County that I saw this single photo of birds that were obviously not CCLOs. After studying the Rising Sparrow Book, I thought there was a good chance they were actually SMLO but the mountain park location is not a place I would expect them so I sent the photo to Mark Peterson for his opinion and he thought they were SMLOs too. It wasn't until last week that I sent them to Jim Rising and although his response is brief he in fact seems to say they are SMLOs which is what I believe they are. They have been recorded in CA and in AZ in September and Oct, so the date is not too early for them in the west. I planned to go back to the same area in late September, this year, but I never made it. For what it is worth, both Brandon Percival and Rachel Hopper, two former CBRC members also think they are SMLOs.
