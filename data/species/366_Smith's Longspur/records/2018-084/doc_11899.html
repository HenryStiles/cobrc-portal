Description of the Bird

I never heard the bird call for sure.  There were LALOs calling and picking out the SMLO amongst the chatter as they would flush and land was just not happening.  When I first saw the bird I immediately knew it was not a LALO just by the very buffy appearance.  My initial thought was Smith's but certainly needed to rule out other species, mainly CCLO.  The bright white wingbar(s), only one was really visible, stood out in favor of SMLO, and when the bird flew and landed it clearly had just two white outer tail feathers, see photos.

Similar Species Discussion

Chestnut-collared Longspur was the only other regularly occurring (and not regular in January) species I really considered at the time, January on the plains of CO.  The tail pattern ruled out CCLO.  The other species that I considered after seeing the bird were VESP, AMPI, and SPPI.  VESP was easily ruled out on overall buffy color and lack of obvious eyering, the same reasons ruled out SPPI.  AMPI is maybe the most like this species at this time of year and although it would be very unlikely at this location, SMLO is more unlikely.  I would expect R2 to have very limited white on AMPI whereas this bird had what appeared to be an entirely white R2.  Also, AMPI has a short primary projection compared to that of this bird.

Resources Used

No resources were used, I did send it to a couple of others who agreed with the identification at the time. 

Previous Experience

At the time I had only seen three Smith's before seeing this bird.  Since then, I have seen close to 100 and all of those were winter birds as well so similar plumage.  I have seen 100s to 1000s of CCLOs in similar plumage, and 1000s of AMPIs.
