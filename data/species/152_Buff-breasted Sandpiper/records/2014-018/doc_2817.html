Description of the Bird

The buff-breasted sandpiper was foraging along the northwest shore of Jackson Lake, along with other shorebirds.  It had the characteristic buffy-tan, small-headed, and short-billed appeareance.  (Note:  the buffy-red appearance in the photo is exaggerated by the light of the setting sun.)

Similar Species Discussion

The buffy-tan color and short bill distinguish this buff-breasted sandpiper from other shorebirds.
