Description of the Bird

I saw a distant juvenile Buff-breasted Sandpiper, on the expansive mud flats or Adobe Creek (Blue) Reservoir.

The shorebird had an upright posture, was completely buffy in coloration underneath, with a few dark speckles on the breast near the bend in its wing. The face was also buffy and the cap was darker gray. The bill was short, thin, and dark. The legs were yellowish and longish. I identified it as a juvenile based on the back pattern, which had a scaly affect. The bird was back from the edge of the water, on the drying mud, and near a large flock of Long-billed Curlews. The bird flew off with a mixed spcies flock of peeps. I noticed that it was long-winged and much larger than the peeps. The wings, while the bird was in flight, did not show any wing stripe. The underwings were white with broad dark trailing edge to the primaries and secondaries. I wanted to photograph the LBCUs and I thought that the BBSA would be easy to refind, but I never saw it again.
Behaviors: Walking in the higher, drier part of the mud, by itself, except when it flushed it joined a small flock of about 8-9 peeps which passed over my head.
Call: non heard
Plumage: junenile

Similar Species Discussion

Ruff- a bit larger bird, with a longer heavier bill. The BBSA was more slender and had a more upright posture, with a delicate look to it.
