Description of the Bird

See included photos. Mid-sized, compact-bodied, yellow-legged sandpiper. Fairly short, straight dark bill, blank looking buffy face with large appearing dark eye gives bird a dove-like cute look. Crown with dense, dark spots. Upper breast and upper flanks with dark spots against buffy background. Crisp, pale edging on upperpart feathers indicate juvenile age. Underparts mostly unmarked buffy white. Wings fairly short, just reaching tail tip.
Behaviors: Foraging on mudflats. Often far from the water's edge, but occasionally within 10 meters of water. Never seen in the shallow water.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow legs rule out many shorebirds. Among the remaining candidates, it is somewhat plover-like for a sandpiper, but lacking many plumage features of the yellow-legged small plovers. Both yellowlegs have much longer bills, different body structure, and substantial plumage differences including a lack of the overall buffy tone and blank buffy face. Solitary Sandpiper legs more greenish, plus has longer bill and major plumage differences including lack of the overall buffy tone and blank buffy face. Other yellow-legged sandpipers such as Spotted Sandpiper, Surfbird, Rock Sandpiper, and Purple Sandpiper show many plumage and structural differences including lack of the overall buffy tone and blank buffy face. Least Sandpiper much smaller and shows major plumage differences including lack of the overall buffy tone and blank buffy face. Pectoral Sandpiper longer billed and shows major plumage differences including lack of the overall buffy tone and blank buffy face. Juvenile Ruff larger and has longer bill. Dowitchers and Stilt Sandpiper have much longer bills and show major plumage differences including lack of the overall buffy tone and blank buffy face.
