Description of the Bird

They were medium-sized shorebirds.

Their posture was more erect than for other shorebirds.

The bill was medium-short, stout, straight, dark.

The cap was medium-dark, with thin black streaks.

The face was a uniform buff, with a whitish eyering around a bold, black eye.

The upperparts were scaly, a light brownish black.

The throat and complete breast were buffy, mostly unstreaked, but with some speckling on the sides and very faint speckling forming a breast band.

The legs were yellowish, not as bright as for a yellowlegs.
Behaviors: During my entire observation, they were always standing or walking on solid ground (damp sand).
Call: none heard
Plumage: not sure

Similar Species Discussion

The very buffy breast and overall color, the erect posture, and the restriction to solid ground (i.e. out of the water) ruled out other shorebirds.
