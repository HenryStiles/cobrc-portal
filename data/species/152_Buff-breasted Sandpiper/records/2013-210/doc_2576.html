Description of the Bird

I was scanning through rather large numbers of Pec Sanpipers when I saw a bird with a clear buffy chest. After my heart stopped pounding, I realized the preening bird was a BB Sand. The bird's underparts were a ochre-buff and this color extended from throat to at least mid belly (unlike Sharp-tailed Sands rusty-buff chest band). The face was plain except for the white eyering. The crown was streaked almost black and white, very finely. The back was scaly in appearance, with feather centers blackished, edged in white to buff. Bill was short and dark. Legs yellowish. Bird a tad smaller than Pecs and more finely built. After finding first individual (the preening bird, or at least preening initially), I found the second bird nearby. Since there was nearly complete turnover in birds from when BB Sands were found earlier this fall at Jackson Reservoir, I presume these were new individuals.
