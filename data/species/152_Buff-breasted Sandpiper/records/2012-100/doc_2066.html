Description of the Bird

Small to medium sized shorebird (larger than Baird's, smaller than L Yellowlegs) with upright posture for most of observation. Bright ochre-buff chest and upper belly fading near white by legs. Plain ochre-buff face in which black eye stood out. Larger and longer legged than Baird's Sands. Yellow legs. Back dull, more like adult, not as bright and scaly as juv. When lifted wings, bright white beneath. When flew, rump solid colored. Bill proportionately short for non-plover shorebird of its size.
Behaviors: Feeding by walking and picking on mudflat amongst hundreds of other shorebirds, mostly peeps and yellowlegs.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

None really. Upland Sandpiper similar in some ways, but not buff beneath and is larger. Baird's present were smaller, have more a face pattern, dark legs, etc.
