Description of the Bird

Medium to small shorebird larger than Baird's Sandpiper and smaller than Lesser Yellowlegs, with strong buff wash to head and breast, and pale buff wash to belly, with mottled gray-to-brown upperparts strongly marked with silver and gold tips to scapulars. Longish yellow legs and short straight bill. Lack of white in wings or tail in flight. Lack of pattern on face. Upright, plover-like posture.
Behaviors: Feeding on mudflats with killdeer-like movements.
Call: none
Plumage: adult and juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Juv. Baird's Sandpiper is smaller with black legs. Sharp-tailed Sandpiper darker rufous on scapulars and crown, heavier-bodies, and marked supercilium. Juvenile Ruff larger with black eyeline. Basic-plumaged Mountain Plover is shorter-billed and lacks pale edgings to scapulars.
