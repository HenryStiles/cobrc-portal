Description of the Bird

Small to medium-size shorebirds, generally buffy colored with a scaly appearance on the upperparts created by dark centered feathers with buff-white edging. Underparts were relatively unmarked and buffy on the throat and breast, becoming light buff or whitish on the belly. Sides of upper chest and hindnect finely streaked, extending onto the crown. The plain face was buffy. Eyes black. Bill relatively thin, short, and entirely black. The legs were yellow. Wings extended to tail tip if not just a little past.
Behaviors: Walking, resting, and feeding on dry mud flat about 30 feet from the waterline.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Juvenile Ruff would be much larger with a longer bill and noticeable eyeline.
