Description of the Bird

Overall buff most noticeably on face and breast.
Relatively short dark bill
Yellow legs
Dark spots on sides of breast.
At times, upright posture.
Small head look.
Scaly back with dark centers to feathers with fresh thin whitish fringes, therefore a juvenile
In flight bright white underwings.
Behaviors: Probed mud, mostly in wet mud areas. First seen on higher drier sandy substrate.

Fed with Least and Baird's Sandpipers and many Killdeer
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Only Ruff is similar but it has much longer bill with drooping tip. Ruff usually has light feathering at base of the bill. Ruff doesn't have the dark spotting at the sides of its breast. Ruff is at least 2 inches larger more if it is a male.
