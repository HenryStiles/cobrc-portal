Description of the Bird

Hatch year bird that was a medium sized sandpiper, about the size of a Dunlin.  As a migrant, the birded had stopped over to forage along the sandy/mud beaches of Barr Lake.  It did not go into the water.  Its striking plumage was highlighted by dark-centered scapulars edged with creamy-white fringes imparting a scaley pattern to the back.  The buffy breast with fine edging to breast feathers was immediately obvious.  The head exhibited a finely streaked crown contrasting with a plain, plale face dominated by a prominent black eye.  Its yellow legs, moderate in length, were strikingly obvious.  The black bill was straight and fairly short which, along with its posture, gave a look that was somewhat reminicent of a plover.  The bird had a "gentle" look that is characteristic of this species.  No vocalization was heard.

Recognition was immediate as I have seen scores of this species in Texas during migration as well as sightings in California (rare) as well as Montana and several other states.

Similar Species Discussion

Upland Sandpiper might be considered, but it has a different posture.  The bird was too large for a Least Sandpiper.  I really had no thoughts of a different species since I am so familiar with Buff-breasted Sandpipers.

Extensive.  I have seen large numbers of migrants in Texas.  In California I have seen and photographed the species on several occasions.  I have submitted a rare bird report of the species in Montana to the MBRC and have had the sighting accepted.
