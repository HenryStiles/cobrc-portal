Description of the Bird

Bird was secretive most of the time. Only once did it perch on some vegetation (see photos). Most of the time it was on the ground hiding under a Russian Olive or looking for seeds in the exposed sand/soil out of sight behind the tree. It was associating with American Tree Sparrows and Dark-eyed Juncos.

A smallish sparrow with a compact shape, large flat crowned head and short tail. Dull pinkish seed bill with buffy/brown auriculars, lores and smudgy streaking on dirty white chest. Obvious white eye ring. The back is mottled tan, brown and back without obvious streaks. The wind has two distinct buffy wing bars with a dirty white, chin, flank and belly. The crown was dark with a light median and lateral crown stripes.

It was windy, 10-20 MPH, so if it vocalized, I didn’t hear it.

Similar Species Discussion

Baird’s Sparrow – darker streaking on back vs tan/brown and black modeling. More markings and color on face vs plainer.

LeConte’s Sparrow – has fine streaking on flanks and chest vs smudgy chest and flank streaks. The face is orange with distinct black post eye line vs a plainer face.

Vesper Sparrow – larger sparrow vs smaller short-tailed sparrow. Distinctive auricular pattern with brown and white vs a buffy brown unmarked.
