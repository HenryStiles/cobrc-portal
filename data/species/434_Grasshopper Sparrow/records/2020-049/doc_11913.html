Description of the Bird

      Sparrow with a rather streaky crown (lacked a prominent light-colored median crown stripe). Had a distinct, white eye-ring and a dark post-ocular stripe with a thin white line above it.  The bill was conical.  Upper mandible of bill was dark; lower mandible was pale.  Both the supercilium and sides of the neck were gray with dark streaks (maroon in color).  Auricular patch was grayish-brown in color.  The breast was buffy in color with sparse, diffuse streaking.  Belly was white.  Sides were lightly streaked.  Tail was short and forked.  Bird was facing us most of the time, but I did notice rufous on the upperparts.  Legs were pink.  

     Aged as an immature based on the rather streaky crown and some sparse streaking on the sides.    

Similar Species Discussion

The identification of Grasshopper Sparrow was straight-forward.  No other sparrows were considered.

Resources Used

No field guides were needed at the time of the sighting.

Previous Experience

      I have had quite a bit of experience with Grasshopper Sparrows, most often in SE Arizona in the winter.  In SW Colorado, I found one previous Grasshopper Sparrow in La Plata County on December 16, 1999.  The location was about 2.5 miles south of Pastorius Reservoir (along CR 305).  I found a Grasshopper Sparrow in Montezuma County on 10/13/18.  My two sightings of Grasshopper Sparrow for La Plata County are the only two reports for the county.  (That was my reasoning for submitting documentation to the CBRC, even though it wasn't required for this species.)
