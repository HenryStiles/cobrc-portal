Description of the Bird

This is to document a late December Grasshopper Sparrow in Colorado. Bob & Bob don't show any December records.

While looking for longspurs on 24 December on Hanover Road in El Paso County, I saw a perched sparrow on a fence so I slowed and stopped. I was shocked to see it was a flat-headed sparrow, for sure an Ammodramus so i started taking lots of photographs. Later, on the camera body, I saw it was a GRSP, based on white central crown stripe border on both sides with broad black markings. The face was dull orangish but without a strong facial pattern and the orange supraloral spot that is the clincher for the I.D. The back of the neck had thin rufous vertical streaks, the back with two white vertical rows contrasting with the rest of the back color. The bill was pink and stout. The tail was short and spiky.
Behaviors: Just sat there on the top rung of a barbed wire fence and was still there when I drove away.
Call: non heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

I tried to make this into a LeConte's (same crown pattern) but there wasn't a gray auricular nor was there any orangish supercilium. I also looked at the description for Nelson's Sparrow but again the face pattern fits perfect for GRSP.

Resources Used

I called Mark Peterson and I asked him if he would check his field guides for other possible Ammo sparrows but then I saw the orange subloral spot and knew it was what I had suspected.

Previous Experience

I have seem 100s of GRSPs, only a few Nelson's and a handfull of LCSPs.
