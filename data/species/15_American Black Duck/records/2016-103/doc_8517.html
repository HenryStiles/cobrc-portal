Description of the Bird

Nick Moore called out "Black Duck!!" I looked thru his scope, and saw one of the darkest Mallard-type ducks I've seen in some time. The body looked nearly black throughout, from tail to base of neck. There were no internal feather markings visible. Tail was same color as body. The head and neck were distincltly contrasting paler grayish brown. There was a narrow dark line thru the eye and a dark crown, the two of which did not meet fore nor aft. The bill was yellow-olive. The face pattern was more distinct and bold than the fuzzy photos would indicate. The wing pattern was never seen. About 5 minutes after Nick found the bird, a large orange hot-air balloon passed over causing widespread panic amongst the waterfowl, and this bird was quickly lost among the thousads of birds present. The face pattern and bill color distinguish this bird from the Am Black Duck at Woods Lake just a week or so before

Similar Species Discussion

Mottled Duck, Mexican Duck, Am Black Duck x Mallard
All should show internal feather markings, and there was no evidence of Mallard phenotype (no green on crown, no curl to tail, the bird was typically Am Black Duck dark, no pale on tail). There was no hint of brown hues on chest as hybrids and Mexis often show. The face was grayer and did not have the "clear" look like a Mottled Duck
