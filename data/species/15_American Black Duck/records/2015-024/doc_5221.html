Description of the Bird

Mallard-type duck. When seated on water, body appeared evenly blackish throughout, excepting neck and head. Body feathers without any internal markings. Tail solidly dark, same color as body. No curled tail feathers. Head and neck paler than body, but grayish not tawny or buff - and not as contrasting as in MEDU or MODU. Head and neck finely streaked throughout. Blackish crown and eyeline. Crown and eyeline did not contrast with face as much as would in Mexi Duck or Mottled, as base color darker. No dark near gape. A bit larger than most surrounding male Mallards.
In flight, underparts (belly through chest) dark. Underwing largely white on coverts (see photos). Above, wing dark with dark speculum that looked dari purple with narrow pale trailing edge. No pale leading edge. Bill greenish yellow. Feet bright orange-red.

Similar Species Discussion

Mexican Duck would not be as dark, would have more prominent border to speculum, some pale on tail (typically). Mexi would also show more contrast between chest and neck/head and have internal markings on contour feathers

Mottled Duck might approach Am Black Duck in darkness, but would have warm colored, far paler, head and neck and internal markings on contour feathers. Additionally, should have dark mark near gape, which this bird lacked. 

Mallard x Am Black Duck should show some sign of Mallard - paler plumage, white on tail, curled tail feathers, white borders to speculum, internal markings on contour feathers, etc
