Description of the Bird

I was scanning Mallard-strewn rocks in ne corner of Woods Lake, when I noticed a duck that looked black. I then watched it off and on for about 20 minutes. It would lift its head up for 15-30 seconds, lay its head on its back for a bit (with eye, eyeline, supercilium, crown in view), then raise its head again. At the end of my observation time, the ducks grew alert, and then started to fly nearly directly west, giving me a good view of in flight, during which time I focused on the "speculum" and the tail.

This was a generally (excepting head and neck) blackish plumaged duck with no internal markings on contour feathers of body. Head and neck paler, with moderately sharp line demarcating dark breast and back from pale grayish-brown head and neck. No buff on throat. Crown black and broader than that of most Mexican Ducks I've seen. Eyeline black, narrow, extending well behind eye and meeting crown at (or is it just above?) lores. Bill yellow with black nail. Legs bright orange-red. Tail dark, no contrast with remainder of upper parts in flight. Entire body uniform (no paler vent, browner/darker chest). Unable to ascertain wingstripe color in flight, but no pale leading edge to speculum and very narrow white trailing edge. Size similar, or modestly larger than, nearby Mallards

Similar Species Discussion

Am Black Duck x Mallard: one would expect a paler bird, with some internal body feather markings to be visible, browner chest. Also, more white on speculum, green on head; a hybrid should show some of these.

Mottled Duck. Should show internal feather markings, some black at gape of bill, paler and warmer colored auriculars/chin (the Am Black Duck appeared to have some markings on face), eyeline not meeting crown.

Mexican Duck: Paler, more white around speculum, etc.
