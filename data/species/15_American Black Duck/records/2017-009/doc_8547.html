Description of the Bird

Swimming in a small pod of breeding plumage male Mallards under high overcast sky which gave rather true colors and shading. The bird was the same size, or a tad larger, than its companions. The body of the bird (while swimming) looked blackish overall, excepting silvery edging to the tertials which contrasted distinctly with the otherwise dark plumage. The sides of the bird showed narrow modestly paler edging to the each feather, but no internal feather markings. The folded wing and back seemed solidly colored as did the chest. The tail was solidly dark and did not show any evidence of upcurling to tail feathers. The neck and head were grayish and moderately marked with darker fine streaks. The crown was dusky dark gray and stretched from base of bill to back. The lower edge was slightly ragged, dipping down a bit in front of eye and meeting the dusky eyeline, so that the pale supercilium (which was colored similarly to the cheek) did not actually reach the bill. The dark eye line stretched backwards well behind eye, but did not meet dusky crown/nape. There was nor black near gape of bill. Bill was yellow with tinge of olive. The speculum on the wing was exposed on a couple of occasions, revealing a purple to dark blue (depending on light angle) speculum bordered by black aft and fore, with a very narrow, crisp white trailing edge. Legs bright orange-red.  

Similar Species Discussion

Mallard x Am Black Duck - should show some evidence of Mallard heritage such as internal feather markings on contour feathers, pale on tail, upcurled tail feathers, broader white speculum edging, etc
Mexican Duck - Paler bodied, broader edges to speculum, more contrast between neck/cheek and chest/body. Not as gray on face
Mottled Duck - Internal markings on contour feathers, paler face lacking streaking. Usually dark mark at gape. Usually, the speculum would not appear purple

I think that the crown meeting eyeline, so supercilium does not meet bill, is a fairly good Am Black Duck mark as well
