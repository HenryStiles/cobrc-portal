Description of the Bird

Flying with quick wingbeats, hovering less than Forster’s Terns usually will and diving more frequently.  Dives were shallow and did not appear to ever actually break the surface.

Very small tern (smaller than Red-winged Blackbirds that were present and about the same size as the abundant Cliff  Swallows).  Had a white forehead, yellow bill, and black leading edge to the wings.  Small size, head and bill coloration, and behavior were all consistent with a Least Tern. 
Less than half the size of the Forster’s Terns in the area.  Forehead had clear white spot, bill was obviously yellow not red, and behavior was unlike Forster’s Tern.
Bird did not call much but the couple of calls heard were softer and higher pitch than the grating call of a Forster’s Tern.

Similar Species Discussion

Much smaller than Forster's Tern.  Behavior was different, vocalization was different and did not assocaite with other terns.
