Description of the Bird

The Least Tern, along with several gulls, was on the marina parking lot on the west side of Jackson Reservoir.  A comparison photo with a California Gull shows how small the tern was.  The tern was identified as an adult in breeding plumage by the yellow bill (with black tip), white forehead, and dark primaries.  The tern was silent when I observed it.

Similar Species Discussion

The yellow bill is unique to Least Tern, eliminating other tern species.  The white forehead is also distinctive, seen in only a few tern species.
