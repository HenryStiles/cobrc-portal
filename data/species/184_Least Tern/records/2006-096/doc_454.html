Description of the Bird

Two Least Terns were observed on the island formed at Big Johnson Reservoir when the irrigation water receeds.

An adult bird in alternate plumage and a bird that is aparrantely a 1st year bird could be seen flying around the northwest end of the island.

Both birds were very small terns. The adult was first seen with Black Terns and at a later date the two brids were observed flying together and perched on the island.

The adult had an all-yellow bill, white forehead, black crown and nape. The was a dark eyeline present. The wings were long and gave the bird a top heavy look when flying. The primaries were gray with the outer two being dark tipped. The legs and feet appeared yellow.

The first year bird was the same size (very small) as the adult but lacked a yellow bill. Its bill was dark. THe entire crown was blackish but with a lot of white showing along with a white forehead and anterior end of the crown. A carpal bar was not seen although one could have been present.
Behaviors: Both birds perched on the beach close to the water, less than 10 feet up from the water. At times they would fly low over the water for a minute and then return to the beach to roost. Often only one bird would fly at one time. At one time, the adult appeared to present a food item to the subadult but I am not entirely sure that food was in its bill. I don't believe that the food item was a fish, as I didn't see a fish tail or head protruding from the bill.
Call: none heard
Plumage: one alternate one first summer

Similar Species Discussion

In North America there are no other terns this small, distinctly smaller than Black Terns with which one bird was seen flying on the first day.

Compared to the subadult Black Tern, the LETE was much lighter, without the dark smudgy breast and with light gray, not dark gray back. BLTE had dark bill and dark ear coverts (not present in the LETE)
