Description of the Bird

The identity was immediately obvious because of its small size, yellow bill, and white forehead. I first saw it resting motionless on the shoreline, apparently exhausted or at least sleeping. It was lying on the shore. The bill was bright yellow with a black tip. The crown was black and extended to the nape. The forehead was white and started at the bill up to the top of the forehead and then narrowed to a sharp point above the eye. The underparts were a bright white. The back including the mantle and wings, except for the outer primaries were a light gray. At rest the wings extended slightly beyond the tail. The tail was well forked and white. The legs were short and bright yellow.
Behaviors: The bird seemed to exhausted and sleeping with its eyes closed on the shoreline. After a while it did attempt a couple of very short flights, but didn't fly more than a few feet before landing. It did not feed while I observed it.
Call: Not heard
