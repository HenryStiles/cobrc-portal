Description of the Bird

I was first alerted to the bird when I heard it calling/singing.  I actually thought it might be a YGVI right from the start as the calls seemed off and unlike anything I had heard from a REVI.  The bird was on the "island" and I was along the south edge so could only hear it.  I was busy doing other stuff and before it finely bugged me enough so I played tape of REVI.  I was going to play YGVI but the Sibley app does not have YGVI.  Either way, the bird immediately flew over to where I was and really started to call.  I initially noticed the bright yellow parts and thought it was too much for REVI but not having any experience with YGVI I looked more at some other features.  The one feature that bugged me the most was the eye line and how it seemed to be rather prominent and maybe too prominent for YGVI.  I noticed that it had a rather large bill as well but to be frank I did not know that was a feature of YGVI.  The other features were an olive-green back, white belly, red eye, white supercilium, grayish face and nape.  The bird was roughly the size of a REVI.

Similar Species Discussion

Really, the only other species I considered was Red-eyed Vireo, not only did I consider it, I decided that is what the bird was.  You could maybe confuse it with Warbling or Philadelphia but both of those would never show the bright yellow of this bird.  They also would not show a red eye.  Philly can be very yellow but it is more of a wash and a different shade of yellow.  Blue-headed and Cassin's were ruled out for multiple reasons but the obvious lack of wingbars rules them out.  I suppose Black-whiskered could be considered but the complete lack of a whisker is a good start to rule it out.  A bright adult Red-eyed to me is about the only thing to confuse this particular bird with.  After looking at my photos (I spent more time trying to photograph the bird than actually looking at it, both good and bad), the large bill kept sticking out at me, and the really bright yellow undertail coverts.  The bill and the calls/song are what finally made me realize this was actually a YGVI and not a REVI.

Resources Used

Sibley, and a few others who I sent the photos to once I realized what it was.

Previous Experience

I have no previous experience with this species.  I am relatively experienced with Red-eyed, seeing a few each year in CO along with some in other states occasionally.
