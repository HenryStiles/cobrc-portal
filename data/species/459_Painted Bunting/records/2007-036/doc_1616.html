Habitat

Rural backyard, mixed conifers and deciduous trees, stream within 50 ft. of house, much native grass and scrubby habitat

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  12X 50 Cabela's Binoculars
Distance:  10 - 12 ft.
Light:  Light conditions were very good. The thunderstorm had just passed and blue sky was present. The weather on subsequent days of observation was very good.

Description of the Bird

Blue head with red eye-ring, red throat and underparts, green back, red rump. Knowing this was a special event I immediately grabbed my camera to document. The bird was so unmistakable...I knew immediately what is was!
Behaviors: This male Painted Bunting arrived during a spring storm. It stayed in the area and fed from our feeders
Call: None heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing remotely similar was present with the exception on the second morning. There was an Indigo Bunting in the yard at the same time. Indigo Bunting was a breeding male and was bright blue overall with slightly darker blue on head. Lacked red eye-ring, throat, underparts and rump. Also lacked slightly yellowish (primarily green) back.
