Description of the Bird

Bird was about same size as Lazuli/Indigo Buntings.
Overall color was dull greenish olive with greener back, wing covert & secondary linings. Primaries were dark olive. Underparts were a dull light olive. The dull colors of this bird suggests a 1st year bird as adult females are bright green.

Bill was thick, finchlike & conical, darker at tip and a lighter flesh color at base. Eye was dark.

The most outstanding feature was the bird's lack of wingbars, streaks, and lack of any mottling which are present on most finches, buntings, and sparrows.
Behaviors: Bird flew into a low perch about a foot from ground, gave observer a brief look, and flew off. It has not been refound as of this record submittal.
Call: None given
Plumage: first year female

Similar Species Discussion

Female Indigo Buntings show faint wing bars and breast streaking. They are brownish and show no green coloration.

Female Lazuli Buntings show more prominent whitish wingbars and show no green colors.

Female Varied Buntings look most similar to 1st year female Painted Buntings but are brownish lacking olive and greenish colors.

Resources Used

Sibley GUIDE TO BIRDS (2000)

Previous Experience

My experience with immature female Painted Buntings is limited to about 5 sightings mostly in Texas & Missouri. I've seen many adult Painted Buntings of both sexes. I've also seen many female & brownish type Indigo & Lazuli Buntings, but more limited experience with female & brownish Varied Buntings.
