Habitat

Kentucky Blue Grass and Perennial Rye is around 3 sides of house but not on the south where the bird was. Locust trees, Pinion Pines, Junipers, Cedars, Honeysuckle are present along with a water feature and lots of native rock. Millet seed and milo seed had been spilled underneath a feeder that was only a few feet away from where the bird was spotted.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  only the camera lens with an auto zoom
Distance:  15 to 20 feet
Light:  morning light - not real bright - but good light

Description of the Bird

-greenish on the upper parts (crown and back)
-yellowish underneath
-about 5 1/2 inches
-bunting shape
-no wing bars
-lighter color just under eye
Behaviors: -no behavior noted
-perched on a dead weed stem on the ground
Call: Not noted
Plumage: female plumage

Similar Species Discussion

I had 3 Indigo Buntings and 2 Lazuli Buntings that same morning that I had been watching - that's what gave me the first indication that it was a bunting. The color of this bird was definitely different than the other buntings. Tanager also came to mind, but the bird was not that large nor did it have that large of a bill

Resources Used

Since the bird was here so briefly, I didn't have time to consult assistance until after it left. I used the Sibley's Guide immediately after the sighting and then sent photos to Brandon Percival shortly afterwards

Previous Experience

I occassionally have both Indigo and Lazuli Buntings visit the yard. I had never seen a Painted Bunting. I have also had a few tanagers.
