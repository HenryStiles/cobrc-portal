Description of the Bird

the bird was about the same size and shape as nearby Indigo Buntings seen immediately before and after the observation. The bill was short and finch-like, and a blueish-gray color. The eye and legs were dark.

The bird's head was almost entirely a dark purplish-blue, with the exception of a small red area eye-ring, and a red throat. The entirety of the underparts were bright crimson red. The back was a intense green color. The rump was the same color red as the underparts. The tail and wings were blackish. Pretty much an unmistakable bird!
Behaviors: the bird spent most of it's time singing from high in a cottonwood tree, though it also sang from lower perches in scrub oaks a few times
Call: The bird was constantly singing, a continuous warbling, similar to nearby Indigo Buntings, but without nearly as many breaks, and no repetitions.
Plumage: alternate
