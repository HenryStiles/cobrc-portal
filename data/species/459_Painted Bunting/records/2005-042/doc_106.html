Habitat

Suburban backyard, landscaped to be bird friendly, lots of shrubs. Also multiple seed feeders and water available. Large half acre lot.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica Ultravid 8X42, Nikon D70 at 900mm equivalent
Distance:  About 20 meters
Light:  Challenging light backlighting the feeder, but sufficient for observation and photography.

Description of the Bird

Slightly smaller and more delicate than nearby abundant House Finches. Vivid indigo head, red orbital ring around eye. Bill blunt, dark gray. Chartreuse cape. Dark wings with green edges to wing feathers. Bright red underparts & rump. Dark tail.
Behaviors: Mostly skulking in dense chokecherry shrubbery. During observation made two trips to seed feeder. Fed for a minute or two on each trip and then returned to shrubbery.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Adult male

Similar Species Discussion

I am not aware of any other bird species that has the vivid coloration pattern of a male Painted Bunting.
