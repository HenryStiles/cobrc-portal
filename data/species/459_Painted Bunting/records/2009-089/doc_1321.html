Description of the Bird

Size - "medium to small", sparrow-sized. Not as large as a White-crowned Sparrow in-the-hand, larger than a Pine Siskin or American Goldfinch.

Shape - passerine, sparrow-finch

Bill - "finch-like" seedeater bill, conical. Mottled in color - mostly dark brown-black areas and flesh-orange. Darker, overall upper mandible, but not solidly colored. Perhaps still showing remnant gape coloring.

Head - evenly light green-olive in color. No stripes, spots, or obvious differences in lores, nape, auriculars, etc.

Eye ring - somewhat wide, even, complete, pale yellow-tan colored

Body - Nape. mantle, and rump all even green-olive, with some bluish tinges.
Sides,flanks - more yellow-olive
Belly, vent - more buffy-yellow
Overall - dark green-olive above, buff-yellow underneath

Wings - overall primaries, secondaries, coverts dark, brown-gray with slight yellow-olive edging. Alula evenly dark with light edging, Lesser coverts with more blue-green with olive edging. Wing Bars pretty indistinct.

Tail - also dark base color with olive edging, possible with growth bars

Skull - skulling (by a novice) showed incomplete ossification
Legs-feet - bluish-black, perching foot,
Behaviors: None observed. Bird was captured in bottom trammel of a mist net, and was on the ground. Upon release, after banding, it vacated immediately. Bird was calm in-the-hand and alert.
Call: none
Plumage: hatch year

Similar Species Discussion

Initially, seeing only the belly from the net I suspected it was a bunting - possibly a female Lazuli Bunting, an expected species, but was jolted when I saw the back of the bird.

Separated from female Lazuli or Indigo Buntings by olive back color, more yellow than brown belly, and less wing-bars.

Separated from sparrows by even color, lack of body spots, striping.

Separated from Blue Grosbeak by body size, bill size, lack of wingbars, color.

Separated from Green-tailed Towhee by size, color, lack of different crown or throat color, breast color.

Separated from young or female tanagers by size, green color, bill size and shape

Additional Information

Comments: Photos are ready for submitting - how do I do that?

I won't pretend I have much experience with Painted Buntings, and have limited experience as a bander handling young or female buntings. I hope, though, that I have provided enough information and photo documentation for you to make an identification of this bird.

I don't have the expertise to confidently sex and age this bird, but thought there might be enough blue on the coverts to make it a young male. I'm somewhat confident that skulling showed it to be a hatch-year bird, as well - but not 100% sure. I'll leave those aspects to experts.
