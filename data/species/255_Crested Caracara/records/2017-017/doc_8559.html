Description of the Bird

I along with Blake Hess were watching for Great Horned Owls when I looked over my shoulder and spotted the Crested Caracara in the top of a nearby old cottonwood tree.  At first when I saw the bird I thought an Eagle due to the white and lighting conditions.  However, once I was able to focus my eyes and my camera on the bird I knew that it was very different.  As I as able to get my lens on the bird and focused I knew immediatily that it was a Crested Caracara and that what we were seeing was extreamily rare for Colorado let along Weld County.  I was able to get a few pictures prior to it taking off and flying to the southwest.  The whole encounter lasted litterally one minute.  


The Caracara did not make any calls and just looked around while perched prior to it taking off.

Similar Species Discussion

I very briefly considered the Bald Eagle as the tree is a favorite of theirs to sit in.  Then quickly realized that it was not an Eagle but a type of hawk or falcon.  Once able to see the bird without branches in my way and able to place my lens on the bird I knew it was the Caracara and told Blake Hess who was trying to get into a better position to get pictures as well.

Resources Used

I knew what the bird was as soon as I saw it in my camera lens due to seeing pictures of it in Sibley's and other resources.  I also knew that this bird is a long way from it's normal home range and rare to northern Colorado.

Previous Experience

I have zero experience with this type of bird (Caracara) but know of several falcon species in Colorado. I have seen several times the American Kestrel, Prairie Falcon, Prairie Merlin as well as on two occasions the Peregrine Falcon.
