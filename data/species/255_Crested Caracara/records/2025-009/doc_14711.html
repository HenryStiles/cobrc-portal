Description of the Bird

This bird was first seen by landowners in the area on Red Creek Springs Road West in early March 2025 (for sure seen on 6 March).

Two photos were taken in early March, we saw the photos on March 10th, we didn't know exactly where the bird was seen, except for around Red Creek Springs Road West and Siloam Road intersection.  I went to this location late in the day on March 10th, and didn't see the bird.  I drove many roads on the morning of March 11th, without finding the bird.  I had thought, it probably left the area.

Luckily Pueblo birder Harry Rurup was able to photograph the bird on March 11th around 3pm, and gave me good directions where he saw, which was about a mile east of the intersection of Siloam Road and Red Creek Spring Road West.

I woke up early on March 12th, and found the bird on a power pole at 7:15-7:23am.  I posted to cobirds that is where I saw it.  Al Gardner got there, and saw it further east along the power poles along Red Creek Springs Road east, about an hour and 15 mintues, after I saw it.  Many more birders came to see it that morning.  I returned around noon time, and watched until 5pm, with many birders.

On March 13th, Kara Carragher and I went to go see the bird, and saw it after 8am, for about 15 mintues.  Many more birders saw it throughout the day.

It was seen by more people on March 14th, though I wasn't there.

On March 15th (Saturday), I spent from 7:15am to 3pm or so, looking at the bird off and on with many people, helping people see it.

On March 16th (Sunday), I saw it until around 9:40am.  There were a lot of people, I think a lot of photographers, wanting a good photo, as the caracara flew to a nearby power pole, and some of these photographers, went walking up to the pole right away, just after it landed there, and two more photographers drove from the other direction to the pole, scaring it off to get flight shots, as everyone was trying to look at the bird in their scopes.  I didn't approve this behavoir from these people.  Luckily, when the bird flew, it didn't go too far, just landed on a juniper tree off the road aways and sat there for a while, so many people were able to see it through their scopes.  After that, it flew over the road to the south.  I decided instead of staying, and helping more people see it, as there were too many photographers there, I left the area.  Luckily, more birders were able to see it for most of the day on March 16th, even though the bird was chased off the power pole earlier.

I think I will have to re-think how I report any rare raptor in the future, since photographers that don't care about birds, just want a good photo, and will scared the bird off to get a photo they want. 

See my photos and photos that John Bruder sent me, for this record.
