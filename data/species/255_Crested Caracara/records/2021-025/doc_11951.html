Description of the Bird

Adult bird with white face and neck, orange face skin and cere, yellow legs, mostly black body with fine bars on back, white primary panels, distinctive dark crown, white patch on tail with dark bar on the end of the tail feathers.  It was sitting on a wood post near a cattle birthing pen.  Flushed it and it went to a telephone poll about 100 meters away.  Private land within about 100m of a state wildlife area along the Rio Grande. No calls.  Private landowners said that it was feeding on the afterbirth from the calves.

 

This is private land, exact location should not be shared! 

Similar Species Discussion

There are no similar species, I was able to determine quickly that the bird was not a Golden eagle, Bald eagle or Osprey that are often seen this time of year. I'm a biologst contracted with Colorado Parks and WIldlife to manage several wetland state wildlife areas in the San Luis Valley and often do bird surveys including mid-winter waterfowl surveys with CPW Biologists from a fixed wing plane, spring migration and breeding bird surveys as part of my work. I'm very familiar with the 'normal' species that frequent the area as I've been doing surveys here for over 20 years.  Given the crested head, white panels on the wings in flight along with the yellow legs and orange cere, it is unmistakable.  I checked both Sibleys and Wheelers Raptors book to make certain and will go back tomorrow with my business partner who is also knowledgable with birds for further documentation (if it's still there).  I also have photos and a solocator photo with the coordinates for further evidence.
