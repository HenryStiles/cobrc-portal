Description of the Bird

A large raptor that stood about 18 inches tall (height determined by comparing to the 9 inch tall alfalfa field the bird was standing in). The bird had a black cap with a “shaggy” crest at the back of the cap. The nape, sides of neck, chin, and throat were immaculate white. The upper back and breast were white with crisp, black horizontal barring. On a sitting bird the belly, back and wings were VERY dark brown (almost black). The undertail coverts were white. The base of the undertail was white with a fairly thick black terminal band. I did not notice any barring in the white portion of the undertail (my photos also do not show any barring). In flight a large patch of white could be seen at the base of the dark primaries. The tips of the primaries were DARK brown or black. The upper side of the tail in flight was also white with a dark brown terminal band. The in-flight view was so quick I did not notice if the white wing patch was broken up with any barring. The legs were yellow and long and had only limited dark brown feathering extending only a VERY short distance down the legs (see photo). The dark eye was surrounded by the red colored cere which extended onto the bill. The red coloration on the very thick, hooked bill extended half way out onto the bill and the outer half of the bill was light gray (see photos).
Behaviors: Bird was initially seen on the east side of the road on the ground in the alfalfa and was being mobbed by a Western Kingbird. The bird flew a short distance to the west side of the road and landed about 50 feet out in the alfalfa (photos taken here). The bird seemed to be hunting by walking around the alfalfa. The bird then flew (while we were still in the car) to a low, dead Russian Olive tree. The bird perched here for awhile and the flew to the ground and started walking the alfalfa again. It caught something small and non-furry (perhaps a large grasshopper?) and consumed it in two bites. The bird then “laid” down in the alfalfa and almost completely disappeared, poking its head up occasionally. John Drummond arrived and we were able to get the “resting” bird in the scope for him.
Call: No vocalizations were heard.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Ferruginous Hawk - Dark morph - tail without dark terminal band, small yellow cere and bill, no black cap and crest, wings not DARK brown.
Rough-legged Hawk - Dark morph - small yellow cere and bill, has streaks, not barring on the chest, no black cap and crest.
Falcons - lack the LONG unfeathered legs.
All other raptors eliminated for the same reasons above (black cap and crest; large, thick reddish bill and cere; white windows in dark brown/black wings; long yellow unfeathered legs).
