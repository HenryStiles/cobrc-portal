Description of the Bird

This quite large raptor had a striking head pattern with a black cap, bright white in the rest of the face, and mottling of black and white on the lower neck and upper breast. The inner half of the bill was quite bright red, and the distal half a lightish blue. The eye was dark. Most of the rest of the body was very dark, although the undertail was white. When it flew, it showed bright white patches at the ends of the wings, although the primaries were much darker. The rump and upper tail were also white but with some dark motteling, and the tail had a broad, black terminal band. The legs were long and yellow.
Behaviors: First seen sitting on the ground in an alfalfa field, then flew to a dead branch about three feet off the ground. Finally saw it walking and sitting on ground.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

There really is no other North American Buteo, other hawks or falcons with this combination of head, upper body and bill pattern. The pattern in flight of white at end of wings and upper tail is also unique.
