Description of the Bird

I observed the bird from over a mile away through scope and binoculars. With the scope, I noticed a very large bird with mostly dark plumage, except for a whitish neck and upperbreast. The crown was dark and had a very distinct crest at the rear. Otherwise, the distance was too great to see much more detail. I did note that the bird had rather long, bare legs.
Behaviors: I initially observed it through the scope as it stood on a fallen log. The bird was near-perfectly silouetted against the sky. Remarkably, was I watched it with binoculars, it hopped from the log and took several strides on the ground. The bird flew away as I was calling other birders in the area.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

A Ferruginous Hawk may be of similar size, but its legs are completely feathered and it lacks a crest.
