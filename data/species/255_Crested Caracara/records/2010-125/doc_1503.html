Description of the Bird

This bird appeared to be a juvenile displaying brown feathering rather than black, along with the juvenile color of pink on the face rather than the traditional orange color on the face of an adult. This bird also had the white plumage towards wing tips as viewed from beneath when in flight. It had the dark head with a crested look and the buff color on the rest of the head and onto its neck and upper chest as well as underneath just in front of the tail. This bird had what appeared to be longer than normal legs and they were without feathers.
Behaviors: It was seen eating dead fish with crows and vultures on the ground at the fish hatchery. in flight it was definitely larger than a crow and it's flight was very majestic, exhibiting the white wing patches towards the ends of the wings along with a white area at the rump just in front of the tail.
Call: none presented.
Plumage: juvenile plumage brown with white
