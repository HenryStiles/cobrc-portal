Habitat

Grassy pasture, less than a quarter mile from a pond

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Vortex Fury 10 x 42; Panasonic Lumix DMC-Z540
Distance:  Bird first appeared on fence about 20' from vehicle.
Light:  Clear and sunny, shortly after sunrise

Description of the Bird

The bird perched on lower strands of barb-wire fence, in between fly-catching forays.   Several Say's Phoebes were also fly-catching from the fence.   No noticeable vocalization.

Similar Species Discussion

Scissor-tailed Flycatcher was eliminated because this bird was shades of black and white.

Resources Used

  Jim Fortune observed the bird while surveying ranch land before hunting season.   Jim described it to wife Irene when he got home.  Irene had seen birds of that descriptions in Panama and Ecuador, and suggested searching for pictures of Fork-tailed Flycatcher.   Jim searched on Google and found pictures of adults.  That's when we both consulted the book "Birds of Northern South America" by Robin Restall et al.  Plate 207 shows both adult and juvenile plummage and tail length.  This bird is likely a juvenile due to darker nape and back and shorter tail length.

Previous Experience

Jim hasn't seen these before but Irene has seen them in Central and South America.  But Irene didn't get to see the one in Larimer County :(
