Description of the Bird

This Fork-tailed Flycatcher was a medium sized tyrant flycatcher. It had a black head, wings and two long black tail streamers longer than the body. The back was medium dark gray. There was white on most of the nape, all of the throat and all underparts. The very rear of the nape did have some medium dark gray. It was a very clean line between the white, black and dark gray. The bill was flycatcher thin and black. The underwing showed extensive white, gray moving to black trailing edge and wing tip. 

The flights were short salies out and back catching flying insects. When it took longer flights, it was more undulating. This bird also opened its two tail streamers often in flight for spectacular looks! During the two days of observation, the bird had favorite perches often flying from one side of the field to the other landing on the same spots. Favorite spots included dead limb of cottonwood tree near the top, cattle guard gates, thicker wood fence posts and the cluster of Russian Olives in the next field.

No vocalizations were heard probably due to the distance from the bird and so many people around. 

Similar Species Discussion

The most obvious similiar species would be the Eastern Kingbird. The coloration between the two is almost identical. The difference is the Eastern Kingbird has a white terminal band on the tail, the back is black and the underwing is predominatly dark gray. The tail length is average for kingbirds. 

The Fork-tailed Flycatcher had an extremely black long split tail with no white terminal band. The back was dark gray not black. The underwing was predominatly light.

Previous Experience

I have seen only one other Fork-tailed Flycatcher. It was in Veracruz Mexico severalyears ago. This bird was also an adult male with similar tail length. I did observe this one for over 30 minutes. I have seen hundreds of Eastern Kingbirds and knew right away it was not one.
