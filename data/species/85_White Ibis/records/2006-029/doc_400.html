Description of the Bird

A white-plumaged, salmon-pink faced ibis. Curved ibis bill bright salmon-pink for the basal third or so, black the rest. Not sure how much of the dark part of the bill was possibly mud. Eye color pale blue. Legs and feet matching bright salmon-pink. Feathering all white except for black primary tips and a bit of pink-orange tinge to throat. Bird a bit larger than plegadis ibis nearby.
Behaviors: Bird was feeding in shallow water and in wet grassy area. Sometimes it joined a flock of 40 or so plegadis ibis, sometimes stayed more by itself.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The bill shape and habit of this bird clearly indicates an ibis. It was larger than plegadis ibis, which could only be this white if albino. The coloration of the bare parts rules out albinism, though.
