Description of the Bird

Initially observed the bird flying over a medium sized pond to the SE of Golden Ponds on the South side of the St Vrain River. The bird was all white with black wing tips.Then observed the Ibis shaped bird standing on concrete outcropping on the pond. The bird was accompanied by a Spotted Sandpiper for a short period of time on the concrete outcropping. The bird had long pink legs and feet with a long curved bill. Pink facial skin extending onto light pink bill that turned dark black halfway through, fading back to a light pink, worn looking tip of the bill. Bird had a crystal clear, light blue eye with a black center.

After observing for 15 minutes, bird flew North across the St. Vrain River and out of sight. I refound the bird 20 minutes later, perched in a tree above a group of Double Crested Cormorants on the Southern Island of Golden Ponds No 2. The brid then moved to the north end of the island and was observed foranging along the edge of the island for about 10-15 minutes.

Similar Species Discussion

Bird is a clear Ibis based on size, structure and shape of bill, ruling out egrets or storks. Coloring of bird rules out the more common to area White-faced Ibis.

Resources Used

I reviewed ID information on All About Birds and eBird, as well as reported information from other birders. This bird has been reported by many others as well and is speculated to be the same bird that has been reported in other areas of the state this year.
