Description of the Bird

Large all-white wader with red facial skin, long red decurved bill, red legs, pale bluish eyes, and black distal ends of outer primaries (seen in flight). Adult, based on plumage. First seen from coordinates 40.1713348, -105.1412201, flying from the western shoreline of Pond #2 to the northern shoreline of the southern island in Pond #2 at Golden Ponds Park and Nature Area. First photographed at 6:04 a.m., feeding by probing along the southern island's northern edge. No calls given. Jamie Simo and I were conducting a monthly point count bird survey together at Golden Ponds for the City of Longmont and we found and identified the White Ibis simultaneously.

Similar Species Discussion

Other white/whitish waders occurring in this area (Snowy Egret, Great Egret, Cattle Egret, Black-crowned Night-Heron, juvenile Little Blue Heron, white morph/juvenile Reddish Egret) eliminated by long red decurved bill, red legs, and red facial skin. White-faced and Glossy Ibis eliminated by all-white plumage, and potential leucistic or albino White-faced or Glossy Ibis eliminated by bright red facial skin extending above and behind eye and down to chin (in contrast to White-faced/Glossy's facial skin, which is limited to the lores), red bill, bluish eyes, and black wingtips.
