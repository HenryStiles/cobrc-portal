Description of the Bird

Medium sized bird a bit smaller than the close Double-crested Cormorants. Mostly pure white plumage with bright red legs and bill with the red reaching the eye in the face.  When it flew, it showed a little black area at the end of the primaries.

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing really to match it as the other possible North American Ibis are dark. No red in the plumage, so not a Roseate Spoonbill. Red bill and legs, so not a Wood Stork.  These latter two birds would be even more surprising in Boulder County than a White Ibis.

Resources Used

None required, as I had just recently seen White Ibis at Fountain Creek Regional Park.  In fact, it is possible that this is the same bird, as it is no longer being seen at Fountain Creek, and White Ibis was reported from the Rocky Mountain Arsenal on May 29.

Previous Experience

Have seen this species, and all the other possible species, many times, and seen White Ibis at Fountain Creek on May 19, 2022.
