Habitat

Flooded field, rural, urban interface.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  500 mm lens.
Swarovski 8.5 X 42 and Swarovski 10 X 42
Distance:  100 ft.
Light:  Good light, overcast to partly cloudy

Description of the Bird

White bird around 25" with long thin, decurved, red bill with what appeared to be black tip (bill was very muddy at the tip) Red facial skin, red legs. Rest of the bird white except for tips of outer primaries, which were black. Yellowish iris
Behavior: Bird was actively foraging and associating with White-faced Ibis in flooded field. At one point a large part of the flock flew giving us excellent views of the bird in flight.
Call: None that could be distinguished
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Seen in the presence of White-faced Ibis. Bird was unmistakable. There is no other bird an adult White Ibis could be confused with.

Resources Used

Sibley's, Stokes, Peterson

Previous Experience

We have seen White Ibis in Texas, Costa Rica, and Ecuador.
