Description of the Bird

the bird was about the size of nearby Double-crested Cormorants, though only when taking into account the long legs. The bird was a classic Ibis, with long legs, a very long decurved bill, and thin and long neck. The bill was bright scarlet red, as was the outer half of the face. The legs were also very long, and also bright scarlet red.

The body of the bird was entirely white, except for a small amount of black on the outer wingtips. This was barely visible when the bird was perched, but in flight was a bit more obvious.
Behaviors: The bird was seen perched on the top of a dead tree near many cormorants. Soon after the observation it was observed flying east with a dark ibis, and was not seen again.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than ibis, including white herons and egrets, were ruled out by the distinctive shape, with long, decurved bill, long legs, and heron-like shape. All other species of ibis were ruled out by the white plumage, and bright red bill and legs.
