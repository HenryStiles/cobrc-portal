Description of the Bird

I was walking along the Fountain Creek Regional Trail near south Rice's Pond when I saw an adult white ibis in the water just below the bank where the trail runs. It was primarily preening. Within ~10 minutes a jogger came by and caused the ibis to fly to the north end of the pond- out of sight in the reeds/ cattails. I was the finder of the bird as no one had previously reported the ibis in this very heavily visited hotspot. I sent out a message to the What's App 4 county group and received multiple replies from birders who wanted to come see this bird and wanted more details of where it was located. When I returned to the area about 30 minutes later, there were multiple birders enjoying good looks at the white ibis.

Similar Species Discussion

I ruled out a great egret or a leucistic/ albino great blue heron or white-faced ibis by the beak length, color and shape. The beak was long, slender, decurved and bright orange-red with black near the tip. Bright red-orange legs also helped rule out the previously-mentioned species. I ruled out it being a captive bird by looking for the presence of a leg band. I also know that no zoos in CO house white ibis.

Resources Used

I've seen many white ibis in FL and TX previously, but I also looked at my Sibley app- primarily to check range as I knew that this was not a species that ventures into CO often.

Previous Experience

I've seen white ibis at multiple zoos as well as in the wild in coastal TX and all over FL. I have seen white-faced and glossy ibis in multiple states as well, including CO and FL.
