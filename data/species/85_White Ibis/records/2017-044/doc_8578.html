Description of the Bird

Bird was clearly heron-like in general shape based on long legs, wading behavior and long neck from a general view without binoculars or a scope.  On closer inspection the unmistakeable characteristics of the White Ibis come into view.  First, and all white body - none of the other Ibises in the US have an all white body in any of their plummages.  Second, the pink/red decurved bill immediately rules out other medium sized all white or mostly white herons (Snowy Egret - which was present at nearby ponds, great egret, blackcrowned night-heron, juvenile little blue heron, etc.).  Third, the bright red/pink legs also rule out all other ibises/herons from consideration.  The only ID with these characters within the realm of possibility would be an Adult White Ibis.

Similar Species Discussion

Snowy Egret (and other herons)/ - eliminated by presence of a red/pink decurved bill and red/pink legs.  White-faced and Glossy Ibis - eliminated by all white body in addition to red/pink decurved bill and red/pink legs.
