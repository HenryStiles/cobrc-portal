Habitat

Marshy pond on the north side of I-76, on the south side of the parking lot of Clear Creek Valley Park. The pond has been drained due to construction activity and has many gravel bars, mudflats, and shallow water providing excellent habitat for wading and shore birds as well as waterfowl. Several other ponds in the immediate area also provide roosting sites for birds such as herons, egrets and the ibis.

Description of the Bird

Wading bird with a long downcurved bill which, along with the face, was a pink/red color as were the legs. Eyes were a bright blue color, body was entirely white except for the tips of the primary feathers on the wing which were black. No calls were made while I observed the bird, it spent the entire time probing around the shallow water for food. 

Similar Species Discussion

Orginially the bird was viewd from too great a distance and lighting was too poor to notice any distingusihing features aside from the white color and so i presumed it was simply another one of the many snowy egrets that were at the same location. When I zoomed in with the camera however it was obviously an ibis with the overall shape, particularly the bill. My initial reaction was that this was a leucistic or otherwise oddly pigmented white faced ibis. When the bird flew a short distance (from one side of the pond to the other) it exposed the very distinct black wingtips which seemed to me that it probably was not a bird that was simply lacking pigment. Once I returned to my car, I looked up "white ibises" on my phone and came across the description of Eudocimus albus that matched exactly what I seemed to have seen. I compared the description again when I got home to the photos I took of the bird and it was a definate match.
