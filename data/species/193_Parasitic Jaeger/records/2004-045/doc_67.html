Description of the Bird

Just slightly smaller than a Ring-billed Gull (Larus delawarensis) which it was seen chasing. The bird also pursued on several occasions Forster's Terns (Sterna forsteri), as well as (and was chased by) a California Gull (Larus californicus). On the several chase sequences of Forster's Terns, the jaeger continuously harassed each tern, but in no instance attempted to knock it out of the air (i.e. predate the tern itself), successfully forcing one tern to disgorge it belly contents. Upperparts were a dark brown, including wings, back, and crown. The was also a brown, faint, disconnected breast band. The belly was white. The vent region was mottled brown and white. The neck was white with a pale yellow wash. The brown cap did not extend onto the hind neck nor under the bill. Also, there was a small pale area immediately above (and adjacent to) the thin, dark bill. This pale area was noticeable in several different lighting effects and flight postures. In flight the bird was very falcon-like, with long, pointed wings and a very direct flight style, especially when in pursuit. The axillaries were whitish (establishing the age as a 3rd-cycle bird and not a full adult). The underwing was mostly dark, except for axillaries, and the outer primaries showed a single white patch. The center rectrices were pointed and approximately 1/4 longer than the rest of the tail. The wings extended beyond the tail when the bird was floating on the water.
Behaviors: See description.

Similar Species Discussion

Adult Long-tailed Jaeger (S. longicaudus) has gray upperparts and black cap. Adult Pomarine Jaeger (S. pomarinus) shows a double white patch on the underwing, the cap extends below the bill, there is no white patch adjacent to the upper mandible, bill is bi-colored, and foraging strategy is radically different by predating terns and gulls, rather than kleptoparasitizing them as this individual did.
