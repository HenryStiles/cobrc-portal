Description of the Bird

Capped appearance, tail shape with distinctively projecting central tail feathers, flight style, and aggressive behavior towards gulls clearly point to jaeger sp. About the size of Ring-billed Gulls and nearly as big as California Gulls it chased. Uniform grayish-brown upperparts, mostly whitish underparts. Pale base to primaries, but no obvious pale base to greater under primary coverts, so no "double flash." Some retained light barring in underwing coverts, thus suspicion of sub-adult age. Weak barring in undertail and uppertail coverts. Long pointed wings, body not especially heavy. Central tail feathers straight and pointy, worn a bit. Nape creamy white. Grayish brown cap contrasts distinctly with nape and sides of neck. Dark extends barely below and around eye, but pale throat extends up to bill. Bill long-looking, not particularly heavy and mostly dark. Weak, wide incomplete darker breast band near base of throat.
Behaviors: The bird alternated between resting on the water and harassing gulls, many to the point where they dropped fish and the bird broke off the chase. On at least one occasion the jaeger made contact with a gull, as a few white feathers were scattered in the aftermath. It didn't stay engaged with any, though, and even the one with which it made contact escaped intact. Once or twice, California Gull chased it for a while.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Subadult probably third cycle pale morph

Similar Species Discussion

Long-tailed Jaeger would be smaller in comparison to the Ring-billed Gulls and show longer central tail projection, stubbier bill, darker cap. Also, Long-tailed Jaeger would be less likely to be as aggressive towards birds like California Gulls. Pomarine Jaeger would look bigger in comparison to the Ring-billed Gulls present, have a heavier-looking bill, a more barrel-shaped chest, and would have dark feathering extending from the eyes to the base of the bill.
