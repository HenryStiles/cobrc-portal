Description of the Bird

An obvious near-adult jaeger, the bird was brown above with narrow wings and long tail with the R1s extending at least 50% again past the rest of the rectrices. These rects were quite pointed, though my pictures don't quite show them to full advantage. The bird had a wingspan just shorter than that of Ring-billed Gulls that it occasionally chased. It flew with distinctive quick, but deep, wingbeats and had no problem catching up to various gulls when it so desired.

The bill was dark, thin, and longish, and contrasted with an obvious white forehead patch. The rest of the crown was dark and contrasted with the brown back. The remiges were blackish, but did not contrast all that strongly with the dark brown mantle. The tail, also, was blackish. The underparts were mostly whitish and lacked any barring on the sides and flanks. There was a vague suggestion of a grayish chest band and the vent region was gray, but this color did not come past the legs. The undersides of the wings were mostly all dark, except with a few retained juvenal-type wing coverts and with an obvious white patch at the bases of the primaries.
Behaviors: The bird chased a few Ring-billed Gulls around the reservoir and then soared around at height (>500 feet) over the reservoir.
Call: none
Plumage: Alternate II or Alternate III

Similar Species Discussion

Long-tailed Jaeger can be quite similar, but that species typically has blunt-tipped R1s and lacks this bird's white forehead patch. Pomarine Jaeger is generally larger, bulkier, with a different head pattern and in that plumage, sports distinct barring on sides and flanks and also usually has a distinct chest band.
