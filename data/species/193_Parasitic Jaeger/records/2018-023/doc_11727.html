Description of the Bird

CBRC Mark Peterson posted on cobirds about this jaeger and two other jaegers, earlier in the morning of 20 September 2018.  When I arrived afternoon time, Jerry DeBoer and his wife were looking at two jaegers, this one the bigger dark bird, compaired to the nearby other lighter smaller jaeger.  In-case others didn't get photos are better, here are my best ones.

Jerry's e-bird description.

Continuing bird. Never saw this bird all that close but was able to observe with good size comparison to Long-tailed Jaeger. This bird was much more of a chocolate brown throughout and of larger, heavier build. From the distance observed, central tail feathers difficult to see but the white patches of the wings clearly identified this bird as a jaeger. In flight, the bird lacked the bulk, strength and flight pattern of the larger Pomarine which easily elimanated that species, yet its larger size than the Long-tailed was easily differentiated as well. The tip of the bill was dark but not to the extent of the Long-tailed.
