Description of the Bird

A jaeger species was found by John Drummond at Big Johnson Reservoir. When I arrived the bird was loafing in front of the seasonal island there. It looked warm-colored and big, not cold and slender. Its head was tawny contrasting strongly with the back. In flight the bird showed a broad white flash in its wings more than in a long-tailed. The bill was bicolored, dark at the tip, and appeared slender but this feature was only viewable through a scope at at least 30X. The tail was tapered and appeared long (but not like an adult jaeger's tail). Both the arm and the hand seemed about the same length and the same width.
Behaviors: Although the bird was reported to chase Ring-billed Gulls it mostly loafed when I was there, but flew four different times before using the wind to lift it high into the air and it flew off beating strongly into a strong wind. In flight it only came off the water a few feet and for a few minutes before settling back on the water where it lifted its wings (photo) for a few seconds.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Pomerine Jaeger - larger stronger flier, more barrel-chested although this bird had somewhat of a barrel chest. Shows a double wing flash that I looked for but did not see. The tail is too long for a juvenile POJA.

Long-tailed Jaeger - more slender, always shows a cold look, not the warm colors of the Big J bird. The wings of this bird were way too broad for a LTJA.
