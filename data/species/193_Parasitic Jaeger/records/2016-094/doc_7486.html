Description of the Bird

A juvenile plumage (based on overall dark but not black color and no apparent paleness to neck and throat) jaeger was observed sitting on the lake.  After 15 minutes, the jaeger chased several gulls.  ID could not be made during my short initial visit.  Later the same day, Mark Peterson joined me in studying the jaeger.  We noted warm tones and no apparent double flash in the underwing.  Our initial thought was a juvenile, intermediate morph, Parasitic Jaeger.  Distant photos were obtained which seemed to eliminate Pomarine Jaeger, but maybe not Long-tailed Jaeger.


The following day, I returned hell-bent on definitively ID'ing the beast.  After some time studying the jaeger, I was able to obtained close up photos that imo, identify the bird as a juvenile, intermediate morph, Parasitic Jaeger.  


Much closer looks revealed extensive white in the primaries on the upper wing, not the P9-P10 white shafts of LTJA.  Short, pointed central retrices were noted and eliminate LTJA, and POJA and support PAJA. Pale bases to the primaries but not the greater coverts in the underwing eliminate POJA and support PAJA.  The ratio of nail-to-bill (small nail, long, thin bill) supports PAJA and eliminates LTJA.  Buffy ground-color to the barring of the undertail coverts also support PAJA.  While eliminating both LTJA and POJA, the features nicely support PAJA.  See Photos.
