Description of the Bird

Powerful gull-shaped bird that was overall dark on top and light in the stomach with a yellow nape. 

Notes copied from eBird report:

First saw the bird fly by fairly close (~50-100 yards). Noticed pointed central tail feathers barely sticking out from the tip of the tail. In flight the bird showed a lot of white on the top and bottom of the wings. It also gave a fairly slender appearance (not barrel chested like a Pomarine Jaeger). The cap also did not seem as bold as a Pomarine usually does. After I went back to the car to grab the camera, the bird stayed fairly distant and was seen chasing gulls and occasionally landing on the water. The size seemed smaller than the Ring-billed and California Gulls that were on the lake.

Similar Species Discussion

Long-tailed Jaeger:
More than 2 white primary shafts on the upper wing.  Brown on back and yellow on nape should be lighter colored.  Tail feathers were also shorter than I would expect on a LTJA.

Pomarine Jaeger:
Size smaller than RBGU/CAGU.  Central tail feathers pointed rather than round. Would have expected the cap to be more bold and the bird to appear a lot bulkier and barrel chested if it was a POJA
