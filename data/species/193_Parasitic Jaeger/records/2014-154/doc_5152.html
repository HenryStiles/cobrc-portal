Description of the Bird

Gull like bird, slightly smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls (shorter wingspan); dark (blackish-gray) upperparts, white underparts; head mostly pale with blackish cap; dark tail with central rectrices extending sligthly longer than rest of the tail, and pointed (observed when bird banked while harrassing a gull, spreading its tail). Underwings dark except for a strong flash of white at base of primaries. Upperwings dark except for a weak flash of white due to white feather shafts, or white coloration of feathers along the shafts of the outermost primaries (4-7 primaries). This was seen best when the bird banked with wings extended; this feature was hard to see through binoculars when bird was in flight. The bird spent time resting (swimming) on water, and flying around, occasionally harassing gulls. Once it chased a Ring-billed Gull until the gull coughed up a food item in mid-air, causing the jaeger to bank and catch the item in the air.

Similar Species Discussion

Pomarine Jaeger would be larger, with wingspan longer than Ring-billed Gull, and with rounded central tail feathers. Skuas would be even larger and with brighter white patches in outer wings, lacking longer central tail feathers. Long-tailed Jaeger adult lacks the bright white patch of white in the underwing, have much longer central tail feathers.
