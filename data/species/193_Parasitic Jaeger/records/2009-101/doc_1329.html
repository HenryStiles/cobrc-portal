Description of the Bird

This was a jaeger based on its bulky build, powerful, purposeful flight and dark coloation.

While on the water it was a long-bodied brown bird with a buffy head and neck. In flight I obsurved the following.

Bill thin with no bulbous tip or gonydeal angle (see photo of screen capture) and two toned, but not short. From bill tip to back of head the shape was triangular. The breast and belly were barred with brown as seen while bird was in flight. The central two tail feather project an inch or two past the outer tail feathers. Each of the projected tail feathers were tapered to a point. At times when the two feathers were without a gap they appeared more blunt, but photographs i have seen show them to be individually tapered.

The wings were pointed. The inner "wrist" was white without any dark coverts so there was a single flash of white, not a double one. The dorsal wrist showed individual silvery shaft streaks contrasting strongly with the dark feathers.

The undertail coverts were heavily barred from the legs posterially to the dark terminal band.
Behaviors: This bird made one awesome flight over the marina cove chasing a Ring-billed Gull (see photo). It pursued the gull until the gull regurgitated. The jaeger picked up the food from the water. In flight the jaeger was acrobatic, easily getting very close to the gulls it encountered.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st winter I think

Similar Species Discussion

Pomarine Skua is just smaller than a Herring Gull. It has a more massive bill and shows a double white flash on its underwing from the brown wing coverts separating the white into two regions. POJA is a more powerful, less agile flier, with wider bill and more noticeably two-toned. It's tail tips are never tapered like this bird's inner rectrices. POJA has a much wider-based wings than the Chatfield bird. This was the easiest of the jaegers to eliminate

Long-tailed Jaeger - has a head that is more roundd and less triangular. It is a colder looking brown without the warm buffy head color of the PAJA. Light-headed LTJAs would have a grayish white head and not the buffy orangish one of the Chatfield bird. LTJA neck band would look gray and not buffy. The upper primary shafts would only show 2 or 3 while in flight not the 7 or 8 seen on the Chatfield bird. The tail projects would be rounded on LTJA and not tapered like the Chatfiled bird and I think the ventral belly/breast barring would stop on the flanks and not reach the belly area. The size would be even smaller and the flight less purposefull with glides and not the powerful stroke of the Chatfield bird. Also, PAJA are known to actively pursue gulls and especially terns, LTJA often will walk on shore an pursue insects. Also, the underwing flash of LTJA, while alsos showing a singe white "flash" would be significantly less than the large white flash of the Chatfield bird.
