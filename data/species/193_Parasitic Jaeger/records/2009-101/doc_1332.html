Description of the Bird

Quickly narrowed down to jaeger sp. by somewhat gull-like appearance, extensive warm brown coloration, and aggressive piratic behavior towards gulls. Similar in size to Ring-billed Gull but handily closed the distance when pursuing Ring-billed or California Gulls under burst of speed despite gulls' desire to get away. Observation too distant to see bill details or feather-by-feather details, but white flash at base of primaries, both on underwing (extensive) and upperwing (not as extensive but still distinct) visible in flight. No second white flash visible at base of underwing primary coverts. Head pale orangish-tan, contrasting with medium orangeish-brown upperparts when seen sitting on the water. Tail spade-shaped without extremely longer central tail feathers.
Behaviors: The bird spent much of the time resting on the water. Periods of rest were interspersed with slow, low patrol flights with bursts of falcon-like speed as it closed the distance to targeted gulls. Upon closing with the gull, acrobatic, gyrating close pursuit would follow, ending by either the gull giving up a prey item (fish), or by the gull descending to the water and withstanding feigned diving attacks. I never saw direct contact, though. At one point a California Gull half-heartedly chased the jaeger for a while.
Call: not heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Orange tones throughout not seen in Long-tailed Jaeger. Aggressive pursuit of gulls not typical of Long-tailed Jaeger. Pomarine Jaeger larger, more barrel-chested, show second light flash on underwings at base of primary coverts. Neither other jaeger species typically shows prominent upperwing white flash at base of primaries. Far too small for Great, Brown, or South Polar Skua.
