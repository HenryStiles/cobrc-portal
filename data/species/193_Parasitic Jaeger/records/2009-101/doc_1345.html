Description of the Bird

Jaeger by coloration and shape on the water and while flying, i.e. while chasing gulls.

In flight almost raptor like with, at times, powerful wing beats, covering ground very rapidly in comparison to nearby gulls. At rest, rufous buffy head and hind neck contrasted with darker brown body. When closest, bill longish and thin with no gonydeal angle, pale with darker tip to the tip of the bill. In flight the tail was short but with two pointed central tail rects, when together giving the impression of being blunt, however they were tapered to a point. Some undertail and breast barring slightly visible at times. Underwing, single white flash present. No darker bar of underwing coverts dividing the underwing white into two parts as in POJA. Bird looked identical to the Chatfiled bird I had seen closer 7 days earlier.
Behaviors: spent much tine loafing far out in the water. Did make two purposeful chases of gulls with acrobatic swoops and dives. At one time flew straight from loafing position towards the dam in a fast, direct flight putting up two gulls but gulls apparently did not have any food in their crops as the jaeger gave up almost immediately.
Call: none heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Pomerine Jaeger was eliminated based on the under wing pattern, especially the single white wing flash vs. a double flash shown by POJAs. This bird was pot-bellied but it was agile and sleek in flight. It chased birds in a manner in which I have seen them chase terns in southern Argentina with a characteristic flight that includes forcing gulls to the water from above. POJA is distinctly larger. This bird was more RBGU size not HEGU size like a POJA.

From LTJA which is what a number of people called it at first. But this bird was warm, especially the orangy buff coloration of the face and hindcollar as contrating with the darker brown body. LTJA would show cold gray tones not the warmer buff coloration of this bird. At first the bird was too far for positive I.D. but it finally landed closer where its long, thin bill and warm coloration was identical to the Chatfield bird. LTJA would have a decidedly shorter bill. The only difference between the two is the Pueblo bird had an injured leg that sometimes dangled when it was in flight.

Resources Used
