Description of the Bird

Light adult breeding plumaged bird observed chasing Ring-billed Gulls (relentlessly) around Lagerman Res. Dark gray-brown wings and body with white-fringed primaries and upper tail covertts, and white collar. Tail had the distinctive central spike. Bill structure was longish and slender with nail accounting for less than half of the length, but not as pronounced as with Pomarine. Compared with RBGUs, slightly smaller with similar or slightly shorter wingspan.


Bird was observed again on Monday, Oct. 8 at approx. 1:30 pm and after 20-30 minutes the bird appeared to depart to the west.

Similar Species Discussion

Long-tailed Jaeger was eliminated based on white in primaries, shape of hood, length of bill and overall chunkyness. In fact, the bird appeard so think in the neck and body, I thought it might be a Pomarine, however, that species was eliminated due to size (a bit smaller than RBGU) and the central tail feather, as well as the extent of white in the primaries. Studying photos, the bill structure is consistent with Parasitic (long and narrow) not Pomarine (thick and strongly hooked).

Previous Experience

I've seen all three Jaegers in Nome, AK. I've seen several juv. LTJA in Colorado as well as a Pomarine in 2016 at Chatfield. I've seen Parasitic (all in juvenile plumage) in NJ.
