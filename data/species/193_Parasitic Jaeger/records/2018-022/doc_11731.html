Description of the Bird

Per Olsen and Larsson's Skuas and Jaegers, the mottling on the underwing coverts indicate a 3rd year bird. The plumage seems muddier than that of a "summer" bird, probably indicating transitional plumage, but not certain of that 

This was a jaeger with a wingspan lesser than that of the many RB Gulls it chased. Its central rectrices were pointed, but perhaps a bit shorter than a typical adult Parasitic. Other key point include
1) bill not as sharply bicolored as typical of Pom, and narrower than that of Pom
2) 4 or 5 white primary shafts
3) Black of cap ending at about gape, with some dusky smudging a bit lower. Narrow white bar on feathering at base of bill, forming narrow pale arc over bill base

Similar Species Discussion

LT Jaeger: smaller, 1-2 primaries with white shafts, more delicate bill, grayer
Pom Jaeger: more dark on face, reaching chin. "Spoon" shaped central tail feathers (though these can break, the central rectrices seemed not particularly worn, same length..). Usually shows "double flash" on underwing. Bill typically more markedly bicolored and larger. Wingspan should easily exceed that of RB Gulls
