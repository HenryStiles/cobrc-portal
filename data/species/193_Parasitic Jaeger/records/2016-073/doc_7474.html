Description of the Bird

Harassing the Ring-billed gulls. Large, larger then the ring-billed gulls, brown over all, streaked underparts, white patch above tail visible in flight (not in photos to well however). Dark bluish beak and legs. Dark coverts with one white wing flash, unlike Pomarine. Under belly not as dark as Pomarine, darker then Long-tailed. Beak is short and thin with no real obvious hook, unlike Long-tailed's large heavy bill with a strong hook look, not as thick beak as Long-tailed either. No long central tail feather ever seen like long-tailed juvenile. Warm coloring to face, more cinnamon colored then Pomarine, not as much white underneath like Long-tailed. Flight in pursuit of ring-billeds was fast and rapid, bringing to mind a falcon for me, but much too big for a falcon and not the right body shape. Ring-billed gulls being chased were giving off alarm calls, more frantic then I have heard before and more constant. This is what alerted me to something different. The gulls were highly agitated this day, rather than loafing around like they do on the shore they would suddenly take to the air at the appearance of this jaeger and circle around, land and repeat as needed. Two terns were flying high overhead, constantly giving an alarm call.

Similar Species Discussion

Originally a thought this to be a herring gull, but my photos showed it was not a herring gull, I don't take credit for eliminated that. Tony Leukering emailed me and told me I had a jaeger and not a herring gull. The behavior of the gulls and the way I watched it chase them had me thinking of jaegers but I did not think this would be a jaeger. As for the other jaeger species this is my process of elimination using the photos I have, and forgive me for repeating some of my past information, Dark coverts with one white wing flash, unlike Pomarine. Under belly not as dark as Pomarine, darker then Long-tailed. Beak is short and thin, unlike Long-tailed's large heavy bill with a strong hook look, not as thick beak as Long-tailed either. No long central tail feather ever seen like long-tailed juvenile. Warm coloring to face, more cinnamon colored then Pomarine, not as much white underneath like Long-tailed.

Resources Used

National Geographic Complete birds of North American, second edition, Edited by Jonathan Alderfer, and Sibley guide to birds, second edition, written and illustrated by David Allan Sibley, other eibrders including Tony who pointed out I had a jaeger and Joe Roller who helped me with some eliminations, but also pointed out jaegers are hard and sometimes impossible to id. also but these two books is what convinced me it was a parasitic.

Previous Experience

It was a good experience to observe the kleptoparasitism. It was clearly this kind of behavior and although I was never able to tell it was successful in stealing food from the gulls it sure had them agitated. Last seen flying over the marina. Never seen again by me.
