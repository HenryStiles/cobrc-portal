Description of the Bird


Juvenile, perhaps knocked down by storms last night. Seen repeatedly harassing RBGU, chasing them in aerodynamic maneuvers with rapid turns, beyond abilities of Pomarine or Long-tailed Jaeger. Size and shape similar to Ring-billed Gull in direct comparison, though appeared larger when against bright background, smaller when against dark water. Shape a bit more like LAGU with slightly longer and narrower appearing wings. Cinnamon cast to head and nape. White base to primaries without second underwing flash (i.e. greater coverts without pale bases unlike Pomarine Jaeger). On upperwing 6 or so bold white primary shafts visible. Tail tip had short 1cm projections, the details of which were impossible to make out.

From Pomarine Jaeger by smaller size, overall structure more acrobatic flight style, cinnamon cast to head and upperparts, lack of double white flash on underwing.

From Long-tailed Jaeger by larger size, repeated chasing of Ring-billed Gulls with fast pursuit and rapid changes in direction, more than 2 - 3 white primary shafts, each of which was also bolder and more conspicuous. Underwing more extensively contrasting white below, without LT's typical dark wedge that comes into white on outer primary. Cinnamon cast to otherwise dark plumage unlike LT.

Previous Experience

I have seen hundreds to thousands of all jaegers and see all three species annually (or nearly so) while leading pelagic trips or birding.

Parasitic Jaeger had long been a nemesis birds for me in Colorado (having even gone and seen reported PAJA, only to reidentify them as either Pomarine and Long-tailed). Needless to say, despite the good timing it was still a big surprise to finally see a Parasitic Jaeger.
