Description of the Bird

At a distance simply appeared chocolate brown. Spent much of time sitting in middle of lake. Would often explode off lake and accelerate at impressive rate and fly like a bullet into flock of RB Gulls, causing much panic among the gulls. Typically, one would be "chosen" and pursued for 5-30 seconds (approx). Wingspread appeared to be a bit shy of that of RB Gull. 

However, when closer, from below: Head and chest chocolate brown. Belly medium brown spangled with buff spots. Undertail coverts barred dark brown and gray (not as black and white as typical of LT and Pom Jaegers). Undertail dark. Two pointed central tail feathers that protruded beyond remainder of tail by well under 1/2 tail length. Underwings (excepting primaries) appeared dark brown heavily spotted in buff. Underprimaries mostly darkish gray or gray-brown with prominent white base. Could never get any feel for number of white primary shafts from below. Legs gray

Above: The bird appeared fairly uniform chocolate brown, except primaries which had white base. 4-5 white primary shafts could be seen and counted when bird spread primaries, such as when making turns. Uppertail coverts had a couple white bars, but nothing as prominent as typical of Pomarine or Long-tailed Jaegers in similar plumage. Bill gray with relatively small black tip

Similar Species Discussion

Pomarine and Long-tailed Jaegers during first all should both have blunt-tipped central rectrices.

Size was too large for LT Jaeger and a bit small for Pomarine. Buff colored spotting differs from gray/white edging typical of LT Jaeger immatures. No or little contrast between upper wing coverts and secondaries, unlike most LT Jaegers. More white primary shafts than is normal for LT Jaeger.

Pomarine should have appeared larger, larger-chested, with more contrastingly marked upper and under tail coverts. Often show "double flash" of white on underwing, which this bird lacked. Bill looked too small for that of Pom.
