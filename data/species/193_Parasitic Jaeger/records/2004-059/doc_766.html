Description of the Bird

An obvious juvenile jaeger (brown, long and pointed wings, distinct white flash at base of underside primaries, pale fringing to upperparts) seen on LUNA (large, unnamed) Reservoir at western edge of Weld Co. The bird was smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls, being lankier and shorter in body length, and with shorter wing span (but relatively longer compared to the body length); no other relevant species was nearby that might have provided a better yardstick.

The bird was pale-medium buffy-brown, darker on the upperparts and wings and tail. The bill was thin and longish and bicolored, with a small dark tip occupying about 1/4 of the otherwise pale bill. The eyes were dark and I could not discern leg color. The nape was paler than the rest of the body plumage with fine darker streaking.

All upperparts feathers were fringed with buff and the blackish primaries were also so fringed, much more so than for either of the other two jaeger species -- I guessed that the fringing extended almost an inch up both edges of the outermost primary. In flight, there was an obvious white flash created by white bases to the primaries. Though this flash was larger and more noticeable on the underside of the wing, it was also quite obvious on the upperside, much more so than is typical for either of the other two jaeger species. Also noted while the bird was in flight were the R1s extending a bit past the rest of the rectrices, creating a small point in the center of the tail. At one point, I was fairly certain that I could see that the R1s were pointed, though I wouldn't swear on it (the bird wasn't all that close).

The upper and under tail coverts were bicolored (brown and buff), but without the distinct banding pattern typical of Long-tailed and Pomarine jaegers. The bases of the rectrices were whitish. The axillars were banded dark brown and buff and, like the tail coverts, without the strong even barring of blackish and white of the other two jaeger species.
Behaviors: The bird would sit swimming by itself for periods of time and then take off after flying Ring-billed Gulls, tail chasing them with a vengeance.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Both Long-tailed and Pomarine jaegers ruled out by: less secondary contrast, poorly patterned axillars and tail coverts (and with light bits buff, not white), apparently pointed R1s, distinct white flash on upper wing, bill shape and pattern.

Most Long-taileds would also have appeared even smaller and lankier than the nearby Ring-billed Gulls and most Pomarines would also have more closely approached that species in size. Pomarine Jaeger is also only very rarely (perhaps never) as pale in juvenal plumage as was the bird in question.
