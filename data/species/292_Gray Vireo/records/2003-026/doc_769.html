Description of the Bird

A small, fairly active vireo (bluish legs, thick, hooked bill), the bird was basically medium gray above and whitish below. It had a thick, dark bill and dark eyes, the latter of which were circled by thin white eye rings. The eye rings were barely connected to whitish loral stripes, which were thicker than were the eye rings. The throat was whitish and contrasted somewhat sharply with the gray auriculars. In the field, I could only discern one white wing bar (formed by the tips of the greater coverts), though my pictures show a hint of a second bar. The tail was fairly long for a vireo and often held cocked like that of a gnatcatcher.

From the brown appearance of the alula and the primary coverts, I would guess that the bird was in it's first plumage cycle.
Behaviors: Foraging fairly actively (almost gnatcatcher-like, but not quite that frenetically) in Russian Olives.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Plumbeous Vireo is often mistaken for Gray Vireo, but that species is more lethargic when foraging, does not show such gnatcatcher tail movements, has a much more pronounced set of spectacles, and much more obvious wing bars.
