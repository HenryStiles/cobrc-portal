Description of the Bird

Small, warbler-sized bird with a short tail. Two obvious bold white wingbars. Forehead, crown and the rest of upperparts cerulean blue. Small but somewhat short, pointed black bill. Dark eye. Some blackish streaking on the back and obvious blue black streaking on the flanks. Tips of primaries appeared blackish. Chin, throat and remaining underparts all white. I was not able to see the front of the birds chest. The upper tail appeared darker blue than the blue on the crown and back.
Behaviors: The bird landed on my deck where I could see it from my office window. It stood on a brick pillar facing right, but only for a few seconds, after which it flew to the top of a very tall Cottonwood tree in my backyard. From the window, I could then see a small bird moving in the top of the Cottonwood. I went outside with binoculars and was able to see two birds in the very top of the tree. One was a Yellow Warbler but the other bird stayed in cover and then flew again. After this, I was not able to re-find the bird despite extensive searching.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Other small blue birds that would appear in this region such as any of the bluebirds do not have this striking combination of blue upperparts, strong white wingbars, white chin and white underparts. Both Northern and Tropical Parula have a yellow throat and breast and a yellowish-green back. Black-throated Blue Warbler has a black throat and does not have white wingbars. Indigo Bunting is blue underneath. Lazuli Bunting has an orangey breast. Other blue birds such as Stellers Jay and Blue Jay are much too large to be confused with this small warbler.
