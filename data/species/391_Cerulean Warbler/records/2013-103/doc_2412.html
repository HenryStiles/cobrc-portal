Description of the Bird

I was birding with Todd Dilley, Tim Mitzen, and Vic Zerbi, early in the morning, below Two Buttes Reservoir dam. High up in the trees there were a flock of warblers, mostly Yellow-rumped, when Todd, said there is a Cerulean Warbler in the flock! I quickly looked at the flock, and was able to see the bird. We yelled for Vic Zerbi, who was down the road. I looked at the bird with my binoculars and saw a warbler that was very white underneath, the upper parts were blue, the head, back, and wings all blue, with two white wing bars. The throat was white, with a blue line below the throat. Below the blue line, the bird was white, white chest, and belly and under tail coverts. The bird had blue stripes on the sides. The bill was thin and short and pointed, typically for warblers. We watch the bird for 10 minutes, and then had to head to Lamar to meet up with people.
Behaviors: Staying high up in the trees with other warblers.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing really looks like a male Cerulean Warbler, the white throat is wrong from Black-throated Blue Warbler.

Previous Experience

I'd seen one female Cerulean in the San Luis Valley before this one. I've seen males since, and there is no doubt about this bird being a Cerulean.
