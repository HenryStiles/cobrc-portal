Description of the Bird

I will let expects determine sex and age from Brenda's very good photos as we are not that experienced with sexing and ageing this species. The dove is small with a short tail and bold black spots on a rufous background primarily on the wings. The head is gray and not as rufous as the body, wings and underparts. The legs are red as is the eye. The back is rufous as is the upper tail. Lack of scaling about the head and chest help rule out Common Ground Dove. In flight I did not notice any white corners in the tail but did see black tail primaries in flight and rufous wings.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Ground-Dove and Mouring Dove. Common Ground-Dove is usually scaly about the head and chest. It is not as rufous as our dove. Mourning Doves has a long pointed tail, lack the rufous color, and red wings in flight.
