Description of the Bird

Very small Dove: much smaller than the several Eurasian Collared-Doves that were also present.  Quite rufous body, with a gray head. Vertical black markings on the wings. Very short tail seen when bird flew in and departed. Not seen by outside observers when descended to the feeder inside the wall. Bird was too rufous to be a Common Ground-Dove.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Ground-Dove was eliminated by the bird being too rufous. Not seen well enough to determine if there were no markings on the back of the head and upper breast.  Much too small with too short a tail to be a Mourning Dove or an Eurasian Collared-Dove.
