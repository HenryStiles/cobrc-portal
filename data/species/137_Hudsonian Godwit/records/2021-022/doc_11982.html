Description of the Bird

Two individuals, male and female were loafing/snoozing at a playa amid what looked like corn stubble along CR BB just south of CR 58. Water levels were quite high in the area, and a number of other shorebirds/waterfowl/ibis were in the area. In the immediate surroundings, over 40 Long-billed Dowitchers were feeding and snoozing as well, providing good size comparisons. Despite the suboptimal light, the color patterns on the godwits were easily discernible in the scope. Male & female godwits with slightly decurved bills, white tail bases with black terminal bands. Male with rich brick-red breast, dark back, and pale gray striped head. Female gray overall with white undertail coverts.

Similar Species Discussion

Marbled Godwit – those should always appear fairly uniform and buffy with a more speckled plumage (very fine dark streaks or spots surrounded by buffy), and certainly not have light-dark banded tails as these birds did. Even female HUGO is distinctive despite its plainer look even in suboptimal light, with a flat gray appearance unlike MAGO.

Dowitchers – no dowitcher is this large and relatively lanky, nor possesses a slightly decurved bill. Sometimes breeding plumage dowitchers can show more richly rufous underparts, but never brick red in a way that contrasts with the neck, throat, and face.

Previous Experience

Just a few days prior, I found 15 Hudsonian Godwits in Boulder County (CBRC report submitted). Those birds were seen by at least 75 others over the course of a day. Before that, I'd only encountered HUGO on two occasions, many years earlier (over a decade) in Larimer and Weld counties.

I've seen numerous Marbled Godwits and Long-billed Dowitchers over the years in Colorado.
