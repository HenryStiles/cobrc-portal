Description of the Bird

The Hudsonian Godwit was among a group of large shorebirds, including Whimbrel and Willet, on the north shore of Lake Meredith.  It was sleeping some of the time and walking around at other times.  Later, it and the other shorebirds became agitated by a falcon.  As a result, some of the shorebirds, including the Godwit, flew off toward the northeast.  The calls I could hear were from Willets.  If the Godwit called, I didn't hear it.

 The Hudsonian Godwit had a rufous belly and a long, slightly upturned, bicolored bill.  The head and neck were mostly gray.  When the Godwit flew, a white rump and black tail could be seen. The wings had a narrow white stripe.  On the underside of the wing, the leading portion was black, and the trailing portion was a lighter grayish color.

Similar Species Discussion

Marbled Godwit:  buff instead of rufous; no white rump or wing stripe

 Bar-tailed Godwit:  no white rump or wing stripe

Black-tailed Godwit:  most similar to Hudsonian Godwit with white rump and black tail; would instead have overall orange color and white underwing
