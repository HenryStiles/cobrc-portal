Description of the Bird

This was a medium-sized, long-legged, long-billed shorebird similar in structure to the more common Marbled Godwit. In direct comparison, this bird was about a third smaller in body size and leg length than an American Avocet it was foraging next to. The godwit's long bill (2-3 times the length of its head) was bicolored, with the distal 30% black and the remainder pinkish-orange, with a slight curvature most noticeable near its tip. The bird's head was slightly silvery on the lores, supercilium, and check patches extending onto the neck, with a contrastingly darker eyeline and crown. The most remarkable feature on this bird was its rich, chestnut-red underparts. The undertail coverts were strongly barred with even-width bars of black and white. The black barring extending up the bird's flanks and lower belly. The upperparts were brown-blackish with some light mottling. I did not note its tail pattern, nor did I see it in flight.
Behaviors: It most foraged in shallow water near shore by probing its bill down into the water (and presumable underlying mud), but much slower than the sewing machine fashion of a dowitcher.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Other godwit species ruled out by combination of chestnut-red underparts and silvery-black head and silvery neck. Long-billed Dowitcher ruled out by upcurved, bicolored bill and silvery-black head.
