Description of the Bird

Medium-large shorebird, appearing distinctly smaller than a Marbled Godwit about 50 yards away and about as long as a sleeping Northern Shoveler (direct comparison). Long-bodied and long-winged but robust, large-headed, short-necked, long bill barely upturned; longish legs. Wings extend well past tail, perhaps by 3/4 inch. Undertail coverts and vent white, strongly barred with black. Rest of underparts brick red from upper chest to legs, including sides. A little patchy black-and-white barring around the edges of this red area. Head pale whitish gray with darker crown and eyeline. Back of neck, scapulars, back darker, each feather streaked dark brown on light brown. Wing coverts and flight feathers much less patterned, medium brown with a grayish wash--look washed out or worn. Rump bright clean white, uppertail black. Underwings bicolored, with blackish underwing coverts and paler flight feathers. Bill bicolored, mostly orange-red in the basal half, dark horn in the distal half. Legs appear dark.
Behaviors: Feeding actively in shallow water, often with a sewing-machine motion, sometimes completely dunking its head; also appearing to sweep its bill through the water at times.
Call: Bird was silent.
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

It was not a dowitcher because it was too large, had too bicolored and upturned a bill, had too much head/underparts contrast. A Marbled Godwit was in the area for direct comparison; it was larger and much more uniform in coloration, buffy without any reddish underparts. In addition, rump color/tail color and underwing color rule out all other species.
