Description of the Bird

A large shorebird (larger than a Greater Yellowlegs and smaller than an American Avocet) with long (6 inches, estimated) bicolored pink and gray bill (basal half pink), gray head and neck with whitish diffuse eyeline, dark eye, and fine blackish spotting on sides of neck (as with Long-billed Dowitcher), deep chestnut chest, belly and flanks, with some black barring on the lower flanks, white vent and under tail coverts with heavy black barring; upperparts mottled with blackish feather centers checkered with buff flecks on back, heavier buff borders on scapulars, but grayish wing coverts and tertials. Primaries extended slightly beyond tail. Legs were dark gray. In flight, tail appeared strikingly bicolored with a white base and a black terminus (possibly white uppertail coverts over a black tail). A long white wing-stripe was evident (I believe this was at the base of the flight feathers, including secondaries and primaries). The underwing coverts were black. The flying bird was easily picked out of a crowd of flying shorebirds (mostly Avocets) as the dark bird. Wing span appeared about 20 per cent shorter than accompanying Avocet wing span, and the flight profile appeared more falcon-like with quick powerful beats of pointed wings.
Behaviors: Foraging in mudflats
Call: None
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Bill morphology (long, thin, slightly upturned, pink base) ruled out all shorebirds besides godwits. Chestnut underparts ruled out Marbled Godwit. Black underwing linings ruled Marbled, Bar-tailed and Black-tailed Godwit. Black-and-white tail pattern ruled out Marbled and Bar-tailed Godwit.
