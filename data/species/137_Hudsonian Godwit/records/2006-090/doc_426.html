Description of the Bird

Overall body bulk approx. equivalent to that of adjacent Green-winged Teal. Roughly twice the body length and depth, back to belly, of adjacent Stilt Sandpiper. Seems proportionately larger-bodied and smaller-headed than dowitchers. Bill long, approx. 2.5x length of head, very slightly upturned, basal half reddish or orange, distal half dark. Legs appear dark. Belly brick-red; wings and back seem grayer, mottled with some buff. Neck and head paler grayish.
Behaviors: Bird was feeding actively in a sewing machine fashion, a la dowitchers, just off the shore in 4-5 inches of water.
Call: Bird was silent.
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was twice the size of dowitchers; also, its bicolored bill, structure, pale head and lack of white below eliminate them. Marbled Godwit is eliminated by size and coloration.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have seen breeding-plumaged Hudsonian Godwits a half-dozen times or so in spring in South Dakota and once in Colorado.
