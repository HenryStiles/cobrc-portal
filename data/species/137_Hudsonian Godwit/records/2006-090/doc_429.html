Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

It was along an open shore of a small lake.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski Spotting Scope and I also saw it through a C102F Telescope.
Distance:  600, estimated
Light:  I saw it from two different locations. From the first location the sun was to the right of us and that made for great looks for the ID of the bird. From the second location the bird was between me and the sun, but slightly closer than the first location, but it was still identifiable just more difficult to see well.

Description of the Bird

It had a dark rufous to purplish belly and breast. Gray head and neck. Top of the head was dark. It had a white supercillium. Bill slightly upturned, orange with a black tip, and slightly shorter than a Marbled Godwit. The back was black with white spotting. Priamries and secondaries were gray. The undertail covert was mostly white with some black barring. Black legs. The size was slightly smaller than a Green-winged Teal. Mostly black tail.
Behaviors: It was feeding in water that was about 6 inches deep.
Call: Didn't hear it.
Plumage: Breeding Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Bar-tailed Godwit - It has clean rufous belly, breast, nack, and head. Black back with a lot of cleam colored spotting. Barred tail.

Black-tailed Godwit - It has an orange neck and breast. Belly a little orange also, but mostly white with black barring. Orange back with dark spotting. Gray primaries and secondaries. Black tail. White supercillium.

Marbled Godwit - Light barred belly and breast. Cream colored and black checkered back. Barred tail. Longer bill. The bird is pretty much all black and cream colored.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide to Birds; before, during, and after the sighting.

Previous Experience

I have seen quite a few Marbled Godwits, but I had never seen any other godwits until this bird.
