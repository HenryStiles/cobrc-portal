Description of the Bird

I went looking for this bird based on Greg Prelich's May 21 report, and I found it feeding in the seasonal marsh just south of Weld County Road 48.  It matched the description of a Hudsonian Godwit in breeding plumage:  medium-sized shorebird; long, upturned bi-colored bill; rufous belly; gray upper neck; dark scapulars; and gray coverts.  I also saw the distinctive black-and-white underwing pattern when the bird flapped its wings (not photographed).

Similar Species Discussion

Marbled Godwits are much more common in Colorado.  Marbled Godwits, and also Bar-tailed and Black-tailed Godwits, can be eliminated by the observed bird's dark rufous belly and gray neck.
