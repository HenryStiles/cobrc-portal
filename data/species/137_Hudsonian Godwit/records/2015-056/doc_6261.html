Description of the Bird

Bird observed through binoculars at this distance looked smaller and darker than nearby Marbled Godwits. After looking through a scope the bird had heavily barred undertail coverts, orange-brown chest and flanks. Mottled back, Bicolored upturned bill. Prominent white supercilium on grayish head. Very dark underwing coverts and axillaries contrasting with lighter secondaries and primary feathers. Bird was feeding near a water edge, hunched over with head almost all the way at water level. Feeding with Marbled Godwits, American Avocet and other nearby shorebirds.  

Similar Species Discussion

This bird stood out like a sore thumb against the group of Marbled Godwits, American Avocet, Long-billed Dowitchers, Stilt Sandpipers, etc.  There was really no question about what species this was. It was smaller and darker colored than nearby Marbled Godwits. Bill was bicolored and upturned unlike darker and more straight of the Long-billed Dowitcher. Stilt Sandpipers were much grayer and more slender. 

Resources Used

Joey Kellner ID'd the bird on sight.  I consulted The Shorebird Guide by O'Brien, Crossley and Karlson for Age/Sex confirmation.  

Previous Experience

My only experience with this bird is from pictures and literature studied.  The Shorebird Guide, Sibley Second Edition, second printing, National Geographic Sixth Edition and Shorebirds of North America, Europe and Asia by Richard Chandler.  I have seen Marbled Godwits, American Avocet, Long-billed Dowitchers, Stilt Sandpipers and all of the other shorebirds present with this bird previously. Roughly two to four years of experience with these birds.  The Hudsonian Godwit was exactly as I've seen in photographs describing Alternate(breeding) plumage adult males.
