Description of the Bird

Medium sized shorebirds with upturned bills, but definitely smaller than the Marbled Godwits seen here earlier in April. Much darker plumage than Marbled Godwit, with deep red breasts and bellies and dark brown backs. The inner half of the bills were reddish.  I saw them fly twice when disturbed by a Bald Eagle, and the second time they came within 100 m when Thomas H took the attached photo. In flight they clearly showed the bright white patch in the upper tail, the thinnish white wingbar and the very dark underwing.

Similar Species Discussion

Marbled Godwit by smaller size, much darker plumage and white in wing and tail. Bar-tailed Godwit by white in the wing and tail. Black-tailed Godwit by darker back and very dark, rather than white, under wing pattern.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Have seen Hudsonian Godwit at least 4 times in CO, twice around Anchorage AK and once on Bolivar flats east of Galveston, TX. Seen Marbled Godwits many times in Boulder and CO.  Seen Bar-tailed and Black-tailed a few times in England, but a long time ago.
