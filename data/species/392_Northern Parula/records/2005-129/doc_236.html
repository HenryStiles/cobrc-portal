Habitat

Large (12 ft. tall) rose bush, sans foliage.

Description of the Bird

BIrd was roughly the same size and shape as the ruby crowned kinglet that was feeding in the same bush. Throat and breast were yellow. Broken white eye ring (above and below the eye). Almost entire back, including cap, nape, upper tail coverts and tail, were slate gray. Mantly was olive. WIngs were gray with two distinct white wing bands. Breast and undertail coverts were white. Orange/ yellow feet contrasted with dark legs
Behaviors: Bird was feeding in a large rosebush. Flitted from branch to branch and made one short fly-catching flight.
Call: just a single soft buzz
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Other birds of this size and shape that might appear in Paonia in November include kinglets, gnatcatchers, bushtits -- none of which have yellow on their throats or breasts. My next guess was a MacGillivray's warbler, which also has the broken white eye ring, but MacGillivray's has a gray throat and a yellow belly and undertail coverts.

Resources Used

During the sighting, we searched Peterson's Field Guide, and found no good match. After the sighting, we consulted National Geographic, and suspected a northern parula, but still had no great match. We finally found the perfect match in the Sibley Guide to Birds, on page 425, which shows warblers in winter plumage.

Previous Experience

I had only seen one northern parula before -- in Vermont -- so I had very limited experience with the species. I am well acquainted with the resident birds of Paonia, however, including kinglets. gnatcatchers, bushtits and warblers.

Additional Information

Comments: My father and I got an excellent look at this bird. We watched it for at least ten minutes, using binoculars, though it was only ten feet away. As we watched, we noted each detail of its plumage, so the other could double-check our observations. I'm completely confident in the description of the bird.
