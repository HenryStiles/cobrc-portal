Description of the Bird

Small yellow-throated, bluish and green backed warbler with white wing bars. Maxilla dark gray, mandible nearly white. Face bluish-gray, no eye-arcs visible (may be sunken into eye sockets.) Auriculars bluish-gray. Crown feathers bluish-gray, tipped with green. Saddle greenish yellow. Chest only slightly more orange-yellow than rest of throat and breast. Underparts below breast unmarked white. Wings dark gray, flight feathers with thin pale edging. Tail a bit lighter gray than the wings. Feet and legs almost black.
Behaviors: found dead on sidewalk
Call: not heard
Plumage: female or immature male

Similar Species Discussion

Tropical Parula ruled out by bluish gray malar (vs. yellow on TRPA) and longer primary projection than TRPA. Bird given to Tony Leukering for measurement and confirmation of ID.
