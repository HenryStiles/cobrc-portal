Habitat

Description of the Bird

The birds were heard only, on both the 22nd and 23rd.  They were first detected from approximately 960 meters (according to Google earth).  I went and got Ted and Bryan as they had gone to bed already and we walked over to confirm what I was sure I was hearing.  During the daylight hours of July 23rd I worked one of the marshes and had a bird respond with a ki-ki-krrr while also hearing another bird give some grunting/growling type calls.  The birds were found in two different marshes.  The two marshes are about 1.4 miles apart with one being entirely bull rushes and the other being almost entirely a cattail marsh.

Similar Species Discussion

Basically there is no other species that call like a Black Rail in the US.  There are species that could mimic the rails but this is highly unlikely in this case.
