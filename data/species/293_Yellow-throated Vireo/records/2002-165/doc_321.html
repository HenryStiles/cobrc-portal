Description of the Bird

Chris and I heard the vireo sing and I said that it was probably a Cassin's Vireo, as it sounded so hoarse. I had heard Cassin's sing in California and it always struck me as sounding a lot like Yellow-throated Vireo. Chris had never heard Cassin's Vireo sing, so he was quite interested in watching the bird sing. The bird was high in a very tall cottonwood and it took us a while to find it, but I finally did and exclaimed, "It's a Yellow-throated!" Of course, Chris said that he thought that it had sounded like a YTVI.

The bird was quite bright, being a yellowy-green on the crown and nape and yellow below (chin, throat, breast) with a white belly with no vent strap. The eyes were dark and surrounded by bright yellow spectacles, except for the dark loral line. We couldn't really see the back. The wings were dark with two white wingbars formed by tips to the greater and median coverts, respectively. The tail looked dark from below. The bill was somewhat thick (thicker than on warblers -- Yellow Warblers were nearby) and the legs looked dark from where we were.
Behaviors: The bird was foraging high in a couple of cottonwoods and singing sporadically
Call: Sang primary song at least 15 times; an obvious vireo song of two-note phrases repeated quite often, it was a hoarse, burry "three-eight", similar to the song of Cassin's Vireo
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

A few warblers (particularly Pine Warbler) are somewhat similar in appearance, but none sing anything like this bird's song. Cassin's Vireo is ruled out by the strong yellow chin, throat, and chest. Yellow-green Vireo ruled out by the bright white wingbars and by the bird's song.
