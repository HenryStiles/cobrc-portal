Description of the Bird

The bird was near the top of an 80' tall deciduous tree, singing loudly.

The song was clearly vireo-like, with a hoarse two-note phrase, a 5 - 10 second pause, and then the two-note phrase again.

The bird was larger than a Warbling Vireo, but with generally similar shape.

It had a bold white belly, unstreaked, and a bright yellow unstreaked breast.

The face showed bright yellow spectacles on a medium background (dark eye, solid medium dark nape and mantle).

The bill was dark, too thick for a warbler.

The wings were dark, but I could not see details from my viewing position.
Behaviors: The bird perched near the top of a tall deciduous tree, sang, and then moved to a different perch. It did not move around quickly through foliage in the typical manner of a warbler.
Call: The song was clearly vireo-like, with a hoarse two-note phrase, a 5 - 10 second pause, and then the two-note phrase again.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The bright yellow throat and spectacles quickly rule out other species of vireo. The closest species in terms of plumage is probably Pine Warbler. The vireo-like song, thick bill, and brightness of the yellow on the throat and spectacles rule out Pine Warbler.
