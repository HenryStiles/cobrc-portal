Description of the Bird

the birds were medium sized finches - notcieably larger than nearby Pine Siskins, but much smaller than a Pine Grosbeak. I only looked at the birds long enough to be sure they were Red Crossbills, but I saw the crossed bill, the brick red coloration of the one adult male I could see, and black wings with no white wingbars.

See below for description of the call.
Behaviors: the birds were first seen flying into the top of an engalmann spruce. They fed upon cones there for a couple of minutes and then flew out, at which point I obtained the recordings of the call.
Call: see below
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than crossbills were ruled out by the size, color, and of course the crossed bill. White-winged Crossbill was ruled out by the brick red color of the male and lack of white wing bars, as well as the different call.

These birds were identified as type-3 individuals by the distinctive call spectrogaph. The shape of this spectrograph is somewhat like an inverted "N", and does not extend up high intially or down lower at the end as in type 2 call notes. The shape was also very different from the calls of type 5 birds, which typically have two elements simaltaneously. Comparisons of the spectrogrpahs to those of known type 3 birds show a perfect match.

Previous Experience

I have seen many Red Crossbills, and since I got recording equipment have recorded many individuals. I am very familiar with type 2 and type 5 call types, the expected types in Colorado, but am much less familiar with type 3.
