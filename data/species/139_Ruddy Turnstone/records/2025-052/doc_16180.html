Description of the Bird

Quite incredible with a group of 4 not found in the State before. People were camping around the lake in their vehicles, dogs were running around with generators running. We birded inbetween the campers & when we came to the west corner, we couldn't believe our eyes. We both said "Ruddy Turnstones" simultaneousl! All 4 were in breeding plumage. The Killdeer kept chasing 1 in particular. 

Similar Species Discussion

The RUTU breeding plumage with face & breast plate are black marked, along with a mixture of black & brown on their backs. A smaller & longer legged Killdeer has a different consistent head pattern, unlike the RUTU,  has double breast band & solid brown back. 

Previous Experience

I have seen the RUTU 3 other times in Colorado with photos. Additionally, I have seen them in Ohio, 12 times in Texas  & 4 times in  Florida. i feel there are not any similar except the Black Turnstone which I have not seen in Colorado.
