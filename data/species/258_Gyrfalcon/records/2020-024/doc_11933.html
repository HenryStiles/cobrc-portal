Description of the Bird

At the gyrfalcon stakeout site about 1/4 mile of Taft Hill Road a falcon  flew up from the grass/prairie area North of the gravel road and lit on  a power line pole.  In flight up to the perch it was clear this was a  falcon; the angular wing shape. Though brief it appeared the main part  of the wing between the body and elbow was broad, with a sharp angle in  powered flight of the forewing.Lighting was poor from my first  vantage point. It appeared the back of the bird was a solid color with  some slight variation in tone, but with the poor lighting this was  difficult to describe in detail. The tail extended slightly below the  primaries when folded. After about 15 minutes I moved position (by  vehicle) to the opposite side of the bird hoping for better lighting  conditions. (Knowing this was risky I also knew I could not see enough  detail of the front of the bird to identify, and was concerned about the  bird taking flight before better observation could take place.Looking  at the front of the bird in good lighting the base color was  white/light with horizontal streaking coming in from the flanks in  toward the central part of the bird, but not completely crossing along  the belly or breast. Above the flank streaking lighter vertical barring  showed from the throat down through the belly. and on the breast  extended outward to the shoulders of the wings. A portion of the tail  was visible from behind the powerline pole and slight horizontal barring  was visible. Head feathering was the same color as the back; a  darker grayish and covered the entire head. The area immediately below  the eye was lighter - almost creating a small whitish patch. There  appeared to be a very faint horizontal white mark above the eye, but it  was faint and slight if present at all. On the side of the face slightly  behind the beak the gray feathering appeared darker, but it did not  make a dark "mustache" .I could only see one foot under the  feathering well enough; the foot was yellow. The beak was hooked at the  tip and also darker at the tip than the middle or base. It looked to be  dull yellow at the base, gray in the middle, and dark gray at the tip.I  have seen gyrfalcon on two other occasions in Minnesota, and have  experience with both prairie falcon (seen an hour before this  observation) and peregrine falcon. I eliminated Peregrine  immediately as it would tend to show a more thoroughly solid colored  head with downward loop in the area of the mustache, and would show  barring all across the belly and breast.   I believe prairie falcon  would show a more defined mustache, be slighter (lithe) in build, and  not show barring on the flanks, but would more likely show heavier  vertical streaking. Also, the overall color I would expect to be lighter  brown, and not grayish.
