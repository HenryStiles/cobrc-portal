Description of the Bird

the bird was a very large falcon, over half the size of a Bald Eagle that flew over the exact same spot a minute later, and appearing substantially larger than a female Northern Harrier flying around in the same area. The bird was heavy bodied and chested, with long and broad wings that were pointed, but not so much as other falcons. The overall look appeared part-way between a normal falcon and a buteo. The bird had a long and broad square-tipped tail. I did not note the color of any of the bare parts.

The head and back of the bird was a medium brown, with a hint of a grayish tinge that was hard to see. The head had a weakly differentiated mustache stripe on the face. Otherwise the face appeared a bit pale brown than the rest of the head and back. The underparts were streaked about the same color brown as the back, to a degree so the browner streaking and paler background were about equally evident. The tail was barred paler brown and darker brown, with the paler brown predmoninating. The underwings were a uniform brown, about the same color as the back, except for slighty but noticeably darker underwing coverts.
Behaviors: The bird was first seen flying in from the right, going after a duck, probably a Mallard, missing, and then landing on a spit of land. It stayed there for about 30 seconds, and then took off to the left, over a hill, and out of site. It flew with powerful wingbeats slower than I would expect from a smaller falcon.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal gray morph

Similar Species Discussion

Peregrine Falcon was ruled out by the more uniform and paler coloration than expected for a juvenile, browner than expected for an adult, larger size and especially heavier build, less pointed and broader wings, contrasting underwing coverts, much less evident mustachial stripe, and more powerful flight with slower wingbeats.

Prairie Falcon was ruled out by larger size, much heavier build, broader and less pointed wings, much less contrast in the underwing coverts and especially the axillaries, more uniform underwings as a whole, flight style with slower and more powerful wingbeats, less evident mustachial stripe, and broader tail.

All buteos were ruled out by falconoid shape, with more pointed wings than expected for a buteo, longer tail, by the different flight style, by the non-buteo head pattern, and by overall patterning not matched by any buteo species and plumage.
