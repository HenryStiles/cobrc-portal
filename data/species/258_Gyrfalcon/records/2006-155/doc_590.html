Description of the Bird

Large falcon first observed by others in flight low over lake shoreline, but first observed by me shortly thereafter standing on shore, then flying off low over shore and water. Superficially like a Prairie Falcon, with uniform pale brown coloration (as seen from a great distance); in flight, long pointed wings; wing-beats slower than typical Prairie Falcon; underwings same pale brown coloration as the rest of the bird.
Behaviors: Flight and resting on shoreline.
Call: n.a.
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Only Prairie Falcon is similarly shaped and plumaged but has blackish underwing coverts and axilars.
