Description of the Bird

Large dark falcon came in harassing the gulls before they all took off.  The back was solid gray and the underside was dark and streaky with thick streaks revealing little white (light) on the underside.  The wings and tail were very broad.  The legs were bluish and the face was plainer and less patterend than a Peregrine Falcon.  Photos show all diagnostic marks.  I was unsure of the identification in the field until doing research at home. 

Similar Species Discussion

Peregrine Falcon should be smaller and have a stronger facial pattern.  The wings and tail are also broader than a Peregrine should be. 

Resources Used

I sent photos around to some falconer friends as well as looked at photos on eBird. 

Previous Experience

I have only seen one Gyrfalcon well enough to identify it (as well as one too far away to identify well, but that was likely a Gyr).  I have seen lots of Peregrines, but I am not too familiar with the dark juveniles.
