Description of the Bird

the bird was a fairly large duck, a bit larger than nearby dabblers including Mallard and American Wigeon. The overall shape was quite stocky, due to a short tail, large head, and overall bulky shape. The bill was black, and quite large, with a triangular shape and straight culmen. The bill had a "stuck-on" look to the head.

The cap of the bird was dark brown, contrasting with lighter brown on the rest of the head. There was a small brownish-white spot between the bill and the eye, and another behind the eye. The res of the body was a fairly dark brown, not quite as dark as the cap.
Behaviors: the bird was swimming around a small pond, not really in the company of other ducks.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Almost all other species of duck were ruled out by the fairly uniform brown color, large size, stocky shape, white spots on the face, and large, triangular bill.

White-winged Scoter was ruled out by the much larger triangular bill with a straight culmen, dark cap, more compact shape, and different shape of the white spots on the face.

Black Scoter was ruled out by very different shape and face pattern.

Harlequin Duck was ruled out by much larger size, much stockier shape, much larger bill, etc.
