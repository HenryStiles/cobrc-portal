Description of the Bird

Warbler was finding food on the ground under leaves. Some of the birders said the bird was giving a chip call which I did not hear. The bird was olive-green with bold stripes on its head. One strip ran through each eye and two more dark brown stripes were on the crown. Between the stripes the color was yellowish. The bill was long and pointed for a warbler. Tail was short but overall the bird was average in size for a warbler. Legs were flesh colored or bordered on the pink side.

Bird was seen by others until a big snow came in on December 5th. The bird was last seen on the 4th. Several days later a neighbor across the street found the bird dead. It is headed for the DMNH in Denver as a specimen.

Similar Species Discussion

The bold head pattern just about eliminates all contenders. Black-and-white Warbler has a bold head pattern but the overall color of the bird is black and white, not olive green. Ovenbirds have a bold head markings but lack eye stripe and have a spotted chest. Golden-crowned Warbler is a close match but the eye stripe is not bold and highly unlikely due to its range. Worm-eating Warbler is quite unique in plumage and it is not likely an experienced birder with a close, good look would confuse it with another species.
