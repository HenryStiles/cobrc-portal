Habitat

Cottonwood riparian and pinon juniper are in the area. Bird was near creek but moving uphill into what is predominantly PJ.
I shared photos with Tony Leukering.
He felt there was no reason to report the bird as anything but 'Observed' for Atlas II data. No suitable breeding habitat.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovksi SLC 7x42
Distance:  <100 at closest - estimate
Light:  Excellent. Mid-day with clouds.

Description of the Bird

Saw black bird fly to top of snag. Crest was immediately visible. Binoculars showed red eye.
It called for awhile then stopped. I went to get my wife so she could see it. Also got camera.
Came back and it was still in the same location.
Got distant and then closer photos.
When it next flew the white patches in the primaries were clearly seen. It moved several times but was going generally away from me and uphill so I could not get closer.
I have seen the bird before in Arizona so it was not unfamiliar to me, just surprising.
Behaviors: Bird stayed near top of trees calling softly. Seemed somewhat agitated but only I got fairly close.
Moved from tree to tree. Would look around and call for awhile before moving to next tree. No additional birds were seen.
Call: Soft series of notes with noticeable pauses
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing similar - solid black with crest, red eye and white wing patches.

Additional Information

Comments: This sighting was not reported to cobirds. It occurred while I was doing Breeding Bird Atlas surveys. I had no chance to post to cobirds or call anyone until I returned home 4 days later. At that point I felt it would make little sense to tell people about a bird that was likely long gone. It was moving out of the canyon as I watched and I saw no evidence that it was staying in the area.
