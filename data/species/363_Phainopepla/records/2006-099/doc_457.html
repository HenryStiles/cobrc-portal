Description of the Bird

This is a straight forward identification.

This was and all gray bird with a ragged gray crest and a red eye. When it flew the bird showed a long tail and rounded wings.

The bird was a slender and was a little smaller than the Ash-throated Flycather that was nearby.
Behaviors: Often perched on top of dead twigs mostly in midstory of bushes but on occassion on top of a one-seeded juniper. Had a jerky flight as if having difficulty flying, especially in response to a tape of its song.


Sometimes perched only a few feet off of ground but almost always on dead branches where it was visible for long periods of time.
Call: on occassion would respond to the tape of its song with a soft song
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The only other similar species is juvenile Cedar Waxwing which is not all-gray as was this bird. The other Silky Flycatcher from Mexico has different coloration.
