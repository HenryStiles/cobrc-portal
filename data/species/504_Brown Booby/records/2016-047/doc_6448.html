Description of the Bird

Please see pics which I believe I'll be able to attach. There are four origianls taken with an iPod 22 June about noon. Additionally, there is one that was cropped and 'optimized' in Lightroom. It was the first picture taken and is almost exactly where I initially spotted the bird. As you can see, it was on some rocks by the side of the roadway. You can see the creek in the background. It seemed to not have any interest in the creek. The hamlet in which I found it - it is not a town - is Rowena. I'd be happy to show you where I spotted it if you wish. But it was pretty close to the center of the community. The bird was never in the creek itself. Right after the sent pic was taken it was overwhelmed by its fear of me and tried to get away by scrambling and trying to fly. It wasn't a very effective escape as it only got across the road. I looked around and never saw anyone to ask about the bird. There were no fences or enclosures in the immediate area through or from which it might have escaped as a captive bird. After about five minutes I decided I'd stressed the bird enough and left. I was cycling up the canyon. On the way back down, I could not find the bird.

I returned Friday, 1 July, and 'interviewed' residents of the four closest homes to sighting site. None had seen the bird much less owned it. All asserted the community is small enough that everyone knows each other and would know about anyone having such a bird. (It, I expect, would make a lousy pet!) Names available on request. Still no sign of the bird or carcass on casual inspection of the area.

Sighting site in front of house with address 8501 Lefthand. Measured distance between sighting site and Lee Hill Rd intersection is between 2.4 and 2.5 miles, measured up and back.
