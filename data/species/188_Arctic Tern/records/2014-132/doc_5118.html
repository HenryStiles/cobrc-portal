Description of the Bird

Light-colored, not Black, tern with long forked tail.  Top of head entirely black.  Bill blood-red without any dark on tip. Bill short (for a tern) and thin at the base.  The black cap extended down very near the bill gape.  The wing coloration on the dorsal surface was evenly light gray.  The breast was medium gray, darker than the wings. There was a thin trailing edge to the primaries and some of the secondaries.  The flight was buoyant.  

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern also has a reddish bill but is usually noticeably longer, thicker and at this time of year might still have a dark tip and not as blood red as in this bird.  The tail on a Common Tern would not seem extremely long while in flight.  The trailing edge of a Common Tern should show a wider black area on the primaires with an area of indentation into the wing in the inner primaries.  Common would more often plunge dive however this bird was seen swooping to the water but never entering it.

Previous Experience

I worked 5 summers in northern Alaska with Arctic Tern being seen on many days but with no Common Terns for comparison. They seem an elegant bird to me when they fly.
