Description of the Bird

First seen on the 8th in late evening light by Mark Peterson. On the morning of the 9th bird was seen sitting amongst a flock of mostly California Gulls where it was dwarfed.  Dark full cap, without any white feathering towards the base of the bill apparent. Long wings extend well past tail.  Bill blood-red and thin at the base. Black cap extends down almost to gape.  Feet just barely visible while it perched on the bare ground.  In flight, buoyant.  Watched fly to water twice, both time plucking object of the surface of the water, not plunge-diving. In flight tail seemed long.  Trailing edge of primaries with thin dark edge extending to beginning of secondaries. In flight dorsal wing view was of evenly colored light gray.  

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern.  Bill color red but not as blood red, more orange-red as this bill with thicker base and longer bill.  Black cap can be present but usually white feathering should appear towards the base of the bill.  On land legs do appear short but not so short as to look like bird's breast is on the ground like in this bird.  Breast of Common Tern not as dark in appearance as this bird.  In flight the trailing edge would show a wider black edge with a noticeable indentation on the inner primaires.  Common Term more likely to plunge dive.
