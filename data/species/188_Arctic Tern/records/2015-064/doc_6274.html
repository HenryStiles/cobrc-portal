Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Vortex Viper 10x42 binoculars, Kowa 20-60X spotting scope, Canon SX-50 camera
Distance:  25 meters at closest (dove into water directly in front of us, also flew over), up to half a mile across the reservoir.
Light:  Sunny morning, some clouds.

Description of the Bird


Saw all red bill (no black tip), gray body, long tail. No calls heard. Passed by three times during the period of birding (1 hour and 24 minutes total). Also viewed while flying much farther away (all three times counter-clockwise) around reservoir.

On third pass, waited and tried to take photos for documentation.

This bird was first reported by David Wade some days earlier.

Similar Species Discussion


Forster's Tern (some were present) was eliminated due to their orange bill and black tip, plus its lack of gray body. The other strong consideration was Common Tern. This species was eliminated due to its orange bill with black tip. Further, the bird in question had what seemed a very long tail.

Resources Used

Previous Experience


I have only seen an Arctic Tern one other time, also in Colorado.

Perviously I have seen some Common Terns in Colorado. However, last month I saw many Common Terns on the Bolivar penisula near High Island.
