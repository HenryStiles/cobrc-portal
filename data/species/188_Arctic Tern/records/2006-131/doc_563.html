Description of the Bird

This bird was a medium sized Tern about same size as nearby Common & Forster's Terns, but it had a longer tail, & smaller head and bill. Thus its "jizz" was different than the other terns, because of its short front end and long extended rear end. This "jizz" was very noticeable in flight compared to the other Terns.

CROWN & FACE: Crown was solid black from nape to base of bill. Black extended over dark eye. Face and throat were white which contrasted with crown & smokey light gray underparts.

WINGS: They were curved, pointed at tips, and exhibited a light grayish mantle. On perched bird the tail extended beyond wing tips. In flight, a thin black line was noticed bordering the primaries, especially on the wing's underside and trailing edge of primaries.

BILL: Bill was short, noticeably shorter than head width from nape to lores, very slender, and scarlet red.

LEGS & FEET: Legs were very short and reddish as were feet.
Behaviors: This bird flew around and island, occasionally dived into water to feed, and then perched on rocks near the island.
Call: none
Plumage: alternate adult

Similar Species Discussion

Common & Forster's Terns are larger headed giving there front end's a more equal and balanced jizz to their rear ends, especially Common Terns which have proportionately shorter tails than Arctic Terns.

Both Common & Forster's Terns can have reddish orange bills but with a black tip. The bird I saw had a red bill without a black tip, and had a noticeably smaller bill than nearby Common & Forster's.

Underparts of Common & Forster's Terns appeared whiter than the smokey grayish underparts of Arctic Tern.

The other terns also were longer legged when perched than the Arctic Tern.

Forster's Terns generally lose their black crowns in September and appear white crowned & in basic plumage. The Arctic Tern seen had a solid black crown indicating alternate plumage.
