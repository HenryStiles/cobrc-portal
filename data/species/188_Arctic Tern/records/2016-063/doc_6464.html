Description of the Bird

Shortly after I pulled up to pond, which is 100 meters+ from the road, I saw a pale tern flying over yonder pond. I assumed it was a FOTE, but as I put my scope on it, I could see its bouncy flight despite little or no wind. I then truly looked at the bird. Crown black. Mantle pale and pearly with a narrow dusky trailing edge to the primaries and paler secondaries. Though pale, there was none of the frosty white look on the primaries that FOTEs have. The tail looked long and deeply forked, head squat and bill smallish, all darkish red, slim (shape as compared to FOTE or COTE). The underparts were notably gray, paler (whitish) on auriculars, imparting a white cheeked look. Underwing white with narrow blackish trailing edge to primaries. As I was watching this bird, a second pale bird flew by. It fit precisely the same description. I couldn't tell the two birds apart. 

Similar Species Discussion

FOTE: Whiter below, large bill that is more orange and has black tip. Larger head. 
COTE. Not quite as gray below. Bill with black tip. Darker upper wing with blackish on primaries. 
Both terns have different flight style. 
Roseate Tern with longer tail, all black bill, white below.
