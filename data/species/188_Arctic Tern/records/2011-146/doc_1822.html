Description of the Bird

Typical Sterna tern size and shape, although no other terns were observed to compare with. The thin bill was entirely black and of medium length. Its forehead, chin, and underparts were white. The remainder of the head (crown, hindneck, post-ocular region) was black. A white hind collar was evident as well. Upperwings were light gray with a slight scaly appearance. A very thin black trailing edge could be seen on the outer primaries (I counted 7 primaries using photos) as well as a faint, slightly darker gray carpal bar. The primaries were otherwise primarily white, and in combination with the white secondary feathers, the tern had a mild Sabine's Gull-like appearance to the upperwings. The tern's rump and forked tail were entirely white, except for thin dark gray/black edging on the outer tail feathers.
Behaviors: Flying around the reservoir, occasionally diving into the water briefly on hunting forays.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern would exhibit a darker carpal bar, dark gray tones to the secondaries, and have a gray rump.
