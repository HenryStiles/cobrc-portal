Description of the Bird

Mostly seen in company of Forster's Tern. Wings nearly uniform pale above except dark 'carpal bar' and narrow dark trailing edge to outer 2/3 or so of primaries. No dark on secondaries. No dark elsewhere on on primaries. Underwings white with narrow blackish trailing edge on outermost two-thirds or so of primaries. Body white. Head white with black "tonsure" typical of immature (and basic plumage) Arctic and Common Terns. Bill small and black. In flight, head looked stubby, flight less direct and "bouncier" than that of Forster's Tern. Perched, legs appeared short. Tail white and forked, appropriate length for immature bird, perhaps a bit longer than I'd expect in immature Common Tern. Carpal bar, perched and in flight, not quite as dark as that of COTE.

Similar Species Discussion

Forster's Tern different head pattern, larger bill, larger bird (as seen in direct comparison), typically has sheen on primaries, even in worn birds
Common Tern immature should have dark on secondaries, more dark on upper wing, broader dark trailing edge to primaries on underwing. Flight usually appears less bouncy, head larger/longer, legs longer. Adult Common Tern should have dark "wedge" onto primaries from trailiing edge, longer tail, broader trailing edge to primaries. The translucency sometimes seen on Arctics wings would not be seen in this direct light without a hint of backlighting. Typically COTE bill looks larger as well.
