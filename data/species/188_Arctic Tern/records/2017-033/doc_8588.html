Description of the Bird

Flying with 2 COTE. Black cap. All red bill. Flight more bouncy than that of COTE. Head "stubbier," bill smaller, tail longer. Wings even pearly gray above. Narrow border to trailing edge of primaries barely visible. At same time, on companion COTEs, the dark wedge on primaries and trailing edge on underside of primaries easily visible. Ashy gray chest and belly extending onto chin causing a white "line" on cheek between black cap and gray underparts. Tail simply appeared white. Too far to see edge darkness. Birds circling about, seemingly searching for food, providing a variety of angles for viewing

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern, Forster's Tern
Common Tern larger bill, with black tip, not as squat-headed in appearance, has dark wedge on upperside of primaries, broader dark edge underside of primaries. Less bouncy flight
Forster's Tern. Larger-billed, bill orange with black tip, flight distinctly more direct, without bounciness. Head distinctly larger. Wings often have shining quality to uppersides of primaries which this bird lacked. Forster's Tern white below
