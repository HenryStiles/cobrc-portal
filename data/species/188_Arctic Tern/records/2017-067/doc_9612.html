Description of the Bird

First seen on buoy well out on lake. Short-legged tern with entirely black crown except a small white spot just above bill. Above pearly gray, folded primaries appearing about same color as remainder of wing. Small white crescent on tip of tertials. Tail tip protruded just beyond wingtips. Bill small and bright cherry to fire-engine red. No black tip. I could not see enough of legs to judge color! Underparts pale but distinctly smokey gray, turning to white on face. 

Buoyant flight. In flight, upper wings appeared an even pale pearly gray, a bit paler on secondaries and adjacent coverts. Underwing nearly clean white, but narrow dark trailing edge to primaries. Back same color as wings, but rump and upper tail white. Could not see black edging to tail. No dusky shoulder mark as in basic/immature Common Tern (and Arctic Tern)

Head looked relatively short, bill still looked small, tail long. 

Viewed in flight for 1-2 minutes.
Camera at car, alas

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern. Should be shorter tailed, larger headed and billed with black tip to bill. It would be most unusual for a Common Tern to still be in full alternate plumage. Upperwing lacked dusky wedge of COTE, and edge to underside of primaries too narrow for COTE. 

Forster's Tern. Should be in basic plumage by now. More dusky on underside of primaries. Primaries should have paler sheen whereas pale area on/adjacent to secondaries should be lacking. Should not have dusky gray underparts. Bill should be larger, more orange, dark tip

Roseate Tern. Larger billed, bill should be black by now, not dusky below.
