Description of the Bird

From the north side of the lake, I spotted a small tern in flight, with characteristic narrow wings and bouncy flight.  For better viewing, I drove to the south side of the lake and watched from a stationary location for over half an hour.  I observed (alternating scope, binoculars, and camera) the bird in flight several times; two flybys were quite close.  Otherwise, I observed the bird perched on a far buoy.  I find tern identification to be challenging, so I took a couple of minutes to look at Sibley's book to remind myself what characteristics I should pay attention to.

When the bird was perched on the buoy, I could see through the scope that the bird had a complete black cap, and it had the long wings of a tern.  The gray color of the mantle matched the gray color of the folded primaries.  The gray colors of the upperparts and underparts were also similar.  No carpal bar could be seen. The bird had a relatively small, rounded head.  I also got the impression that it had short legs but am not confident in that observation.  The bill appeared to be red, but the color depended on the angle relative to the sun.

When the tern was flying, it was at times close enough so that I could see the bill color better -- dark red.  No black tip was apparent, either through binoculars or later in photos.  The bill appeared small, and photos confirm this characteristic.  The extensive black cap was obvious.  I could also see the gray wash on the breast and belly.  Uppersides of the wings appeared pale gray, with the trailing part (including secondaries) slighty paler than the forward part.  I paid close attention to the undersides of the wings but could never see dark markings near the primary tips.  Photos show a very thin dark mark at the tips of the primaries.  Photos show a relatively long tail, for a tern.

The tern occasionally dove headfirst to the water's surface, catching small fish.  I didn't hear any calls but was rather focused on visual observations.

Similar Species Discussion

I considered Common and Arctic Terns, which have the observed reddish bills and gray wash on the undersides in breeding adult plumage.  Several characteristics pointed toward Arctic rather than Common Tern for the observed bird:  small bill; dark red bill color without black tip; small, rounded head; long tail; and very thin band of dark color on primary tips on undersides of wings.
