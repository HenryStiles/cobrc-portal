Description of the Bird

I was co-leading a birding tour, and we stopped at Jim Hamm Pond for a bathroom break. I checked the pond, and saw a tern flying by. The bird was certainly a sterna tern, it had a black cap, and all short red bill, with no dark color. The wings were gray and pointed. It had a white tail that seemed long, when the tail was spread it had deeply forked tail. The belly was gray, not white. The under tail was more white. The bird was only seen in flight, and it flew around the pond for 15 minutes, and disappeared.
Behaviors: Flying around the pond.
Call: none
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Forster's Tern -- black tip on the bill, that is usually orange. Underparts more white, not gray. Longer bill. More in white primaries, not gray.

Common Tern -- longer bill, with black tip. More black in the wings, which would have been noticeable if the bird had this.
