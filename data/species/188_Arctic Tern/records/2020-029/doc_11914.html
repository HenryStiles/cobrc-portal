Description of the Bird

This bird was silent and flying over south Rice's Pond.  Flight was boyant with quick changes in direction and a couple of fluttering dives towards water but the bird did not land.   This bird was white in appearance with a black around head with some white on forehead.  Wings had  gray over top along leading edge and down on the upper side of the primary flight feathers..  Otherwise, bird was white.  The tail was forked.  The bill was orange/red with black at the tip.  The feet were orange.  The bird was more delicate and smaller in my experience than Forster's Tern or Common Tern.

Similar Species Discussion

I initally called this a Forster's Tern as that was more likely there at this time of year but was bothered by the size of the bird that it wasn't a Forster's Tern.   I had no experience with Arctic Terns previously to this so I put the photo out on a couple of groups on Facebook including the CFO birding page and the Colorado Rare Bird Page for id.  Several people agreed this isn't a Forster's Tern and that Arctic Tern was most likely.  The immature Forster's tern would not have that much black on sides and back of head.  Also, my feeling was that this tern was smaller than Forster's or common Terns. 

Resources Used

Sibley, Ibird Pro, Facebook pages for Colorado Field Ornithologists and Colorado Rare Bird, also better birder friends who commented on my own Facebook page from my photos.

Previous Experience

I am knowledgable with Forster's Tern and Common Tern's but not with Arctic Tern.  This was the first time I had ever seen one other than in photos.
