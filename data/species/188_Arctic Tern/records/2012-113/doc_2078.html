Large lake about 1.5 mile circumference surrounded by cottonwoods on the west & north, maintained grassy parklands on east & south sides. Residential neighborhoods west and east of lake, grassy prairie and scrub oak canyon lands to south and north of Lake respectively.

Optics:  15-60 zoom lens Swift spotting scope & 10x40 Zeiss binocular

Light:  Adequate-bird was mostly west of observer. Weather--low clouds and drizzle.

Description of the Bird

A medium sized tern about same size as nearby Common Tern, but with a smaller head, narrower wings and an overall daintier appearance than Common Tern.

Arctic's black cap extended to nape & top of crown.
Forehead was white. Silvery white mantle showed no dark markings at all including none of the usual thin dark lines outlining primaries often seen on Arctic Terns. Primaries & outer secondaries appeared paler than rest of mantle suggesting 2nd cycle bird. Distance(closest view about 100 yards)may have prevented my seeing these lines. Underside of wings were paler than mantle with no dark markings nor lines observed. Bill was dark but distance made its size & shape difficult to assess.
I did not see legs well.

Very distinctive were the bird's "jizz" & flight pattern. Its small head made the bird's center of gravity appear weighted to its rear, even though its forked white tail did not appear unusually long.
Its flight pattern appeared somewhat labored with slight but rapid increases & decreases in elevation depending on respective downstroke & upstroke of wings, giving the flight pattern a more bouncy & buoyant pattern than nearby Common Tern's whose flight pattern was more flat-lined and gull like.
Behaviors: I observed this bird only in flight as it circled and foraged around the lake occasionally diving to catch prey.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 2nd cyle or possibly winter adult

Similar Species Discussion

Nearby Common Tern showed much slate gray in primaries, appeared larger headed, broader winged, with a more direct less buoyant flight pattern than the Arctic Tern.

Forster's Terns can appear fairly pale mantled like Arctic. But in September, Forster's are totally white capped with blackish colors confined mostly to rear of its eye.
