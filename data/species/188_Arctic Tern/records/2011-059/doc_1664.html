Description of the Bird

This was a very short-legged tern with small rounded head. The bill was dark-red based with some black remaining on the distal end and was much shorter than in Forster's Tern, the common CO tern. In flight the bird appeared to me to be neckless, maybe front heavy to some degree. The wings were unicolor gray on the dorsal surface. Underneath the trailing edge of the primaries had a narrow black bar noticable at close range. The tibio-tarsus was reddish and barely visible the one time I saw it perched which alerted me to the fact it might be an ARTE. The bird had a dark cap with a tiny patch of white just behind the bill. While the bird was perched, I was unable to she the relationship between the wing length and tail length.
Behaviors: Buoyant in flight. Acitvely fed by looping down and pulling up at the surface of the water to snatch some insect (midges) always faci
ng the wind while feeding and then using the wind to carry it back to start the feeding again and again for over an hour that I watched it.
Call: none heard
Plumage: molting to adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern is the only other ABA-area bird that comes close with the red, not orange bill.

Separated with difficulty from COTE, mostly by the shorter legs while it was standing (see photo), thinner trailing black primaries edging, no sign of darker dorsal primaries of COTE, slightly shorter bill, slimmer, more front end heavy, and apparently a big factor is the small spot of white just behind the bill.

Previous Experience

I worked for 5 summer in Alaska where ARTE was the breeding bird but I don't have any comparative experience with COTE and ARTE side by side. I have to say I thought it was an ARTE but I asked for expert opinion for confirmation.
