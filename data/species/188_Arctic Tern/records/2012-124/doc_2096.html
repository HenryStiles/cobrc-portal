Description of the Bird

A medium sized sterna tern in full breeding plumage. The tern was uniform pale gray above including the total upper surface of wing. The underside was nearly completely the same shade of gray, paler at the throat and white from the vent back to the undertail. A large, black cap extended down below the eye, nearly touching the gape. The face was white below the cap with only a sliver of white between the gape and black cap. The tail was long and forked and protruded slightly beyond the folded primaries (see photo). The folded primaries were slightly darker than the mantle and gave me some concern about the ID. The legs were very short and red and the bill was small and completely dark red and appeared too small for COTE.
Behaviors: Flying and diving for food, successfully catching fish then landing and sitting on bouys in the middle of the lake. Flight style was 'buoyant'.
Call: none
Plumage: adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

this bird is mostly easily confused with Common Tern, but has a smaller head and bill. Also the bill is completely red, not bi-colored orange. Legs of ARTE are shorter and tail is longer, protruding beyond the folded primaries in sitting bird.
