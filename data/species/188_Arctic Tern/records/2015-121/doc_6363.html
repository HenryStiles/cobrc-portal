Description of the Bird

Small tern observed in flight and plunge-diving in the company of a dozen or so Bonaparte's Gulls.

Smaller and more delicate than Bonapartes, with long, narrow wings and forked tail. Black cap with white forehead and short, thin black bill. Wings appeared almost uniformly white but with bright white secondaries and with some contrast in the primaries - not the more obvious black tips and darker secondaries you would see on a juv. Common Tern. Perhaps a hint of a darker carpal bar, but not obvious.

Dowell and Nunes were able to photograph the bird.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern would be the most similar in terms of size, behavior and plumage, but Common Tern would have dark primaries and secondaries, and likely more obvious dark carpal bar. Forster's Tern would show less black in the cap and would be overall bulkier, with broader wings and stronger flight (wind was really gusting and the bird's flight was being affected...

Resources Used

I used the Sibley app in the field and then consulted Sibley 2nd Edition at home.

Previous Experience

I'm quite familiar with Common & Forster's Terns, but have only seen one Arctic - an adult in 2013. This bird was a juvinile and I was not positive about the identification in the field, although I suspected it was an Arctic. I was able to study it in good light but at a distance through a scope in gusting wind. Later I was able to study the field marks in Sibley to confirm the ID to the best of my knowledge.
