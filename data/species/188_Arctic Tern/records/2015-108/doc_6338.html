Description of the Bird

A juvenile sterna tern, age based on scallopped-looking coverts and scapulars.  White forehead with extensive black on nape, crown and cap.  Gray mantle and primaries, with faint dark carpal bar seen when resting.  Short legs and small, round head and small, dark bill.  In flight, wings were gray except secondaries were white with white-tipped secondary coverts.  Very small dark spots on extreme tips of primaries.  No secondary dark bar was evident.  The bird would fly to catch fish and return to the rocky shore to roost.  In flight, the downward motion of the wings cause the bir to rise each wing beat, giving it a buoyant looking flight.  

Similar Species Discussion

The nearly complete dark cap eliminated Forster's Tern.  Juvenile plumage Common Tern is very similar except the wing pattern is much different.
