Description of the Bird

As widely reported, this bird spent time roosting on the sand spit area and then would periodically fly out and feed over the reservoir.  I also saw the bird from the NW corner of the reservoir, the marina area, on the Jeffco side, and was fairly certain that it spent some time feeding in Jeffco water.  

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern considered and eliminated because this bird had sharply defined blackish tips on undersides of primaries (in flight); also, legs seemed too short for Common.  This report submitted mainly to add photos.
