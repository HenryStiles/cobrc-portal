Description of the Bird

Smaller headed and shorter billed than COTE present. Shorter legged. Head white with more extensive black tonsure than COTE (black from just in front of eyes wrapping around back of head to other side), extending up onto crown. Bill mostly or entirely black. Underparts white. Mantle pearly gray except dusky carpal bar and white secondaries extending onto (or so appeared ) innermost primaries and part of posterior secondary coverts , so that white formed poorly defined triangle at rear if wings centered on secondaries. Primaries appeared did not have significant black on them. A narrow hint of dusky on trailing edge of primaries. Below, wings white with narrow black trailing edge to primaries. Bouncier flight than COTE, which was seen in close proxmity

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern should have dark secondary bar in first fall, perhaps secondaries might look same darkness as remainder of wing, but COTE should not show white secondaries. Also, the pattern of white bleeding onto rearmost secondary wing-coverts and innermost primaries is unique to Arctic Tern among N Hemisphere terns. Carpal bar wrong for Forster's Tern. Small bill, head projection, legs all c/w Arctic Tern vs other similar terns. More extensive dark on crown c/w Arctic as well
