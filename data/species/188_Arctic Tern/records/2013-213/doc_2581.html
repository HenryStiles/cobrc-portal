Description of the Bird

This was a sterna tern, medium sized with a long tail, the wings above were uniform silvery gray with very small black tips to the primaries as seen fron beneath, but that could not always been seen.  The head a was small with a complete black cap, the bill was tiny and the small head protuded little from the wings compared to other terns.  See photos

Similar Species Discussion

The bird was examined carefully for up to an hour to eliminate the possibilty of Common Tern.  The limited amount of black to the primary tips, the silvery gray upper surface of the wings, the tiny bill and head and deeper wingbeats eliminated Common Tern.
