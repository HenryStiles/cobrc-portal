Description of the Bird

The Arctic Tern was flying over the western part of the lake and diving occasionally.  The acrobatic flight and rapid wingbeat rate indicated that it was a small/medium-sized tern.  Through the scope, we could see that it had a complete black cap and a short bill.  The back and upperside of the wings were mostly a uniform pale gray color, and there was a narrow, white trailing bar on the secondaries.  A review of our photographs revealed that the bill was red, and that there was thin black edging on the wing tips.

Similar Species Discussion

Forster's Terns were also present at the lake, and we were able to contrast the incomplete cap and long bill on them to the complete cap and short bill on the Arctic Tern.  Common Tern was also considered but was eliminated based on the observed short bill, very thin nature of the black edging on the wing tips, and lack of a carpal bar.
