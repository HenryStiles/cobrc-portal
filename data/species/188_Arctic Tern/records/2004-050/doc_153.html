Description of the Bird

White face and forehead. The mid-crown back to the nape was black. Black mask extended from eye across mid-crown to nape. Some black streaking on central crown. Small black bill. While sitting the tail appeared shorter than the wingtips and the upperparts appeared an even pale gray. Very short black legs, which were difficult to see when the bird was standing. Rounded belly almost touching the ground. In flight the underparts were lighter than the mantle and wings, at times appearing almost white. Tail was white, forked and short. Secondaries were white and appeared translucent in direct sunlight. The primaries were translucent except for the very tips, which were black. A faint carpal bar was present. There was no black trailing edge to the wing.
Behaviors: The tern was actively flying, hovering and diving for several minutes at a time. It would often go and stand on a sand spit for short periods and then resume its feeding and hunting behavior.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: 2nd cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern: Common Tern has longer legs. Outer primaries are more extensively dark gray or blackish. The underwing of Common is more silvery-gray, not whitish as in Arctic. Common Tern also has a very prominent carpal bar at the leading edge of the inner wing, and a grayish rump as compared to white rump in Arctic. Forster's Tern: Forster's Tern, at this time of year, has a distinctive black ear-patch that contrasts with its white forehead and crown. Lack of a carpal bar. Grayer tail.
