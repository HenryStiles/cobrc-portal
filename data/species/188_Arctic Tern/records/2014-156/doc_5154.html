Description of the Bird

First photographed overhead in strong late-afternoon light showing short neck, white secondaries and red bill (see 2 photos from 9/14/2014). Photographed again on 9/15/2014 resting at swim beach. Also flew around briefly, landed back at swim beach, then flew off again. Very short red legs, shorter than other tern species present. Very pale gray mantle and primaries, with gray carpal bar very evident, but not as dark as seen on some of the hatch-year Common Terns. Entirely red bill, slightly shorter than that of Common Terns. Inside of bill very bright red when bill held open. Head rounder than other terns present, with partial blackish cap but not as dark as that of Common Terns; white forehead. Extended wings when backlit, showing bright white, translucent secondaries. (trailing edge of inner wing; see photo from 9/15). In flight, smaller than Forster's Terns nearby, and appeared short-necked. Does not show well in photo, but belly had a faint gray wash, raising questions about this bird's age, i.e. a juvenile or HY versus a subadult, or SY.

Similar Species Discussion

Common Tern juv has longer, darker bill, and longer, thicker legs, and miore sloped forehead.

Previous Experience

I have had a few encounters with Arctic Terns in Colorado since 2002, but never in juvenal plumage, except for a subadult seen perched in November at Pueblo Reservoir many years ago may have been this plumage. I have seen a small number (usually 1-5) juvenal Cpmmon Terns in Colorado most years since 1998, but usually they are much more strongly marked with datk gray, This fall was a good year for juvenile Common Tern at Boyd Lake, with at least 8 birds passing through. One or two of these were pale like this Arxtic Tern, but otherwise a good fit for Common Tern, i.e. structurally.
