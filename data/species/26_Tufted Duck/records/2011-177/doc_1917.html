Description of the Bird

Brown Aythya duck with blotchy gray and brown sides. Vent white. Sides separated from vent by dark brown vertical strap. Back solid blackish brown. Chest mottled brown, darker than sides, but not dramatically so. Head showed tuft consistent with adult female (but could 1st fall male show a tuft of this length?). Head dark glossy brown, with no whitish, including around base of bill. Bill blue-gray with black tip and distinct white subterminal band. Bill shape narrower than that of scaup, typical of Tufted Duck
Behaviors: Mostly sleeping, occasionally preening
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The tuft eliminates all other species. Hybrid with scaup or RN Duck still possible in tufted duck. However, tuft length totally consistent with Tufted Duck, thus making hybrid rather unlikely (especially F1). Bill pattern and shape were typical of Tufted Duck, further reducing likelihood of hybrid. Side to back contrast more than that of scaup. Head shape showed no RNDU influence

Previous Experience

Moderately extensive. I've seen about 25 TUDUs in USA and a couple hundred in Eurasia. I've also found about 10 hybrids, males and females, in USA.
