Description of the Bird

This was a medium sized duck, very similar in size to the adjacent American Wigeons. It had an all dark head, with a medium sized tuft coming from the top of the back of the head. The eye was bright yellow. The bill was mostly a blueish gray, with a black tip and nail. The back and tail were a pretty dark shade of black, but not jet black. The breast was dark, but the sides were gray with the shade differing whether it was in direct sunlight or not. When it stretched its wings, it had an extensive wing stripe (like a Greater Scaup) which was bright white and not gray. The legs were dark and did not have any bands.
Behaviors: The bird was in a small patch of open water where it dived quite frequently, identifying it as a diving duck not a dabbling duck. It also stood on the ice some giving an excellent look at the leg color.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Ring-necked Duck: Different bill pattern with no light ring near the end of the bill. Didn't have the vertical white patch near the front on the sides which shows up well even on young Ring-necked Ducks.
Had a reasonable sized tuft, which Ring-necked Ducks do not.

Greater and Lesser Scaup: Had a reasonable sized tuft, which Scaup do not. The back was dark as opposed to gray on Scaup.

Resources Used

Looked at Sibley before arriving, looked at other guides afterward, especially my European guides.

Previous Experience

Have seen one 1st winter Tufted Duck at Walden Ponds in Boulder before. Have seen many thousands in England and Europe, where it is very common. Last seen there in March 2010.
