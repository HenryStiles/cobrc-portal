Description of the Bird

A duck floating on a small pond. The bill was blue gray with a large blackish nail. Just behind the nail was a lighter, whitish area. The head had, in some light, a purplish sheen. The breast was dark brown but mottled with white and lighter than the face. The back feathers were dark brown but some, the tertials had a glossy bluish sheen. The flanks where brown with with whitish edges to most of the feathers giving the bird a mottle look. The individual flank feathers appeared worn in my photos. The bird had a long whitish wing stripe (see photos) that included both the secondaries and the primaries. It had whitish undertail coverts. The prominent feature was a dark tuft at the rear crown of the bird, separating it from all other duck species except hybrids.
Behaviors: Dove, swam slowly, preened, a extended its wings on occasion.
Call: silent
Plumage: 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

The only similar species are hybrid ducks.

Male Pochard x female Tufted Duck has darker yellow eye and overall more like an adult male TUDU and not with the dark brown back of this bird.

Tufted Duck x RNDU, often more similar to RNDU with the white line behind the blackish breast and adult hybrid with whitish sides, but with a tuft.

Tufted Duck male x Pochard female often looks like a Greater Scaup with a tuft.
