Description of the Bird

A predominantly blackish and brownish Aythya duck with a prominent tuft. Bill mostly bluish-gray with a large black tip. Head brownish black with yellow eye. Tuft much more prominent than LESC, swept back to overhang the nape and variable in prominence depending on wind direction and state of relaxation. Flanks brownish gray, with lighter tips on flank feathers. Tail dark. Wings with very wide white stripe visible on upper surface, mostly whitish underneath with dark leading edge. Belly and lower breast whitish. Legs seen to be unbanded with halluxes intact.
Behaviors: Alternated between active feeding, sleeping, and resting on the ice with a little bit of preening now and then. Made a few short flights from one end of the open water (rest of pond frozen) to the other.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult female or immature male

Similar Species Discussion

Eye color and lack of reddish head or pale facial crescent behind bill rules out many Aythya. Tuft long enough to rule out scaup sp. & RNDU. Width of wing stripe also good for TUDU. No sign of hybridism or captivity evident. Only debate seems to be gender, with the preponderance of expert opinion leading towards adult female but some experts still not totally ruling out immature male.
