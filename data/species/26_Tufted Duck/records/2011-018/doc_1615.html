Description of the Bird

Scaup-sized duck with longer feathers forming a distinctive "tuft" at rear of head. The overall coloration appeared brown, with head and back darker brown than the light milk chocolate brown chest and mottled milk chocolate/whitish flanks. On the side profile photo submitted, the head appears to have a slight purplish sheen behind the eye. This would indicate a male; however, I hesitated identifying age/sex as I am unsure if male Tufted Ducks can have white undertail coverts, which is shown on another submitted photo. The bill was bluish with a substantial black tip with a less evident whitish subterminal band. Eyes were bright yellow.

I observed the bird flap several times and noted an extensive white wingbar that reached the 2nd or 3rd outermost primary. The belly was pure white becoming mottled on the lower chest then brown at the neckline. Posteriorly, the white belly faded to the milk chocolate color around the vent. The undertail coverts were white with large, brown mottling.
Behaviors: Occasionally diving for extended periods (~10-20 seconds) in the small waterhole remaining of an otherwise frozen pond. Appeared to attempt a take-off on two occasions while I was there, but quickly settled back into the water without actually taking off.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Obvious tuft rules out all other duck species. All hybrid pairings would show a reduced or absent tuft.

Resources Used

Birds of Europe (1999) after the sighting. I looked at all of the major North American field guides, but they seemed useless in helping to establish sex for anything but adult Tufted Ducks in alternate plumage.

Previous Experience

I've seen two adult male Tufted Ducks on the East Coast and an adult female in California, both many years ago.
