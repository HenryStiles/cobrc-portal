Description of the Bird

The Harris's Hawk was initially perched in a tree about 30 feet off the ground next to Horsetooth road, about 100 feet from my vehicle. I oberved it for about a minute. It flushed from it's perch when additional traffic passed by. I was able to photograph the bird in flight as it past by me and flew off across Topminnow Natural Area. 

I was joined by three other birders and we were able to relocate the HASH while it was perched on a fence post in the middle of the Topminnow Nat. Area. This viewing was at about a .25 mi. distance.

Large dark hawk; black breast, belly, primaries, and secondaries. Rich rufous color to lesser secondary coverts and underwing coverts. White rump/upper tail and with under tail coverts with broad black band on tail with narrow white terminal band. Large bill with yellow cere. Yellow feet.

Similar Species Discussion

Three similar species common in this area:

-Red-tailed Hawk (dark morph)

Eliminated as the HASH had a dark tail with white terminal band and dark underwing primaries, whereas the RTHA(dark morph) has reddish tail and pale underwing primaries.

-Ferruginous Hawk (dark morph)

Eliminated as the HASH had a dark tail with white terminal band and dark underwing primaries, whereas a FEHA (dark morph) has pale underwing primaries and tail.

-Rough-legged Hawk (dark morph)

Eliminated as the HASH had dark underwing primaries, whereas the RLHA (dark morph) underwing primaries are pale in color.
