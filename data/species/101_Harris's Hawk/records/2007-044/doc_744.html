Description of the Bird

A dark hawk, with gray & yellow color on the bill, with big hook, rufous in the wings, white under tail, yellow legs and feet, chololate brown in color overall. See photos.
Behaviors: I was cutting weeds in my yard, when I noticed a dark bird sitting on my phone box on the hillside behind my house. I thought it was probably an American Crow, since all I could see without binoculars was a dark bird. It was around 630pm. The bird flew toward me and landed in pine tree on the north side of my duplex. In flight, I saw white in the tail, and rufous color on the wings, and underparts. So, I quickly realized this was a Harris's Hawk and not an American Crow. The bird flew again to the north and landed on a house. On the way to get my binoculars, scope, and camera, I quickly called Mark Peterson, so could let the word out, while I attempt to get photos. I was able to take a few photos of the bird on top the house. I was able to reach Van Truan, and he and Kristyn Truan were the first people to get to Pueblo West to see the bird. Then Bill Maynard, Mark Peterson, Brad Steger, and Ira Sanders all were able to see it until dark on June 30th. The bird sat in a tree on Harmony Drive for a while, and then flew and sat on top of a house and then went to roost in a willow. On July 1st, the bird was seen through 1:30pm by many birders: Tom McConnell, John Drummond, Lisa Edwards, and David Chartier were the early group. Then Joe Roller, Glenn Walbek, Norm Erthal, Loch Kilpatrick, Alison Hilf all appeared to see it. Then Charlie Lawrence and Cici Lee. The bird was absent for a hour, then came flying back into my yard, just before Andrew Spencer appeared. Then, I re-found the bird up the road, and showed it to Tony Leukering, Larry Semo, and Doug Faulkner. It went back to my yard. Not long after that, Joyce & Connie Takamie saw the bird in my yard, and the last person to see it was Steve Larson. We last saw it between Acorn Drive and Harmony Drive.

The bird stayed in trees and sit on roof tops mostly.
Call: none
