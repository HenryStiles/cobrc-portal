Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized hawk, about the size of a Red-tailed Hawk though it appeared slimmer because of a longer tail and thinner proportions. The bill was dark gray, and the cere and gape were bright yellow, as were the legs. The legs also appeared fairly long, and were unfeathered. The irides were very dark brown, and there was a thin dull yellow orbital ring.

The head and back were mottled dull dark brown with slightly paler brown highlights. The flight feathers were black with some obscure dark brown highlights. The wing coverts were rufous-chestnut color, the the lower parts mottled with the same dark brown with paler brown as the flight feathers. The underparts were also dull dark brown with slightly paler brown highlights, but with some indistinct paler streaks. The feathering at the base of the legs was chestnut colored, and the undertail coverts were white. The tail was black with a broad white tip; the tail was also actively molting, with the old feathers having worn tips, and the new ones fresh. The older feathers also had some indistinct barring. There was also some ongoing molt in the flight feathers, though I didn't note which ones were molting.

In flight the bird showed white uppertail coverts that contrasted with the dark back and tail.
Behaviors: The bird flew with fairly fast, choppy wingbeats. For most of the observation it remained hidden in the foliage of various trees, mostly conifers. It also perched on top of a house for a short amount of time. The bird was somewhat wary, and did not allow too close of an approach.
Call: none heard
Plumage: second cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Other dark-morph Buteos were ruled out by the chestnut wing coverts and feathering at the base of the legs, as well as the contrasting white upper and undertail coverts. The overall shape, with long, unfeathered legs and long tail imparting a slim shape also did not match other Buteos. Accipeters were ruled out by the dark color overall and bulkier shape. Northern Harrier was ruled out by the very different plumage and behavior. Common Black-Hawk was ruled out by the much longer tail, chestnut feathering, browner plumage, and different shape. Zone-tailed Hawk was ruled out by longer tail and legs, chestnut feathering on the wing and legs, longer legs, and white under and uppertail coverts.
