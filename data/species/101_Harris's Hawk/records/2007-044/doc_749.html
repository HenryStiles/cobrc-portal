Description of the Bird

A large buteo approximately the size of a Red-tailed Hawk. Most of the head, upperparts and chest were dark brown, with some light stipling on the chest. The lower belly and undertail coverts were white, as were the uppertail coverts. The lesser and median wing coverts were rufous with thin, dark centers - indicating, along with other features, an immature bird. The tail contained feathers of two ages, with the innermost rectrices, R1, newly molted and still growing - nearly black with broad clean white tip -, and the remaining feathers barred with worn dull white tips. The legs were long and bright yellow. The very large cere was also bright yellow. While perched, the wingtips fell just slightly past the rump. Both legs were seen well and did not have jesses or bands.
Behaviors: First observed perched in a residential yard elm tree, then briefly in flight, before perched again in a residential conifer.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: 2nd year molting

Similar Species Discussion

The rufous shoulder patch rules out all other raptors except Red-shouldered, White-tailed, and Ferruginous hawks. Red-shouldered is ruled out by size and lack of strongly barred tail. White-tailed Hawk is ruled out by lack of white breast or head markings, and wings not extending past tail. Ferruginous Hawk is ruled out by lack of feathering on legs and black/white tail pattern.
