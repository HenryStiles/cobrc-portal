Description of the Bird

First Observation - Dec. 12, 2017. 7:20 - 7:35 a.m. Riverbend Ponds Natural Area.
Good views of perched bird. Perched in willow tree approximately 20 feet off the ground and was perched about 80-100 feet from a nearby road busy with morning commuter traffic. Medium size hawk, about the same height, maybe a bit smaller that a RTHA. Black head, breast, belly, and back, with some reddish coloration to the shoulder. White under tail coverts, dark tail with white terminal band. Yellow legs and yellow cere. During the observation, the perched bird seemed relaxed and continually  scanned the scrub area near its perch.


Second Observation - Dec. 15, 2017. 3:20 - 4:05 p.m. Hageman Recycling Center next to Running Deer/Cottonwood Hollow Natural Area.
Good views of perched bird from about 200 feet away. Buteo sized bird, dark brown belly, breast, head, and back, with rufous tones to the shoulders. Yellow legs. Yellow bill with dark tip, large in comparison to head. White under tail coverts and white terminal band on dark tail. Photo of bird in flight shows enlarged crop indicating recent feeding.

45 minute observation. Flew into cottonwood tree and perched about 50-60 feet above ground. Perched and preened for about 15 minutes (while cars, machinery, and people moved about below it) before moving over to another perch in the same cottonwood grove, where it stayed for the duration of the observation (30 minutes) and was still present when I left. 

Similar Species Discussion

Three similar species common in this area:
-Red-tailed Hawk (dark morph)
Eliminated as the HASH had a dark tail with white terminal band and dark underwing primaries, whereas the RTHA(dark morph) has reddish tail and pale underwing primaries.
-Ferriginous Hawk (dark morph)
Elimiated as the HASH had a dark tail with white terminal band and dark underwing primaries, whereas a FEHA (dark morph) has pale underwing primaries and tail.
-Rough-legged Hawk (dark morph)
Eliminated as the HASH had a long tail with primary wing projections only extending about halfway down the tail, and presence of dark underwing primaries, whereas the RLHA (dark morph) primary wing projection rests nearly at the end of the tail on a perched bird and underwing primaries are pale in color.
