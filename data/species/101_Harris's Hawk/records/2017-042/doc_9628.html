Description of the Bird

Continuing bird. initially seen in flight, flapping and gliding toward me. Reminded me of a Red-shouldered hawk in its m-shaped glide profile. As it came closer I saw the yellow cere and base of bill and once it turned I got good views of the chestnut brown body and white undertail coverts. Saw it about 30 minutes later at the Running Deer N.A. parking lot (no list) where it was perched in a tree about 250 m away, but directly in front of the sun, so my photo is useless. It flew off after a few minutes and I was able to see the white uppertail coverts as it flew east towards the Colorado Welcome Center, before perching in a tree on the south side of the Hageman's site.

Similar Species Discussion

Initially red-tailed, which eliminated by the cupped wings when gliding, followed by the yellow cere and then the all chestnut-brown body with white undertail coverts. White undertail coverts eliminate RTHA, brown color eliminates ZTHA, while it was too small for an immature GOEA or dark morph FEHA.
