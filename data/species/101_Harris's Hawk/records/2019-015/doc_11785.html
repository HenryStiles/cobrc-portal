Description of the Bird

Large hawk about the size of a Red-tail. Perched on small shrub. Overall brown bird (belly, throat, head, and back), coverts were reddish/rusty brown. White undertail coverts, black tail with white terminal band. Large hooked bill, bicolored. Tip blueish white, base yellow. Yellowish legs.

The bird remained perched for the duration of the observation. No calls given.

Similar Species Discussion

Three similar species common in this area:
-Red-tailed Hawk (dark morph)
Eliminated as the HASH had a dark tail with white terminal band and dark underwing primaries, whereas the RTHA(dark morph) has reddish tail and pale underwing primaries.
-Ferriginous Hawk (dark morph)
Elimiated as the HASH had a dark tail with white terminal band and dark underwing primaries, whereas a FEHA (dark morph) has pale underwing primaries and tail.
-Rough-legged Hawk (dark morph)
Eliminated as the HASH had a long tail with primary wing projections only extending about halfway down the tail, and presence of dark underwing primaries, whereas the RLHA (dark morph) primary wing projection rests nearly at the end of the tail on a perched bird and underwing primaries are pale in color.
