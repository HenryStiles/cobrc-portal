Description of the Bird

Bird was a small flycatcher with an overall small size and primary extension indicating it was in the genus Empidonax.  The head appeared big in proportion to the rest of the body.  The overall color was yellow-green above and pale yellow below.  It had a very inconspicuous eyering.  The lower mandible was bright yellow with a somewhat dark tip.  Tail appeared to be short and of relatively even width throughout.  Wingbars prominent and tan.  Throat light yellow.  See photos attached to this report for further details. 

At the time of this observation the bird was sitting quietly, as if digesting/ resting after an active foraging period, on the outside of a small thicket at a height of 5 feet off the ground.  It did not flick its wings that I remember or make any calls.  It is quite possible this same individual was present both the day before and the day after this observation.  The previous day I had an empid that just went as 'unidentified' which in retrospect fit this bird's description.  The next day I either saw this same bird or a second individual  Yellow-bellied Flycatcher 100 meters or so north of this report's location (this report was due east of the LCC Library, the sighting on the 16th was near the north end of the LCC Woods). 

Similar Species Discussion

Because of the inconspicuous eyering, I tried to make this bird a "Traill's" Flycatcher.  But the smallish size, short tail, strongly yellowish upperparts, pale yellow throat, all in combination, just didn't fit Willow or Alder.  Bird was structurally somewhat like Least but head size and throat didn't seem to fit for that species.  All yellow lower mandible seemed to eliminate Dusky or Hammond's.  Primary extension not long enough for Hammond's or pewees.  Lack of extensive white eyering, teardrop shape, overall structure, head shape, did not work for Cordilleran.  Coloration not right for Buff-bellied.

Previous Experience

I have seen several Yellow-bellied Flycatchers in the East, most noteably one on Mt. Washington in 2006 which was observed and photographed extensively.  I have seen one Yellow-bellied Flycatcher at Flagler SWA a year ago, which was documented by Steve Mlodinow and accepted by the CO RC.  I have extensive experience with all the usual Colorado empids, and have seen 5 or Buff-bellied Flycatchers in AZ many years ago.
