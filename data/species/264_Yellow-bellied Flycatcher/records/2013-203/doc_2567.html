Description of the Bird

I was pishing in a small copse of trees when an Empid flew in and landed, facing nearly straight away, about 15 or so feet from me at just below eye level. The bird's back, nape, crown were emerald green and the bird flicked its tail and wings frequently. The primary extension was medium, shorter than Hammond's Fly and towards long end for Least Fly. The bird chased an insect (or so I presume) and landed again facing me, and I could see that it was washed in moderate yellow from throat to vent with some olive on sides of chest. These colors remained consistent whenever the bird was in reasonable light. The eyering was prominent, tinged yellow, and basically round, with a narrower portion at the upper forward portion of the eye, much as is seen in many Least Flycatchers. No paler loral spot. The green from the crown came down through the auriculars, so that the auriculars were similarly bright green, and the line of demarcation between yellow throat and green auriculars was moderate. The bill was entirely pink-orange below, without any dusky at tip. The shape was fairly broad at base, and bill was fairly short, so that bill shape resembled that of Least Flycatcher. The head was proportionately large and crown rounded (no crested appearance), tail short and narrow, again giving a Least Fly-like structural jizz. With wingbars and tertial edges were white, contrasting brightly against dark feather centers. The primary length was judged from side view, bill shape from when bird was directly overhead. 

The bird's overall size was roughly that of a Least Flycatcher and similar (or a tad bulkier) than nearby Hammond's Fly (which had distinctly narrower bill).

Similar Species Discussion

"Western" Flycatcher has distinctly larger bill, peaked crown, almond-shaped eyering, larger/longer tail; YBFL with more yellow and less olive on underparts than Western Flys, and YBFL with more distinct auricular/throat contrast and lacks any hint of pale collar extending up behind auriculars as compared with Western Flys

Least Flycatcher no yellow on throat or eyering. Even brightest bird, duller on back, particularly crown. 

Hammond's Flycatcherh as gray throat, peaked grown, smaller/narrower bill.
