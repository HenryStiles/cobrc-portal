Description of the Bird

Flycatcher: bright greenish yellow; yellowish throughout the chest as well as head and back with head and back more of a darkish yellow-green; round head; small bill (with darker upper mandible); bright white wing-bars on black wings with top one one-half the length of the bottom one; wings showing white edges on the secondaries; round buffy-white eye-ring; white outside edges on the tail, seemingly short primary projections.
Behaviors: The bird was first noticed outside my office window flycatching in a barren honey-locust tree. It would perch very still and the swoop out and return to another perch. This was like a wood-pewee, but lighter and more fluttery. The bird was very close to my window and flew out of view after a couple of forrays. When I had the opportunity to go outside, I stepped onto a balcony at the top of a second story staircase, and was amazed to see the bird perched right in front of me on the rail. It remained in this position for 30 to 60 seconds (actually it was checking me out just as I was it!)
Finally it flew down to the ground and then off.
Call: NA
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

By behavior, size, shape and general coloration the bird was clearly a flycatcher, probably an empidonax. The bright yellow chest and belly ruled out all empids including Buff-breasted (as well as Tyrannulet) but "western" and Yellow-bellied.

The bird had a distinctly rounded head as well as bright white wing-bars and an eye ring that was clearly not as white as the wing-bars. In addition the eye-ring had a fairly round, not tear-dropped shape. The bird also had distinct sharp white edging on the folded wings, especially on the edges of the tertials. These characteristics cause to to rule out both "Western" Flycatchers in favor of Yellow-bellied.
