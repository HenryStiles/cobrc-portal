Description of the Bird

An empidonax flycatcher based on size, olive and yellow color, wingbars and tail flicking.  The bird was sulphur yellow below, including throat and malar, with dark gray wings with buffy wingbars.  The bird has a rounded head and near complete, round, yellowish eyering.  The primary projection was moderte to long.  The tail was dark and somewhat short.  The bird was colored much like a Cordilleran Flycatcher but structurally was more similar to a Least Flycatcher, but longer winged.  The pale edges of the secondaries did not extend all the way to the lower wingbar (pale tips of greater coverts).  The wing was dark, but not as black as an adult of this species and the wingbars were buffy, suggesting an immature bird.  See photos.

Similar Species Discussion

The only empids as yellow as this bird are Cordilleran and Pacific Slope flycatcher.  Since these two species are identical in the field, possibly conspecific, the differences described will apply to both.  The main difference was in structure.  The YBFL had a round head, longer wings and shorter tail than Western Flycatcher, structurally similar to Least Flycatcher.  Also, it's eyering was round, not almond shaped.  These differences are subtle but noticable in the field and in photos.

Previous Experience

I have only seen / photgraphed YBFL once in Michigan an adult bird on breeding territory.  COFLs however breed behind my house and are plentiful during migration.  I have scrutenized many photos of young COFLs taken on the Front Range.
