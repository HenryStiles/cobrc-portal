Description of the Bird

When I saw the bird it was being held by a bird bander.  I noticed these features as the bird was measured.  Eyering yellow and complete with hint of teardrop. Base of bill broad. Leg color gray.  Head greenish and not peaked on the crown. Wingbars yellowish. From photos, but not in the field, and after reading the article titled: "Simple technique for identifying Yellow-bellied Flycatchers from Cordilleran and Pacific-slope flycatchers" by Mathew J. Baumann et al. the length of the buffy edges of the secondary feathers on the folded wing are noticeably shorter than they would be in COFL and PSFL. Wing panel very dark showing the longish primary extension.  Measurements showed the tail to be short and shorter than either COFL or PSFL.  A number of measurements used to separate YBFL from COFL and PSFL are included in the photos. 

Similar Species Discussion

COFL and WEFL eliminated by measurements and characteristics noted in last box.
