Description of the Bird

The Empid flushed from within a tree, alighting on a tree with less foliage, in the open, at about eye level. It sat there for about 30 seconds, mostly facing away, before flying to a taller tree that was almost denuded of branches. The bird landed facing away, but quickly shifted so that it was facing me. The upperparts were entirely a bright green (think Townsend's Warbler, Blue-headed Vireo), greener than that of any Empid we'd seen earlier in the trip, including Alder Flycatchers (I'd not seen the upperparts of the Cordilleran a couple days before). The underparts were entirely a pale yellow, including the throat. This yellow was remarkably uniform, except for a hint of olive (rather limited in extent) at sides of chest. Both underparts and upperparts were seen in varying light conditions, and the colors held true, inculding the yellow on throat. The green of the crown extended down thru the auriculars to meet the yellow throat with a moderately sharp demarcation. The eyering was white, or certainly appeared so, and round... not the almond shape of a "Western" Flycatcher nor the oblong and (usually) interrupted shape of a Least. The eyering was bold, as in LEFL but not ALFL. The wingbars were prominent, washed in yellow, and contrasted sharply against the black portions of the wing coverts. The tertials were black with contrasting white edges. The secondaries were narrowly edges in yellow-green. The primary extension was shortish, at the long end of the range found in LEFL. The bill was shaped similar to that of a LEFL, but a bit longer. The overall shape of the bird (large headed, short tailed) was very much like a LEFL as was the head shape. 
    Steve Larson came up towards the end of my observation, and watched the bird for 30 seconds to a minute. He also saw the completely yellow underparts and rather bright green upperparts. He agreed with the ID

Similar Species Discussion

No other Empid, except the Western Flys, should show yellow on the throat. The eyering, bill length, and primary extension were all incorrect for Western Fly. Acadian Fly can also show a bright green back, but should have a much larger bill, much longer primaries and a white throat. Alder Flycatcher should be white throated, not as bright green on back, narrower eyering. Least Flycatcher should not have yellow throat, should have different shaped eyering, lack yellow-green edging to secondaries.
