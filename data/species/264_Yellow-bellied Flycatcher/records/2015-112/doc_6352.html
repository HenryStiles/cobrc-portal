Description of the Bird

Empid sized and overall appearance of Empid. Bright green upper parts. Yellow from throat through undertail coverts, brightness varying somewhat with lighting, but always fully yellow and brightest yellow on throat. Primary extension shortish. Eyering narrow (broader than ALFL), round, crisp, bright, tinged slightly yellow. Wing bars bright, varying from pale yellowish-green to yellow-buff depending on lighting. Tertials and secondary black edged in bright white (tertials) or yellow-green (secondaries). Head rounded, not crested. Intermittently flicked wings and tail together. Overall aspect not dissimilar to a brightly colored Least Flycatcher with more rounded head. Bill orange-pink below without any dark. Shape similar to that of Least, but a bit longer. Note that in photos shot from behind, the primaries look rather long. This was not the appearance in the field. I think that the angle of those shots, looking straight up drooping primaries, allows for more primaries to be visible, and also makes the primary extension look far longer than it did in the field. In the field, the primary extension seemed similar to that of Least. 

Similar Species Discussion

Only Western Flycatchers should have yellow on throat. They have a differently shaped eyering, longer/larger bill, duller (less-contrasting) edges to tertials, crested head shape, longer/larger tail, and are duller green above. 
Acadian Flycatchers should have whitish throat, have much larger bill and longer primaries.
Least Flycatchers should not be so green above, have a different shaped eyering, lack yellow-green edges to secondaries, whitish throat
Hammond's Fly smaller bill, gray head and throat
