Description of the Bird

A yellow-green empidonax, perched high in the cottonwoods.  Looking up I noticed deep yellow throat, pale, wide lower mandible and yellow overall cast to head and underside.  The wings were dark with off-white wingbars.  The secondary edges were white and stopped well short of the lower wingbar.  The primary projection was medium. A complete white to off-white, circular eyering was evident.  The head was round and the bird looked plump, like a Least Flycatcher.  The bird was observed for several minutes from 15 - 30 feet away by qualified birders who all agreed on ID after careful study and photos.

Similar Species Discussion

The deep yellow throat and yellow overall cast is only seen in Western Flycatchers and Yellow-bellied Flycatcher.  The narrow, round eyering, limited-length, pale, short secondary edges, dark wings, longer primary projection, round head and paler belly helped eliminate the Western Flycatchers.
