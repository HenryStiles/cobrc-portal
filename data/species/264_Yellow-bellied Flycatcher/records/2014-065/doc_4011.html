Description of the Bird

Bird viewed at close range with sun to back from eye level to waist level (that would be my eye/waist level, not the bird's). Shape and action much like a Least Flycatcher. Back striking green in sun, easily visible in bright shade. Crown and auriculars similar in coloration. Eyering round, unbroken, buff-tinged, thicker than that of ALFL, similar to that of Least. No pale in lores. Throat/auricular border sharp. Underparts were yellow, excepting pale greenish smudged sides to chest, with smudging covering about 2/3 breadth of chest. Bright pale yellow on undertail coverts up onto lower belly. Paler towards chest and on throat, where yellow would be deemed a wash. Wing blackish with sharply contrasting white edges to tertials and secondaries. Wingbars bold and washed yellow-buff. Primary projection towards long-end for Least. Again, bird had rather large headed appearance, with narrowish tail of LEFL and was very active, often flicking wings and tail. Crown flattish, as in Least. Bill shaped similar to LEFL, but a tad larger with an all orangish mandible and blackish maxilla. The chee-lick call of YBFL (matching up nicely with recordings on hand) was heard a 3-4 times. Not as dry and brief as similar che-bek of Least

Similar Species Discussion

Acadian: Lacks green on sides of chest, has much longer primary extension, has white throat
"Western": "Almond" shaped eyering, crested appearance, darker chest, larger bill, less abrupt contrast between auriculars and throat, longer primary extension
Least: Duller back, now yellow on throat, white wing bars
Call eliminated all of above as well
