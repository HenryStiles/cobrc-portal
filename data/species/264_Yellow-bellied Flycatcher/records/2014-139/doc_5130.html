Description of the Bird

Empid with basic shape/structure reminiscent of that of Least Flycatcher. Green above. Yellow beneath, excepting modest olive sides to chest. Round eyering, white or near white. Bill similar to that of LEFL, but a tad larger. Wingbars washed yellow. Secondaries edged bright yellow green. Tertials edged bright white. Much contrast between edges of flight feathers and blackish centers. 

Similar Species Discussion

Western Flycatcher- Peaked crown, longer primaries, larger bill, almond shaped eyering
Hammond's Flycatcher - gray or white throat, smaller bill, longer primaries
Least Flycatcher - gray or white throat, Typically as small break at superior anterior portion of eyering. Not as bright yellow on belly. Lacks yellow-green in secondaries. 
Acadian - longer primaries, larger bill, typically not yellow on throat
