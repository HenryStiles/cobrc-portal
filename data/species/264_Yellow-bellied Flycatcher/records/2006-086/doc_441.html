Description of the Bird

see photos. One deceptive artifact of the pics are that the eye-ring looks more almond-shaped than it was in life after being released. When seen from a distance in the scope the eye-ring was a touch elongated but was even around the eye.
Behaviors: Once released the bird flew up into cottonwoods and disappeared up high. Later in the afternoon it was refound foraging low in Russian Olives perching from 2-20 feet up and feeding often.
Call: upslurred saweeep or sweeep
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The vocalizations I heard in the afternoon eliminate all other species of empid except perhaps Pacific-slope Flycatcher. PSFL and COFL were eliminated by wing measurements. See RMBO data
