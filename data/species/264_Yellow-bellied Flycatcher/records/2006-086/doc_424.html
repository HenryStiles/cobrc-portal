Description of the Bird

This bird was viewed in the hand for about 5 minutes and perched in a tree after realease for about 3 minutes and then observed foraging high in a plains cottonwood for about 1 minute

This report is strongly influenced by the measurements made by Brian Gibbons at the RMBO CBR banding station.

The first thing I noticed when the bird was removed from the bag, was the bright yellow coloration.

The brightest areas were the throat and belly being lemon yellow, separated by an olive green breast band. The head was ROUNDED, with a dark olive green head and nape. (It did not show the posterior peak of "Western Flycatcher". The eyering was yellowish, round with a teardrop at the posterior end (similar to "Western Flycatcher but narrower and yellower...I observed two Cordilleran Flycatcher in the week following the observation and the eyering is much broader and lighter in the CO COFLs. The top of the eyering of the report bird was the same width as the bottom, unlike the compressed top of the eyering in COFL.

The "lower" mandible was pink, not orange as I see in COFLs in CO.

The wings were dark brown or blackish with strongly contrasting whitish wingbars. The edgings on the wing coverts were yellowish.

The rump was light olive green compared to the brownish green seen in COFL in CO.

The tail was a bit longer than I thought it should be but see Brian's measurements as it fits the range for YBFL.

The wings showed a moderate primary projection, see photos, but see also Brain's meaurements.
Behaviors: When I saw the bird it was mostly in the hand, but it did fly up to a 20 ft. tall tree limb and perched and then moved up to a 30 ft tall tree limb and perched before resuming feeding. While perched the bird did have its crest erect and the posterior part of its head appeared crested, but this was not the case while the bird was in the hand. Eventually, after 2-3 mintues the bird moved to the top of a tall plains cottonwood and foraged on the tops of leaves in the sunlight, mostly hover gleaning. While perched the bird was observed at 30x through a Lecia APO spotting scope and while foragging, the bird was observed through 10x42 Leica binoculars.

I have extremely limited experience with this species, however I made a point to look at COFLs in CO and compare to the YBFL at CBR. The YBFL was much yellower, darker olive green on its dorsal surface, with a narrower, more yellow, rounder at the top eyering. The rump was noticeably greener than that of the COFLs.
Call: not heard but heard later by Brian Gibbons giving its winter grounds/migration call "pyew" a descending call
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Acadian has sickle-shaped long wings and contrasting white chin and pale malar that contrast with a white throat unlike this bird

"Western Flycatcher" has brownish green rump, more orangy "lower" mandible compared to pinkish, wider lighter eye-ring compressed at the top, and longer tail and shorter wings
