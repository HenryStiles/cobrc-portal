Description of the Bird

Empidonax flycatcher with clear lemon-yellow throat and upper chest fading into pale ochre-yellow belly and vent with some olive wash at sides of lower chest. Overall structure rather reminiscent of Least Flycatcher, with largish flattish head, shortish tail, moderate primary extension. The bill was perhaps a bit larger than that of a Least and entirely orangish beneath. Base color of wings blackish with contrasting white tertial and secondary edges and white wingbars. Back was uniformly deep emerald green through crown and into auriculars, including nape, giving almost a helmeted appearance different from other Empids, which usually have some gray wash on nape, head or auriculars. Green auriculars thus sharply cut off from lemon yellow throat. Eyering round and washed yellow. Viewed at close range for about 45 seconds by me, longer by Dave
Behaviors: The bird had the habit of repeatedly flicking its wings and tail.
Call: not heard
Plumage: fall adult

Similar Species Discussion

"Western Flycatchers" have differently shaped eyerings, longer bills (to my eye), longer tails, tend to hold their head feathers in a crested position, and have some wash of brown or gray onto sides of nape. Back tends to be washed with some brownish. No other Empid should have a yellow throat. If an aberrant Least were to have a yellow throat, it would be unlikely that its back would be so green, the nape unwashed in gray, and that its eyering would be shaped as this bird's was.
