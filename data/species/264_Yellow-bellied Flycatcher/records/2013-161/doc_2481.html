Description of the Bird

Empid with bright green back and yellow underparts shaped somewhat like a Least Flycatcher (large head without crest, relatively small tail). Yellow on underparts bright but not heavily saturated, with some olive on sides of chest. Yellow underparts included throat. Eyering round (ie, not almond shaped) with yellow wash. Bill not well noted, but exceptionally large. Wings blackish with highly contrasting white wingbars and edges to tertials. Also, contrastingly pale edges to secondaries, color not noted. Primary projection medium to longish.

When bird first noted, silent. It sang three times then flew off in pursuit of some bug. When I could not find it, I played tape, and it came in immediately, landing within 10 feet. As soon as I moved, it departed and could not be lured back in.
Behavior: Flicked wings and tail actively
Call: dju-lick. Slightly buzzy. Not dissimilar to that of Least, but buzzier and duration of song longer.
Plumage: breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Cordilleran/PS Flycatcher have differently shaped head and larger bill, larger tail. Also, in my experience, "Western" Flycatchers not as green on back and have a bit of a hind border to auriculars which this bird lacked. Typically, Western Flys not as contrasting on wings. Finally, song was distinctive. No other Empid all yellow beneath (in USA/Canada).
