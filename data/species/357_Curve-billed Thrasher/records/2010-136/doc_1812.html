Description of the Bird

The bird was about 11 inches with black curved bill, yellowish-orange eye, irregular spotting on dirty white breast, buffy under tail, dark tail on top and dark underside and white toward tip. Gray bird with very small hint of wingbar if one at all. Tail and breast suggest race oberholseri but wingbars suggest palmeri.
Behaviors: Bird would run with tail up at times into dense thicket. I watched the bird feed at the Red Rock's feeders eating sunflower and millet for almost 7 months. He would use his bill in a sweeping motion to search and dig for seed.
Call: several chatters and whistles also a wit-wheet call
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Bendire's Thrasher is smaller with shorter bill and straight mandible, triangular spots on chest. At times the head peaks on top.
