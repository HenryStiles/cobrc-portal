Description of the Bird

First appearance 10/8/2017.  Bird appeared hungry and jumpy.  Was hitting the bird feeders, but was very nervous.  I could barely move near the kitchen window or it would fly.  Flew and hid in the sumac or salt bush shrubs, then sneak back to the feeders.  No calls given.

Bird returned to feeders on 10/14/2017 approx. noon and was seen on and off at the feeders until mid-afternoon.  

Description:  Slight curved bill; grey-green in color;  white throat with darker edge; long tail with white ends; orange eye; light grey breast (not obviously spotted); dark/grey legs.  Slightly larger than a red-winged blackbird.


Thrasher has been a regular visitor to feeders through early December.

Similar Species Discussion

Sage thrasher:  this bird's bill too long and curved compared to sage thrasher; breast was not spotted.

Resources Used

iBird Pro application for iPhone; also reviewed Sibley Guide to Birds, Sibley Field Guide to Birds Western North America, and National Geographic Birds of North America books to double check range maps and descriptions.

Shared photo and description with West Slope Bird Network to get opinion from other birders to verify identification.

Previous Experience

Regularly see sage thrashers in our general area.  Had seen curve-billed thrasher in Sierra Vista, AZ in 2003 at a birding training.
