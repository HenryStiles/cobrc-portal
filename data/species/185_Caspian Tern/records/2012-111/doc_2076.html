Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski 8.5 X 42 and 10X 42, 500 mm lens
Distance:  50-75 ft.
Light:  This morning it was quite foggy and misty. Very overcast. Even in those conditions the size, color and shape of the bird were very apparent. This afternoon it was sunny. This afternoon, especially when the bird was at the east end of the lake, flying towards us, the light was very good.

Description of the Bird

Very large tern, larger that Ring-billed Gull in vicinity. More than twice as large as Black Tern and Forster's Tern, both of which were flying in the vicinity. All black cap, stout dark red bill with slightly dusky tip. Black legs. In flight wingbeats were shallow, with dark under the primaries.
Behaviors: Bird was at first resting on a small island in the center of the lake. Later it flew to the opposite shore. Would fly quite high up, with short, stiff wing beats.
Call: Harsh, deep almost scream
Plumage: Adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Also seen in the area were much smaller Forster's Tern and Black Tern also seen with Ring-billed Gull. Eliminated Elegant and Royal tern due to size and shape of the bill. Bill was too heavy, too red and had a darker tip, which would be lacking in the Elegant and Royal. This bird showed showed no crest. Also Elegant and Royal would be extremely unlikely. Eliminated from other "red-billed" terns due to size and leg color. This bird had black legs. Arctic, Common and Forster's have red legs.

Resources Used
