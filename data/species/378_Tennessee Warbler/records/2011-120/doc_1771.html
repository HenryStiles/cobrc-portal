Description of the Bird

The bird had a bright yellow supercilium, throat and chest. The eye line was dark and very prominent. Belly and undertail coverts were white. The upperparts, nape and crown color were green. The bird had faint wing bars. The underparts were without streaking. The bill was very pointed and appeared relatively long. The tail was noticeably short with the very little tail extension beyond the undertail coverts. The size of the bird is on the small end of the scale for warblers due to the short tail. I was judging the size to be slightly smaller than an Orange-crowned Warbler (less than 5 inches in length).
Behaviors: The bird was probing into clusters of leaves on the box elder tree. It easily went out of sight behind leaves. The bird frequently hung upside to forage. The bird was very active.
Call: No vocalizations were heard.
Plumage: First winter

Similar Species Discussion

The Tennessee Warbler had white undertail coverts. An Orange-crowned Warbler would have had yellow undertail coverts and would have had diffuse streaking on the belly. A Warbling Vireo also has a prominent supercilium. However, the bill on the vireo would be thick and the tip of the culmen would curve. The Tennessee Warbler's bill was relatively long and pointed.
