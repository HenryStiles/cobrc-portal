Description of the Bird

I found this bird in November, and since it stayed until December 1, 2013, I am submitting the sighting to the CBRC, for a first winter sighting for Colorado.  The warbler was smaller than Yellow-rumped Warblers, a little bigger than Ruby-crowned Kinglets.  These photos all taken on December 1st, should be good enough to tell it is a Tennessee Warbler.  White undertail coverts, short tail, sharp pointy bill, no wing bars, green back, yellow throat, mostly white belly, dark line through each eye, all make this bird a Tennessee Warbler.

Similar Species Discussion

White under tail elimnates Orange-crowned, which has yellow undertail.  Everything else, elimnates everything else.  See photos.
