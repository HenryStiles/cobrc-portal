Description of the Bird

I observed the bird as it was standing on the shore of a lake, with no other birds nearby.

It appeared to be a medium-sized gull (my size estimate was uncertain, with no other birds for comparison).

It had a completely gray (not black) hood, with no white. The hood came down only to the middle of the nape.

They eye was very inconspicuous (a dark eye on a dark head).

The bill was black, with a yellow tip (outer third of the bill).

The underparts were a clean white.

The back was a clean gray, about the shade of the back of an adult Ring-billed Gull.

The wing tips (seen when folded) were blackish, with three tiny white spots visible.

On the shaded (i.e. in shadow) side of the head, there appeared to be a narrow black line between the gray hood and the white neck. This feature was not apparent on the sunlit side.
Behaviors: It was standing on the shoreline of a lake during my entire observation.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult breeding plumage

Similar Species Discussion

This was a very easy gull to identify. The other possibilities are the other species of small gulls with dark hoods in breeding plumage:
Franklin's
Laughing
Bonaparte's
Little
Black-headed

The complete lack of eye crescents rules out all except Little Gull. The dark bill with a yellow tip, and the narrow black band at the bottom of the hood, narrow the identification to Sabine's Gull. (It was a real treat to observe this bird at close range through a scope!)

It was only with the publication of this record in the 38th Report of the CBRC that I realized the sighting was noteworthy. My observation predates the other one by five days.
