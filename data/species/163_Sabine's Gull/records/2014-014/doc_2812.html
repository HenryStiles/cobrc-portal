Description of the Bird

A Sabine's Gull in typical juvenal plumage, it was a small gull with long wings, the bird's upperparts feathers from crown through lower back were a grayish-brown with contrasting pale fringes and contrasted with white forehead and rump.  The underparts were white, except for grayish-brown sides/flanks.  The eyes, bill, and legs were dark/blackish.  The open upperwing had a distinctive tri-colored pattern of black outer pp and pp covs, white inner pp and pp covs and secondaries and greater secondary covs, and grayish-brown median and lesser secondary covs.   The tail was white and notched, with a black band that was terminal on the outer rectrices and subterminal on the inner rectrices.

Similar Species Discussion

No other species are particularly similar; Black-legged Kittiwake and all other smaller gulls with at least a vague M pattern on upperwing lack this bird's grayish-brown upperparts and sides and the distinctive tri-colored upperwing.

I document this individual because the record provides one of few acceptable records in Colorado from west of the eastern foothill edge, it was one of the latest individuals ever recorded in the state, and it accounted for about the highest-elevation record of the species in the state.
