Description of the Bird

Associating with 3 Pectoral Sandpipers.  Similar size to PESA, but more plump and with shorter bill.  Yellowish legs.  Rufous crown.  Thin white eye ring.  Breast washed with peach color.  Small dark spots on breast; larger, V-shaped marks on flanks.  No calls heard.

Similar Species Discussion

Pectoral Sandpiper is most similar.  Plumage characteristics are particularly helpful for eliminating Pectoral Sandpiper and supporting Sharp-tailed Sandpiper:  rufous crown, white eye ring, spotting not confined to breast band but instead extending to flanks and including V-shaped marks.
