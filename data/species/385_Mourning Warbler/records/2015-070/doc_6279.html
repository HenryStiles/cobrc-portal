Description of the Bird

2nd year male based on Pyle.  Sang a Mourning Warbler song (but not the galloping song heard in TV commercials) and gave a loud MOWA one-note call. Bird was well-studied in the hand.  The few white eye feathers were a concern with the slight possibility of a MOWA x MGWA.  Pyle does not show any measurements for undertail coverts as of value for separating MOWA vs. MGWA.  Greenish backed with gray hood and dark eye with a few white feathers below both the left eye and the bird's right eye. Dark bib.  Black lore, pink legs and feet.  Based on call note and song I would call it a Mourning Warbler.  When evaluating the entire population of both species hybrids would be very rare in nature.  Mourning Warbler is already rare enough in CO. 

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler is similar but with two rows of white eye arcs on top and below the eye.  All of the books I consulted say MGWA always with strong white eye arcs.  CONW has very long undertail coverts that almost reach the tail, unlike this bird and it has a complete white eyering. 

Resources Used

I looked in Pyle and in Crossley guide, new Big Sibley, Stokes Birds, Garrett and Dunn Warbler guide and searched for photos of hybrids online and for variation in Mourning Warbler photos on the internet.

Previous Experience

I have only seen a few MOWAs in CO but I did see a few in North Dakota.  Very familiar with MGWA.
