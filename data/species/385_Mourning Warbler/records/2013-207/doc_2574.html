Description of the Bird

This bird was found the previous day by Nick Komar in his backyard. I was told this bird was also photographed by Brad Biggerstaff the previous day, and saw either his or Nick's picture on Cobirds before coming to look for the bird.

Immature. Eye-ring crescents not as bold as MacGillivray's (conveniently nearby for comparison). Bright yellow belly, broken breastband marks clearly viewed, yellow throat also clearly viewed multiple times, hint of hood. Fairly secretive. We followed this bird along the bank of the canal (it never strayed from the canal), getting views as it popped up or out of bushes or grasses. At one point the bird came out into clear view on a branch for approximately ten seconds for a perfect view. Photos were taken by Nick Komar and Austin Hess while I was there (Austin had a good shot that I believe will clearly identify the bird as an immature Mourning Warbler, hopefully he'll also file a report with this photo). Occasional call, but not as loud or nearly as often as the MacGillivray's seen directly behind Nick Komar's house along the same canal.

Similar Species Discussion


MacGillivray's Warbler was considered - eliminated by the yellow throat and less bold eye-ring crescents. The broken breastband marks only matched the description for an immature Mourning Warbler.
