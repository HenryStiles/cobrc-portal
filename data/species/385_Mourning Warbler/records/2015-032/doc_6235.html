Description of the Bird

Jason Beason plucked the bird from a mist net during regular operation of the Barr Lake banding project run by the Rocky Mountain Bird Observatory.  It was obviously a Mourning or a MacGillivray's, the yellow throat and thin, nearly complete eye rings ruling out the latter species.

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler ruled out by plumage characters and measurements (though I do not have those measurements); Common Yellowthroat ruled out by the split eye rings; the thin, olive necklace; the extensively yellow underparts; and the presence of rictal bristles.

Resources Used

Pyle (1997)

Previous Experience

I have seen >75 Mournings and >100 MacGillivray's in the field and have captured/banded >15 of the former and >50 of the latter.
