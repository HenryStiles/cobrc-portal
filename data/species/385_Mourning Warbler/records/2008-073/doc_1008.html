Description of the Bird

An chunky-looking oporonis with a thin, nearly complete eyering. Yellow underparts, with olive green back. A very pale grayish hood that was incomplete in front, allowing the breast and throat to remain pale yellow.
Behaviors: foraged low in a Lilac bush
Call: none
Plumage: first fall or spring

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler - this species shows thick, short, blunt eye arcs above and below the eye, and has a grayish breast and throat even in fall plumage

Connecticut Warbler - is paler, larger with a complete eyering, only occasionaly incomplete at rear. This bird also walks rather than hops.
