Description of the Bird

Warbler with olive upperparts and olive hood washed in gray. Hood broken on mid chest by yellow that penetrated into throat, which was largely moderate yellow in color, nearly as bright as belly. Underparts o/w bright yellow with some olive on sides. Bill similar to that of MacG (in other words, not like OCWA, for instance) in size and color. Narrow, white, nearly complete eyering with narrow break anteriorly and posteriorly. Eyering much thinner and more extensive than that of MacG. Weak yellowish supraloral smudge. Back greenish. Undertail coverts longer than those of MacG (bird was facing away with tail cocked).
Behaviors: Moved about slowly in undergrowth of Russian Olive thicket. Initially, responded very well to pishing, popping into open, giving stunning frontal view. Then skulked back into cover, cocking tail, giving nice look at long undertail coverts.
Call: Jit or Djit, an excellent match for recording played immediately after hearing bird -- poor match for call of MacG Warbler
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Bill shape, long undertail coverts eliminate former members of what was "Vermivora." MacG Warbler eliminated by eyering extent and shape, yellow throat, longer undertail coverts, yellow supraloral mark, and of course, call note.
