Description of the Bird

This is a 2nd year male as per a bird bander. Yellow undertail coverts (photo) and length longer than Mac and much shorter than Connecticut. Eye arcs with a few white feathers both above and below dark eye which fits with 2nd year male as per Pyle vol. 1.  Olive back and olive patch on gray head. Black chest patch. Pinkish legs and feet. Dark gray lore and gray throat. Called on occasion and sounded like a "chit" note. 

Similar Species Discussion

The only concern was MOWA x MGWA because of the small amount of white in the eye arc area. I asked two bird banders if this was typical of 2nd year MOWAs and they both said yes.  

Resources Used

I looked through all the photos of MOWA on ebird. 

Previous Experience

A long time ago I worked as a backcountry ranger on Isle Royale National Park in Lake Superior where they breed. Saw a few there but actually I have seen more at Chico Basin Ranch where they now seem to be annual with at least three there in 2019 including an adult female. Overall not a lot of experience and non with hybrid MOWA x MGWA
