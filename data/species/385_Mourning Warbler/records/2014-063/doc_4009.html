Description of the Bird

Seen at 30 feet or so for nearly a minute total during two observational periods. Hooded "Oporornis" with olive back. Lemon yellow underparts except head and chest. Very limited and modest olive wash on sides. Head and chest gray forming hood with black chest patch. Lores modestly darker gray than rest of head. Extremely thin white eyering which seemed incomplete. Undertail coverts longer than those of MacG. Upperparts olive-green with gray nape onto crown. Bill fairly stout for a warbler, thicker than that of OC Warbler and similar species. Legs pinkish

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler has thicker, more crescent shaped, eyering. That species also has more limited black marking on chest, shorter undertail coverts, and often, more greenish on sides. Orange-crowned Warbler looks rather different and has more "needle-like" bill. Connecticut Warbler, larger, huskier, lacks dark chest patch, even longer undertail coverts
