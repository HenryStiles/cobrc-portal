Description of the Bird

"Oporornis" similar in size to MacGillivray's in size and shape overall. The bird appeared to have a gray hood with incomplete bib, so that yellow throat was in contact with yellow underparts. Clearly from photo, some brownish hues are present on crown. Eyering thin but distinct narrowly broken anteriorly, only. White. No appearance of "arc" quality to eye markings. Photos make eyering look more apparent than in field, though it was still easily seen. 
Back olive. Underparts bright yellow with some olive on sides. Long undertail coverts extending about 50% of distance of tail as seen from below. Legs pink. Bill somewhat thick for a warbler, with some pink.

No wing bars. No streaking anywhere on plumage. 

Similar Species Discussion

MacG Warbler with shorter undertail coverts, arcs for eyerings, even when extensice. Should not have yellow throat, especially with incomplete bib. 
OCWA would have thinner bill, some hint of streaking, shorter undertail coverts, etc.
CONW would have been larger billed, longer undertail coverts, larger bird, not so yellow on throat, complete bib
