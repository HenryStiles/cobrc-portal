Description of the Bird

Birding was skulking through dense cover under Russian Olive and Black Locust offering intermittent views of 5-10 seconds that were excellent, though brief. Modest hooded effect in field. Hood widely broken on throat/chest with yellow that extended from chest to throat. Bright yellow underparts. Modestly, relatively narrow, olive sides. Remainder of underparts vibrant yellow. Back green. Crown green with gray washed in. Roundish eyering with no break (in field) seen posteriorly, narrow break anteriorly. Eyering slightly washed in yellow. Undertail coverts longer than those seen in MacG. Bill thick as typical for Mourning/MacGillivrays. Legs bright pink. 

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler. More restricted and crescent shaped eyering. Hood not broken by yellow, so throat white or gray. Undertail coverts shorter. 

Cpnnecticut Warbler. Complete and bolder eyering, larger bill, longer undertail coverts

OC Warbler: smaller billed, shorter undertail coverts, overall different build, pale superciliium
