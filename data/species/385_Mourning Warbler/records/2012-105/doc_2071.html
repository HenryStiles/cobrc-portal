Description of the Bird

Warbler size and general appearance, though not slender like a Wilson's nor huge like a chat.

Olive backed. Crown, auriculars and sides to chest gave a somewhat hooded appearance and were olive with some hint of grayish wash. From lower chest to vent bright yellow, excepting some olive wash to sides. "Hood" interrupted at center, with yellow of belly extending to a moderately bright yellow throat (nearly as bright as belly. Complete, very thin, white eyering without any hint of forming arc, as in MacG Warblers. Eyering nowhere as thick as Nashville or Connecticut Warblers. Dull yellow narrow "supraloral" stripe. Bill thicker than species such as Nashville and OCWA.
Behaviors: Skulking low in Russian Olive thicket
Call: Sharp "jit" or "djit" given repeatedly in response to pishing. Immediately played recordings of Mourning and MacG Warbler calls for comparison, and match with Mourning was excellent, with MacG match poor
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Thick bill with pale pinkish at base eliminates former members of "Vermivora." MacG eliminated by voice, eyering shape and extent, yellow throat, yellow supraloral mark. Connecticut would not have broken hood and would have bolder eyering.
