Description of the Bird

I was working low dense willows (with taller trees overhead) along the western edge of Flagler Reservoir. I'd been pishing, and ten or so Wilson's Warblers had come in. A bird popped up from low down. 

The bird had a grayish hood, with extensive yellow throat, and narrowly complete bib (no black streaks, though). It had an eyering that was narrow, bright white, and very narrowly broken in front and behind, much like the bird I photographed at Jackson, except with the narrow break behind as well as in front. A weak yellowish supraloral stripe was present. The back was olive. The underparts bright yellow with some olive wash on sides. The undertail coverts were about half length of tail. The bill appropriate for MOWA, MacG but thicker than that of Nashville, Orange-crowned. Some pale on bill. Overall shape and size c/w Mourning/MacGillivray's warbler.

I had several clear nice looks at bird, and every time I was satisfied that I'd gleaned what I could from that view, I'd lift the camera, and the bird would dive into cover.

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler would have shorter thicker white eye markings forming arcs, shorter undertail coverts, no yellow on throat.
Connecticut Warbler would have bolder eyering, no supraloral marking, longer undertail coverts, larger bill, etc.
Nashville and other former Vermivora would have thinner bill among other marks.
