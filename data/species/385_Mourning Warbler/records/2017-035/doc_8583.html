Description of the Bird

First heard giving fairly loud and flat call note. Then song: CHEER-CHEER-CHEER-reachya. Or something like that. A bit burry. Richer (lower pitched?) than that of MacG. Fairly certain that I had a MOWA, I tried playing a recording. Silence. The bird shut up for quite a while. Pishing. Nothing. "Mob tape" caused the bird to call again so that I could find it skulking in underbrush across ditch.

Body: Upperparts olive. Underparts bright lemon yellow, though duller on sides. Not as dusky/olive on sides as MacG. See below for chest pattern.
Head: Gray in general. Some olive creeping from back onto nape (maybe hindcrown). Lores dark gray. Gray of head extending down onto chest ending in black crescent on chest. 

Bill thicker than in most warbler (eg, OCWA). Legs pink. Undertail covert length/tail length not assessed. 
Wonder if this is a first-spring bird. 

Similar Species Discussion

MacG should have white around the eye, not as solid/dark black on chest. In my experience, MacGs have more olive on sides than Mournings.
