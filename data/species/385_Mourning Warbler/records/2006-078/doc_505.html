Description of the Bird

Medium sized warbler with yellow-olive upperparts. Head with gray hood. Lores black. No eye-ring present at all. Dark eye. Upper mandible light gray with lower mandible slightly lighter in color. Chin and throat gray. Black feathering along lower border of throat and upper chest forming a "black bib." Rest of underparts and flanks yellow. Undertail coverts yellow.
Behaviors: The bird flew up from some dense leaf litter on the ground to a low, exposed branch in the open. It then flew away out of sight across a field towards a running stream.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Connecticut Warbler is larger and has a bold complete white eye-ring. Does not have black bib or black lores. MacGillivray's Warbler has a bold broken white eye-ring. Also, MacGillivray's has a less dense black bib that usually extends up onto the throat. In contrast, this bird had no eye-ring at all and a very obvious black bib.
