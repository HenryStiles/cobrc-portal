Description of the Bird

This bird was of similar size and shape to a MacGillivray's Warbler (one of which was nearby).

Underparts: there was a solid gray hood with blackish at the bottom of the hood (i.e. on the throat), just before the sharp transition to a fairly bright yellow breast and belly. the tail was short.

I saw the face well, but briefly, several times in good light. It was a dark gray, with no white eye markings at all.

The back and wings were an unmarked greenish yellow.
Behaviors: It was foraging in foliage, 5' to 10' above the ground.
Call: no vocalization heard
Plumage: breeding plumage

Similar Species Discussion

The main species to confuse this with was the common MacGillivray's Warbler. The total lack of any white (eye ring or eye crescent) around the eye was the key distinguishing feature.

Resources Used

I did not use a field guide when making the identification, but did consult with the Sibley guide later.
