Description of the Bird

Typical warbler shape and size - about five inches. Dark gray head, neck and upper breast; darkish green back and tail; lower breast and undertail bright yellow. Dark eye, and bright reddish legs. I got about eight good looks at this birds of 10 secs or longer, and I could discern no eye arcs or light coloring around the eyes on this bird at all. On at least two occasions the bird turned so that I could see that the lower part of the gray on the breast was darker than the rest of the gray. I did not note bill color, undertail color or the length of the undertail coverts. Soon after seeing this bird, I saw a male MacGillivray's warbler. The eye arcs on this bird were easily noted every time I looked at it, and the gray did no go down quite so far on the breast. Otherwise, the birds looked quite similar.
Behaviors: The bird was actively feeding low down in two willow trees that were not very leafed out. This
provided unobstructed views of the bird for about
three to four minutes. It did not seem quite as
skulking as the MacGillivray's, but this is difficult to assess given the relatively short observation time.
Call: None Heard
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's - no eye arcs and gray lower on breast
Connecticut - no eye ring and much darker head, neck and upper breast.
All other North American warblers have substantially different spring plumages.
