Description of the Bird

Medium-sized warbler moving thru low brush. Back and wings were greenish. The bird had a mousey-gray "hood," which included head, nape, and chest, excepting smudgy black on lower end of bib (ie, chest) and diffuse blackish lores (not as dark/well-defined as MacG) bleeding into immediately adjacent parts of face. Underparts bright yellow with limited greenish wash on sides. Undertail coverts long compared to tail -- longer than that seen on MacGillivray's Warbler. No white around eye at all. No wingbars. Bill moderately hefty for a warbler (c/w Mourning/MacG). Leg and bill color not noted. 

Similar Species Discussion

MacGillivray's Warbler has white crescents around eye and shorter undertail covert projection onto tail. Also, MacG typically has better defined/darker black on lores, more extensive olive on sides, less black on lower bib.

Connecticut Warbler with larger bill, complete eyering, no blackish in lores...
