Description of the Bird

Gull that was swimming near a shore in a lake. Small yellow bill with no marks on it. Dark eyes. Back of head, nape, and mantle were a uniform shade of gray. All coverts were grey. Front half of crown and malar were white, prominent vertical dark streak behind eye. White tail, tips of primaries were black. White belly and breast. This gull was considerably smaller than the common ring-beaked gulls that I have seen.
Behaviors: This bird was swimming in a small area near shore and seemed disoriented. It did not swim away when I approached it.
Call: None heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Gull was smaller, bill was shorter and appearance was definitely different than other gulls shown in my bird books.

Resources Used

I reviewed several bird books and was not sure of the bird type. I posted the picture on the Cobirds web site and several people (Brandon Percival, Rachel Hopper, Bill Maynard) immediately confirmed that it was a Black legged Kittiwake

Previous Experience

I have seen other gulls and this one was definitely different interms of coloring, bill size and shape, and overall size
