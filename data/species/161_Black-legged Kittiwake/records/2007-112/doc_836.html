Description of the Bird

the bird was a small gull, noticeably smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls, with proportionally shorter wings giving it a bulkier look, but noticeably larger than nearby Bonaparte's Gulls, and also appearing bulkier. The bill was fairly short, and relatively thick, for a gill. It was all black.

The bird's head was white, except for a small black spot behind the eye. The bird had a bold and broad black nuchal collar. The underparts were white. The wings had a Sabine's Gull-esque pattern, with black outer primaries and leading edge of the coverts contrasting with white inner primaries and secondaries, which in turn contrasted with the gray secondary coverts and scapulars. The bird had a bold black band across the secondary coverts, such that the bird appeared to have a black "M" when this band was combined with the black primaries and leading edge of the wing. The back was clean gray, the same color as the gray on the inner wings; this gray was about the same shade as that on a Ring-billed Gull.

The tail was slightly fork-shaped, and broadly tipped in black. The underwings were almost entirely white, with a small area of black near the tip.
Behaviors: The bird was flying around at about eye-level, which from the Lake View overlook meant the bird was about 25 feet over the water. It flew back and forth quite closeby, until it flew to the north and around a bluff towards the Marina. It could not be relocated later. It only loosely associate with any other gulls, most often Ring-billed Gulls but one close to a Bonaparte's.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species of Gull were ruled out by the small size of the bird. Ring-billed Gull was ruled out by the very different wing patter, black bill, black nuchal collar, and very different tail shape.

Bonaparte's Gull was ruled out by the larger size, bulkier and shorter bill, bold black nuchal collar, bolder wing pattern lacking the white in the outer primaries, and different tail shape.

Sabine's Gull was ruled out by the gray rather than white inner wing, bold black "M" on the upper wing, and less bold patterning of white and gray "triangles" on the wing, as well as gray back, heavier bill, and bold black nuchal collar.

Ross's Gull was ruled out by very different tail shape, bold black nuchal collar, bulkier proportions especially in the bill, larger size, etc.

Little Gull was ruled out by larger size, bold black nuchal collar, bulkier bill, and slightly fork-shaped tail.
