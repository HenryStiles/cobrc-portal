Description of the Bird

First observed in late afternoon (after 4) on 19 - Nov at Highline Lake SP - west side.

Observed again with Nic Korte on the morning of 20 Nov. - from the east side and at Mack Mesa Res.

 

Description:

larger than Bonaparte's Gull and smaller than Ring-billed.

large head with black eye and dark (black) arc behind the eye.

hindneck is a light gray - much lighter than the mantle or the arc behind the eye.

yellow bill - not much of an angle between the forehead and the bill - seems almost straight.

black legs - short legs give it a duck like appearance while standing.

 

while swimming, one notices

large head with dark eye and dark arc behind the eye, yellow bill.

the hindneck is a much lighter gray than the dark gray mantle.

behind the dark mantle and back of the bird is a white border (wide tertial crescent)

the wings extend beyond the tail and are black with no white mirrors.

more white showing on the side behind the shoulder above the waterline

 

when flying  the upperwings show

the mantle, back, and secondaries are a dark gray, with pale gray primaries and a black tip on the wing with no white mirrors evident.  All white tail.

 

when flying and seen from below,

the underwings show very light pale gray secondaries and axillaries,  white primaries, and a black wing tip with no white mirrors.

All white tail; black legs.

 

Behaviors:

steady, fast, buoyant flight.  (Dashing)

when diving into the water, the bird circles or stalls in the air, then dives straight down into the water before settling and swimming.

with good close looks, the bird seemed to wiggle its back end and wings while swimming.

bird flew when one tried to approach it  and get closer.

 

Similar Species Discussion

When I first saw the gull flying, I expected it to be either a Ring-billed Gull or a Bonaparte's Gull. Once I had a good look at the gull, I could tell it was not a Ring-billed Gull because of the smaller size and head pattern.  As I watched and studied the bird, I determined it was not a Bonaparte's Gull for the following reasons:
1. bigger yellow bill as opposed to more slender black bill;
2. the dark ear spot seemed to be an arc rather than just a spot;
3. in flight the bird showed a dark gray mantle, lighter pale primaries with black wingtips as opposed to all white primaries with black edge to wintips.

With the yellow bill, I considered a Mew Gull briefly, but the head pattern didn't fit and the bird didn't show any white tips or white wing spots or mirrors.

Wing pattern is different than Sabine's.
