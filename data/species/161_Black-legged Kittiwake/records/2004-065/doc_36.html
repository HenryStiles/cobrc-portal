Description of the Bird

The bird was flying around over the water, occasionally landing on the water.

It was noticeably larger than the numerous Bonaparte's Gulls, and comparable in size to Ring-billed Gulls.

When it was sitting on the water, I could see a narrow dark bill, black eye, small gray ear patch, and white crown, upper nape, and underparts.

It had a broad blackish collar at the nape/mantle transition, a somewhat scaly gray back, and a bold, black horizontal band (carpal bar) just above the water (as viewed when it was sitting on the water).

In flight, a bold black "M" pattern was obvious on the back and wings. The outer part of the M formed the leading edge of the wings. The inner part of the M had gray in front of it. Behind the M, the upper wing surface was white.

The under surface of the wings was light gray in front, white behind (low contrast between these two regions).

The white tail had a narrow black terminal band.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of its time flying around over the water, and occasionally landed on the water. This behavior and its overall appearance supported its identification as a gull, but I did not use the behavior to help identify it to species.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The bold black "M" on the wings rules out most species of North American gull, leaving the following:
Little Gull
Bonaparte's Gull
Common Black-headed Gull
Ross' Gull
Sabine's Gull
Black-legged Kittiwake
Of these, only Black-legged Kittiwake and perhaps Common Black-headed Gull are comparable in size to a Ring-billed Gull. Common Black-headed Gull has a much weaker neck/collar mark, and fainter wing markings.

The thick, dark collar mark, in contrast with largely light upperparts, rules out all or almost all other species of NA gulls just by itself.
