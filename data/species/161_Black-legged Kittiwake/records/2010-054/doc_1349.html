Habitat

Relatively small reservoir, the bird utilized east side of north shore (as were other gull species)

Description of the Bird

Medium sized gull; smaller than Ring-billed, larger than Bonapart's (both direct comparisons). Shape comparison with Ring-billed Gull: the Black-legged Kittwake had slimmer body, proportionately narrower and longer wings with more pointed wingtips, bill narrower and pointed, tail slightly forked. Plumage: Overall white bodied, head mostly white, pale gray on back of crown, black post ocular spot and hind collar. Upper wing: black carpal-bar, outer covers, and outer primaries (classic W appearance in flight), middle primaries with black subterminal spots, otherwise upper wing gray (as the mantle) and fading to light gray towards the end of the secondaries and inner primaries. Underwings mostly white. Mantle neutral gray. Tail white with thin (but distinct) black terminal band. Bill dark (black). Eye dark (black). Legs not observed or noted.
Behaviors: Feeding as other nearby gulls (Ring-billed and Bonapart's). Mostly observed in flight 10-30 feet above water surface, five to fifty feet from shore, diving on just below surface prey (presumably minnows). Once prey was captured, this bird retreated from feeding area towards middle of lake and sat for one to ten minutes (alone or with Bonapart's Gulls, not with Ring-billed), Once observed "bullying" its way into the middle of a tight group of larger feeding Ring-billed Gulls and take prey.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Combination of black W pattern, contrasting black hindcollar, and forked black tipped tail is unique to this species. Most similar species would be a juvenile/1st winter Sabine's Gull - which lacks the contrasting black collar and black carpal-bar of the kittiwake. Juvenile smaller hooded gulls eliminated by various characters; Bonapart's by direct comparison - lacks hind collar, thinner less distinct upper wing markings, straight or slightly rounded tail, Little - too small, Black-headed - dark inner primaries and tipped secondaries, lacks hind collar.
