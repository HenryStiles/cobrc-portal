Description of the Bird

Gull just a little bit smaller than a Ring-billed Gull. Bill was dark to black, but it had some yellow on it also, mostly towards the base of the bill. Also was fairly long and thin. Black legs. Dark eye. Black ear spot on a white head and it had a bold black stripe on the back of the neck. It had a black "M" shape on the leading edge of the wings, then at the joint it shot back towards the back of the wings. Pale inner primaries and secondaries with a gray mantle. Tail had a white tail with a bold black tip. Underside of the wings were entirely white with the exception that the tip of the wings had a small amount of black. The rest of the underside of the bird was white.
Behaviors: The bird was flying through the strong wind. I first saw it flying from the west towards the east just off shore, it then turned back and disappeared towards the west. A few minutes later it returned from the west. It then stayed on the east edge of the county line dam for several minutes. It then came out to the tip of the land that I was on. It just flew around in that area for about 20 minutes, sometimes disappearing below the little cliff that was below me, but then returning to view after seconds of being out of view.
Call: Didn't hear it
Plumage: 1st Winter

Similar Species Discussion

4 year gulls - this bird was to small to be a 4th year bird.
MEGU and RBGU - Doesn't have a black "M" on the back and wings. Doesn't have a black ear spot or black band on the back of the neck.
black headed gulls - None of them have a black band on the back of the neck, none of them have any yellow on the bill, and none of them have black legs.
Sabine's Gull - No yellow on the base of the bill, no black on the back, no black on the back of the neck.
Ross's Gull - Bill not small enough, didn't have a wedge shaped tail, no plumages have both the black ear spot AND black neck band.
Red-legged Kittiwake - Bill was not small enough, didn't have pink to red legs, RLKI doesn't have the black "M" on the wings and back.
