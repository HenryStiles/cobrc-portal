Description of the Bird

The belly and breast was white. It had a black medium sized bill. Also had a dark eye. It has a black ear stripe and a black bar going across the back of the neck. The back and top of the head that wasn’t black was very light gray. It has black fairly long wings. The back is about the same colored gray as a Ring-billed Gull. It also had some black feathers on the tertials. It was also slightly smaller than the nearby Ring-billed Gulls.
Behaviors: When we saw it the bird was sleeping with a group of gulls which included Bonaparte’s, Ring-billed, and California Gulls.
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The ear spot eliminates everything except Red-legged Kittiwake, Ross’s, Ross’s, Bonaparte’s, Black-headed, Little, Franklin’s, and Laughing Gulls. The gray colored back eliminated everything except Bonaparte’s, Black-headed, and Little Gulls. The black bar on the back of the neck eliminates all of the birds except Red-legged Kittiwake, which the size of the bill eliminates Red-legged.
