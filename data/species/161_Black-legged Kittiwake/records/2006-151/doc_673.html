Description of the Bird

A medium sized gull, with medium gray mantle, dark M pattern on extended upper wings with white ends to secondaries and inner primaries, creating a white trailing edge to most of the extended wing. Outer few primaries were dark, coupled with dark carpal bar creating the bold M pattern on the upper wing. Underwing was white. Bill and leg color were black. A dark spot appeared behind and low of the eye, and a dark neck-boa was evident on an otherwise white head and neck. Tail was white with a thin, dark terminal band.
Behaviors: Flying and feeding in open water of Cherry Creek reservoir. Bird would pick morsals from near the surface and continue flying or land in the water.
Call: none noted
Plumage: juvenile 1st winter

Similar Species Discussion

Bonaparte's Gull, Sabine's Gull, Little Gull, Black-headed Gull. The combination of white-trailing edge of wing, dark neck-boa, black legs and bill, and white underwing eliminates these species.
