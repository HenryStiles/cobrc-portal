Description of the Bird

A medium-sized gull (about the size of nearby Ring-billed Gulls), the bird had a white head with gray to blackish smudging around the eyes and a bit on the peak of the crown. There was a distinct blackish ear spot connected to a more vague band of medium gray. The nape was smoothly gray (no black nuchal collar), so the bird had obviously molted out of juvenile plumage.

In flight from above, the back and wing coverts showed medium gray (a bit darker than that of Ring-billed Gull), but each wing sported a distinct black band running from the base of the inner greater coverts diagonally cross the median coverts to the distal lesser coverts. This band then connected to the black of the alula and lesser primary coverts and continued down the through the greater primary coverts and the outer 3-4 primaries to the wingtip. Neither the tertials nor the secondaries sported any black.

In flight from below, the bird was mostly white, with only the head pattern, the black tail tip, and the tips of pp5-10 sporting any other color, and all, except for some of the head pattern, was black.

The notched tail was white, with a distinct black tip. The underparts were also white and unmarked. The eyes, bill, and legs were all dark.
Behaviors: Mostly foraging, flying circuits over the water at heights of 3-15 meters, occasionally lighting on the water to snag some morsel or other.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic I

Similar Species Discussion

All other gulls with a distinct 'M' pattern (Ross's, Little, Bonaparte's, and Black-headed) can be ruled out by various of the following: tail shape, leg color, bill color, and secondary color.
