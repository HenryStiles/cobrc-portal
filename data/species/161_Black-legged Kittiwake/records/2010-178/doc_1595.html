Description of the Bird

As I walked the trail to the lake, I saw a gull flying away from me toward the lake. It had very distinct dark wing tips with a pattern of black and white in the wing in which the black areas formed a "v" shape on the front of each wing with a bright white area behind the black "v". I lost sight of it and attempted to relocate it. I saw it again in a few minutes flying away from me and found it sitting on the water about 100 feet from where I was observing. The gull had a white head, black eye, and black bill with a small black area immediately behind the eye and a wide black line/bar at the back of the head/neck separating the head from the back, as well as a lot of black in the folded wings, showing black in the wingtips and black on what appeared to be the median or lesser coverts. The bird had a gray mantle, and the nape of the neck was white behind the black line/bar. As the bird flew away the final time, I saw that it had a white tail with a black band at the tip.
Behaviors: The bird was seen three times in flight briefly and seemed to have a typical gull flight. It sat on the water rather quietly for about 7 minutes before taking flight again.
Call: None
Plumage: First winter

Similar Species Discussion

Eliminated most gull species because they lack the black "v" pattern only on the front of the wing with bright white behind the "v" (having mostly all dark, all gray, or all white wings) and lack the white head with two dark areas behind the eye (and many don't have the black bill). Eliminated 1st winter Little Gull because it does not have the bright white in the rear of the wing and lacks the black bar separating the head from the back (and has a dark cap); 1st winter Bonaparte's Gull because it does not have the bright white in the rear of the wing and much less black on the leading edge and has only a single black area behind the eye; 1st winter Black-headed Gull because it does not have the bright white area in the rear of the wing (has a black trailing edge) and much less black on the leading edge and has a much smaller single black area behind the eye and has black from the eye to the top of the head; 1st winter Ross's Gull because it does not have solid black in the primaries at the tip of the wings, has a white rather than gray mantle, and has only one black area behind the eye; and 1st winter Sabine's Gull because it does not have black on the inner part of the wing so its "v" appearance is black on the outer part but brownish on the inner part and no different than the mantle color and because it does not have the two distinct black areas behind the eye on an otherwise white head.
