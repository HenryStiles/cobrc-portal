Description of the Bird

The was a medium sized gull, slightly smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The mantle color was a few shades darker than the RBGUs. The all yellow bill was short and at times "disappered" as there was no dark tip to contrast with the water coloration. The head was pale with a dark eye and a smudge below the eye. The most noticeable feature was the remains of the dark gray or blackish head that formed an arc at the rear of the bird's head extending dowwards to the auricular.
Behaviors: Mostly loafing but occasionally got up for 2-5 seconds and then landed again.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Red-legged Kittiwake is closest but it has a darker mantle and a shorter bill with a downward hook on the tip. Mew Gull and Ring-billed Gulls are about the same size as this BLKI. RBGU has dark tip to the bill; it is a few shades darker-mantled, is actually a bit larger than the BLKI and they (RBGUs) were present for comparison. MEGU has a similar colored,, but thinner bill and the mantle is slightly less dark than the kittiwake. MEGU, however never has the distincive blackish hood line behind the head that extends down to the auricular on the BLKI.
