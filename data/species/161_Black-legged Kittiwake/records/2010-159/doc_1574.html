Description of the Bird

We saw the bird flying around the middle of the reservoir. It had a black M on the gray back, the strange thing about this bird was the M wasn’t as bold as I am use to. We then noticed that it had a white tail with a black bar at the end of the tail. It had a white underside other than a small black tip at the end of the wing. It had black bill and legs. We then saw the black bar on the back of the neck and black ear spot. It was a little smaller than the nearby Ring-billed Gulls, but larger a than the Bonaparte’s Gulls.
Behaviors: It was flying around the middle of the reservoir, it would occasionally land/dive and sit on the water for up to 30 seconds.
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The black bar on the back of the back of the neck eliminate all other gulls. The M on the back eliminates everything but Ross’s, Black-headed, Bonaparte’s, and Little. The size eliminates Little, Ross’s, and Bonaparte’s. Then amount of black on the underside of the wings eliminate Black-headed.
