Description of the Bird

A mid sized larid with a distinctive black M pattern in the wing tops and back, dark nuchal collar, and black terminal tail band, all visible despite the heavy snow and fairly long distance to the bird. The underwings appeared to be white, with wingtip details difficult to make out.
Behaviors: The bird was only observed foraging up and down the rocky south shore with other gulls. It would wheel and dive down to join other gulls harassing mergansers, but couldnt be seen on the water. Its flight was distinctively different than the other gulls present, using lots of dynamic soaring compared to the more frenzied flapping of the other gulls.
Call: not heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Juvenile Sabines Gull shows the dark outer surfaces on the upper wings, but lacks the inner limbs of the M and has brown scapulars and upper wing coverts. Rosss Gull is much smaller and has a spade shaped tail instead of the straight tail seen on this bird. Little Gull much smaller. Bonapartes Gull smaller, lacks obvious nuchal collar.
