Description of the Bird

I first noticed the bird by the thick black M on the back of the bird. The underparts were white other than a small black wingtip. The back was gray. The primaries and secondaries were white. It had a white tail with a black bar at the end of the tail. Then at a closer inspection it had a black bill and black legs. It also had a black bar on the back of the neck and a black ear spot. It was just a little smaller than the nearby Ring-billed Gulls.
Behaviors: It was flying around the reservoir occasionally landing on the water for up to 20 seconds before taking off again.
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The black M on the back of the bird eliminates everything except Ross’s Gull, Little Gull, Bonaparte’s Gull, and Black-headed Gull. Then the black stripe on the back of the neck eliminates all these other species. The amount of black on the wingtip eliminates Black-headed Gull. Tail shape eliminates Ross’s Gull. The size of the bird eliminates Little Gull and Bonaparte’s Gull. Also, the lack of black on the trailing edge of the primaries and secondaries eliminates these last three birds.
