Description of the Bird

The bird was generally similar in size and shape to an American Coot.

It had yellowish legs, with very long, completely unwebbed toes.

It had deep purple underparts; a dull, dark, olive green back; and unmarked and lighter iridescent purple sides.

It had white undertail coverts.

It had a red bill with a yellow tip.

The frontal shield was pale blue, with a worn appearance.
Behaviors: It was up in a conifer tree when I first saw it! Later, it flew down onto a lawn. This is obviously not typical behavior for this species.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

This was obviously a generally coot-like marsh bird. It was far too vividly blue and purple to be a Common Moorhen. The only potential species for confusion would be a Purple Swamphen. The bright yellowish legs, olive green back, and pale blue frontal shield all rule out Purple Swamphen, and support Purple Gallinule.
