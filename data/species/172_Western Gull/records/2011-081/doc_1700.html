Description of the Bird

This was a large, but not huge, breeding-plumaged Gull. The head, underparts and tail were pure white. The bill was yellow with a medium sized red spot, and a quite large gonydeal angle. The legs were pink, but not very bright. The mantle was rather dark, but was not black. The bird had a broader than average white tertial crescent. The tips of the primaries on the upper wings were black, plus they were darker than the rest of the underwing. There was a quite large white mirror on P10 only. Most of these features can be seen on the photographs.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of its time loafing on the sand spit and feeding from a dead fish on the sand. It stretched its wings several times, and took short flights on two occasions.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Herring Gull: Has considerably lighter mantle color, a pale iris and usually has a white mirror on P9 as well as 10.
Thayer's Gull: Has much lighter mantle color, darker legs, red orbital ring and much lighter wingtips and underwings.
Glaucous-winged Gull: Has much lighter mantle color and underwings, pink orbital ring and several subterminal spots.
Slaty-backed Gull: Has slightly darker mantle color, white subterminal spots and broader tertial crescent.
Yellow-footed Gull: Has bright yellow legs, very slightly darker mantle.
Lesser Black-backed Gull: Is much smaller, has yellow legs and a red orbital ring.
Greater Black-backed Gull: Is much larger, has a darker mantle color and a red orbital ring.
