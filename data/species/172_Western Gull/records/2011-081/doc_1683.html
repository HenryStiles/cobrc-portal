Description of the Bird

This is a report for the Chatfield SP bird first reported by Glenn Walbek on 6/1.

Bird was first observed by myself at 6:10pm MDT on 6/1 just north of the swim beach inside the north entrance of the park, in Jefferson County.

The bird was alone, and appeared fairly large, relative to my impression of a ring-billed, although no other gulls were present for a direct comparison. The bird's head was clean white, with a large, bright yellow, bill, with a red spot on the lower mandible, and a down-curving tip. The bird had a yellow eye-ring, surrounding a pale iris.
The bird showed a dark gray back. White trailing wing edge showed when bird was standing with folded wings, dividing back from mostly black primaries. Standing bird did show white tips of primaries, and a broad white tip was visible on the underside of the far wing when standing, and on both wings in flight. Legs showed pale pink when standing and in flight.
Behaviors: Bird appeared somewhat lethargic, or restful. It was lying for much of the 80 minutes I observed it on the West side of the lake. Alternating tucking its head with yawning, a bit of picking at the ground and some yawning. The bird did stand several times, revealing an injury to the left leg. The bird would not place weight on the leg, but did use it at times for head scratching. Eventually the bird took flight, heading east, in the general direction of the South Marina Sand Spit where it was originally seen. I returned to that side of the lake, and photographed the same bird, showing the same field-marks, more poorly in the fading light, and favoring the same leg.
Call: N/A
Plumage: adult alternate
