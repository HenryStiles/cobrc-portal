Description of the Bird

A large, dark-mantled gull with dull pinkish legs, noticeably larger than nearby Cal gull.
Head white without any signs of streaking. Iris light and orbital orange-yellow and contrasting with surrounding white feathers. Bill orangy yellow, bulbous at distal end and wide-based. Noticeable steep angle to gonys with red-orange large spot. Folded wings dark with row of white feathers visible from each shingled primary feather.
Spread wings, dorsal view, P10 with elongated subterminal white spot contrasting with gray on either side. P8 and P9 with small white terminal tip but without white mirrors or subterminal white spots. Mantle slate gray and about two shades darker than adult CAGU which was about 5 feet from the WEGU. Broad white trailing edge to wings in flight. No "string of pearls" in flight. Underwing perched but spread wing (see photo) leading edge white except for outer 6-7 primaries which formed contrasting dark wingtip with elongated white mirror on P-10. Outer 6-7 primaires with small white tips. lighter gray tongue on P6 or P5. Tail white.
Behaviors: Mostly loafing, sometimes walking with gimpy leg. Flew twice and landed by dead carp on which it fed. After eacy feeding it walked to the water and drank and splashed water with its head. Sometimes used its left foot to seemingly push at its throat as if something was lodged there. Howerver, swallowed chunks of dead fish with seemingly not trouble.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Most like Slaty-backed Gull - Slaty-backed has the "string of pearls" a series of subterminal white spots on many of its outer primaries that can be seen in flight and maybe a shade darker mantle. SBGU has a white mirror on P10 AND on P9. This bird had no string of pearls and no P9 mirror.

Lesser Black-backed Gull - much smaller, much more attenuated and with YELLOW, not PINK legs.

Yellow-leged Gull - similar wing pattern and size but with YELLOW, not PINK legs

Hybrid - Glaucous-winged Gull x Western Gull - will look similar but with duller gray wingtips (not blackish) and more closely resembles Slaty-backed Gull and/or Thayer's Gull. Orbit of hybrid a mix of yellow and pink, but our bird had solid yellow orbit and heads of GWGU x WEGU have smudgy dark marks and not the pure white head of this bird. Backs are usually at least a shade lighter than the light race of Western Gull.

Great Black-backed Gull - larger with darker, blacker mantle, bill with lesser pronounced gonydeal angle. Has a LARGE WHITE MIRROR on P9, our bird did not.
