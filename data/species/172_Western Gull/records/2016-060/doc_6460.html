Description of the Bird

Large stout gull, clearly larger than nearby California and LBB Gulls. Bill rather large (in comparison with nearby gulls, or Herring Gull) and mostly black with distinct gonydeal angle. Some pinkish at base. Legs pink. Back dark gray, wings mixed gray and brown. Rump white. Tail appeared entirely black excepting dull pale narrow terminal band. Wing projection past tail short. Moderate secondary skirt which appeared blackish as did folded primaries. Diffuse smudgy gray on head, mostly concentrated around/behind eye. Broad diffuse dusky gray smudgy streaks on sides of otherwise whitish chest and belly. Neck white with narrower dusky diffuse smudgy streaks. Eye starting to show some pale

Leg banded as seen on photo consistent with WEGU banded on the Farallon Islands: These is one of our WEGU, banded as a chick in 2015. White over metal on the left. - Russell Bradley, Farallon Program Leader, Point Blue Conservation Science.
