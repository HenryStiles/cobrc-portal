Description of the Bird

Shortly after arriving at Timnath, I noted a bulky dark gray-mantled gull across the reservoir. Lighting conditions were about as good as they get at Timnath, sun mostly behind me, few heat waves, no wind... but the distance was great. Still, the bright yellow-orange bill, short wings, bulky build caught my eye. As I maneuvered for a better view, it disappeared. I convinced myself that I'd hallucinated. Later, I saw the bird again, in worse light but at much closer distance. I could see many of the features mentioned above, plus a rounded (compared with Herring or LBB Gulls) head, small eye, unstreaked head (and neck/chest/etc). I was pretty sure it was a Western Gull, but I was not convinced until it floated by about 100 yards off the parking lot, with sun nearly directly behind me, no heat wave interference. 

The bird had a darkish gray mantle, darker than that of local California Gulls but not as dark as most adult Lesser Black-backed Gulls (one was present as well). The underparts, head, tail, neck were pristine white. The thick bill was an intense yellow-orange with red spot near gonydeal angle. It had a moderate gonydeal angle (not as large as that of YF Gull nor, proportionate to bill thickness, as large as that of many Herring Gulls). The eye was dusky dark (at same distance, white eye of Herring and Ring-billed Gulls was readily apparent). The folded wings showed a broad white trailing edge to the tertials and a white crescent near base of tertials (I am embarrassed to say that I am not certain what feather tract is involved here; in any case, the small but distinct crescent is present on many gulls, including Western and LBBG). A secondary "skirt" was noted. The uppersurface of the folded primaries were jet-black with white spots, consistent with what is typical of Western Gull. 

The bird was considerably larger/bulkier than the nearby California Gulls. Its primaries were considerably shorter than those of the Cal Gulls (and also shorter than that of the LBBGs and Herring Gulls present). The head was relatively rounded (compared with that of LBBG or Herring Gull) with a proportionately small eye. The upperwing in flight was gray blending into black on outer primaries; prominent white trailing edge to wing present. White mirror pattern not noted. Underwing was white on underwing coverts, gray on undersurface of secondaries and primaries, becoming blackish on outer primaries. Wing pattern in flight not as well seen as that was view occurred when bird not as close, or lighting conditions as good, as during best view of bird on the water. The legs, seen briefly, appeared intense pink.

The last and best view was for several minutes, and the dang bird disappeared (as in flew off) while I was note-taking, and I could not refind it.

Similar Species Discussion

Western x GW Gull: Would have been paler backed, lighter on primaries. Bill would typically have been more flat yellow or straw yellow and head should have had some streaking.

LBBG: Smaller bodied, longer-winged, flatter-headed, white-eyed, no orange tinge to bill color, smaller/narrower bill

SB Gull: Blacker backed, white eyed, duller bill color

YF Gull: Blacker backed, white eyed, bulkier bill

Kelp Gull: Black backed, dull-colored greenish legs.

GBBG: Black backed, heavier billed, even larger body size, often some head streaking and flatter headed.

Note that the head shape with small eye is fairly stereotypical of Western Gull and would be rather atypical of other dark-backed gulls.
