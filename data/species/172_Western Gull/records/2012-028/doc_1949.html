Description of the Bird

On the morning of 17 March 2012, at about 7:45 (45 minutes or so after sunrise), I was looking at a number of gulls feeding on dead fishnalong the edge of Prewitt Reservoir. On a somewhat distant log, I noted a dark gray (but not blackish) gull of goodly size. I immediately thought "Western Gull."

The bird was a large and chunky gull with relatively short wings. It was approximately the length of a couple nearby Herring Gulls, but it was rather bulkier. The bird?s underparts and head were completely white, excepting perhaps, some faint dark markings near the eye. The eye was dark (I could see the pale eye of the nearby Herring and Ring-billed Gulls). The feet were pink, the same color as the nearby Herring Gulls. The back and wing-coverts were a smooth dark gray, a bit paler than that of most Lesser Black-backed Gulls, but darker than that of California Gulls. The tertials, on the other hand, were ragged and worn, and gray-brown in color. Their tips were paler, but not broadly or evenly enough to form a "tertial crescent." There was a moderately prominent white "scapular crescent." The primaries were jet black without any white spots or crescents. The bill pattern was difficult to ascertain with certainty. The base was bright yellow, the tip straw yellow, and in between there was some black.

The bird was viewed for between 5 and 10 minutes at about 100-150 yards in good to excellent light, without wind or heatwaves. I was using a Kowa 88 mm scope with a 20-60x eyepiece. The bird departed by flying directly away from me, thus giving me no opportunity to discern further marks.

Similar Species Discussion

LBBG: Would be longer winged, smaller, more delicate and have light eye, and most often, yellow legs.
California Gull: Smaller, longer-winged, paler backed, non-pink legs, dark eye among other marks
GW x Western Gull: would be paler mantled and more markings on head.
SB Gull: Darker backed, more head streaking, pale eye.
Kelp Gull: Darker backed, non-pink legs.
