Description of the Bird

By looking at the size of the bird and shape of it's bill, it was evident that the bird was a tanager. The bird was splotchy looking with much red on the head and through the chest, and some red on the back. The belly was yellow and the wings, back, and tail were greenish colored with some red splotching.
Behaviors: The bird was observed feeding (much like a bluebird),
dropping down low on the ground or in shrubs a few feet off the ground. The area was adjacent to an old ranch house in what once had been a well-kept lawn with an orchard and now years later had reverted back to a wild or natural state. After feeding on the lawn and shrubs, the bird flew to the old orchard area.
Call: did not sing.
Plumage: 1st spring male

Similar Species Discussion

Upon seeing the bird initially, I was able to ascertain the bird was a tanager because of the profile and bill size/shape. Western Tanagers were moving through the area and are fairly common. Compared to a Western Tanager, the red on the head, throat, and chest was much more extensive and the bird had a splotchy appearance. The colors of the bird were red,yellow, and greenish while the Western Tanager shows black/white on its wings. There were some red splotches on the wings and back of the bird as well. Compared to a female Eastern Summer Tanager, the red on the head, throat, and chest were much pronounced and extensive and were definitely red without any orangish shading.
