Description of the Bird

This large tanager was dull yellow underneath from the throat to the undertail coverts and yellowish-olive on most upperparts. It looked noticeably larger than the Western Tanagers it was with in a loose flock. The wings and back were more olive and there was no sign of any wingbars. The grayish bill was heavy and relatively long for a tanager. The crown was olive-yellow, similar to the back, and the face was also olive-yellow though slightly lighter than the crown. There appeared to be darker eye line, but the distance was too great to be sure.
Behaviors: This bird was foraging in cottonwood trees along side a dirt road in a riparian area. There was a stream close by the roadway.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Lack of darker wing or any wingbars combined with large bill distinguished this bird from Western or Scarlet tanager. General yellow and yellow-olive coloring and lack of gray auriculars distinguish this bird from Hepatic. Also, throat was same color as underparts and not brighter as female Hepatic.

Resources Used

Sibley to be sure that bird was not a female or immature Hepatic.

Previous Experience

Very familiar with Western, Scarlet, and Summer Tanagers from Ohio and SW Colorado. I've seen Hepatic several times in Arizona and one male in Colorado.
