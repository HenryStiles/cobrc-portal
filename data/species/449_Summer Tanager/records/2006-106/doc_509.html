Description of the Bird

the birds were about the size of Western Tanagers seen earlier in the day, and significantly larger than Yellow Warblers nearby. The bill on the one male I saw well was brownish yellow, and fairly large for a passerine, and especially so for a tanager.

The overall body color of both males I saw was intense bright red, with no other colors visible. The wings and tail were the same color.
Behaviors: The birds were singing and calling from along the riparian corridor along Yellow Jacket creek, apparently defending two seperate territories.
Call: Both males continuously uttered a loud and fairly unmusical "pitty-tuck" call, and were orignially tracked down by listening for this. At one point I heard two different birds at the same time singing a robin-like song, but quite fast, and with some burry-er phrases interspersed in between.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Most other species were ruled out by the combination of size and overall bright red color. Hepatic Tanager was ruled out by the lack of grayish coloration on the flanks and auricular area, by the more intense red color, and by the brownish-yellow bill. The call also did not match that species. Scarlet Tanager was ruled out by the complete lack of black on the wings and tail. Western Tanager was ruled out by the complete lack of yellow on the bird.

Northern Cardinal was ruled out by the lack of a crest, lack of black around the face, much thinner bill that was not red, very different song and call, etc.
