Habitat

General area is ponderosa pine/ juniper with American plum in drainages. Around our house, where all observations took place, there are dormant gardens, rock gardens and stored equipment, like utility trailer, lumber, etc.

Description of the Bird

Bird was slightly smaller than an Am. Robin (which were nearby), slender, rosy red over the body, tail, wing coverts, with wings showing black tips and a mid edge of black on the outer primaries. The legs were pale whitish including the toes. The bill was long, relatively thick and had a decided 'tooth' on the upper mandible at mid-length. The bill was pale gray, which in bright sunlight appeared pale yellow or grayish. The bird often raised a crest on the head as it looked around. When flying, the primaries and outer secondaries showed black with the rachis red.
Behaviors: The bird actively flew to the ground and caught insects and ate them (it was noon, and about 60 degrees F, so insects were active). The bird was next to our house and flew into low bushes, rock garden, and on a utility trailer as it looked for insects. The bird stayed in this area of about 100'x100' for an hour, then flew to the east about 200' before disappearing.
Call: Bird was silent during entire observation period (1 Hour)
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

I am very familiar with other Tanagers in the US, having lived in MI for 30 years. Scarlet Tanager has distinct black tail and wings. Western Tanager at this time of year lacks any red in males; Hepatic Tanager males have noticeable grayish areas on back and wings and on the auricular patches. The wings have brownish bars interspersed with gray.
