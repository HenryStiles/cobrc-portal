Description of the Bird

The bird was a tanager that was comparable in size to a blackbird, possessing feathers that were mostly greenish-yellow overall, with bright red feathers mixed in. The bill was larger than a Western Tanager, and the bird had a stockier appearance. The face was plain, and it was uniform in that it lacked wing-bars and eye rings and related field marks.
Behaviors: The bird was first observed flying out towards the road, presumably flycatching. It was then observed perched in the trees nearby in the campground. When observed later in the day, it was perched as to shelter itself from the wind, engaged in bill stropping after being perturbed from the presence of humans for so long (inferred from the fact it was watching us intently) and then flew to a nearby tree to eat the buds.
Call: None detected
Plumage: 2nd Year Male

Similar Species Discussion

I eliminated similar species based on the red feathering occurring throughout the body, the uniform yellow-green feathering on the bird, and the lack of wing bars or gray in the face. All of the observed field marks pointed towards Summer Tanager.
