Description of the Bird

DESCRIPTION – Robin was feeding big dishes of grape jelly to attract and photograph birds. His idea brought in a huge number of migrating birds, sometimes 50 or more Western Tanagers were in his yard, along with many Bullock’s Orioles, one 1st cycle Bullock’s x Baltimore hybrid (photographed), and this 1st cycle male Summer Tanager. By shape and size the bird was the same as the many Western Tanagers in the area. The bill was stocky and straw/gray colored like the Western Tanager’s, more streamlined than a Black-headed Grosbeak, many of these were around as well. The head, throat, nape, breast, back, and upper tail were red with many yellow feathers mixed in. The bird’s belly was yellow and the wings mostly olive-colored. The wings also had some darker and reddish feathers mainly in the secondaries, and notably no wingbars. Of note, a first cycle male Summer Tanager was seen and photographed by Ken Neubecker in Carbondale on 5/07/11 about 12 miles away. Another sighting was at the home of Vic Zerbi in Glenwood Springs on 6/01/11, about one mile away. These all could be sightings of the same individual bird.
Behaviors: Repeatedly came to grape jelly at feeders and associated with Western Tanagers.
Call: N/A
Plumage: 1st Cycle Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The alternate plumage of 1st cycle male Summer Tanager is unique and I don’t believe can confused with other species. Male Western Tanager has far less red on the head, none on the body, and strong wing-bars. 1st cycle Flame-colored Tanager has a similar appearance to SUTA with orange feathers and again strong wing-bars. This would be way out of range as well. The other tanagers are also eliminated by plumage, although some are similar to adult Summer Tanager. By shape, size and plumage not one of the finches, Cardinal, or Vermillion Flycatcher either.
