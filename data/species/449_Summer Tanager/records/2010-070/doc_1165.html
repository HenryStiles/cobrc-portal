Habitat

The bird was captured at the banding station located in the riparian forest, consisting of mainly cottonwood and willow species, surrounding Barr Lake.

Description of the Bird

The body plumage of this bird was orangish yellow with an olive tinge and the greatest amount of olive on its back. Its flight feathers were olive, and no wing bars were present. Some greater coverts were washed orange. Its uppertail coverts and crown were washed with reddish orange, and its underparts were dull yellow with the exception of its undertail coverts, which were washed bright orange. The mandible, which lacked a distinct tooth, was mostly dark gray, and the maxilla was pale, pinkish-tan. Its wing chord measured 93 mm and tail 76 mm.
Behaviors: Normal behaviors of the Summer Tanager were not observed during its capture and banding.
Call: not applicable
Plumage: yellowish overall with olive wash

Similar Species Discussion

Its mandible lacked a distinct tooth, which is characteristic of both HETA and SCTA. The length of its bill (nares to tip) was 13.5 mm, which also eliminated the shorter-billed HETA and SCTA. This bird also lack wing bars, which are usually present and distinct in WETA.
