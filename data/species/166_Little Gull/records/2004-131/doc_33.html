Description of the Bird

A very small gull, obviously smaller than even nearby Bonapartes Gulls. The bill was dark, small, straight, and thin. The head was mostly white, with a sharply defined small dark ear spot. A faint gray bar rose from the eye over the crown, and a faint gray smudge connected the dark ear spot to the eye. The head was rounded, giving the bird a gentle look. The eye was dark. The mantle was fairly pale gray, about the same shade as nearby Bonapartes Gulls. The underparts were clean white. On the folded wings there was no black visible on the upper side, but a bit of the dark underside was visible in the far wing. The upper primary tips visible in the folded wing were white, and the underside tips visible in the far wing were white. The wings weren't especially long, giving the bird a bit of a stubby look. In flight, the extensive dark gray underwings were visible, contrasting sharply with the rounded white wingtips and thin white trailing edge. Additionally, the tail is seen to be completely white and rather short when the bird is in flight.
Behaviors: The bird was alternatively resting out in the lake, flying around foraging, and feeding with a groups of 5 to 20 Bonapartes Gulls when they discovered shoals of baitfish.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Size alone rules out other gulls and kittiwakes, as do the white wingtips and dark underwing pattern.
