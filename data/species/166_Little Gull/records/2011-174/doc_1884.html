Light:  Sunny, sun to SSW, bird flying at about 150-200m N to NNE. Th elighting was superb.

Description of the Bird

The juvenile Little Gull had not been seen since morning and I had been there about an hour. It finally made an appearence flying northeast from the southwest side of Union in perfect light conditions and was easy to identify. It had the notable black "chevrons" or "M-pattern", a noticeable dark cap with dark ear spot, and a small black tail band on the trailing edge. One striking feature was the short tail which gave it an unusual appearance to me. The mantle appeared light gray, but I did not pay much attention to that because the bold wing pattern was the most notable feature.

Similar Species Discussion

Bonaparte's and Black-headed Gulls. Upper wings did not have flashes of white, but the dark chevrons with dark where the light flash should be. The tail was very short and too short for either two species. Size did appear small, but such a subjective measurement at that distance with nothing to compare was not a good characteristic. Many Bonaparte's were at the reservoir including 1st winter juveniles. Although one juvenile Bonaparte's looked smaller than the other Bonaparte's (was in the wrong lighting - I was on north side), it showed a flash of white on its upper wing finally.
