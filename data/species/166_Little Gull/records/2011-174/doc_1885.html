Description of the Bird

Around 2:30 pm on 1 December, I was birding along the south side of Union Reservoir. The snow had stopped, and the sun was fighting to break its way through the high cloud layer. In front of me, at about 100 feet, there was a flock of 20 or so Bonaparte's Gulls feeding. One caught my eye because of the very dark M made by its wing markings, and I realized that it was a first cycle Little Gull.

The bird was smaller than the surrounding Bony's, with proportionately rounder wings giving it a less elegant flight style. Each wing had a blackish-brown chevron formed by a dark "carpal" bar from rear of wing (near body) to front of wing (away from body) and a by blackish brown outer primaries that met the dark carpal bar at the anterior wing at the bend of the wing. The inner primaries were not as dark, though I did not note the paler inner webs (visible in photos) in the field. The remainder of wing and back appeared pearly gray, roughly the same color as that of the surrounding Bony's. The underparts were completely white. The base of the tail and the uppertail coverts were white. The tail had a fairly thick black terminal or subterminal band. The tail was rounded, not wedged shape. The bird's head appeared whitish with a grayish cap and spot behind eye. The bill was small, and seemed black, though I couldn't be certain. I watched the bird for about 10 minutes, called a bunch of folks, and then drove quickly to and from my house, returning with camera. Rachel Hopper appeared first on the seen, followed shortly by Nick Komar and Cole Wild. Views after return were 100+ yards.
Behaviors: see above. Dipping to water to feed like Bonaparte's Gull

Similar Species Discussion

Bonaparte's Gull would have had white on outer primaries, not as dark markings on upper wings. Also size and shape. Ross's Gull would have had wedge-shaped tail, lacked cap.
