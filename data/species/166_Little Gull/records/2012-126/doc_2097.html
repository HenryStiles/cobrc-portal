Description of the Bird

A tiny larus, not much larger than the many Black Terns at the lake. It had a dark brown mantle and crown witha dark eye spot behind the eye on a white-ish face. The gull was white beneath with a white tail and dark terminal band. A black 'M' pattern was formed on th back of the wings when the bird was in flight. See photo.
Behaviors: Flying around remaining water of drained lake, occasionally resting on the mud.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

bird is similar to juvenile Sabine's Gull, but face pattern including eye-spot are different
