Description of the Bird

This was a very small gull, and was on Terry Lake, not Clark Res.  Was first found flying over the lake by means of the very dark M pattern on the upper wing, small size and black tip to the tail.  The black tail tip is shared by the first winter Bonaparte's Gulls that were also present.  It then sat on the lake at a distance of about 400 yards.  Even at that distance the black on the top of the head was visible, which was not present on the adjacent Bonaparte's Gull. Both species share the black spot behind the eye, and have black bills.  When we returned from Ish Res at about 9:40 am, the bird was sitting on a sand spit only about 60 yards from us, as were several first winter and adult basic Bonaparte's Gulls.  Telescope views filled the frame.  The bird was quite a bit smaller than the Bonaparte's Gulls, and was in first winter plumage.  The bird had a very black, broad carpal bar and black on top of the head, and the black bill and pink legs were both smaller than the Bonaparte's Gulls.  The back was gray, and the showing wing tips were black.  After 20 minutes all the birds flew, and the Little Gull was easily picked out by the very black M wing pattern.  The bird did not show a white wedge in the outer primaries, which was shown by the Bonaparte's Gulls of all ages.

Similar Species Discussion

First winter Bonaparte's Gull is the only real contender.  Told by smaller size, darker M pattern on the upper wing, no white wedge in the outer primaries, and smaller bill and legs.  Black-headed Gull is even rarer in Colorado, and is considerably larger than a Bonaparte's Gull.

Resources Used

None needed at the time of sighting.  Did check in the National Geographic and Sibley guides before I went.

Previous Experience

Have seen Little Gull three times in Colorado, and once in the Eastern USA.  Have seen the species a few times in Europe.  Am very familiar with Bonaparte's Gull, which I see every spring and fall in Colorado.  Am very familiar with Black-headed Gull, which is abundant in England.
