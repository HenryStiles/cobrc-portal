Description of the Bird

Small gull, picked out from nearby Bonaparte's Gulls because of extensive dark on mantle. Had dark primaries connecting with dark "carpal" bar, forming part of an "M" shape, which was connected across back, as back there was still much black on back. Secondaries dark. Greater wing coverts pearly gray, contrasting greatly with remainder of upperwing. Underwing white except narrow dark trailing edge to primaries. Smaller than nearby Bonys with more rounded wing tips. White beneath except blackish shoulder mark as in juv. Crown and nape blackish as well, with blackish spot behind eye. Small bill

Similar Species Discussion

Immature Bonaparte's/BH Gull larger, no blackish markings on upperwing, white on upper primaries. Ross's Gull lacks black on the crown, had wedge shaped tail (which this bird did not have)
