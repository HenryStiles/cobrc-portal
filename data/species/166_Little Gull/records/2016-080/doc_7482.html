Description of the Bird

Gull feeding in the air above reservoir with Franklin and Ring-billed Gulls. Distinctly smaller than Franklins. Primaries mostly black or blackish. Black forming a "carpal" bar on upper wing coverts. Back mostly black/blackish. Combination of carpal bars and back lead to an "M" appearance dorsally. Some combination of innermost primaries, posterior wing coverts and secondaries formed a roughly a roughly triangular pale gray area. Also, a small portion of anterior/proximal wing coverts near shoulder pale gray. Crown blackish extending onto neck and a bit onto upper back. Blackish auriculars. Tail mostly white with black band near or at end. Under wings mostly white with black on trailing edge of outer primaries. Under parts mostly or entirely white, not much attention paid to this region.

Similar Species Discussion

No other gull (except Ross's Gull) really has this combination of wing markings and head markings. Vaguely similar to Sabine's Gull, but solid brown or gray area on upperwing coverts, not blackish bar; underwings with more dark in Sabine's. BLKI is larger, has differently marked head. Bonaparte's Gull not as darkly marked above, more white on primaries, browner and less extensive head markings. As for Ross's Gull, tail not wedge-shaped (as in Ross's) and white line extending into dark primaries not noted (I did not specifically not presence or absence of secondary bar).
