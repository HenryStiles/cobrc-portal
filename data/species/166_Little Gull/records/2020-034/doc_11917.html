Description of the Bird

I first noticed 3 smaller gulls on the water at the S. Platte delta on Chatfield S.P. . Of the 3,  one was smaller than the other two.

All 3 gulls were swimming together and picking stuff off the water. The larger two were identified as Juv Sabine's Gulls. The third gull was noticeably smaller, had a small black bill, dark crown, dark smudge around the eye and a dark post ocular spot. Mantle was dark, extending into a partial collar. Scapulars were dark with a white terminal end, giving the bird a barred appearence. Primaries were dark. In flight the bird had a classic 'M'  appearance on top, the tail was white white a dark terminal band.

Similar Species Discussion

Given the proximity to the Juv Sabine's gulls, the only other gull to consider was Bonaparte's Gull and I considered this bird to be  a BoGu for awhile. Juv BoGu have a paler color to the crown, the mantle and scapulars are a lighter brown/gray and lack the white terminal edges.
