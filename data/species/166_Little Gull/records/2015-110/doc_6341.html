Description of the Bird

A very small gull was standing/preening in shallow water in the company of dozens of Franklin's Gulls. This small gull was considerably smaller than the Franklin's Gulls, perhaps 70-80% of length, approx 50% in mass. The body was short, giving the gull a shape that reminded me of a football, very different than the long extended look of a Sabine's Gull, which had also been present in the flock. The compact appearance was partly due to short wings. Underparts (belly, breast, throat) was clean white. Underwings (the bird stretched its wings upwards, exposing the underwing) were also mostly white. I'm pretty sure I noticed darker tips of the outer primaries. Upperparts (wings, back) were mostly dark (almost black), although there was a limited pattern of white in the scapulars that I did not discern - I focussed my attention mainly on the head area until I reached an identification and then turned my scope over to one of my companions (Austin Hess). The head was white except for a diffuse blackish ear spot and a blackish hindneck, nape and crown. The eye appeared black. The beak was black, very thin, and of medium length. The length was longer than the tiny bill of a Ross's Gull, and slightly shorter than a Bonaparte's Gull (although neither of these two were present for direct comparison). The length was similar to that of the Sabine's Gull, maybe slightly longer. While I was looking up juvenile Little Gull in my gull ID book (Howell and Dunn) to confirm my suspicion, my companions declared that it took flight. I did not see it well in flight, but another companion, David Wade, declared that he saw a white tail with a thin black terminal tail band, and white trailing edge of the inner wing.        

Similar Species Discussion

Juvenile Bonaparte's Gull is the most similar, but nape and crown are brown, not black, and shape is longer. Ross's Gull and Sabine's Gull also have a long, extended appearance, more tern like. Ross's has lighter brown upperparts and crown. Sabine's has a grayer nape and crown. All three have longer, more pointed wings.

Resources Used

Howell and Dunn; Sibley Guide - consulted briefly at the time of the observation.

Previous Experience

I've seen one juvenile Little Gull previously (Prewitt Reservoir, about 15 years ago, chased). I've never seen juvenile Bonaparte's or Ross's Gulls. I've seen many (dozens) of juvenile Sabine's Gulls. I've seen Little Gulls of other ages/plumages in Colorado, Massachusetts and France (less than 2 dozen total).
