Description of the Bird

This was a tiny gull, with very short thin, black bill, white forehead, dark cap, white face except for dark smudge in front of eye, the dark eye, and a dark auricular patch. The white extended to the sides of the neck where on the dorsal surface there was a dark collar that extended down to the sides of the white brest. While sitting on the water the back was mostly dark but I could see white mottling here and there. In flight, I could see a broad dark M patern formed from the wing tips to the bird's wrist, down the secondaries to the lower back contrasting with a litter broad triangle of lighter coloration posterior to the M. The tail was mostly white but in flight when the bird banked I could see a dark tail tip. The undersides of the wings in flight were light but dar at the wing tips.
Behaviors: Spend equal time flying and sitting on the water. When it sat it was always by itself. In flight it was mostly with Sabine's Gulls, one adult a few juveniles with which it was noticeabley smaller and the wings darker. On occasion, it banked and came down to the water to investigate sometimes pulling up and continuing its flight in the middle of the reservoir and sometimes landing.
Call: none
Plumage: junenile

Similar Species Discussion

Ross's Gull juvenile is very similar and at this distance difficult to separate save for the tail length and shape, pointed with black tip in juvenile Ross's Gull ( I have seen over 3,000 ROGUS at Barrow, AK, and of those a few were juveniles even though that plumage is rarely observed). ROGU has many of the same features including the dark crown, M on the dorsal surface in flight, dark back and white tail with a dark tip, but i believe I would have seen the pointed tail tip even at this great of a distance.

Bonaparte's is fairly similar but in fllight BOGU has less dark on the leading edge dorsum, the leading primaries so that the M is not complete and there is white along the legs of the M where it would be thick and dark in Little Gull like the bird at Chatfield.

Resources Used
