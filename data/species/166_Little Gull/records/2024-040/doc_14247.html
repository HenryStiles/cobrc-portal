Description of the Bird

Brandon Percival discovered the bird and reported it on September 17.   Today, the bird was on calm water, with a very light breeze.   Occaisional boat traffic eventually caused it to fly further away from original sighting.   In flight was able to see the "M" pattern distinctive to this species.   Before flying, it was paddling around and pecking continuously at the water.   Able to observe and photograph both profiles during this motion.   Able to get a direct size comparison when a large white gull swam up to and  bumped into it in the water.   I have a fuzzy photo of the two gulls from right before that encounter.

Plumage closely matches The Sibley Guide to Birds, 2nd Edition of a Juevenile; small black bill, dark wing backs, black cap and black ear patch. 

Similar Species Discussion

I considered this bird much darker on the back and neck than either  a juvenile Bonaparte or Ross's Gull

Previous Experience

My second sighting of a Little Gull, both with this plumage type.   Familiar with Bonaparte, only have guidebook knowledge of Ross
