Description of the Bird

distant views of flying and bird perched on the water, sometimes in direct comparison to juvenile Sabine's Gull and adult Ring-billed Gull.

Short and thin, dark bill. In flight a black "W" seen on wings. Black terminal band on tail. Black on head extending down to shoulder was distinctive.  Compared to nearby juvenile Sabine's Gull, slightly smaller on the water.  In flight, a black area just behind the head noticeable.  Wingtips thinly edged in black.  Rounded wings in flight and seemingly buoyant flight similar to tern flight at times.

Similar Species Discussion

The only bird that comes close is juvenile Ross's Gull which has similar markings on head and back but that species has a black wedge-shaped tail when it is in juvenal plumage.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Not much experience with this species, only the 3rd one I have seen. Lots of experience with Ross's gull but only seen one juvenile as they quickly molt out of juvenile plumage but saw it and photographed one at Barrow, Alaska, page 203 in August 1989 issue of Birding magazine.
