Description of the Bird

I arrived at West Fishing Road on Saturday, 5 October, before sunrise, looking for the previously found Little Gull, among other birds at Pueblo Reservoir.  Around 7am, I saw the juvenile plumaged Little Gull, flying over the water.  I quickly identifed the gull as a Little Gull, very small gull, black cap, dark dot, behind each eye, the blackish wings and back.  Small dark bill and white underparts, all seen while on the water, a little smaller than Sabine's Gull, that was nearby at times.  In flight, showed bold black M patten, black on the tip of tail.  See photos in flight.

Similar Species Discussion

All gulls are bigger.  Juvenile Ross's Gull, has pointed tail, not as bold wing markings in flight.
