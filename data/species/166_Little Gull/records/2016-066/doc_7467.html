Description of the Bird

A small juv. gull about half the size of nearby Ring-billed Gulls.  Body & head white with a dark partial collar wrapping around the neck and onto the shoulders.  The bird sported a large, isolated black cap, black eyes and small, thin black bill.  A large, black, round earspot behind and below each eye. Tail white with a wide black terminal band.

Underwings white;  Upperwings: Black outer primaries and primary coverts.  Inner primaries dark tipped with white.  Secondaries white, secondary coverts with black “bar” extending from bend of wing to mid-body.

Similar Species Discussion

Bonaparte's Gull is really the only contending gull - has white upper primaries, no large black cap and collar
Sterna terns not similar
Black Tern - more slender bodied, While it has a collar and earspot in juv plumage it does NOT have the wing pattern of a juv Little Gull
No other contenders

Resources Used

None needed.  I have seen several Little Gulls (in this plumage) in Colorado over the last 30 years.

Previous Experience

I have seen hundreds of Bonaparte's Gulls in all plumages and several Little Gulls in a variety of plumages.
