Description of the Bird

There were two individuals, with very similar markings.

I observed the birds on the water, and in flight.

On the water, they had a slender profile, with a thin, black bill.

Their facial markings reminded me of a juvenile Red-necked Phalarope. They had a dark cap, a white forehead, a bold but narrow black patch running back from the eye, and a broad white supercilium.

The nape was dark, as were nearly the entire upperparts and wings. The wings were somewhat mottled, with white visible in one area near the middle of the folded wings.

The wing tips were dark. When one of the birds preened, I could see a narrow black terminal band on the tail. The tail appeared to be slightly forked. The underparts were entirely white.

In flight, I noted a tiny gull with narrow wings, clear white underparts, and a bold black shoulder mark. The wings had a bold, black, relatively narrow "W" on a light background.
Behaviors: They flew around over the water, occasionally landing on the water and then later taking off (i.e. typical gull behavior)
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

These gulls were much smaller than Ring-billed Gulls, so the main other possibilities are:
Bonaparte's
Black-headed
Ross'
Franklin's
Sabine's
The bold black "W" mark on the upper wing surface rules out all except perhaps Bonaparte's and Ross' Gulls. The forked tail rules out Ross' Gull. Juvenile Bonaparte's Gull (an unlikely plumage on Sept. 18) is ruled out by the black cap and the prominence of the black shoulder mark.
