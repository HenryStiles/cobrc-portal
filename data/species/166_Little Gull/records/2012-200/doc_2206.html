Description of the Bird

The entire time that I watched the bird, it was floating with a group of Bonaparte's Gulls. The Little Gull was noticeably smaller than the surrounding Bonaparte's Gulls. The Little Gull's bill was entirely black. The top of its head had a decent amount of black coloring on it, unlike the Bonaparte's Gulls. The Little Gull had a dark eye with some dark feathering immediately adjacent to the eye. It had a decent amount of white on its face, except for the black spot behind its eye. The back of the Little Gull was mostly gray, but the folded wings were very black, especially the carpal area. The carpal was much darker and striking than the Bonaparte's Gulls'. The Little Gull's primaries were black. I just saw a little bit of the body under the tail and it was white. The sides of the bird were white and under the chin was white.
Behaviors: Floating with other Bonaparte's Gulls. This is apparently a common practice of Little Gulls.
Call: No call or song heard
Plumage: Firts winter

Similar Species Discussion

Bonaparte's Gulls are similar, but the Little Gull was distinctly smaller thereby eliminating any other species of Gull that is in North America. The other distinguishing features were the dark cap and the extremely dark carpal bar. With all three of these ID features, we could eliminate all other species of Gulls.

Resources Used

Dave Leatherman really helped me see all the distinguishing features. Along with help from other birders, I used a Sibley Guide to Birds.

Previous Experience

I have never seen a Little Gull before, but I have seen Bonaparte's Gulls several times in the past.
