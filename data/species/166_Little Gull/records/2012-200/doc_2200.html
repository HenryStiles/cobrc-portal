Description of the Bird

Very small gull (smaller than accompanying Bonaparte's Gull) with thin black bill, white head with small black auricular spot, and black patch on crown, with gray nape. Underparts completely white, including underwings. Saddle pale gray. Tail mostly white with black terminal band observed in flight. Wings with heavy black markings, forming the letter "M" when spread, and white trailing edge (seen in flight).
Behaviors: Flying over lake (hunting), and sitting on lake surface.
Call: none
Plumage: juvenal/first basic

Similar Species Discussion

Immature Bonaparte's Gull is larger, has less bold black markings on spread wing, with black bar along trailing edge of wings. Immature Ross's is larger with obvious wedge-shape tail. Immature Black-legged Kittiwake is larger, with heavier, yellow bill, and darker gray mantle, and black nuchal bar. These three similar species all lack black patch on cap. Terns would have longer bills, thinner more pointed wings.
