Description of the Bird

Adult with full black head seen with several Bonaparte's and Franklin's gulls. Seen at a fair distance, such that distinction from Bonaparte's was subtle while at rest on the water, and obvious only when the bird was in flight. At rest, looked similar to Bonaparte's in size, mantle shade, and head color; differed mostly in slightly stubbier wings with more distinct white primary tips. In flight, strikingly different form Bonaparte's, with conspicuously blackish underwings framed by neat white trailing edge and tip.
Behaviors: Seen resting on water the water, and also flying low to the water periodically.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult breeding
