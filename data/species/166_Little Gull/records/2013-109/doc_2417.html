Description of the Bird

This bird was a tiny gull, similar in size to the Bonaparte's Gulls with which it cohorted. The bird exhibited a bouyant flying style, frequently touching the water, then flying up on a sharp angle. Bird would fairly frequently turn toward and away from observers. With underwing visible, bird showed dark color with pale edges, giving an overall more rounded appearance of shape to the wings. As bird showed upperwing, surface was pale gray, again with pale wingtips. As light hit the bird well, dark hooded head was appreciated. Bird too distant to view bill color or presence or absence of eye arcs. Dark underwings were distinct, allowiing the bird to be tracked readily in a spotting scope or pair of binoculars.
Behaviors: Feeding, with bouyant almost butterfly-like flight apparent. Similar flight to cohort of about 15 Bonaparte's Gulls. Ring billed gulls in area flew in a more direct pattern, without the ups and downs to the waterr's surface. No other gull species identified in area.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Nearby Bonaparte's gulls showed similar flight and feeding patterns, but wingtips were obviously dark, and underwing was light. When these gulls showed upperwing, hooded appearance could be appreciated, birds were pale mantled, butthis angle also allowed dark wingtips to be seen. Franklin's gulls excluded by paleness of mantle in all gulls present, also lack of black and white on wingtips typical of that species.
