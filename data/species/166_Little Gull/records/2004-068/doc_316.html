Description of the Bird

A very small gull, smaller than Bonaparte's present, the bird was mostly gray and white. The head was white with a diffuse grayish-black crown vaguely connected to a black ear spot. The eyes were dark and the very small bill was black. The mantle was pale gray, a touch paler than that of the nearby Bonaparte's Gulls. The tail and underparts were entirely unmarked white.

The upperwing was pale gray, though the remiges, particularly the primaries, were a darker gray with a wide, white trailing edge of even length virtually throughout the wing, getting wider only at the very wingtip (P9 and P10). The only black on the upperside of the wing was the basal 2/3 of the leading edge of P10, that feather also having a very extensive white tip (about 1/5) the feather length beyond the corresponding primary covert. There was no white wedge in the outer wing as on Bonaparte's and Black-headed gulls (and other species).

The underwing lesser and median coverts were whitish with a variable gray cast -- not the dark gray to black typical of adults of the species (see below). The underwing greater coverts (primary and secondary) and the undersides of the remiges were the same smoky gray, though the remiges had extensive white tips forming a wide, white trailing edge (as on the upper side).

The underwing was aberrant for the species and I don't know the cause. Some references mention adult-like birds without black wing linings, but suggest that they are younger adults. However, there was no other indication in the bird's plumage that it was a sub-adult (e.g., it had no black spots in the wingtip). Some suggested that it might be leucistic, but no feather tracts, other than the wing linings, were oddly colored.

For a small gull, the wingtip shape was different, being quite rounded rather than the angled, pointed wingtip of all other small gull species that I have seen (Black-headed, Bonaparte's).
Behaviors: flying circuits of the pond, occasionally dropping to the water's surface to secure some morsel or other
Call: none heard
Plumage: Basic possibly Definitive

Similar Species Discussion

Bonaparte's and Black-headed gulls are ruled out by wing shape, wingtip pattern, and underwing coloration. Ross's Gull is eliminated by tail shape, wide, white trailing edge to primaries all the way out to P10, and the lack of a wider, white trailing edge to the inner pp and outer ss.
