Habitat

Rural backyard with mixed deciduous, conifer trees, lilacs, open field and stream nearby

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8.5 X 42 swarovski bins and 500 mm lens
Distance:  10 to 15 ft.
Light:  Very good, clear with some scattered clouds. Bird was in the bright sun on several occasions during the middle of the day

Description of the Bird

Oriole with all black hood extending to "v" in front, orange belly, orange on back, distinct white wing bar, and orange shoulder patch. Orange outer tail coverts. Sharp, dark, slender bill
Behaviors: Bird was feeding at oranges and attempting to feed and compete with Bullock's Orioles. Appeared several times throughout the day. Each time it would either go to the feeder the Bullock's prefer and eat from oranges in a similar fashion, stretching to reach feeders, hanging with it's feet in some cases to eat oranges.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Was seen with Bullock's Orioles and Black-headed Grosbeaks. Eliminated from Bullock's due to full black hood and lack of black eye-line and orange auriculars. Orange shoulder patch, white wing bars as opposed to white wing panel. Slightly smaller than Bullock's
Eliminate from Black-headed Grosbeak due to beak shape and size, and coloration patterns of birds.

Previous Experience

Bullock's Orioles and Black-headed Grosbeaks nest in our yard. Had Baltimore last year and have seen it in Cost Rica, Oklahoma
