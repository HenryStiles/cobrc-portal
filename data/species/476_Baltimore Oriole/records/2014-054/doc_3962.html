Description of the Bird

The bird was bright orange underneath with a completely black head and back.  First thought was male Black-headed Grosbeak which would seem much more likely for the area, but the bird had a long, sharp-looking dark bill and black wings with a slim white wing bar. There was also a v shaped orange area on the upper part of the wing and he appeared black on top and orange underneath.  The bird was foraging in very small shrubs, sometimes turning nearly upside down while doing so. It visited several different plants and never seemed very concerned with our presence.  The bird was silent while we viewed it. 

Similar Species Discussion

Adult male Bullock's Oriole was eliminated by the completely dark head and different wing pattern (less white on wing on our bird and orange shoulder patch on our bird).  Scott's Oriole was eliminated by color (it is yellow, this bird was bright orange underneath).  Adult male Orchard Oriole is more of a dark rusty orange while this bird was just bright orange.  The slim, sharp bill on this bird eliminated adult male Black-headed Grosbeak which has some of the same colors.
