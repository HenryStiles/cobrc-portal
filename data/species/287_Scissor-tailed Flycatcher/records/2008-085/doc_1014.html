Description of the Bird

Bird was sitting on fence wire along the north side of road Co 128. Immediate recognition of bird as it sat on wire with long tail hanging down. Realising what I had had just seen, we drove a short ways down road to find a turnaround spot and headed back. As we approached the bird it flew across in front of car again revealing the two long tail feathers as it flew past on its rather moth like flight. Bird landed on fence right below the car about 20-25 feet away. Lovely pale grey breast and head, dark eye. Small head. I believed the bird to be a female because I did not think that the tail was as long as usual as in an adult male. Unfortunately, we did not have any binoculars or camera with us. I headed home to call hotline and abouut half way there realised I could call Rachel Hopper and she would put it on the hotline, which she did. Paula Hansley showed us in a short while and we continued to look for the bird but could not refind it. Later David Waltman and Cheryl found the bird in apprioximately the same place at 7:30 p.m. It was 5:30 pm when we spotted the bird.
Behaviors: Used the wire strands of the fence for perching. Flew out flycatching revealing the long black tail feathers . Funny moth-like flight patterns that are well known to me from hundreds of observations in Oklahoma and Texas.
Call: No call or song heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Definately not a Western Kingbird as there were some in area and no confusion as to identity. Have seen hundreds of Scissor-tailed Flycatchers in Oklahoma and Texas and have never had any difficulty in their differentiation from other slightly similar species such as western kingbird or even shrikes..
