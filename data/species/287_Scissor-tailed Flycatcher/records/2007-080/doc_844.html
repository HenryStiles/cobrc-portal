Description of the Bird

the bird was a flycatcher with a body slightly smaller than a kingbird, but with a very long tail that gave it a larger overall look. The overall shape was slimmer than a kingbird, again accentuated by the long tail. The bird had a tail about as long as it's body.

The head of the bird was a very pale gray, slightly darker on the nape. The underparts were also extremely pale gray, almost white. There was the faintest hint of pale pink on the lower belly, almost not noticeable. The bill and eye were black; the bill was short and thin. The wings were black, with bold white edges on the secondaries and coverts. The upperside of the tail was black, the underside was white. The back was pale-medium gray.
Behaviors: the bird was seen in some small trees adjacent to the road. For the entire observation it sat on branches near the top of the trees and did not fly.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

all other species were ruled out by the combination of size, extremely long tail, flycatcher shape, and gray color with black wings.

Fork-tailed Flycatcher was ruled out by the lack of a black cap, white underside to the tail, less contrasting plumage, etc.
