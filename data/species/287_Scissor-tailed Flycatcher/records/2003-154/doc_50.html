Description of the Bird

The bird had a body similar to that of a Western Kingbird, but with a very long tail.

Its head was very similar to that of a Western Kingbird, with a similar shade of gray on the face, and a similar sized dark bill.

The back was a light brownish gray.

The wings were darker than the back, unmarked.

The throat and breast were whitish, unmarked.

The belly had a strong pink wash.

The tail was completely forked, and was very long.

When the bird was perched, the underside of the tail was all dark, except for white from half way to the outer quarter (reading my notes, I'm not sure if "half way" was in the longitudinal or transverse direction)

As the bird displayed in flight, it opened its tail wide (scissors-like).
Behaviors: It acted like a large flycatcher - perching on a branch, flying up into the air to catch insects, and then returning to a perch.
Call: not recorded
Plumage: adult breeding plumage

Similar Species Discussion

The bird's size, posture, and behavior immediately establishes it as a tyrant flycatcher. The long tail rules out all North American species except Scissor-tailed and Fork-tailed Flycatcher. The pale back and pinkish wash to the belly rule out Fork-tailed.
