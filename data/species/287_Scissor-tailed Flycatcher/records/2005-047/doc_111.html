Description of the Bird

A Kingbird-sized flycatcher with an exceptionally long tail flew up to and perched on a fence wire. The bird was mostly whitish-gray below, with a salmon wash to the lower underparts, pale gray on the upperparts with a dark gray line through a black eye, small black bill, and a forked black-and-white tail about equal in length (or slightly greater) to the bird. Length of the body was estimated at 7 inches; thus total length was estimated at 14-16 inches.
Behaviors: Perched on a wire Kingbird-style with frequent sallies presumably for catching insects. Alternated between perching on the fence wire and on low vegetation (weed-like vegetation) a few inches from the ground.
Call: not calling
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Only two other species would have very long "scissor" tail and these would be Fork-tailed Flycatcher (which is dark gray and blackish on the upperparts rather than pale gray, and lacks salmon wash to lower underparts), and hybrid Scissor-tailed Flycatcher x tyrannid kingbird (such as Western Kingbird) whould would probably show some yellowish to underparts and lack the very long tail.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Extensive experience with Scissor-tailed Flycatcher during multiple birding trips to Texas in the early 1980s, and to El Salvador in the late 1980s, where the species is an abundant spring migrant, and with a vagrant individual in Massachusetts in the early 1980s and with a nesting pair in Prowers County, CO in the current decade.

Experience with Fork-tailed Flycatcher in Massachusetts (a vagrant individual in the late 1970s) and in Argentina in 2003 where they are abundant.

I have no experience with hybrid tyrannid flycatchers; one photo I have seen in the past of a Scissor-tailed Flycatcher x Western Kingbird lacked the very long tail.
