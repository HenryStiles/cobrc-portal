Habitat

ag land and ranch house yard with multiple plains cottonwood trees, adjacent black-tailed prairie dog town and adjacent fallow fields. There is a large irrigation canal to the north about 250 feet.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica APO 77mm scope with 32x wideangle lens
Distance:  closest about 120 feet
Light:  sometimes good and sometimes backlit

Description of the Bird

Actually this isn't so critical because it is a straightforward I.D. The very long deeply forked tail and pink underparts with the gray head and back and dark wings, eye and bill make the choice only Scissor-tailed Flycatcher. The tail has a white edge on the outer rectrices with black tips. The tips almost looked similar to those of most motmots species but were not quite racketed. The sex of the bird is really an unknown, but I was leaning towards a short-tailed male.
Behaviors: Active. Perched 4 times on various open perches including powerlines, guy wires, and fence wires. Also seen perched in a plains cottonwood tree. At times actively flycatching at which time it arched its tail up, spread out to show the deep fork in it.
Call: calls heard sounded like pik pik and also kapik
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Fork-tailed Flycatcher - has a dark cap and shows no pink underneath.
