Description of the Bird

This Tyrannus type flycatcher was grayish white over-all with dark wings and tail and pink underwings, flanks and belly. The bill, legs and eyes were black. The tertials, greater and secondary scapular feathers were edged in white.The black and white tail was about 1 to 1.25 times the body length.
Behaviors: This bird was found flycatching in a cottonwood tree in the middle of a farm hedge row.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Adult probable male

Similar Species Discussion

The combination of tail length and pink markings elinimate all other Tryannus flycathers.
