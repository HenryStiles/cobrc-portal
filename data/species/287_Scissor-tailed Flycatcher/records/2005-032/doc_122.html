Description of the Bird

A mostly white Tyrranus flycatcher that had an extremely long tail. Grayish wings and back. Slamon-orange wing lining. The tail was mostly black with white tips on most of the feathers. Bill black
Behaviors: The bird flew past, landed on a small shrub for a couple of minutes and flew over to some cottonwoods and disappeared.
Call: None noted
Plumage: one definitive adult

Similar Species Discussion

The only other species in N America with such a long tail is Fork-tailed Flycatcher. FTFL has a black cap and black on the back.
