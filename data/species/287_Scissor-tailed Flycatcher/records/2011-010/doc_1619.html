Open grassland which is part of Boulder Open Space.

Optics:  10x binoculars.

Description of the Bird

Medium sized bird with a very long, forked tail. The head, upper back and throat were gray, with a dark eye and bill. The lower body was light colored, tinged with salmon pink. The wings were black with extensive white fringes to the feathers. The tail was also black with extensive white markings. When the bird flew, it revealed much brighter red markings between the forewing and the body, with much of the underwing a light pink.
Behaviors: The bird perched on the available fence lines and on the top of plants a few feet high. It would then fly off on a sally to chase an insect. If it caught one, or not, it would fly back again to a prominent perch, either to eat the insect or to look for another to chase.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The only other bird of this size with such a long tail is Fork-tailed Flycatcher. This bird didn't have a black head, dark back and tail, or a white breast.

Resources Used

Didn't need any help with this identification.

Previous Experience

Have seen Scissor-tailed Flycatchers in Colorado and Texas many times. Have seen Fork-tailed Flycatcher in Massachusetts and in Central America.
