Description of the Bird

the bird was a large flycatcher, with a body a little smaller than a Western Kingbird, but with very long tail feathers giving the bird a larger look overall.

The head, back, and chest were pale gray. The bird had strikingly bold pink coloring on the underwings and belly. The flight feathers were dark, contrasing with the paler back and head. The bird had very long outer tail feathers, about a third again the length of the body, and much shorter central tail feathers. At times in flight the tail was spread to give the bird a "fork-tailed" look. The tail was mostly black, with much white in the outer tail feathers.

Assumed to be male because of the brightness of the pink coloration and length of the tail.
Behaviors: The bird was only seen in flight, first found a couple of hundred yards out as a small speck, but eventually flying right overhead allowing for stunning looks, before dissapearing to the north.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of bird were ruled out by the combination of the very long, forked tail, bright pink underwings and belly, and otherwise pale gray coloration with dark wings. Fork-tailed Flycatcher was ruled out by the pale head, pink coloration on the belly and underwings, and white in the tail. Western, Cassin's, and Eastern Kingbirds were ruled out by the pink belly and underwing, very long, forked tail, and in the case of Eastern, pale head.
