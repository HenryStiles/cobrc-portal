Description of the Bird

This male scissor-tailed flycatcher was sitting on the north/south fence that parallels Weld County Road 41 1/2 approx. 0.2 miles south of Weld County Road 38. The wind was from the northwest and he was sitting facing due east. We (my coworker and I) passed him and I initially thought he was a shrike. I knew something wasn't right about that ID, so we backed up our vehicle the 50-80 meters to where he still perched. The truck was immediately next to him, just 2 meters away, and he sat just as calmly as could be, looking at us, and of course, looking around for bugs. Being that this was my first time spotting one, I wanted to confirm my opinion by seeing him in flight to get a better sense of his movement, body language, and coloration. We watched him for 10 mins. After all that time, it donned on us that we should be getting photos (duh!). My co-worker stepped out of the truck on the driver's side and came around the front of the vehicle and our handsome fellow decided it was time to move on and gracefully took to the air, showing his splendid split tail and pale-pink flanks. He flew with the wind at 10-15 meters off the ground until going out of site.

Similar Species Discussion

As noted, at first glance it was thought that it was a loggerhead shrike. This was easily discounted given the very close proximity and lengthy amount of time given to observe him. While watching him, we were able to pull up images on our smart phones. He was clearly not as dark gray, lacked the distinct black mask and black and white primaries, and of course, his long slender tail.
