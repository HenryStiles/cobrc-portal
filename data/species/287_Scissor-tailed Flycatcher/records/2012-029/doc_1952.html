Habitat

Open grassy/weedy area with nearby cottonwoods, willows, shrubs and marsh

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss 10x40 binocs, quite old but very good
Distance:  Approx 80 feet
Light:  Overcast; position of sun at roughly 300 degrees in relation to observer (bird at 360 degrees obviously), and high overhead. Light conditions allowed for a decent photo

Description of the Bird

Very long-tailed passerine with salmon-colored vent and sides, dark wings, white breast and throat and whitish-grey head. All black bill not particularly long or short, nor stout or thin.
Behaviors: 1st noticed perched on a powerline, then flew down to ground and apparently caught a food item before returning to perch on wire where photo was then obtained.
Call: None observed
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Distinctive species, but eliminations made as follows: distinguished from Fork-tailed Flycatcher by lack of black cap and salmon wash on sides and vent, and from other kingbirds by long tail and salmon color.

Resources Used

Essentially none. Sibley Guide to Birds briefly referred to in an attempt to sex the bird, but otherwise not needed

Previous Experience

3rd observation of STFL in Colorado. FTFL observed in Panama (2010) and Costa Rica (2009) in large flocks. STFL observed on these trips as well. Somewhat similar kingbird species observed frequently in Colorado.
