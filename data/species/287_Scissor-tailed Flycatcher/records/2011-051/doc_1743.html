Habitat

Chain Link fence adjacent to road and lake. Cattail marsh south along the fence. Open area with small trees and shrubs for the most part. Small grove/cluster of trees to the south of open space.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Vortex Stokes DLS 8x42
Distance:  observed at various distances from 15 - 40 yards.
Light:  Light conditions did not impact the quality of the observation as I was able to view the bird from several different angles (both in shade and direct light).

Description of the Bird

Light gray bird with almost white head and very long black and white tail. When perched, faint salmon color on the belly. When the bird sallied out to flycatch, the bright pink/orange underwing coverts were very conspicious as well as a black and white pattern on the tail. Overall size and shape (except for the long tail) were similar to Western kingbird when perched on fence wire.
Behaviors: The bird was perched on a wire fence separating Marston Reservoir from the houses in Twin Shore Subdivision. He flew out to fly catch, then returned to the same perch or close by. I observed the bird sally out and return to the fence. I also observed the bird use a small bush (4 -5 feet tall) as a perch to sally forth.

The area was somewhat open with a road adjacent to to Marston Lake, fence line, open space area with small trees/shrubs, and a cattail marsh. Small grove of trees about 100 yards distant.
Call: did not call
Plumage: adult plumage

Similar Species Discussion

I knew the bird was a Scissor-tailed Flycatcher - the long tail is distinctive as well as the bright pink/salmon underwing coverts when it sallied forth.
