Description of the Bird

Easy to identify, this long-tailed bird (tail approaching nine inches) had a face including chin and throat, white as was breast, salmon pink belly. The tails are distinctive and in flight they spread and scissored while the bird foraged. Dark wings and bill contrast with of light gray body.

See photo.
Behaviors: Foraged actively in a mostly bare ground winter feeding pasture for cattle. Perhaps because of the high density of cow and horse droppings, there appeared to be plenty of insects on the ground for the bird to catch.

Perched mostly on low bushes but also on lower limbs of trees.
Call: none heard
Plumage: breeding

Similar Species Discussion

The only species of flycatcher with long tails like this bird, at least in North America, is Fork-tailed Flycatcher. which has an all-dark head contrasting with its darker gray back and all white, with no salmon underneath. Fork-tailed's tail is also darker and lacks the white edges of a Scissor-tailed.
