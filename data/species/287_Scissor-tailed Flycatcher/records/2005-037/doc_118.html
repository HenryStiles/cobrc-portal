Description of the Bird

Very pale gray, long-tailed flycatcher. The entire body was a very pale gray, except for a pinkish wash to the lower belly, a deeper red on the body that "wrapped" around the bend of the folded wing (exposed auxillaries); the wings dark brown with large pale edging to the greater and median coverts; the dark tail showed white on the outer tail feathers, and exceeded in length that of the bird's body; the eyes were dark; the bill was short, thin, pointed, and entirely dark.
Behaviors: Observed flycatching from a dead woody perch approximately one foot off the ground in the middle of an open weedy field.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: adult
