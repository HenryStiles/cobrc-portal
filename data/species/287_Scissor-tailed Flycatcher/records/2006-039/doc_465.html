Description of the Bird

Head, nape, and sides of neck gray with darker gray lores. Dark eye. Dark brown bill. Whitish underparts with somewhat pale salmon-colored axillaries, flanks and undertail coverts. Brown wings. Inner rectrices black, outer rectrices white. Rectrices progressively longer from inner to outer creating a forked tail. Legs blackish. The pale salmon color along with tail length indicates that this bird is not an adult male. This is either a second year male or a female bird.
Behaviors: Actively flycatching from both a fence and from high bare branches in trees.
Call: not heard
Plumage: See description

Similar Species Discussion

Fork-tailed flycatcher has dark cap and lacks the salmon coloration of a Scissor-tailed Flycatcher. Long tail separates this species from all other NA birds.
