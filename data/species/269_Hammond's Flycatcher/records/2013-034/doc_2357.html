Description of the Bird

Bill and Dan Maynard found a small flycatcher, early in the afternoon on private property on the Penrose Christmas Bird Count. Bill called me, and wondered if I could come over and try to get photos of the bird, since empidonax flycatchers are not expected in Colorado during the winter time. I came over there with Aaron Shipe, and we were able to see the bird and photograph it. The bird appeared to be a Hammond's Flycatcher, short mostly dark bill, a little tear drop to the back of the eyes, grayish head, yellow/greenish under tail.
Behaviors: feeding over a stream
Call: none
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Well all the empidonax flycatchers, are similar. I feel like everything listed above eliminate other species. The photos were looked at by many, and there seems no doubt that the bird is a Hammond's Flycatcher.
