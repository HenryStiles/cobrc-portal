Habitat

Densly wooded area at the top end of a small, narrow canyon with very dense, large fallen logs, trees, stumps and vines, a good spot for finding Winter Wren in December.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10x42 bins
Distance:  As close as 30 feet
Light:  At times very good, sometimes in dense shade.

Description of the Bird

Empidonax by size and eyering. The bird was short-billed with bill also being quite narrow. I made sure to look closely at the bill. The head was gray and contrasting with the back and belly. The wings appeared long, and there was a noticeable longish primary projection. The tail was longish, not short like in Least Flycatcher. The breast was yellowish.
Behaviors: Foraged low along small spring, flycatching sometimes and also catching some food items on the ground within a few feet of the bank.
Call: None heard
Plumage: basic

Similar Species Discussion

Trail's type flycatcher - this bird was way too small, especially the bill, for one of these.

Gray Flycatcher - Bill was way too short for one of these.

Dusky Flycatcher - the contrasting gray head and the thin and short bill eliminated this one.

"Western" Flycatcher - the shape of the teardrop eyering eliminated this species pair.

Yellow-bellied - not yellow enough on breast, shape of eyering wrong, primary projection wrong, bill too short and too narrow for this one.

Resources Used

None

Previous Experience

This is a very common migrant at Chico Basin Ranch in the fall where they come low to the ground during migration. See hundreds there and some more during breeding mostly up very high. Also was fairly common breeder on my plots where i did breeding line transect surveys in the Sierra National Forest where they could be compared to Dusky Flycatchers.
