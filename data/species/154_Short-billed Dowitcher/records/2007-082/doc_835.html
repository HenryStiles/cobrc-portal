Description of the Bird

A medium-sized shore bird with a long, straight dark gray bill that was black towards the tip. Crown was light reddish-brown with fine black streaking. There was an obscure whitish supercilium. Upper breast and neck were orangish with fine spotting on sides of neck. Flanks were uniformly spotted with black on light orangish base. Belly faded to off-white. Back feathers were dark centered with off-white edges. Tail barring seemed uniform between white and black. Tail was decidedly light grayish above when bird flushed away from nearby fishermen - seen well twice. Bird seemed trim and not hunch-backed like LBDOs.
Behaviors: Feeding in shallow water at lake edge. Feeding with typical sewing machine like motion.
Call: The low pitched mellow "tututu" call that seemed to descend in pitch was heard twice when flushed by nearby fishermen.
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Call when flushed by fishermen was diagonistic. All other field marks confirmed that this was a Short-billed Dowitcher of Hendersonii subspecies. Long-billed would have appeared slightly longer legged, more hunch-backed and markings on sides of neck and flanks would have been bolder including some chevron-like marks. Also, tail would have been distinctly darker when bird was flushed.
