Description of the Bird

I observed 8 birds, but they all looked the same so will describe them as one. Body and bill shape and size identical to Long-billed Dowitcher as far as I could tell. The main differences were in the plumage. One of the first things I noticed was the mottled white and rust underparts with black splotching (not barring) on the sides and flanks - more so barred on the flanks, but definitely spotted elsewhere. There was variation in the amount of white to the underparts between individual birds, but for the most part the belly and vent region were more extensively white than the breast. All of the birds retained some gray basic-plumaged feathers on the wings; otherwise, the upperpart feathering was boldly fringed in rust with black centers.
Behaviors: Actively foraging at the breakwater of a small suburban pond.
Call: none heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Long-billed Dowitcher is the only candidate to rule out. That species only very rarely retains basic-plumaged feathering in spring (all of these birds did so). LBDO also is a deeper rust color throughout the underparts, whereas these birds were variably more white below, especially the belly and undertail region. On the upperparts, LBDO typically has white tipped feathers, but the feathering of these birds was clearly and boldly rusty colored.
