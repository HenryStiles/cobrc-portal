Description of the Bird

This is to document a hendersoni adult Short-billed Dowitcher from Pueblo County in the Spring.

Dowitcher by long bill length.

Bill, as compared to LBDO behind this bird, is broad-based, blunt-tipped, and with a noticeable kink downwards about at the proximal 1/4 of bill.

Breast with light orange or light salmon wash. Flanks and undertail coverts with spots and only a couple bars.

Back with feather centers dark surrounded by orange that is less orange than that of LBDO back feathers. Belly slightly less orange than other ventral feathers.

Legs yellow green and longish, but noticeably shorter than the LBDO's legs. Neck light orange with only light spotting, unlike the barring on LBDO.
Behaviors: This birds was in with a flock of LBDOs. The two birds in the photo slept the entire time, while the remaining LBDO flock of about 9 other birds foraged about 1 foot from the bank in shallow water.
Call: characteristic call not heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Long-billed Dowitcher

See comments under description but...

Look at the photo for these comparative notes...

SBDO (front) has shorter legs, fewer bars on flanks, lighter orange surronding the black centered back feathers, lighter orange wash on belly, bill with noticeably wider base, and not curved throughout length like LBDO but kinked near the tip, about a quarter of the way in from the blunt tip, which appears a bit blunter than in LBDO, with which I compared nearby. Another very noticeable feature in the photo is the flat back of the SBDO compared to the hunched back of the LBDO.

Resources Used

I was almost positive that I had a hendersoni SBDO so when I got home I compared the photos found in The Shorebird Guide which convinced me that I had a confirmed hendersoni SBDO.

Previous Experience

Although I have seen a number of juvenal SBDOs which are straight forward as far as ID, this is the first alternate plumaged bird that I IDed as a hendersoni SBDO.
