Description of the Bird

A medium-sized shore bird with a long, straight, dark gray bill that was darker at the tip. Crown was brownish with fine streaking. There was an obscure eye line. Upper breast and neck were orangish with fine spotting on sides of neck and distinct, but smudged barring on breast. Belly was off-white. Flanks were strongly marked with blackish barring on orangish-red brown and off-white. Undertail coverts were similar to flanks but showed more white. Back was fairly dark and strongly mottled. The feather edges of the scapulars were rusty and grayish without any white. The tail was black and white with the bars of approximately equal width.
Behaviors: Birds were feeding at edge of lake with typical up-and-down probing of dowitchers. They stopped feeding as we approached to about thirty feet.
Call: Silent while feeding. Call was a fairly low-pitched and harsh ke-to-to-to, when flushed.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The breast coloring and markings; the white belly; the edges to the scapulars; and the call all identified this bird as a Short-billed Dowitcher of the griseus subspecies.
