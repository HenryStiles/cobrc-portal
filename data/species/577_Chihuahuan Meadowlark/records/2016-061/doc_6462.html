Description of the Bird

Found by "bzzzzrt" call so different from that given by WEME. I really could not describe bird better than it is shown in photos. The white pattern on tail fits Eastern juv better than Western juv (see discussion and photos of specimens at https://www.flickr.com/photos/36088296@N08/albums/72157658379567708.

The lack of dark along veins of tail feathers would strongly indicate liliana subspecies. 

The extensive dark on uppertail coverts was visible in field, as was contrasty head (more so than many nearby WEME). The throat was distinctly yellow and malars entirely white. 

Similar Species Discussion

WEME: See description. Eliminated by call, tail pattern, uppertail covert pattern, contrasting head pattern, rather white malars. Also, Pyle felt that scaps and back color EAME like rather than WEME like
