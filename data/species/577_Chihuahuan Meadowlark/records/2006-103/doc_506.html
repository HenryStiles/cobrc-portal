Description of the Bird

This Meadowlark was a chunky robin sized bird with a short tail and long flesh colored legs. Its bill was fairly long & dagger shaped with a sharp pointed tip. Upper mandible was darker than lower.

CROWN: Narrow white median stripe flanked by thick black stripe on each side of white stripe.

FACE: Dark eye with black eyeline, whitish supercilium, and yellow lores. Auriculars were grayish off-white and unstreaked. Lower half of auriculars bordered by thick white malar stripe. The malar stripe sharply contrasted with narrow yellow throat patch. The white malar stripe made it easy to see that the yellow on the throat did not extend into the auriculars.

UNDERPARTS: Breast had a broad black V. Upper belly was yellow. Lower belly & undertail coverts were white. Flanks were white with heavy dark streaks, especially on upper flanks.

UPPERPARTS: Mottled brown back had dark brown streaks in center, becoming somewhat scaly looking on sides of back. Wings were short, mottled brown, wtih assorted dark speckles and bars on the coverts. Primary tips were dark and barely extended to upper tail coverts.

TAIL: Narrow central tail feathers were brown with horizontal dark barring. Broad white outer tail feathers covered more than half of upper surface of tail, and nearly all the under surface. White tail feathers were mostly unmarked except for a couple of diffuse spots, sometimes visible on under surface of tail. The broad white tail feathers and light auriculars of this Meadowlark suggest Lillian's subspecie.
Behaviors: Bird often perched on juniper or fence post repeating its "dreezt" note. It occasionally flew to ground and foraged.
Call: This bird mostly gave a repeated high-pitched burry "dreezt." In flight it gave a high-pitched burry "tititititititi." Occasionally gave a high drawling "seeyu, seeyehr."
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The following differences between W. Meadowlark(WEME) & E. Meadowlark (EAME) were observed:

VOICE: WEME also gives a single note chirp, but it's a husky lower-pitched "churk." Its rattle is also slower & lower-pitched than EAME. At no time did observed EAME give call notes or song suggesting WEME.

AURICULARS of WEMEs are streaked and often congeal into a dark "ear patch," in contrast to unstreaked auriculars flanked by white malar stripe of observed EAME. Yellow throat often extends into auriculars on WEME, especially males.

FLANKS on WEMEs are mostly spotted; on observed EAME the flanks were mostly streaked.

CROWN: Stripes of WEMEs are lighter & brownish versus the blackish crown stripes of EAMEs. The lighter crown stripes were especially noticeable on female WEMEs.

TAIL: Tails of WEMEs show broader dark central feathers and narrower white outer tail feathers than EAME. On WEMEs I observed the dark feathers covered at least half or more of upper tail surface. On some WEMEs brown edgings were noticed on edges of the white tail feathers. On observed EAME the white tail feathers were nearly unmarked.
