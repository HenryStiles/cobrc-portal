Description of the Bird

Two meadowlarks were a lot of white in the tail, and the face. The male giving call mentioned above. It looks like a pair, with a nest. Photos are attached of the male and female.
Behaviors: The male was first noticed on a juniper, then a fence post, and then in the grass. The female was mostly seen in the grass, with food in its mouth.
Call: The male gave a buzzy "bzeert" call many times. The female didn't make any noise.
Plumage: adult male and female

Similar Species Discussion

Western Meadowlark - dark face, more white in tail, does not give "bzeert" call.
