Description of the Bird

Meadowlark with white malar, pale back, and white face compared to dark eye stripe and crown stripe. Showed significant white in the tail; 3 full tail feathers pure white, plus a fourth tail feather mostly white, as expected on a Eastern Meadowlark of the Lilian's subspecies. 
Behaviors: Adult bird showed foraging behavior, picking insects to feed to the juveniles, which I also observed. The two juveniles seemed to wait for the adult to make the first move, and would softly call to the adult for more food as the above adult foraged. 

Similar Species Discussion

Western Meadowlark was considered first, but the tail patterning soon eliminated pure Western. White malar in addition to the white cheek and bold eye and crown stripe also eliminated WEME. A 'Magna' Eastern was also considered, but again the pale back and white in the tail appeared to agree more with the 'Lilian's' Eastern than the 'Magna' Eastern.

Resources Used

National Geographic Field Guide to Bird of North America, All About Birds by Cornell Lab of Ornithology, and the Colorado Field Ornithologists Facebook group.

Previous Experience

I have a bit of experience with Western Meadowlarks from watching them a lot at this patch. I bird McIntosh frequently and so am familiar with their behavior and coloration. This bird stood out from all the WEME that I've seen there. I have no experience with EAME, so my photos were sent off to many local and regional experts who confirmed this sighting.
