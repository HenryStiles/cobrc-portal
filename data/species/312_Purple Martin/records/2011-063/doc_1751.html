Description of the Bird

I was watching several Barn Swallows over an open area adjacent to Kodak SWA, when two distinctly larger swallows joined their feast. The male looked all blackish as the lighting was not such to show off its purple, and it was about 150 feet away. The female was similar in size to the male, had a pale belly with some dusky on sides and chest (not sure if throat was white or dusky), and it had a grayish collar around nape. Besides the nape collar, the upperparts looked uniformly dark. It reminded me of the birds I am used to seeing in Washington. Both birds had moderately forked tails, generally swallow shaped, flew like swallows, and were rather large in direct comparison with Barn Swallows. I watched them for about 45 seconds as they flew about as if they were pursuing insects with the Barn Swallows. Then they drifted off to the east. They did not fly like Eurasian Starlings bug-catching. The lighting was good enough to see the detail on the female moderately well.

Similar Species Discussion

There are no swallows the size of a Purple Martin that occur in the USA except as extreme vagrants. Eurasian Starlings are sometimes mistaken for Purple Martins, but the female had a pattern inconsistent with starling, and both birds did not fly like insect-hawking starlings and they had forked tails.
