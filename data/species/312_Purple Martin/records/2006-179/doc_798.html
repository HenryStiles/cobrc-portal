Description of the Bird

Originally found by David Leatherman, Duane Nelson arrived at the location of the Purple Martin house and immediately found two 2nd-cycle male Purple Martins perched atop the box. Eastern Purple Martins are rare on the Eastern Plains of Colorado and details of their occurrence, especially potential nesting attempts, is important. No females were observed associated with the two males and I do not think that breeding was attempted.

They were very large for swallows, being longer-winged and having slower wingbeats. The head, upperparts, wings, flanks, and tail were a mixed dark blue and gray. The throat on each bird was whitish-gray and blotchy, while the mid-breast, belly, and undertail coverts were white. The bill and irides were blackish. The tail was distinctly, but not deeply forked. They were both similar to adult females, except for the extensive amount of white (not gray) below. After taking a few photographs, the birds took flight and foraged through the neighborhood, calling often.
Behaviors: Perched on nest box and flying through neighborhood.
Call: A series of throaty, gurgly tchew-wews alternated by a pew, pew.
Plumage: 2nd cycle

Similar Species Discussion

Gray-breasted Martins are somewhat similar but are extensively more gray and have darker foreheads.
