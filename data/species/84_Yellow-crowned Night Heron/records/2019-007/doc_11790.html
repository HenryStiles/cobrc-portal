Description of the Bird

The bird was a chunky heron with a thick, dagger-like bill perched in a fallen tree with numerous branches preventing a clear view. On the first night, the bird sat with its head pulled down into its shoulders, preventing a good, clean look at the face, but we could see heavy black on the side of the face and a white or yellow head plume (exact color was difficult to assess in the dusk light). The bird had a streaked gray back typical of an adult Yellow-crowned Night-Heron. The bird did not move noticeably during our observation, which consisted mainly of moving from one vantage point to another trying to get the best looks and photographs possible. The next morning Derek went back to check on the bird’s continued presence and got a clear look at the bird’s head, including the yellow crown above a thick black eyestripe that met with a black chinstrap at the back of the head, leaving a small white stripe under the eye. No vocalizations were heard.

Similar Species Discussion

The only other bird with a similar size and shape is Black-crowned Night-Heron, which would have a solid black and gray back if an adult.  An immature Black-crowned Night-Heron would be brown or have brownish tones depending upon exact age.

Resources Used

We primarily drew upon our experience with the species, and both of us recognized the bird as a Yellow-crowned Night-Heron when we first saw it, despite the initially obstructed, back-only view. Upon realizing the scarcity of this species in Colorado, Derek checked the Sibley Birds V2 app to confirm the identification.

Previous Experience

As Easterners, both of us are fortunate to have had breeding colonies of Yellow-crowned Night-Herons in our home counties. We’ve each seen the species more than 40 times in a variety of locations and habitats, and have seen Black-crowned Night-Heron a comparable number of times. Derek has also had the opportunity to pick single Yellow-crowned Night-Herons out of large colonies of Black-crowned Night-Herons on at least two occasions.
