Description of the Bird

On May 6th the bird was standing still along the west shore of the pond.  On the 7th, it was in mostly the same position until it flew off to the east about a quarter of a mile and then perched in a tree further down on the golf course.
This is a mostly gray bird with a nice streaky pattern in the back and wings formed by off white wavy streaks.  It is a small to medium sized heron with a stocky body and a very thick, short neck.  The head is black with a horizontal light/white patch on the cheek.  The head is crowned with a light whitish yellow color.  It had a long plume on the back of the head.  The bill was moderately short and thick and of a gray color.

Similar Species Discussion

There really wasn't much consideration with other species aside from thinking it would be a Black-crowned when it was first noticed without binoculars or having a good look at it.  The moment it was seen better the head pattern and plummage pattern immediately showed it to be the YCNH.

Resources Used

I knew this one, but I did pop on to allaboutbirds.org afterwards just for verification.

Previous Experience

I've seen several of these in OK, AR, and CO, mostly adults.  I've seen thousands of BCNHs and other herons.
