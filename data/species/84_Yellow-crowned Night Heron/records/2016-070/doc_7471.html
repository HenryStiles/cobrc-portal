Description of the Bird

Walking along edge of marsh, feeding, neck held up and out like "normal" heron, not as is typical of BCNH. Neck looked longer than that of a BCNH, as did legs, imparting a rather different overall jizz. Bird was dark and grayish brown, grayer than BCNH. Underparts from throat to upper belly with more dark than light, as opposed to YCNH, which looks pale with brown streaks. Head more square, less elongated (hard for me to describe headshape differences between two species, but they do look somewhat different), than BCNH, with shorter and distinctly stouter bill. Face dull, darkish, no malar marking. Wing covert pattern showed dark feather center with narrow white edge on either side and small white spot at tip. Legs yellow-green.

Similar Species Discussion

Black-crowned Night Heron. BCNH has distinctly longer and proportionately more slender bill than this bird. BCNH browner (or warmer brown) rather than gray brown. BCNH lacks distinct white edges to wing coverts. Spotting on wigs of BCNH typically larger. Below, BCNH paler, with more striped appearance. BCNH squatter, shorter in neck and legs
