Description of the Bird

Juvenile flew in alongside an Adult Black-Crowned Night-Heron, landed on fallen branches over the lake. Stood on a branch for a while, and as a took a photo it got nervous so I backed away slowly and continued onward. At the time I assumed it was a Black-Crowned Night Heron, but photos showed it to be a Yellow-Crowned Night Heron.

Similar Species Discussion

Black-Crowned Night Heron is very similar, but this bird stood in a less hunched over posture, had more distinct streaks, almost completely dark bill, lacked white spots on the wing of a BCNH juvenile, instead had fine spots and lines. 

Resources Used

Looked in Sibley's bird guide, and look at other photos online of BCNH and YCNH. I also shared a photo with other birders, who agreed that it was a Juvenile YCNH

Previous Experience

I often see BCNH of all ages in the summer, and at the time hadn't bothered to check the species when I saw the bird until about a week later. I have previously seen one juvenile YCNH in the Bahamas 18 months prior to this, and have seen adults in Mexico in 2015.
