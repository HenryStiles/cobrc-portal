Habitat

The habitat is that of shallow ponds with extensive cattail marsh with some cottonwoods and Russian Olive.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Bushnell 10 X 50 Binoculars
Distance:  as close as 50 ft
Light:  The sky was partly cloudy with mid-day sun.

Description of the Bird

Size and shape: About 2 ft. tall, similar in shape to the Black-crowneds but slimmer and longer necked.
Bill: Stout, pointed and all dark in color.
Head: Face and crown heavily streaked grayish brown, darker on the crown.
Eye: Reddish orange.
Upper parts: Neck, back and wings grayish brown with fine white tips on the feathers.
Under parts: Neck and breast heavily streaked grayish brown becoming paler on the belly. Under wings grayish brown.
Legs: Long and yellowish
Behaviors: Behavior: The birds were observed standing at the edge of the marsh and as I approached for a better look eventually flew to the East side of the pond.
Call: NA
Plumage: Immature/juvinile

Similar Species Discussion

Black-crowned Night Heron. This species in immature plumage has larger white spots on wing coverts, a mostly yellow bill, shorter legs and a stouter overall look to the body shape.
