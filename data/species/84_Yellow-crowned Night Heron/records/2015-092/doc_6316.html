Description of the Bird

Adult. Continuing rarity found a few days prior. Photographed. Snoozing at NW corner of lake when I arrived, then later roused up and flew to SW corner where it was foraging. Unmistakable with bold black and white pattern on head (broad white forecrown with pale yellow cast tapering to point at rear crown, broad white line from below eye onto auriculars), fine white plume curling wispily on rear of crown, slaty gray neck and body, wings with dark to blackish feathers with white edges forming nice pattern. Yellow legs (with some dusky) looked longer than on nearby BCNH, longer necked when neck extended, bill appeared heavier to me. Eye somewhat less red with prominant pupil. Observed with Scott Somershoe.

Similar Species Discussion

Clearly a Night-Heron. ID of adult pretty obvious. Black-crowned has different head pattern, black back, paler unerparts, unpatterned wings, shorter neck
