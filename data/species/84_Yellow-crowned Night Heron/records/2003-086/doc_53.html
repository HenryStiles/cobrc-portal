Description of the Bird

I observed the bird as it stood on an exposed shoreline in the open. Nearby, a juvenile Black-crowned Night-heron stood in a similar location, making for a great comparison.

Both were small, heron-like birds, similar in size to a Snowy Egret, but much stockier.

The Yellow-crowned Night-heron had a thicker bill (relative to its length). The end of the bill was quite rounded; the bill did not have the dagger-like appearance and sharp point of a Black-crowned.

The bill of the Yellow-crowned was all dark (blackish or dark brown). The lower mandible of the Black-crowned was mostly yellow, except near the tip.

The back and folded wings of the Yellow-crowned were a dark chocolate brown, compared to the light brown of the Black-crowned.

Both birds had bold triangular white spots on their wings, but only the Yellow-crowned had white edges to the feathers.

Both birds had dark pupils with bold orange irises. They had yellowish legs and boldly streaked brown and pale underparts.
Behaviors: Standing on the shoreline of a wooded pond.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

With a juvenile Black-crowned Night-Heron nearby for comparison, I could quickly narrow the choices to small herons and egrets. The chunky posture further narrowed the options to a Night-Heron or an American Bittern. The drab coloration, dark bill, and lack of bold, colorful throat stripes ruled out a bittern. The differences between this bird and the nearby Black-crowned Night-Heron are discussed above under the bird description. In particular, the thicker, all dark, more rounded bill, darker back, and white feather edgings on the wings all helped with the ID of a Yellow-crowned Night-Heron.
