Habitat

a grove of live and dead cottonwoods, the latter killed by the falling water table caused by an adjacent gravel mine. The trees contained a large colony of breeding Great Blue Herons - 71 nests according to my count that year. The reservoir started flooding the grove in early May, and completely covered the ground by May 19, the date of the first observation of these birds

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Binoculars, camera
Distance:  50-100 ft
Light:  Good

Description of the Bird

I did not takes notes at the time. I remember it as a Night-heron by shape and size, black and white face stripes, and gray breast and belly. See Jan Justice's color slide for complete verification.
Behaviors: Perched in the heronry. Though seen a week apart, the bird(s) did not stay to breed in the heronry.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Black-crowned Night-Heron: different facial pattern, black back, white breast.
