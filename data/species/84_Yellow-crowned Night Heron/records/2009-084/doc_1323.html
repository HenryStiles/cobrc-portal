Documentation of Yellow-crowned Night Heron
2009-084

Observer Information

Reporter:  Eric Defonso  2950 Golden Eagle Cir,   Lafayette, CO  80026
E-mail:  bay.wren@gmail.com
Other Observers:  John Streeter (from Centennial, CO); Yes, I believe I am the original finder. Cole Wild reported seeing 1 of these birds at this location 3 days later.

Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Yellow-crowned Night Heron
First Date/Time:  9/2/2009
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  30 minutes
County:  Logan
Specific Location:  Just west (a few feet) of the intersection of Logan County Roads 95 and 68, there is a bridge over an inlet canal that flows north and east into Jumbo Reservoir. All three birds were in this canal, and at their furthest were right at the point where the canal bends eastward out of view from the bridge towards the reservoir.
Number:  3
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

In the inlet canal, with significant water flow for this time of year. Sandy banks in places, rocky in others but with overhanging and resident vegetation interspersed.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Zeiss 7x42 T-FL binoculars and Kowa TSN-823 spotting scope with 20-60x zoom eyepiece. Photographed with a Canon Rebel XT DSLR with 300mm zoom telephoto lens.
Distance:  Initially at ~150 ft, but after flushing, ~ 500 ft away. Measured via Google Earth.
Light:  My position on the bridge was south of where the birds were the entire time I view them. The sky was mostly sunny and viewing conditions were excellent with light or no wind.

Description of the Bird

All three birds appeared identical, in streaked brown plumage standing upright along the stream bank. The bills were very dark and quite thick and heavy, especially toward the base of the bills. The eyes were yellow and large. The necks seemed lengthy, and the heads were rounded. All three individuals showed very fine spots like little triangles at the tips of the wing coverts, along with clean white edging to those coverts.
Behaviors: The first bird I saw was flushed from a location closer to the bridge I was on. It flew directly to a spot along the stream near a bend, right across from 2 other herons, perhaps siblings. They mostly stood still as I watched, occasionally stepping slowly along the water's edge. They looked intently on the water, and I witnessed on at least 2 occasions a bird jabbing into the water and pulling out a crayfish.
Call: no sounds heard.
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Black-crowned Night-Heron. These birds were lankier and had much chunkier bills than BCNH. BCNH also should have smudgier spotting on the edges of the wing coverts, and generally have a more hunched-over posture. Behavior was also distinctive - these birds were going after crayfish, a very atypical prey item for BCNH, although admittedly not impossible.

Resources Used

Sibley Guide to Birds of North America primarily, which I had in hand as I was viewing the birds. I also called Nick Komar during the viewing to confirm ID details.

Previous Experience

I've seen and photographed adult YCNH on 2 or 3 occasions during trips to Florida in the Everglades and at Ding Darling NWR, most recently in 2007. I've encountered adult and juvenile BCNH numerous times living in California and Colorado, usually every year.
