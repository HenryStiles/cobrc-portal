Habitat

On small island in the middle of S. Platte River. Water was low but following steadily; the area is well-sheltered with shrubs. Sheer luck that I spotted it as I walked along the bike trail.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  300mm camera lens.
Distance:  100 ft.
Light:  It was still light enough for good photos but it was a clear summer day as the sun was setting.

Description of the Bird

Stout bill; slender, long neck as opposed to juvenile Black-crowned's stouter appearance. Narrow streaks on breast and neck as opposed to BCNH's broad, blurry streaks. Tiny white spots and thin white edges on wings unlike BCNH's larger white spots.

Overall appearance was much more delicate than the juvenile BCNHs which I have photographed much more frequently along this same area of the S. Platte River/S. Platte Park.
Behaviors: The bird was well-hidden behind shrubs bordering the bike trail. I watched it for several minutes during which it did not move but stood perfectly erect and still as shown in photos.
Call: Silent
Plumage: 1st Summer Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

I have numerous photos of juvenile BCNHs spotted along this same area. There's a distinct difference and explained above.

Resources Used

Sibley's and posted on CFO's Face Book page and several experienced birders believed this is a YCNH. Specfically, Joe Roller communicated with me at length about this bird. Joe also confirmed the ID.
