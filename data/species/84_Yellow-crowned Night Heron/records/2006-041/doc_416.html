Habitat

It was an area of the pond that had a bunch of willows around the pond and it was sitting on a pile of sticks that was about 20 feet out in the water.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica Binoculars and a Swarovski Spotting Scope
Distance:  We got to about 40 feet from the bird
Light:  The sun was directly behind us at one point. It was great for seeing all field marks on the bird.

Description of the Bird

It was a medium to small sized heron. It had a yellow head with two plumes coming off the back of the head. It also had a yellow eye. It had a black face, except fot a comma shapes check patch. The body was blueish gray, except for a small white patch on the upper front part of the wing. It had yellow legs. The gray bill was long and thick, but not as long as most herons.
Behaviors: It was sitting in a pile of sticks that was in the water of a medium sized pond. It was standing there and didn't move, except its head the whole time we were watching the bird.
Call: Didn't call or sing
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Black-crowned Night-Heron - It has a black crown and a black back. The general shape is it looks likeit is crouching. Not a black, but a gray face. A lighter grayer body.
Green Heron - Green back, rufous neck and orange legs.
Little Blue Heron - All blue bird with a blueish bill with a black tip and yellow-green legs.
The other herons are to big to be this bird.
Egrets have a mostly white to all white body.
