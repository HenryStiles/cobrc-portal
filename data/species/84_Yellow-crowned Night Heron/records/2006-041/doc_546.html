Habitat

Pond was fairly secluded with some edges having shrubs and small trees about ten feet tall. Parts of pond edge were open and grassy. Surrounding area was mixture of riparian woods and grassy fields and paths.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Ziess 8x42
Distance:  As close as 75 feet.
Light:  At the time of sighting there was a light cloud cover, although lighting was very good.

Description of the Bird

Bird was a medium-sized stocky heron. Head was striking with white crown with white breeding plume extending behind head. There was a bold, broad black streak that extended from the bill to the back of the head. Under the eye was a white crescent shaped patch that extended from the eye to behind the auriclars. The chin was black which extended to the back of the head below the white patch and joined the black streak extending through the eye. The bill was a very dark gray that was almost black at the tip.
The neck and underparts were a uniform slate gray. The wings and upperparts were grayish with some feathers having balck centers and whitish edges, but the overall appearance was quite gray.
The leg was yellowish and fairly long for a night-heron. (The second leg was tucked under the body or missing, as I never saw the bird change position in about thirty minutes.)
Behaviors: The bird was perched motionless on a branch at the edge of a pond. This perch was surrounded by shrubs and small trees. The bird was nearly motionless except to watch me as I passed on the far side of the pond about 75 feet away from it. The bird was perched on one leg. About a hlaf hour later, I passed the bird again and it had not moved.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: Alternate Adult

Similar Species Discussion

In shape and structure only the Black-crowned Night-Heron is similar. However, it is markedly different with a black crown and black back and the underparts are light gray fading to near white on the belly. Also, the area of the head below the eye is light gray or whitish.
