Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 77mm scope with 32x eyepiece
Distance:  as close at 90 feet
Light:  variable but we were very close to this bird. When it was perched in the tree the light was bad and the bird was backlit. When it was on the ground we moved into the best available light, although it was a dingy light day.

Description of the Bird

This was a chunky and short-legged blue-gray heron. It had dull yellowish tibio-tarsi, a wide dark gray-black bill, a blackish head that had a distince narrow rectangular white cheek. The longish neck was concolorous with the body, more dark grayish with bluish cast. The crown was creamy white with long filoplumes sticking straight out behind its head. The orbit was bright red in good light.
Behaviors: Mostly solitary at the edge of a cattail marsh, but it did fly up about 18 feet into a leafless tree for about 15 minutes before flying down to the water at the edge of the marsh. It was hunting, but i did not see it catch anything although there was some organic matter sticking out of its bill.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult non-breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Black-crowned Night-Heron has a different face pattern, no white cheek surrounded by black and no buff-white crown and a whitish not a grayish belly.

Boat-billed Heron has much, much broader bill, is more of a tropical species, has a face pattern more similar to BCNH, has a rufous lower belly, and a lighter gray back.
