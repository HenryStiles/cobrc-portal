Habitat

It was at the edge of a small cattail marsh while I was able to see it, but it was in the marsh (not visible) for the hour I was there searching for it before I saw it.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski Spotting Scope
Distance:  60 feet
Light:  It was to the northeast of me and the sun was towards the southwest, but it was partly cloudy.

Description of the Bird

A dark gray large bill. It has a black head with a white cheek patch and a yellow stripe on top of the head with long yellow plumes coming off the back of the head with a red eye. It had a blue-gray body and neck. It also had yellow legs. The bird overall was fairly small and slim for a heron.
Behaviors: It was actively feeding during most of the time I was watching it. Which means it was standing still most of the time waiting for a fish (or other small creature) to swim close to the heron.
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The black head with white cheek and yellow stripe on the head eliminates all other species. Also the blue-gray body eliminates everything except Great Blue Heron, but the over size of the bird also eliminated Great Blue Heron.
