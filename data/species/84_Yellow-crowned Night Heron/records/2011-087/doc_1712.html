Description of the Bird

The bird was similar in size to Black-crowned Night-Herons with it was nesting in the same tree. The night-heron had a distinctly patterned head, which was basally black with a large white cheek patch and a broad, off-white crown stripe. At least two long, whitish ornamental feathers extended back from the hindcrown. The dark grayish bill was dagger-shaped - thick in depth, but rather short and tapered to a blunt point. I mostly only saw the bird's upperparts which were ashy gray with black centers to the back and wing feathers.
Behaviors: Sitting on a stick nest in a heronry, apparently incubating or brooding.
