Description of the Bird

The chunky heron had a thick, all dark bill, longish (for a night-heron), thickish neck with dull yellowish legs, brown-gray back with small whitish spots. The throat and breast had light grayish vertical streaks and the undertail coverts were white. From my distant photos, it looks like the iris is yellowish orange.
Behaviors: It stood quietly just out in view from the cattail marsh and pond where it was located.

Never saw it move more than its head.
Call: None heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The only other North American night-heron to compare to is Black-crowned Night-Heron which should show thinner neck, light lower mandible, slightly less egg-shaped head, and noticeably large spots on its back. This bird had an all dark bill that was less dagger like (thicker), small spots on its back, and egg-shaped head.
