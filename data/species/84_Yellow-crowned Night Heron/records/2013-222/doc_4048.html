Description of the Bird

The heron was easily found in the location reported previously by Kaye Lafreniere, Ingrid Huskey, and Mark Peterson -- the small pond along Birdsall Road.  It was moving around slowly and silently, apparently foraging in the pond, during the period I observed it.  Some of the important features for identification are:  (1) transitional plumage with relatively light crown stripe and cheek patches starting to appear, (2) a mix of light and dark gray on the bill, (3) a long neck, and (4) thin, white edges on the covert feathers.

Similar Species Discussion

Among stocky herons expected in this part of the world, only immature Black-crowned Night-Herons and Yellow-crowned Night-Herons have a similar appearance to the observed bird.  Multiple details indicate the observed bird is a Yellow-crowned Night-Heron, including the relatively light crown and cheek patches, the long neck, and the thin white edges on the covert feathers.
