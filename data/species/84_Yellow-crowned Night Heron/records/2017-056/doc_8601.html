Description of the Bird

In a repeat of our May 26th encounter, as we walked across the bridge below the spillway, a large (heron/egret-sized) gray-backed bird popped out of the marsh and flew over the dam.  Once atop the dam, we relocated the bird sitting on some downed limbs near the "DO NOT CROSS FENCES" sign.  

All I saw initially was a medium sized heron-like bird with a solid gray back.  Joey noted the pale face-patch.  

Again, I knew it was either BCNH or YCNH just based upon size and even-toned gray back/wings spread in flight.  It was too small for a GBHE which also has two-toned wings.

Once the bird was relocated, the pale crown and the white cheek patch ruled out BCNH and LBHE.

The bird then flew from where it was initially over to a patch of willows where it very conveniently sat up for all to view well.
At this point the long yellow/orange legs, glowing orange eye as well as the all-gray body were visible.

We believe that this is likely the same bird seen on May 26th.
