Description of the Bird

Sean and I were standing in a back corner of Stalker, the area usually best for shorebirds. The water level was a bit high, with minimal mud. As I was looking at the few shorebirds present, Sean said, "I see a Yellow-crowned Night Heron." I stammered. He said, "Well, it has pale yellow on the top of its head." He got me on the bird, and all one could see was the head, which had a dusky crown with a broad butter-yellow center. The cheeks were grayish white. The bill stout and black. I didn't see any plumes, though short plumes might have been present. I had about 20 seconds to view the bird before it flew up into the Russian Olives. Sean and I tracked it down, and it flew out of the trees in my direction, then (upon approaching as close as 30 to 40 feet, it veered and flew mostly straight away.) It did not call.
     When the night heron flew, it had a shape similar to that of a Black-crowned, but the legs were distinctly longer, with as much tarsus beyond the tail as the length of the feet (in other words, the length of exposed leg was equal to the length of the feet). The bill, again, was noted to be stout and solid black. The bird had dusky gray streaking on the sides of the chest and upper belly (perhaps more extensive) and a medium gray back, similar to the color of an adult. Wing pattern was not noted. The legs and feet were a bright yellow. When the bird first flew, the head pattern was again noted, and looked as described above. The bird landed a couple hundred yards away in a location that would've been very difficult to access

Similar Species Discussion

Black-crowned Night Heron. Should not have the yellow on crown, should have shorter legs, and should not have an all black bill, nor a bill that short and thick. I am also not certain that I've seen a Black-crowned Night Heron with gray streaking beneath. The bird looked somewhat similar to "subadult" YCNHs I've seen in Baja (for examples, go to https://www.flickr.com/photos/36088296@N08/14512490868/in/photostream/ and the photo following.
