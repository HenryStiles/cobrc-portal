Description of the Bird

We saw this bird feeding along the rocks at the water's edge on the face of the dam at Bonny Reservoir as we drove slowly across the dam. The bird was obviously an immature night-heron, as its brownish overall plumage, thick dagger-shaped bill, and long legs indicated. Most of the six observers were quite familiar with the points of difference between the two night-heron species, so we concentrated on those. The salient differences noted were as follows: 1)The white spots on the wings and back were tiny dots, as opposed to the larger white blotches of BCNH. The tiny-speckle effect was very pronounced, no large white spots or marking were on the bird. 2)The overall plumage color was a very dark gray-brown, not the sandier color of BCNH. 3)The bill was entirely dark 4)There was a pale grayish area above the base of the upper mandible which contrasted with the rest of the plumage on the head. 5)The bird had a long thin neck and had it fully extended throughout the time we observed it. It was crouching and leaning out with the head low over the surface of the water. When it was in this posture, it looked very slim in comparison with the much chunkier BCNH.

Similar Species Discussion

see above discussion. BCNH would be stockier, lighter, have a mostly pale bill, be much lighter brown, and the white markings on the wings and back would be more numerous, much larger, and considerabley coarser in appearance.
