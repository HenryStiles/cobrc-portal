Description of the Bird

The bird was initially seen by myself only as it popped out of the marsh just below the dam and flew over the spillway.  Once we climbed up onto the dam, I relocated the bird hidden behind a sign. 

All I saw initially was a medium sized heron-like bird with a solid gray back.  I knew it was either BCNH or YCNH just based upon size and even toned gray back/wings spread in flight.  It was much too small for a GBHE which as two-toned wings.

Once the bird was relocated, the pale crown and the white cheek patch were instantly obvious which quickly ruled out BCNH.  

The bird then flew from where it was initially over to a patch of willows where it very conveniently sat up for all to view well (and many photos were taken).

At this point the long bright-orange legs were visible as well as the all-gray body.
