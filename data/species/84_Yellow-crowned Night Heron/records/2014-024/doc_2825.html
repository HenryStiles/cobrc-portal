Description of the Bird

Immature based on plumage, night-heron based on bill shape.  Long yellow-green legs, back and wings light gray with white equilateral triangular spots.  Bill mostly dark gray with lighter area at base of lower mandible. 

Similar Species Discussion

The only other species occuring in CO is the more common Black-crowned Night-Heron.  Elliminated because BCNH has shorter legs and its bill is dark on upper mandible and yellow green on lower mandible.  The best observable feature is the shape of the white spots on back, wing coverts and wings, which are elongated triangles in BCNH. I did see the bird in flight (long time ago) and noticed the longer neck on this bird, much longer than the photo shows and thinner than what shows for BCNH.

Resources Used

At the time, I used Nat Geo and Big Sibley. Now for night-herons I use the photos in Crossley ID Guide to Eastern Birds which shows accurate photos of both species.

Previous Experience

I used to work for two 9-month periods in Big Cypress National Presserve and Everglades National Park in Florida where I saw YCNHs periodically (maybe 15-25 per year) and BCNHs on almost a daily basis.  I have seen BCNHs a lot too, in CO and 5-7 YCNHs here over the years.
