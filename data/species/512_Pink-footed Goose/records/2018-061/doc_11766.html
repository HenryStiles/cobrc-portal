Description of the Bird

An obvious Anser goose with head and upper neck noticeably darker brown than the lower neck and most of the rest of the body plumage.  The eyes were dark brown and the bill was black with roughly half of the maxilla pink (from nail to nostrils).  The dark wing coverts and tertials had obvious pale tips/fringes.  The tail was extensively white (see in flight), with just a brown center surrounded by white.  The back end of the under side of the body was extensively white, much more so than the other geese present, such that one could pick it out solely on that feature.  After a long back-and-forth discussion with Peter Pyle and extensive review of photos of the species, he and I agree that the Colorado bird is an adult, despite my original contention that it was in its first plumage cycle.

Similar Species Discussion

The two species of Bean Goose, and all other similarly colored Anser, are ruled out by the bill color and pattern. Greater White-fronted Goose is also ruled out by tail pattern and color.
