Description of the Bird

Small goose, slightly smaller than nearby Cackling Geese, shorter necked.  Brown head, paler cinnamon colored neck and chest, Pale belly, white undertail coverts and
tail tip. Silver-gray scapulars, darker tertials, black primaries. Pink legs, pink in midportion of bill, with darker base and tip.

Was affliliated with varied other geese, including Cacklers, Canadas, Snow Geese, a Ross's Goose, a Brant, and a Barnacle Goose. Seen variously swimming, standing on ice, stretching, and in flight.

Similar Species Discussion

GWFG ruled out based size and bulk, legs that were pink (not orange), dark base of bill with pink horizontal band near base, pink at midportion of bill, and dark tip (not more uniform orangish/pinkish bill, larger bill), more frosty grayish upperparts (not brown), and total lack of barring on underparts. Clearly not other species of goose with which it was affliated (no white anywhere on face; body not black, white, blue/gray or dark brown).

Resources Used

Initially, bird pointed out by other birders. Resources later used included National Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America (7th Ed, Dunn and Alderfer), and The Sibley Guide to Birds (2nd Ed, Sibley).

Previous Experience

None with this species (or Barnacle Goose).
However, while spending some time as a compiler for the Colorado RBA, I had occasion to do some "light" research using the IUCN Red List site (www.IUCNredlist.org). I also performed a general Google search looking for geese for sale as collection/barnyard animals. That search showed that while breeding aged Barnacle Geese were more readily available for seasonal sale in nearby states (MI and WI come to mind), PFGO don't appear on the same websites. This committee will need to decide if either or both of these species are likely wild or captive birds...
