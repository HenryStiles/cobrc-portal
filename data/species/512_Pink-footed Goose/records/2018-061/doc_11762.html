Description of the Bird

This Pink-footed Goose was actively feeding on a grassy edge to a gravel pit in Weld County. This pit was on CR 7 about ½ south of Hwy 119 in Longmont. The nearest hotspot is St. Vrain State Park. The bird stayed in the same general 15 feet area during my observation. This bird was in a flock of Cackling and Canada Geese also in the grass. On the water nearby was both geese species as well as some Northern Shovelers. This goose spent 90% of the time with its head down actively feeding. On the rare occasions that it lifted its head, many photos were taken by me and all the birders around.
Size – the PFGO was just a little bigger than the Cackling Goose nearby and much smaller than the Canada Geese.
Color – the head and neck was a dull brown with the head seemed small compared to the body size. The back and wings were medium to dark gray with light white edges. The vent was clean bright white which stood out from the white cheek geese. The chest was buffy moving to medium dark brown mottled. The legs and feet were bright pink which also stood out from all the other black legged geese in the flock. The bill was short, duck like, black, with pinkish colored ring and up to the gap. the nail was black.

Sounds – I didn’t hear any sounds from this bird.

I also observed the bird at Milavec Reservoir on 12.19.18 from 1:35 - 2:22 PM. This bird was amoung about 5,000 Cackling and Canada Geese. sitting onthe water. Also in the flock was a GWFG and several hybrids. Luckily for me, there was a strong wind. As a result, all the geese were facing the same way which made it easy to scan the whole group. I will also attach a few photos from this observation which were much closer. The bird was about 30 yards away and was sitting on the water the whole observation time. I also got another female birder from Golden on the goose.

Similar Species Discussion

Taiga Bean-Goose was eliminated due to small patch of orange vs. Taiga mostly orange bill. The PFGO had pink legs and feet vs. orange of Taiga. Finally the Taiga has a darker back and my bird was a little more pale.

Tundra Bean-Goose was eliminated due to orange legs and feet vs. pink. The bill has a larger orange patch than extends to the tip. The head and upper neck is black vs. our brown headed goose and all striped black and white neck.

White-fronted Goose was eliminated due to full orange bill, legs and feet vs. mostly small black bill with a small flesh patch. The chest has a light buffy patch that changes abruptly to grey and black vs. a smooth transition and lighter color at belly. The white circle around the base of the bill would have been an obvious sighting which the PFGO didn't have.
