Description of the Bird

An obvious goose, that was a little larger than the thousands of Cackling Geese it was with.  Darkish head, with a dark eye and a bicolored bill that was about 60% pink, but dark near the head and at the tip.  Brown back with thin white lines that were horizontal over the upper back but were along the feather edges along the lower back. Brownish on the sides of the upper breast with more white horizontal lines, but the rear half of the breast was pure white that reached back to the tail.  The bird was not seen in flight, but just sitting on the water.  When it swam, the upper part of the legs were pink, and this is shown better in the attached photo by Jim Esten that I downloaded from Cobirds. It is difficult to see from this photo, but Joe Mammoser, who saw the bird close up at the first location it was found, said the hallux on both legs were intact.

Similar Species Discussion

Cackling, Canada and Brant Geese by head pattern. Greater White-fronted Goose by bill color and no white around the bill. Bean Goose by bill color being partiall pink, not yellow, and by Pink-fronted being considerably smaller than Bean Goose.

Resources Used

National Geographic Field Guide.  I returned to the Frederick Recreation area on Saturday December 22, and saw the bird again for 10 minutes.

Previous Experience

Have seen many Pink-fronted Geese in England, although that was many years ago by now.  Have also seen Bean Geese in England, also many years ago.  Am very familiar with also the geese occuring in Colorado.
