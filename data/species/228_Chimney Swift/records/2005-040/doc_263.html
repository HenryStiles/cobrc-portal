Description of the Bird

A small, dark, long-winged swift. The bird, seen overhead, appeared dark gray or almost black underneath the body and wings, perhaps a shade lighter on the throat. Wings much longer than body, swept back. Tiny bill nearly invisible, blunt head, and squared-off tail makes body reminiscent of a cigar. Quick, wiry wingbeat typical of Chaetura swifts.
Behaviors: The bird appeared to be migrating, flying purposefully towards the north.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The flight style and wing proportions ruled out swallows. Black Swift ruled out by faster wingbeat and small size. White-throated Swift ruled out by lack of white in the underparts. Vaux's Swift not expected E. of the Rockies in Colorado. The throat also didn't show as much contrast as expected in Vaux's Swift, and observers present felt the shape of the wing and speed of the wingbeat wasn't consistent with Vaux's.
