Description of the Bird

 Dark rufous shorebird , body size a little larger than adjacent Baird's but longer legs and long dark drooping bill . Dark rufous breast and face showing signs of moult to basic plumage , as blotchy white feathers beginning to show . White supercilium evident and back feathers dark gray. In flight in comparison to the Bairds that it associated with could be picked out as a dark bird with a white rump and white upper wing stripe . Observed preening and also feeding by probing into water about two or three feet in from waters edge .

Similar Species Discussion

Unique dark rufous coloration and profile makes this an easy ID . Red Knot and Sanderling have rufous breeding plumages but do not have the long drooping bill , Stilt Sandpiper has somewhat similar profile but never dark rufous breast or face plumage , bill is not as droopy , longer legs are yellow , not black.
