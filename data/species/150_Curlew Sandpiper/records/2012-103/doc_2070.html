Description of the Bird

Shorebird somewhat reminiscent of Dunlin in size and shape, but legs proportionately longer and bill finer tipped. Bill and legs black. Size larger significantly larger than nearby Least Sand, smaller than Killdeer, about midway between, roughly speaking. Legs longer than peep, shorter than Stilt Sand, relatively slim appearance. Bill very long (though not as long as dowitcher, for instance) broader at base, somewhat decurved, ending in fine tip. Distinct whitish supercilium contrasting with dusky auriculars. Underparts white except light dull brown markings at side of chest (this area not carefully scrutinzied). Remainder of underparts white. Back and scap feathers were dusky and fringed in rufous giving somewhat scaly effect; no white braces or rusty scaps. Wings similar in pattern but not as contrasty and not as carefully studied.
Behaviors: Fed on mudflat by walking and picking. No repeated rapid probing, as often seen in Dunlin. When resting or alert, posture somewhat upright.
Call: not heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Dunlin subtly different in shape, but also should be gray, without buff or other bright markings above. Juv Dunlins rarely migrate before molting, but rarely do (I once saw one in WA). However, such birds have more extensively marked underparts with blackish patch, and more extensive buffy or chestnut edges to upperparts, eliminating scaly look. Juv Stilt Sandpiper longer legged and pale legged.
