Description of the Bird

This bird was distinctly smaller than any other bird at our feeders (Dark-eyed Juncos, American Tree Sparrow). Its throat area was a very distinct white. It had a very dark malar stripe and just above that was a white submoustacial stripe. Its breast and belly were a plain grayish without any spotting whatsoever. Its undertail was white. Its bill was pinkish/orangish with a very tiny dark tip. Its lores were black. Its supraloral area was white. Its malar area was a medium to light brown. It had a black eye stripe and a double broken eye ring. Its supercilium was white. Above its supercilium was a red crown with black streaking. In the middle of that was a white stripe. Its nape was gray and its back was brown with dark streaking oriented in line with its body ( up and down). Its rump was gray and its tail a darker gray than its rump.
Behaviors: It was always either eating our bird food or just sitting in our Linden tree with the juncos.
Call: On February 23 I started hearing it give its trill song. I was standing there watching it actually open its mouth and sing. I saw it sing every time after.
Plumage: Winter Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Really only American Tree Sparrow. This bird did not have a central breast spot.
