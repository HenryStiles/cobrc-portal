Description of the Bird

I heard the unmistakable song as soon as I stepped outside into my backyard.  It was perched 20 feet from me.   I went back inside to get binos and camera (iphone).  It had flown into my neighbors yard, ~60' away.  Still easy to see with binos.  Very reddish color, white throat, dark bands on tail, and long bill.   I played the calls & song on the BirdsEye West Iphone Application and it flew back into my yard.  I recorded an 11 second video but it didn't sing.   I then watched/listened to it from my office window.  It was close by and singing and calling frequently from 11-12.  Heard it less and farther away from 1200-1:15-ish.

Similar Species Discussion

I heard it 1st and knew it was a canyon wren song.  Expected to see a mockingbird, but it was a canyon wren.  I played the song to double check that it was the same.  Compared to pictures of other wrens - distinguished from others by slightly larger size, reddish color, long bill, and barred tail.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

Been birding since 1991.  Really like all wrens, especially canyon wrens.  So, I always listen for them when I am in their typical habitat.  I recognized the song instantly.
