Description of the Bird

A larger empid, with brownish-olive upperparts. Crown grayish and somewhat peaked, darker than the back. Dull incomplete white eye-ring somewhat broader posteriorly but not tear-shaped. Lores dull white. Dark eye. Sides of face and nape darker and grayer than the back. White chin and throat contrasting with the face. Whitish underparts with flanks and lower belly having a slight yellowish wash. The bill appeared wide and somewhat long and was completely yellow-orange on the lower mandible and blackish on the upper mandible. Fairly long wings, grayish-black in color with two obvious contrasting white wing-bars. Tertials and secondaries edged white on outer web, primaries tipped with narrow white. Medium-long primary extension. Broad, medium length tail. Upper tail similar in color to wings but not quite as dark. Legs and feet blackish.
Behaviors: Calling and flycatching in low willows.
Call: Quite loud "pip" note, heard often.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Wood-pewees have no eyering, are larger with longer wings that extend farther down the tail, longer primary extension, and a noticeable peaked crest. Large, all yellow bill, and no strong contrast between head and back eliminates Hammond’s Flycatcher. Dusky Flycatcher appears more gray-olive, with a dark tip to the lower mandible and have a shorter primary projection. Least Flycatcher is smaller and more large-headed with a much bolder eyering, shorter and more triangular bill, and shorter primary projection. Gray Flycatcher has a dark tip to the lower mandible, and is much lighter gray in color. Cordilleran has a broken eye-ring with a distinct teardrop shape posteriorly, and shorter primary projection. Yellow-bellied Flycatcher would appear much more yellow underneath with a broad yellow eyering. Buff-breasted can be ruled out by coloration and structure. Acadian Flycatcher has a larger bill, pale yellowish eye-ring, paler face, grayer throat, buffy vs. white wingbars and longer primary extension. Willow flycatcher has a very indistinct to no eye-ring. Less contrast between the crown and the back. Wings dark grayish brown vs. grayish-black with less contrasting wingbars. Uppertail grayish brown rather than grayish-black. Also, call note is very different.
