Description of the Bird

I pulled over to do notes and heard a rrrreee-bee-o out my window. The song had a buzzy quality, especially to the first two portions. This song differs greatly from the "Fitz-bew" of a Willow Flycatcher with its more explosive first note. Willows can give a more leisurely song, but that doesn't match the first note of the Alder, with its rrrrr beginning switching over to the eeee finishing portion. Also, I've not heard a Willow truly (or consistently) tripartate song. 
     I got out of the car, and found an Empid across a narrow creek in some willow thickets. It had a bright green back, fairly darkish green head and auriculars. The head was flattish. The bird had a weak narrow white eyering. The wings were blackish with broad buff wingbars and bright white, highly contrasting, edges to the tertials and secondaries. The primaries were moderately long. The underparts were yellowish on vent and lower belly, fading into off-white on upper belly. Chest had a vague olive-gray band/vest. Throat was white (brighter than indicated by photo), which contrasted markedly with auriculars (again, not shown well in photos, in part due to the bird being in shade at time, and part due to distance/low shutter speed of photo). The tail was like that of Traill's in general in size. The bill seemed a bit smallish for a Willow

Similar Species Discussion

The song eliminates all other species. Additionally, Willow typically has more peaked crown, more blurry border between auriculars and throat, paler/gray-hued head. Least is not as green above, has more prominent eyering, smaller bill, shorter primaries
