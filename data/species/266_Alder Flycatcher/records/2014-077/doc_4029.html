Description of the Bird

Identified to genus by face pattern and size.  Identified by call distinctive call note that was not a "whit" but more like a "pip". Photo included shows the thing complete eyering and the greenish, or rather, not brown back.  For an Empid it was on the large size so only WIFL was also considered.

Similar Species Discussion

Willow Flycatcher, the other look alike  "Trails" type was eliminated by the call note not being a "whit" and by the thin but complete eyering and the back coloration that was more green than dull brown. 

Resources Used

I did not use a book for this one. I studied the many photos I took and listened to both WIFL and ALFL calls on Xeno-Canto.

Previous Experience

I have a lot of experience with WIFL.  I was paid to look for the SW WIFL race in all of New Mexico for a breeding season and I banded some in northern CA of two subspecies.  I worked above the Arctic Circle in Alaska for four summers and I had ALFLs where I lived for two summers.  I see one or two almost every year at Chico Basin Ranch or birds I call this species.
