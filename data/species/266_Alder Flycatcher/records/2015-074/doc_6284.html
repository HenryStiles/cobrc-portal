Description of the Bird

Green backed Empid with green extending up through nape and crown and down through auriculars. Green auriculars contrasted sharply with white throat. Very narrow white eyering sometimes visible, sometimes not, depending on angle. When seen, had break in upper/forward part of ring. Bright wingbars washed in yellow-green on upper wingbar. Lower wingbar looked white. Tertials black with bright white edging. Yellow-green edges to secondaries. Underparts with yellow wash from undertail coverts well into belly. Modest olive wash on sides of chest. Bill looked chubby from side view. Tail wide and relatively short. Primary extension long. Crown flat (that is rounded, not square or crested). Found by typical Alder "Pleep" call it was giving, which was heard repeatedly as I pursued it in attempts to gain a photograph

Similar Species Discussion

All other Empids, except maybe HAFL, eliminated by call note. This call note was different from variant I've heard from WIFLs. W WIFL not as colorful, shorter primaries. E WIFL should still show some peak to crown, gray on head (lacks yellow edging to secondaries?) and shows less cheek to throat contrast. Bill maybe larger. At least in some individuals. HAFL much smaller bill, less contrasty wings. LEFL bigger eyering (HAFL as well) with narrower tail, shorter primaries
