Description of the Bird

All three birds shared the following characterstics:
Moderately bright green back and crown, with back and crown similar if not identical in color (ie, no contrast)
In all three birds the green of crown extended down thru auriculars, often appearing to have some gray hues washed in. 
In all three birds the throat was strikingly white, with the auricular/throat boundary being moderate to quite prominent, somewhat dependant on posture and lighting.
In all three birds the eyering was visible most of the time (but not necessarily in all lighting/viewing angles). The eyering was thin, white, and never looked complete, with break being towards front and top.
All three birds showed black/blackish tertials (varying a tad with lighting) contrasting sharply with bright white edges.
The upper wingbar showed some dedgree of yellow-buff wash on all three. The lower wingbar would seem white at times, washed slightly in yellowish at other times; again, consistent amongst all three individuals
All three had a similar bill shape (see photos). Van Remsen described this bill as "fat" - from side view - which seems appropriate and seem somewhat distinctive for ALFL (except eastern WIFL may be/is similar).
All three had rather broad and, proportionately, somewhat short tails. All three usually/always showed a flattish head without crested appearance of WIFL. 
All three birds repeatedly utterd a "pleep" call that is different from any of the odd utterances that I occasionally hear from WIFLs and matches recordings of ALFL very nicely. Bird 1 and 3 gave a wee-BEE-o call with typical tone and inflection for that species. Bird two never sang, though responded well to ALFL recordings, and may have been (I'd presume so) a female, as birds 1 and 3 sang aggressively in response to tapes 
All three bird had a moderately bright yellow wash to belly and white throat. In birds 2 and 3, there was some olive-gray at sides of chest. In bird 1, the olive gray met in the center of the chest and there was limited olive down sides. 

Similar Species Discussion

The song and call allows ID from all other Empids.
Furthermore, the head shape was typical of ALFL and different from that of E WIFL
Least Fly can pose an ID challenge, but has much shorter primaries and lack yellow wash to secondary edges shown by all three birds (forgot to mention primary extension and secondary edging in actual description, sorry). Also, LEFL should show a much bolder eyering, shorter bill, thinner tail. 
Acadian Fly, amongst other marks, would have had a much longer bill
YB Fly a yellow throat. 
Hammond's Fly bolder eyering, gray on head, tiny bill
