Description of the Bird

This bird was very similar in size & plumage to Acadian Flycatcher, but with shape & "jizz" of a Willow Fycatcher.

Fairly bright olive green back with no traces of brown first caught my attention.

Head & crown were darker olive than back, except for nape, which showed little contrast with olive back. Dark olive auriculars contrasted with a fairly bright white throat. Lores were pale, partially obscuring narrow pale eye ring. Eye was dark.

Underparts were creamy white, separated from brighter white throat by obscure dusky off-white breast band.

Wings were blackish with two very bright white wing bars on each wing. Tertials were blackish but with bright crisp white edgings. Primaries were rather longish with pointed tips extending to base of tail. But slightly notched dark tail appeared rather longish, giving bird an overall slender appearance.

Bill was noticeably larger than Dusky & Hammonds, about Cordilleran Flycatcher size and color, with dark upper mandible and bright yellow lower mandible. Legs appeared dark.
Behaviors: Bird perched on small shrubs and mullen never getting very far from ground. It often flicked its tail up and down.
Call: none given
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Eastern type Willow Flycatchers can sometimes be inseparable from Alders. But Willows show more brownish tones in back. Wing bars & tertial edges are not as bright as Alders, except possibly juvenile Willows. But juvenile Willows show more yellow underparts which this adult Alder lacked.

Perhaps underestimated are similarities of Acadian Flycatchers to Alders with extemely emphasized field marks, which I feel this Alder had. But it was more slender looking, more round headed, & smaller billed than Acadian. Acadians also appear stockier and shorter tailed than Alders due to the former's extremely long primaries. I also feel Acadian's eye ring is somewhat more distinct than Alder's.
