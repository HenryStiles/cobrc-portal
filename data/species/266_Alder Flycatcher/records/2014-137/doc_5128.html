Description of the Bird

Empid initially encountered at a distance of 15 feet or so in bright shade. Moderate green back and crown with gray wash on sides of nape. Auriculars olive washed in gray - dark. Contrasting with whitish throat. Underparts otherwise white excepting pale yellowish wash vent/undertail coverts and vague olive wash to sides of chest. Crown held flat. Otherwise shaped like a Traills but bill seemed a tad small. Longish primary extension. Very narrow but distinct whitish eyering. Circular and unbroken. High contrast between flight feathers and edges, similar to LEFL in this respect. Wingbars white. Tertial edges white. At one point came into pishing and uttered a "Peep" call twice while being watched

Similar Species Discussion

Call eliminates Willow Fly. The green back and crown and dark auriculars contrasting with white throat inconsistent with Willow Fly, even Eastern. Head and bill shape more like that of Alder as well. Even Eastern Willows tend not to show as much of an eyering....
However, Traill's like over all size and shape combined with very narrow eyering wrong for other Empid species. Least Fly, closest of other Empids, would have bolder eyering, larger head, narrower tail, shorter primaries
