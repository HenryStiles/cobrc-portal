Description of the Bird

A largish empie, the bird was brown above with a strong olive cast. The eye ring was very narrow, yellowish, and almost complete (possibly broken on top). The whitish throat contrasted quite sharply with the darker auriculars. The chest had a grayish-olive wash separating the white throat from the very pale yellow belly. The wings were blackish with strong orange-buffy wingbars. The primary projection was fairly long, being equal in length to something more than half the length of the longest tertial. The tail was dark with paler outer webs to the R6s. The bill was fairly wide with a black maxilla and with the mandible mostly yellowish-orange; about the distal 20% was dark. The legs were dark.
Behaviors: foraging among cottonwoods and rushes
Call: I heard the bird twice give the typical lower-pitched "peip" call
Plumage: Juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Willow Flycatcher can be quite similar, but usually has little or no eye ring and, at least western birds typically lack strong green tones. Willow Flycatcher call notes are quite different from that heard from the bird (being a medium-hard, breathy "whit").
