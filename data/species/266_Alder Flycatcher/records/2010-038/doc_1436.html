Description of the Bird

A bird was captured for banding. We noticed that is was a large greenish Empidonax flycatcher with a fine, nearly complete eyring. The bill was wide and long for an empid and was bright orange below. The overall green color and dark-centered feathers of the crown made me think this was an ALFL. Also the contrastingly clean throat (whitish). MEasurements were performed and all but one lined up with ALFL and not Willow Flycatcher the only other possibility.

Similar Species Discussion

Willow Flycatcher is the only other similar species. Eliminated by the bright greenish color of the upperparts, nice fine and nearly complete eyering and the following wing formulas.
wing-tail = 11mm (all measures in mm)
primary projection = 13.7
longest primary-P6 = 5.1 P=primary
P10-P5 = 1.9 (out of range for ALFL)
P6-P10 = 3.6
P9-P5 = 8.1
sharp cheek contrast is good for ALFL

All measures were in range for ALFL except as noted there were a couple that were out of range for WIFL
