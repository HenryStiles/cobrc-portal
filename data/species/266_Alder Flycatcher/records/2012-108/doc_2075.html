Description of the Bird

Empidonax flycatcher with longish primaries, black tertials with white edging. Dark wing coverts with broad moderately buff wingbars. Greenish back, nape, crown, with olive gray auriculars, set off from white throat. Traill's bill. Relatively large tailed, like Traill's, but crown consistently flat, typical for Alder and not for Willow Flys. Quite narrow white eyering. Belly and vent washed with pale yellow. Chest with some dusky to sides.
Behaviors: Seemed well fed. Mostly sat perched, occasionally sallying forth for insects.
Call: not heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Though generally considered unidentifiable by sight, several highly respected birders believe otherwise (eg, Chris Wood). The uniform green color above should not be seen in any WIFL. Western birds are dull brown or brown gray above, eastern birds can have bright green backs, but have some gray washed into nape and crown. The auricular pattern is different as well. The eyering is more typical of ALFL, though some e. WIFLs can show this
