Description of the Bird

Bird 1: Empid with green back, nape, crown; green extended down into auriculars, perhaps with a hint of gray. Border between auriculars and white throat varied from moderate to substantial, depending on lighting and position. Eyering very narrow, white. Sometimes not visible (depending on light and angle), ususally appearing broken anterior/superior, rarely appearing complete. Underparts with yellow washed belly, olive wash to sides of chest. Wingbars washed faintly in yellow-green to yellow-buff. Bright white tertial edges contrasting sharply against remainder of tertials, which were black. Secondaries edged in yellow green. Primary extension moderate to long. Bill "fat" appearing from side. Tail wide and proportionately shortish. Crown relatively flat (ie, gently rounded) without hint of crest. Sang typical wee-BEE-o or gave "pleep" call over and over. 
Bird 2: heard giving "pleep" call when playing song at bird 1. It came in and was seen for 2-3 minutes before disappearing. Shape same as bird 1, with broad and shortish tail, longish primaries, fat bill, rounded crown w/o crest. Contrasting tertials also noted. Back and crown green. Underparts not seen well. Never sang. Call of two birds heard simultaneously and were inseparable except for direction from which calls came.
Only bird 1 was photographed

Similar Species Discussion

Vocalizations eliminate all other Empids. Eastern Willow also should show less contrast between throat and auriculars, more of a crest on crown  -especially when agitated. Eastern Willows typically lack green in secondaries and have grayish on crown? Least more conspicous eyering, shorter primaries, shorter bill. Tail would be narrower in Least.
