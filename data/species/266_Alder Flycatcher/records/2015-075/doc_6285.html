Description of the Bird

Bird initially found by Steve Larson who exclaimed that he had an Empid with a bright green back. I relocated it and studied it as it faced away initially. Back and crown bright green (not as bright as YBFL but brighter than LEFL) without any gray on nape or crown. It looked over its shoulder towards me, and I could see a bright white throat contrasting sharply with green auriculars (washed with some gray?). The birds tail was wide and relatively short. The tertial edgings were white, brightly contrasting with the body of the tertials. Wing bars were bright and seemed white. Primary projection long. The bird sallied forth and landed facing me. Underparts from undertail coverts through much of belly washed in clear yellow. Sides of chest with vague olive wash. Central chest whitish. Throat bright white. The bird had a narrow white eyering that seemed present only along posterior half of eye

Similar Species Discussion

E WIFL should have had less contrast between auriculars and throat, some gray on nape/crown. Also, E WIFL should show some peak on crown (this bird's crown was gently rounded... flat compared to most Empids... lacking square or crested look). Bill too large for LEFL and LEFL should have had more of an eyering, shorter primary projection.
