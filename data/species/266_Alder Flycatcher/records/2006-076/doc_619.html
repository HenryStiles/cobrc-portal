Description of the Bird

A large and large-headed Empidonax flycatcher, the bird's bill was quite long (being at least as long as the distance between the base of the bill and the back of the eye) and wide and was black above and yellowish-orange below. The head was greenish-gray, contrasting somewhat with the greener back. The rump was the same color as the back and both were a bit brighter than olive. There were thin, but obvious, yellowish eye rings that were virtually complete. The grayish (with slight green cast) auriculars contrasted strongly and sharply with the white chin and throat.

The wings were blackish, with strong whitish-yellow wing bars and strong white fringes to the tertials. The primary projection was fairly long and was equal to about 40% of the length of the longest tertial. The tail was dark brown with pale outer webs to the outer rectrices.

The chest was white with vague grayish smudging. The rest of the underparts were whitish except that the flanks and some of the vent area were washed variably with a pale lemon yellow. The legs and eyes were dark.
Behaviors: Foraging among Russian Olives
Call: Heard to call once, the bird gave a short, somewhat quiet, "peip"
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Willow Flycatcher (WIFL) is most similar; eastern WIFL very similar, but ruled out by strong and, particularly, sharp auriculars-throat contrast (this area blends in WIFL) and by call note; western forms easily eliminated by the bird's strong green tones on the upperparts and by other features as in eastern WIFL. Acadian Flycatcher (ACFL) ruled out by duller green of the bird, particularly on the head, and by the shorter and narrower bill than ACFL usually sports.
