Description of the Bird

A small flycatcher with greenish tones on the upperparts. Song and call were primary ID confirmation for me, but size along with head and body proportions immediately suggested Empidonax genus. Green tones, weak (at best) eye-ring, largish bill suggest Trail's-type empid.
Behaviors: Foraging in willows along small pond.
Call: Both call and song heard. Song the typical, iconic two-part zree zreet, upslurred on end every time. Call heard as I got closer, a very flat, plain short peet. I compared the call with that on Sibley's eguide in the field in real time and found the match to be perfect.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Song and call ruled out other empids. Willow Flycatcher would look most similar (and I'd be uncomfortable narrowing a Trail's Flycatcher to species in a seen-only case) but nothing else sounds like a singing Alder Flycatcher.
