Description of the Bird

The bird was a large Empidonax flycatcher that was obviously larger than a nearby Least Flycatcher. The crown, nape, back, and rump were all the same deep green that was brighter than olive. The bird's bill was comprised of a black maxilla and an orangish mandible and was fairly long and wide. The dark eyes were nearly completely surrounded by very thin yellowish eye rings, broken only slightly on the top. The auriculars contrasted strongly and quite sharply with the white chin and throat.

The wings were blackish, with strong white wing bars that had a slight yellow cast and white-fringed tertials. The primary projection was quite long for an Empidonax, at least equalling 1/3 of the length of the longest tertial. The tail was dark with a strong olive cast and the outer rectrices sported whitish outer webs. The belly, sides, flanks, and vent were pale and washed variably with pale yellow. The legs were dark.
Behaviors: Foraging low in Russian Olives
Call: None heard
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Eastern Willow Flycatcher most similar and ruled out by virtually complete eye ring and strong and sharp auriculars/throat contrast; Western Willow Flycatcher also eliminated by bright green upperparts. Acadian Flycatcher ruled out by narrowness and incompleteness of eye rings. Both Eastern and Western Wood-Pewees are eliminated as they both have much longer primary projection, typically lack even the thin eye ring of the bird in question, and neither are ever quite as green as it was.
