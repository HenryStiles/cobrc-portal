Description of the Bird

The bird was about 6 inches long. The over all shape was typical Empid or pewee.
Bill: Slightly hooked and flattened. Wide at the base. The lower mandible was pale pink with a darkish tip.
Upper parts: Bright olive-green on the back and rump. The head was darker and grayer.
Wings: Very dark with bright whitish wing bars and tertial edges. Primary projection appeared to be moderate.
Under parts: Pale olive across the breast and yellowish in the belly. The throat was white showing good contrast with the breast and the rest of the head.
Face: Noticeable white eye ring.
Behaviors: The bird was very active in the low willows. It stayed along the edges and would glean insects off of the branches and frequently sallied out to catch flying insects. Posture was very upright when perched. It would occasionally flick the tail upwards.
Call: Call was a loud pip. The song was a burry reee-beeah, with the accent on the bee, as described in most field guides.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Other Empidonax (especially Willow) are eliminated by the song and or call note. If this bird had been silent I would have left it as Empid sp. Wood pewees are duller and larger.
