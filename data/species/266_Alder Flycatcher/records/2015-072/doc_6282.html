Description of the Bird

Moderately bright green back, nape and crown extending down into auriculars forming a moderately sharp border with bright white throat. Eyering narrow, white and weak to absent in front. Wingbars tinged in dull yellow-green. Tertials black with sharply contrasting white borders. Primaries long. Tail white and proportionately shortish. Crown relatively flat (meaning modestly rounded, not peaked/crested). Belly washed in pale yellow, chest with olive wash on sides. When found, giving typical ALFL "Pleep" call, when recording played by Van Remsen, the bird started to sing typical wee-BEE-o

Similar Species Discussion

Song and call eliminate other Empids
Long primary extension eliminates W WIFL
E WIFL should have some crest, gray on crown, less contrasting auriculars.
LEFL with bolder eyering, shorter primaries, shorter bill
ACFL with larger bill, bolder eyering, etc
