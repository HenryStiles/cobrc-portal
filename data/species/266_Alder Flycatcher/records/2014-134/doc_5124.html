Description of the Bird

First heard the "peep" call of this bird, perhaps ten times. Walked in its direction, and about 40 or so feet away it flycaught and landed in open, still calling. I followed for the next few minutes, getting a number of good looks in good lighting. 

The back was olive-green, perhaps not as bright as that of some Alders I've seen, but since they molt on wintering grounds, fall birds might be expected to be a bit duller. The olive green back color extended onto the crown and down onto the auriculars, which were also washed slightly in gray. Quite narrow, but distinct, eyering. Whitish. Long primaries. Crown held flat. Whitish beneath with No yellow hues but some dusky wash restricted to sides of chest. Overall shape and size appropriate for Traill's Fly (larger than Least/Hammond's/Dusky) though bill a bit smaller than that of Willow. Wings blackish with bright contrasting white edges to tertials and bright white, washed in yellow) edges to secondaries. Oddly, upper wingbar with buff-yellow color and lower wingbar was white (not shown clealry in photos). Beak had dark maxillary and yelllow-orange mandible.

Similar Species Discussion

Willow Flycatcher- Call note different, with even some of the peculiar sweeter (ie, more "peep"-like calls) being different, typically with a slight burriness. Head usualy crowned in Willow. Had Alder like green crown extending down into auriculars and contrasting sharply with the white throat, unlike a Willow, even Eastern Eyering bolder than that of Eastern Willow

Hammond's Fly- call vaguely similar (more LB Dowitcher like), but bill smaller, head gray, eyering bold, etc
