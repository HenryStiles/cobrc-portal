Description of the Bird

This bird was captured and banded at the RMBO banding station at Barr Lake SP. When the bird was first brought out of the bag, I immediately grew interested by its size and overall green coloration. This was a decent-sized Empidonax with a medium-long bill, extensive green coloration on the upperparts, yellowish tinge to the underparts, thin white eyering, and distinct pale lores. Meredith McBurney provided the following measurements (all in millimeters). P6 was not emarginated which is a characteristic shown by only Willow, Alder, and Acadian Flycatchers. Wing = 70, tail = 57, wing-tail = 13, bill tip from nares = 8.4, bill width = 6, p10>p5.
Behaviors: In the hand at the banding station.
Call: None heard
Plumage: hatch year

Similar Species Discussion

Of the Empidonax whose p6 is not emarginated, Acadian was ruled out based on bill length measurement of 8.4 (Acadian should be longer at 9.2-10.1). The bird did not have a honking, huge bill that I expect to see on Acadian. Eastern Willow as ruled out by the bright olive green upperparts which on a hatch-year Willow should be more brownish-olive.
