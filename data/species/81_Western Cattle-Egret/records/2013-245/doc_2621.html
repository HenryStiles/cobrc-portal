Description of the Bird

The bird was walking around near about 25 Canada Geese, on the bank of Pinon Lake.  I started about 60 yards from the bird and walked slowly toward it and taking pictures.  When I was within 50 feet, it took off toward the Island on the lake (where he had been reported the day before). I heard no calls.

Similar Species Discussion

I considered snowy egret and great egret.  Our bird was smaller than any of these 2 I'd seen before and I took 1 picture of it next to a goose for size comparison. The yellow bill is shorter than that of snowy or great egrets.  The legs were very dark gray and no crown, which made it a juvenile. I consulted Sibley, national geographic and golden books; as well as 'whatbird' and 'all about birds'.  All evidence came up, juvenile Cattle Egret.
Also, 2 of our Weminuche Audubon birders (much better birders than me) reported to me that this was a Cattle Egret.  My photos clinched the deal.

Resources Used

3 bird books, 2 websites, 2 other birder's observations and my photos.  See previous statement. I also checked ebird for Archuleta County and found only 1 other report for this species in Archuleta County.

Previous Experience

This is my first ever Cattle Egret.  I've been birding since 1982 and have observed Great and Snowy Egrets many times.
