Habitat

The bird was captured at the banding station located in the riparian forest, consisting of mainly cottonwood and willow tree species, surrounding Barr Lake.

Description of the Bird

The bluish-gray crown and face contrasted boldly with the olive nape and back. The wing bars were whitish, and the lores and eye rings, which were broken in front of the eyes, were thick and white. The throat and underparts were white with yellowish olive flanks. With the exception of the tertails, which were edged in white, the edging of the secondaries was green. The edging of the outer rectrices was distinct and white. This BHVI was aged as Hatching Year due to the pale, grayish white upper mandible lining and incomplete pneumaticization of its skull.
Behaviors: Normal behaviors of the Blue-headed Vireo were not observed during its capture and banding.

Plumage: blue crown olive upperpts white underpts

Similar Species Discussion

Several plumage criteria separated this BHVI from the other two Solitary Vireos. The bluish-gray crown and face of this bird contrasted boldly with the olive nape and back, which is characteristic of a BHVI, whereas the crown, nape, and face of a CAVI contrasts only slightly with its olive back, and the upperparts of a PLVI are gray overall and usually lacking in olive wash. The flanks of this BHVI were mainly washed with yellow and contrasted distinctly with the white breast and belly; the flanks of a CAVI are washed olive and contrasts little with its breast and belly, and the flanks of a PLVI are washed grayish. The outer rectrices (R6) were distinctly edged in white on this BHVI; this is a characteristic that is usually lacking in CAVI. This BHVI can also be separated from the PLVI based on its overall size. The difference between the wing chord (75 mm) and tail (55 mm) measurements for this BHVI was less than the typical range of a PLVI. The bill, with an exposed culmen of 9.5 mm, was smaller than the bill of a PLVI.
