Habitat

Riparian woodland, with cottonwoods, Russian-olive, American hackberry, tamarisk. The understory is an allergy-riling assortment of exotics including giant ragweed. Also fairly good stand of golden currant shrubs.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  8X42 B&L Elite binocular.
Distance:  Average distance was about 15 yards.
Light:  Sunny day, light somewhat filtered by cottonwood overstory above the height of the bird. Sun was to the south. I was east of the bird, looking west. Good light conditions.

Description of the Bird

Typical "large" vireo with longish tail and longish, thick bill (bill too thick for a warbler). Basically a "bright" bird for a vireo, gray on the head, greenish on the back, yellowish wash on the upper sides, whitish on the belly. Immediate impression was "Solitary" group. Broad, white eyering, broken slightly at the front by an incursion of the medium gray that colored the rest of the head. Bill gray. Eye black. Head color mousy gray and even (but not blackish). Demarcation between the head and the white of the throat not razor sharp in close view (rather, when photos were cropped) but from a distance this seemed fairly sharp, more so that Cassin's usually looks to me. Back olive green, unstriped. Wing grayish-olive, with two, broad, light yellow wingbars. Sides of the breast washed with olive, fading to strongly yellowish along the flanks just below the leading edge of the primaries. Front of breast washed with very light yellow band that went completely across to the other side, fading to light gray on the lower breast and belly with just a tinge of yellow on the belly. Undertail fairly bright white. Wings mostly black with whitish edges to the primaries. Tail of average proportions for a "solitary" vireo type (not somewhat stubby like a Philadelphia or Hutton's). Did not note leg color.
Behaviors: Foraging in cottonwoods at a height of 10-20 feet up, fairly active but not "flitty" or rapid like a typical warbler or kinglet. I believe it was after rough stinkbugs (Brochymena spp.), as I have seen "solitary" vireos do on numerous occasions in eastern CO riparian areas. Did not see it interact with any other birds or ever figure out what was the source of its irritation (i.e., the reason for its scold notes on occasion).
Call: heard the harsh scold note a few times ("sshepp..sshepp..sshepp" (?) hard to describe, otherwise silent
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Black-headed is eliminated on the basis of head not being black enough, the position of the break in the eyering (below the front of the eye vs B-h which breaks above the front of the eye), and the lack of red coloration to the eye. Cassin's is the species closest to the bird in question. This bird seemed brighter than even the brightest Cassin's, with a stronger demarcation between the pale neck and darkish gray head. The yellow wash contrasted strongly with the greenish-gray of the back. Head and back contrast was also fairly obvious (gray vs. green, respectively). Too colorful to be a Plumbeous. White eyering on this bird and lack of white supercillium eliminated Red-eyed/Yellow-green Vireos.

Resources Used

Standard field guides, mostly Sibley.

Previous Experience

Lots of experience with Blue-headed in East, have seen more than 10 in the West. Lots of experience in Colorado with Cassin's (75+ birds).
