Description of the Bird

The first bird we encountered when entering First Creek Park from the north, moving only a few feet from the ground in a cottonwood, where photographs were taken. We lost it higher in the same tree but then repeatedly encountered it at slightly greater distance at all heights in additional cottonwoods as it slowly worked eastward along the creek.

See photos. Seems like it would need to be a very bright and clean Cassin’s otherwise. Dark blue-gray head (evoking more of a Plumbeous than the average Cassin’s) contrasting well with greenish back, clean white throat and spectacles sharply contrasting with head plumage with a well defined margin, not diffuse at the edges. Bright yellow flanks and vent strap, contrasting with clean white below. 

Similar Species Discussion

Cassin's Vireo, more typical of the region and seen at this location early in the year, and more regularly in Denver, was considered carefully since bright birds can be very difficult if not impossible to separate. I consulted this guide thoroughly in reviewing my memory of observations and the photos I took:
https://www.surfbirds.com/Features/Solitary_Vireos/identification.html

See my description text for the specific features considered. Additionally, I considered the time of year, at which any vireo would be considered rare in Denver, as an additional point in favor of the possibility of a Blue-headed, which during the fall was observed at other locales in the region including Barr Lake State Park nearby a week earlier.
