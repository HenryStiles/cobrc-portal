Description of the Bird

Most obvious field mark were extremely prominent white wing bars, eye ring and secondary edging. Throat was bright white contrasting well with dark slaty color of the head. Yellow wash on the flanks was clearly defined and a bright yellow color. The dark slaty head contrasted well with a greenish back.
Behaviors: Bird was foraging in the open in a few small trees.
Call: Silent
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Obviously a bird in the Solitary Vireo complex. Yellow wash and greenish back eliminate Plumbeous Vireo. My original reaction to the bird was to note the very bright white edging on the secondaries and bold white wing bars. This is a good indicator of Blue-headed as Cassin's should have more limited white in the edging as well as smaller wingbars. This field mark was so prominent it was visible with the naked eye when I first saw the bird. Other feature that support the identification was the bright white throat contrasting sharply with the dark head, Cassin's Vireo should show a diffuse border between throat and the slate color. The yellow wash on the flanks was a bright yellow, some Cassin's can have bright flanks other ID marks were more important in my judgement. Finally the head contrasted well with a greenish back. The bird I observed was clearly two toned, with a dark slate colored head and a greenish back. The transition between the two colors was very sharp. All these features made the bird look very bright and outside the range of Cassin's Vireo.
