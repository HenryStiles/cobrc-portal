Description of the Bird


I observed a Blue-headed Vireo (BHVI) at 6:30 pm and watched it forage for 10 minutes.  The bird was foraging in a boxelder tree and then flew into some cottonwood trees and stayed at mid canopy height gleaning insects from leaves.  The location of the sighting was McMurry Natural Area in Fort Collins Larimer county at Latitude -40 36’ 05.84” , Longitude 105 05’ 11.00 , elevation = 4,979 feet.  This individual had bright yellow flanks as seen in the attached picture (BHVI_1) and very bold white spectacles with a strong contrasting dark gray head.  The second picture (BHVI_2) shows a clean white throat characteristic for this species.  I took the pictures using my smart phone through my Leica Ultravid 8X42 binoculars.  This BHVI had a much more defined yellow flank than a Cassin’s Vireo as well as a darker gray head and clean white throat.  No vocalization was given.
