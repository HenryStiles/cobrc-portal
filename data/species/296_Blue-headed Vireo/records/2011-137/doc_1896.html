Habitat

Riparian habitat of the Uncompahgre River, shrubs, narrowleaf cottonwoods and a few P/Js.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski 10 by 42. Brenda was using Swarovski 8 by 32.
Distance:  20 feet, close to my near focus
Light:  Lightly overcast. We we looking to the southeast and the sun was to our southwest but we had filtered light which produced very good viewing conditions.

Description of the Bird

The bird was about the size of a warbler. The bill was heavy, not thin like a warbler but longer than a sparrow's bill. The back was olive and the two wingbars had a noticable yellow ting. The tail was average in length and uniform in color expect some lighter color on the other feathers seen only in flight. Vireo was very brightly colored with a lot of yellow on flanks and lower belly. The throat was white and set off a great contrast with the blue-gray head. The spectacles, white, were very bright against the blue-head.
Behaviors: Vireo was sitting in a juniper tree. As we watched the bird it sat very still for a minute or two then started to move about. Without warning it flew directly across the Uncompagre River to narrowleaf cottonwoods and began to feed. The bird was now some distance away so we could not see just what it was up to.
Call: None heard
Plumage: adult or hatch year male

Similar Species Discussion

Plumbeous, Cassin's and Blue-headed Vireos has similarities. Our local Plumbeous does not have yellow on belly and flanks. Cassin's do but I have never seen a Cassin's that had so much bright yellow on flanks and lower belly. The real clincher for me was the white throat and the bold white eye-rings and spectacles. Yellow-throated Vireo has yellow eye-rings and a yellow upper chest and throat.

Resources Used

National Geographic, Sibley's among others. I also check records in "Colorado Birds, 1992" but there is only a small attempt to split Cassin's and Blue-headed records. Cassin's are found each fall on the west slope but there are on three reports of Blue-headed that I know about. In western Colorado most of the Cassin's records are before the 1 October.

Previous Experience

I have spent a lot of time with all three of these vireos. Plumbeous nest near our house. Cassin's migrant through each fall. Brenda and I have spent 4 winters in Mexico and have seen many Blue-headed Vireos along the gull. Cassin's are common in west Mexico.
