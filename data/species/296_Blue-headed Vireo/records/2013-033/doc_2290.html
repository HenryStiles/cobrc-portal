Description of the Bird

Solitary Vireo type.

Blue-gray head with bright white spectacles.

Bright white chest and belly with bright yellow flanks and lower sides.

Sharp demarcation between gray of cheeks and the clean white throat.

Obvious contrast between the blue-gray head and the olive-green back.
Behaviors: Primarily working the tops of deciduous trees and spontaneously singing.
Call: Song matched eastern "Solitary Vireo" song.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The other "Solitary Vireo" species.

PLVI - should not have yellow on its sides and the transition from the head to the back is solid "plumbeous" - with no demarcation

CAVI - The yellow on the flanks was brighter than I've ever seen on a CAVI. The head had a very blue tone and the contrast between the head and the greenish back was quite dramatic.
