Habitat

Siberian Elms along and near a canal and ranch house isolated in the middle of short and mixed grass prairie.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Brunton 8.5 x 44 Epoch binoculars
Distance:  10-20 feet
Light:  Clear – partly cloudy; Light was good and the birds were seen in multiple lighting situation from full backlight to sun shining upon the bird with the sun to the observers back.

Description of the Bird

Small warbler-sized bird with a thick beak, slight hook at the tip. Dark gray head exhibiting a sharp demarcation between the hood and the white throat. Bold white “spectacles” contrasted with the gray hood. Sides of breast, and flanks with a yellow-green wash. Throat, center of breast, belly and undertail coverts clean white. Tail short. Back gray-green contrasting with gray hood. Wings gray with yellow-green edges to the flight feathers. Two bold white wingbars.
Behaviors: Two Blue-headed Vireos (BHVI) were found associating with each other and a small mixed flock of passerines (see notes at bottom of report). These birds were seen on the west side of Bent County Road 35 and 150 feet south of the canal. The BHVIs were foraging in Siberian Elms from near the tops to about half-way down. The group of birders watched these two for about 10-15 minutes. We then crossed the county road and birded along the canal to the east where we found a third BHVI!
Call: No vocalizations were heard.
Plumage: Fall

Similar Species Discussion

Warblers ruled out by the thick, slightly hooked bill and bold white spectacles.
Plumbeous Vireo – by the gray-green back contrasting with the head; extensive yellow-green wash on the sides and flanks and the yellow-green edges to the flight feathers.
Cassin’s Vireo – by the gray-green back contrasting with the head; the STARK contrast between the gray hood and the throat
Black-capped Vireo – Head black (or VERY dark gray in immature birds); olive-brown back; lack of bold white wingbars; immatures have a dirty white throat.
Other vireos – lack bold white spectacles and wingbars.

Resources Used
