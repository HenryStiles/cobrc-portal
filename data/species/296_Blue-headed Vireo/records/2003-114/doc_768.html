Description of the Bird

An obvious 'Solitary' Vireo (white spectacles on dark head, white throat, white wing bars), the bird had a strongly blue-gray head, except for a bright white throat that contrasted strongly and sharply with the blue-gray auriculars. The head also contrasted fairly strongly with the bright green back and rump. The underparts were white except for bright greenish-yellow flanks and vent strap.

The bill was slightly two-toned with a blackish culmen and a dark blue-gray to the base of the mandible. The eyes were dark, the legs were blue-gray.

The wings were black with two whitish wing bars and each remex was edged with white (outer primaries and tertials) or pale yellow (secondaries).
Behaviors: Foraging fairly lethargically in cottonwood
Call: none heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Cassin's Vireo can be quite similar, but even the brightest of such would not show such strong head-back contrast nor throat-auriculars contrast. Plumbeous Vireo is occasionally similar to the dullest of Blue-headed Vireos, but this was not at all a dull Blue-headed.
