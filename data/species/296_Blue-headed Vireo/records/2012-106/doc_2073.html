Description of the Bird

Brightly and contrastingly colored "Solitary Vireo." Deep blue-gray head contrasting sharply with snowy white throat. Moderately bright yellow sides without any infusion of olive. Back bright green (similar to that of TOWA in color) with moderately sharp demarcation with blue-gray nape. Relatively broad white outer tail edges. Secondaries brightly edged in yellow-green. Tertials with bright white tips. Spectacle quite contrasting and white. Wingbars bold and white.
Behaviors: Moved slowly through woodland, mostly at mid-level in tall thickets
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Even bright Cassin's Vireos do not show colors as bright as seen in this bird, nor as much contrast between auriculars and throat, nape and back.
