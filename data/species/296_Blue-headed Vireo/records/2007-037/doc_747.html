Description of the Bird

This was a very dull Vireo from the Solitary Vireo group. The bright yellow sides and flanks that faded around to the vent was too bright for CAVI. What led me to believe BHVI was the clean white throat with good clean contrast with the slaty-blue head. The head to back contrast was not very striking as it should be for BHVI. This was the only mark that was not solid for BHVI. The relatively bright green back color was good for BHVI. Another mark that was good for BHVI was the outer web of the outer rectrix (r6). This area was completely white and very wide, wider than CAVI would show.
Behaviors: none noted as bird was in hand
Call: vireo-like fussing in hand
Plumage: dull female definitive

Similar Species Discussion

Cassin's Vireo and Plumbeous Vireo. I feel both of these species were eliminated by the combination of plumage characters described above in the description
