Description of the Bird

The bird's plumage was very bright -- head blue-gray, contrasting with the bright green nape, back, and rump. The head had bright white spectacles, bright white chin and throat, with a sharp contrast between throat and auriculars; dark gray bill. The breast and belly were bright white and the flanks were strongly washed bright yellow. Both the primaries and secondaries were edged green. The rectrices were dusky with R6 edged white, R1-R5 edged green.
Behaviors: N/A - bird in the hand

Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

I eliminated Plumbeous Vireo because it is a larger bird with a measurably larger bill, and has a gray head and gray back with little to no contrast between the two. The primaries and secondaries are edged grayish, not green.

I eliminated Cassin's Vireo because its overall plumage is not as bright or contrasting -- its head is duller gray, contrasting slightly with a dull greenish-gray back. It has a weak contrast between the throat and auriculars. R6 is indistinctly or not edged white. Flanks are washed dull olive, not nearly as extensive and bright as Blue-headed.

Previous Experience
