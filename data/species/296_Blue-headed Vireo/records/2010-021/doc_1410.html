Description of the Bird

This was a spectacled vireo. It had a noticeable greenish back. From the side, its head was a bluish gray that contrasted with the dull greenish back. The flanks were yellowish that contrasted with the very white underparts. The undertail coverts were white and the tail was grayish on the central rects and greenish on the outer ones. The face was blue gray and where it had contact with the white chin and throat there was a distinct demarcation with the blue-gray face, not an indistinct one.
Behaviors: Sluggishly moved about, foraging in the blossoms of some low willows, eventually showing all of the characteristcs listed above.
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

The Solitary Vireo Complex are at times all somewhat similiar in appearance. This bird was separated from Plubeous Vireo by the extensive yellow wash on the flanks and the very green, not gray back.

CAVI comes closest and we in fact first thought our bird was a CAVI because of the back coloration. However, after we observed the face a number of times, we believed the contrast between face and throat was noticeably distinct, i.e. there was no gradual gradation of face color into the white of the throat, the textbook definition, but not the Ted Floyd definition. This was confirmed when we examined a number of photograhs taken in the field on our digital cameras.
