Description of the Bird

The short blunted bill was typical of a vireo, not like the sharp pointed bill of a warbler. The size of the bird was about the size of a typical warbler but the bird was chunkier in body. The head was slate gray with white "spectacles" around the eye that met in the front over the bill. The back was olive-gray. The tail was blackish with what looked like paler edges to the outer feathers. The wings were blackish with 2 yellowish-white wing bars and the tertials and secondaries had yellowish edging. The underparts were white at the throat (contrasting with the slate gray face). the white extended down the breast and the center of the belly. The flanks were yellow. The vent area and undertail coverts were yellow. The slate gray crown and nape contrasted noticeable with the olive-gray back. The bird would work slowly along the dead branches foraging for bugs. Dave Leatherman has photos of the bird.

Similar Species Discussion

The blunt bill tended to rule out most warblers. The thinish bill and coloration tended to rule out all sparrows. The contrasting slate gray head and white spectacles tended to rule out all of the vireo complex that have whitish superciliums and the Yellow-throated Vireo. The coloration rules out White-eyed Vireo and Gray Vireo. The larger size rules out Bell's Vireo and Black-capped Vireo. This leaves the "Solitary" Vireo complex. The olive gray back rules out the Plumbeous Vireo and the contrasting slate gray head to the back and the white throat rule out the Cassin's Vireo.
