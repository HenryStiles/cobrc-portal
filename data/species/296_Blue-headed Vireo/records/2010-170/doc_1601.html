Description of the Bird

Small warbler-sized bird with a thick beak, slight hook at the tip. Dark gray hood with a sharp line of demarcation between the hood and the white throat. Bold white “spectacles” contrasted with the gray hood. Sides of breast, and flanks with a dirty yellow-green wash. Throat, center of breast, belly and undertail coverts clean white. Vent with a slight yellow tint. Tail relatively short. Back gray-green contrasting with gray hood. Wings gray with discernable edge color to the flight feathers (maybe a worn adult?). Two bold white wingbars, the lower being "bolder".
Behaviors: The bird was foraging and feeding on insects it found in the Siberian Elms along the street.
Call: No vocalizations were heard.
Plumage: Fall adult?

Similar Species Discussion

Warblers ruled out by the thick, slightly hooked bill and bold white spectacles.
Plumbeous Vireo – by the gray-green back contrasting with the head; extensive yellow-green wash on the sides and flanks and the yellow-green edges to the flight feathers.
Cassin’s Vireo – by the gray-green back contrasting with the head; the STARK contrast between the gray hood and the throat
Black-capped Vireo – Head black (or VERY dark gray in immature birds); olive-brown back; lack of bold white wingbars; immatures have a dirty white throat.
Other vireos – lack bold white spectacles and wingbars.

Resources Used

National Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America, fourth edition used after the sighting when generating this report to specifically eliminate an immature Black-capped Vireo.

Previous Experience

I have seen a dozen or so of this species in Colorado, all in the fall.
