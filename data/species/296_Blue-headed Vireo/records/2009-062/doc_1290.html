Description of the Bird

Vireo of the " Solitary " Complex . Most striking aspect of initial view of bird was blue gray head contrasting strongly with pure white throat , white spectacles and even greenish upper back. Two strong white wing bars and faint yellowish wash on flanks.

For comparison purposes a dull probable HY Cassin's Vireo was seen a few minutes later which empasized the very strong contrast between gray head and white throat of the first bird.

Also a HY Blue-headed Vireo seen in the hand and banded by Brian Gibbons at Chico on 9/16 showed less contrast between gray head and throat
Behaviors: Bird was sheltering from very strong NW winds in the lee of the dam wall around the small pond at Chico Basin Ranch HQ , Pueblo County. This was the day of large migrant fall out with many Wilson's Warblers also staying low in bushes and dead Russian Olive branches. A Great -crested Flycatcher was also in the same area.
Call: No call
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Not Plumbeous Vireo as not uniform gray head merging into gray back. Not Cassin's Vireo because of stong contrast of blue-gray head with white throat and greenish back.
