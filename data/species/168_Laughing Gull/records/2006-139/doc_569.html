Description of the Bird

A gull, intermediate in size between Franklin's Gull and Ring-billed Gull; appearance while sitting (floating) mostly white head with dark gray smudges on or near auriculars, and paler gray fine streaking on nape (finer than the streaking of the nape of nearby basic plumage Ring-billed Gull); Head flatter than those of nearby Franklin's Gulls; white orbital around black eye evident; mostly black bill with red tip to upper mandible; bill longer than accompanying Franklins, with an angular gonys evident (unlike Franklin's). Underparts mostly white, with some gray smudging on breast; upperparts dark gray, matching shade of Franklin's Gull mantle; small white scapular crescent evident; larger white tertial crescent present; black wingtips extended barely beyond white tail, with just two white-tipped primaries evident (these probably had not finished growing yet). The white tips were smaller than those of accompanying adult Franklin's Gulls. Tops of legs observed to be dark (seen while swimming). In flight, a strong white trailing edge to secondaries was evident, and black wingtips seemed to be restricted to outer two or three primaries and then a primary with only the terminal half black (basal half gray), and then inner primaries fully gray.
Behaviors: Sitting on water, briefly flying low over water, sometimes feeding on food floating on water.
Call: none
Plumage: Adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gull smaller-bodied; rounder headed; darker-headed; smaller billed. Other "black-headed" gulls even smaller (Black-headed, Bonaparte's, Sabine's, Little); Black-headed Gull and Bonaparte's would be paler-mantled; Two "hooded" gulls of similar size that also are large-billed (even moreso than Laughing Gull) are Sooty and White-eyed Gull from the Middle East. Both would have darker gray or brown coloration to heads, even in basic plumage, and neither would have white tips to outermost two primaries. Adult Sooty Gull has pale bill. Gray-headed Gull from South America is similar in size and bill morphology, but has paler gray mantly, more striking gray-white-black wing pattern (which lacks white trailing edge) and has an evenly hooded appearance even in basic plumage.
