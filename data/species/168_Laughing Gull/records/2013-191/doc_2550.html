Description of the Bird

Bird was sitting on shore line, foraging along shore, and flying along shore. Intermediate in size between Franklin's Gulls and Ring-billed Gulls in its company. Elongated body like Ring-billed Gull. Dark gray upperparts with black wingtips. Whitish underparts darkening to pale gray on upper breast. Head pale, mostly whitish with gray smudge on nape and sides of the head. Black eye and bill. Flat-headed appearance. Dark gray legs and feet. Bill heavier and longer than that of Franklin's Gulls, and slightly drooped. Back feathers paler gray than definitive adult. In flight and when sitting, primaries totally black lacking white tips. Secondaries and inner primaries tipped white, forming a white trailing edge to the wing in flight. Outermost primary, p10, unmolted and worn on both wings. p9 apparently missing (dropped off) from both wings. Apparently almost fully molted into 2nd basic plumage. Tail was entirely white. No vocalizations.

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gull is smaller, rounder-headed, shorter-bodied and shorter winged, and smaller-billed. Other "hooded" gulls in North America are much smaller (Sabine's, Black-headed, Bonaparte's and Little). White-headed gulls in basic plumage can appear hooded, particularly Mew Gull which is about the same size and also with a dark gray mantle but has a pale bill and round head, and adults have much less black in wingtip. Younger Mew Gulls with extensive black in wingtips also have black tailbands. Black-legged and Red-legged Kittiwakes have smaller yellow bills and round heads. Grey-hooded gull (and other potential vagrants from South America and Asia, such as Brown-headed Gull) in basic plumage is similar in size and plumage but with extensive white in outer wing. Heerman's Gull has extensive black in tail, as do several species in South America that share some field marks with Laughing Gull: Black-tailed, Belcher's, Olrog's, Gray, and Lava Gulls.
