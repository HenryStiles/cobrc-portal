Description of the Bird

Laughing Gull is not listed in Huerfano County on CFO website. Hence documentation is required.

This was a juvenile plumaged bird standing on lake shore(Orlando Reservoir) near water's edge. It was mostly still & stood in an alert position due to my presence. It made no sound. After about 15 minutes it flew off & not seen again on 7-28-15 nor on subsequent days.

Jizz: Bird was a somewhat slender shaped gull with wings extending well beyond tail; it was slightly smaller than nearby Ring billed Gulls.

Head: Smoky unmottled brownish-gray head melding to a paler whitish on forehead & throat. Bill was solid black with a slight drooping angle at distal half; bill fairly prominent at least as long as head was wide.

Neck & flanks: Smoky unmottled grayish-brown with little contrast to nape & crown's rear. Undertail coverts were pale & whitish.

Back & wings: Heavily mottled brownish gray scalloped with white feather edgings & dark feather centers; Primaries were black showing no white markings.

Legs & eye were black. Legs somewhat proportionately longish for a gull; eye was largely surrounded by whitish color contrasting with smoky colored face.

Tail: I didn't see this well when bird flew off. 
 

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gulls including juveniles are not as mottled & darker on back & wings than juvenile LAGUs. In juvenile & winter plumages, head of Franklin's more blackish with sharper contrast with white forehead than on LAGUs. Black primaries on Franklin's often show smaill white markings. Flanks of juvenile Franklin's are white sharply contrasting with dark back. LAGU I saw had flanks washed in brownish-gray.
