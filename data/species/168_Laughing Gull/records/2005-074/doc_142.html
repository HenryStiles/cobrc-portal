Habitat

Prairie lake of about 100 acres with few trees; industrial park on its north side, cattle grazing on all other sides.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swift 15-60x zoom lens spotting scope
Distance:  50 yards estimated when bird was at rest. 150-200 yards estimated when bird was in flight.
Light:  Clear skies--bird was southwest of observer. On the whole light conditions were very good.

Description of the Bird

Size: medium size Gull about 3/4 size of nearby Mallards.

Head: smokey off-white with prominent black smudge behind eye. At most angles off-white nape did not contrast with rest of the head and face. But in certain light a pale dappling of gray appreared on nape, though dappling was difficult to see.

Body & wings: At rest, back was solid dark gray becoming a bit lighter in the wing coverts. Primaries were solid black, long, and extended well beyond tail. In flight the gull also appeared fairly long winged with dark gray mantle melding into darker black primaries. Rump and tail were white with narrow but prominent black terminal tail band appearing to extend to outer rectrices.
Underparts were smokey white and showed no discernible contrasts with off-white portions of head.

Bare parts: Bill was solid black, slightly downcurved at tip, and long, appearing as long as head length measured from nape to lores. Eye was solid dark. Legs and feet were solid black.
Behaviors: Bird was first spotted swimming with nearby Mallards. Flew to shoreline were it often stood motionless or walked about a few yards
Call: none given
Plumage: first summer

Similar Species Discussion

Immature Franklin's Gull shows extensive black on nape. In most plumages, Franklin's Gull exhibits white tips on primaries of birds at rest. Franklin's also have smaller bills than Laughing Gull. The former's bill is only about 1/2 size of head length measured from nape to lores.
