Description of the Bird

Gull, smaller than Ring-billed Gull, mostly white head with dark gray smudge behind eye extending to nape. Narrow, longish black bill. Upperparts gray (about the shade of California Gull) with white scapular and tertial crescents, and jet black wing tips (at rest). Underparts mostly white with pale gray wash to sides of upper breast. Legs black. Wings with long extension 1-2 inches beyond tail. In flight, wings long and pointed; slow wingbeats made the bird appear like a large gull in flight; Secondaries of wings and their coverts gray blending into all black primaries and primary coverts. Underwing appeared dark in flight (at a distance of several hundred feet). Trailing edge of wing white.
Behaviors: Walking along beach scavenging for food, flying over water and sitting on water
Call: none
Plumage: sub adult 2nd cycle

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was identified by noting:
1. Size smaller than Ring-billed Gull
2. Mantle dark gray (rules out Bonaparte's and Black-headed Gull)
3. Bill all dark, longer and heavier than expected for Franklin's
4. Primaries all black (lacking heavy white tips) and extending well beyond tail
5. Head lacking hooded appearance of subadult or winter plumage Franklin's gull.
6. Shape and flight pattern unlike Franklin's Gull.
