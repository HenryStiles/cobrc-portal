Description of the Bird

This was a medium-sized, dark gray mantled gull with a complete black hood. The bill was reddish. The wings were long with dark primaries folded well past the tail. There were small whitish eye arcs above and below a dark eye. The feet and legs were dark and the rest of the bird was white.
Behaviors: Loafing with Franklin's Gulls on a sand beach of an island.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Ths bird was compared with its look-alike Franklin's Gull. 1st summer FRGU can have a complete black hood and is a bird often misidentified as a LAGU. The LAGU was longer-bodied with longer wings with dark primaries. The white wing spotting was not visible but the spots were visible on the nearby FRGUs. The bill on the LAGU was stouter and noticeably longer than the bills of the nearby FRGU.

Little Gull and Bonaparte's Gulls are much smaller and were never considered. Black-headed Gull has a reduced amount of black hood on the back.
