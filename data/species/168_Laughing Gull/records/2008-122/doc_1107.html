Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized gull, slightly smaller than the Pomarine Jaeger that was seen chasing it, and about the same size as a Ring-billed Gull. The overall shape was similar to that of a Ring-billed Gull, but with relatively more slender wings that gave the bird a longer-winged impression. The overall shape appeared a bit more slender than that of a Ring-billed Gull as well. The bill was relative long, and I believe that I could see that it was "blob-tipped"; regardless, it appeared larger and longer relative to the size of the head than I would expect for a Franklin's Gull. It was completely black. I could not see the leg color.

The head of the bird was white, with a small patch of pale to medium gray from the eye back to the nape, kind of like a faded phalarope mark. There were also a few faint gray markings just above this, though these were hard to see. The remainder of the nape, and the entirety of the underparts were clean white.

The mantle of the bird was on the dark end of medium gray, about the same shade as a Franklin's Gull. The wingtips were extensively back, blending cleanly into the gray of the wings about a third of the way up the wing from the tip, with no white between the black and gray. The edges of the primaries and secondaries were white. The underwings were white, the underside of the secondaries were medium gray, darkening towards the primaries to where they were solid black at the tip; there was no white between the primaries and secondaries.

The tail was completely white.
Behaviors: The bird was first observed being chased by a Pomarine Jaeger. It then turned around and appeared to be chasing the jaeger, but soon took off in a different direction. I followed it as it flew around and eventually behind a tree where I could not see it. It was not observed again.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of Gull were ruled out by the medium size, clean dark end medium gray upperparts, long and thinner wings, gray in the head, and bill shape. Lesser Black-backed Gull was ruled out by the smaller size, different head pattern, black bill, different outer wing pattern, narrower wings, and different underwing pattern.

Franklin's Gull was ruled out by the larger size compared to the Jaeger, by the extensively black outer wings without white between the black and gray of the mantle, by the much darker undersides of the flight feathers, again with no white between the black of the outer primaries and gray of the secondaries, by the much paler head with limited markings, by the lack of white crescents over and under the eye, and by the larger bill.
