Description of the Bird

This interesting gull was standing about 5 feet from a typical Ring-billed Gull. This gull appeared smaller and darker, with some gray markings on its head. So it was obviously not a Ring-billed. I consulted my bird guide and came up with three adult non-breeding possibilities: Sabine's, Franklin's, or Laughing Gull. This gull had a relatively long bill, drooped at tip. It lacked the yellow tip of a Sabine's bill. It did not have the more extensive hood of a Franklin's, so it appeared to me to be a Laughing Gull, especially after I got home and could enlarge it on my computer screen.

The Sibley picture on page 211 shows a 2nd winter example that has some gray marking behind and below the eye, similar to this gull, so I guess it is a 2nd winter Laughing Gull. Also Peterson Gulls by Howell/Dunn shows on page 93 photo 13.9 a second-cycle non-breeding Laughing Gull with dusky neck sides. Perhaps a gull expert would rule this an adult nonbreeding Laughing Gull, not a second-cycle or 2nd winter. I would defer to the expert, am not sure.
Behaviors: A bit wary. When I got my tripod and camera out, the Ring-billed Gull flew away. The Laughing Gull pivoted from a lateral position to a rear one toward my camera. It looked back at me for a while, then took off, despite the long distance between us. There were no other birds at all on the west side of the reservoir near me. All the geese, gulls, and ducks were quite far away toward the east side of the reservoir next to I-76 freeway.
Call: None
Plumage: Maybe 2nd winter, per Sibley p. 211
