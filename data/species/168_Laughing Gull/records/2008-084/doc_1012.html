Description of the Bird

A teal-sized gull noticeably smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gull, white body, dark gray mantle, jet black hood, with white eye arcs, white tertial crescent, small white scapular crescent, jet black primaries extending well beyond tail, giving the bird a long, tapered appearance, black legs and feet, apparently black bill, long and heavy for a hooded gull, creating a longish, droopy appearance to the front of the bird.
Behaviors: Walking rapidly along muddy shoreline, foraging for food.
Call: not heard
Plumage: definitive adult

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gull shows much more white in primaries, and has shorter wings with less tapered look, shorter, smaller bill. Bonaparte's, Black-headed and Sabine's, Little Gulls all much smaller, smaller-billed, paler mantled.
