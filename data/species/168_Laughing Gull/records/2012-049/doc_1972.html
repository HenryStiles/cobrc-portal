Description of the Bird

Medium-small gull with a black head and white eye arcs. Mantle darker grey than that of a Ring-billed gull. Narrow white scapular cresent. Tertial cresent also a narrow white band. Primaries solid black. Legs black. Bill blackish red with slight downturn towards the end.
Behaviors: Did not see flying, only standing and stretching. Initialy was hidden behind other gulls on the Marina boundary logs. May have been sitting on the log initially. Saw only standard gull resting and preening behavior.
Call: none heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Other Black headed gulls:
Little Gull: pale wing tips, no eye arcs.
Bonaparte's Gull: slender straight bill, no scapular cresent
Black-headed Gull: no scapular cresent, lighter mantle, brown/black hood curves up to back of head.
Franklin's Gull: Brighter red bill, has large white spots on tips of primaries, broader tertial cresent than Laughing Gull. Has a more prominent "thicker" white eye arc.
