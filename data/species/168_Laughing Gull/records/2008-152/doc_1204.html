Description of the Bird

I observed a medium-sized juvenile gull on the south east shore of John Martin Reservoir. The bird was standing in a group of about 25 other gulls that included adult Ring-billed Gulls and adult Franklin's Gulls.

This particular individual was roughly intermediate in size between the RBGUs and the FRGUs The overall plumage was a warm, rich brown with some scaly appearance to the upper parts. The folded primaries were black and extended well beyond the mainly hidden tail. The bird had a prominent blunt black bill and fairly faint white crescent markings by the eye.
Behaviors: The bird was standing in a group of other gulls that allowed for size, bill and plumage comparisons.
Call: NA
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The bird's was bigger than the Franklin's Gulls and smaller than the Ring-billed Gulls that it was standing with. The size, warm brown tone and large black bill ruled out other gull species.
