Description of the Bird

This was a medium sized gull with a Ring-billed Gull nearby for size comparison. The dark bill was longish and noticeably drooped at the tip. It had the appearance of being long-bodied with long legs and long wings (although I only saw it perched and never in flight). The head at some dark feathers enough to show it as a dark-headed gull. The mantle was a few shades darker than the nearby RBGU. The dark wing tips did not show any white spots.
Behavior: Foraging in a cove, very close to (about a foot from) shore.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 2nd summer

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gull comes closest in size but it has wide, distinct eye arcs. FRGU has shorter wings and shorter bill and has more dark feathers in a different pattern on its head at this age. FRGU would show white spots on the folded primaries. Other dark-headed gulls have shorter, thinner bills and short bodies and wings unlike this LAGU.

Resources Used

Looked at a couple of field guides to see what other dark-headed gulls this comes closest to.

Previous Experience

I worked on the coast of Virginia at Assateague Island National Seashore for four summers where Laughing Gull was the most common gull species. This plumage was not common but it was viewable there during the summer months.
