Description of the Bird

The bird was an obvious gull, a little smaller than the size of the Ring-billed Gulls it was standing next to. The overall shape was fairly slender and attenuated, with long wings sticking out well beyond the tail, and a smaller head than RBGU giving it a more slender look than RBGU.

The face had a patch of black roughly rectangular shaped, and going from the eye to the nape, somewhat reminiscent of the face-mark of a phalarope. The crown, throat, and the rest of the face were white. The nape and back were a medium-dark slate-gray, and this gray color extended out onto the sides of the chest and upper flanks. The wing coverts were a mixture of medium brown and the gray color of the back, with the gray color predominating. The belly and lower flanks were white. The wing-tips were completely black, and with no white between the black and the brown/gray of the wing coverts. In flight, the black on the wing looked to be a between a third and half the wing. The tail was white with a very large, bold black tail band, that was at least the terminal third of the tail. In flight some very faint dusky markings could be seen on the underwings, but not as many as I would expect for this plumage. This may be due to the fact that I never got a great look at the underwing.

The bill was fairly long (a little more than half the head length), and black. It was heavier than I would expect for Franklin's Gull, and had a very slight droop towards the tip. The legs were entirely black.
Behaviors: The bird spent most of its time loafing around on sandbars with other gulls, though it sometimes flew over the reservoir, and would dissapear for long periods to who knows where.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st basic

Similar Species Discussion

Most species of gull were ruled out by smaller size, as well as black patch on the face, clean gray back and gray on sides of chest and flanks, and smaller head.

Ring-billed Gull was ruled out by slightly smaller size, black patch on face, darker gray back with gray on sides of chest and flanks, longer bill that was black, and more slender shape.

Bonaparte's Gull was ruled out by the lack of a white wedge on the upperwing, as well as darker gray back, heavier build, larger size, and gray on sides of chest and flanks.

Franklin's Gull was ruled out by the gray nape, more limited black on face, limited to an area behind the eye, rather than a half helmet, very large and bold black tail band, longer and thinner wings, with very extensive black on the outer half of the wings, no white between the black and gray/brown of the wing coverts, longer and thicker bill, gray on sides of chest/flanks, and overall more attenuated and longer shape.
