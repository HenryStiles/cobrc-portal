Description of the Bird

[Note: this record is a replacement for my original 9/4/12 laughing gull record, which contained errors.]

Key features I used to identify the bird as "laughing gull" are (1) dark legs; (2) long, thick bill that droops slightly at the tip; and (3) extensive black and gray on the undersides of the wings. Other characteristics -- gray uppersides of wings and white belly -- are also consistent with those shown in Sibley (2011). The plumage was either "adult nonbreeding" or "second winter", distinguished by minor differences in amount of dark streaking on the head and gray wash on the neck and sides of breast. Since the head streaking and gray wash appeared to be very limited, the plumage description of "adult non breeding" seems more appropriate. No other birds were near the gull, so a size reference is not available.
Behaviors: The bird was initially moving along slowly near the southwest shore of Harrence Lake, occasionally spreading its wings. Just before I left, the bird flew, circling the west side of the lake a couple of times.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult nonbreeding

Similar Species Discussion

The dark legs and long, thick, all-dark bill distinguish the laughing gull from the similarly-sized, more common ring-billed gull. The size of the bill and the pattern of gray streaking on the head distinguish laughing gull from Franklin's gull.
