Description of the Bird

[Note: this record is a replacement for my original 8/18/12 laughing gull record, which contained errors.]

Key features I used to identify the bird as a laughing gull are (1) dark legs, (2) long, thick bill that droops slightly at the tip, and (3) size -- similar to that of a ring-billed gull that it was initially next to, larger than that of a Franklin's gull that it was next to later. The classification of the plumage as "adult nonbreeding" is based on the dark bill and limited gray streaking on the head.
Behaviors: Immediately after I first saw the laughing and ring-billed gulls on an island in Harrence Lake, the laughing gull flew. Several minutes later, I saw the laughing gull again, standing on a very small island in Harrence Lake. During the few minutes I watched it, it remained on the small island. The laughing full walked toward a nearby Franklin's gull when the latter flew in from an unknown location.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult nonbreeding

Similar Species Discussion

The size comparison with ring-billed gull eliminates small and large gulls. The dark legs and long, thick, all-dark bill distinguish the laughing gull from ring-billed gull. The size of the bill and the pattern of gray streaking on the head distinguish laughing gull from Franklin's gull.
