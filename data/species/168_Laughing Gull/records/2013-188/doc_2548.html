Description of the Bird

I first noted the gull standing among a group of Ring-billed Gulls. I was surprised to see such a dark gull that appeared, initially, to be the same size as a Ring-billed. As the birds shuffled about, I was able to tell that it was a bit smaller than the nearby RBGUs but distinctly larger (later seen in direct comparison) than Franklin's Gulls. The primaries were black and quite long, with substantial extension beyond tail when perched. The mantle was medium dark brown (when bird perched) with a similarly brown head and chest. The belly was paler. There was some scaly edging visible on the back. The area in front of the eye and the frontmost portion of the forehead where dingy whitish. A narrow broken eyering was present. The bill was black and larger than that of a FRGU with a noticeable gonydeal angle. The overall impression gave a slight drooped look, which I've noted in LAGU before but not FRGU. The legs were black or blackish

In flight, underwings ween poorly (as I was rather above the bird), but extensive dark on the undersides of the primaries was noted. In flght, the upperwings were darkish brown, with blackish-brown secondaries and the outermost half of primaries (all primaries, not each individual primary) were dark brown. As the bird wheeled low over the reservoir, I could see a modestly contrasting grayish tinge to the greater secondary coverets. The rump was white as was the basal half of the tail, the distal half being black. 

Similar Species Discussion

The only truly similar bird would be Franklin's Gull. This bird was larger than a FRGU, had more extensive brown on head, neck and chest, had a narrower eyering, and had more black on underside of primaries, plus more black on uppertail. The bill was longer and thicker than that of a FRGU. Note that this bird appeared to be in juvenile plumage, not first winter.
