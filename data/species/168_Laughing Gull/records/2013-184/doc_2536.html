Description of the Bird

Moderately dark gray mantled gull (appeared darker than FRGU). Above, blackish outer primaries and secondary bar contrasting with gray inner secondaries. Remainder of upperwing gray, except some white on trailing edge. Underwing showed dark outer primaries; remainder of underwing not seen well. No white on outer primaries.

Rump and tail appeared white (limited dark on tail could've easily been missed). Head white with gray smudging on nape forming. Landed next to RBGU, which it approached in size and thus rather larger than FRGU. Long wings. Bill thicker and longer than that of FRGU. Bill color could not be determined. Plumage tattered.
Behavior: Perched on edge of mud at drying reservoir, away from other gulls. Flew past us and landed next to a RB Gull. Did not associate with many Franklin's Gulls present
Call: not heard
Plumage: probably 2 year old bird

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gull should have more dark on head, be smaller in size, more delicate bill, shorter wings. Bird very similar to second-cycle bird at http://erikjohnsonphotography.blogspot.com/2011/07/age-related-timing-of-laughing-gull.html
