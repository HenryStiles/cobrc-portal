Habitat

Sitting on a few lakes

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovski Spotting Scope - HTS 65 HD
Distance:  1st - 150 feet, 2nd - 100 feet
Light:  1st sighting - the bird was to the east of us and the sun was to the south-east, but the clouds were covering the sun. Could see the bird very well.
2nd sighting - the bird was also to the east of us and the sun was also to the east of us, made for poor lighting, but we were still able to get great looks for the ID.

Description of the Bird

When it was sitting: The bird was slightly larger than the Franklin's Gulls that was around. It had black primaries, a dark gray mantle and scapulars (the same color throughout the back, not dark and light gray), it had gray on the hind neck and sides of the breast area. quite a bit of black on the head (not as much as 1st summer, but more than 2nd winter). Had a larger bill than Franklin's Gulls and it was drooped at the tip, it was a black bill, and there was no black on the tail while it was sitting.
When it was flying: It had very little black on the tail and extensive black on the upper and lower primaries.
Behaviors: It was around Franklin's Gulls, but it didn't habg out with them. Swimming around in the water.
Call: didn't hear it
Plumage: In between 1st Summer and 2nd Winter

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gull - Franklin's has more white on the wing. They have white spots on the upper primaries and they have less black on the lower primaries. They have a smaller bill. It couldn't have been a Juvenile or 1st winter because the bird was the same dark gray everywhere on the back and everything else older than that has more white on tehe primaries. 1st summer Franklin's Gulls don't have any black in the tail. They don't have very much gray on the hind neck and sides of the brest. During flight it has very little black on the primaries!!!!
Other Small Hooded Gulls (light backed) - The other Small Hooded Gulls have lighter gray backs, Too much or too little black on the head, all but Black-headed Gulls are a lot smaller and Black-headed Gull has a red bill.
White-headed Gulls - They don't have the amount of black on the head that this bird had. All but Mew Gull and Ring-billed Gull is a lot bigger than this bird. Mew Gull and Ring-billed Gull has a light gray back.
Kittiwakes - All but Juveniles have yellow on the bill and juvenile Black-legged has a black M shape on the back and Red-legged has a bill that is too short.
Ross's Gull and Sabine's Gull - They are both too small. Ross's has very little black on the head and Sabine's either have a black tip to the bill or they look very brown.

Previous Experience

I have a lot of experience with Franklin's Gulls from watching them here in Colorado. I have watched Laughing Gulls in Texas and in Florida, got great photos, and studied them so I would be abla to ID a Laughing Gull if I saw one in Colorado or anywhere else.
