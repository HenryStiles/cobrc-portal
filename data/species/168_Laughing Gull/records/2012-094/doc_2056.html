Description of the Bird

Black-hooded gull that appeared as large as nearby Ring-billed Gulls and substantially larger than 2 nearby juv FRGUs. Head black, with black extending farther down anterior neck than posterior neck. Narrow white crescents above and below eye. Bill blackish with some reddish hues. Bill distinctly thicker, and longer, than that of FRGU, giving an slightly curved look to bill. Distinct gonydeal angle, but nowhere as heavy as Herring Gull, for instance. Back slaty gray, darker than that of nearby California Gulls. Folded wings same color as back, except black primaries devoid of white mirrors; also bird had very narrow white crescent at distal end of tertials separating the gray from the black. Legs black or blackish. Overall structure longer winged (wingtips extended well beyond tail tip) and flatter headed than Franklin's Gull, giving the bird a more aggressive look.
Behaviors: Stood amongst mostly Ring-billed Gulls, with a couple California and 2 juv Franklin's Gulls present.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Adult Franklin's Gull is smaller, with more petite bill, shorter wings, and usually with white spotting on folded primaries evident. Also, Franklin's Gull with rounder, less flattened forehead.
