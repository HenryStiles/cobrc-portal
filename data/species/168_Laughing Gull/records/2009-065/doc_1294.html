Description of the Bird

A medium-sized gull distinctly larger than nearby Franklin's Gulls and slightly smaller than nearby Ring-billed Gulls that this bird was associating with in a mixed-species flock. This gull was easy to pick out in the flock as its grayish body coloration stood out from the whitish body coloration of the other two species. Besides overall body coloration, I paid particular attention to its bill's color, size and shape, which was black, considerably longer than those of nearby Franklin's Gulls, and slightly drooped at the tip. The bill looked slightly longer and thicker than those of nearby Ring-billed Gulls. The whitish facial feathering around the bill extended onto the forehead and throat, contrasting slightly with the light to medium grayish head and nape. All of the Franklin's Gulls nearby had white napes. White, incomplete eye arcs bordered the eyes in a similar pattern to Franklin's Gull. The mantle, upper breast, and scapulars were light to medium gray. The wing coverts were dark brownish and the blackish primaries lacked any white edging or apical spotting, in stark contrast to Franklin's Gull. I did not see the tail or legs.
Behaviors: Loafing in a mixed-species gull flock on the reservoir.
