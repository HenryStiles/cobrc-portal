Description of the Bird

The bird was standing with other larger gulls.  The wing tips were black, and were going way beyond the tail (this elimnates Franklin's Gull).  The bill was all black, the legs were black and thin.  The mantle was very dark gray.  See photos.

I didn't hear any noises.  

I saw the bird several times between 1 Sept 2013 and 21 Oct 2013 at Pueblo Reservoir.  Mostly at Sailboard Launching Area.  On October 5th, Bill Maynard and I saw it fly by at Hobie Cat Road.  On October 21, I saw it on the water at the SWA on NW side of Pueblo Reservoir, with Margie Joy, Clif Smith, and Pearle Sandstrom-Smith.

Similar Species Discussion

Franklin's Gull -- has more black on head, wings don't go beyond the tail.  See photos.

Previous Experience
