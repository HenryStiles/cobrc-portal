Description of the Bird

I was birding with a group of birders from Durango, and was ahead of them a little ways, they were stopped behind me looking at birds.  I didn't notice they were a little ways behind me.  I was looking into a Russian Olive tree, and noticed a warbler sized and shaped bird toward the top of the tree, though somewhat hidden.  I could see the following:  bright yellow on the throat and belly, with dark streaks going below the throat, like a necklace.  The head was gray with a bold white eye ring, the back and wings was also gray without wing bars, the bill was thin like a warbler.  I quickly figured out that this was a Canada Warbler, and called out for the group.  The bird moved to the other side of the Russian Olive, and we all looked for the bird, and couldn't re-find it or any other bird.  I'm not sure where it went.  Since I could only see the bird for 20 seconds at the most and somewhat hidden, I wasn't able to see all of the bird, though I saw some important parts of it, including the gray head and back, necklace of streaks, on a bird with bright yellow underparts, plus the bold eye ring and wings that didn't have any wing bars.  I couldn't see the undertail coverts or tail very well.  I think the bird was either a female or immature bird.  It wasn't an adult male, the streaks weren't as bold as an adult male would have.  This bird was not far west of Valco Ponds, parking lot in Pueblo, below Pueblo Reservoir dam.

Similar Species Discussion

Wilson's Warbler - which is the most common warbler around, at this time, doesn't have gray back, streaks like a necklace or eye ring.
The gray head and back with eye ring, and lack of wing bars all elimnate other species, like Magnolia Warbler.
