Description of the Bird

The bird was yellow underneath with verticle black marks from an necklace on its breast. The back was blue-gray. The face was stricking especially the bold eyering which had yellow on the top part and yellowish white on the bottom of the eyering. Just behind the bill was a black patch which extended up to the front of the crown.

The lore was yellow contrasting with a black patch just below the eye, which extended to the yellow chin and throat, making it a very stricking bird. The black "necklace" broke up the yellow of the throat and breast.
Behaviors: First found foraging low, only a couple feet off the ground and then a minute later foraging in small willow about five feet off the ground. Very nervous, fidgety feeder.

Later I saw it move about high in a Siberian elm.
Call: non heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

No others with that head and breast pattern of yellow and black. Kentucky somewhat similar but without black breast streaks.
