Description of the Bird

A wood warbler, blue-gray above, yellow below, white undertail coverts. A dark face with bold whitish eyering, dark lores with yellow supraloral. Dark streaks forming a neckless on the breast.
Behaviors: Feeding in deciduous trees and shrubs, from below eye level to 15 feet high or so. The bird was never in the treetops of the tallest trees.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Nashville Warbler is superficially similar, but is green above, lacks dark neckless and white undertail coverts (among other differentiating marks)
