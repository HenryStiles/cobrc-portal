Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized warbler, about the same size as nearby Black-and-white Warblers, and a little larger than nearby Wilson's Warblers. The bill was black with some pinkish coloration towards the base. The legs were a dusky pinkish color.

The forecrown of the bird was a dusky olive-greenish color. The remainder of the crown, face, nape, back, wings, and tail were all a clean slate-gray color. The bird had a bold yellow supraloral and a bold white eyering. The malar was gray with a few black feathers mixed in. The underparts were bright yellow, with the exception of the white undertail coverts. There was a necklace of dusky gray streaks on the chest. These were noticeable, but not particularly bold.
Behaviors: the bird was mostly by itself, though occasionally it seemed to be in the company of two Black-and-white Warblers or a few Wilson's Warblers. It foraged at mid to upper levels in the trees, but once came down to below eye level.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic female or first basic male

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of warbler were ruled out by the clean gray upperparts, yellow underparts with dusky necklace, and face pattern.
