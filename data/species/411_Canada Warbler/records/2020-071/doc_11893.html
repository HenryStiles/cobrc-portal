Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Canada Warbler
First Date/Time:  5/3/2020 4:30:00 PM
Last Date/Time:  5/3/2020 6:20:00 PM
Duration (total time in view):  ~ 1.5 hours
County:  Kiowa
Specific Location:  Queens Reservoirs (Neeskah Reservoir area)
Number:  1
Age:  Adult
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Breeding

Habitat

Wooded area next to reservoir. Bird was mostly feeding somewhat low in brush in line of small trees closest to reservoir.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x42 binoculars
Distance:  Closest observation was ~ 6 feet. Range was 6 ft. to 30 feet, but moss observation was less than 15 feet.
Light:  Clear skyies; bright sun

Description of the Bird

Crown, nape, back, rump, uppertail coverts, top of tail and wings when closed were a consistent slate-gray.  No wingbars.  Bold bi-colored eyering, yellow on top, white on bottom.  Yellow subloral stripe extending from bill to front of eye.  Black lores and throat boarder.  Plain yellow throat with bold black necklace with black stripes extending down necklace into breast.  Plain bright yellow breast, sides and belly extending just past tarsus.  White undertail coverts.  Long tail.  Tail underside dark-ish gray.  Pink legs.  Bill with wide base was dark gray, but more flesh-colored on lower mandible near base. 

Only vocalizations noted were a couple low, falling chips.

Bird was actively forging in understory--primairly in shorter row of trees closest to reservoir in wooded area that is seperated from larger trees by a small area of clearing with some bush-like vegitation.  Forging occurred mostly at eye-level or lower, but bird did occassionally forge slightly above eye level.  Noted tail flipping during forging.    

Similar Species Discussion

Magnolia warbler considered, but eliminated based upon bi-colored eyering.  Consistent slate-gray back.  Given close range observation and very distictive flied marks of this species, not many other similar species were considered or needed to be eliminated.  

Resources Used

Sibley Field Guide and The Warbler Guide. 

Previous Experience

Seen Canada Warbler on mumerous occassions while birding in Eastern US.  Also have seen Magnolia Warbler many times in Eastern US.
