Description of the Bird

The CAWA had a gray back, yellow front including breast and face, creamy-white eye ring and black necklace. The necklace was not as bold as others I've seen so I would guess this was a first year male or a female.
Behaviors: I observed the CAWA for about ten minutes feeding on the ground and flying into low tree branches. I got as close as 10-12 feet at one point.
Call: None heard
Plumage: See description below

Similar Species Discussion

I knew that a CAWA had been seen in the Woods. I identified the warbler by the yellow front, necklace, and white eyering.

Resources Used

I saw many CAWA's up close when I lived for 2-1/2 years in Minnesota. They would often come to our campsite during the summer months.

I also checked Sibley's for the sex and age due to the lighter necklace than I had seen on other occasions.

Previous Experience

Viewing CAWA's in Minnesota.
