Description of the Bird

The bird was a distinctive yellow and grayish-blue warbler. The upperparts were bluish-gray, though the forehead was yellow, and the underparts were bright yellow, except for distinct dark streaking on the chest forming a vague necklace and bright white undertail coverts. There was a pre-ocular spot of darker blue overtopped by yellow suparloral stripe. The bird had eye ring that were mostly white, but fairly bright yellow in the upper-forward quadrant. The bill was mostly pink and the legs were yellowish.

Only now, while reviewing my pictures, I notice that the bird seems to have white or whitish on virtually the entire outer web of the R6s and even on the inner web of, at least, the right R6. I don't recall this species having that feature and the most recent field guides do not so illustrate them. I cannot explain the whitish in the rectrices, but it certainly seems to be real and not an artefact of the lighting, as the two photos that I have submitted both show it, despite quite different lighting.
Behaviors: foraging in elms and cottonwoods
Call: none heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

No other warbler species really comes close to the multi-colored eye ring and dark necklace of streaking on the chest.
