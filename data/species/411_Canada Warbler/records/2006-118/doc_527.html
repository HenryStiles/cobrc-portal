Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium-sized warbler, a little larger than nearby Wilson's Warblers. The bill was fairly thick and long for a warbler, but still thin and pointed. I did not note the color of the bill. The legs were dusky pinkish.

The bird's forehead was a dusky olive-green color. The crown, nape, and face were a clean slate-gray. The bird had a bold yellow supraloral connecting to a bold white eyering. The malar had a few blackish feathers mixed in with the gray. The upperparts were entirely slate gray, including the wings and tail. The underparts were bright yellow, except for white undertail coverts. The bird had a dusky gray necklace of vertical streaks on the chest that was fairly bold, but not strikingly bold like an adult male.
Behaviors: the bird was with a very large flock of mixed warblers. It was somewhat more lethargic than many of the other species present, but still quite hard to follow. I saw it from 30 feet high to below eye-level.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic female or first basic male

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of warbler were ruled out by the clean gray upperparts, face pattern, and yellow underparts with dusky necklace. None of the other species of warbler that are yellow below have clean gray upperparts without wingbars, and all have a substantially different face pattern.
