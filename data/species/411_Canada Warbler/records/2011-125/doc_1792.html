Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x40 binoculars.

Light:  It was bright and sunny, but the bird was frequently in shade because it was in the lower parts of the trees.

Description of the Bird

About the same size as the Yellow-rumped and Townsend's Warblers it was first seen with. The upperparts were uniformly gray, including the tail, and the bird did not have wingbars. There was a distinct and clear white eye ring, and a little yellow between the dark eye and bill. The chin, breast and underparts were completely yellow, except for a not-very-bright, but distinct, necklace of short gray streaks. This is what makes me think it was an adult female, although a first-year male is quite difficult to eliminate as a possibility. The under tail coverts were bright white, and the legs were quite light and not dark.
Behaviors: Seen feeding trees with other warblers, Gnatcatchers, etc, both fairly low and at mid-levels, not in the tree tops. A few times seen trying to catch insects, but unsuccessful.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Fall adult

Similar Species Discussion

No other North American warbler has this combination of no wingbars, white eyering, yellow underparts with a gray necklace, and white under-tail coverts.
