Habitat

Brushy area on edge of dry creek bed with large cottonwoods.

Description of the Bird

The bird was small (about the size of the nearby Wilson's Warblers) and the features that jumped out at first were the solid blue-gray back and yellow chest.  Once we got closer looks we noticed the bird had a complete white eye-ring.  There also may have been a slight hind of a necklace.  The bird was foraging low some shrubs and twice I heard a metalic chip that was different from the Wilson's Warblers that were in the area.  This chip likely came from the Canada Warbler.

Similar Species Discussion

The only other species that came to mind was Northern Parula.  The solid blue back, the complete white eye-ring, and the lack of a white belly eliminated this species. 

Resources Used

none.

Previous Experience

I have seen several Canada Warblers in the eastern US, but none as dull as this one.  I am very familiar with Northern Parulas.
