Description of the Bird

Was resting in the back of the banding station between runs when I noticed a small bird on the east side of the clearing working along the outer branches of a bush. The dark top and pale bottom immiediately intriged me, so I walked over to get a better look as the bird retreated into the bushes. I got a clear few of the front half of the bird and immediately recognized it, and raced back to get my camera and alert the others at the banding station. Upon returning 30 seconds later, I was unable to get a photo, and a few others were able to see the bird. 

My ebird notes: 

Dark above, yellow below. Mostly black head, with some black spots on breast forming a necklace of sorts. White, complete, bold eyering. Between banding station and river. Seen clearly, but couldn’t get a photo. Active warbler, in the undergrowth usually less than 10ft off the ground. Constantly moving yet almost always well hidden.

 

Submitting this several months later (completely forgot), I do not remember if it was vocalizing, but I don't think it was. Other birders later that day and in next two days obtained photos, which can be seen here: https://ebird.org/media/catalog?regionCode=US-CO-059&date.yearRange=YALL&taxonCode=canwar&mediaType=Photo&sort=rating_rank_desc

All photos of Canada Warbler in Jefferson County are of this record.  

Similar Species Discussion

Facial pattern rather unique. Complete eyering eliminated MacGillivray's, necklace eliminated the likes of nashville and mourning. Solid gray back and yellow in front of eyes eliminated Magnolia Warbler. 

Resources Used

Double checked a field guide after the sighting, but this bird was in perhaps its most recognizable plumage. 

Previous Experience

I had only heard a single Canada Warbler before this. I'd seen a few Nashville and Magnolia Warblers before, and a lot of MacGillivrays, but no Mourning Warblers.
