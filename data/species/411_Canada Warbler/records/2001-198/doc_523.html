Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized warbler, in hand about the same size as a Yellow Warbler. The shape of the bird was similar to that species as well. The bill was fairly typical of a warbler, thin and pointed. It was mostly very dark gray, but with a very faint pinkish tinge at the base. The eye was dark. The legs were dull flesh-colored.

The bird's cap was a slate gray, with a distinct greenish tinge on the fore crown. The nape and rear part of the auriculars were also this color. The bird had a bold yellow supraloral that connected with a bold white eye ring. The auriculars were slate-gray, with a noticeable black tinge bordering the throat. The throat and underparts were bright, but not intense, yellow, with the exception of the undertail coverts, which were white. There was a faint band of dusky streaks across the chest, but these streaks did not extend to any area outside the chest.

The back, wings, rump, and tail of the bird were the same slate-gray color as the crown and nape, creating uniformly colored upperparts. The underside of the tail was also slate-gray.
Behaviors: the bird was mostly seen when in the hand. When released, it flew to a nearby tree, and hopped around for a few seconds before flying away.
Call: none heard
Plumage: first basic

Similar Species Discussion

All other species of warbler were ruled out by the clean gray upperparts, yellow supra-loral and white eyering, faint dusky streaks on the chest, etc.
