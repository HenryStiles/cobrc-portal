Description of the Bird

No behaviors to note, or calls, as the bird was captured in a mist net, and processed in the hand. It was not seen again, once released. 

It was a relatively large warbler, larger than the Wilson's and Orange-crowneds I had been handling all morning, and maybe a little bigger than a Common Yellowthroat.

Obvious was the large, yellow and white eye-ring, the bright yellow throat and underparts, and the white undertail coverts. The back was bluish and the wings dark gray. The face was gray where not bright yellow, and there was a faint gray necklace.

In the hand I was able to age the bird by the width and edging of the primary coverts, and the relative width and roundness of the tail, and determined it was an After Hatch-year female.

Similar Species Discussion

With the large eye-ring - not MacGillivray's, since the ring was solid. Not Connecticut (which I have handled twice), with the body color, and white rather than longer yellow undertail. Not Magnolia - eye-ring too large, other coloration.

Resources Used

Also I was able to compare photos on my iPad from Stevenson and Whittle "Warbler Guide" and species, age and sex from the McGill Labs species guide for Canada Warbler, that I had already stored.  Also iBird photos from the app.  I had plenty of time - I was holding it!

Previous Experience

I've SEEN the bird several times in Eastern States, during migration, but not closely. This was the first time I'd handled/banded one.
