Description of the Bird

Size, coloration, and actions immediately suggested "warbler".  For a warbler, the first impression of this bird was it was big and long-tailed.  Overall the bird was gray above and yellow below.  It had a prominent white, complete eyering.  Lores yellow.  "Necklace" of fairly wide, short gray vertical stripes completely across the upper breast.  Legs yellowish.  Cap, back, wings, and upper side of tail deep gray, basically same shade throughout.  Underparts except for undertail rich yellow, including throat, breast, and belly.  Undertail white.  Did not see or photograph undertail pattern. Long, not overly wide tail was "wagged' on a regular basis.

Bird was quite active, staying mostly in the understory (mostly golden currant and wolfberry shrubs) of a cottonwood gallery forest along the west side of Willow Creek.  The bird often perched on the trunks and lower branches of the cottonwoods.  Occasionally it went to the ground.  Objects of its foraging seemed to be mostly unidentified moths and lacewings. 

Bird was silent.

Similar Species Discussion

I suppose Kirtland's looks most like this bird but Kirtland's has white wingbars, broken white eyering, and a black-striped back, none of which this bird had.  Grace's is mostly gray above and yellow below but has no eyering.  The facial pattern of Yellow-throated is much different, plus it has no necklace and its stripes are on the flanks, with yellow below going no further down than the lower breast.
