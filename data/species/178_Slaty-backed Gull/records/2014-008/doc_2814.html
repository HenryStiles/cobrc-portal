Description of the Bird

The dark-mantled gull in the upper right in the photos was observed sleeping and walking around on the ice shelf near the marina at Aurora Reservoir.  Observations made at the time of the sighting were:  (1) black back, (2) bright pink legs and feet, (3) slightly larger than most of the Herring Gulls but about the same size as the largest of the Herrings Gulls, (4) clean white head, and (5) pale eyes. Additional characteristics seen in the photos are a wide white tertial crescent, lightish bill color, and hints of a dark eye line. The gull was not observed in flight, and no sounds were heard from this particular bird.

This gull appears to be the same dark-mantled gull, later recognized to be the long-staying Slaty-Backed Gull, photographed at the same location 18 hours later by Kris Peterson:

The pattern on primaries 6-10 in Kris's photos confirm that the gull present the morning of December 16 was indeed the Slaty-Backed Gull.  The gull I observed on December 15 is a very good match in terms of overall size, back color, leg color, bill color, eye characteristics, and the wide tertial crescent.

Similar Species Discussion

The most likely dark-mantled, pink-legged gull at this location would be Great Black-Backed Gull.  The gull observed on December 15 is a better match for Slaty-Backed Gull in overall size (Herring-gull sized or slightly larger) and leg color (bright pink).  Great Black-Backed Gulls instead are typically much larger and have paler pink legs.
