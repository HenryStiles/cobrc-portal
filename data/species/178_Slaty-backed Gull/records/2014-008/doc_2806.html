Description of the Bird

Despite the long distances, the excelllent lighting allowed for surprisingly reasonable views. The bird was initially seen flying by, with sun directly to my back. At that time, I noted a bird heftier than a typical HERG (more like a GWGU in build in flight) with a black mantle. I was able to see the "string of pearls" above and below, and note that the flight feathers were dusky (not black or blackish) beneath, except between the "pearls" and the edge of the tip of the wing... a small area. I then immediately started shooting photos. We refound the bird well out on the reservoir near sunset. Then we watched it for ten minutes or more. It was stockier than the nearby HERG, with short primaries and a huge tertial crescent. It was easy to pick out due to its black back. The bill appeared straw colored. Precise size of bill not determined. Presence or absence of head marking hard to tell with certainty.

Similar Species Discussion

The wing pattern is truly unique to SBGU (or hybrids). The only character on this bird atypical for an adult SBGU in January was the lightly marked, or unmarked head. A hybrid with Vega or GWGU should be equally heavily marked on head. Looking at the Japanese Gull Site, photos taken on 5 January showed one bird with an absolutely white head and one with a lightly marked head out of 10-15 adult SBGUs. The back color was very dark, typical for SBGU, and would be atypical of a hybrid. The straw colored bill is an additional point in favor of SBGU. By mid-Feb, many SBGUs have white (or nearly white) heads

Resources Used

Ujihara's Japanese Gull website, mostly to check for white-headed birds in mid-winter

Previous Experience

I've seen about 15 SBGUs in mid-winter, from OR to BC plus one many years ago in CO. I've seen more in AK during summer, but that experience is of less importance. I've seen a number of Vega Gulls (100+, almost all adults during summer in AK). I've not seen any hybrids to my knowledge.
