Description of the Bird

 I was the finder of the bird.  1 bird only

Boyd Lake SP-far north parking lot on west side of reservoir.  Ice cover of the lake at the time approximately 80% 

I first picked the bird out with a group of gulls approx 150 yards off shore resting on the ice.  Mostly Herring Gulls.  I noted an Adult Glaucous Gull in that flock and 2 dark-mantled gulls.  One had bright yellow legs and the other had bright pink legs.  Immediately, my thought was either Western or Slaty-backed.  The mantle was too dark for a Vega Herring Gull.  The tertial skirt was wide and obviously larger than the Lesser Black-backed Gull it was standing next to.  It was visibly larger than the LBBG and smaller than the Glaucous Gull and comparative in size to the neaby Herring Gulls.  The smudy eye was obvious and at the distance looked like a smudgy streak through the eye.  The nape appeared slightly dirty but it seemed to me to be molting back into breeding plumage as the head and neck overall were quite white.  The bird had a blocky looking head with a thick neck.  The smudgy eye/dirty nape/blocky head look eliminted the Western Gull possibility.  While standing next to the LBBG, I noted that the white on the leading edge of the wings extended further past the covert feathers than it did on the LBBG.  I did not notice the "string of pearls" in flight at Boyd Lake.  I did note this in a subsequent sighting 20 minutes later at nearby Horseshoe Reservoir where I only got on the bird for a 30 second observation as it was flying away, presumably back to Boyd Lake where it was relocated a short time later by David Wade and Nick Komar.  I did not submit an ebird checklist for that location.  No vocalizations noted.  Unfortunately, no photos obtained.  Luckily, many photos were obtained by other birders.
