Description of the Bird

Behavior - the Slaty-backed Gull adult was loafing, sleeping, preening, and then flying from a gravel bar from the southwest portion of Horseshoe Lake in Larimer County. It relocated on a gravel bar just north on the same lake due to a juvenile Bald Eagle flying over. All of the gulls on that gravel bar took flight and relocated on two different spots. Luckily for us, the SBGU relocated on the closer gravel bar. The group also relocated to get better views and photographs up the road and onto the back deck of a resident. From this location, the SBGU took a few steps and then started sleeping.

Calls - none heard

Finder - found Sunday 3/20/22 at Boyd Lake State Park by Josh Bruening. Refound several days later by Nick Komar at Kyger Open Space. Refound on 3/26/22 by Joe Kipper as part of the scouting team during the field trip. 

Age/Plumage - Using the Gulls of North America, Europe, and Asia by Klaus Malling Olsen and Hans Larsson, I observed an adult bird that had almost completed the transition from winter/basic to summer/alternate plumage. Our bird most closely resembles image 518 in the …The stout looking adult had slaty-gray upperparts and large white tertial crescent. The legs were bubblegum pink, thicker and shorter compared to the bird’s size. The large head had light brown streaks with the neck showing light brown spots. Clearly the bird was almost completely transitioned to summer/alternate plumage. The iris was pale with brown smudging around the eye. The bill was of average length and bright yellow with a small bright red gonys spot. The orbital ring wasn’t visible in my photos or during the observation. The string of pearls was evident in the flying photo that I was able to take when the Bald Eagle flushed all the gulls off the gravel bar. Gulls of North America

Similar Species Discussion

Herring Gull – much smaller than SBGU with a lighter back color and lighter pink legs

Lesser Black-backed Gull – smaller body size, longer legs in proportion, yellow legs, darker back, and smaller tertial crescent to name a few than SBGU.

Western Gull – dark iris vs SBGU light iris. Less white or string of pearls in the wing tips. Smaller tertial crescent at rest.

Glaucous-winged Gull – longer legged, less stout, and obviously lighter backed than SBGU.
