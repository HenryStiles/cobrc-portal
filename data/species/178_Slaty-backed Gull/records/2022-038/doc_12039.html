Description of the Bird

Originally found by Josh Bruening earlier in the day further north in the reservoir. Refound later by David Wade on the south side. Large gull with very dark back, large white band near tips of the wings when perched, dark streak through the eye and some streaking on the head and neck. It spent most of the time resting and preening on the ice, but occasionally spread its wings and flew short distances. Also spent some time swimming and bathing.

Similar Species Discussion

Greater and Lesser Black-backed Gulls were considered, but this gull was much smaller than a Greater and larger than the nearby Lesser Black-backed gull. Also this bird had very pink legs vs. yellow for the LBBG. Similar in size to the abundant HERG, but significantly darker back than those and the legs were a darker shade of pink.
