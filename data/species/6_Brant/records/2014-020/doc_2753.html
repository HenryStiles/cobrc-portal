Similar Species Discussion

I believe this bird to be a Gray-bellied Brant because of its barely complete anterior neck collar (as opposed to widely separated, as in Atlantic Brant) and dusky underparts (as opposed to blackish, as in Black Brant, or white, as in Atlantic Brant). These marks would be less reliable in an immature, but this bird is an adult. Note that this birds plumage was somewhat worn, maarking the neck collar markings not as prominent as in some of the fresh plumaged birds in captivity at Flickr below

See discussion starting with photo at http://www.flickr.com/photos/36088296@N08/10684374354/ and going through photo http://www.flickr.com/photos/36088296@N08/10684541536/

Also, see  Mlodinow, S.G., and M. Axelson. 2006. Gray-bellied Brant: Identification and vagrancy. Birding 38:48-55 -- a PDF of which I'd be happy to provide
