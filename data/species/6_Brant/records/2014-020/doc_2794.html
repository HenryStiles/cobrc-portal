Habitat

Reservoirs on rural/suburban interface. Commercial and residential buildings nearby, as well as bare fields and short grass fields. On Oct. 25 I saw the bird along the south edge of Equalizer Lake. On Oct. 30, Nov. 13, Nov. 20, Nov. 26 and Dec. 1 I saw the bird on and next to adjacent Houts Reservoir. On Dec. 1 I also observed the bird in a bare agricultural field on the west side of Equalizer.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Pentax DCF WP 8x42 binoculars, Kowa TSN 822 spotting scope at approx. 40x
Distance:  On Oct. 25, approx. 75 feet. At other times 20 - 300 feet.
Light:  Good. Initial observation was near sunset, mostly sunny, 60F. Last observation was partly cloudy, approx. 40F, water partly frozen.

Description of the Bird

Small goose.  Black bill, eyes, feet and legs.  Black head, neck and breast except white broken patch on top of each side of neck.  Dark brownish above with black horizontal markings.  Gray-brown belly.  Flanks gray-brown with whitish bands.  White tail and undertail coverts w/ blackish tip.  Associated with 3 Canada Geese on many visits to Houts Reservoir.  I never heard the bird vocalize on any of my visits.  I initially identified the goose as a Brant of the Atlantic subspecies but it has been identified by others more knowledgeable than me as a Brant of the Gray-bellied subspecies.

Bird was observed in flight, on water and on land usually close to water.  Bird swam and grazed.  On December 1 it was flushed by a dog from alongside Houts Reservoir and the bird flew to a bare agricultural field to graze with a flock of geese (Canada and Cackling Geese as well as one Ross').

Similar Species Discussion

No other species was considered as Brant are fairly distinctive when seen well.  However, there are three subspecies in North America.  The bird at Equalizer Lake and Houts Reservoir showed a sharp contrast between the black breast and the brownish-gray belly.  This small goose had a black bill, eyes, feet and legs.  The plumage on the head, neck and breast was black w/ whitish broken band on top of neck.  Plumage was dull, dark brownish above. White tail w/ blackish tip.  I eliminated the Black/Pacific subsp. based on that subspecies black belly that does not have much contrast with the black breast.  I eliminated the Gray-bellied subsp. based on that subspecies fairly dark belly that does not contrast sharply with the black breast, according to the plate in the Sibley guide.  But the National Geographic guide shows a lighter plumaged Gray-bellied bird and states that this subspecies is variable.  The photo on the Padilla Bay NERR website shows a photo of a bird that appears to be not as dark as the Gray-bellied Brant in the Sibley guide.

Previous Experience

I have seen Brant of the Atlantic subspecies many times along the coast from Virginia to Massachusetts over the last 20 years.  I have seen Brant of the Black subspecies in California in 2001.  I saw distant Brant of unknown subspecies in Washington in 2008.
