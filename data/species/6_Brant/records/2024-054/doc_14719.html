Description of the Bird

Found by Archer Silverman. When I first spotted it, I noticed the white flanks, black head, and the black upperparts. My mind immediately went BRANT. I lost it for a minute within the 2500 Cackling Geese, and thought I had just seen something before spotting it again. After this I got a bit closer and studied the bird until it flew off just before sunset. After I saw it I swiftly alerted the Denver Whatsapp group and the Colorado Birding Discord group, and one person was able to come by and see it before it flew off. Other people searched in the following days but no one was able to refind it. I go further into the identification in the Similar Species section. 

Similar Species Discussion

The similar species that were considered were other branta genus geese within Colorado, those being Cackling and Canada Geese, and to lesser extent due to the rarity, Barnacle Goose. The completely black head, back, wings, and chest along with the white tail rule out all of the geese mentioned earlier. For the subspecific identification, the white neck ring extends almost all around the neck, only being broken in the back of the neck. The Atlantic subspecies, or B. b. hrota has more of a white neck spot than a ring, and when the white extends it seems to be not as "clean" as this birds neck ring is. The more important part of the sub specific identification is the flank and underpart coloration. On Atlantic birds there is a sharp contrast between the white and black on the underparts. The black also stops at the chest. On my bird their is brown extending further into the side of the bird, where as Atlantic would have white in that spot.

Previous Experience

I have seen Brant three times, all of those being the Black subspecies. I have a lot of experience with Cackling Geese as every winter I have a flock of about 4000 in my patch that I study often.
