Description of the Bird

This goose shaped bird was found among a flock of primarily Canada (lesser) and Cackling geese (Richardson). When standing the bird was about the same size as neighboring Cackling geese. Black face, bill, neck, and upper breast. Thin white neck collar extending from sides of the neck across the front of the neck. Mantle, Upper, and Lower Scapulars gray. White flanks with some barring near the rear of the flanks. White vent and lower tail coverts with black on the tail.

Similar Species Discussion

Similar species include Canada and Cackling Geese. Eliminated by absence of white on cheeks, lighter breast.
Barnacle Goose was eliminated by lack of white face.
Greater White-fronted Goose was eliminated by presence of dark face and bill, dark neck and breast and white neck ring.
Dark Snow Goose was eliminated by smaller size and white under and upper tail coverts.
