Description of the Bird

Goose. Smaller than Cacklers, but not as small as Ross's. Plumage seemed fresh. Black head, neck and chest exept for a small triangular patch at side of upper neck which was finely striped black and white. Back and wings grayish, contrasting markedly with black chest/neck "stocking." No pale edges to coverts to indicate that this bird was in juv plumage. Tertials and primaries blackish. Sides whitish with narrow grayish brown linear markings perpendicular to body length (in other words, markings went from edge of wing to edge of water through white sides; slightly curved). Vent white. Tail pattern not observed. Bill black and delicate, typical of brant
