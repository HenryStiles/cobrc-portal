Description of the Bird

The bird was not calling. It was more active and moved around more than the Canada Geese it was associating with. I initially thought it was the Pale-bellied/Atlantic version (hrota), but it seems that more experienced observers think it is Gray-bellied (see COBIRDS discussion here: https://groups.google.com/forum/#!topic/cobirds/-8D1VB_ZH_U ). As you can see in the attached photos, the bird was smaller than the Canadas, has a dark neck with a broken white collar. The legs are black. The belly is pale, as opposed to the darker belly that would be present on a black Brant. Please see the attached photos for better documentation.

Similar Species Discussion

The only species that may be similar would be the other Branta species. In this area, these would be Canada, Cackling, and Barnacle. Those three species can be eliminated from consideration as they all have some amount of white on the face. The Chatfield Brant, and Brant generally, have black faces (albeit with a white collar, but it is clearly a collar). As for which version of the Brant this bird is, that is something I cannot say. As I mentioned in the previous question, I initially thought this bird was the Atlantic subspecies, but there is also not a Gray-bellied form listed in my field guides. As the bird has been well seen and photographed, it seems that people with more Brant experience have decided that it is Gray-bellied. It will be interesting to see this commitee's determination of subspecific form, if it does make such a determination.

Resources Used

In the field, I did not bring any guides as the bird is pretty obviously different than the Canada Geese it was associating with. In my apparently ill-fated attempt to determine the subspecies, I used the Sibley Guide, National Geographic Guide, the Golden Guide, and an older Audubon Field Guide to Birds of Eastern North America. I was using my pictures to compare with these guides. I have only uploaded two pictures here, but I have more (though of varying quality) if needed.

Previous Experience

I have no experience with any Brant, this being my first.

With Canada Goose, like many birders, I have lots of experience due to how common they are in the various places I have lived.

In regards to Cackling, I have moderate experience, especially given my predeclination to be conservative in what I think a Cackling Goose is, from a field perspective. I probably underestimate the full range of variability in that species.

I have no experience with Barnacle Goose.
