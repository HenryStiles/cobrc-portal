Description of the Bird

The bird in question was clearly a Brant, in my determination, a Gray-bellied Brant.   First, it was a small sized goose, seen among Cackling and Canada Geese.  Pictures will be included.   The bird was silent and feeding on grass, moving regularly.   It did seem to favor one leg so could be an injured bird.

I understand that this is a difficult call.   My reasoning for believing this bird is a Gray-bellied is based on:  the amount and extent of side markings and the lack of a clear cut dark line between the dark neck\throat pattern and the belly.   Being from the east, I have more experience with the Pale-bellied Brant which normally has a more distinct demarcation than this bird.  Also, the white necklace does not seem to go all the way around the front of the neck as can be seen in the picture of the bird feeding.  Although subjective, I think that the back is darker than what would be seen on a "typical" Pale-bellied Brant.   I also did some internet research including the 4-12-2011 Sibley posting on his website re Gray-bellied Brant and a Birding Frontiers article on the subject which reaffirmed my ID.  Finally, and totally subjective!!, my sense is that as Pale-bellied winter in the Atlantic, they would be less likely to appear in Colorado than a Black or Pale-bellied Brant based on migration patterns.  I do know, however, that is "playing the averages" and that almost any bird can appear anywhere but it does add one more point.

Similar Species Discussion

The other two "species" considered were Black Brant and Pale-bellied Brant.   The belly of this bird did not come close to being black and there was more demarcation between the throat\neck and belly than would be normal for a Black Brant.   The reasons for eliminating Pale-Bellied are outlined in the prior section.

Resources Used

Binoculars, camera (Canon SLR with 400mm Prime lens), field guides, internet and personal experience with Pale-bellied and Black Brant.

Previous Experience

I have been birding for over 40 years and am now retired, spending much of my time birding including trips in much of the USA and Canada.  My experience with Black Brant is limited in that more of my birding is done in the Eastern US than the west (although I do bird regularly in SE Ariz, S. Calif. and Colorado).   My experience with Pale-bellied is much greater as I have spent much of my life in coastal New England.
