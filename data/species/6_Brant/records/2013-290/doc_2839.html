Similar Species Discussion

(Black) Brant by color on undersides

This report only gives two subspecies choices.  It fits better wit Eastern High Arctic Brant (from the hrota group) or maybe Western High Arcitc Brant a.k.a. the newly described, but not yet designated subspecies, Gray-bellied Brant and information here is based entirely on the article in Birding, May/June 2006, titled "Gray-bellied Brant Identification and Vagrancy" by Mlodinow and Awelson

Resources Used

Birding article on "Gray-bellied Brant" in the May/June 2006 issue introducing the idea of a Brant type in between Atlantic and Black in coloration.

Previous Experience

I have seen a couple large flocks of Black Brant on the coast of Washington, maybe 60in northern Alsaska, and a small flock of Atlantic Brant in November at Chincoteague NWR, VA, a long time ago, plus the two Atlantic Brant at Chatfield two winters ago.
