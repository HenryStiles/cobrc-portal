Description of the Bird

 Long, straight neck and bill.  Short tail and medium legs.  Seen soaring, with long broad wings, slightly tucked.  Wingspan was estimated at 1.5 meters, with pretty good accuracy ( thanks to stereo cameras ).  

No human observers.  This bird was detected and photographed automatically by a prototype Identiflight unit operating at Boulder Imaging headquarters. See https://www.identiflight.com/

The bird was tracked briefly as it flew by.  It was observed coming from due south, and leaving towards the northeast.  

Similar Species Discussion

Whooping and Sandhill cranes were considered, as they have a have a similar structure in flight.   However, the pattern of dark showing in the underwing does not match either.  

Imm. White Ibis may be the closest similar match, but that would seem to be ruled out by the daintier, longer feet, and pale neck.  

Other Wading birds were considered, but can be easily eliminated due to structural mismatches.   

Previous Experience

I was not able to see the bird directly.  I found it the next day, while reviewing imagery recorded from the station across the entire day. At that time, the recent wood stork sighting in Denver was still fresh in my mind, so I was able to consider Wood Stork very quickly.  This was noted before the (likely same individual) Stork appeared for a lengthy stay in Broomfield.   I had previously observed Wood Storks during a trip to Florida.
