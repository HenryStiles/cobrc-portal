Description of the Bird

Initially seen with two WAVI and one REVI. Shorter tail than that of WAVI quite apparent as they flew back and forth overhead. Distinct yellowish wash on underwing coverts was also noted, which was not visible in WAVIs. Throat, chest and vent moderately bright yellow, with flanks also yellow. Most often, lower and mid belly appeared whitish, but sometimes the entire underparts looked yellow, though belly not as bright. Back greenish, no wingbars. White superciilum faintly washed in yellow. Dusky eyeline extended forward to bill and backward as far as supercilium. Dull whitish crescent below eye. Bill smallish, but can not say with certainty that it was smaller than that of (presumed) e. WAVIs nearby. Did not focus on this much. Crown washed grayish with slightly darker area at lower edge of crown above eye and a bit forward of eye.
Behaviors: Fed deliberately, mostly above eye level, near top of tallish shrubs.
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo can look very yellow beneath, particularly in fall, either due to actual color or reflected light, but I've never seen a WAVI that looked yellow with this distribution of yellow (which is typical for PHVI). Dark lores should eliminate WAVI (though I have seen one bird, presumably pure WAVI, with dusky lores). Border to crown highly atypical for WAVI. Shorter tail rarely so easy to be certain of, but direct comparison excellent. Finally, I did not know that underwing covert color might be a mark, but it is shown as different in Sibley and appears different in the (admittedly limited) sample on line from Slater Museum (Google: Slater Museum Wing Collection).
