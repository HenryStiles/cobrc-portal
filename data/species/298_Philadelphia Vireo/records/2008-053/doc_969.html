Description of the Bird

This bird was small with vireo-like bill, small hook, and legs, strong and stouter than a warbler. It had a medium gray crown blending into a greenish gray back. A dark eye was surrounded by a white broad ring, through which a dark eyestripe ran, in the front forming a distinct dark lore.

The throat was noticeably yellow, fading on the belly to a creamy color that had some yellow tints. The undertail coverts again became noticeably yellow.

The wings were without wingbars, the tail was short and the over appearance was of a chubby, small vireo.
Behaviors: I found the bird in a net where I assumed it would be one of the previous day's Tennessee Warblers, but this bird had distinct vireo features and not those of a warbler.

In the net, the bird did very little.
Call: none heard
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

There are two other species that can look like this one, Tennessee Warbler which from a distance can look quite similar but, unlike the vireo TEWA has a greenish back, lacks a hook at the tip of a much thinner bill, is a smaller, daintier bird, with much thinner tibia/tarsi. TEWA usually shows faint yellowish wingbards, and has very white undertail coverts. Females in May are often yellowish on the chin and throat so from afar can appear similar to PHVI, but not in the hand.

Warbling Vireo, also a vireo without wingbars, has pale, not black lores giving the bird a different look. It is larger, has a longer tail, and lacks the yellow wash on the breast at this season.

Bell's Vireo - always has one thing wingbar and often show a faint upper one, unlike the vireos without wingbars.
