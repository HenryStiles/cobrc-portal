Description of the Bird

A small warbler-sized passerine with a short, thick "vireo" bill. Underparts tinged with yellow, brightest on the throat and vent. Gray above with green tinge on back, blue-gray crown, whitish eyeline above and around eye, with darker gray lores. Short tail relative to other vireos. Wings plain without obvious wing bars. Wing coverts with narrow pale edges visible at certain angles. Tertials edged whitish, and primaries dark gray (almost black) with pale edges.
Behaviors: Flitting in trees from 10 - 25 feet above ground, gleaning food among leaves, particularly in Hackberry trees with obvious psyllid infestations, and often in direct sunlight.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling vireo is grayer overall, including both underparts and upperparts, with longer tail and longer bill, and lacking blackish primaries. Bell's vireo has more obvious wingbar and brighter yellow on sides compared to throat. Huttons, Yellow-throated, White-eyed and "Solitary" vireos all have bright wingbars. Red-eyed and Yellow-green vireos have much larger bill, and white throats. Ruby-crowned Kinglet has bright wing-bars and thinner bill.
