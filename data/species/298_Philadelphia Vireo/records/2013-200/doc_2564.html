Description of the Bird

This bird was one of the first ones I saw when I was birding near Black Bear Hole in Lyons.  I pished and it popped up on a wire, where it stayed for about 2-3 minutes, allowing for careful views and photos. 

The bird had a bill shape conclusive with a vireo.  Size was similar to Warbling Vireo, or perhaps slightly smaller.  It had grayish colored upper parts with slight olive tint to the back.  The wings were unmarked without wing bars.  The top of the head was also a uniform gray color.  The chest and flanks were a notable, but diffuse lemon-yellow color, being slightly more intense yellow in the middle of the abdomen and slightly paler on the throat region.  The facial pattern showed dark lores and a thin dark line extending back behind the eye.  The supercilium was paler and the area below the eyeline was as well.  The bird lacked any eyering.   

The behavior was not particularly notable - it hopped up on the wire, watched me for a bit, then eventually flew away into the trees.  It did not vocalize.    

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo was the first species to be considered and expected, however they lack the bright yellow in the middle of the abdomen that this bird showed.  Also they generally have paler lores and less distinct facial markings.

Tennessee Warbler share a superficial resemblance to this bird, but the bill shape is not consistent with a warbler(being slightly hooked at the end) and the back was not as green in color as expected for TEWA.  This bird also appeared somewhat larger in size than a Tennessee Warbler would be. 

Resources Used

I sent the photos to several more experienced birders than myself who confirmed that it looked good for a PHVI.  I also consulted Stokes and Sibley's Field Guides and looked at photos online.

Previous Experience

I am inexperienced with this species, however I have moderate experience with Warbling Vireo to compare it to.
