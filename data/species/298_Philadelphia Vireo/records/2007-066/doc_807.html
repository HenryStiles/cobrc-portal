Habitat

Kentucky blue grass in large back yard
Trees: locust, hawthorn, elm, crabapple, apple, Pinion Pine, Austrian Pine, Cedar and Juniper
Shrubs: Wood rose, Euonymous, Russian Sage, Honeysuckle, other potted flowering plants, some native wild flowers and herbs
Native rock in landscape and a couple of small water features
Otherwise this is a dry hilltop and is several miles from water or anything very green

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10x50mm field binoculars and Olympus optical zoom 8X automatic digital camera
Distance:  10 feet to 30 feet
Light:  good daylight

Description of the Bird

Contrasting dark crown, white eyebrow or supercillium, dark lores, whitish under eye, grayish cheek, drab back, darker area on primary coverts, dark edges on primaries, bright yellow in central throat, whitish breast, paler yellow undertail coverts, short tail
Behaviors: -somewhat secretive
-moved from tree to tree if I got too close
-fed on insects in the tree
Call: not noted
Plumage: Bright adult

Similar Species Discussion

Sent photos to friends for confirmation; the Warbling Vireo that I had before was overall paler and had whitish throat

Resources Used

Sibleys Guide to Birds, and photos sent to friends who are experts in identification

Previous Experience

Not much. I have had a couple of Warbling Vireos make a brief visit to the yard, but I have never seen a Philadelphia Vireo before
