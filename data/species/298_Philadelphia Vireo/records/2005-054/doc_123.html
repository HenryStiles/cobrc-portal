Description of the Bird

A medium-sized vireo without wingbars. The bird was olive above and yellow on the throat fading to whitish belly. A dark eyeline and the contrasting pale supercilium made the face more striking than the very similar Warbling Vireo. Bill was dark gray
Behaviors: One bird foraged in a willow and another foraged in a Juniperus sp tree. Both birds foraged at low to mid-level heights.
Call: none noted
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo is the most similar. This species was eliminated by the bright yellow throats of our bird. This also eliminates the similar Red-eyed Vireo.
