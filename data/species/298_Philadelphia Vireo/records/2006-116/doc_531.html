Description of the Bird

the bird was a vireo the same size and shape as nearby Warbling Vireos. The bill was a bit shorter, appearing more stubby, and was black. I did not note the color of the legs.

The bird's cap was dark gray, noticeable darker than on Warbling Vireos seen that day. It contrasted strongly with a very pale yellowish or whitish eyebrow. The bird had a dark eyeline, including in front of the eye, that also contrasted with the eyebrow. The back and wings were olive-green, though not particularly bright. The tail was the same color. The throat and upper chest was bright yellow, contrasting with a paler yellow belly and flanks.
Behaviors: the bird was foraging high in a tree, and was moving through the canopy fairly fast, making it hard to see.
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Warbler species were ruled out by larger size, by the shorter and thicker bill than expected for warblers, and heavier shape. Tennessee Warbler in particular was ruled out by the much heavier bill on this bird, and the larger size and heavier build.

Warbling Vireo was ruled out by the darker cap and eyeline, especially in front of the eye, contrasting more strongly with pale areas on the face, and by the bright yellow throat and upper chest, contrasting with paler areas on the underparts.

Previous Experience

I have seen a number of Philadelphia Vireos in the east and Costa Rica, and a couple in Colorado. I have seen tons of Warbling Vireos and Tennessee Warblers.
