Description of the Bird

This bird looked like a Warbling Vireo, except for very yellow underparts. It was about same size as nearby Warbling Vireo, but perhaps a bit smaller.

CROWN & FACE: Crown was grayish. White supercilium was flanked below by dark eyeline extending thru lores. Eye was dark.

BILL & LEGS: Bill was dark, short, thicker than warbler with small hook at tip. Legs were dark.

UPPERPARTS: Brownish olive-green with darker contrasting slaty colored wings. There were no wingbars on this bird.

UNDERPARTS: Practically solid yellow, which is typical of many fall-plumaged birds. It was slightly less yellow on belly, but had a very bright yellow breast.
Behaviors: Bird sluggishly foraged in willows and narrow-leaf cottonwoods with other vireos & warblers.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Fall plumage

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo (WAVI) tends to be plainer faced without a distinct supercilium & eyeline; WAVI has lighter lores and slightly lighter wings than Philly Vireo.

WAVIs can have yellowish underparts but not as bright as Philly Vireo. On the former the yellow is usually confined to the flanks and rarely present on the breast except as a very pale wash.
