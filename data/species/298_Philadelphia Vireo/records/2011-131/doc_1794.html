Description of the Bird

Around 2:30 pm on 3 October 2011, I parked in the lot at Stalker Pond, just outside of Yuma, Yuma County, CO. I began to pish near a tree that was about 12 feet tall. To my surprise and delight, it began to fill with birds. Most were Yellow-rumped Warblers, but I glimpsed a vireo moving in and then saw it directly overhead. At first I could see only the underparts, from legs backwards. To my surprise, this area was a clear distinct lemon yellow. I thought, excitedly, Philly Vireo, I need a BETTER LOOK. I stepped backwards a bit, the bird moved down, and I could see that it was entirely yellow beneath and that the lores were dark.

Description: Underparts entirely clear lemon yellow, not as saturated as that of typical Wilson's Warbler and not with the greenish hues often seen in an Orange-crowned Warbler, but rather more like the bright clear, somewhat pale, yellow shown by many Yellow Warblers in fall. The yellow was brightest in vent/undertail coverts and across chest (center of chest as bright or brighter than sides), though notably, the throat was distinctly yellow. Overall, the variation in intensity of yellow beneath did not vary dramatically from region to region. The back was rather bright green, reminiscent of a Tennessee Warbler. The crown (extending onto nape, perhaps all of the way to back) was medium gray. The supercilium was off-white and extended a short way behind eye. There was a dusky transocular line from bill, through lores, and continuing behind eye. Below the eye, there was a grayish short line/crescent, not as prominent or as long as that of a Warbling Vireo. The combination of bolder and longer eyeline and weaker mark below eye meant that the bird did not have the "open faced" expression of a Warbling Vireo. The bill was stout, like that of a vireo, and it did not appear dramatically different from that of a Warbling Vireo (which is similar to my experience when living in Chicago, though knowing that WAVI is supposed to have a distinctly larger bill than that of PHVI). The tail did appear shorter, a mark that I'd not really discerned in past. Wing pattern (other than lack of wingbars) and auricular color not noted.
Behaviors: Foraged slowly in foliage, traveling with loose flock or YR and OC Warblers.
Call: not heard
Plumage: fall

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo: I have seen some strikingly yellow (beneath) Warbling Vireos before, but they were always brightest on flanks and undertail coverts, lacking yellow on center of chest and throat. The facial pattern was different from WAVI, not only in dark lores, but in other marks noted. The green on back was brighter than that I've ever seen on a WAVI.

Red-eyed Vireo: bolder face pattern, not as yellow beneath, larger bill.

Tennessee Warbler: bill size and yellow vent eliminate this species. Bill size, birds relatively large head (compared to most warblers), eliminate all warbler species.
