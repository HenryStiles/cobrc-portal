Description of the Bird

I initially thought the bird was a Warbling Vireo when first observed with the naked eye. Once I put bins on it, however, I noted the extensive yellow underparts, dark lores and eyeline, and darkish gray cap. I started taking pictures immediately and didn't look at the bird again in the field as it left shortly afterwards.
Behaviors: Responded with scolding call to my pishing.
Call: I initially detected the bird while pishing and it responded with a typical raspy vireo scolding call.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

I'm most concerned with swainsonii Warbling Vireo as that subspecies can have some yellowish to the underparts and a darker edging on the cap. I sent my photos to Tony Leukering and Steve Mlodinow for their opinions and they wrote:

TL: "PHVI (It's just too yellow underneath)"

SM: "Also, the bill size and short tail are PHVI, and I think I can see a bit of a black line above the supercilum like a PHVI"
