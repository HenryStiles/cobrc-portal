Description of the Bird

feeding together. Both birds were yellow beneath, brightest on throat and chest, dark line through eye, smallish bill (but not thin like warbler), remiges contrastingly darker than wing coverts, green back, gray crow, pale supercilium. One bird was rather bright yellow throughout underparts, the other mostly on throat, chest, and vent.
Behaviors: Feeding moderate height in trees. Photographed with spotted cucumber beetle in beak.
Call: not heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Red-eyed Vireo not as yellow beneath, Yellow-green much larger bill (sorry, not being facetious, in this state...), Warbling with pale lores, yellow (if present) differently distributed. Bill too thick for Tennessee Warbler or OCWA. Also, contrast between remiges and wing coverts supposedly reliable for separating PHVI from WAVI
