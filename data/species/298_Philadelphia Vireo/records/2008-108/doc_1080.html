Description of the Bird

the bird was a vireo just about the same size as a Warbling Vireo that was often in the same tree. The overall shape was similar to the Warbling Vireo, except the tail was relatively shorter, the head appeared a bit more rounded and the overall shape appeared a bit bulkier. The bill was short (relatively shorter than expected for a Warbling Vireo), and was black with a pale pinkish color at the base of the lower mandible. It was slightly but noticeably hooked, and thicker than expected for a warbler. The legs were relatively thick and short, and were blue-gray in color. The irides were dark.

The bird had a dark gray cap, contrasting with a white supercilium, which in turn contrasted with a black eye-line that extended from the base of the bill to the nape. There was a small area of white under the eye, larger than the eye-arc of many birds but not very extensive. The nape and back were dusky olive-green. The wings were grayish with some dusky olive-green highlights. The short tail was gray underneath.

The throat was a fairly bright dusky yellow color, brightest in the center and duller towards the sides and bottom of the throat. The belly and chest were lightly but noticeably tinged pale yellow; the flanks were also tinged this color, albeit a bit brighter than the belly. The undertail coverts were brighter than either the flanks or the belly, but not as bright as the throat.
Behaviors: The bird was observed foraging in willows, from about 15 feet to 35 feet up.
Call: none heard
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Warbler species were ruled out by the bulkier shape, thicker, hooked bill, shorter, thicker, blue-gray legs, and more lethargic behavior. Most vireo species were ruled out by a combination of the face pattern and yellowish underparts. Yellow-green Vireo was ruled out by the shorter bill, more subdued face pattern, and underparts pattern with yellow throat brightest in center and overall yellowish wash.

Warbling Vireo was ruled out by the bulkier shape, bright yellow throat brightest in the center, shorter bill, dark eye-line that included the lores, and overall much yellower coloration.
