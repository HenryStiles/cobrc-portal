Description of the Bird

A vireo (see bill photo) was captured at the RMBO Banding Station at Chico Basin Ranch. It had a gray head the contrasted with an olive back. A dark gray eyeline was darkest in the loral area and it extented (less dark) behind the eye. The chin and throat were lemon yellow and the breast was cream-colored. Above and below the eye was a small pocket of white feathers contracting with the cronw and the darker auriculars. Some of the features were affected by the bird being captured and hand-held, so the crest was slightly erected and the true shape (round) of the head was not apparent. The bird had what I would call a very long primary projection (photo). Although Sibley says the ends of the flight feathers are darker than the rest of the wingtips, the photograph does not show this aspect.
Behaviors: In the hand when I saw it.

Plumage: after second year (adult)

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo can look similar, especially in fall, but Western WAVIs are usually much duller in spring and never have the bright yellow throat coloration. WAVIs face usually has less contrast between the crown and face. The lore on WAVI is generally much lighter and shows less contrast with the face coloraton. The tail of WAVI is usually noticeably longer than PHVI.

Tennessee Warbler, especially in the fall, can show some yellow, but the TEWA bill is longer and thinner without the vireo hook at the tip.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have only seen a dozen or so PHVIs, maybe only 7 in CO, but I have seen lots of WAVIs in spring and in fall and studied both species a lot.
