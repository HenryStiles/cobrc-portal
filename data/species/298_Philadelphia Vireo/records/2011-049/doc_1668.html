Description of the Bird

Kara Carragher spotted the vireo, and asked me to make sure it was a Philadelphia Vireo. As soon as I saw it, I could tell it was, the vireo shaped bill (thicker than warblers, and not pointed), plus the black eye line, and yellowish throat and under tail all convinced me of the bird being this species. See photos.
Behaviors: High up in the trees.
Call: none heard. Others said, it sang earlier in the day. So probably a male.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Warblers have thin bills, not thick bills.

The black going through the eye eliminates all other vireos with the yellowish throat.
