Description of the Bird

I was standing in the riparian area below the dam at Stalker Lake. Finally, I had a flock of birds at hand. A WAVI popped up into view, and then flew off. As I continued pishing, another vireo (coming from same general vicinity as WAVI) flew in and landed on same exposed branch. It was entirely rather bright yellow beneath. It was unmarked (no streaks, wingbars, etc) above and fairly bright green, excepting a medium gray crown and nape that contrasted with the back. The supercilium was white. A blackish line started just below eye and extended forward (thru eye, so to speak) to bill. There was only a hint of the vague but large white eye arcs seen on WAVIs. The bill appeared small for a WAVI. I watched the bird for a while as it fed, getting views in shade and sun, and the colors held true. 

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireos rarely show dark on lores, especially this dark. They also have yellow limited to flanks and vent when present. If yellow present elsewhere, still paler than side and vent color, which was not case with this bird. Eye arcs typical of WAVI not present and bill smaller. Also, the brightness of upperparts would be rather unusual in WAVI. Note that the bird's overall size was similar to a WAVI (in other words, this bird was not tiny like a Bell's Vireo or larger like a REVI).
