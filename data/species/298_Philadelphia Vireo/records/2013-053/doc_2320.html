Description of the Bird

-drab gray-green crown and back
-no wing bars
-dark crown contrasted with the conspicuous white eyeline
-dark lores
-yellowish undertail and sides
-brightest yellow on throat
-yellowish white on lower belly
-assumed to be drab adult because it did not seem as bright as the one I photographed in my yard
Behaviors: stayed hidden from view most of time in cottonwoods but it did become visible several times as it moved from tree to tree - fairly secretive
Call: Duane Nelson identified it by it's song - we were then able to follow it and get a visual
Plumage: drab adult?

Similar Species Discussion

The throat was yellow as opposed to the light colored throat of the Warbling Vireo. Also the strong contrast between the crown and eyeline and the dark lores were more dominant than the "pale face" of the Warbling Vireo. It was not as large as the Red-eyed Vireo, and again the yellow throat differentiated it from the REVI.
