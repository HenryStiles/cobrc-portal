Description of the Bird

Small passerine - about 5 inches - with a short blunt blackish bill, a short drab tail, shape and behavior (slow flitting) of a vireo. Upperparts grayish green turning to gray on wings and darker gray on crown; underparts pale whitish with lemon-yellow wash, brightest on upper chest. Whitish, diffuse supercilium and as well as feathers under the dark eye, separated by a dark gray diffuse eye line and lore; pale gray tips to greater coverts forming the suggestion of a diffuse wingbar; olive edges to secondaries; darker gray wingtips (primaries).  

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling vireo is grayer overall, lacks yellow on throat and chest, and is longer tailed and longer billed. Red-eyed vireo is greener above, whiter below, heavier billed, with well defined head pattern. Bell's vireo has brighter yellow on sides and a better defined whitish wing bar. Brown-capped Vireo from Central America has a similar face pattern but lacks yellow on chest and had brown front, not gray.

Small warblers have thinner bills. Kinglets have even smaller and thinner bills.
