Description of the Bird

Small, greenish, warbler-like bird with short tail and no wing bars. [This is a brief description, but at the time that is exactly what I observed; I presumed it was an Orange-crowned Warbler, and because it was at close range, I took a photo without looking closely at the bird. I identified the bird retrospectively by looking at the photo. Key identification marks on the photo include yellow wash to underparts, short and stubby bill, and off-white supercilium highlighting a facial pattern reminiscent of Warbling Vireo].
Behaviors: Flitting in a shrub
Call: none
Plumage: alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Short, stubby bill rules out warblers, including Orange-crowned Warbler. Yellowish wash to underparts combined with olive upperparts rules out Waarbling Vireo, which in May would have gray upperparts and whitish underparts. Lack of strong eyeline/eyebrow pattern (and short bill) rules out Red-eyed and Yellow-green vireos. Lack of wing-bars rules out other vireo species and Ruby-crowned Kinglet.
