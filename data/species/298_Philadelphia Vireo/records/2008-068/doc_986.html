Description of the Bird

This was a wingbar-less vireo with a short dusky bill, a white supercillium, a grayish head, that only contrasted slightly with the greenish back, a YELLOW THROAT, the contrasted with the lighter whitish belly, and a yellowish wash on the undertail coverts. The best I.D. feature was the dark lore that continued from the eye to the base of the bill, creating an eyeline, directly below the white supercillium.
Behaviors: Foraged sluggishly at mid- and low levels in mostly Russian olives but two days later in junipers. Paused a number of times long enough to digiscope.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult
