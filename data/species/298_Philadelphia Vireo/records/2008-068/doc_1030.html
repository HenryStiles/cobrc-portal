Description of the Bird

A plump vireo, pale yellow below, olive-grayish above. Yellow was darkest in the throat, palest in the belly. A gray cap, with contrasting white supercilliam. A dark eyeline both behind and in front of eye formig dark lores.
Behaviors: Feeding deliberately in shrubs around pond.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo is certainly the most likely bird to be confused with PHVI. Even bright WAVIs show th deepest yellow in their flanks, not their throats. Also, lores were too dark for WAVI.
