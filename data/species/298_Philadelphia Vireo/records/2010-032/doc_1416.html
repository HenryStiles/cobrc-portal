Description of the Bird

On May 19th I saw the Philadelphia Vireo around 8:00 am and observed it for approximately 6 minutes. When I first spotted this bird it was about 15 feet away in a young cottonwood tree, 10 meters tall. I had a head on view and then it turned slightly for a profile view and seemed to be about 5 inches in length. I first saw a distinct facial pattern and dark crown with a roundish head. The bill was also a bit thicker compared to a warblers’ bill. The facial pattern had a whitish supercilium and dark eyeline that started at the lores and ended toward the nape. Wings and nape were oliveish and I did not see any markings on the wings. This individual had had a bright yellow throat that washed out into the breast with pale yellow belly and undertail coverts. The bird then flew to a larger cottownwood tree and was foraging with 2 Yellow Warblers hopping from twig to twig. I had a couple of short underside views where the tail was noticeably short. I lost it in some nearby cottonwood trees and couldn’t refind it. The habitat in the surrounding area was riparian interspersed with cottownwood trees and willows. The Philadelphia Vireo was 981 feet South East where the Poudre bike trail goes under E. Lincoln Ave. Here are the approximate coordinates 40 35'14.69"N, 105 3'58.99"W (WGS 84).
Behaviors: This bird was hopping from twig to twig and had some short flights from branch to branch.
Call: No vocalizations were heard.
Plumage: probably definitive basic plumage

Similar Species Discussion

I eliminated Warbling Vireo based on the yellow throat and more distinct eyeline as well as the shorter tail. Tennessee Warbler was eliminated based on bill structure and the eyeline starting at the lores. The Philadelphia Vireo plumage was also more olivish gray than a Tennessee Warbler.
