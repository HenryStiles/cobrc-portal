Description of the Bird

This is the year for this species in CO so much that I would like to nominate it to be removed from the Review List. This bird was captured at the RMBO/Chico Basin Ranch Banding Station in southern El Paso County.

Vireo by hooked bill. The cap was gray green. The back was greenish gray. The ventral surface was light, washed in yellow with the brightest yellow coloration being on the upper breast. In front of the eye was a strong dark lore that was emphasized by the light area above and below the dark eye. The wings were greenish gray and without wingbars. When not in the hand, it appeared to be short tailed for a vireo.
Behaviors: It was mostly observed in the hand. When foraging this individual forage low in the olives.
Call: none heard
Plumage: 1st year

Similar Species Discussion

Ths species can be separated from congeners by its yellow undersides with the yellow being brightest at the breast and especially by the dark lore and short tail along with the absence of wingbars.

Eastern Warbling Vireo comes closest in fall, but it always has that dull facial look, no dark lore and the brightest yellow is on the flanks. Also its bill would measure noticably longer than short-billed PHVI.
