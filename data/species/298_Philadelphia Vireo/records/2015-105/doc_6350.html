Description of the Bird

Small vireo actively foraging during both observations in small / medium deciduous trees in Denver Botanic Gardens. Relatively tame. During observation on the 28th, I encountered the bird after being stopped on a narrow trail by other visitors to the Gardens (who were photographing a rabbit). The bird foraged above us as I waited for the other visitors to finish their photos and pass by. I observed and photographed the bird for 3-5 minutes from about 5 feet away, before other Garden visitors approached and the bird flew into Cheesman.

No vocalizations.

Yellow flanks & belly. Dark, grayish lores and white above and below eyes. 

Similar Species Discussion

Warlbling Vireo: On the 23rd, I initially identified this bird as a Warbling Vireo. Chris Rurik took longer looks and noticed darker lores than we would expect with the Warbling. I took a series of photographs hoping to confirm this.


Red-eyed Vireo: On the 28th, my first reaction when seeing this bird was Red-eyed Vireo, but having learned my lesson from the 23rd, I took longer looks and noticed the smaller bill and yellowish wash on the underside and realized this was likely the same bird as observed on the 23rd.
