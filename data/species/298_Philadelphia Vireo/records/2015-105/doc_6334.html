Description of the Bird

Northwest corner of west pond. Vireo shape and bill. Foraging 6-25 ft high in deciduous trees (I saw it grab and eat two berries as well). The dark lores were obvious at all angles (i.e. not a product of the photo's angle) -- which is what made me look closer in the first place. Pretty drab and washed out color-wise. Yellow only a light wash, sometimes hardly discernible, but was concentrated in throat area and around tail. It made no noise.

Spent maybe 5 minutes watching it with great looks. Jared was only able to get three photos in the last 30 seconds before it flew off. Could not refind.

Similar Species Discussion

We initially ID'ed it as a warbling vireo, the far more expected species (though getting late for it). The dark lores made me look closer, then I spent time observing the yellowish coloration on the undersides. In warbling vireo the yellowish should be distributed more around the flanks rather than the throat. The dark lores also helped eliminate WAVI.

We were still unsure after the observation (having never seen PHVI before), so we sent photos to the CO birding community and got several experts who agreed with our ID (David Dowell, Steve M, Mark P, and a few others).

Resources Used

Binoculars and field guide in the field. Later, sending a photo to experts.

Previous Experience

Never seen a philadelphia vireo. According to eBird, I have seen 56 warbling vireos in my life. I have birded with experienced birders who have explained how to know if you're seeing a rare philly rather than warbling vireo.
