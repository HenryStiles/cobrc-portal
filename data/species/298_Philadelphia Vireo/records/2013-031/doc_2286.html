Description of the Bird

Structurally and size-wise, similar to the more common WAVI.

Dull, dark gray crown with a somewhat lighter supercillium. Darker line through the eye with a more of the off-white below the eye. The eye itself was dark.

Drab green back and wings. No wingbars noted. Underside a fairly uniform tone of yellow from the throat, through the chest, belly and vent
Behaviors: Flitting in the tops of the trees.
Call: N/A
Plumage: Fall

Similar Species Discussion

Fall WAVI is the most similar due to the structural similarities, however a WAVI will not have a charcoal crown nor does it have such a dark eyestripe. Additionally, a fall WAVI may be washed with some yellow, it isn't quite as rich or extensive as that of this PHVI.

REVI has a fairly distinctive red eye and lacks the yellow wash through the underside.

BEVI is smaller, more compact without a charcoal crown. BEVI should have a white throat and a thin wingbar.
