Description of the Bird

Not a very bright individual.
Gray crown, bordered by white supercilium that extended from the base of the bill to past the eye. Gray line through eye.
Yellow on underside was soft, extended from breast, down flank to the under tail. Gray/green above. No wing bars.
Gray bare parts (bill and legs).
Behaviors: Alone. Working wild rose bushes at ground level up to a couple of feet above ground. Actively feeding.

The area was full of migrants (warblers, thrushes, sparrows, empids, etc.). This was the only individual of this species I saw. Nicely enough, there was a Tennessee Warbler directly above it, about 12-15 feet off the ground feeding in the trees. This gave for a great comparison.
Call: silent
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Demarcations on the head were too sharp for a Warbling Vireo (darkness of crown, sharpness of eye line), also wings were too dark for a WAVI.

Tennessee Warblers were around, I saw 3-4 that day. I already mentioned the proximity of this bird to a TEWA which gave for nice comparison.

Resources Used

One of my boys had Sibley with him that we looked over together so they could see the markings and differences.

Previous Experience

I am from the Midwest, I have seen this bird annually for 20 years on migration - Spring and Fall. Also seen it on territory in Ontario.
