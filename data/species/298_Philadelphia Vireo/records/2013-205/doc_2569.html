Description of the Bird

I had pished in several Warbling Vireos (and a couple Wilson's Warblers) when I caught a glimpse of a bird washed yellow beneath, much the color of many fall Yellow Warblers. When it popped into view again, I could see that the bird was a Warbling/RE type vireo, washed from throat to vent in pale to medium clear yellow (no green hues), shorter tailed and billed than the nearby WAVIs. As I followed it through the trees (it circled me as I did a E Screech Owl whistle), I could eventually see a bright green back, gray crown, and black line through the eyes with subdued pale arc below eyes (c/w WAVI). The bird also had a moderate whitish supercilium. No wingbars.The yellow beneath was even, no brighter on flanks than mid chest or throat, in all lighting, crappy and decent. The bird was 20-30 feet from me while being observed

Similar Species Discussion

Warbling Vireo almost always lacks black(ish) lores. Has larger bill, longer tail, flatter head and lacks yellow throat. Tennesse Warbler has thinner blacker bill. Orange-crowned Warbler also with thinner blacker bill among other differences
