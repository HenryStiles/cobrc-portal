Description of the Bird

Hummingbird by size and behavior
Archilocus genus by very narrow inner primaries compared to broad outer primaries (see photo)
Call given which sounded like a buzz and not something I was familar with.
Small hummer
Forked-tail with white-tipped outer primaries (see photo)
Light green wash to breast but not throat
No evidence of any rufous in tail

Shy, came twice to feeder but did not feed, only hovering twice about 10 feet above feeders
perched twice at high branches in neighbor's Siberian elm and also perched once at sunflower stem in back yard but only for three seconds

Similar Species Discussion

Wing pattern of broad outer primaires with noticeably thinner inner primaires at P6 or P5 eliminates all but BCHU and RTHU = inner primaires noticably thinner than outer primaries starting at P6 or P5 (see photo)
P8-P10 narrow and pointed - would be broad and rounded in BCHU (see photo)
crown and nape golden green - would be duller and more brown in BCHU (see photos)
bill short without drooped tip - bill longer in BCHU, especially in a female BCHU
tail notched with white-tipped outer rectrices = tail not this notched in BCHU (see photo of perched bird).
In brief feeder visits while hovering above feeder - no tail bobbing which is better for RTHU and not good for BCHU

Resources Used

Bird previously identified with above combination of characters and other viewers saw one or two red throat feathers I did not see, characteristic of first year male RTHU.

I looked at and read all the descriptions and photos of both species (and other species) in both Steve N.G. Howell's Hummingbirds of North America, The Photographic Guide, and in Sheri Williamson's Hummingbirds of North America.

Previous Experience

I see BCHUs and photograph them perched and feeding every year in the summer at Colorado Springs Utilities Xeriscape Garden.  I have old experience with RTHU where I grew up in Ohio but limited experience with them in Colorado, especially little experience trying to I.D. immature birds.
