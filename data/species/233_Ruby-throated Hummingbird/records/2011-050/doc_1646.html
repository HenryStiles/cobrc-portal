Description of the Bird

At first glance we thought it was a Black-chinned Hummingbird...slender, small headed...definite black chin and white spot behind eye...then it flashed...ORANGE. Iridescent green back and forked tail. Tail was definitely longer than wings. Green mottled underparts with, extensive white fore-collar. Whitish median stripe down center of underparts.
Behaviors: Bird was quite actively flashing and exhibiting territorial behavior...chasing off female Black-chinned hummingbirds but deferring to larger orioles. Fed extensively from small window feeder and larger feeder. Would light and look around for several moments at a time. Then would flash and display.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Male

Similar Species Discussion

Was seen at the same time and compared to similar Black-chinned female hummingbird. Bright orange gorget. Tail was much longer than wing tips. Tail was more deeply forked. Compared to male Black-chinned, tail more deeply forked, extends well beyond primaries. Does not exhibit "pumping" behavior of Black-chinned. Bill slightly shorter and straighter.
Eliminated as Broad-tail because of definite white dot behind eye with black head and chin...this bird had white median stripe...Broad-tail does not. Bird was smaller and more slender than broad tail and had no sign of white strip behind eye. Gorget was more extensive and blended into dark head and chin.
Eliminated from Rufous as the only orange on the bird was the gorget. Back was green.
