Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Farm headquarters for cattle operation and dryland wheat farming operation; viewed and photographed the bird in the backyard of the farmhouse. The bird visited two feeders, checked out a small honeysuckle, yellow mums, Russian Sage, and perched in a Honey Locust.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  field binoculars
Distance:  1' to 20'
Light:  The 20 to 30 mph wind for the first three days created a dusty haze which made observation more difficult. The last several days had good sunlight

Description of the Bird

Bright green crown and back, between 3 1/2 and 4 inches in length, white patch behind eye with grayish patch below contrasting with chin, a single red spot on gorget, light breast, buffy green sides and flanks, green tail (black bar on each side with 3 rounded white tips - notch in center)
Behaviors: The bird had difficulty in feeding in the hard wind and pumped its tail some. It darted in and out about every ten minutes and disappeared to the north of the house for a windbreak. On the 6th the wind let up, the bird fed more leisurely and did not pump its tail.
Call: not noted
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

On the 6th, in the sunlight, he flashed the single red spot in the gorget.
