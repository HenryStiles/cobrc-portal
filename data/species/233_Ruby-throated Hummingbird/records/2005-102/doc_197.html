Description of the Bird

The bird was a small hummingbird, fairly slender in appearance. The bill was black, fairly short (a little longer than the heas), and with a very, very slight decurve.

The bird had a brilliant greenish cap and nape, with a slight golden tinge. There was a black auricular patch starting just in front of the eye, extending along the egde of the green crown and the white throat, which was boldest in from of the eye, and hazy gray behind the eye. There was a bold white spot behind the eye. The throat was white, with bold grayish streaks extending into the center of the throat from the lower edge. There was single brilliant spot of scarlet red on the center of the lower edge of the throat, visible in certain lights as black, and in others as red.

The bird had a large white collar across the chest, wrapping around onto the upperparts on the nape. The back, rump, uppertail coverts, and uppertail were the same brilliant green color with a slight golden tinge was the crown. The underparts had very large grayish patches on the flanks, almost meeting on the belly, but with a thin white vertical line seperating the sides. There was small buffy patch on the lower flanks, just above the feet, and the undertail coverts were white.

The tail was fairly long (possibly about the length of the bill), and the outer tail feathers were black, with greenish bases, and bold white tips. The central tail feathers were entirely green.

The flight feathers were black. I did not get a good enough look at the primaries to note shape.
Behaviors: The bird was frequently visiting two hummingbird feeders in the yard, starting at first light, and occasionally perching in the nearby trees.
Call: none heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Broad-tailed Hummingbird was ruled out by the smaller size, more slender build, an almost entire lack of rufous on the underparts, especially on the sides, lack of rufous on the tail, and less strongly marked throat.

Rufous Hummingbird was also ruled out by the lack of rufous on the sides, tail, as well as more elongated and slender look.

Calliope Hummingbird was ruled out by lack of rufous on the sides, longer tail, much more elongated appearance, slightly longer bill, and black around eye.

Anna's Hummingbird was ruled out by more slender build, grayish flanks without any green spots, white undertail coverts, stronger white collar on the chest, slight decurve to the bill, and strongly marked throat.

Costa's Hummingbird was ruled out by more elongated look, longer tail, strongly marked throat with a red spot, straighter bill, and lack of a white supercilium and prescense of black around the eye.

Black-chinned, of course, was the hardest to rule out, and the main field mark ruling out that species was the brilliant spot of red on the gorget. Other supporting field marks include shorter bill, entirely green crown with no gray on the forecrown, lack of tail bobbing in flight, and blacker auricular patch.
