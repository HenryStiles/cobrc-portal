Description of the Bird

One bird was very aggressive towards the 2nd bird - the aggressive one made several physical attacks and chased the other away from feeder continually.
I sent a series of photos to expert birders for help with identification with comments back from the following: Tony Leukering, Mark Peterson, Glenn Walbek, Nancy Newfield, and Sheri Williamson.  
Heard no calls.
Emerald green from crown all down back, no rufous coloring, dark line connecting bill to eye, "forked tail"

description from Nancy Newfield about the aggressive bird:  "The super aggressive bird IS an immature male Ruby-throated.  I note the very bright green back, the relatively short bill, the blackish mask, and the shapes of the individual rectrices....the P6 appears to be emarginate, which is exclusively a male Ruby-throated characteristic."

Similar Species Discussion

from Sheri Williamson:

"Not one but TWO juvenile Rubies, female on the feeder not looking too good and male taking advantage of it. The male's primaries would be more obviously RTHU, but (fortunately) the shapes of the inner primaries are less generic in juvenile female RTHU than in j-f BCHU. Both also show the proportionally shorter, straighter bill, deeper tail notch, and deeper green upperparts of RTHU, so it's not just the primaries.  Hope the female survived all that harassment!"
