Description of the Bird

On the afternoon of 10/3/2013, I noticed a hummingbird that was similiar to the regular Black-chinned Hummingbirds which I have great experience with.  This bird was not pumping his tail.  I began to notice the behavior was slightly different so I grabbed my binoculars and started to observe the fine details of the bird.  He had a shorter bill that was very noticeable, green crown, and the forked tail(much greater than Black-chinned) stood out when perched.  No matter how much I tried I could not rule out the more rare Ruby-throated Hummingbird. So to confirm my suspicion, I had a friend of mine in Louisiana send the photos to a hummingbird expert(Nancy Newfield) he knew. Here is Nancy's response to the request:

I don't mind at all.  The challenges are terrific and fun.  I have already received several similar requests from Colorado from Tony Leukering so my comments to you are copied to him as well and I will forward your post as well.  I have studied hummingbirds in Louisiana and elsewhere since about 1975.  If you don't already have my recent booklet 'Louisiana Hummingbirds' [from BTNEP], send your postal address and I will mail you a copy.

 She confirmed as a immature male Ruby-throated:

I believe this little guy is a valid Ruby-throated and for the characteristics you've cited.  It is an immature male and judging by the buffy feather edges on his crown, it fledged only fairly recently.

Similar Species Discussion

I had to rule out immaure Black-chinned Hummingbird.  This bird did not wag tail, greater forked tail, green crown(not gray).  The bill seemed much shorter than the Black-chinned Hummingbird that I'm very familiar with.   By looking at the photos you can see a shorter tail and less contrast between face and throat.. 

Resources Used

First of all I have great expierence with Black-chinned Hummingbirds that I have multiple pairs throughout the summer.  I looked at multiple field guides including Peterson's Hummingbirds of North America.  I also sent the photos to a hummingbird expert in Louisiana.

Previous Experience

I have extensive expeience with Black-chinned Hummingbirds that is the primary hummingbird at my residence.  At times in the fall I can have up to 10-15 birds in the yard of all ages.  My experience with Ruby-throated is very limited. other than birding vacations to the midwest and south.
