Description of the Bird

The bill of the hummingbird was all black. Even though it is a subjective feature, the bill appeared shorter and much straighter than a Black-Chinned Hummingbird's bill. The feathers at the base of its bill ,on the top side were slightly grayish, while from just above that (there is almost no way for me to accurately describe the placement of that transition point) on up the head and over the crown were green. From there on down the back was about the same shade of darker green except for a few lime colored feathers mixed in. The wings were black, with the last primary being pointed and somewhat narrower than the primary just before it. The primary projection was not quite to the end of the tail. The top of the tail was green all the way to where it turned black with white spots at the end. In photos taken by someone else the tail feathers on this bird were more rounded than sharp. The under tail coverts were bright white. The belly and sides were washed with a slight gray. The throat was bright white with no feathers of any other color other than a few that had small amounts of gray on their tips. The eye was black and there was a white spot behind the eye, not around or completely over top the eye. Bordering the throat and extending down from the eye was a gray area almost like a stripe. In front of the eye and extending to the bill was a very dark, almost black stripe. In general the contrast of the dark head and the brilliant white throat was what first caught me off guard (besides the fact that we have not had any hummingbirds at our feeder for two weeks).
Behaviors: It fed at our feeders or just sat in the tree and made ocasional chattering. While it was at our feeder and while it was flying it never pumped its tail. I did video tape it several times at our feeder to support that feature. It seemed quite cold the second morning it was here. It would try and sit in the direct sunlight and it would frequently strech its wings from side to side and fan its tail. It looked really funny when it would do this.
Call: A chattering. I did manage to tape it if that would be any help. I don't know how to distinguish between Ruby-throated and Black-chinned calls though.
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

Even though it is a subjective feature, the bill appeared shorter and much straighter than a Black-Chinned Hummingbird's bill. The feathers at the base of its bill ,on the top side were slightly grayish, while from just above that (there is almost no way for me to accurately describe the placement of that transition point) on up the head and over the crown were green. As far as I know, Black-chinned Hummingbirds do not have a dark green crown.The wings were black, with the last primary being pointed and somewhat narrower than the primary just before it. Again, I don't think Black-chinned Hummingbirds have pointed primaries. The primary projection was not quite to the end of the tail. In photos taken by someone else the tail feathers on this bird were more rounded than sharp as in Black-chinned. The throat was bright white with no feathers of any other color other than a few that had small amounts of gray on their tips, while Black-chinned has more gray. The eye was black and there was a white spot behind the eye, not around or completely over top the eye like Anna's or Costa's. In general the contrast of the dark head and the brilliant white throat was what first caught me off guard because the female Black-chinneds I have seen do not have as much contrast.
