Description of the Bird

Small female hummingbird dark green above, white below, lacking any buff or rufous on flanks or in the tail. Tail pail green basally, with black subterminal band, tipped white on outer 3 rectrices. Bill black, short and straight. Black eye with a white spot behind it. Tail extends beyond wings at rest.
Behaviors: Resting on tree limb and visiting a feeder.
Call: soft, single note, somewhat warbler-like, given repeatedly at rest.
Plumage: adult?

Similar Species Discussion

Black-chinned Hummingbird has longer bill, and grayer shade to front of crown. Anna's Hummingbird has grayer underparts and paler crown and more white above and around eye. Costa's and Calliope has shorter tail. Calliope and Broad-tailed and Rufous and Allen's have buffy flanks. These last three also have cinnamon in tail.
