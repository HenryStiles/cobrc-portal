Description of the Bird

I was watching hummingbirds at my backyard feeder at my cabin in the Wet Mountains, when I noticed this odd bird, that wasn't any of usual ones. I commonly see Broad-tailed, Rufous, and Calliope, as well as Black-chinned once in a while here. This bird was similar to Black-chinned, though it had green head, shorter bill, and didn't pump the tail like the back chinned was doing. Photos taken.
Behaviors: Coming a few times to feeders in back yard.
Call: none
Plumage: fall plumaged

Similar Species Discussion

Black-chinned -- pumps tail a lot, gray forehead, not green like this bird. Bill much longer, than this bird.

Resources Used

none

Previous Experience

Seen lots of Ruby-throated Hummingbirds in the east and a few males in Colorado. Seen lots of female Black-chinned Hummingbirds, as well as males, and this bird was quite different.
