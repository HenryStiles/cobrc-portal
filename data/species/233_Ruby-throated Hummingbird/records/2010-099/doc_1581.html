Description of the Bird

A medium-small hummingbird dominated by green back, grayish underparts, and a few (3, I believe) central gorget feathers. The bill was of moderate length and nearly straight. The crown was fairly bright green (considering the poor light, with the green coloration continuing down the back. The face featured a white post-occular spot, but no white above or in front of the eye. A prominent gray auricular contrasted with the whitish throat, which featured rows of fine dark spots and the aforementioned iridescent red gorget feathers that would go from black through pink and ruby-red depending on the angle. The rest of the underparts were light gray, a bit darker on the flanks than on the breast or belly. The tail was deeply notches, with the wingtips falling well short of the tail tip. I didn't obtain good views of the primary shapes.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding on agastache flowers and perching to rest in between feeding bouts. When hovering to feed, no tail flicking was observed beyond that expected for flight control.
Call: Mostly silent but a few faint smacking chips heard.
Plumage: Immature Male

Similar Species Discussion

Among hummingbirds, the most similar species to rule out is Black-chinned. The red gorget feather color goes a long way towards eliminating Black-chinned, as does the shorter, straighter bill, deeper green crown, deeper notched tail, shorter wing projection, and lack of persistent tail flicking when hovering. Among red-gorgeted hummingbirds, Anna's can be ruled out by the lack of white over and in front of the eye, Broad-tailed can be ruled out by the gray auricular and lack of rufous tones in the tail and flanks, and Calliope can be ruled out by size and lack of buffy tones on the underparts, and Allen's/Rufous by lack of rufous tones on flanks and tail area.
