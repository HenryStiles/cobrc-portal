Description of the Bird

Moderately small hummingbird with fairly short, straight bill. Underparts pale gray, nearly totally lacking in any buffy or rufous tones except for faint buffy wash on rearmost upper flanks. Upperparts contrasting dark green. White post-occular spot but no white above eye. Lores nearly black. Aruicular dark gray. Throat and forecollar nearly white. 3 gorget feathers iridescent red. Tail lacking rufous tones, with wide white tips to tail feathers. Tail notched, with central feathers distinctly shorter and each side forming rounded profile. Wings dark, with wingtips falling well short of tail tip.
Behaviors: The bird was seen feeding on agastache flowers and perching in my back yard. When feeding it didn't pump or flare its tail significantly.
Call: not heard
Plumage: immature

Similar Species Discussion

Lack of rufous tones on underparts and tail eliminate Selasphorus species. Larger and less compact-looking than Calliope, which also should show more color in the underparts. Lack of white above and in front of eye eliminates Anna's. Red gorget and wingtip placement on tail eliminates Costa's. Closest comparison species is Black-chinned. Despite not being able to see the primary shapes well this species can be eliminated due to the gorget color, shorter & straighter bill, more deeply-notched tail, and comparison of wingtip to tail length (which should be about even on Black-chinned.)
