Description of the Bird

This was a smallish imm/female hummer that came flying (first seen about 10 meters away) into my fuchsia t-shirt, hovering in front of it for 6-7 seconds and then flying off. As soon as I saw it coming toward me, I expected it to be a RTHU, due to the easterly CO location and the propitious month. The bird's back was bright green; the crown was grayish-green and separated from the whitish lower cheek and throat by a distinct -- but thin -- blackish mask; the underparts were whitish, except for a bit of buff on the sides/flanks; the tail lacked rufous, in fact being mostly dark, except for large white tips to, at least, the outer rects; the bill was of medium length, black, and straight (no downcurve at the tip). The tail was not wagged at all during the time it hovered in front of me.

Similar Species Discussion

Given the overall coloration and, particularly, the black mask, BCHU is the only real ID contender.  However, that species typically wags its tail repeatedly while hovering, its bill is longer and sports a downward curve to the distal third (or so).  Additionally, that species generally has duller coloration above, unlike the nearly emerald back of the bird that I saw.  Finally, ANHU is also a contender due to the mask, but that species underparts are dingy, not the whitish that strongly contrasted with the buff flanks and black mask.

Previous Experience

I have seen >1500 RTHUs in the eastern US and Canada, Mexico, and Costa Rica.  I have seen only one previously in Colorado.  I have seen >300 BCHUs in the western US and >400 ANHUs in California and Mexico.
