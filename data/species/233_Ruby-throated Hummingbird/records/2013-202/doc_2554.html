Description of the Bird


This bird was observed about 1.5 miles south of Rye CO at my house on feeder (8155 Birch Drive}.

My first impression of subject bird was of a female Black-chinned Hummingbird(BCHU). But the bird only occasionally flicked its tail, and did not exhibit the usual consistent tail pumpling of BCHU. When on my feeder, I immediately looked at the primaries which had narrow pointed  tips, exhibited by Ruby-throated Hummingbirds(RTHU). Subject bird was similar in size to nearby BCHUs.

Bird was solid green above from crown, nape, back, to uppertail coverts. It was white below especially on brighter unmarked throat contrasting with dull whitish flanks & belly. A white post ocular dot contrasted wiith dark lores & grayish white auriculars.

Tail extended noticeably beyond pointed wing tips. Tail was mostly black except for white spots on tail tip. No brownish nor rufous coloring was noticed in the tail. Bill was long & slim similar to female BCHU, but not as long as some female BCHUs I noticed.
 
Bird made no sound nor call notes.

Similar Species Discussion

Female RTHU & BCHU are very similar but the latter has broader, blunter, & rounded wing tips. Bird I saw had narrow pointed wing tips. I think this is possibly the best field mark for separating these two hummers. The lack of consistent tail pumping on bird I saw also is more consistent with RTHU than BCHU.  Bird I saw had a tail noticeably extending beyond wing tips, where most BCHU's tails barely clear wing tips.
