Description of the Bird

Hummingbird size and shape consistent with Archilocus. Bright green back and crown. Face with blackish lores and dark green auriculars, contrasting with bright white, unmarked, throat. Underparts whitish with olive sides and just a tiny bit of buff (visible from only some angles) on rear flanks by legs. Bill all black, shorter than that of BCHU in direct comparison. Important tail features not visible except in photos. Plumage looked moderately worn, consistent with aging as an adult. Useful photos, incorporated into this report, were taken when the bird was dusted off the feeder by a BCHU. When feeding, the bird did not spread, nor wag, its tail at all. At one time, feeding for a goodly 30 seconds uninterrupted. 

Similar Species Discussion

BCHU 
1- Should not be as deep green on back
2- Should be especially duller, typically gray-green, on crown
3- Usually does not have such substantical contrast between auriculars and throat (due both to darker auriculars and whiter throat)
4- In adult female BCHU, the tips to R3 through R5 should be pointed. The tips are rounded on the Cheraw bird. 
5- In adult and young female BCHU, R3 should be longer than R4. On this bird, R4 is distinctly longer
6- Bird aged as adult due to relatively worn plumage, extremely limited buff below, no markings on throat (which some imm female BCHU may lack, but all imm males should have)
7- The BCHUs at the feeder pumped their tails, at least intermittently. This bird did not. 
8- The bill was shorter in direct comparison with a BCHU
