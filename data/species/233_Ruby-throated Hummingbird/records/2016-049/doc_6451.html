Description of the Bird

This bird was just north of Purgatoire Ave. about 100 yards east of its jct with Colorado Blvd., Colorado City, SW Pueblo County.

Small Hummingbird about size of nearby Black-chinneds briefly hovered & perched on shrub covered with pea-sized ruby red berries not unlike color of its throat. Throat was dark ruby red becoming blackish below base of needle shaped bill. Back and nape were emerald green. Dark tail was noticably forked and extended past wings when bird was perched. When bird hovered & flew off it made no sounds with wings nor chip notes. Because of observation's brevity, I was unable to see more subtle details, but above description should be sufficient for ID.

Similar Species Discussion

Adult male Black-chinned Hummingbirds show violet purple throats, not the reddish throats of Ruby-throateds.

Adult male Broad tailed Hummingbirds show a more uniform reddish throat but a paler more melon colored red lacking blackish tones below base of bill. These field marks contrast with darker ruby red throat & blackish color to throat below base of bill in Ruby-throated. Broad-tails often give easily heard ringing sound in flight; the observed Ruby-throat exhibited no audible sound.
