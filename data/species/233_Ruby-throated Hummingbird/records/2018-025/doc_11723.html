Description of the Bird

I saw at least three hummers in the yard, though only two well enough to ID to species; the third was probably another RTHU, given the white underparts and similar size and shape to the RTHU chasing it.  I am submitting photos of both birds.

The first one that I ID'ed was an immature male, which I saw come in to the feeders twice, but stayed only briefly the first time; my photos of it are from its second visit, which lasted nearly a minute.  It had fairly bright green back and rump, though with thin buff fringes to all feathers.  The nape and crown were duller green with whitish-buff fringes.  There was a distinct blackish mask extending from bill to eye and an obvious white post-ocular spot.  The throat was white with parallel columns of green feathers that each had a whitish fringe.  The underparts were whitish, but with vague dark streaking on the lower sides that extended into the grayish flanks and onto the lateral under-tail coverts (which were white).  The tail appeared mostly dark (black and/or dark green), with the outer rects having large white tips.  The bill was black, straight, and of only moderate length.


The second bird was an immature female, which came to the feeders at least three times and also perched on a yard decoration for a while, with total viewing time lasting about three minutes.  This bird was quite similar in appearance to the above-described immature male, except that it lacked the columns of green throat feathers, its throat instead being nearly unrelieved white.  Additionally, its crown was grayer than that of the other bird.


Neither bird wagged its tail during hovering, excepting only very infrequently and desultorily.  Both birds had relatively pointed outermost primaries.

Similar Species Discussion

BCHU is really the only ID contender, and that species is ruled out by the shape of the tips of the outermost primaries, the brightness of the upperparts, the shape and length of the bill, and the relative lack of tail wagging during hovering flight.  ANHU is ruled out by the whiteness of the underparts and the contrast of those underparts with the mask and upperparts.
