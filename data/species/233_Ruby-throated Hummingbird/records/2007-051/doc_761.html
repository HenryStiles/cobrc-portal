Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon
Distance:  75 ft
Light:  Clear and sunny. The bird perched on a fence near a hummingbird feeder and faced due east, with the sun at its back. This provided excellent views of the birds gorget.

Description of the Bird

Hummingbird, approximately the size of a Broad-tailed Hummingbird. Gorget color: red. Deeply forked tail when perched and deeply forked tail when spread in a hover. No wing buzz when flying. Tail irregularly bobbed during feeding.
Behaviors: The bird was perched on a fence near a hummingbird feeder. The bird was also seen feeding at a hummingbird feeder. When feeding whilst hovering, the bird bobbed its posterior in an irregular manner and less emphatically than a Black-chinned Hummingbird.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

Male Broad-tailed Hummingbird, male Black-chinned Hummingbird and male Anna's Hummingbird. Lack of wing buzz and the presence of a deeply forked tail eliminate Broad-tailed Hummingbird. Lack of iridescent crown when view from many angles eliminates Anna's Hummingbird. Red gorget and less emphatic tail wagging whilst feeding and deeplu forked tail suggest Ruby-throated Hummingbird over Black-chinned Hummingbird.

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I have many years of experience with Ruby-throated Hummingbird in New England. I am also familiar with Broad-tailed Hummingbird from Colorado, Black-chinned Hummingbird from Colorado and Washington State and Anna's Hummingbird from Washington State and California.
