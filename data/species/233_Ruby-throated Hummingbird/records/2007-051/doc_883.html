Description of the Bird

Red-gorgeted hummingbird with black auriculars and black all the way up to the eye above the red gorget feathers. Small white postocular spot. Moderate-sized hummingbird with moderate-length bill, very slightly downcurved. Back metallic green, belly grayish green, breast and upper flanks nearly white. Vent area nearly white. Tail dark, nearly black.
Behaviors: Visited residential feeder several times, aggressive towards Calliope Hummingbirds. Perched briefly in a tree nearby feeders.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Among red-gorgeted N.A. hummingbirds, adult male Lucifer Hummingbird is smaller and has a proportionately much longer, more curved bill. Adult male Anna's Hummingbird has a red helmet in addition to the gorget. Adult male Calliope Hummingbird is smaller, has a proportionately smaller and straighter bill, has a striped rather than a solid gorget, and lacks the black auriculars and black area between the eye and gorget. Adult male Broad-tailed Hummingbird lacks the black auriculars and black area between the eye and gorget. Additionally, adult male Broad-tailed Hummingbirds habitually trill their wings in a distinctive buzz, which wasn't heard, even in aggressive pursuit of a Calliope Hummingbird. Adult male Allen's and Rufous Hummingbirds have more of an orange gorget, and orange flanks, bellies, and auriculars. Adult male Black-chinned Hummingbirds quite similar, showing black gorgets as this bird did when not flashing color, but they flash to purple, not red. Additionally, Black-chinned Hummingbirds have longer, more curved bills.
