Description of the Bird

A medium-sized hummer, Chris first noticed the bird coming to a Trumpet Creeper hedge a block north of Hwy 144 and called out "Ruby-throated Hummer!" The bird was, apparently, a female, as it lacked any red gorget feathers. The bird had a longish, straight black bill and dark eyes. The crown, nape, back, and rump were all bright green. There was a relatively distinct dark auricular patch below and behind the small white post-ocular spot and separating the green of the crown from the white of the throat. The throat and chest were bright white and the sides and flanks were splotchy greenish and white creating the appearance of being washed with grayish-green. The undertail coverts were pale and unmarked, as far as I could tell.

The tail was green (basally) and black, with obvious white tips to the outer 2 or 3 pairs of rectrices (I couldn't be sure whether 2 or 3 in the brief look I had at the spread tail). The tail extended more than a bit beyond the wingtips when the bird was perched. The bird's "shoulder" (lesser coverts) were the same bright green as the back and the remiges were blackish. Through the scope, we could easily see that P10 was narrow and pointed, rather than wide and more rounded as in Black-chinned.
Behaviors: hovering in front of Trumpet Creeper flowers and perched very low (~3' off ground) in a small tree
Call: none heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Black-chinned is quite similar, but, ultimately, ruled out by the shape of P10. Other features that assisted in this determination was the brighter green of the upperparts (less gray), the straightness of the bill, the relationship of tail tip to wingtip when perched, and the darker auricular patch.

Other expected hummers (Rufous, Calliope, Broad-tailed) ruled out by lack of rufous in the tail and/or size (too large for Calliope) and/or tail-wing placement (Calliope tail tip and wingtip about equal). Calliope was also present at this hedge, so direct size comparison easy.

Other rare hummers ruled out by bright white chest (Anna's), dark auricular patch (Costa's), and overall size (Magnificent, Blue-throated, and Broad-billed).
