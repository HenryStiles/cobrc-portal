Description of the Bird

Bewick's Wren (Thryomanes bewickii): Medium-sized wren with rather long white eyebrow. Longish, slightly decurved bill. Upper mandible blackish, lower mandible lighter gray. Fairly long tail. Feathers under the tail barred, some with white spots. Upperparts grayish-brown, throat whitish, underparts whitish, gray on sides, flanks and legs.
Behaviors: Moved rapidly among shrubs and bushes on the hillside. Waged tail from side to side.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Carolina Wren is very rusty above with buffy underparts. Bewick's Wren is smaller with grayer underparts and a longer tail. Carolina Wren does not have white spots on the underside of the tail. House Wren has only a very faint dusky eyebrow and lacks white spots on the undertail.
