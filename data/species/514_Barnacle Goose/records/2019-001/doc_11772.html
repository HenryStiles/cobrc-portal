Description of the Bird

Small goose (size of small cackler), black chest, head, and neck; white face and throat, dark eye, white belly and sides with vague flank streaks, blue-gray scaling on back and wings, black bill, grayish legs (paler than Canada). Seen lounging with other geese, sometimes swimming in open water, sometimes picking at stuff on the ice, sometimes asleep. Vocalized regularly, but voice inaudible over the noise of the other geese.

Similar Species Discussion

Nothing really similar. Canada rarely shows a face pattern like Barnacle (one such bird was in the flock), but chest is still pale and wings and back are brown.

Resources Used

I know Barnacle Goose well, having seen hundreds in Sweden in September 2018. Sweden has seen an explosion of introduced Canada Geese, of which I saw many with the Barnacles. I carry photos of Barnacle Geese with Graylags on my phone. I also carry the Collins Guide to the Birds of Europe on my phone, which has good illustrations and a recording of flight calls. 

Previous Experience

I've seen Barnacle Goose in captivity and wild birds in Sweden. No similar species, even in other parts of the world.
