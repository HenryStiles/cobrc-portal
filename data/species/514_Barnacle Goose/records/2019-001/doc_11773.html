Description of the Bird

Small goose with a white face surrounded by a black bill, black on top of head, and black neck. Dark back with black and white lines across back. Bright white flanks, rump and undertail, with a black tail. Black eye and dark legs. No calls given.  The attached photos by Bill Schmoker show that the hallux is in tact on both legs, and no bands on legs. 

Similar Species Discussion

The pattern of this goose eliminates all other species.  Perhaps the closest in appearance is the dark phase of Ross's Goose, but it is smaller with dark flanks, an all white tail and pinkish bill and legs.  Brant is eliminated by the white face, and Emperor Goose by the black neck.

Resources Used

National Geographic and Sibley field guides.

Previous Experience

I have seen flocks of Barnacle Geese in England, but a long time ago.  Have seen one dark phase Ross's Goose and two Emperor Geese. Am familiar with all the geese that regularly occur in Colorado.
