Description of the Bird

This bird was the same size as a Vesper Sparrow that was seen while chasing it. It was overall a brownish bird above and had at least two white outer tail feathers on each side. It had a large dark eye which contrasted with the lighter brown face, and the crown was darker. When seen on the ground it had two thin white wing bars and patterned wing coverts and upper back. The bill was long and the legs were pinkish. The throat was white but I never saw the front of the bird well.
Behaviors: The bird flushed several times while we chased it. It had a characteristic flight and went to the ground swiftly every time it landed. It then ran through the short vegetation and was very difficult to see well.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Vesper Sparrow: Bird was lighter overall and didn't have the face pattern of dark cheek and white malar. No prominent eye ring and much longer bill.
American Pipit: Bird was much browner and crisply marked and had pink legs.
Juvenile Horned Lark: No spotting on head and upper back and white in tail much more extensive.
Chestnut-collared and McCown's Longspurs: White pattern in tail not correct, crisper brown plumage and pink legs.
Smith's and Lapland Longspurs: Much browner plumage and pink legs.
