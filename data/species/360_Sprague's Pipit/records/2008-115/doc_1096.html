Description of the Bird

the bird was about the size of a Horned Lark, but with a somewhat different shape - the tail was shorter and wider, and the bird overall seemed a bit chunkier. The bill was thin and fairly short. I could not see the legs.

The underparts of the bird were white with a slightly off-white tinge. There was a necklace of black streaks across the chest, fairly bold and easy to see. The upperparts of the bird were on the light end of medium brown. The tail was black with two white outer tail feathers.
Behaviors: The bird was only observed flying overhead, though it was close, in excellent light, and provided a fairly long and excellent look.
Call: The bird emitted a Barn Swallow like "squeet" about eight times at it flew overhead, mostly in closely spaced doublets.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Sparrow species were ruled out by the thin bill, distinctive shape with wide, short tail, small headed look, and by the call. Horned Lark was ruled out by the shorter and wider tail, necklace of streaks, and by the call. American Pipit was ruled out by the shorter tail with more extensively white outer tail feathers, by the much less extensively streaked underparts with the streaking limited to a necklace across the chest, and by the very different call.
