Description of the Bird

Bill: slender and pointed, mostly pale especially at the base.
Face: rather plain with the appearance of an eyering. The eye was dark and large giving the bird a “big eyed” look.
Crown: brown and streaked
Back: brown, streaked with a scaly appearance.
Underparts: white with a faint buffy wash and a necklace of streaks on the upper breast
Tail: short, dark in the center noticeably edged with white
Legs: pink
Behaviors: When the bird flew it climbed quite high and then dropped like a stone into the grass.
Call: Not heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

American Pipit is a browner overall bird with a plainer back, lacking bold wing-bars, and dark legs. Longspurs have a conical bill and the McCowan's and Chestnut-collared have much more white in the tail. None of the above species exhibit the habit of flying up quite high and then dropping into the grass.
