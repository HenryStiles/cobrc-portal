Habitat

Habitat was a traditional spot on the slopes of a hill with little bluestem and horse weed where this species has been seen in the past during October.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10x42 Ultravid bins
Distance:  I flushed it first from about 50 feet away.
Light:  Okay, sun had dropped over the hills.

Description of the Bird

The bird was mostly identified by its flight call, behavior in flight and from the photographs that accentuated a couple of distinctive characteristics of this species.

In flight the tail was very short, contrasting outer 2-3 tail featers with darker central tail rects and the wings are very broad and very blunt tipped with a distinctive light vs dark pattern best shown on page 420 of Big Sibley but sort of seen in my distant, rather poor, photo of the bird with wings outspread.
Behaviors: The behavior separated it from all other species combined with its call note. When flused the bird would ascend, not in steps like it sometimes does, but to a height of about 100 or more feet above the ground and then it would drop like a rock straight towards the ground but then leveling off and flying another 80 or more feet before dropping the final 10-15 feet and then disappearing into the taller grasses. This pattern was repeated each time the bird was flushed.
Call: flight call when flushed from ground was high-pitched and one-noted, distincitve.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

In flight, no other species looks quite like this except other pipits and AMPI has a totally different jizz and habitat requirements and different flight calls.
