Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Eurasian Wigeon
First Date/Time:  11/8/2006
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  30 minutes
County:  Mesa
Specific Location:  Pond ~1/4 mile north of Colorado River on east side of 29 Road
Number:  1
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Male
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

open water. 4-5 acre pond--old shallow gravel mine

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  binoculars and spotting scope
Distance:  Estimated 60 to 100 yards
Light:  Fair. Sun high and perpendiclar to line of sight.

Description of the Bird

Head red with golden "pate" and forehead. Faint hint of geenish mask. Bill blue with black tip.
Back and sides gay; horizontal white slash on side.
Undertail coverts black; white patch on flank separating black of coverts from gray sides.
Behaviors: Swimming liesurely and resting. Nothing different from American Wigeons.
Call: none
Plumage: alternate gray morph

Similar Species Discussion

Distinctive duck which would be difficult to confuse with anything else.
American Wigeon has similar pattern, but gray cheeks, strong dark green mask, vinaceous sides, and no horizontal white slash on sides.

Resources Used

Sibley & Nat'l Geog. guides consulted after observation.

Previous Experience

I have seen this species on two other occasions in Colorado and also in Washington state.

Notes

Notes made AFTER observation

Additional Information

Comments: Greenish mask suggested possible hybridization, but Internet search revealed that this trait is not uncommon at least in in east Asian menbers of this species. http://www.birdskorea.org/hybducks.asp was quite helpful.
