Description of the Bird

The bird was with American Wigeons on a pond.

It had a similar size and shape to the American Wigeons.

The bill was bluish gray.

The face was a uniform dark reddish brown. The eye (or eye patch) was darker, blackish in color.

The forehead and crown were a golden color.

The breast was a dull reddish gray, mottled.

The back and sides were gray, with white wing patches visible.

The flanks were a clean white.

The tail and the base of the tail were black.
Behaviors: swimming in a pond with other puddle ducks (including many American Wigeon)
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult basic plumage

Similar Species Discussion

This was a very easy ID. An adult male, seen well, does not look at all like any species of North American duck. Probably the key issue is whether it was a pure Eurasian Wigeon or a hybrid Eurasian-American. The lack of any greenish color on the face, plus the classic orange face with yellowish forehead, suggests to me that it was not a hybrid.
