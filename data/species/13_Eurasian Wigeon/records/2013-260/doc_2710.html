Description of the Bird

The bird was on a medium sized lake with a group of American Wigeons of mixed sexes.  It stayed with the group and I did not hear it vocalize while I was observing the bird, although I did hear the American Wigeons vocalize a few times.

The bird had dark rufous on the head and neck, with a buffy forehead.  The sides were light or pale gray.  The tail and coverts were black, with a white patch in front of them.

Similar Species Discussion

The similar species of American Wigeon was elimitated by the dark rufous on the head, which was easily contrasted with the nearby American Wigeons.

Previous Experience

I have seen American Wigeons quite frequently in the past.  This was my first experience seeing a Eurasian Wigeon.
