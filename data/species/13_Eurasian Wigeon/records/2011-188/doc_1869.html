Description of the Bird

At first it didn't appear that there were any ducks on this lake where a Eurasian wigeon had been reported. There were, however, a few Amerian wigeon on the seepage pond below the dam. I was just about ready to go back and check them over again when I spotted a few green-winged teal and American wigeon up on the muddy shore across the lake. They all had their heads down feeding. I spotted the Eurasian wigeon intermingled with the 30 American wigeon feeding on the shore. It's gold crown was readily apparent among the female and male American wigeons. Structurally, he was like the American wigeons, but has a russet-colored head and gold crown. His sides were also grayer as was his back. Even at a couple of hundred yards the male EUWI stuck out like a sore thumb. I observed the bird on 3 seperate occasions today. Everytime it was feeding on the shore and I never saw it near the water. At one of my stops here, I ran into Bob Spencer.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding on the muddy shoreline with American wigeons.
Call: not observed
Plumage: breeding/alternate

Similar Species Discussion

This bird was with American wigeon and allowed direct size comparison. It was the same size and structurally the same as the American wigeon. It had a dark rufous head, with a golden forehead and crown patch as opposed to the AMWI's white forehead, green supercilliary area and gray cheek. Also the Eurasian wigeon had gray flanks and side, unlike the American wigeons brownish sides and chest. The chest on the Eurasian wigeon was pinkish-rust color and contrasted with the gray sides sharply unlike the American wigeon, which showed no marked contrast here.
