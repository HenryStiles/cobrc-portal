Description of the Bird

An obvious male Eurasian Wigeon, the bird had a head that was mostly reddish-brown with a contrasting patch of yellow extending from the bill up through the forehead to the middle of the crown. The chest and upper sides were a pinkish-brown, contrasting strongly with the medium gray middle and lower sides. The rest of the underparts were white, except for a protrusion of gray on each side from the lower sides to near the legs. The bright white flanks contrasted sharply with the black undertail coverts. The upperparts were gray, with the rump slightly paler than the back. The tail, the outer parts at least, was pale gray. On the folded wing, a bright patch of white was evident on the lower edge of the wing immediately distal to the greenish speculum. The bill was pale grayish-blue with a black tip. In flight, the axillars did not contrast with the gray wing linings.
Behaviors: swimming on reservoir and foraging on beach
Call: none heard
Plumage: Definitive Basic

Similar Species Discussion

American Wigeon is only remotely similar, but readily told by the bird's reddish head, pink chest, and gray sides and axillars.
