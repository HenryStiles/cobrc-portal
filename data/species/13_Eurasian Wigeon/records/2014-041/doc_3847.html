Description of the Bird

Rusty head, buffy [ochre yellow] forehead.
Very gray sides with rust on breast and large white wing patch.
Does not dive.
A very striking bird that stands out among the other ducks on the pond.
It associated loosely with American Wigeons, although they seemed not to accept it and occasionally would poke at it to send it away.
On 3/16, it poked about from one side of the pond to the other, fiddled along the west shoreline, and walked up on the shore a couple of times before returning to the water.

Similar Species Discussion

American Wigeon: same size, but white forehead, rusty sides. Essentially the reverse of Eurasian.
Other puddle ducks don't seem similar.
