Description of the Bird

Rusty ("chestnut") head excepting forecrown, without evidence of paler regions (as is typically present in AMWI x EUWI) nor green. Forecrown from bill to about mid-crown was pale butter-buff. Bill blue gray without black at base of bill. Tip black. Chest pinkish. Body mostly gray, with black undertail coverts and vent, with white strap on side in front of that black. Tail longish, typical of wigeon, shorter than pintail. White from wing visible on side as bird swam. Size similar to that of male AMWI

Similar Species Discussion

AMWI x EUWI. 
Should have some blotchiness or bimaculation to rust/chestnut of face, often with green on head, black near base of bill
