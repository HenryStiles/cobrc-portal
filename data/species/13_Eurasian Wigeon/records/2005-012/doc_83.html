Description of the Bird

I walked up to Pueblo City Park pond, where there were several Arkansas Valley Audubon members, just meeting up for their field trip, when I noticed a male Eurasian Wigeon on the pond. I said to all there's a male Eurasian Wigeon out there. So, everyone looked at the bird. It had a all red head, with the yellowish crown, not like the white crown of the American Wigeons. The bird has gray sides all well.
Behaviors: Bird was swimming on the pond, and then later on the shore.
Call: none
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

American Wigeon - This species has the white crown and lack of red head.
hybrids - the bird's head was all red, not hint of green, so I think the bird is a good one.

I've seen a few Eurasian Wigeons in Pueblo County before this one, all males. And have seen the species in California. I've seen American Wigeons in many places.
