Description of the Bird

A medium-sized dabbling duck with a burnt orange head and neck with a creamy patch running from bill through the forehead to the middle of the crown. The dark eyes were surrounded by a very thin circlet of dark green. The bill was bluish with the distal 1/4 black and with only the very thinnest of black basal border. The upperparts and middle and lower sides were vermiculated gray and white, with the rump paler than the back. The chest and upper sides were a distinctive pinkish-brown. There was a distinct white flank patch bordered behind by the black vent/undertail coverts region. The tail was dark and fairly long and pointed.
Behaviors: Swimming and foraging in water and on the lawn.
Call: None knowingly heard
Plumage: Basic using Pyle terminology

Similar Species Discussion

No other species are all that similar to male EUWI. However, the trick is to eliminate back-crossed hybrids. However, the bird showed no particular American Wigeon characters.
