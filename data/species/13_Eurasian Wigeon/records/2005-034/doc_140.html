Description of the Bird

Dabbling duck identical in shape and size as the abundant American Wigeon (Anas americana) that were on the same pond. This individual, however, had a rusty-colored head with cream-colored crown stripe; I noticed a very faint iridescent green patch just behind the eye - faint enough that it was not obvious in all light/head angles; a peach-colored breast; light gray sides, wings, and back; the tertials as seen on the folded wing were boldly black-centered; the vent region was also black; the short bill was grayish with a large black tip.

The poor photo was taken during a snow squall, but it shows the head and crown stripe coloration.
Behaviors: It swam with American Wigeons for the time I was there.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

American Wigeon is the most similar in size and shape, but coloration is entirely different - gray head, large green post-ocular stripe, white crown stripe, and brownish body.

The bird did not exhibit any signs of hybridization such as a large green post-ocular stripe or mixed body plumage (brown and gray).
