Description of the Bird

Sitting on water: Medium sized dabbling duck. Body entirely gray except pinkish breast, blackish tertials, black vent with small area of white in front of black. Head rich chestnut reddish color excepting pale buff stripe from bill up along crown. Bill bluish gray with black tip. No black at base of bill. White wing coverts visible intermittently as wing drooped. Tail longish, as is typical of both wigeon. No green on head. 

Similar Species Discussion

No similar bird except Eurasian x American Wigeon hybrid. The most noticable feature of these hybrids is some infusion of American Wigeon phenotype on face, which this bird lacked. It also had no buff on sides and no black at base of bill.
