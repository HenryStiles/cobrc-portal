Habitat

Industrial Park Pond; small man-made pond on the outskirts of town, possibly favored by waterfowl in fowl because there is no hunting at this location

Description of the Bird

When first observed, on 10/16/2018, a dabbling duck, similar to American Wigeons on the pond, but with a redish head and cream-colored pate.  This wigeon was observed along with 100+ American Wigeons, and 1 probable Storm Wigeon (a Storm Wigeon was observed on this pond in fall of 2017, and I was looking to see if it had returned).  The Eurasian Wigeon did not have gray flanks, and I thought it might be an American x Eurasian Wigeon, and posted it on Western Slope Birders News (WSBN) web page. Steve Mlodinow saw the post, and sent an email to me suggesting it was likely a Eurasian Wigeon, and encouraging me to get better photos.  I returned on several days, and eventually obtained photos good enough for Steve Mlodinow to review and confirm it was a Eurasian Wigeon, transitioning from basic to breeding plumage.

The Wigeon remained at this location from 10/16/2018 to 11/21/2018 (last observation I see on eBird). 

Similar Species Discussion

I considered American x Eurasian Wigeon, but Steve Mlodinow advised the bird was Eurasian Wigeon, and over a period of about 3 weeks, the bird molted to adult male plumage.

Previous Experience

I've observed Eurasian Wigeon twice before: adult male in Maryland many years ago, and several adult males in spring near Nome, Alaska.
