Habitat

Freshwater Lake with a good deal of exposed muddy shoreline.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon 16-48x65 Spotting Scope
Distance:  Approximately 250-300 feet
Light:  The sun was to the southeast of the observer and the bird. The bird was being observed to the south and a bit west. There was a slight hazy cloud cover but light conditions were favorable.

Description of the Bird

Completely rufous colored head except for white colored strip running from base of bill up forehead. Bill was a very pale blue. Eye appeared dark. Pale rosy colored breast. Sides were grey and so were the scapulars. The tertials and primaries looked black. White hip patch was apparent. Uppertail coverts and undertail coverts appeared black and tail itself was black. Bird was identical in size to American Wigeons which were swimming around it. Unable to observe bird in flight.
Behaviors: The bird was feeding most of the time it was being observed. This involved the bird putting its head underwater and its tail straight up in the air. The l It was feeding among many other ducks including many American Wigeons, Northern Shovelers, and Mallards. When not feeding the bird was swimming among other ducks.
Call: Unable to hear due to distance and so many other birds vocalizing
Plumage: Adult Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

The rufous colored head causes the Eurasian Wigeon to stand out from the American Wigeon. The white colored strip on the forehead and the rosy colored breast separates it from other redheaded ducks such as Canvasbacks and Redheads.
