Description of the Bird

The duck was feeding in the grass with a flock of American Widgeon. It had a rufous colored head that was slightly darker towards the rear of the head. The forehead was a buffy yellowish color running longitudinally from the top of the bill upwards to the top of the head. The bill was silvery gray with a black tip. The breast had a rose colored hue. The back was a medium gray leading down to black tertial feathers. The flanks were a lighter gray with a horizontal white line that appeared between the flanks and scapulars. The belly was white. The undertail coverts and uppertail coverts were black. The tail feathers were darker gray coming to a point. The legs were dark.
Behaviors: The bird was walking about munching on grass with other ducks.
Call: N/A
Plumage: Adult Breeding

Similar Species Discussion

This plummage of this bird is fairly diagnostic among dabbler ducks. Cinnamon Teal have a rufous head but lack the light forehead patch and gray color on the body. Green-winged Teal have some rufous on the head but a broad green patch as well. A Redhead is similar but it does not have the forehead patch and its breast is black.
