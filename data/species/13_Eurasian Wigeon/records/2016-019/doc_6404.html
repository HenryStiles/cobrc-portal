Description of the Bird

The bird was waddling along among many American Wigeons and Mallards, often putting its head dowm to feed. We could not hear the birds from our location.

Similar Species Discussion

American Wigeon was eliminated by the bird's reddish head, gray flanks, and buffy forehead. The bird's head appeared to be a solid red which seemed to eliminate the possibility of an American Wigeon X Eurasian Wigeon hybrid. 

Resources Used

Memory of last time I saw a Eurasian Wigeon at Pastorius in 2007 and Sibley Guide to North American Birds.  Later after returning home, I looked at photos of EUWI X AMWI hybrids online.

Previous Experience

I found a Eurasian Wigeon at Pastorius Reservoir in 2007 and also saw several in England in 2013.
