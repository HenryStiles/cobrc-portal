Description of the Bird

I first observed the bird foraging in an ag field with perhaps 100 American Wigeon, several Canada geese, and 8 snow geese, many mallards, and later 2 northern pintail. The bird was quite similar in size to the American wigeon, and did not appear to have an affinity to any other individuals. It seemed like it was often near the snow geese. The bird stood out, at the times I was actually able to pick it out, due primarily to it's bright red head and cream-colored forehead. I have seen perhaps a dozen Eurasian Wigeon, a few of which were probably hybrids, so I was really trying to key in on the homogeneity of the red on the head to determine if an American Wigeon-type facial pattern was present. I was not able to detect anything that would lead me to believe that this bird was a hybrid. The body of the individual was as expected in a pure bird, fairly clean gray with a contrasting pink breast. The body of this bird did not show any off-color splotches which might indicate hybridization (or molting). 

As my photos are sub-par, I recommend that you take a look at the Durango Bird Club Yahoo Page, where Robert Winslow has posted some more helpful images.
