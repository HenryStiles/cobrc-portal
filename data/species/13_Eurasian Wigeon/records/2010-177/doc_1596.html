Description of the Bird

This individual clearly was different from the abundant American wigeon in a small feeding group. Although the size and shape was quite similar to a typical Am. widgeon, the dark cinnamon colored head with tan top stripe plus a very prominent white wing patch in comparison with adult and juvenile Am. wigeon were clearly present. The back and sides were much grayer also in direct comparison. Both adult and juvenile male Am. wigeon were in the same group, which allowed immediate and thorough comparison.

I did not detect any hybridization appearances on this individual.
Behaviors: This individual was feeding in surface "dabbling" fashion not unlike the other American wigeon (20+) in this localized flock on Lake Catamount. The total estimated Am. wigeon on the lake that day was around 1500 (posted on eBird). The waterfowl were well scattered in shallower areas of the lake mixed with other expected waterfowl species in fall migration.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult, fall (fresh)

Similar Species Discussion

American wigeon of any age will have lighter head, whiter head stripe, and small wing patches when on water. No other typically occurring species was seriously considered.
