Description of the Bird

the bird was a medium sized duck, exactly the same size and shape of the adjacent American Wigeon. The bill was blue-gray, and fairly short for a duck.

The head of the bird was rufous-brown, with a contrasting paler buffy crown. The body of the bird was medium gray, with the lower belly and flanks contrastingly white, and the undertail coverts contrastingly black. I saw the bird flap once, and the underwings appeared solid gray, though this bird was far away and it was hard to be certain of this.
Behaviors: The bird was swimming near the edge of a playa, mostly obscured by tall grass but at times in the open. It associated with a number of American Wigeon and a few Mallards for the entire observation.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

All other duck species were ruled out by the shape and size of the bird, in addition to the head and crown color. American Wigeon was ruled out by the gray sides and back, brown head, buffy crown, and gray underwings. Redhead was ruled out by the gray chest, black undertail coverts, shape, and buffy crown.

Hyrbid American-Eurasian Wigeon was ruled out by the apparent clean gray underwings, as well as a complete lack of American Wigeon plumage features.
