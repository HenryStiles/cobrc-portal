Description of the Bird

I saw a small heron like bird flying from the north to the south. The first thing I noticed was how small it was, which then ruled out all the other herons other than Green Heron and Least Bittern. Then I saw that it had a white belly and a light brown breast and half of the wings. It had a dark cap. Also, a light bluish colored back and wing tips. It had yellow legs.
Behaviors: I saw the bird flying from the north end of the pond to the south end. It landed in the same area where I observed the birds in both 2008 and 2009.
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The white belly and brown breast eliminates all the birds, especially all the egrets, American Bittern, the other large herons, and even Green Heron. The bluish colored wing tips eliminates juvenile Black-crowned Night-Heron and the light colored brown body eliminates juvenile Yellow-crowned Night-Heron and again all the other herons.
