Description of the Bird

The bird was a small heron, about the size of a Green Heron, maybe smaller, with a long, spike-like bill, pretty long legs, and very short tail. It was mostly black on top, including the back and the crown, and mostly pale below, whitish on the belly with lots of straw-colored wash on the sides of the head and streaks of this color extending down the underparts of the bird. Large buffy wing patches were visible in flight, when the bird flew from one patch of reeds to the next.
Behaviors: The bird was a real skulker, singing from low in the cattails well back from the edge, only becoming visible on rare occasions when it would move towards the edge of the cattails or fly across the channel between patches.
Call: The bird gave the primary song of the species, a typically 4- noted "cu-cu-cu-cu," very low-pitched and slightly husky, in a slightly accelerating series.
Plumage: unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Black-billed Cuckoo sounds somewhat similar, but views of the bird eliminated that possibility handily. Juvenile night-herons and other small herons all ruled out by buffy wing patches, dark back and crown, and vocalizations.
