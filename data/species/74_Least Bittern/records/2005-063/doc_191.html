Description of the Bird

Very small long legged wading bird. Long, pointed orange bill with dark, almost purple culmen, yellow iris. Loral patch purplish red. Crown and nape purplish gray. Face mostly orangish brown. Overall body orangish brown, with white vertical streaks on breast transitioning to all white throat. Back primarily dark blueish gray with white stripe in scapulars. White patch also visible in body feathers just in front of wing. Legs and feet were greenish yellowish-green.
Behaviors: The bird was mostly hidden in cattails, persistently singing. A few times the bird was seen clinging to cattail stems a foot or two above the mud, but it was very secretive and mostly stayed out of sight. Twice during my observation the bird flew across a small gap in the cattails. The bird was still vocalizing when I left at about noon.
Call: Repeated, persistent song given all morning long with infrequent breaks, presumably as the bird moved. Song typical of Least Bittern, with two, three, and four syllable coo-coo-coo notes.
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Most other herons and bitterns are much larger, certainly too large to cling halfway up a cattail stem. Green Heron is also small but not this small, sounds different, and has different coloration.
