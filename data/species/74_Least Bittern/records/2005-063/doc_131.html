Habitat

Relatively small patch of cattails at the southwest
corner of Cottonwood Marsh. I did not see the bird leave this small patch of cattails in almost 2 hours.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  10X binoculars and 30X telescope.
Distance:  15' - 20' estimated.
Light:  Bright sunshine behind and to left of observers.

Description of the Bird

A very small, but obvious member of the Heron family. Black head and eye contrasted with orangeish colored bill, face and neck. The bill was
relatively long and pointed for the size of the bird, which was about a foot long. The back was also black, and there were obvious, large creamish patches on the wings. The breast was light, and the legs were yellow. I only saw the bird moving at the base of cattails, and did not see the bird fly.
Behaviors: The bird was moving slowly through the base of a quite small cattail marsh. It was probably looking for food, even though it was singing frequently.
Call: The bird sang very frequently and persistently over
the two hours I was there, and was recorded by
Nathan Pieplow.
Plumage: Alternate

Similar Species Discussion

No other North American heron is anywhere near this small, and none have the bright crean colored patch on the wings, this face pattern, bill color etc.
The only bird like it is the Little Bittern from Europe and Africa. This species has darker legs than Least Bittern, and is extremely unlikely to reach Colorado.

Resources Used

My eyes and binoculars, and European Field Guide to look up Little Bittern.

Previous Experience

Have seen Least Bittern several times in the US and Central America. Have seen Little Bittern a couple of times in Europe and Africa, most recently fall 05.
