Habitat

The habitat was a 30 acre marsh consisting of areas of cattails and open water. The cattails were flooded with several feet of water. A walkable levee surrounds the marsh. Catfish, bass and carp inhabit the marsh.The marsh is located in a riparian area next to the Arkansas River.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Leica 10X42
Distance:  Est. 20 to 40 yds.
Light:  Don't remember. Light was not a factor.

Description of the Bird

Very small heron-type bird. Seen briefly in flight. Light chestnut-colored coverts on dark wings. Dark back. Dark crown. Yellow legs.
Behaviors: The bittern was flushed from the shoreline of the cattail marsh. It quickly flew low over the cattails and dropped back into the marsh. Not seen again that day.
Call: Made a loud squawk when flushed.
Plumage: Adult or juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

American Bittern - is twice as large. Does not have chesnut coverts.

Green Heron - larger and darker than the bittern. Does not have wing patches.

Night-Herons are large and bulky. Gray colored.
