Description of the Bird

I flushed a small heron from the bulrushes along Chico Creek south of May Camp.  It flew and I was able to photograph the bird in flight. I continued walking and it flushed a second time and flew back upstream and was in view as it passed.  It was silent.

It was smaller than a Green Heron but with a heron-type bill.  The overall impression was of a small heron or large rail with predominately orange and brown coloration. I obtained photos which showed me much more including the yellowish bill,  dark brown back, brown-and-white streaked breast, dark cap, and lighter patterns inside the dark-tipped wings and legs with yellowish feet trailing behind as it flew very low.  Very little habitat for it to retreat into.  I did not try to relocated it after I saw the photos.  

Similar Species Discussion

Green Heron is much larger and the wing pattern in flight is noticeably different between the two species and Green Heron almost always gives a loud sqwack when it flies up out of a marsh.  I did at first sight think about Yellow Rail because of the small size and contrasting wing pattern but this was a heron, not a rail.

Resources Used

I didn't use anything other than I looked at my camera playback. The brown back separated it as a female and not the black back and black crown of a male.

Previous Experience

I have seen a few when I worked and lived in the northern part of the Florida Everglades and I have seen five or so in Colorado.
