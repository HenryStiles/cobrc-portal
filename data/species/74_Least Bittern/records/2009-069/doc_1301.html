Description of the Bird

It was a very small heron, roughly the size of a Green-winged Teal with a moderately short, pointed, yellowish bill. In flight, it’s short, stout wings were dark brown across the primaries, primary coverts, and outer secondaries, contrasting with an orangish base to the wing. In flight, yellow legs trailed the tail. When perched, the top of the head, back, and tail were a dark grayish-black. The face was a buffy brown and the eyes were yellow. The throat, breast, and belly were white with diffuse tan vertical streaking across the breast. The bill was mostly yellow, blending to gray across the top of the upper mandible.
Behaviors: The bird was seen in flight about four times. It also was seen perched in cattails.
Call: None heard
Plumage: adult or immature

Similar Species Discussion

American Bittern is much larger, has much darker streaking across the breast, and lacks the black and orange contrast to the wings when in flight.
