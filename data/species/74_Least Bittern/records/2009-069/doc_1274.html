Description of the Bird

small wader with short yellow legs, yellowish bill, and orangish face. Darkish gray back with large orangish wing pannels, seen both on a perched bird and in flight where the wing panels stood out. Cap color grayish but not dark gray. In flight with tips and trailing edge dark gray contasting with inner panel of beautiful orangish.
Behaviors: Responded to tape by flying three times, once over head and always coming very close. Perched as close as 50 feet, feet grasping cattail fronds about 2 feet above the ground.
Call: non heard by me
Plumage: adult female or imm.

Similar Species Discussion

Green Heron comes closest except for Strip-backed Bittern of South America that is totally unexpected. There really is no other wader with the orange and black pattern. Size smaller than imm. Green Heron.

Resources Used

I used Bird of North South America to find the name of the Least Bittern look-alike.

Previous Experience

I have seen a fewer than 15 but I have seen them closely in costal Texas and in Everglades National Park. I have seen one Stripe-backed Bittern in Brazil, flying in a manner similar to the Least Bittern. I have seen 100s of imm. Green Herons while working as a field biologist in South Florida for two years.
