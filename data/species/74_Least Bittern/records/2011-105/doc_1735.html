Description of the Bird

I went to look for a previously reported Least Bittern. I saw the bird twice, very briefly both times in flight. I saw a small heron with buffy wingbars from the dorsal view that had a light, but a proportionately long bill. The sides of the head and its outstreched neck were rufous and it had a black cap. The underwings were medium gray and the feet extended past the tail in flight. I didn't really see anything else in the time the bird was in view.
Behaviors: Twice in a very low and short flight over a cattail marsh, Holcim Marsh.
Call: No vocalization heard
Plumage: possibly juvenile but unknown

Similar Species Discussion

Green Heron is the only look alike species in the U.S./Canada and there were two of this species flying about and giving loud call notes. They were noticiablly darker over all, seemed larger, and were much, much more conspicuous. I saw them first and they were in the air on four occasions for as long as 15 seconds at a time.

Resources Used

I looked for size differences between Green Heron and Least Bittern in the Stokes Field Guide to the Birds and for gizz differences between the two species based on photographs.

Previous Experience

I have seen a ton of Green Herons while working for two winters in Everglades National Park and vicinity and there, here, and in southeastern most Oklahoma I have seen about 12 individual Least Bitterns.
