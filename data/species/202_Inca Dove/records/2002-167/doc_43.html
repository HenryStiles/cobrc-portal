Description of the Bird

It looked something like a miniature Mourning Dove, with a very similar color to that species.

Differences between this bird and a Mourning Dove:

The flanks and the sides of the breast were barred.

It had very scaly underparts. Because the bird was above me, I did not get a good look at its upperparts.

It had a long, square-tipped tail.

When the bird was perched, I could only see a single whitish outer tail feather (on each side).

It had a very distinctive, two-note call.
Behaviors: perched in the foliage of a conifer in a neighborhood
Call: distinctive two-note call
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

The size, shape, and behavior clearly show that this bird was a dove. Its small size immediately rules out larger doves such as White-winged or Eurasian Collared Doves. I rule our Mourning Dove by the much smaller size, the very scaly appearance, and the very different call. Both Common and Ruddy Ground-Doves can be ruled out by the long tail, by the extensive scaly underparts, and by the bold white feather observed on the side of the tail.
