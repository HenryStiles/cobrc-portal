Description of the Bird

A small, long-tailed dove, the bird was among Eurasian Collared-Doves in the town of Kit Carson. It was very much smaller than the EUCDs and had a short and thin dark bill and a whitish face. The upperparts feathers were extensively fringed in black. The tail was nearly as long as the trunk of the bird. The underparts were pinkish-buff with desultory blackish barring.
Behaviors: First seen flying over my vehicle, I found it perched in a snow-covered bare deciduous tree.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

There is nothing particularly similar. Various ground-dove species are ruled out by the long tail and the very scaly appearance.
