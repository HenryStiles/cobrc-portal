Description of the Bird

Pigeon or dove by short bill, small head, short neck, short legs and grayish plumage.  Inca Dove by small size, scalloped look caused by the dark tips of most of the bird's feathers on its back head and breast.  Long tail relative to body and in comparison with the other small dove, Common Ground Dove and much smaller than the nearby Eurasian Collared-Doves.  Gray wings, short with very little primary projection. Silent and seen by me only perched near the trunk of an aspen up about 7 feet off the ground on a small diameter branch.  Seen at times with its eyes closed.  

Similar Species Discussion

Similar in size and looks most like a Common Ground Dove but that species has a shorter tail, black markings on its wings and wing coverts, and its back and wings are without the black feather tips, so no scaled look.  Eurasian Collared Dove is much larger with longer wings, much heavier and with a noticeable black, half-collar.  Both species have a long tail. The Inca Dove also has a white tail as seen from underneath as can be seen in my photos.

Resources Used

None, but I did look at photos of Common Ground Dove in two field guides that have photographs of both species. 

Previous Experience

I used to live in SE Arizona with Inca Doves being seen on a somewhat frequent basis.  Also, have seen Common Ground Doves in SE Arizona but less often and they were more secretive compared with Inca Doves that foraged in parks on the ground and out in the open. Eurasian Collared-Doves are common in Colorado Springs, a few nesting where i live.
