Description of the Bird

Small pale brown dove, scaly look to upperparts, relatively long square tail with white outer tail feathers, dark rufous flight feathers.
Behaviors: Sitting on patio eating bird seed; then flew up to a small tree.
Call: Voice not heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

All other North American doves are much larger than this species except for ground doves, which have shorter tails. Scaly Dove (Columbina squamata) of South America is similar in size and shape and also appears scaly. However, this species has heavier black scaling marks on breast and black secondaries giving the appearance of black outer wings when sitting. This bird lacked that plumage feature (see photo).
