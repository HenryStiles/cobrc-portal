Habitat

Large, open areas of grass with scattered rabbitbrush and junipers. Nearby were the cliffs of the canyon with seeps, chokecherry, and scrub oak. In the riparian were cottonwoods and maples.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  No time to get binocs on them
Distance:  20-25 ft
Light:  sun behind me, excellent light

Description of the Bird

While the two birds had apparently been foraging in a patch of open ground in the bottom of Cottonwood Canyon, I did not observe them until they flushed.
However, I did see them almost immediately and had a good view from about 25-30 feet, at eye level. I was able to observe that they were small doves (specifically, smaller than Mourning Doves), with long tails that were rounded, not pointed. I focused on the tails and noted white outer rectrices their full length. Also, rufous primaries were easily seen. I am familiar with INDO through time spent in Mexico.
Behaviors: The birds flushed from the ground, where they had apparently been foraging in the bare areas between clumps of grass, and flew away at 5-10 feet above the ground
Call: None
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

The birds appeared to be smaller than MODOs, and did not have the pointed tail.

Resources Used
