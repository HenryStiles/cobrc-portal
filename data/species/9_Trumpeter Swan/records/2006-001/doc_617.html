Description of the Bird

These were all adult swans -- very large birds with all-white plumage and very long necks that they held essentially erect, that is, with no kink at the base, as in Tundra Swan (TUSW). The bills were all dark except for salmon-colored cutting edges. The meeting of bill and feathering was angled diagonally throughout, without the vertical aspect of that meeting on TUSWs. There was no yellow on the bills of any of the birds. They dark eyes were not isolated from the bills of the birds as on TUSWs.
Behaviors: Foraging; the behaviors of the birds suggested that they were comprised of two mated pairs.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Adult

Similar Species Discussion

TUSW and Mute Swan both eliminated by posture and head and bill structures
