Description of the Bird

Four very large, white waterfowl. Although I saw only one bird with its head up (the other three were sleeping), all four birds were similar in size and I believe of the same species. The one bird I did see with its head up showed classic Trumpeter Swan characteristics - large, entirely black bill with a straight culmen that adjoined the forehead on the same angle; thick black loral patch connecting the bill with the dark eye; very thick-necked appearance for a waterfowl; and incredible size in comparison to nearby Canada Geese that I attributed to one of the larger races.
Behaviors: Mostly sleeping with heads tucked under wings.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Tundra Swan is the only competitor, but that species is ruled out by bill features (lacking yellow, Tundra has a smaller bill with an obvious curved culmen), thick-necked appearance where Tundra is more slender, and the impressive mass of these birds.
