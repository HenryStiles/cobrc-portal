Description of the Bird

There were two large swans seen at Lake Minnequa in Pueblo. The one that is being documented is an adult in fresh alternate plumage. The fact that it was at times feeding with an adult Mute Swan was most interesting.

I concentrated on the feet, which were huge. I have a photo in case one is wanted. The body was white and large, the neck when not feeding was straight and white, and the head washed in orangy buff with a longish all black bill. The proximal end of the bill ended at a point by the all black eye. Head on the bill formed a "V" at its base. Looking at the profile of the bill, it was easy to see a rather straight edge, following it from the eye to the mandible. The profile of the top surface of the maxilla is straight-edged, not concave. The legs and feet were black, and as mentioned the size of the feet were enormous.
Behaviors: Spent a lot of time feeding, tipped up with rear end sticking straight up and with the front half of the bird submerged. It pulled up large strands of some unknown aquatic vegetation. Although it mostly stayed away from the Mute Swan, there was a 5-minute period when the fed side by side.

Similar Species Discussion

Tundra Swan - quite similar except for size. Tundra Swan has a different profile to the bill, both the top edge at the base that cuts mostly straight across the bird, unlike this one which was "V"-shaped. Also the profile of the bill of Tundra Swan is different, especially where it makes a noticeable curve down by the gape. The Minnequa bird was cut straight from top to gape without the curve.

The other feature that I remembered to look for were the size of the feet. In Tundra, its feet are quite a bit smaller than the large feet of this bird, seen quite well when the bird fed.

The other large swan, actually larger, Whooper Swan, has about half of its bill yellow. This bird had an all-black bill. I have seen Whooper Swan at Klamath National Wildlife Refuge and it is noticeably larger than Trumpeter Swan.
