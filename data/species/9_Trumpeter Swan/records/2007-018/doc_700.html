Description of the Bird

A large whitish bird. It had a lot of dark markings on the bird that makes it look like it is going into its first summer plumage. It was white on the sides and breast of the bird and gray on the head, neck, and back. It was gray The bill is black and has no yellow on it. It also came to a point (or "V") on the forhead. The bill was also pretty straight, not curved. The lores go to the eyes. It had black legs. It was much larger than the Canada Geese. The back appreared to be rounded.
Behaviors: It was swimming around on a small pond, seemed like it was hanging out with several Canada Geese. It didn't act like it was very scared of a train that ran right beside the pond.
Call: Never heard it call
Plumage: Juvenile into first summer

Similar Species Discussion

Tundra Swan - The bill had a "V" shape on the forhead, not a "U" shape. It was also sloped, not curved. It didn't have any yellow on it. The back was evenly rounded. First summer birds are a lot whiter than first summer Trumpeters.
Whooper Swan - It was an all black, it didn't have a half yellow bill.
White Geese - This bird was a lot larger than the Canada Geese. It didn't have any black on the wings or pink / orange bill.
American White Pelican - This bird had a small black bill instead of a really large orage bill with a pouch. It also didn't have black on the wings.
