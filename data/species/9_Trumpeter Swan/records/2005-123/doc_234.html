Description of the Bird

the birds were obvious swans, very large in size, with very long, thin necks and all white plumage. The birds were noticeably larger than nearby Tundra Swans. The neck was long, only slightly shorter than the length of the body. The back was evenly rounded. The head was peaked to the rear of the crown, giving the bird's head a more sloping look than the nearby Tundra Swans.

The bills on the birds were all black, and large and long, with a fairly straight culmen. The cheek contour was evenly curved along its entire length, with no angle. The eye was connected to the bill by a strip of black skin a little wider than the eye, so that the eye looked like it was part of the bill and not isolated on the face. The feathering at the base of the bill on top was pointed, and went further down the length of the bill than the feathering in the same area on the nearby Tundra Swans.
Behaviors: the birds were hanging out with 3 Tundra Swans and numerous Canada Geese on the edge of a pond.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

All species other than Swans were ruled out by the large size and all white plumage. Mute Swan was ruled out by the black bills and straight necks.

Tundra Swan was ruled out by large size in comparison to the nearby Tundra Swans, large bill with straight culmen, evenly curved cheek contour, eye connected to the bill by a wide strip of black skin, pointed shape to the feathering on the top of the bill, sloping look to the head, and evenly rounded back.
