Species, Date, Time and Location Information

Species:  Trumpeter Swan
First Date/Time:  12/29/2005
Last Date/Time: 
Duration (total time in view):  15 minutes
County:  Chaffee
Specific Location:  Farm pond on County Road 163, east of Big Bend Recreation Area. Also pasture on County Road 160, between and opposite Frantz Lake and Mt Ouray State Wildlife Areas.
Number:  6
Age:  Unknown
Sex:  Unknown
Plumage:  Other/Unknown

Habitat

Spring-fed farm pond, open water across the road from the Arkansas River and open pasture lands in the surrounding area.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Swarovksi AT-80 scope
Distance:  farm pond at road edge - pasture approx 50yds from road
Light:  Late afternoon, low, clear light.

Description of the Bird

Large, white birds with long, dark bills. Edge of bill is straight at gape, lores dark and well-connected to the eye. No yellow in lores.
Behaviors: Birds were at rest in a pond near the Arkansas River or in pastures nearby. Later observers noted a lot of preening, bathing behavior. Nothing particular to identification.
Call: none heard
Plumage: all adult

Similar Species Discussion

Separated from Tundra Swan by straight edge of mouth at gape, thick black lores well-connected to eye, long bill. One bird appears slightly divergent... eye more separated from lores, but in size is consistent with other birds in the group.

Resources Used

Sibley North America, Bob & Bob to check the county records, and Tony Leukering.
