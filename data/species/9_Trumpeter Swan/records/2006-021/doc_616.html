Description of the Bird

These were all adults and were very large, all white-plumaged birds, with black bills. They held their necks without a kink at the base. The bills were very long and met the forehead in a long, continuous slant, without the short step typical of Tundra Swan (TUSW). All lacked yellow patches at the base of the maxilla and all sported orangish-red cutting edges. The eyes were not isolated from the bill, as the head's feathering did not pinch them off as in TUSW. Their behaviors suggested that the six birds were comprised of three mated pairs.
Behaviors: Sleeping, loafing, preening, and foraging

Plumage: Definitive

Similar Species Discussion

Posture, head structure, and bill structure rule out the other two swan species.
