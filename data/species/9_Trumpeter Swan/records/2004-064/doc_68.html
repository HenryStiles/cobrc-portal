Description of the Bird

Very large, white waterfowl dwarfing all other waterfowl on the lake. Pure white, except for some rusty coloration on the head and neck. Eyes black. Bill solidly black, large, straight, with the angle of the upper mandible on the same plane as the forehead so that there was no break in angularity. The base of neck curved backwards "resting" on the bird's back.
Behaviors: Swimming with other waterfowl.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Tundra Swan (C. columbianus) has a smaller bill with a curved culmen so that the bill and forehead are not on the same angle; black lores are thinner, so that the eye is more distinct; while swimming, Tundras hold their necks straight up instead of "resting" on their backs; and the forehead feathering is U-shaped.
