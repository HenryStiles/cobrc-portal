Habitat

Along a very small creek with planted native trees, most being Englemann Spruce. The remainder of the habitat is open fields and one field with planted grasses.

Description of the Bird

This is just to document a Merlin that is not a Prairie or richarsonii race, but rather the darker nominate columbarius subspecies.

Dark mantled Merlin with strongly dark anchor marking on its flanks. Crown gray and not brown. Mantle gray and not brown. Mustachal mark indistinct blending into brown, not contrasty face pattern. Light supercillium mostly abscent. Strongly marked dark, not medium brown breast streaks.
Behaviors: Saw it fly into the very top of an Englemann Spruce in Rock Ledge Ranch, a part of Garden of the Gods City Park in Colorado Springs. Sat on top perch for 2 minutes before flying out of sight.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

This Merlin can be separated from the extremely dark, and Pacific Coast suckleyi subspecies that appears much darker than this bird. Suckleyi has a darker head, darker back, darker breast and flank streaking than this bird.

Ricarsonii, or Prairie Merlin has noticeable light brown breast and flank streaks, has a gentle sweeter look than the more fierce expression of this bird and has a lighter mantle with stronger supercilium, and lighter face pattern, especially auriculars.

Previous Experience

I have seen Taiga Merlins in South Texas in late April and on Florida's Dry Tortugas in mid-April, and also in Denali National Park as a breeding species a few times in June when I worked there. I have seen many richardsoni Prairie Merlins in Colorado in migration and in the winter.
