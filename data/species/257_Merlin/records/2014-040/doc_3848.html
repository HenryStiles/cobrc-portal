Description of the Bird

The reason, I'm documenting this bird, was it was the suckleyi subspecies from the Pacific North West.  I think this subspecies is rare in Colorado, though I'm not sure how rare.  The Merlin was quite dark (see photos), was eating on some rodent.  I was driving along Harmony Drive, south of my house in Pueblo West, when I found this bird, in someones front yard tree.  I spent two minutes photographing it.  The landowner was nice, asking what I was photographing, and seemed okay that I was photographing a bird.  I mentioned to the landowner, it was a Merlin, and she saw it.  I didn't mention that it was a rare bird around here.  I've never seen this sub-species around Pueblo.  I think I remember seeing a dark Merlin in SE Colorado, a long time ago.  

Similar Species Discussion

Merlins of the richardsonii subspecies is lighter, as is the columbarius subspecies.  These subspecies are much more likely in Colorado than this black one.

Resources Used

Both Tony Leukering and Steve Mlodinow both agreed that this bird is a "Black" Merlin, after looking at my photos.
