Description of the Bird

While photographing and observing waterfowl along the South Platte River Trail in the section of the trail bordered by the Allgolf Overland Golf Course between W. Evans Ave and W. Florida Ave. in Denver County, I observed a very dark bronze colored goose sitting on a small spit of land within the river. I first observed this bird at 12:45 PM. I observed the bird for 13 minutes. During this time, the bird was mostly resting, but it did turn about 60 degrees to get a brief drink before returning to resting again.

In color the bird was a dark bronze throughout and significantly darker than all geese in the vicinity. The back was very slightly darker than the sides and chest. It had a prominent white ring at the base of the neck. When I first encountered the bird it was facing toward me. It was favorable that the bird turned during my observation time so that the bird had sunlight on it from the side and it was obvious that the dark color was not an effect of the light. Even with direct sunlight the goose was very dark with only a minimal difference in color from breast to sides. It’s white cheek patch appeared to be somewhat more limited than some of the B. c. parvipes around it.

When I was observing the bird is was in the company of several hundred other Canada and Cackling geese along the river. The majority of the geese in the vicinity were B. c. parvipes, however there were at least two dozen B. c. moffitti/maxima within my sight and a small number (approx five) cackling geese also visible. It was quickly apparent that the bird was a Canada Goose as it was significantly larger than all of the Cackling Geese, and had the longer culmen and head shape of a Canada Goose. It was also quite evidently smaller than the Moffits/Maximas in the area with less bulk and a smaller bill (and thus also, theoretically, ruling out B. c. fulva). The bird was closest in size to Lesser Canada Goosse, however it was slightly, but distinctly, larger than the Lesser Canada Geese it was in the company of. Upon evaluation of the photos (though not specifically noted in the field) the culmen length also appears to possibly be slightly longer than B. c. parvipes.
Behaviors: During my observation the bird was mostly resting, but it did turn about 60 degrees to get a brief drink before returning to resting again.

Similar Species Discussion

As this bird has the structure of a Canada Goose and was larger than a B. c. parvipes, Cackling Goose can be easily eliminated. In regards to the sub-species of Canada Goose Lesser Canada Goose is eliminated predominantly by color. Even the darker populations of Lesser Canada Goose are more gray in tone, not bronzy like this bird. It is, however, more difficult to be confident about eliminating Vancouver Canada Goose (Bc fulva), though a fulva should be much closer to the size of a moffit rather than that of a parvipes. Dusky Canada Geese normally fall between Bc moffitti and Bc parvipes in size, matching this bird well.
