Description of the Bird

Medium-sized plover (about Killdeer-sized) with drab sandy-gray upperparts, whitish underparts, dusky wash to sides of upper breast, whitish eyebrow, short thin black bill. In flight, lacks bright contrasting plumage.
Behaviors: Running and standing on muddy shoreline of playa lake. Flying low to ground.
Call: none
Plumage: undetermined

Similar Species Discussion

Killdeer more brightly plumaged, with two black bands across chest. All golden-plovers larger with more patterning all over. Mongolian Plover (aka Lesser Sand-Plover) with dark smudge across upper breast, less obvious pale eyebrow, and smaller size. Greater Sand-Plover can look very similar in non-breeding plumage (see http://www.arkive.org/greater-sand-plover/charadrius-leschenaultii/image-G54421.html). I believe the bill of this species is larger relative to the head than for Mountain Plover.
