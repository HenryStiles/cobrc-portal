Description of the Bird

The bird was a small loon, barely larger (in length) than nearby Western Grebes. It was distinctly two-colored, being dark on the upper side from crown to tail and white on the underparts from chin to vent. It was distincly shorter- and thicker-necked than were the Western Grebes with a shorter (about 4/5 distance from bill base to nape), quite thin, and darkish bill. The eyes appeared dark on the white face. The meeting of the dark of the upperparts and white of the underparts was distinct and sharp from the crown, over the eye, and down the neck to the shoulder.

The upperparts were spangled with small white dots. The sides were a bit mottled dark and light, though appeared mostly white in all but the best views. The head was held slightly above the horizontal.
Behaviors: Swimming on surface and diving from surface after food.
Call: none
Plumage: basic
