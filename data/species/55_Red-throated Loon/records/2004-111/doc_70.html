Description of the Bird

I recognized the bird as a loon as soon as I saw it (long body, thick neck, and upright posture). It was difficult to tell feather details due to the distance, but striking features that I didn't need a scope to see were the bright white parts including the entire face, entire neck except the hindneck, and sides (the bird was floating high in the water). The crown, hindneck, and back were dark gray. The light gray bill was very thin and pointed, with a slight upward angle.
Behaviors: Swimming (resting on water).
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

The bright white sides of the neck and slim, angled bill eliminate all other species of loon. Aechmophorus grebes are eliminated by the bird's long body, short neck, and thin gray bill. The bird was in close proximity to several Western Grebes for good comparison of body structure.
