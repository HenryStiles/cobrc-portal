Description of the Bird

I first saw the loon on the afternoon of 17 March.  The weather was quite windy with whitecaps on the lake and difficult viewing. I called Nic Korte and he came out and took some photos  On the evening of March 17, Nic Korte sent some photos out to Coen Dexter, Mark Peterson, and David Suddjian among others.  The experts indicated it was a Red-throated Loon. I went back the next morning and had excellent looks under calm conditions with the wind at my back and was able to confirm for myself that it was a Red-thrpated Loon. 

The loon was about the same size as the Western Grebes close by.  It had a thin, pale upturned bill with dark forehead and head peaked in the rear.  Mostly white face and neck with gray hind neck and darker body with no white speckling on the back.  Faint light gray spot on side of neck and darker spot on throat in good light.  Showed some white on sides while swimming.

Similar Species Discussion

The pale bill angled up reminded one of a Yellow-billed Loon, but the bill was too thin and the loon too small.
