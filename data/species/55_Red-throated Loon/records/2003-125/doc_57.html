Description of the Bird

I observed the bird in the water, next to a Common Loon and several Western Grebes.

It had a similar build to the Western Grebes, but its neck was shorter (their heads were farther above the water with the neck extended).

Compared to the Common Loon, it was much paler, somewhat shorter, with a very much lighter build (lighter body, much thinner neck).

Its back was a medium brown, with light spots.

The back of its neck was a pale gray. The front half of the neck was whitish, with a gradual transition to the darker nape.

The face was whitish from about the eye on down, with a gradual transition to a light brownish cap.

The bill was narrow and pale, with an obvious upturned appearance.
Behaviors: It was swimming in the water and occasionally diving, like a loon or grebe.
Call: none heard
Plumage: nonbreeding

Similar Species Discussion

From its lanky build and diving behavior, I can rule out all species except:
grebes
loons
cormorants
It was much too pale to be a cormorant. The only grebes with long necks are: Western, Clark's, and Red-necked Grebe. By direct comparison, this bird had a much shorter (and dingier colored) neck than a Western or Clark's Grebe. The whitish face, thin bill, and white spots on the back rule out Red-necked Grebe.

Red-throated Loons are usually easy to separate from other species of loons. The very light build (by direct comparison with a Common Loon) strongly indicates Red-throated Loon. The pale face, white spots on the back, and the lack of sharp transitions between dark and light portions of the plumage, all favor Red-throated Loon.
