Description of the Bird

The bird was a small loon with a long, thin, pointed, blue-gray bill that was upturned. The head was somewhat rounded and was a bit snake-like in shape. The crown and nape were a dark gray-brown. The lores, face, throat, side neck, breast, and belly were white. The upperparts were grayish brown, heavily sprinked on back with small white spots. The flanks had dark brown diffuse streaks. The wings appeared black. I was able to get nice comparisons between it and nearby Common Loons and the Red-throated was noticeably smaller.
Behaviors: Observed swimming and diving.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Red-throated Loon differs from Yellow-billed by much smaller size, smaller, thinner gray bill, more rounded head shape, the amount of white on the face and throat, the lack of zig-zag patterning to the upperparts, and the lack of dark facial spots. Common Loon is larger, lacks the extensive white coloration, and has a thicker, straight bill. Pacific Loon has a smaller, straight bill, much broader gray across the sides of the neck, and lacks the white spotting across the upperparts, although they do have scaled, white fringing to the mantle feathers. Aechmophorus grebes differ by having black crowns and napes, have yellow bills, and have longer necks.
