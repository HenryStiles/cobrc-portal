Description of the Bird

I was scanning the lake from Sailboard launching area with my binoculars and noticed a loon shaped bird way across the lake, out from the South Shore Marina. I then got out of the car, and got the scope out to see what it is. It was a small loon, with thin up turned bill, grayish back, and a dark throat patch. I quickly realized the bird is a Red-throated Loon. Too small for Common Loon, bill too thin and upturned for Pacific Loon. I couldn't make out that throat patch was reddish, though, from the distance the bird was from me, that wasn't surprising.
Behaviors: Swimming and diving on Pueblo Reservoir.
Call: none
Plumage: breeding plumage adult

Similar Species Discussion

Pacific Loon - larger bill, that is held straight, not thin and upturned.

Arctic Loon - larger bill, bigger bird.

Common Loon - larger bird, larger bill, bill held straight.

Yellow-billed Loon - much larger bird.
