Description of the Bird

Two birds - one report

Alternate bird - This was a slender and seemingly short- necked, smallish loon (as compared to the nearby Common Loon) with very narrow delicate (for a loon) upturned bill. From a great distance this bird seemed to be uniformly medium to dark gray with a noticeable reddish throat when seen in just the right light. The bird was identfied mostly by its profile as described above. The bill was thin, short and dark.

Basic bird - This was also a slender and short-necked smallish loon as compared with Western Grebes nearby. The narrow upturned bill was easy to see, even from a great distance. The bird had a whitish throat and light gray nape that contrasted with the darker gray back and dark bill. Also noticeable where whitish flanks that contrasted with the darker back coloration. Both birds were often seen low in the water compared to the large grebes, even when they were loafing with their heads tucked.
Behaviors: Both birds spent a lot of time on the surface loafing and the alternate plumaged bird spent a large amount of time with its head tucked. It did dive on three occassions.
Call: none heard although I played a recording of song for attempted response but got none.
Plumage: one basic and one alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Noticeably smaller and more petite bill than nearby Common Loon

Superficially similar to stockier Pacific Loon. Pac Loon will also hold head at upright angle at times but bill is stouter and seemingly longer and neck is thicker and often cocked at an angle that reminds me of a coiled snake ready to strike.

Arctic Loon has an even more massive bill than RTLO and PALO and even though has similar whitish flanks, has a more powerful build.

Previous Experience

I have seen a few in CO, mostly in the fall. I have seen quite a few breeders in Alaska and a few migrants along the Oregon coast in flight where the back color differences between other loon species is noticeable as well as the bill thickness contrast.
