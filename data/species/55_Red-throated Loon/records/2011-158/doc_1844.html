Description of the Bird

A small loon with a small, pointy bill that was held above vertical at almost all times the bird was under observation. The bill was thin and pointy and a fairly neutral gray, not yellow and large eliminating Yellow-billed Loon. The angle at which it was held probably rules all other loons except Yellow-billed.

The head was dainty and the neck slender. The face showed a pure white cheek with a dark cap and evidence of an eye ring or crescent. Behind the cheek the neck and throat were generally a diffused pale gray in contrast to both the white cheek and dark cap. The throat was not white nor was there a strong contrast between neck and throat. The generally grayish throat and neck with little or no contrast rules out all other loon species.

The back was dark with paler speckling evident. The flanks were bright white, but that may have been accented when the bird rolled slightly to the side.
Behaviors: Diving for food often on a large reservoir; at one point the loon caught a relatively large fish and swallowed it. Then it proceeded to rest/sleep.
Call: n.a.
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

Bill shape and posture and throat and neck color and pattern rule out all other loons.
