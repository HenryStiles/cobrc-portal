Description of the Bird

This was a small loon; about the same size as nearby Western Grebes, but with a shorter neck and a more substantial body. The crown down to the eyeline was a darkish gray; the rest of the head and neck were light but mottled. The rest of the visible bird was also gray and with some mottling on the back. The bill was a light color. The bird was sleeping quite a bit, but when it raised its head, it immediately went into the position where the bill is raised about 10 degrees above the horizontal. This is the same way that a D-c Cormorant holds its bill.
D-c Cormorants were also on Baseline Res, but this bird was distinctly smaller and differently shaped.
Behaviors: The bird was sleeping quite a bit, but raised its head three or four times while I watched it. I did not see it dive; it always remained on the surface.
Call: None Heard
Plumage: First Winter

Similar Species Discussion

The other Loons are larger than this bird, and do not hold the bill above the horizontal. This was a rather delicate loon with a small bill. The back mottling also separates from the other loon species.
Smaller than a D-c Cormorant and swims differently than both D-c and Neotropic Cormorants.
