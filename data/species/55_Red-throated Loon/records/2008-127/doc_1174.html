Description of the Bird

We first spotted this bird far away and noted that it seemed "small". Originally, we thought that we were most likely observing a Pacific Loon, because that species would be more expected. One of the first things we noticed about the plumage was the amount of white on the flanks, which made us excited as we discussed the possibility of an Arctic Loon! We then decided to change locations since the bird was moving and had become closer to another parking area. Once we arrived at the new location the bird was much closer and we able to see the bill shape better and realized that we were probably viewing a Red-throated Loon. The lower mandible was noticeably upturned. Also, with our closer view we noted the amount of white extending up to the eye and around the eye which is more indicative of a Red-throated Loon. We also noted the barring on the back of this loon and it seemed more characteristic of a Red-throated Loon (a series of v's going acrossed the back in rows).
Behaviors: This loon, even when far away, seemed to take notice of our presence and was constantly attempting to get as far away from us as possible. At one point, the loon took flight and we thought it was going to head for another waterbody. It flew over the reservoir a couple of times and eventually landed.
Call: none heard
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Pacific Loon - the bill shape was not correct for Pacific Loon. Also, the amount of white on the flank eliminated this species.
