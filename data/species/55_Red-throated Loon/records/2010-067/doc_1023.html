Habitat

large plains reservoir.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  15 X 45 zoom scope
Distance:  about 200 to 400 yards out into the lake
Light:  The sky was mostly clear with the sun lowering to the Southwest giving very good light on the bird. It was very windy so the bird had to be viewed through some fairly choppy water.

Description of the Bird

Size: At these distances it was difficult to judge actual length but the bird appeared to be slightly longer than the Mallards and Western Grebes that were seen nearby.
Bill: Gray, sharp and appeared upturned.
Head: Face was clean white and the crown and nape were gray.
Upper parts: Hind neck was gray. The back was dark gray with rows of small white spots.
Under parts: Throat, breast and belly (seen when bird rolled to preen) were white.
Feet: Set well back to the rear of the bird (seen when bird was preening), webbed.
Behaviors: The bird sat low in the water, lower than the grebes and ducks that were nearby. It was observed diving frequently and at one point stopped to preen, rolling on it’s side to get to the breast and belly.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Adult Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon is larger and lacks fine white spots on back. Pacific and Arctic have dark unspotted backs and darker face and neck.
