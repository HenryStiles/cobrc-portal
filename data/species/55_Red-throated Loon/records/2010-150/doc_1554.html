Description of the Bird

A very small bird with a long lengthy body. It had a short thick neck. The bill was generally pointed upward and it was a small gray bill. The body was fairly dark showing a little white patch on the lower flank. Most of the neck (front and back) was gray as was the head. The adult bird had a pretty much all gray neck and face, showing that it was molting from alternate to basic plumage.
Behaviors: First found it flying around, then it landed and started swimming and diving, saw it several times during the day
Call: Didn’t hear it
Plumage: 1 adult molting from alternate to basic

Similar Species Discussion

An all gray neck and face eliminates all other loons and grebes. The upward posture of the bill would suggest it only being Red-throated and Yellow-billed, but the small bill would easily eliminate Yellow-billed. The short thick neck would eliminate Clark’s and Western Grebes.
