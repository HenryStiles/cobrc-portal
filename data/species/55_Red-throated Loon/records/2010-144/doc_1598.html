Description of the Bird

Adult: small loon with thin bill; lower mandible slightly upturned at the gonys. Head small and rounded (not blocky like a COLO or YBLO); Face, chin, throat, and chest white with a clear, straight line of demarcation on the side of the neck between the white front of the neck and the grayish crown and hind neck. Eyes were visible in the white portion of the face. Head and beak held above horizontal. Back dark (almost black) with white flecking;

Immature: small loon with thin bill; lower mandible ?upturned? at the gonys. Head small and rounded (not blocky like a COLO or YBLO); Front, sides and rear of neck dirty tan fading to a light tan at the front of the neck; front of neck with a small patch of reddish-brown coloration in the center of the neck; chin white.
Behaviors: Both loons were found swimming and diving together along the face of the dam.

Adult was seen November 10-27, 2010
Immature was seen by me only on November 10, 2010
Call: No vocalizations were heard
Plumage: 1-adult basic, 1-first basic

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon - Large loon (not small), massive straight bill, head typically held at horizontal and not above; large block-headed look, eye not completely in the white of the face. Demarcation between dark brown of the rear of neck and white front of neck is not straight, but instead contains indentions.
Yellow-billed Loon - Large loon (not small), massive pale to bright yellow bill; large block-headed look, eye not completely in the white of the face. Demarcation between dark brown of the rear of neck and white front of neck is not straight, but instead contains indentions. Pale tan ear spot behind the eyes.
Pacific Loon - Dark brown to dark gray loon with rounded crown; bill thick and straight not thin and upturned;
Cormorants - adults all black with orange-yellow gular sacs and beak; Beak longish and hooked at the tip.
