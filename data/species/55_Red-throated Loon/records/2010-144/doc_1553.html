Description of the Bird

Both birds were the same size, which was a long body, with short neck and a very small bill, which was help upwards most of the time. There were no other birds hanging out with these two. They had gray neck both without any patterning. One had a darker gray neck than the other. They both had a little white near the lower flanks. The white in the face went to/slightly above the eye. They both had black backs.
Behaviors: Swimming and diving close to the dam
Call: Didn’t hear it call
Plumage: 1 Adult and 1 Juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

The upturned bill would suggest that is only Red-throated or Yellow-billed Loon, but the small bill would easily eliminate Yellow-billed. The white in the flanks would eliminate everything other than Red-throated or Arctic and also Clark’s Grebe. The short thick neck would eliminate Clark’s and Western Grebe. No patterning in the gray on the neck would eliminate all the other loons as would the sheer size of the bird.
