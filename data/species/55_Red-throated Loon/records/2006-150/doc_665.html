Description of the Bird

A small "dainty" loon, body size a little larger than a Western Grebe's (Aechmorphorus occidentalis) body. Back and side dark brown. Sides and back of neck dirty gray. Front of neck dirty white with a dark "patch" just below the throat. Crown gray. Auriculars, cheek, chin and upper throat white. Eye dark reddish. Bill drab and dirty straw. Bill thin, relatively short (compared to other species of loon) with the lower mandible slightly angled upwards towards the tip. Head and bill held above horizontal. Crown rounded. No clear line of demarcation between gray of hindneck and lighter color of throat (like a Pacific Loon (Gavia pacifica) would have).
Behaviors: Swimming and diving. I did not see this bird surface with anything in its bill. No interaction with nearby Western Grebes.
Call: None noted
Plumage: first year basic

Similar Species Discussion

Common Loon - LARGE and large billed. Noticeable "invasions" of white into the dark of the side neck. Block headed not rounded like Red-throated.
Yellow-billed Loon - LARGE and large billed. Noticeable "invasions" of white into the dark of the side neck. Block headed not rounded like Red-throated.
Pacific and Arctic Loon - lower mandible not angled upwards to the tip; sides of neck show a clear demarcation of the dark hindneck with the white of the throat. Overall much darker back, neck and crown that Red-throated

Previous Experience

Have seen all five species of loons in Colorado. Many Common and Pacific Loons and lesser numbers of Red-throated and Yellow-billed Loons. Arctic Loon three times - twice in Colorado.
