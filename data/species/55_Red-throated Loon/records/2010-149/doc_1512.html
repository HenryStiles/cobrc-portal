Description of the Bird

This was a small loon in comparison to the larger COLO nearby. The bill was almost always angled upwards from the head as it swam. The back appeared gray brown in mostly backlit situations. The back had very small buffy streaks, not spots, on it. The throat was dark with a triangle of white encroaching from the whitish breast. The neck was thin for a loon. The bill was grayish, the lower "mandible" sharply angled upwards about for the distal 1/5 of its length. The chin was whitish, contrasting with the dark neck, face and back. There appeared to be a small peak at the back of the crown. The dark face separates it from an adult winter bird.
Behaviors: The loon was loafing but alert. I did not observe it dive. It looked, but otherwise did not respond to a playback of a recorded RTLO call.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenile

Similar Species Discussion

No other loon species has a neck this thin and all other loons have larger bills. Only YBLO holds head angled up like this RTLO and it is a much larger bird with much different coloration and build.
