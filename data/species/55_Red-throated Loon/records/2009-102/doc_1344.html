Description of the Bird

This was one of the 5 loon species. Overall the coloration was light gray. The bill, short, thin and light gray was frequently held at an angle above the horizontal. The face was whitish and the back was medium gray and checkered. The neck was grayish without noticeable contrast or markings.
Behaviors: Rolled forward into a dive freqently.
Call: none heard
Plumage: First winter

Similar Species Discussion

Because the bird was relatively close, it could be separated from other loon species by its lighter gray coloration, lighter face, shorter upturned bill, unpatterned neck and posture when cruising, with bill pointed at a low angle above the horizontal.
