Description of the Bird

the bird was a small loon, about the size of Western Grebes it was right next too, but with a shorter and thicker neck, larger head, and shorter bill, but with a similar overall shape, with a long body riding low on the water. The bill was quite short and thin for a loon, and for much of the observation held above the horizontal. The overall shape, while bulkier, with a shorter neck than Western Grebe, was thin and "snakey" for a loon.

The head of the bird was on the pale end of medium gray, as was the back of the neck. The throat and front of the neck were also gray, with some white mottling, especially on the throat. The back was darker gray, and the bird had white flanks running the length of the body. The chest was white.
Behaviors: The bird was diving actively, often, and for long periods of time, but managed to stay in the same general vicinity, always close to and in association with Western Grebes.
Call: none heard
Plumage: juvenal

Similar Species Discussion

Western Grebe was ruled out by the much shorter and bulkier neck, shorter and thinner bill, overall slightly bulkier shape, and grayer plumage overll without sharply demarcated white and black.

Common and Yellow-billed Loons were ruled out by much smaller size and slimmer porportions, especially the neck, head, and bill.

Pacific Loon was ruled out by the slimmer proportions, less "puffy" head giving the bird a somewhat "snakey" look, and extensive white flanks.
