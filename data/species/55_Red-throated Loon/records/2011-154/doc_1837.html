Description of the Bird

Bill Maynard and I were at Sailboard launching area, about to leave, when I spotted a loon that appeared to be small. After looking at it in the scopes, we could tell it was a small loon, with a thin gray ish colored bill, which the bird held up tilted. The bird was light gray overall. The head and back were gray, with a whitish colored face. The bird was loon shaped and smaller and lighter colored then the Common Loon we had just seen. The bird was also lighter gray then the two Pacific Loons that we saw after this bird.
Behaviors: Swimming on the large reservoir.
Call: none
Plumage: basic plumage

Similar Species Discussion

Pacific Loon -- small loon with straight bill thicker than this bird.

Arctic Loon -- small loon thicker up tilted bill.

Common Loon -- bigger loon with bigger bill.

Yellow-billed Loon -- big loon with yellowish bill.

Previous Experience

Lots in CA and other states. Regular in the fall it seems at Pueblo Reservoir. Seen all the loon species. I know all of them well. I've seen 3 Arctic Loons, some Yellow-billed, a lot of the other three species.
