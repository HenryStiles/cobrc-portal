Description of the Bird

loon with thin bill pointed upwards some of the time. This bird was in bad light and far away but there were Common Loons nearby for size and shape comarison. This is based on the jizz, size, and shape, especially the bill. I took photographs but the heat shimmer kept photographs from being diagnostic although scope view were. Brandon Percival found the bird and we tried to photograph and digiscope it but were mostly unsuccessful with that endeavor.
Behaviors: Actively feeding, diving and under surface for as much as 25 seconds at a time. In the middle of the reserovoir with large greebes, mergansers, and Common Loons nearby.
Call: none heard
Plumage: first winter

Similar Species Discussion

Yellow-billed Loon holds neck and bill in similar posture but that is a large loon and this one was slender-neckes and thin-billed

Pacific Loon - I would have been able to see the contrats between the throat and hind neck that this bird did not show. Pac Loon also has different shaped bill.

Common Loon - there were lots of these loons to compare with the RTLO and they were noticeably larege with large bills and the heads where peaked an longer appearing compared to the rounded headed look of the RTLO
