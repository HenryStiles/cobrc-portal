Description of the Bird

Bird flushed from by my side, about hip or thigh level and flew across clearing with light to my back as I watched it. Initial presumption was Poorwill as it was clearly a goatsucker, but it lacked the very short-tailed bat-like appearance. I got my bins up quickly and noted the following:

Larger, longer-tailed and browner than Common Poorwill. Buff on outer tail feathers seen easily as bird spread tail to come to landing. Wings rounded, not pointed like Chuck-wills-widow. Overall wingspread seemed closer to Common Nighthawk than Common Poorwil, but did not appear longer than that of Common Nighthawk. No white on wings. Tail not forked like a nighthawk

Similar Species Discussion

The Caprimulgids most similar to this bird (taking it as a Eastern/Mexican Whip-poor-will) would be Poorwill, which I've flushed many of this year. They appear very stubby of wing and tail and have a bat like appearance. If flushed into sunlight, they appear rather gray. This bird was larger, with its longer tail and wings immediately apparent. It also flushed from a tree branch rather than the ground. The buff on the outer tail feathers is consistent with Whip-poor-will and does not fit either Chuck-wills-widow or Poorwill. This bird was also browner than a Poorwill but not as chestnut as a typical Chuck. The bird was not as large as a Chuck-wills-widow and Chucks have even longer tails and pointed wingtips.

Previous Experience

I've not seen a Whip-poor-will fly for some time. I have flushed a fair number of Common Poorwills over last couple years. I've only caught glances at Chucks in flight.
