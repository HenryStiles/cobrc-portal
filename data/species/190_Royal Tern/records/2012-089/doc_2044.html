Description of the Bird

This was a large tern, larger than nearby Ring-billed Gulls and nearly as large as nearby California Gulls.
It was considerably larger than the commonly seen Forster's and Common Terns. The head had a black stripe running from just behind the eyes round the back of the head. The forehead and the top of the head were white. The bill was orange/light red and was not too long and quite thick, especially near the base. The back and upper wings were light gray, except for the primary tips, which were rather dark and blackish. The underparts and both upper and underside of the tail were pure white. Leg color was quite difficult to see, but was certainly dark. I didn't see the bird nearly as close as the photo uploaded.
Behaviors: Sitting on an island in the middle of a large plains reservoir. I also observed it flying around and plunge diving in typical tern fashion: diving into the surface, but not deep at all because it would take off rather quickly and resume flying around.
Call: None heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Elegant Tern is the closest in plumage. However, this bird was too large for Elegant, the bill was too short and too thick, and the primaries were too black. Also, the top of the head was white whereas I think it is usually dark in Elegant Tern.

Nearer the same size is Caspian Tern. However, that bird is much bulkier and heavier than Royal Tern, and the bill is much larger and blood red in all plumages. Also, Caspian doesn't show white on the top of the head in basic plumage.

The other North American terns are much smaller.
