Description of the Bird

This was a large tern as compared to the Forster's Tern that was nearby and the Cal Gulls it was roosting near. The large bill was orange without darkening towards the tip and it stood out in the early afternoon harsh light. The other most noticeable feature was the spiky black head feathers that extended in a narrow, but broadening line from behind the eye to the nape where they formed an unrully looking muff that contrasted strongly with the white head, cheeks, and hindneck. The rest of the face was white and the wing feathers were light gray. The legs were blackish. The folded primaries were a darker shade of gray than the light gray back. I saw it briefly in flight and could see what I thought were darker edges on the leading and trailing edge of the primaries.
Behaviors: Other than a brief flight to change locations on the island beach where it loafed, the tern mostly stood at attention by Cal Gulls and Canada Geese.
Call: no calls heard
Plumage: early molt - maybe winter adult

Similar Species Discussion

Lesser Crested Tern - comes closest perhaps in comparison but highly unlikely in CO and with more slender bill and upperparts are described as two-toned - something I did not observe - the bill on this bird was stout and would never be called thin

Caspian Tern - is much larger and its bill is larger and has a dark tip; with much more areas of dark on wingtips. Also never has the face pattern of a non-breeding Royal Tern with this bird's white forehead and head.

Elegant Tern - bill thinner and tapers to a tip that is sometimes downturned with lighter yellowish or yellow orange bill. Black of crest of Elegant includes the eye unlike on Royal, but it was too far to see this for sure although it appears to be a space between the eye and the remains of the dark crest on this bird (see photos).

Resources Used

Previous Experience

I worked 5 summers on Assateague Island Nat'l Seashore where Royal Terns bred and have seen many off South Padre Island, TX, in spring. Seen Elegant Terns in Southern California and both Royal and Elegant together in winter in Panama but haven't seen ROTEs up close for a few years now.
