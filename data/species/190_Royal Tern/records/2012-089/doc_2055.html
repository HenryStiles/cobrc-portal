Description of the Bird

Large tern, roughly size of Caspian Tern, with similarly colored mantle. Head white except relatively narrow tonsure of black feathers from just behind eye (but not meeting eye) back around nape. These feathers formed a short crest when ruffled by wind or raised (rarely) by bird. Eye appeared black Bill orange tinted yellow, thickness and length nearly what one would expect from a Caspian Tern, but a bit narrower. No black or dark markings on bill. Underparts uniformly white. Upperwings gray, similar in color to Caspian Tern, excepting primaries. The outer 2/3 of primaries or so where dusted with a darker gray. The underwing was mostly white, with some dark edging on the primaries. The tail was white, a bit more forked than that of a Caspian, but not as deeply forked as a Forster's. Size
Behaviors: Mostly stood amongst flock of Franklin's, Ring-billed, and California Gulls with a couple Forster's Terns salted into mix. Occasionally would preen. Every once in a while would circle around lake, plunge diving, obtaining prey (small silver fish about size of bill, presumably chad?) about 1/3 of dives.
Call: not heard
Plumage: adult basic

Similar Species Discussion

Caspian Tern would have had redder bill, smudged with black, black underprimaries, different head pattern. More similar Elegant Tern would have had narrower and proportionately longer bill, longer crest behind head, and black of tonsure reaching eye. Usually redder bill as well
