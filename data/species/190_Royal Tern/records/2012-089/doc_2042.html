Description of the Bird

Large tern (similar in size to nearby Franklin's Gulls) with mostly pale gray upperparts. Wingtips appeared to be unmarked, black. Legs black, proportionately kind of short. Head mostly white with limited black high on nape & short black crest. This black region did not appear to reach eye, which stood out separately in whitish face (a bit of smudging may have reached the eye but only subtly so.) Bill yellowish-orange, long and modestly heavy and with only slight decurvature.
Behaviors: During my observation the bird rested on a low-lying muddy island just offshore, sometimes above water and sometimes standing in the shallow water. The bird alternated between sleeping and looking around. The island roost was shared by Franklin's, Ring-necked, and California Gulls, Great Blue Herons, American White Pelicans, and American Coots.
Call: not heard
Plumage: Basic

Similar Species Discussion

Body structure & bill proportions points to a tern. Size (directly comparable to Franklin's, Ring-billed, and California Gulls) rules out most tern species which are smaller. Black Skimmer much darker, shows unique underbite & bicolored bill. The only real ID contenders other than Royal Tern (ROYT) are Elegant Tern (ELTE) and Caspian Tern (CATE). ELTE shows a shaggier crest and the dark band through the nape extends to the eye, giving it a more masked appearance. Also, ELTE has a thinner-looking, longer & more decurved bill, and I would expect it to perhaps be a darker orange. CATE has an even more robust, darker bill. In basic plumage CATE has a nearly complete dark cap that encompasses the eye and lacks the short shaggy crest.
