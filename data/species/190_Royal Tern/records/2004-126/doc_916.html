Description of the Bird

It was a large, stream-lined, pearl-mantled white tern with rather slender, long orange bill and well-forked tail. The crown/cap was black and raggedy to the posterior. The legs were fairly short and black. The face, neck, throat, and belly were clear white. The scapulars and coverts were a light gray. The primaries were a medium gray, blending toward black. It was smaller, although not substantially, from nearby Ring-billed Gulls.
Behaviors: The bird stood along the lakeshore the entire time I observed it.
Call: None heard.
Plumage: Adult alternate

Similar Species Discussion

Caspian Tern is larger, has a more squared-off rear of the black cap, has a thicker, stouter, and redder bill, and has somewhat darker primaries, and has slightly longer legs. Elegant Tern is smaller than Royal Tern, has a longer, thinner, drooped orange bill, has a longer, shaggier crest to the black cap, and has lighter colored primaries.
