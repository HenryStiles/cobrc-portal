Description of the Bird

Obvious male quail with a black head plume. Black eye and throat, with a white eyeline and white vertical stripe. White spots on the hindneck and scaled underparts with a chestnut belly patch distinguish this species from Gambel's Quail. White streaking on the sides with a dark gray back; dark yellowish legs: see attached photographs.  The bird called quite frequently, and sang once on the morning of April 14.

Similar Species Discussion

Gambel's Quail eliminated by the white spotting on the hindneck, and scaled underparts with a chestnut belly patch; see attached photos.

Resources Used

Previous Experience
