Description of the Bird

I first heard the quail giving a low caw call and some of its sputtering call. I then spotted it under some sagebrush just south of the parking lot at the Canyon Visitor Center feeding on the ground.

It was a typical male quail with a head plume and and chunky body. I distinguished it from Gambel's Quail due to the darker crown, buffy patch above the bill, strongly scaled nape, scaled belly with a reddish center and brownish flanks. Also when it did give its Chi-ca-go song, it was clearer and stronger than what I expect from Gambel's Quail.

I thought that the sighting was unusual at the time but didn't get any documentation at the time since I didn't realize how rare this species was and I have way too many pictures of California Quail in my yard. Only when I got to the hotel that night did I check ebird and the CFO website and realize that this species hasn't been photographically documented in Colorado before and I felt pretty stupid.

I convinced my wife to go back to the Visitor Center the next morning to document the quail and luckily enough it was still there. I was able to mimic its song well enough to get it to fly up to some honey locusts in the parking lot and obtain photos and some ok audio recordings.

Similar Species Discussion

Gambel's Quail is pretty similar but this bird had a darker crown, a buffy patch above the bill, strongly scaled nape, a scaled belly with a reddish center and brownish flanks.

Previous Experience

I get California Quails in my yard every day in Salt Lake City with up to 30 at a time in winter. Gambel's Quail are also common in southeastern Utah and I get to see them on trips down there about 3 or 4 times a year.
