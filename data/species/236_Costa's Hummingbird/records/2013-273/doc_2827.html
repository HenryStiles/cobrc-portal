Description of the Bird

Small hummingbird with greenish back and dull white belly. Some purple feathers visible on gorget, longest on birds' right side, and over time some additional purple feathers visible on side of face.   On 20 February 2014 bird had full purple gorget. Bill short and maybe slightly curved near tip. Tail short. On perched bird wingtips appear to extend just beyond tail tip.  Sides of face with gray cheeks with noticeable white supercilium.

Behavior variable over time as it tried to survive a 5-day subfreezing cold spell so it increased the number of visits per hour as the cold approached.  Also visited honeysuckle with a few flowers and he attempted to feed at base of unopened flowers on a few honeysuckle plans. 

(The bird was first found on 1 November by non-birders.  They contacted Aiken Audubon for information on trying to keep it alive during November cold but the message was not seen until later in the month.  Three of us, Jeannie Mitchell, Mark Peterson, and I were permitted to view it on 26 November and on subsequent dates.) 

Similar Species Discussion

The other species considered were Black-chinned Humminbird which has a longer bill, longer tail, no color on sides of head, and the close relative, Anna's Hummingbird which has pink, not purple, gorget feathers. 

Resources Used

The best guide for I.D. was the Howell, Hummingbirds of North America, but also helpful was the Peterson Guide, Hummingbirds of North America by Sheri Williamson.

Previous Experience

I have seen fewer than 10 in Arizona only until this one in El Paso County.
