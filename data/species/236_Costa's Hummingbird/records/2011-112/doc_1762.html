Description of the Bird

This bird was smaller than nearby Broad-tailed and Black-chinned Hummingbirds, and about same size as Callliope. Tail was very short and similar in shape to Calliope. Interior rectrices(R-5 & 6) were especially short. Bill was thin and longer than Calliope but shorter than most female Black-chins.

Crown was greenish, auriculars grayish with broad white supercilium extending behind the eye & curving downward around grayish auriculars. Thin black eyeline extended into lores to the bill.

Back & nape were greenish; throat was solid white as was rest of underparts, except turning a dusky off-white on the flanks. There was no buff on the underparts nor any streaks on the throat.

Tail was very short and dark excepting white tips on outer rectrices.
Behaviors: Bird was observed coming to Hummer feeder; fed at 5 to 10 second intervals. Actively pumped and wagged its spreaded tail while feeding & approaching feeder.
Call: None heard
Plumage: insure if immature or adult

Similar Species Discussion

Female Black-chins have noticeably larger tails closer to Broad-tail Hummers;Costa's tail more similar to Calliope in size & shape. Black-chinned females have a more distinct post-ocular white dot obscured in Costa's by broad white supercilium.

Female Calliope have buffy flanks and lack the whitish flanks of Costa's. Female Calliope's show streaked throat and not the solid unmarked throat of the Costa's I observed. Female Calliope lacks the broad white supercilium of Costa's.
