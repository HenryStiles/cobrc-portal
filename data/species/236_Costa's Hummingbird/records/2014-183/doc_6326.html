Description of the Bird

To begin.  I was not the finder nor identifier of this bird.  This was a chase for a state bird.

From Steve Bouricius on November 6, 2014:
"This morning I captured and banded a Costa's Hummingbird in the Redlands area of Grand Junction.  The bird has been using a feeder and flowers in a small private court yard.  When I first saw the bird the initial impression was that it looked and behaved like a female Black-chinned, partly because it pumped it's tail continuously while hovering, as Black-chinned typically do.  Good thing I didn't dismiss it so readily.  Ten minutes after setting up the trap I gently closed the door and could immediately tell it was a Costa's by it's distinctive chip notes.  Measurements, examination of plumage characteristics, and the smooth bill confirmed the ID as an adult female Costa's Hummingbird.  She is in good shape weighing a normal 3.2 grams and is carrying a moderate amount of fat which suggests she may yet migrate or wander farther."

My description of the bird at the Cooksey's:
  Smaller hummingbird
  When sitting, had a hunched posture
  No apparent neck (head rested right on the shoulders)
  Metallic green extending from top of head to the tail.
  Wings dark - blackish
  Undersides largely pale white-gray
  Very limited greenish tones on flanks (visible in my photo)
  Very pale gray auricular patch (visible in my photo)
  Bill solid black, longish for the size of the bird and slightly de-curved
  Wings extended very slightly beyond the tail (visible in Glenn's photo)
  Outer feathers in tail had white tips (visible in Glenn's photo)
  Metallic chip call.

Similar Species Discussion

Colorado has four regularly occuring hummingbird species:

Broad-tailed: Has significant tail projection, buffy flanks and spotted cheeks.  Chip call wrong.

Black-chinned: Has a neck, slightly longer tail projection, grayer belly, grayer crown and some gorget area streaking.  Chip call wrong.

Rufous: Has significant tail projection and at a minimum rusty flanks.

Calliope: Has buffy undersides, thoat striping and a thin straight bill.

The other related rarity:

Anna's: Has longer tail projection, straight bill, extensive greenish to gray on the lower belly and streaking on the gorget area.
