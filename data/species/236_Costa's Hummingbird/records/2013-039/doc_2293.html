Habitat

Suburban yard - various thick bushes and shrubs at property line, several fruit trees, one evergreen. The feeder was on the porch with a heat lamp due to the cold weather.

Viewing Conditions

Optics:  Nikon 10x42 and Canon 7d with 420mm lens
Distance:  50 feet - estimated distance
Light:  The sun was obscured by thin cloud cover, however there was plenty of light for observation, and the sun was to our backs illuminating the bird well.

Description of the Bird

We observed a small sized female humming bird. While perched the wingtips projected just beyond the tail length. Pale underparts were very visible and pale area above the eye was noted although harder to pick out.

A photo is attached.

In addition, this bird was captured earlier in the day by a hummingbird bander. Here is his description:

"We set up two hummingbird traps, one in place of the existing feeder and another in a new location. It wasn't long before the bird came to check out the new feeder and we gently closed the trap door. After banding, taking data and a few photos, the bird returned to the feeder immediately after being released. She weighed 3.2 grams which is substantial and healthy for a Costa's, but she had no visible fat reserves which suggests she may be done migrating for now. Given our experience with the wintering hummers in the past, I believe this hummingbird will do just fine so long as insects and sugar water are available." - Steve Boricious
Behaviors: The bird was sitting for long periods of time in bushes about 25 feet away from a feeder on a porch. It did visit the feeder for short amounts of time between sitting. The bird was totally silent while we watched.
Call: No vocalizations while I was present.
Plumage: adult

Similar Species Discussion

Female Black-chinned does not have clean white front, and lacks pale area above the eye. Other similar sized female hummingbirds such as rufous, broad-tailed and calliope have buffy flanks.
