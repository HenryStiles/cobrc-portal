Description of the Bird

I observed it during two of its visits to a feeder. Both times, I studied it through a spotting scope.

It was a small, compact hummingbird with a pot-bellied appearance.

It had a short, straight, all dark bill, and uniform greenish upperparts, except for the tail. I didn't note the color of the central part of the upper tail surface, but there were bold, white tips to the tail.

The face showed a dark eye, a prominent white spot behind it, and a broad gray auricular patch running down and bit back from the eye. An obvious vertical whitish band separated the auricular patch from the nape, except right at eye level where there was no gap.

The throat and breast were whitish. The bird had broad gray flanks with no trace of buff, and clean white undertail coverts.

When it was perched, I studied the relative length of the wings and tail. They were very nearly equal.

It pumped its tail vigorously while feeding, and somewhat less so when perched.
Behaviors: It flew, hovered, and fed like a hummingbird. The only behavior which might be useful for identification to species was:

It pumped its tail vigorously while feeding, and somewhat less so when perched.

I am not sufficiently familiar with Costa's Hummingbird to assess the value of this behavioral trait.
Call: Not heard

Similar Species Discussion

The closest species are probably:
Anna's
Black-chinned
Ruby-throated
I ruled out Black-chinned by the white vertical stripe behind the gray auricular patch, and the total absence of buff in the flanks. The same characteristics of the auricular patch, plus the nearly equal length of wings and tail when perched., rule out Ruby-throated. I ruled out Anna's by 1) the clean white throat and breast, 2) the nearly equal length of wings and tail when perched.
