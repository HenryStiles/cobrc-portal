Description of the Bird

This was a small female plumaged hummingbird. It had a green head and back, a pale front and black wings. It had a short black bill, which was very slightly curved. It had a pale eyestripe and small post-ocular spot. It had an area of gray extending backwards and downwards from the eye, which is characteristic of Costa's, and the throat was light. The short tail had no rufous at all, was mostly green with black towards the lower part of the retrices. The outer three retrices were tipped with white. When perched on the lamp next to the feeder, it was quite easy to see that the tail and the wingtips were almost exactly the same length. This is another good fieldmark for Costa's. When at the feeder, the bird pumped its tail continuously in a regular fashion. Costa's do this in contrast with Anna's, which hold their tails relatively still.
Behaviors: The bird was seen sitting at the top of a bush at on the lamp near the feeder, and drinking from the feeder. The
location was a suburban backyard in Clifton. The tail pumping was used to help separate from Anna's hummer.
Call: No sounds heard
Plumage: winter

Similar Species Discussion

Anna's - tail length, lighter breast, pattern of gray in face, slightly curved bill, and continuous tail pumping.
Black-chinned - tail length, pattern of gray in face, bill shorter and not as curved, different shape and posture:
calypte are shorter and squatter with not much neck, whereas archilochus stand taller with an elongated neck.
Ruby-throated - tail length, pattern of gray in face, different shape and posture.
Broad-tailed, Rufous, Allen's and Calliope - no rufous in tail and elsewhere.
Broad-billed, Blue-throated and Magnificent - smaller size, completely different face pattern, tail length, etc.
